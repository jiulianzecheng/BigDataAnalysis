 '''Sedgwick''' is a village and [[civil parish]] in [[South Lakeland]], [[Cumbria]], England. In 2001 there were 380 people living in Sedgwick. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.ons.gov.uk/help/localstatistics|title=Local statistics - Office for National Statistics|website=www.ons.gov.uk}} < /ref >  The name Sedgwick comes from the Norse language  " Sigg(e) " . < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.visitcumbria.com/sl/sedgwick/|title=Sedgwick|publisher=}} < /ref >   
 
 It has 5 [[listed building]]s. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/england/cumbria/Sedgwick|title=Listed Buildings in Cumbria|website=britishlistedbuildings.co.uk}} < /ref >  These include two structures, a bridge and an [[aqueduct]], which were built for the [[Kendal to Lancaster canal]].  The canal once went through Sedgwick. However, it has been drained of water. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.thecumbriadirectory.com/Town_or_Village/location.php?url=sedgwick|title=, Cumbria|website=www.thecumbriadirectory.com}} < /ref >
