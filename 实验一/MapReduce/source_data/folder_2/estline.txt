 '''EstLine''' (sometimes abbreviated: '''Estline''') was an [[Estonia|Estonian]]-[[Sweden|Swedish]] [[Shipping Line]] founded in [[1990]] which operated [[Ferry|ferries]] between [[Stockholm]], [[Sweden]] and [[Tallinn]], [[Estonia]]. it was a company owned by both ''Nordström  &  Thulin'' and the ''Estonian Government''. 
 
 She is best known for [[MS Estonia|Sinking the Estonia]] during a [[storm]], known to have claimed 852 lives. It would finally be dissolved in the year [[2001]], and its ships would be sold to [[Tallink]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Virolainen Estline-varustamo konkurssiin|url=http://www.kauppalehti.fi/avar/plehti/trindex.jsp?xid=41416 & date=2001/08/02|url-status=dead|publisher=Kauppalehti|language=Finnish|accessdate=2008-01-28|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080422013134/http://www.kauppalehti.fi/avar/plehti/trindex.jsp?xid=41416 & date=2001%2F08%2F02|archivedate=2008-04-22}} < /ref > 
 
 == Ships == 
 Note: is a incompleted list of EstLine fleet. 
 
 * [[M/S Vana Tallinn|MS ''Nord Estonia'']], later MS ''Vana Tallinn'' (1990–1994) – Scrapped in [[Aliağa]], [[Turkey]], 2014 
 * [[MS Estonia|MS ''Estonia'']] (1993–1994) – Sank 1994 
 * MS ''Nord Neptunus'', later MS ''Neptunia'' (1992–1999) – Scrapped in [[Aliağa]], [[Turkey]], 2007

 * MS ''Baltic Kristina'' (1997–2000) – Now MS ''Rigel'' for [[Ventouris Ferries]]
