{{nihongo|'''''Bunka'''''|文化}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Kyowa|Kyōwa]]'' and before ''[[Bunsei]].''  The period started in January 1804 and ended in April 1818. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005).  " ''Bunka'' "   ''Japan Encyclopedia,'' p. 91. < /ref >  During this time, the emperors were {{nihongo|[[Emperor Kokaku of Japan|Kōkaku]]''-tennō''|光格天皇}} < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA546  " Kōkaku Tennō, " ] p. 546. < /ref >   and {{nihongo|[[Emperor Ninkō|Ninkō]]''-Tennō''|仁孝天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA716  " Ninkō Tennō, " ] p. 716; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac]]. (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA420  ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 420-421.] < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Bunka'' era== 
 [[File:A portrait of Hayashi Jussai 林述斎像稿本.jpg|thumb|right|Portrait of Hayashi Jussai who advised [[Emeror Komei]] on foreign affairs in the 1st year of ''Bunka'']] 
 * '''1804''' (''Bunka 1''): [[Hayashi Jussai]] explained the foreign policy of the [[Tokugawa shogunate]] to the emperor in Kyoto. < ref > [[Louis Cullen|Cullen, Louis M.]] (2003). ''A History of Japan, 1582-1941: Internal and External Worlds'', pp. 117, 163. < /ref > 
 * '''June 1805''' (''Bunka 2''): [[Genpaku Sugita]] was granted an audience with [[Shogun]] [[Tokugawa Ienari]] to explain differences between traditional medical knowledge and Western medical knowledge. < ref > Sugita Genpaku. (1969). ''Dawn of Western Science in Japan: Rangaku Kotohajime'', p. xvi. < /ref > 
 * '''25 September 1810''' (''Bunka 7, 27th day of the 8thmonth''): < !-- NengoCalc 文化七年八月二十七日 -- >  Earthquake in northern Honshū (Latitude: 39.900/Longitude: 139.900), 6.6 magnitude on the [[Richter Scale]]. < ref name= " NOAA " > [http://www.ngdc.noaa.gov/ U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration] (NOAA), [http://www.ngdc.noaa.gov/nndc/struts/results?bt_0= & st_0= & type_17=EXACT & query_17=None+Selected & op_12=eq & v_12=JAPAN & type_12=Or & query_14=None+Selected & type_3=Like & query_3= & st_1= & bt_2= & st_2= & bt_1= & bt_4= & st_4= & bt_5= & st_5= & bt_6= & st_6= & bt_7= & st_7= & bt_8= & st_8= & bt_9= & st_9= & bt_10= & st_10= & type_11=Exact & query_11= & type_16=Exact & query_16= & bt_18= & st_18= & ge_19= & le_19= & display_look=1 & t=101650 & s=1 & submit_all=Search+Database  NOAA/Japan: Significant Earthquake Database]; retrieved 2011-12-13. < /ref >   
 * '''7 December 1812''' (''Bunka 9, 4th day of the 11th month''): < !-- NengoCalc 文化九年十一月四日 -- >  Earthquake in Honshū (Latitude: 35.400/Longitude: 139.600), 6.6 magnitude on the Richter Scale. < ref name= " NOAA " / > 
 * '''24 December 1813''' (''Bunka 10, 2nd day of the 11th month'') < !-- NengoCalc 文化十年閏十一月二日 -- > : Former-[[Empress Go-Sakuramachi]] died. < ref > Meyer, Eva-Maria. (1999). [https://books.google.com/books?id=6wEvo4wBojcC & pg=PA186  ''Japans Kaiserhof in der Edo-Zeit'', p. 186]; [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/117/index.html 後桜町天皇 (117)]; retrieved 2012-5-27. < /ref > 
 * '''1817''' (''Bunka 14''): Emperor Kōkaku traveled in procession to Sento Imperial Palace, a palace of an abdicated emperor. The Sento Palace at that time was called Sakura Machi Palace. < ref > {{Cite web|date=|title=NATIONAL ARCHIVES OF JAPAN {{!}} Digital Gallery|url=http://jpimg.digital.archives.go.jp/kouseisai/category/emaki/sakuramachi_e.htm|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080119222417/http://jpimg.digital.archives.go.jp/kouseisai/category/emaki/sakuramachi_e.html|archive-date=2008-01-19|access-date=2022-11-25}} < /ref > 
 * '''7 May 1817''' (''Bunka 14, 22nd day of the 3rd month'') < !-- NengoCalc 文化十四年三月二十二日-- > : Kōkaku [[Abdication|abdicated]]; and the [[Order of succession|succession]] passed to his son (''senso'').  Soon after, Emperor Ninkō's role as [[monarch]] was confirmed by [[Ceremony|ceremonies]] (''sokui''). < ref > Meyer, Eva-Maria. (1999). [https://books.google.com/books?id=6wEvo4wBojcC & pg=PA50  ''Japans Kaiserhof in der Edo-Zeit,'' pp. 50]; [[H. Paul Varley|Varley, H. Paul]]. (1980). ''Jinnō Shōtōki'', p. 44;  a distinct act of ''senso'' is unrecognized prior to [[Emperor Tenji]]; and all sovereigns except [[Empress Jitō|Jitō]],  [[Emperor Yōzei|Yōzei]], [[Emperor Go-Toba|Go-Toba]], and [[Emperor Fushimi|Fushimi]] have ''senso'' and ''sokui'' in the same year until the reign of [[Emperor Go-Murakami]]. Compare ''Kunaichō'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-about/seido/seido06-ph.html  Ceremony of Accession (''Sokui-no-Rei'')]; retrieved 2012-6-30. < /ref >
