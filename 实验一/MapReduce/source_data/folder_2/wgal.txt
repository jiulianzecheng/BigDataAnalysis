'''WGAL''' [[NBC]] television channel in the [[Lancaster, Pennsylvania|Lancaster]]/[[Harrisburg, Pennsylvania|Harrisburg]]/[[York, Pennsylvania|York]] ([[Susquehanna River|Susquehanna Valley]]) area. It can be viewed on channel 8. Its studios are located in Lancaster, PA. 
 
 ==History== 
 WGAL-TV first appeared on television on March 18, 1949 on channel 4 as the fourth television station in Pennsylvania. It was owned by the Steinman family, owners of WGAL radio (1490 AM) and Lancaster's two major newspapers. At the time, Lancaster was the smallest city in the country with its own television station.{{fact|date=February 2008}} On 31 December 1952 WGAL moved to its current location on channel 8. On January 1, 1954 WGAL showed its first color television program, the [[Tournament of Roses Parade]]. WGAL has always been an NBC television station, but also carried some programs from [[CBS]], [[DuMont Television Network|DuMont]], and [[American Broadcasting Corporation|ABC]] until 1963. The Steinmans sold WGAL-TV to Pulitzer in late 1978. Pulitzer sold WGAL-TV to [[Hearst-Argyle Television]] in 1999. The change took place on March 18, the station's 50th anniversary.
