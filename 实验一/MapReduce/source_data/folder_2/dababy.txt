 '''Jonathan Lyndale Kirk''' (born December 22, 1991), known professionally as '''DaBaby''' (formerly known as '''Baby Jesus'''), is an American rapper. After releasing several mixtapes between 2014 and 2018, he became [[Popularity|popular]] in 2019. 
 
 His first album, ''Baby on Baby'' (2019), peaked at number seven on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. It featured the single  " [[Suge (song)|Suge]] " . His second album, ''Kirk'' (2019) debuted at number one. It featured the single  " [[Bop (DaBaby song)|Bop]] " .  
 
 His third album, ''Blame it on Baby'' was released in 2020. Its single  " [[Rockstar (DaBaby song)|Rockstar]] "  (featuring [[Roddy Ricch]]) reached number one on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. 
 
 DaBaby won the Top Rap Song award at the 2021 Billboard Music Awards for  " Rockstar " . 
 
 He has also been involved in many controversies including killing a man at a [[Walmart]] in 2018, going on a [[Homophobia|homophobic]] rant during his 2021 [[Rolling Loud]] performance, and fighting his ex-girlfriend [[DaniLeigh]]'s brother at a bowling alley in 2022. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/dababy-walmart-shooting-death-1341829/|accessdate=August 29, 2023|title=Exclusive: Video Undercuts DaBaby’s Self-Defense Claim in 2018 Killing|publisher=[[Rolling Stone]]}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/dababy-homophobic-rolling-loud-comments/|accessdate=August 29, 2023|title=DaBaby Receives Backlash for Homophobic Comments While Performing at Rolling Loud Miami|publisher=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.67950/title.dababy-fight-danileigh-brother-brandon-bills-bowling-alley-video|accessdate=August 29, 2023|title=DaBaby Gives Ex-GF DaniLeigh's Brother Brutal Beatdown at Bowling Alley|publisher=[[HipHopDX]]}} < /ref >
