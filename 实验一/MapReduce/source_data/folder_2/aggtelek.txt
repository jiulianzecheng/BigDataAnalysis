 '''Aggtelek''' is a village in [[Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén]] county. 
 
 == Location == 
 Aggtelek is in the [[Hungary|Hungaro]]-[[Slovakia|Slovakian]] border. 
 
 === Connections === 
 Aggtelek got a minor road heading between [[Szin]] and [[Sajókaza]]. The village also got a small road that connects [[Slovakia]] with it. 
 
 == History == 
 The village was already exist before the [[Hungarian conquest of the Carpathian Basin|Hungarian conquest]]. The cave was known even that time, [[Mihály Vitéz Csokonay|Csokonay]] and [[Sándor Petőfi|Petőfi]] was in it. In 1858, a fire destroyed the entire village; only the school and one residential buildings remained. In the [[World War II]], the soviet troops ruined Aggtelek. When new mines opened near [[Kazincbarcika]], Aggtelek got several commercial chances. In 1995, the [[Baradla caves]] become part of the [[UNESCO|UNESCO Heritage Sites]].
