 '''''Hammam''''' is a [[Turkish]] [[steam bath]] and also a [[Bathhouse|public bath]]. < ref name=BBC > {{cite news|publisher=[[BBC]] Travel|accessdate=July 8, 2021|title=The origins of bathhouse culture around the world|url=https://www.bbc.com/travel/article/20121129-the-origins-of-bathhouse-culture-around-the-world|date=November 29, 2012|author=Suemedha Sood}} < /ref >   This is common in the [[Middle East]]. Hammam is not used just to be clean. Hammam is also about [[Wudu|spiritual purity]] or ablution.   
 
 == History == 
 Bathhouses were found throughout the Mediterranean world. They were used to the mid-6th century in the early [[Byzantine Empire]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6nGPDwAAQBAJ|title=A Cultural History of Bathing in Late Antiquity and Early Byzantium|last=Zytka|first=Michal|date=2019-03-28|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-351-13409-5|language=en}} < /ref >   
 
 In Europe hammam was also popular, especially in [[Victorian era]].
