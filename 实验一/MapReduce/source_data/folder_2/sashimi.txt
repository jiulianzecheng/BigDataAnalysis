 {{nihongo|'''Sashimi'''|刺身|さしみ}} is a type of  [[Japanese food]].  It is very fresh [[fish]] which has been sliced into thin pieces. < ref name= " nussbaum " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA343 & dq=  " Sashimi " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 343. < /ref >   Some sashimi is cooked, but most seafood is served fresh. 
 
 The most popular fish in sashimi include: 
 * {{nihongo|[[Salmon]]|鮭 |Sake}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Squid]]|いか |Ika}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Shrimp]]|えび |Ebi}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Tuna]]|まぐろ |Maguro}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Mackerel]]|さば |Saba}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Horse Mackerel]]|あじ |Aji}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Octopus]]|たこ |Tako}} 
 * {{nihongo|Fatty [[Tuna]]|おとろ |Otoro}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Japanese amberjack|Yellowtail]]|はまち |Hamachi}} 
 * {{nihongo|Puffer Fish [[Takifugu]]|ふぐ |[[Fugu]]}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Scallop]]|ほたて貝 |Hotate-gai}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Sea Urchin]]|ウニ |Uni}} 
 * {{nihongo|[[Whale meat]]|鯨肉 |Gei-niku}} 
 
 The art of making sashimi is in the choice of fish and the way it is cut into pieces. < ref name= " nussbaum " / >
