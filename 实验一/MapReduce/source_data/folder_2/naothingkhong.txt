 '''Meidingu Hongnemyoi Khunjao Naothingkhong''' ({{lang-omp|Meitingu Hongnemyoi Khunchao Naothingkhong}} < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & q=naothingkhong+hongnem+yoi+khunjao & pg=PA83|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref > ) was a [[Meetei people|Meetei]] ruler of [[Ningthouja dynasty]] of [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). He is the successor of [[Ura Konthouba]] and the predecessor of [[Khongtekcha]]. He ran away from the [[Kangla]] at the age of about 10 or 12 years and went to live along the [[Shelloi Langmai]]s in the [[Nongmaiching Hill]] in the eastern [[Manipur]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & q=naothingkhong+kangla+10+12+shelloi+langmeis+nongmaijing+hills & pg=PA83|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref >  He was mad in love with [[Pitang-nga]], a Langmai girl and married her. < ref name= " Concept Publishing Company " / >  They lived in her parents' house. This is the first reference to the matrilocal residence in the [[history of Manipur]]i ethnicity. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & q=naothingkhong+matrilocal+residence+reference & pg=PA83|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref >  According to ancient sources, he has six wives from different clan principalities of [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & q=naothingkhong+six & pg=PA83|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref >  In fact, he has nine total wives, out of which three are childless. So, in general cases, it is regarded as he has six wives. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NkMwAQAAIAAJ & q=naothingkhong+nine+wives+children+three|title=Politics, Society, and Cosmology in India's North East|last=Brara|first=N.
