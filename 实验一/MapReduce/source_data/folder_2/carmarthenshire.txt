'''Carmarthenshire''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ər|ˈ|m|ɑr|ð|ən|ʃ|ər|,_|-|ʃ|ɪər}}; < ref > [https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/carmarthenshire  " Carmarthenshire " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181107012108/https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/carmarthenshire |date=2018-11-07 }}, ''British  &  World English Dictionary'', Oxford Dictionaries Online < /ref >  {{lang-cy|Sir Gaerfyrddin}}; {{IPA-cy|siːr gɑːɨrˈvərðɪn|}} or informally ''{{lang|cy|Sir Gâr}}'') is a [[subdivisions of Wales|unitary authority]] in [[South West Wales|southwest]] [[Wales]], and one of the [[historic counties of Wales]]. The largest town in Carmarthenshire is [[Carmarthen]], a market town with a population of 14,000.  The area has many farms, and the land is very rural and has many hills. There are many towns, such as Llanelli and Ammanford.
