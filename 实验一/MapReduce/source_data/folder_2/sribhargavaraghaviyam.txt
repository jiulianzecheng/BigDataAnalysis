 '''''Śrībhārgavarāghavīyam''''' ({{lang-sa|श्रीभार्गवराघवीयम्}}) (2002), meaning ''Of Parashurama and Rama'', is a [[Sanskrit]] [[Epic poetry|epic poem]] ([[Mahākāvya]]) made by [[Jagadguru Rambhadracharya]] (1950–) in 2002. It is made of 2121 verses in 40 Sanskrit and [[Prakrit]] [[Meter (poetry)|metres]] and is separated into 21 cantos (''Sarga''s) and each contains 101 verses. < ref name= " kkbvp " > {{cite web |url=http://www.kkbirlafoundation.com/downloads/pdf/vach-2007.pdf |title=वाचस्पति पुरस्कार २००७ |trans-title=Vachaspati Award 2007 |language=Hindi |publisher=K. K. Birla Foundation |accessdate=8 March 2011|archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110713154542/http://www.kkbirlafoundation.com/downloads/pdf/vach-2007.pdf|archivedate= July 13, 2011}} < /ref >  The epic is the story of the two Rama [[Avatar]]s – [[Parashurama]] and [[Rama]]. This is found in the [[Ramayana]] and other [[Hindu]] books. ''Bhārgava'' is used for Parashurama, as he was born in the family of the religious teacher [[Maharishi Bhrigu]], while ''Rāghava'' is used for to Rama as he was born in the royal family of king [[Raghu]]. The poet was awarded the [[Sahitya Akademi Award]] for Sanskrit in 2005, < ref name= " sa2005 " > {{cite web | title = Sahitya Akademi Awards 2005 | year = 2005 | publisher = National Portal of India | url = http://india.gov.in/knowindia/sakademi_awards05.php | accessdate = 24 April 2011 | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20110409185243/http://india.gov.in/knowindia/sakademi_awards05.php#sanskrit | archivedate = 9 April 2011 | url-status = bot: unknown }} < /ref >  and many other awards for this work. 
 
 A copy of the epic with a Hindi commentary by the poet himself was published by the [[Jagadguru Rambhadracharya Handicapped University]], [[Chitrakuta]], [[Uttar Pradesh]]. The book was released by the then [[Prime Minister of India|prime minister of India]], [[Atal Bihari Vajpayee]] on October 30, 2002.
