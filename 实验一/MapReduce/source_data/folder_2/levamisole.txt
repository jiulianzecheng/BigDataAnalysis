'''Levamisole''' is a [[drug]] that was used to treat [[infection]]s with [[parasite|parasitic]] [[worm]]s, and for some forms of [[chemotherapy]], to treat [[cancer]]. The drug was discovered in 1966. It was withdrawn from  the [[US]] and [[Canada|Canadian]] markets in 2000 and 2003, because of a serious risk of [[side effect]]s; better drugs can replace it. < ref > {{cite news |url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m3374/is_18_22/ai_68536218/ |title=Janssen discontinues Ergamisol |last=Frederick |first=James |work=Drug Store News |date=November 27, 2000 |accessdate=2009-08-13}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.pharmacists.ca/content/hcp/tools/drugnews/discontinued.htm |title=Products Discontinued from the Market Since Publication of the 2000 CPS |publisher=Canadian Pharmacists Association |accessdate=2009-08-13 |archive-date=2008-09-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080919134815/http://www.pharmacists.ca/content/hcp/tools/drugnews/discontinued.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >   
 
 Currently, levamisole remains in [[veterinary medicine|veterinary]] use as a [[dewormer]] for livestock. The drugs has also been used as an [[adulterant]] in [[cocaine]] sold in the US and Canada.
