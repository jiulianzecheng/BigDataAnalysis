{{nihongo|'''Eien'''|永延|}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name|Japanese era]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Kanna (era)|Kanna]]'' and before ''[[Eiso]].''  This period started in April 987 and ended in August 988. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA170  " ''Eien'' " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 170. < /ref >   The reigning emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Ichijō|Ichijō]]''-tennō''|一条天皇}}. < ref > [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac]]. (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA150 ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 150-151]; Brown, Delmer ''et al.'' (1979). [https://books.google.com/books?id=w4f5FrmIJKIC & pg=PA302 ''Gukanshō'', p. 302-307]; Varely, H. Paul. (1980). ''Jinnō Shōtōki'', pp. 192-195. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of ''Eian'' era== 
 * '''987''' (''Eien 1, 10th month''): The emperor visited the home of [[Fujiwara no Kaneie]]. < ref name= " titsingh150 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA150 p. 150]. < /ref > 
 * '''987''' (''Eien 1, 11th month''): The emperor visited [[Iwashimizu Hachiman-gū]]. < ref name= " titsingh150 " / > 
 * '''987''' (''Eien 1, 12th month''): The emperor visited the [[Kamo Shrine]]. < ref name= " titsingh150 " / > 
 * '''988''' (''Eien 2, 11th month''): The emperor visited the home of Kaneie on his 60th birthday. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA151 p. 151]. < /ref >
