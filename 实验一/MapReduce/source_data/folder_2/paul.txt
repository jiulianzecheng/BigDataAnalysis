 The name Paul [ pawl ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Paul {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/paul|access-date=2023-04-03|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a Latin name derived from the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] family name Paulus, which is derived from the [[Latin]] word  " paulus "  meaning  " small "  or  " humble " . It is also associated with the Latin word  " paullus "  meaning  " little " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=Paul name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Paul-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-08|website=babys.name}} < /ref > 
 
 The name Paul is a popular name in many countries around the world, including the United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, England, Scotland, Ireland, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Portugal, and many more. This name was ranked #257 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-04-03|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref > 
 
 The name Paul is often associated with the [[Bible|Biblical]] figure [[Paul the Apostle|Saint Paul]], who was an important figure in the early Christian Church. He was a missionary, theologian, and author of several books of the New Testament.

 *[[Paul Hunter]], English snooker player 
 *[[Paul Vallas]], American politician and school administrator 
 *[[Paul McCartney]], British rock musician, former member of The Beatles and Wings
