 '''Alitalia''' was an [[Italy|Italian]] [[airline]] It was once the [[flag carrier]] and largest airline of Italy. It ended operations on October 14, 2021 and was replaced as Italy's flag carrier by [[ITA Airways]], which began operations the next day. 
 
 == History == 
 The  " first "  Alitalia was founded on [[September 16]], [[1946]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.boeing.com/commercial/aeromagazine/aero_05/textonly/a01txt.html|title=Alitalia Airlines|website=www.boeing.com}} < /ref >  Its first flight was on [[May 5]], [[1947]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.seatmaestro.com/airlines-seating-maps/alitalia-airlines-air-one/|title=Alitalia Airlines - Air One Seat Maps|website=SeatMaestro}} < /ref >  In [[1960]], Alitalia started flying [[jet aircraft]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://corporate.alitalia.it/en/history/history/index.html|title=Alitalia|website=corporate.alitalia.it|access-date=2019-02-14|archive-date=2019-02-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190214160145/http://corporate.alitalia.it/en/history/history/index.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  In [[1995]], Alitalia tried to merge with [[KLM]], but it failed. < ref > {{cite book| author = Ivo Van Bael| title = Competition Law of the European Community| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=qJutEOtr39kC & pg=PA1311| year = 2005| publisher = Kluwer Law International| isbn = 978-90-411-2309-1| page = 1311}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  [[1998]] was the first profitable year for Alitalia. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://airwaysnews.com/blog/2014/06/25/etihad-rescue-alitalia/ |title=Etihad to Rescue Alitalia, Buy 49% Stake, Analysis |access-date=2016-02-15 |archive-date=2015-05-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150523114310/http://airwaysnews.com/blog/2014/06/25/etihad-rescue-alitalia/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In 2001, Alitalia joined [[SkyTeam]]. The airline lost more than €3.7 billion since 1999 until 2008. < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/sppage023-lk25141-oisbi-idINLK2514120081121|title=Alitalia to lose 1 bln euros in 2008, takeover sealed|newspaper=Reuters |date=21 November 2008|via=www.reuters.com}} < /ref >  On [[January 12]], [[2009]], the  " first "  Alitalia shut down. The next day, on [[January 13]], the  " second "  Alitalia began. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2009/BUSINESS/01/12/alitalia/|title=Alitalia lands international partner - CNN.com|website=www.cnn.com}} < /ref >  In June 2014, [[Etihad Airways]] bought 49% of Alitalia. On [[January 1]], [[2015]], a  " third "  Alitalia began. This one is 49% owned by Etihad and 51% owned by Italian [[shareholder]]s. < ref > http://www.etihad.com/en/about-us/etihad-news/archive/2015/the-new-alitalia/ < /ref > 
 
 == Destinations == 
 Alitalia flew to 102 destinations in 41 [[country|countries]]. 28 of these cities are in [[Italy]]. Alitalia's main hub was at Rome Fiumicino Airport.
