 '''Drumchapel''' is a part of [[Glasgow]], [[Scotland]]. < ref name=PeakinHM > {{cite web |url=https://www.holyrood.com/articles/inside-track/side-side-focus-bearsden-and-drumchapel |title=Side by side: a focus on Bearsden and Drumchapel |author=William Peakin |date=December 28, 2014 |website=Holyrood Magazine |publisher=Dods Group plc |access-date=January 24, 2017 |archive-date=May 24, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190524221333/https://www.holyrood.com/articles/inside-track/side-side-focus-bearsden-and-drumchapel |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It became part of Glasgow in 1938. < ref name=PeakinHM/ >  Housing for about 30,000 people was built in the 1950s to help with overcrowding in Glasgow. < ref name=PeakinHM/ >  At first there was only one [[post office]] and one [[Telephone booth|telephone box]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.theglasgowstory.com/story/?id=TGSFG03 |title=The Glasgow Story: Modern times: 1950s to The Present Day |author=Allan Craig |publisher=TheGlasgowStory |access-date=January 24, 2017}} < /ref >  Drumchapel did not get its first [[shopping centre]] until the early 1960s. Local residents call it  " The Drum " .
