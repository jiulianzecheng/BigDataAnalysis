 The '''Burmilla''' is a [[Cat breeds|breed]] of [[cat]]. It is a cross between the [[Chinchilla Persian]] and [[Burmese cat|Burmese]] cats. 
 
 == Origin == 
 In 1981, a woman named Miranda Bickford-Smith bought a male chinchilla Persian for her husband's [[birthday]] present. < ref name= " ACA " > {{Cite web |title=The Asian Cat Association - Showcasing the beauty of the Asian Cat Breed |url=https://asiancatassociation.co.uk/about-the-asian-cat-breed/ |access-date=2023-09-08 |website=The Asian Cat Association |language=en-GB}} < /ref >  The cat was named Jamari Sanquist. Miranda's neighbor had a female Burmese cat named Bambino Lilac Fabergé. The two cats were unintentionally crossed. This led to the creation of Burmilla. < ref name= " ACA "  / > < ref > ASPCA:  " Complete Guide to Cats " . Chanticleer Press, 1999 < /ref > < ref name= " GCCF " > {{Cite web|last=Governing Council of the Cat Fancy|title=Asian - Cat Breed|url=https://www.gccfcats.org/getting-a-cat/choosing/cat-breeds/asian/|access-date=8 September 2023|website=gccfcats.org|language=en-GB}} < /ref > < ref name=AGCS > {{Cite web |title=The Breed – Asian Group Cat Society |url=https://asiangroupcatsociety.co.uk/about/ |access-date=2023-09-08 |language=en-GB}} < /ref > 
 
 == Appearance == 
 Burmilla is a medium-sized cat, with physical traits similar to the Burmese cat. It has a short, dense and soft coat. Burmilla cats have the longhair gene like Chinchilla Persian. The long-haired version of the burmilla is a separate breed called [[Tiffanie (cat)|Tiffanie]]. < ref name= " LOOF " > {{Cite web|title=LOOF standards for asian, english burmese and burmilla|url=http://www.loof.asso.fr/download/standards/std_asi_bur_bml-2012.pdf|archive-date=October 17, 2011|access-date=2014-05-16|website=loof.asso.fr loof.asso.fr|publisher=[[Livre officiel des origines félines]]|format=Pdf}} < /ref > < ref name= " FIFé " > {{Cite web|title=Standard du burmilla de la FIFé|url=http://www1.fifeweb.org/dnld/std/BML.pdf|archive-date=2014-01-01|access-date=2014-05-16|publisher=[[Fédération internationale féline]]|format=Pdf}} < /ref > < ref name= " TICA " > {{Cite web|title=Standards du burmilla et burmilla longhair de la TICA|url=http://www.tica.org/members/publications/standards/bm.pdf|archive-date=2015-06-10|access-date=2014-05-17|website=tica.org tica.org|publisher=[[The International Cat Association]]|format=Pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150610171824/http://www.tica.org/members/publications/standards/bm.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 == Temperament == 
 The burmilla temperament is a combination of burmese and chinchilla breeds. They are active and playful like burmese cats in general. However, it is not as active as the original burmese breed, as it is balanced by the calm and graceful nature of the chinchilla. < ref name=kucingkita > [http://www.kucingkita.com/ras-kucing/burmilla Burmilla].
