 A '''viaduct''' is a [[bridge]] composed of several small spans. It is built for crossing a [[valley]], dry or [[wetland]], or forming an [[overpass]] or [[flyover]]. < ref name= " mer-web " > {{cite web|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/viaduct|title=Definition of VIADUCT|website=www.merriam-webster.com|access-date=27 March 2018}} < /ref > < ref name= " thecol " > {{cite web|url=http://www.dict.org/bin/Dict?Form=Dict2 & Database=gcide & Query=Viaduct|title=dict.org- Viaduct|website=www.dict.org|access-date=27 March 2018}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dict.org/bin/Dict?Form=Dict2 & Database=wn & Query=Viaduct|title=dict.org- Viaduct|website=www.dict.org|access-date=27 March 2018}} < /ref > < ref name= " oxf " > {{cite web|url=http://oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/viaduct|title=viaduct - Definition of viaduct in English by Oxford Dictionaries|website=Oxford Dictionaries - English|access-date=27 March 2018|archive-date=18 April 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160418200325/http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/viaduct|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " Coll " > {{cite web|url=http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/viaduct|title=the definition of viaduct|website=Dictionary.com|access-date=27 March 2018}} < /ref > 
 
 The word ''viaduct'' has two parts from [[Latin]]: ''via'' for road and ''ducere'', to lead. The [[Ancient Rome|ancient Romans]] did not use this word; it was invented in the nineteenth-century by [[analogy]] with [[aqueduct]]. < ref name= " oxf " / >
