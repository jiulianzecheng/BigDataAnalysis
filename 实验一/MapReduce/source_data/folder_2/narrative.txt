A '''narrative''' is a [[literary term]] for the [[event]]s that make up a [[story]]. It is the way the events connect and make up the plot of a story or book. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://literarydevices.net/plot/|title=Plot - Examples and Definition of Plot|date=3 November 2013|publisher=|accessdate=25 August 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 A narrative reports connected [[event]]s, presented to the person [[reading]] or listener in a [[sequence]] of written or spoken [[word]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/us/definition/american_english/narrative |title=narrative |author= < !--Staff writer(s)-- >  |date=n.d. |website= |publisher=Oxford University Press |accessdate=3 October 2014 |archive-date=3 September 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160903164833/http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/us/definition/american_english/narrative |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 A common term for narrative is '''plot'''. The study on structure in narratives is called [[narratology]].
