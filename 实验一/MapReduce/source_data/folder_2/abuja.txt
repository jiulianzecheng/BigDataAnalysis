 '''Abuja'''  ({{IPAc-en|ə|ˈ|b|u:|dʒ|ə}}) < ref > {{cite web|title=Define Abuja's at Dictionary.com|url=http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/abuja's|website=dictionary.com|publisher=Random House, Inc.|access-date=14 April 2015}} < /ref >   is a [[city]] in [[Nigeria]]. It is the national [[Capital (city)|capital]] of Nigeria. Around 2.5 million people live there. Abuja is in the middle of the country, in the center of the Federal Capital Territory, Nigeria. < ref > http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/abuja's < /ref >   It replaced Lagos, the country's most populous city but very small in size, as the capital on 12 December 1991. < ref > {{Cite news|date=2007-02-13|title=Life of poverty in Abuja's wealth|language=en-GB|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/6355269.stm|access-date=2021-05-04}} < /ref > 
 
 
 Abuja is a [[New town|planned city]], which means that [[architecture|architects]] designed it specifically to be Nigeria's capital city. Construction of the first parts of the city was finished in the late 1980s. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.oasdom.com/history-of-abuja-how-it-was-created/|title=History of Abuja - How it Was Created  &  Chosen as Nigeria's Capital|first=Steve|last=Oke|date=9 October 2016}} < /ref >
