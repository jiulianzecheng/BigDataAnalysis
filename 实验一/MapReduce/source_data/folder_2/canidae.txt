 '''Canidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[carnivorous]] and [[omnivore|omnivorous]] [[mammal]]s of the [[Order (biology)|order]] [[Carnivora]]. Animals that belong to the family Canidae are called '''canids'''. The family Canidae is divided into two tribes: [[Vulpini]] ( " true foxes " ), and [[Canini]] ( " true dogs " ). 
 Canids of the tribe Vulpini (foxes) are called  " vulpines " . Canids of the tribe Canini (wolves, dogs, coyotes and others) are called  " canines " . Some people call all these canids, including Vulpini,  " canines " , but this is wrong. 
 
 Examples of canids are [[dog]]s, [[wolf|wolves]], [[fox]]es, [[coyote]]s, and [[jackal]]s.
