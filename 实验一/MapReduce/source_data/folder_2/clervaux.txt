 '''Clervaux''' ({{lang-lb|Klierf}}, [[German language|German]]: ''Clerf'') is a ''[[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]]'' and town in northern [[Luxembourg]], administrative [[Capital city|capital]] of the [[Clervaux (canton)|canton of Clervaux]]. 
 
 The city is in the valley of the [[Clerve]] river and is part of the [[Natural Park of the Our]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 The city was the site of heavy fighting during [[World War II]], in the December 1944 [[Battle of Clervaux]], part of the  " [[Battle of the Bulge]] " . 
 
 In 2012, the ''commune'' of Clervaux was combined with the ''communes'' of [[Heinerscheid]] and [[Munshausen]].

 ==Geography== 
 The ''commune'' is in the [[Oesling]], a region within the greater [[Ardennes (forest)|Ardennes]] area. It has an area of {{Convert|85|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name=stats / > 
 
 The highest point in the ''commune'' is in ''Schwaarzenhiwwel'' near the town of Marnach that is {{Convert|544|m|ft|abbr=on }} high, and the lowest point is {{Convert|274|m|ft|abbr=on }} high that is in the valley of the [[Our (river)|Our]] river near the town of Roder. < ref name= " Gemeinde2014 " > {{cite web| url=http://www.clervaux.lu/files/public/Docs/La-commune-de-Clervaux.pdf |format=PDF |title=La commune de Clervaux: Situation géographique |publisher=clervaux.lu |language=fr |access-date=26 May 2016 }} < /ref >  The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|208|-|428|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. At the [[Train station|railway station]], the altitude  is {{Convert|360|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |access-date= 10 June 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 To the east, the [[Our (river)|Our]] river forms the border with [[Germany]] and, to the north, is the ''commune'' borders [[Belgium]]. In this ''commune'' is the place where three countries meetː Luxembourg, Germany and Belgium.

 ==Places of interest== 
 * The [[Clervaux Castle]]. Here is on permanent display [[The Family of Man]], a famous exhibit of photos collected by [[Edward Steichen]]. The castle also includes the Battle of the Bulge Museum, with an extensive collection of objects from [[World War II]], and an exhibition of models of the castles and palaces of Luxembourg. 
 * The [[Clervaux Abbey|Abbey of St. Maurice and St. Maur]] is situated close to the town of Clervaux. [[Halldór Laxness]], 1902–1998, the [[Nobel Prize|Nobel]] Prize-winning [[Iceland]]ic writer, stayed at the abbey for some time. 
 * Church of Saints Cosmas and Damian, built in the years 1910-1912.
