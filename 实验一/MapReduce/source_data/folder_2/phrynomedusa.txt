 '''''Phrynomedusa''''' is a genus of [[Hylidae|tree frogs]] from [[Brazil]].  They live in the [[Southeast Region, Brazil|Southeast]] and [[South Region, Brazil|South]] parts of Brazil. Scientists used to think these frogs were part of the genus ''[[Phyllomedusa]]'', and thy called them the  " ''[[Phrynomedusa fimbriata|Phyllomedusa fimbriata]]'' group. " < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Baêta et al. 2016 " / >  They are called '''colored leaf frogs''' < ref name=Frost/ >  and '''monkey frogs''' in English. < ref name= " Baêta et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Ecology and conservation== 
 ''Phrynomedusa'' frogs live in mountain streams and nearby ponds in the [[Atlantic Forest]]. The female frogs lay their egg [[clutch (eggs)|clutch]]es in cracks in the rocks above the water; the [[tadpole]]s grow in the water. < ref name= " Baêta et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ''Phrynomedusa'' are rare and humans do not know much about them. Except for ''P. appendiculata'' and ''P. marginata'', scientists have only seen a few individual frogs from each species. Conservationists are sure that two species live in protected areas, but they do not know about the other frogs. < ref name= " Baêta et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 ''Phrynomedusa'' are small [[tree frog]]s: adult males are {{convert|26|-|47|mm|abbr=on}} from [[snout-vent length|nose to rear end]], and females {{convert|28|-|45|mm|abbr=on}}. The [[Iris (anatomy)|iris]] has two colors: a horizontal dark stripe in middle of the eye with dark yellow in the upper and lower surfaces. The [[Dorsum (anatomy)|back]] of this frog is smooth. There is only a little webbing on its front and back feet. They do not have flash colors on their sides, thighs, or othe rbody parts, like other phyllomedusid frogs do. Males frogs have [[vocal sac]]s and vocal slits. < ref name= " Baêta et al. 2016 " / >
