 '''''Rienzi''''' is an opera by [[Richard Wagner]].  Its full title is '''''Rienzi, der Letzte der Tribunen''''' (''Rienzi, the Last of the [[Tribunes]]'').  It is one of the earliest operas Wagner wrote.  There are five acts.  Wagner wrote the [[libretto]] himself, using the story from a [[novel]] by Edward Bulwer-Lytton.  It was composed between [[1838]] and [[1840]]  It was first performed at the [[Semperoper|Hofoper]], [[Dresden]] on 20 October [[1842]].  It was the first time Wagner had a success as an opera [[composer]]. 
 ==Story== 
 The story of the opera is set in [[Rome]] and is based on the life of Cola di Rienzi ([[1313]]â€“[[1354]]), a [[medieval]] [[Italy|Italian]] populist figure who manages to trick the [[noble]]s and their followers.  He helps the people to get power.  At first he is very popular, but then he has to use force against the nobles, and the [[Church]] turns against him.  In the end the [[Ordinary People|ordinary people]] burn the [[Campidoglio|Capitol]] where Rienzi and his friends are [[shelter]]ing.
