 '''Knoppix''', stylized '''KNOPPIX''', is a [[Linux distribution]] based on [[Debian]] designed to be started directly from a [[Compact Disc|CD]] / [[DVD]]. Knoppix is one of the best-known [[LiveDistro|Live Distro]]s. It was developed by Linux consultant [[Klaus Knopper]]. When starting a program it is loaded from the [[optical disc]] and decompressed into a [[RAM drive]]. The decompression is [[transparency (computing)|transparent]] and [[on-the-fly]].
