 A '''treeshrew''' is a small [[mammal]] in the order '''Scandentia'''. 
 
 They live in the tropical forests of [[Southeast Asia]]. They make up the [[Family (biology)|families]] [[Tupaiidae]] and [[Ptilocercidae]]. There are 20 species in five [[genera]]. 
 
 Although called treeshrews, they are not true [[shrew]]s, and are not all [[arboreal]]. Among other things, they eat ''[[Rafflesia]]'' fruit. They used to be classified in the [[Insectivora]].
