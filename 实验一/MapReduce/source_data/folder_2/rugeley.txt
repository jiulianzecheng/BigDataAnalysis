 '''Rugeley''' (pronounced ''Rooj-lee'') is a market town in [[Staffordshire]], [[England]]. It is 7.9 miles (12.7 km) north of the [[City status in the United Kingdom|cathedral city]] of [[Lichfield]] and 9.8 miles (15.8 km) southeast of [[Stafford]], the [[county town]]. The 2011 census revealed that just over 24,000 people live in Rugeley. 
 
 It is on the north-eastern edge of [[Cannock Chase]] and is next to the [[River Trent]]. 
 
 The [[Trent  &  Mersey Canal]] flows through the town. 
 
 Although the town is in [[Staffordshire]], it is in the [[WS postcode area|Walsall postcode area]], a town in the [[West Midlands (county)|West Midlands]], and the WS15 postcode district. 
 
 The town has two [[railway station]]s, [[Rugeley Town railway station|Rugeley Town]] and [[Rugeley Trent Valley railway station|Rugeley Trent Valley]]. Rugeley Trent Valley is on the [[West Coast Main Line]] and provides long-distance trains to [[London]], [[Birmingham]] and [[Crewe]]. Rugeley Town is on the [[Chase Line]] and provides direct local trains to [[Walsall]] and [[Birmingham]]. 
 
 Rugeley used to have its own [[newspaper]], the ''Rugeley Times''. It was published from Bow Street in the town. However, the paper was sold to the [[Staffordshire Newsletter]].
