 '''Googleplex''' is the head office of [[Google|Google, Inc.]] The office is located at 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway in Mountain View, Santa Clara County, California, United States, near [[San Jose]]. 
 
 The original complex has 2,000,000 square feet (190,000 m < sup > 2 < /sup > ) of office space. It is the company's second largest building. The Bay View addition to the building was added in 2015. This made it part of the biggest collection of Google buildings. It has 3,100,000 square feet (290,000 m < sup > 2 < /sup > ) of space. < ref > {{cite web|last=Strickland |first=Jonathan |url=http://www.howstuffworks.com/googleplex.htm |title=HowStuffWorks  " Introduction to How the Googleplex Works "  |publisher=Howstuffworks.com |date=2008-08-04 |access-date=2013-05-24}} < /ref > 
 
 == Facilities and history == 
 
 === Original campus === 
 
 ==== SGI Campus ==== 
 The place was owned by Silicon Graphics in the past. The office space and corporate campus is in a bigger 26-acre (110,000 m < sup > 2 < /sup > ) site. The place also includes Charleston Park and better connection to Permanente Creek, as well as roads that connect to Shoreline Park and the Bay Trail. The project was started in 1994. It was built on the site of one of the working farms in the area.The city owned it at the time. < ref > {{cite book| last = Perry| first = Nicholas| title = Mountain View| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=VpNa9UckT24C & pg=PA95| access-date = 2013-06-15| year = 2006| publisher = Arcadia Publishing| isbn = 978-0-7385-3136-6 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://laserfiche.mountainview.gov/WebLink/DocView.aspx?id=14618 & page=1 & dbid=0 & cr=1|title=Error|website=laserfiche.mountainview.gov}} < /ref > 
 
 ==== Google Campus ==== 
 Google got the facilities in early 2003. < ref > {{cite news| first=Stefanie| last=Olsen| title=Google's movin' on up with Sujeet Kumar and Manohar Patti| url=http://news.cnet.com/Googles+movin+on+up/2110-1032_3-1025111.html| work=CNET News.com| publisher=CNET Networks, Inc.| date=2003-07-13| access-date=2007-01-04 }} < /ref >  Clive Wilkinson Architects redesigned the inside in 2005. Google bought some of Silicon Graphics' properties in June 2006 for $319 million. < ref > {{cite news| first=Elinor| last=Mills| title=Google buying its Mountain View, Calif., property| url=http://news.cnet.com/2061-10812_3-6083899.html| work=CNET News.com| publisher=CNET Networks, Inc.| date=2006-06-14| access-date=2007-01-04}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > < ref > 
 {{cite news| url= http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-15621514_ITM| title = Google to purchase Mountain View buildings| work= San Jose Mercury News| date= June 14, 2006| publisher= [[AccessMyLibrary]]| access-date=2009-11-07| first=Katherine| last=Conrad 
 }} < /ref > 
 
 The buildings are not tall, but the complex covers a large area of land. The inside of the headquarters has items like shade lamps and giant rubber balls for decoration. The lobby has a piano and a projection of current live Google searches. There are also facilities like free laundry rooms, two small swimming pools, volleyball courts, and eighteen cafeterias with different menus. Google has also put in models of [[SpaceShipOne]] and a dinosaur skeleton. < ref > {{cite news| last = Weinberg| first = Nathan| title = Yes, Google Has The Dinosaur| publisher = google.blognewschannel.com| date = 2007-11-08| url = http://google.blognewschannel.com/2007/01/22/| access-date = 2013-01-23 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web| last = Mohney| first = Chris| title = 25 things to see at the Googleplex before you die| work = [[Valleywag]]| publisher = [[Gawker Media]]| date = 2007-02-06| url = http://valleywag.gawker.com/tech/google/25-things-to-see-at-the-googleplex-before-you-die-234103.php| access-date = 2009-08-08| archive-date = 2009-09-25| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090925112329/http://valleywag.gawker.com/tech/google/25-things-to-see-at-the-googleplex-before-you-die-234103.php| url-status = dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Since 2007 the place has a set of solar panels covering the roofs of eight buildings and two solar carports. These can produce 1.6 megawatts of electricity. 30% of the peak electricity demand comes from the panels in their solar-powered buildings. < ref > {{cite web| url= http://www.google.com/corporate/green/footprint.html| title= Reducing our Footprint |publisher=Google Inc.| access-date=2010-09-30| quote= In Mountain View, CA, for example, we currently have a 1.6 megawatt solar power system that generates 30% of the peak power necessary to fuel the buildings on which they are located.}} < /ref > 
 
 === Bay view addition === 
 In 2013 construction started on a new 1.1 million square foot headquarters campus called  " Bay View " . This was next to the original campus on {{cvt|42|acre|m2}} leased from [[NASA]]. The estimated cost of the project is $120 million with a target opening date of 2015. < ref > {{Cite web|url = http://www.nasa.gov/centers/ames/pdf/255793main_June.08.Agram.smallfile.pdf|title = Google announce lease at Ames Research Center|date = June 2008|access-date = 2015-12-28|website = www.nasa.gov|publisher = NASA}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news|url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424127887324503204578320753525354828.html |title=Google Starting Construction on New Campus - WSJ.com |publisher=Online.wsj.com |date=2013-02-22 |access-date=2013-05-24 |first=John |last=Letzing}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|last=Goldberger |first=Paul |url=http://www.vanityfair.com/online/daily/2013/02/exclusive-preview-googleplex |title=Exclusive Preview: Google's New Built-from-Scratch Googleplex |publisher=Vanity Fair |access-date=2013-05-24}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2283789/Googleplex-Google-build-120m-futuristic-HQ-garden-roof.html |title=Googleplex: Google to build $120m futuristic HQ with a garden on the roof  & #124; Mail Online |publisher=Dailymail.co.uk |date=2013-02-25 |access-date=2013-05-24 |location=London}} < /ref >

This is also the first time Google has designed its own buildings. < ref > {{cite news|last=Russell |first=James S. |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-04-24/google-s-new-campus-has-light-fresh-air-low-power-use.html |title=Google's New Campus Has Light, Fresh Air, Low Power Use |publisher=Bloomberg |date=2013-04-24 |access-date=2013-05-24}} < /ref >
