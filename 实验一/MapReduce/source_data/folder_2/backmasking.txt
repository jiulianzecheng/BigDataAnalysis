'''Backmasking''' (also known wrongly as ''backward masking'') < ref > Backward(s) masking has two other meanings See {{cite web |last=Crispen |first=Bob |title=Backward Masking & nbsp;… another pious fraud |url=http://www.crispen.org/rants/backward.html |publisher=The Crispen Family |access-date=2006-09-01 |archive-date=2007-01-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070126230708/http://www.crispen.org/rants/backward.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  is a [[sound recording|recording]] technique. In backmasking, a sound or message is recorded backwards onto a track that is meant to be played forwards. It was made popular by [[The Beatles]]. Some people think that backmasking causes [[subliminal]] persuasion.
