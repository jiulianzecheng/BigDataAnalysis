'''Denofa AS''' is an industrial company from [[Norway]] that was established in 1912 as De Nordiske Fabriker A/S, or De-No-Fa for short. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=Du har søkt etter en side som ikke finnes på vår web.|url=http://www.denofa.no/?ItemID=1090|access-date=2023-04-27|website=www.denofa.no}} < /ref >  The company produces oil, [[Protein|proteins]], and fatty acid (lecithin) for the food processing industry, the fodder industry, and the pharmaceutical industry. They started refining whale oil, but later switched to using soy. < ref > {{Citation|last=Antonsen|first=Robin|title=Denofa|date=2023-01-21|url=https://snl.no/Denofa|work=Store norske leksikon|language=no|access-date=2023-04-27}} < /ref > < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 The first production facility was located in [[Fredrikstad]] and was planned and constructed by [[Carl Fredrik Holmboe]]. The company merged with the soap company Lilleborg in 1960 to form DeNoFa Lilleborg. Later, it was demerged in 1996 to again form a separate company, but remained part of the Orkla Group. Denofa currently owns a production facility in Leknes, acquired after buying 100% of the company Marine Lipids. 
 
 Denofa has 60 employees and the current [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] is Bjarne Rask Thomsen. The company is currently owned by Orkla (20%), Unikorn (40%), < ref > {{Citation|title=Strand Unikorn AS|date=2023-01-25|url=https://snl.no/Strand_Unikorn_AS|work=Store norske leksikon|language=no|access-date=2023-04-27}} < /ref >  and the Brazilian company Inlogs (40%). < ref name= " :0 "  / >  They also own 60% of the Polish company Nagrol. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Denofa in Poland|url=http://www.denofa.no/?ItemID=1116|access-date=2023-04-27|website=www.denofa.no}} < /ref >  The company imports soybeans from [[Brazil]] and uses grain from Norway. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Iversen|first=Gunnar Steen|date=20 January 2005|title=Nye eiere sikrer virksomheten|url=http://www.f-b.no/article/20050120/ARBOKO/31575477|website=Fredriksstad Blad|access-date=27 April 2023|archive-date=31 July 2012|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120731122649/http://www.f-b.no/article/20050120/ARBOKO/31575477|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The company currently has 60 employees, and the current CEO is Bjarne Rask Thomsen
