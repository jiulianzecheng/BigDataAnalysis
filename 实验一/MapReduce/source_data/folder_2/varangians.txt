 The '''Varangians''' or '''Varyags''' was the name given by [[Greece|Greeks]] and [[East Slavs]] to [[Vikings]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?term=Varangian|title=Online Etymology Dictionary|work=etymonline.com}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/427466/Oleg|title=Oleg|encyclopedia=Encyclopedia Britannica}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.thefreedictionary.com/Varangian|title=Varangian|work=TheFreeDictionary.com}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://snl.no/v%C3%A6ringer|title=væringer|work=Store norske leksikon|date=26 March 2021}} < /ref >  Between the 9th and the 11th centuries they ruled the [[medieval]] state of [[Kievan Rus'|Rus']]. They were the personal bodyguards of the Byzantine Emperors, < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.historybits.com/the-vikings.htm|title=The Vikings - Rus - Varangian Guard|work=historybits.com}} < /ref >  and formed the [[Byzantine]] [[Varangian Guard]]. < ref > {{cite book |title=Atlas of Russia and the Soviet Union |last=Milner-Gulland |first=R. R. |year=1989 |publisher=Phaidon |isbn=0-7148-2549-2 |page=36 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=S8RoAAAAMAAJ }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book |title=Culture and Customs of Russia |url=https://archive.org/details/culturecustomsof0000schu |last=Schultze |first=Sydney |year=2000 |publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group |isbn=0-313-31101-3 |page=[https://archive.org/details/culturecustomsof0000schu/page/n24 5] }} < /ref > 
 
 A group of Varangians known as the [[Rus' (people)|Rus']] settled in [[Novgorod]] in 864, under the leadership of [[Rurik]]. Rurik's relative [[Oleg of Novgorod|Oleg]] conquered [[Kiev]] in 882.  He made the state of [[Kievan Rus']], which was later ruled by [[Rurik dynasty|Ruriks descendants]]. < ref name= " Duczko 2004 10–11 " > {{cite book |title=Viking Rus |last=Duczko|first=Wladyslaw|year=2004|publisher=[[Brill Publishers]]|isbn=90-04-13874-9|pages=10–11|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hEawXSP4AVwC & pg=PA10|access-date=December 1, 2009}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/512998/Rurik-Dynasty|title=Rurik Dynasty|encyclopedia=Encyclopedia Britannica}} < /ref >  The name of the [[Rus' people|Rus']] is the origin of the name [[Russia]]. 
 
 Varangians did [[trade]], [[piracy]] and [[mercenary]] activities. They move around using the river systems and portages of [[Gardariki]].  The Gardariki was the areas north of the [[Black Sea]]. They had trade routes that connected [[Dark Ages|Dark Age Europe]] with rich [[Arab Caliphate]]s and the [[Byzantine Empire]]. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=H8R9LKwsM8AC & dq=vikings+baghdad & pg=PA7|title=Vikings|isbn=9781573103565|last1=Schofield|first1=Tracey Ann|date=March 2002}} < /ref >  Wanting the riches of [[Constantinople]], Rus' Varangians started a number of [[Rus'-Byzantine Wars]]. Most of them, both in Byzantium and in Eastern Europe, were converted from [[paganism]] into [[Orthodox Christianity]]. Most scientific experts, including the ethnic [[Russians|Russian]], [[Ukrainians|Ukrainian]] and [[Belarusians|Belarusian]] people themselves, confirm the modern-day [[East Slavs|East Slavic]] ethnic group to be the direct, ancestral descendants of the [[Rus' people|Rus']].
