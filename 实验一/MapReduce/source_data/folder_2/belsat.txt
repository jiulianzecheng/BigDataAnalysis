 '''Belsat''' ({{lang-be|Белсат}}; {{lang-pl|Biełsat}}) is a [[Poland|Polish]] [[Television network|television channel]] for people in [[Belarus]]. It is a branch of the [[broadcasting]] organization Telewizja Polska. < ref name= " Le Monde " > {{cite web|last=Iwaniuk|first=Jakub|date=2017-01-30|title=La télévision Bielsat s'inquiète pour son avenir|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/actualite-medias/article/2017/01/30/incertitudes-autour-de-l-avenir-de-la-television-bielsat_5071413_3236.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210316055813/https://www.lemonde.fr/actualite-medias/article/2017/01/30/incertitudes-autour-de-l-avenir-de-la-television-bielsat_5071413_3236.html|archive-date=2021-03-16|access-date=2018-01-29|publisher=[[Le Monde]]|pages=8|language=fr}} < /ref > 
 
 Agnieszka Romaszewska-Guzy is the head of ''Belsat''. < ref name=belarusdigest-20121129 > {{cite web |url=https://belarusdigest.com/story/belsat-tv-struggles-to-survive/ |title=Belsat TV Struggles to Survive |last=Astapenia |first=Ryhor |website=BelarusDigest |date=2012-11-29 |access-date=2020-06-18 |archive-date=2020-06-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200619115606/https://belarusdigest.com/story/belsat-tv-struggles-to-survive/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  [[Intellectual property]] of ''Belsat'' is made by mostly Belarusian journalists. Journalists also work for Belsat in Belarus.  
 
 On April 26, 2007, [[Alexander Lukashenko]] said that the channel is bad. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2007-04-26|title=Лукашенко: Независимый телеканал -  " глупый, бестолковый и недружественный "  проект|url=https://news.tut.by/politics/86821.html|access-date=2019-08-29|publisher=[[TUT.BY]]|language=ru|archive-date=2017-11-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171115155842/http://news.tut.by/politics/86821.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The government of Belarus has been making troubles for ''Belsat'' all the time and does not not give the channel an approval. ''Belsat's'' journalists have gotten many punishments for work. In March 2008, for example, many ''Belsat'' journalists were arrested and technical things were taken away. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://fm4v2.orf.at/connected/222150/main.html|title=Weißrussland: Sendeschluss für freie Radios|publisher=[[FM4]]|language=de|access-date=2017-12-01}} < /ref >  To 2017, they have spent a number of 125 days in detention and been fined $94,296 US, simply for doing their work. 
 
 In 2017, the channel's [[Minsk]] house was searched and [[Computer|computers]] from there were taken away. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/belarus-belsat-tv-police-raid-confiscate-equipment/28402065.html|title=Belarus Police Confiscate Equipment Of Independent TV Channel|publisher=[[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]]|language=en|access-date=2019-08-29}} < /ref >  The same year, Belsat TV got a [[veto]] by a Minsk court on using its [[trademark]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.fairobserver.com/region/europe/belarus-alexander-lukashenko-human-rights-protests-news-71210/|title=What’s Going On in Belarus?|last=Rom|first=Anna|last2=Ash •|date=2017-06-15|website=Fair Observer|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-29}} < /ref >  Since then, ''Belsat's'' journalists have used other trademark that has letters from the channel's name – the [[Cyrillic script|Cyrillic]] “BLS” (БЛС) and “tv”. The most recent search of Belsat's Minsk house was done in 2019. 
 
 It has been writing about the [[2020 Belarusian protests]] and [[2020 Belarusian presidential election]], so many journalists of ''Belsat'' have been tried and arrested for that. In 2020 Belsat journalists were detained 162 times, while their things were taken away very often. < ref > {{Cite web|last=S.A|first=Telewizja Polska|title=Trial of Belarusian journalists underway|url=https://polandin.com/52206486/trial-of-belarusian-journalists-underway|access-date=2021-03-25|publisher=polandin.com|language=en}} < /ref >  26 journalists were arrested, out of which six journalists were arrested two times, and one journalist was arrested three times, giving 34 arrests. They had been sentenced to a number of 392 days in prison. Seven of arrested journalists were beaten by the police. Seven journalist following detention were taken to [[Hospital|hospitals]]. During the crackdown on the protests of August 9–12, ''Belsat's'' journalists were putting their lives on the line, and two of them were injured. ''Belsat's'' journalists got fines in $26 353,44 for their work in Belarus in 2020. 
 
 In February 2021, [[Katsyaryna Andreeva]] and [[Darya Chultsova]] of ''Belsat'' were sentenced to two years in prison by a [[court]] in [[Minsk]] for informing on anti-government protests held around the house where [[Raman Bandarenka]] was beaten to death in November 2020. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2021-02-18|title=Two Belarusian journalists sentenced to two years in prison for doing their job|url=https://europeanjournalists.org/blog/2021/02/18/two-belarusian-journalists-sentenced-to-two-years-in-prison-for-doing-their-job/|access-date=2021-02-18|publisher=European Federation of Journalists}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Mary|first=Ilyushina|date=2021-02-18|title=Two journalists, 23 and 27, get two years in jail for live-streaming a protest in Belarus|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/02/18/europe/belarus-journalists-jailed-intl/index.html|access-date=2021-03-25|publisher=[[CNN]]|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Agencies|first=Minsk|date=2021-02-18|title=Belarus jails two journalists who covered Lukashenko protest|url=http://www.theguardian.com/world/2021/feb/18/belarus-jails-two-journalists-covered-lukashenko-protest|access-date=2021-03-25|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The site of ''Belsat'' can't be opened in Belarus since August 2020. < ref name= " :1 " > {{cite web|last=Штейн|first=Евгения|date=2020-08-29|title=В Беларуси заблокировали «Нашу Нiву» и naviny.by, журналистов иностранных СМИ лишают аккредитации|url=https://tech.onliner.by/2020/08/29/nasha-niva|publisher=[[Onliner.by]]|language=ru|access-date=2021-03-25|archive-date=2020-09-29|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200929075549/https://tech.onliner.by/2020/08/29/nasha-niva|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|date=2020-08-22|title=В Белоруссии заблокировали несколько десятков информационных сайтов.  In July 2021, Belsat's content was declared extremist, meaning distributing it can be punished by a 30-days jail. On November 3, 2021, the [[Ministry of Internal Affairs (Belarus)|Ministry of Internal Affairs]] declared Belsat an extremist group. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/belarus-declares-poland-based-belsat-news-channel-an-extremist-organisation-2021-11-03/|title=Belarus declares Poland-based Belsat news channel an 'extremist' organisation|work=[[Reuters]]|date=3 November 2021|accessdate=12 January 2022}} < /ref >  Creation of an extremist group or participation in it is a criminal offence in Belarus. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://baj.by/en/content/baj-demands-stop-using-anti-extremist-legislation-restrict-freedom-speech|title=BAJ demands to stop using anti-extremist legislation to restrict freedom of speech|website=[[Belarusian Association of Journalists]]|date=17 November 2021|accessdate=22 December 2021}} < /ref > 
 
 In January 2023, Daria Losik, the wife of [[Ihar Losik]], was sentenced to two years in prison for an interview with the channel under a criminal article on promoting extremist activity. < ref > {{cite web|title=В Беларуси суд приговорил к двум годам колонии Дарью Лосик|url=https://www.currenttime.tv/a/daryu-losik-prigovorili-k-2-godam-kolonii/32230394.html|website=[[Current Time TV]]|date=2023-01-19|accessdate=2023-03-24|language=ru}} < /ref >  In the fall of 2022, a criminal case for an interview with the channel was opened against Natalya Suslova, the mother of Belarusian volunteer Pavel “Volat” Suslov, who was [[Russian invasion of Ukraine|killed in action in Ukraine]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=В Беларуси против матери погибшего в Украине белорусского добровольца завели уголовное дело|url=https://www.currenttime.tv/a/32148685.html|website=[[Current Time TV]]|date=2022-11-25|accessdate=2023-03-24|language=ru}} < /ref >
