 A '''Hingchabi''' ({{lang-mni|ꯍꯤꯡꯆꯥꯕꯤ}}, {{lang-omp|ꯍꯤꯡꯆꯥꯄꯤ|hing-chaa-pee|giantess}} < ref name=google1998 > {{cite book| last = Singh| first = Moirangthem Kirti| title = Recent Researches in Oriental Indological Studies: Including Meiteilogy| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=LVpuAAAAMAAJ| year = 1998| publisher = Parimal Publications| isbn =  }} < /ref > ) is a mythical creature in [[Meitei mythology]], [[Meitei folklore|folklore]] and [[Meitei religion|religion]] ([[Sanamahism]]) of [[Antique Kangleipak]] ([[Ancient Manipur]]). She has characters similar to those of the [[vampire]]s < ref name= " google1998 "  / >  as well as the [[witch]]es. < ref > {{cite book| title = South Asian Anthropologist| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=XSmCAAAAMAAJ| year = 1994| publisher = Sarat Chandra Roy Institute of Anthropological Studies| isbn =  }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| last = Parratt| first = Saroj Nalini| title = The Religion of Manipur: Beliefs, Rituals, and Historical Development| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=WEQqAAAAYAAJ| year = 1980| publisher = Firma KLM| isbn = 978-0-8364-0594-1 }} < /ref > 
 The terms,  " hing "  means  " [[raw]] "  and  " chaa "  means  " [[eat|to eat]] "  in [[Meitei language]] ([[Manipuri language]]). < ref name= " google1998 "  / >
