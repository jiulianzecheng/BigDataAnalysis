A '''praetor''' was a person who served as a [[judge]] in [[Ancient Rome]] during the time of the [[Roman Republic]]. < ref name= " McKay " > {{cite book| last1 = McKay| first1 = John P.| last2 = Hill| first2 = Bennett D.| last3 = Buckler| first3 = John| title = A History of Western Society| url = https://archive.org/details/historyofwestern0002mcka_i4u9| year = 2006| publisher = Houghton Mifflin Company| location = Boston| isbn = 978-0-618-52266-8 }} < /ref >  Also, when a [[Roman consul]] was away, the praetor would act as consul. < ref name= " McKay " / >  Praetors were officials in Rome that were responsible for rendering justice. Some of them could also be responsible for the administration of a province.
