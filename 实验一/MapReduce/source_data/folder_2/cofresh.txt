 '''Cofresh Snack Foods''' is a company that makes savoury snacks. It is based in [[Leicester]], United Kingdom. < ref > BBC, ''[[The Kitchen Cabinet (radio show)|The Kitchen Cabinet]]'', https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m0009jbp < /ref >  The company earns Â£28 million a year. 
 
 Cofresh was started in 1974 by an [[India|Indian]] family who moved from [[Kenya]] to Leicester, < ref > Leicester Mercury https://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/news/business/taste-india-new-range-healthier-1617997 < /ref >  and used their fish and chip shop to make snacks, such as [[Bombay Mix]].  
 
 ==Products== 
 Cofresh's main range of Indian snacks includes versions of [[poppadum]] curls, potato grills, spiced nuts, Bombay Mix and [[Ganthiya]]. 
 
 In 2017, Cofresh released  " Eat Real " , which is a brand of [[Gluten-free diet|gluten-free]] snacks.  " Eat Real "  snacks are mostly made from [[hummus]] or [[quinoa]].
