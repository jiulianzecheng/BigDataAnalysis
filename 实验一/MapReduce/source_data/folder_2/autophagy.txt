 '''Autophagy''' (which means  " eating itself " ), is one of the basic [[cell]] mechanisms. It allows the controlled breaking down of cell parts which do not work, or are not needed. < ref name=klionsky > {{cite journal |pmid= 18567941 |last1= Klionsky D.J. 2008 |title= Autophagy revisited: a conversation with Christian de Duve |volume= 4 |issue= 6 |pages= 740–3 |journal= Autophagy |year= 2008 |doi=10.4161/auto.6398|s2cid= 6198427 }} < /ref >  The cell parts can then be recycled as required. < ref name= " mizushima 2011 review " > {{cite journal|last1=Mizushima N.  &  Komatsu M. 2011 |title=Autophagy: renovation of cells and tissues.|journal=Cell |year=2011|volume=147|issue=4|pages=728–41|doi=10.1016/j.cell.2011.10.026|pmid=22078875|s2cid=18697221}} < /ref > < ref name=Kobayashi2015 > {{cite journal|author=Kobayashi S 2015 |title=Choose delicately and reuse adequately: the newly revealed process of autophagy |journal=Biological  &  Pharmaceutical Bulletin|year=2015 |volume=38|issue=8|pages=1098–103|pmid=26235572|doi=10.1248/bpb.b15-00096|s2cid=42838133 }} < /ref >   
 
 There are several ways this happens. Targeted cell parts may be isolated from the rest of the cell in an ''autophagosome'' (a double-membraned [[vesicle]]). < ref name= " mizushima 2011 ARCDB review " > {{cite journal|last=Mizushima Noboru; Yoshimori, Tamotsu  &  Ohsumi, Yoshinori 2011|title=The role of Atg proteins in autophagosome formation |journal=Annual Review of Cell and Developmental Biology |year=2011 |volume=27|issue=1 |pages=107–132|doi=10.1146/annurev-cellbio-092910-154005|pmid=21801009 }} < /ref > < ref name= " klionsky 2007 review " > {{cite journal|last=Xie Z.  &  Klionsky D.J. 2007 |title=Autophagosome formation: core machinery and adaptations.|journal=Nature Cell Biology|year=2007|volume=9|issue=10|pages=1102–9|doi=10.1038/ncb1007-1102|pmid=17909521|s2cid=26402002}} < /ref >  The autophagosome fuses with [[lysosome]]s and the contents are broken down and recycled. In the extreme case of starvation, the breakdown of cellular components promotes cellular survival by maintaining cellular energy levels.
