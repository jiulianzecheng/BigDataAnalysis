 '''Korumburra''' is a town in the [[Australia]]n [[States and territories of Australia|state]] of  [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]]. It is {{convert|120|km|mi|0}} south-east of [[Melbourne]]. The town is {{Convert|227|m|ft|0}} above the sea level. In 2006 there were 4465 people living in Korumburra. 
 
 ==History== 
 The word ''Korumburra'' is believed to be an [[Australian Aborigines|Aboriginal]] word meaning  " maggot " . < ref name=vp > {{cite web |url= http://www.victorianplaces.com.au/korumburra |title=Korumburra  & #124; Victorian Places |first= |last= |work=victorianplaces.com.au |year=2015  |accessdate=20 August 2015}} < /ref >   
 
 The first European settlers began farming cattle in the area in 1846. < ref name=vp/ >  In 1878 coal was discovered at what was called Coal Creek. < ref name=vp/ >  Farmers began to create small farms in the area. In 1887 the town was planned to be along the railway line which would go to the coal mine. < ref name=vp/ > 
 
 The first Post Office in the area opened on 1 September 1884. The [[South Gippsland railway line|railway]] arrived in 1891. < ref name=vp/ >  The first school opened in 1897. 
 
 ==The Town today== 
 Korumburra is the home of [[Coal Creek, Victoria|Coal Creek]] Community Park and Museum. This museum shows life in the area from the 1870s to 1920s, as the town rapidly expanded following the discovery coal.{{Citation needed|date=July 2015}} The outdoor museum covers {{convert|30|acre|m2}} of bushland, including 60 exhibits. These include the Giant Earthworm, National Bank, Anzac exhibit, mining exhibits, dairy exhibit at the Boston Carriageworks and Railway Museum. The Park is free entry and open Thursdays to Mondays, and seven days a week during school holidays. A tramway runs on weekends around the lower end of the park with an old-time farm and bush oval.{{Citation needed|date=July 2015}} Many local organisations use the Park and environs and special events are held during the year.{{Citation needed|date=July 2015}}

 ==Transport== 
 Korumburra is also the home of the popular tourist railway [[South Gippsland Railway]] which operates a heritage railway service between [[Leongatha, Victoria|Leongatha]] and [[Nyora, Victoria|Nyora]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.sgr.org.au/ |title=South Gippsland Railway  & #124; Enjoy The Ride |first= |last= |work=sgr.org.au |year=2015 |accessdate=20 August 2015 |archive-date=12 April 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080412032936/http://www.sgr.org.au/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Heritage trains are operated each Sunday and on public holidays.   
 
 Korumburra was on the [[South Gippsland railway line|South Gippsland railway]] line went to Leongatha until 1993. A V/Line road coach service replaced the rail service. The line beyond Leongatha is being used as a rail trail for public use.
