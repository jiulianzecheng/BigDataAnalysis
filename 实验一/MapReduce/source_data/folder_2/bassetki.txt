'''Bassetki''' is a small village in [[Iraq]], in [[Dahuk Province|Dohuk Governorate]] of autonomous [[Iraqi Kurdistan]]. Several important [[Archaeology|archaeological]] finds were made there. 
 
 == Archaeological finds == 
 In 1975 a fragment of a figure of Naram-Sin of [[Akkad]] was found near Bassetki. The statue is now called the Bassetki Statue.  
 
 In August–October 2016,  the Institute for Ancient Near Eastern Studies from the [[University of Tübingen]] and Hasan Qasim from the Directorate of Antiquities in [[Duhok|Dohuk]] did archaeological work near Bassetki. < ref name= " sd " > {{Cite web|url=https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2016/11/161104102136.htm|title=Significant Bronze Age city discovered in Northern Iraq|date=4 November 2016|publisher=[[Science Daily]]|access-date=7 November 2016}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.sci-news.com/archaeology/bronze-age-city-iraq-04341.html|title=Archaeologists Unearth Bronze Age City in Iraq|date=6 November 2016|publisher=Sci News|access-date=7 November 2016}} < /ref >  They found the remains of a large [[Bronze Age]] city.  The city was started around 3000 [[Anno Domini|B.C.]] It lasted for more than 1,200 years. From around 2700 B.C., the city had a wall protecting the upper part of the city from invaders. The city had a large road network, many residential districts and a palace. A contemporary cemetery was located outside the city. The city was connected to other regions of [[Mesopotamia]] and [[Asia Minor|Anatolia]] by a roadway dating from  < abbr > around 1800 B.C < /abbr > . The archeologists also discovered settlement layers dating from the Akkadian Empire.  The finds were announced by the University of Tübingen on 3 November 2016. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.uni-tuebingen.de/newsfullview-landingpage/article/bedeutende-bronzezeitliche-stadt-im-nordirak-entdeckt.html|title=Bedeutende bronzezeitliche Stadt im Nordirak entdeckt|date=3 November 2016|publisher=University of Tübingen|language=German|access-date=7 November 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 In the summer of 2017, archaeologists from the [[University of Tübingen]] in [[Germany]] uncovered a collection of [[Assyria|Assyrian]] [[cuneiform]] tablets hidden inside a collection of [[ceramic]] jugs. These tablets are 3,200 years old. They indicate that the ancient lost royal city of [[Mardaman]] was where Bassetki is today. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.newser.com/story/259326/ancient-legendary-city-found-by-accident.html|title=An Ancient, Legendary City Has Just Been Found by Accident|first=Arden|last=Dier|date=16 May 2018|website=Newser}} < /ref >
