 A '''prophage''' is a [[bacteriophage]] [[genome]]. It is part of the circular bacterial DNA chromosome, or it is a [[plasmid]] outside the circular chromosome..  
 
 This is a [[latency|latent]] form of a phage: the viral genes are there in the [[bacteria|bacterium]] but cause no disruption of the bacterial [[cell (biology)|cell]].  
 
 Pro means  " before " , so, prophage means the stage of a virus inserted into host DNA ''before being activated inside the host''. < ref name= " Fortier-Sekulovic-2013 " > {{cite journal|title=Importance of prophages to evolution and virulence of bacterial pathogens|journal=Virulence|year=2013|volume=4|issue=5|pages=354–365|doi=10.4161/viru.24498|last1=Fortier | first1=L.C. | last2=Sekulovic | first2=O.|pmid=23611873|pmc=3714127|doi-access=free}} < /ref >
