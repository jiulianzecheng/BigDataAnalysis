'''Imogen''' or '''Imogene''' [ '''im'''-''uh''-jeen ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Imogene {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/imogene|access-date=2023-04-06|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is an [[English language|English]] first name. Imogen is an exquisite name that has been increasingly popular in modern times. The name boasts a noteworthy heritage and profound significance. Imogen has its roots in the Celtic culture and signifies  " maiden "  or  " innocent. "  The name has been in use for centuries and has been linked to many notable figures across history. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Imogen name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Imogen-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-06|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The first recorded use of the name is under the [[United Kingdom|English]] playwright [[William Shakespeare]]. It is chiefly used as a given name in several countries. They include [[Canada]], the [[United States]], [[Ireland]], [[Australia]] and [[South Africa]].
