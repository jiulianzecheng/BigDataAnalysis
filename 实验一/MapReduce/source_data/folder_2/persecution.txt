 '''Persecution''' is the mistreatment (bad treatment) of an individual or group by another group. < ref name= " webster " > {{cite web|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/persecution |title=Persecution - Definition and More from the Free Merriam-Webster Dictionary |publisher=Merriam-webster.com |accessdate=2011-11-01}} < /ref >  People can be persecuted for any reasons; it is usually because of their [[Religious persecution|religion]], [[Racism|ethnicity]], [[Political persecution|politics]], [[sexuality]], [[gender]] or [[gender identity]]. 
 
 [[Religious persecution]] is persecution of people that are part of a religion, or who are not part of a different religion. The most important organization to persecute people was the [[Christian Church]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://oxford.universitypressscholarship.com/view/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199278121.001.0001/acprof-9780199278121-chapter-5|title=Christian Persecution, Martyrdom, and Orthodoxy|last=de Ste. During the next three hundred years, the Christian Church started to have a great effect by becoming the most important persecuting organization in [[human history]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > {{Rp|201}} Persecution by Christians began in the 4th [[century]] [[Anno Domini]], and the Christian Church was the most important persecuting organization for 1,500 years. < ref name= " :0 "  / > {{Rp|201}} The Christian Church was very active in the [[persecution of Christians]] – especially Christians the Church said were [[heretic]] < nowiki/ > s – and in the [[persecution of Jews]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > {{Rp|201}} In [[classical antiquity]], the [[Roman Republic]] started to control the religion of [[Bacchus]] in 186 [[BC]]. Although the [[Roman Senate]] did not make the religion [[illegal]], it stopped some parts of the [[cult]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > {{Rp|234}} The Roman Empire's persecution of Christians, and then its persecution of non-Christians were both motivated by the religions of the persecutors ([[paganism]] and [[Christianity]]). < ref name= " :0 "  / > {{Rp|203}}
