 '''Neuss''' ([[Low Franconian]] and [[Ripuarian language|Ripuarian]]: ''Nüüs'', ({{lang-la|Novaesium}}; officially apelled ''Neuß'' until 1968) is a city in the west of [[Germany]], in the [[States of Germany|state]] of [[North Rhine-Westphalia]]. In the local dialect it is called ''Nüss'' or Neuß. Neuss has about 150,000 [[inhabitant]]s. It is on the [[Rhine]], across from the big city of [[Düsseldorf]]. 
 
 Neuss was founded by the [[Ancient Rome|Romans]] about 2,000 years ago. The Romans called the town ''Castra Novesia''.
