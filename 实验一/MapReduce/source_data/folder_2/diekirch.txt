 '''Diekirch''' ({{lang-lb|Dikrech}}; from ''Diet-Kirch'', i.e.  " people's church " ) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] with [[List of cities in Luxembourg|city status]] in north-eastern [[Luxembourg]], [[capital city]] of the [[Diekirch (canton)|canton Diekirch]] and the [[Diekirch (district)|district of Diekirch]]. The city is situated on the banks of the [[Sauer]] river. 
 
 The [[National Museum of Military History (Luxembourg)|National Museum of Military History]] of Luxembourg is in Diekirch. 
 
 ==Name== 
 Diekirch is mentioned in 938 as ''Theochirica'' and then as ''Diecirke'' in 1182 and ''Diekirke'' in 1221. The word Diekirch means, in Luxembourgish,  " people's church " . < ref > {{in lang|nl}} Maurits Gysseling, ''Toponymisch Woordenboek van België, Nederland, Luxemburg, Noord-Frankrijk en West-Duitsland (vóór 1226)'', Tongres, 1960. ({{cite web |url=http://www.wulfila.be/tw/facsimile/ |title=Online version |access-date=28 April 2014 |archive-date=16 June 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120616092839/http://www.wulfila.be/tw/facsimile/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:Diekirch sauer.jpg|thumbnail|left|The [[Sauer]] river flows through Diekirch]] 
 The town of Diekirch, which lies in the south of the ''commune'', has an area of {{Convert|12.42|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|187|-|396|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. At the [[church]], the altitude  is {{Convert|200|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |access-date= 10 June 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 Diekirch is surrounded by four ''communes'': [[Tandel]] ([[Vianden (canton)|Vianden]] canton) to the north, [[Bettendorf, Luxembourg|Bettendorf]] to the east, [[Vallée de l'Ernz]] to the south, and [[Erpeldange]] to the west.
