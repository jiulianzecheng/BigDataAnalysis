A '''covenant''' is a formal agreement, a promise made between two people. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/british/covenant |title=covenant noun |work=Cambridge Dictionary Online |year=2011|access-date=15 October 2011}} < /ref >  It is often used as a [[religion|religious]] promise. It can also be an item which is a [[symbol]] of keeping the promise. Some covenants include stone tablets, written documents, and other sacred items. In [[Judaism]], the [[Ark of the Covenant]] was a wooden box which held the stone tablets with the [[Ten Commandments]] written on them. The Jewish people saw the box as a symbol of God being with them and leading them. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/british/the-ark_2#the-ark_2__3 |title=the ark noun (BOX) |work=Cambridge Dictionary Online |year=2011  |access-date=15 October 2011}} < /ref >
