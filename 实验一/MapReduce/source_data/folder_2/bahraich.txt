 '''Bahraich''' is a city and a [[municipal board]] in [[Bahraich district]]  in the state of [[Uttar Pradesh]], India. Located on the [[Saryu River]], a tributary of the [[Ghaghara]] river, Bahraich is {{convert|125|km|abbr=off}} north-east of [[Lucknow]], the state  capital. The districts of [[Barabanki district|Barabanki]], [[Gonda district|Gonda]], [[Balrampur district|Balrampur]], [[Lakhimpur Kheri]], [[Shravasti]] and [[Sitapur]] share local boundaries with Bahraich. A factor which makes this town important is the international border shared with [[Nepal]]. 
 
 ==Geography and climate== 
 It has an average elevation of {{convert|126|m|abbr=off}}. Bahraich has a warm humid subtropical climate with  hot summers from April to July. The rainy season is from July to mid-September when Bahraich gets an average rainfall from the south-west monsoon winds, and occasionally frontal rainfall will occur in January. In winter the maximum temperature is around {{convert|25|°C}} and the minimum is in the {{convert|-1|to|7|°C}} range. Fog is quite common from late December to late January. Summers are extremely hot with temperatures rising to the {{convert|40|to|47|°C}} range, the average highs being in the high of 30s (degree Celsius). Average annual rainfall is {{convert|1900|cm|abbr=off}} (approx). < ref > {{cite web |url=http://dcmsme.gov.in/dips/DIP,%20Bahraich.pdf |title=District Industrial Profile of Bahraich district |publisher=MSME-Development Institute -Allahabad, Government of India Ministry of MSME |access-date=20 November 2019 |archive-date=12 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150412113757/http://dcmsme.gov.in/dips/DIP,%20Bahraich.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >

 ==Demographics== 
 As of [[2011 Indian Census]], Bahraich had a total population of 186,223, of which 97,653 were males and 88,570 were females. Population within the age group of 0 to 6 years was 24,097. The total number of literates in Bahraich was 119,564, which constituted 64.2% of the population with male literacy of 66.5% and female literacy of 61.7%. The effective literacy rate of 7+ population of Bahraich was 73.7%, of which male literacy rate was 76.4% and female literacy rate was 70.8%. The [[Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes]] population was 9,584 and 170 respectively. Bahraich had 30460 households in 2011. < ref name= " Census2011Gov " > {{cite web |title=Census of India: Bahraich |url=http://censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=197909 |website=www.censusindia.gov.in |access-date=9 October 2019}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Politics== 
 Bahraich is divided into two [[Parliament of India|parliamentary]] constituencies, [[Bahraich (Lok Sabha constituency)|Bahraich]] and [[Kaiserganj (Lok Sabha constituency)|Kaiserganj]]. The former is held by [[Akshaibar Lal]] of the [[Bharatiya Janata Party]] (BJP) and the latter by [[Brij Bhushan Sharan Singh]], also of the BJP. 
 
 There are seven [[Legislative Assembly of Uttar Pradesh|State Assembly]] constituencies: 
 * [[Bahraich (Assembly constituency)|Bahraich]] – [[Anupma Jaiswal]] (BJP) 
 * [[Matera (Assembly constituency)|Matera]] – [[Yasar Shah]] ([[Samajwadi Party]]) 
 * [[Mahasi (Assembly constituency)|Mahasi]] – Sureshwar Singh (BJP) 
 * [[Kaiserganj (Assembly constituency)|Kaiserganj]] – Mukut Bihari Verma (BJP) 
 * [[Balha (Assembly constituency)|Balha]] – Saroj Sonkar (BJP) 
 * [[Payagpur (Assembly constituency)|Payagpur]] – Subhash Tripathi (BJP) 
 * [[Nanpara (Assembly constituency)|Nanpara]] – Madhuri Verma (BJP) 
 
 ==Transportation== 
 
 ===Roads=== 
 Bahraich is well connected with other districts of [[Uttar Pradesh]]. [[UPSRTC]] and private operators provide road connectivity to [[Lucknow]], [[Kanpur]], [[Allahabad]], [[Varanasi]], [[Bareilly]], [[Haridwar]], [[Delhi]], [[Jaipur]] [[Balrampur]], [[Gonda district|Gonda]], [[Barabanki district|Barabanki]], [[Pratapgarh, Uttar Pradesh|Pratapgarh]], [[Shimla]], [[Mathura]], [[Jhansi]], [[Jaunpur, Uttar Pradesh|Jaunpur]], [[Gorakhpur]], [[Varanasi]], [[Shravasti]] and [[Agra]]. There are buses for Lucknow in every 15 minutes. [[National Highway 927 (India)]] connects the city to [[Barabanki, Uttar Pradesh|Barabanki]] and state capital [[Lucknow]]. 
 
 ===Railways=== 
 [[File:Bg train.jpg|thumb|Broad Gauge train at Bahraich]] 
 [[File:YDDM4.jpg|thumb|Metre gauge engine at bahraich meter gauge yard.]] 
 [[Bahraich railway station]] is a main railway station in [[Bahraich district]], [[Uttar Pradesh]]. Its code is ''BRK''. It serves Bahraich city. The station consists of 3 platforms, two for broad gauge and one for meter gauge. Bahraich to Jarwal Road is a Broad gauge station 55 kilometers (34 miles) from district headquarters and lies on Delhi–Barauni line. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://m.jagran.com/uttar-pradesh/bahraich-rail-line-18579562.html |title=बहराइच से जरवलरोड तक बिछे रेल लाइन |access-date=20 December 2018 |date=27 October 2018 |language=hi}} < /ref >  Plans for converting the Bahraich – [[Gonda, Uttar Pradesh|Gonda]] tracks to broad-gauge have been in talks since October 2018. < ref > {{cite web |title=नवरात्र में बहराइच से गोंडा के बीच चल सकती ट्रेन |url=https://www.amarujala.com/uttar-pradesh/bahraich/71539017461-bahraich-news |website=[[Amar Ujala]] |access-date=20 December 2018 |date=8 October 2018 |language=hi}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news |title=Broad gauge rail line from Bahraich to Burhwal {{!}} Allahabad News – Times of India |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/allahabad/Broad-gauge-rail-line-from-Bahraich-to-Burhwal/articleshow/4129523.cms |access-date=20 November 2019 |work=The Times of India |date=14 February 2009|language=en}} < /ref >  It is complete as of July 2020, but due to lack of electrification, only diesel locomotives are run in these tracks.
