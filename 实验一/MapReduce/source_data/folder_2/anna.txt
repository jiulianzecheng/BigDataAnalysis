The name '''Anna''' [ah-nuh] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of anna {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/anna|access-date=2023-03-14|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a female [[given name]] with multiple variants. In [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]], it means  " favor "  or  " grace " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=anna name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-anna-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-14|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The variant ''Anne'' was originally [[French language|French]], although it has been in use in [[English language|English]] for hundreds of years. The variant spelling ''Ann'' was originally English. There are countless famous people, [[queen]]s and saints under this name and its variants(The year when the name Anna was most popular is 1904. In that year, the number of births is 5330, which represents 1.823 percent of total female births in 1904). < ref > {{Cite web|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/|access-date=2023-03-14|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref >  This name was ranked #84 on the US Popular Female Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-27|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >   
 
 The [[Spanish language|Spanish]] variant ''Ana'' has been used for [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] [[tropical cyclone]]s. There are also many composite variants of '''Anna''', including ''Annabelle'' (which is French) and ''Annabella'' (which is [[Italian language|Italian]]).
