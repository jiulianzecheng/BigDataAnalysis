 The '''Basques''' are an [[ethnic group]] from the western [[Pyrenees]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Basque|url=http://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/oi/authority.20110803095450510|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924123542/http://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/oi/authority.20110803095450510|archive-date=24 September 2015|access-date=3 November 2016|work=Oxford Reference online}} < /ref >  Basques speak the [[Basque language]] and share [[Ancestor|ancestry]] with ancient [[Vascones]] and [[Aquitanians]]. < ref name= " GüntherValdiosera2015 " > {{cite journal|last=Günther|first=Torsten|display-authors=etal|year=2015|title=Ancient genomes link early farmers from Atapuerca in Spain to modern-day Basques|journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences|volume=112|issue=38|pages=11917–11922|bibcode=2015PNAS..11211917G|doi=10.1073/pnas.1509851112|pmc=4586848|pmid=26351665|doi-access=free}} < /ref > < ref name= " Olalde2019 " > {{cite journal|last=Olalde|first=Iñigo|display-authors=etal|year=2019|title=The genomic history of the Iberian Peninsula over the past 8000 years|journal=Science|volume=363|issue=6432|pages=1230–1234|bibcode=2019Sci...363.1230O|doi=10.1126/science.aav4040|pmc=6436108|pmid=30872528}} < /ref >   
 
 Basques are [[Indigenous peoples|indigenous]] to area known as the [[Basque Country (greater region)|''Euskal Herria'']], located on the coast of the [[Bay of Biscay]] on the [[Iberian Peninsula]] and south-western [[France]]. < ref name= " Olalde2019 "  / > 
 
 == Culture == 
 
 === Language === 
 {{main|Basque language}} 
 The Basque language ([[Exonym and endonym|endonym]]: e''uskara'') is spoken by 25%-30% < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.eke.eus/en/kultura/euskara-the-basque-language/basque-sociolinguistics|title=Basque sociolinguistics|publisher=Eke.eus|access-date=2019-10-21|archive-date=2019-08-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190802142337/https://www.eke.eus/en/kultura/euskara-the-basque-language/basque-sociolinguistics|url-status=live}} < /ref >  of people in Basque Country. Basque is a [[language isolate]] and is unlike other languages. 
 
 ===Sports=== 
 [[Basque pelota]] is a sport played in [[Basque Country (greater region)|Basque Country]] and other places of the world. [[Jai Alai]], a type of Basque pelota, can be found in the [[United States]] and [[Macau]]. 
 
 === Religion === 
 Traditionally, Basques are [[Catholicism|Catholic]]. [[Ignatius of Loyola|Ignatius Loyola]], founder of the [[Society of Jesus]], was a Basque.
