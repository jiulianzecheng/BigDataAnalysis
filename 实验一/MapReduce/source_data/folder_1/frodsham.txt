'''Frodsham''' is a small market town in the [[county]] of [[Cheshire]] in the north west of [[England]], north west of [[Chester, Cheshire|Chester]], on the [[River Weaver]], and overlooking the [[River Mersey]] [[estuary]]. 
 
 The town is of Saxon origin, its 11th century church is mentioned in the [[Domesday Book]]. When a salt refinery was opened in 1964, salt was brought by river from [[Northwich]].
