 '''Rodez''' ({{lang-oc|Rodés}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in southwestern [[France]] in the [[Departments of France|department]] of [[Aveyron]], of which it is its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]. It is in the [[Regions of France|region]] of [[Occitanie]]. 
 
 It was the [[Capital city|capital]] of [[Rouergue]], a former [[France|French]] province. Rodez is also the [[Capital city|capital]] of 3 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']]: [[Canton of Rodez-1|Rodez-1]], [[Canton of Rodez-2|Rodez-2]] and [[Canton of Rodez-Onet|Rodez-Onet]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Existing from at least the 5th century BC, Rodez was founded by [[Celts|Celtic]] people, the [[Ruteni]]. Here they built an ''[[oppidum]]'' ([[Hill fort|fortified]] place) which, after the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] occupation, was named ''Segodunum'' ( " Fortified place " ,  " high hill " ). The town later was given the name of ''Civitas Rutenorum'' ( " City of the Ruteni " ) and then only ''Ruteni'' and, finally, Rodez. < ref name= " amicale " > {{cite web |url=http://www.amicale-ruthenoise.com/?page_id=57 |title=Son Histoire: L'Antiquité |publisher=La Ruthènoise |language=French |accessdate=27 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 After the fall of the [[Western Roman Empire]], the city was taken by the [[Visigoths]] and then by the [[Franks]]; in 725, the [[Moors]] took the city and destroyed the old [[church]]. Later it was occupied by the [[Army|armies]] of the [[Duchy of Aquitaine|Dukes of Aquitaine]] and of the [[Counts of Toulouse]]. [[English people|English]] [[Army|troops]] occupied Rodez during the [[Hundred Years War]]. < ref name= " amicale "  / > 
 
 With the creation of the Aveyron department in 1790, Rodez became [[Prefectures in France|préfecture]] of the department. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/12his.html |title=Historique de l'Aveyron |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=27 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The ''commune'' of Rodez is on the western part of the central [[plateau]] (''[[Massif Central]]'') of France. The [[Aveyron (river)|Aveyron]] river and the ''Auterne'' [[stream]] flow through the city. 
 
 Rodez has an area of {{Convert|11.2|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }}, < ref name= " insee-geo " > {{cite web |url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/statistiques/1405599?geo=COM-12202 |title=Commune de Rodez (12202) |work=Comparateur de territoire |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |format= |language=French |accessdate=18 March 2017 |date= }} < /ref >  and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|572|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|627|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Rodez-12000/ |title=Rodez |publisher=Map-France.com |language= |accessdate=27 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref >

 == Administration == 
 Rodez is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Aveyron department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Rodez|Rodez]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of three [[Cantons of France|cantons]]: 
 * [[Canton of Rodez-1|Rodez-1]], with 11,905 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Rodez-2|Rodez-2]], with 12,051 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Rodez-Onet|Rodez-Onet]], with 14,105 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Rodez Agglomération'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération Rodez Agglomération}}).

 ==Places of interest== 
 Some interesting places in Rodez are: 
 * The '''Rodez Cathedral''' ({{lang-fr|Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Rodez}}) is a [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholic]] [[cathedral]];  is the main [[Religion|religious]] building of the department. 
 * The '''Episcopal Palace''' ({{lang-fr|Palais épiscopal de Rodez}}), built in the 15th century. 
 * The '''Saint-Amans [[Church]]''', built in the 12th century and then completely rebuilt from 1758 to 1761 with materials from the original building. 
 * The '''''Hôtel de la préfecture''''', built in the first half of the 18th century. It is now home to the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Aveyron department.
