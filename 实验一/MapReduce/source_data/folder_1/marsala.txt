 '''Marsala''' ({{lang-scn|Maissala}}; {{lang-la|Lilybaeum}}) is an [[Italy|Italian]] city in [[Sicily]]. It is in the [[Province of Trapani]]. In 2017, 82,640 people lived there. 
 
 The city is known for the docking of [[Giuseppe Garibaldi]] on 11 May 1860 (the ''[[Expedition of the Thousand]]'') and for its [[Marsala wine]]. 
 
 Marsala is built on the ruins of the ancient [[Ancient Carthage|Carthaginian]] city of Lilybaeum, and is a famous archaeological site of the island of [[Motya]], an ancient Phoenician town.
