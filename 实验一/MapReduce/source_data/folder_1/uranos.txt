'''Uranos''' is the [[sky]] in [[Greek mythology]]. It is also spelt '''Ouranos''', and in [[Ancient Greek language|Ancient Greek]]: Οὐρανός -  " sky "  or  " heaven " .  He is the son and husband of the primordial goddess [[Gaia (mythology)|Gaia]] and father of the [[Titans]], [[Hekatonkheires]], and [[Cyclopes|Elder Cyclopes]].  
 His Roman equivalent was [[Caelus]], the husband of [[Terra (mythology)|Terra]], as Gaia was known to the Romans. 
 
 The [[planet]] [[Uranus]] is named after Uranos. So is the [[radioactive]] [[chemical element]] [[uranium]].
