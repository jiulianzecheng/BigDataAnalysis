 '''Adele Laurie Blue Adkins''' (born 5 May 1988) is an [[England|English]] singer, songwriter, musician and multi-instrumentalist. In 2023, she appeared at number 22 on the ''Rolling Stone'' magazine's  " 200 best singers of all time "  list. < ref > {{Cite web|last=|first=|last2=|first2=|date=2023-01-01|title=The 200 Greatest Singers of All Time|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/best-singers-all-time-1234642307/|access-date=2023-01-12|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Early life== 
 Adele Laurie Blue Adkins was born on 5 May 1988, at North Middlesex University Hospital in [[Edmonton, London]]. She is the daughter of a Welshman named Mark Evans and Penny Adkins. She has a half-brother called Cameron. She lived with his father and he started singing from the age of four, < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5Dr_AgAAQBAJ & dq=adele+born+london+hospital & pg=PT8|title=Someone Like Adele|last=Sanderson|first=Caroline|date=2012-03-13|publisher=Omnibus Press|isbn=978-0-85712-992-5|language=en}} < /ref >  She [[graduate]]d from The BRIT School in [[Croydon]] in May 2006.  
 
 In July 2006, Adele published two [[song]]s in the fourth issue of [[online]] arts [[magazine]] PlatformsMagazine.com. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.platformsmagazine.com/4/contents.htm|title=Platforms Magazine – Issue 4}} < /ref >  After graduation, Adele began to play small shows at venues all over [[United Kingdom|Britain]]. She has also toured with best friend [[Jack Peñate]], as well as [[Jamie T]], Raul Midon, [[Devendra Banhart]], Amos Lee and Keren Ann since summer 2006. Adele went on her first UK headlining tour in October 2007. 
 
 ==Singing career== 
 Adele's first [[Single (music)|single]] released was  " [[Hometown Glory]] " ''.'' On it, she sings about Tottenham. The single was released on 22 October 2007 on Jamie T's [[Record label|label]] Pacemaker Recordings. It is a limited edition 7 "  [[album|vinyl]] with B-side  " [[Best For Last|Best for Last]] " . She has since signed to independent music label XL Recordings. In January 2008, she released her second [[song|single]]  " [[Chasing Pavements]] " ''.'' 
 
 Adele has become quite popular on [[MySpace]]. She is following the example of fellow Londoner [[Lily Allen]] by using the [[internet|web]] as a [[media]]focus and a place to see what people think of her songs. Adele performed ''Daydreamer'' on [[BBC2]]'s ''Later With Jools Holland,'' alongside [[Paul McCartney]] and [[Björk]]. She was next on BBC1's ''[[Friday Night with Jonathan Ross]]'' on 7 December 2007, singing ''[[Chasing Pavements]].'' 
 
 On 10 December 2007, Adele was awarded with the first [[BRIT Awards]] Critics’ Choice prize. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.brits.co.uk/|title=Brits.co.uk}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.the-tls.co.uk/|title=Home Page – The TLS|website=TheTLS}} < /ref >  She has been called the  " new [[Amy Winehouse]]. "  In January 2008, she was voted winner in the annual BBC 6 Music poll of industry experts ''[[Sound of...|Sound of 2008]],'' for acts to emerge in the coming year. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/7169307.stm|title=Soul singers top new talent list|publisher=BBC 6 Music|date=4 January 2008|access-date=2008-01-04}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2011 Adele released her second album, ''[[21 (album)|21]]'', named after her age at the time it was made. It was number one in more than 26 countries. It became the biggest selling album of the 21st century. 
 
 Her third studio album, ''[[25 (Adele album)|25]]'', was released on 20 November 2015 and debuted at number one all around the world. The music video for the lead single  " [[Hello (Adele song)|Hello]] " , which also debuted at number one in many countries, broke the record for the shortest amount of time for a [[YouTube]] video to reach one billion views. The song was also certified 4× Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America faster than any other song in history. To date, it has been certified 7× Platinum by the RIAA. 
 
 Adele returned to music in October 2021 and announced her fourth album, ''[[30 (Adele album)|30]]'' and the single  " [[Easy on Me]] " , which was released on 15 October 2021, and was her first single after five years. The album was released on 19 November 2021. 
 
 In 2022, she announced that she will sing the theme song for the upcoming [[Nick Jr.]] series ''[[Destiny's Side]]'' which premiered on December 1, 2022. 
 
 ==Personal life== 
 She has a son, Angelo, with her then-boyfriend, Simon Konecki, born in October 19, 2012. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/21/adele-baby-boy-simon-konecki_n_1855810.html|title=Adele's Baby Boy: Singer Welcomes Son With Simon Konecki|date=21 October 2012|via=Huff Post}} < /ref >  In 2013 she won an [[Academy Award]] and [[Golden Globe Award]] for Best Original Song and the Brit Award for Best British Single for the title track for the [[James Bond]] movie ''[[Skyfall]]''. The song also won the award for Best Song for Visual Media at the Grammy Awards. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/music-news/10502550/Grammy-nominations-2014-David-Bowie-among-Robin-Thicke-and-Jay-Z.html|title=Grammy nominations 2014: David Bowie among Robin Thicke and Jay-Z|last=Agencies|date=7 December 2013|via=www.telegraph.co.uk}} < /ref > 
 
 On December 19, 2013, Adele was given an [[MBE]] by the [[Prince of Wales]]. < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-25452604|title=Singer Adele awarded MBE at Palace|work=BBC News |date=19 December 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2017, Adele confirmed that she is married to Konecki. In April 2019, it was reported that the two ended their marriage. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/a27238870/why-adele-simon-konecki-split/|title=Why Adele and Simon Konecki Ended Their Seven-Year Relationship|last=Bailey|first=Alyssa|date=2019-04-23|website=ELLE|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-20}} < /ref >

 ==Grammy Awards== 
 The [[Grammy Award]]s are awarded each year by the [[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]] of the United States for outstanding achievements in the [[Recording Industry Association of America|music industry]]. Some think they are the highest music honor. < ref > {{cite book|last=O'Neill|first=Thomas|title=The Grammys: The Ultimate Unofficial Guide to Music's Highest Honor|publisher=Perigee Trade|year=1999}} < /ref > 
 
 As of February 13, 2017, Adele has received 15 Grammy Awards from 18 nominations. In 2012, Adele became the second female artist to win six awards in one night, after [[Beyoncé|Beyoncé Knowles]] did in 2010. Adele is only the second artist (following [[Christopher Cross]]) to have won all four of the General Field ( " Big Four " ) awards at the Grammys.
