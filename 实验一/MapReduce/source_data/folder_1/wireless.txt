 The word '''wireless''' is used to refer to any type of [[electricity|electrical]] or [[electronics|electronic]] operation which is done without a  " hard wired "  connection. '''Wireless communication''' is the transfer of information over a distance without the use of [[wire]]s. The distances involved may be short (when using a television remote control) or very long (thousands or even millions of kilometers for radio communications). 
 
 ==Uses== 
 One of the most popular wireless technology is the [[mobile phone]], with more than 6.8 billion subscriptions worldwide as of 2013. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://qz.com/179897/more-people-around-the-world-have-cell-phones-than-ever-had-land-lines/|title=More people around the world have cell phones than ever had land-lines|first1=Tim|last1=Fernholz|first2=Tim|last2=Fernholz|website=Quartz|date=25 February 2014 }} < /ref >  The term '''wireless technology''' is generally used for mobile [[information technology]] (IT) equipment. Examples include [[mobile phone]]s, [[Personal Digital Assistant|personal digital assistants]] (PDAs), [[Global Positioning System]] (GPS) units, garage door openers, wireless [[Computer mouse|computer mice]] and [[Keyboard (computer)|keyboards]], [[satellite television]] and cordless [[telephone]]s. 
 
 ==History== 
 The world's first wireless telephone conversation happened in 1880, when [[Alexander Graham Bell]] and Charles Sumner Tainter invented [[photophone]], a telephone that made conversations wirelessly using light beams. < ref > {{cite web | url=http://infoage.umwblogs.org/2011/09/28/alexander-g-bell’s-photophone | title=Alexander G. Bell's Photophone « History of the Information Age — Fall 2011 }} < /ref >     In 1946, a driver in Saint Louis, Mo. placed the first [[cell phone]] call in history. The cell phone was invented by [[Bell Labs]] and future [[AT & T]] CEO, H.I Romnes.   < ref > {{cite web|url=https://about.att.com/innovation/technology_research_museum|title=Technology, Research  &  Museum|website=about.att.com}} < /ref >
