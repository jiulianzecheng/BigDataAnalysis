 '''Tlazolteotl''' (or ''Tlaçolteotl'') is an [[Aztec]] [[goddess]]. She is the goddess of [[Ritual purification|purification]], [[steam bath]], [[Midwife|midwives]], [[sin]] and [[Adultery|adulterers]]. In [[Nahuatl language|Nahuatl]], the word ''tlazolli'' means [[wikt:vice|vice]] and [[disease]]s. Thus, Tlazolteotl was a goddess of filth (sin), vice and sexual misdeeds. However, she was also a purification goddess. < ref > An Illustrated Dictionary of the Gods and Symbols of Ancient Mexico and the Maya, by Mary Miller  &  Karl Taube Publisher: Thames  &  Hudson (April 1997), p. & nbsp;168 < /ref >  Her dual nature is seen in her [[epithet]]s; ''Tlaelquani'' ('she who eats filth [sin]') and ''Tlazolmiquiztli'' ('the death caused by lust'), and Ixcuina or Ixcuinan ('she of two faces'). < ref > Soustelle, J., (1961) The Daily life of the Aztecs, London, WI, pp. & nbsp;104, 199 < /ref > < ref name= " Sahagunp23 " > Bernardino de Sahagun, 1950–1982, Florentine Codex: History of the Things of New Spain, translated and Edited by Arthur J.O. Anderson and Charles Dibble, Monographs of the school of American research, no 14. 13. parts Salt Lake City: University of Utah Press, p. & nbsp;23 < /ref >  Her sisters include Tiacapan (the first born), Teicu (the younger sister), Tlaco (the middle sister) and Xocotzin (the youngest sister). < ref name= " Sahagunp23 " / > 
 
 She is the mother of [[Centeotl]] and Yum-Kax, the [[Maya people|Maya]] [[maize]] god. < ref > [http://www.crystalinks.com/aztecgods.html Aztec Gods and Goddesses] < /ref >
