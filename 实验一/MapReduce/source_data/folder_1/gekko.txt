 '''''Gekko''''' is a common [[genus (biology)|genus]] of colorful  [[southeast Asia]]n lizards. The [[type species]] is ''Geckko gekko''. < ref > " Gekko " . The Reptile Database. www.reptile-database.org. < /ref >  It is also a quite large family, the '''Gekkonidae'''.  
 
 There are 85 [[species]]. Fossils of gekkos date back to the [[Eocene]]. < ref > {{cite web |title=â€ Yantarogekko Bauer et al. 2005 (gecko) |publisher=Fossil works |url=http://fossilworks.org/bridge.pl?a=taxonInfo & taxon_no=90339 |access-date=2019-02-25 |archive-date=2018-04-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180417023732/http://fossilworks.org/bridge.pl?a=taxonInfo & taxon_no=90339 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 Gekkos eat flies and other insects. Their extraordinary feet have thousands of tiny hairs. They can walk up vertical walls quite easily. 
 
 They often live in buildings in the [[tropics]]. They do not tolerate other gekkos in their territory.
