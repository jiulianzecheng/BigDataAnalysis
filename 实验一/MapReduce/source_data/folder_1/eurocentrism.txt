 '''Eurocentrism''' (also '''Western-centrism''') < ref name=hobson > {{cite book | last = Hobson | first = John | title = The Eurocentric conception of world politics : western international theory, 1760-2010 | url = https://archive.org/details/eurocentricconce0000hobs | publisher = Cambridge University Press | location = New York | year = 2012 | page = [https://archive.org/details/eurocentricconce0000hobs/page/185 185] | isbn = 1107020204}} < /ref >  is a worldview about the works and criticism of [[Western culture|Western civilization]]. The exact beliefs of the term changes from the entire [[Western world]] to only [[Europe]] or even just Western Europe (especially during the [[Cold War]]).  
 
 When applied to history, it may refer to a stance towards European [[colonialism]] and other forms of [[imperialism]]. < ref > [https://www.historians.org/publications-and-directories/perspectives-on-history/january-2001/eurocentrism-and-its-discontents Eurocentrism and its discontents], ''[[American Historical Association]]'' < /ref >
