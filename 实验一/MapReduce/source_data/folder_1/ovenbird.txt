 The '''Ovenbird''' (''Seiurus aurocapilla'') is a small [[songbird]] of the order [[Passeriformes]]. Its [[family (biology)|family]] is Parulidae. The ovenbird was named after its [[dome]]d [[nest]] with a side entrance, which looks like an [[oven]]. < ref name=britannica > {{cite encyclopedia |url= http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/435852/ovenbird |title=ovenbird (bird) -- Britannica Online Encyclopedia |first= |last= |encyclopedia=britannica.com |year=2012 |accessdate=25 May 2012}} < /ref >  This [[migration|migratory bird]] is found in eastern [[North America]] and winters in [[Central America]], many [[Caribbean Islands]], [[Florida]], and northern [[Venezuela]]. < ref name = sc8994 > Stiles  &  Skutch (1989), Curson ''et al.'' (1994) < /ref >  The ovenbird walks, unlike most other members of its family, which [[wikt:hop|hop]]. < ref name=britannica/ >
