 Artocarpus heterophyllus is the scientific name for jackfruit, and is family of [[Moraceae|Moracea.]] '''Jackfruit''' (also called  " ''Jakfruit'' " ) is a type of [[fruit]] from [[India]], [[Bangladesh]] (National fruit){{fact|date=April 2009}} and [[Sri Lanka]]. < ref > {{cite web | last =Morton | first =Julia | title =Jackfruit | work = Fruits of warm climates| date =1987 | url =http://www.hort.purdue.edu/newcrop/morton/jackfruit_ars.html#Origin%20and%20Distribution | access-date = February 4, 2009}} < /ref >  When a Jackfruit ripens, it changes from [[green]] to slightly [[yellow]]. 
 
 == Parts of the Fruit == 
 The [[Seed|seeds]] are cooked and taken food, the green like unready [[fruit]] is cooked as a [[plant]] used for food, and the brown made ready is taken [[food]] a little [[cold]]. 
 
 == Jackfruit Tree == 
 The jackfruit [[tree]] can be up to 15-20 meters, < ref > {{Cite web|date=2023-05-08|title=Jackfruit {{!}} Description  &  Uses {{!}} Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/plant/jackfruit|access-date=2023-06-04|website=www.britannica.com|language=en}} < /ref >  and its [[Leaf|leaves]] are a stiff, glossy green color and measure up to about 15 to 20 centimeters long. This tree is considered the largest tree-borne fruit in the world < ref > {{Cite web|date=2017-11-26|title=Largest tree-borne fruit|url=https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/503857-largest-tree-bearing-fruit|access-date=2023-06-04|website=Guinness World Records|language=en-gb}} < /ref >  weighing near 18 kilograms and reaching up to 60 cm long in length.
