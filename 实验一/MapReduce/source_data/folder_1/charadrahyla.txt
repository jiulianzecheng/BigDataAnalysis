 '''''Charadrahyla''''' is a [[genus|group]] of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  They live in southern Mexico. < ref name=Frost/ >  The name of this genus comes from the [[Greek (language)|Greek]] word ''charadra'' ( " ravine " ) and ''Hyla''. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  Accordingly, common name '''ravine treefrogs''' has been coined for the genus. < ref name=Frost/ > 
 
 ==Taxonomy and systematics== 
 Scientists made this genus in 2005 after looking closely at all the frogs in Hylidae. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  The frogs in ''Charadrahyla'' are those that used to be in the ''[[Charadrahyla taeniopus|Hyla taeniopus]]'' group as defined by {{interlanguage link multi|William Edward Duellman|fr|lt=William E. Duellman}} in 1970. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  The genus was originally made  using molecular markers and no morphological [[synapomorphy|synapomorphies]], meaning that scientists only used the frogs' genes to tell whether they should be in this group.  They did not use what the frogs looked like or how their bodies worked. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  The [[sister taxon|closet related group]] to ''Charadrahyla'' is the genus ''[[Megastomatohyla]]''. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / > 
 
 Five of the frogs in this genus were in the genus ''[[Hyla]]'' before 2005. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  Since then, scientists discovered two species of frogs for the first time, and placed them in ''Charadrahyla'' too. < ref name=Frost/ >  Another two were moved into ''Charadrahyla'' from ''[[Exerodonta]]'' in 2018 after scientists studied their [[DNA]]. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 The frogs in ''Charadrahyla'' are large for frogs. They lay eggs in streams in [[cloud forest]]s and humid pine-oak forests of central and southern Mexico. < ref name= " Campbell et al. 2009 " / >  Most of the male frogs in this genus are {{convert|44|-|81|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} long from nose to rear end and females {{convert|60|-|81|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. But the former ''Exerodonta'' species, ''[[Charadrahyla juanitae]]'' and ''[[Charadrahyla pinorum]]'', are much smaller, with males {{convert|28|-|36|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} and females {{convert|35|-|40|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / >  Most species have a brownish [[Dorsum (anatomy)|skin on their backs]] with large spots (exception is ''[[Charadrahyla altipotens]]''). < ref name= " Campbell et al. 2009 " / >

 * ''[[Charadrahyla altipotens]]''  < small > (Duellman, 1968) < /small > < ref name= " Duellman 1968 " / >  — [[yellowbelly voiceless tree frog]] 
 * ''[[Charadrahyla chaneque]]''  < small > (Duellman, 1961) < /small >  — [[fairy tree frog]]  
 * ''[[Charadrahyla esperancensis]]''  < small > (Canseco-Márquez, Ramírez-González, and González-Bernal, 2017) < /small > 
 * ''[[Charadrahyla juanitae]]''  < small > (Snyder, 1972) < /small > 
 * ''[[Charadrahyla nephila]]''  < small > (Mendelson and Campbell, 1999) < /small > < ref name= " Mendelson and Campbell 1999 " / >
