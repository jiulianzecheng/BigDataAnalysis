 '''Samaton''' (/saa-ma-ton/) or '''Samadon''' (/saa-ma-don/) is a [[mythical]] winged [[Deity|divine]] horse in [[Meitei mythology|Meitei mythology]]. He is one of the most recognised creatures in [[Meitei folklore|Meitei folklore]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gxqeDwAAQBAJ & dq=winged+horse+samadon+ayangba & pg=PA111|title=Glimpses of Manipuri Culture|last=Devi|first=Dr Yumlembam Gopi|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=978-0-359-72919-7|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VawSEAAAQBAJ & q=The+Manipuri+pony+is+said+to+have+descended+from+his+mount%2C+Samadon+Ayangba%2C+a+fierce+winged+animal%2C+whose+wings+Marjing+severed. & pg=PT118|title=The Tale of the Horse: A History of India on Horseback|last=Chandra|first=Yashaswini|date=2021-01-22|publisher=Pan Macmillan|isbn=978-93-89109-92-4|language=en}} < /ref >  Legend says that Samaton is the ancestor of the present day [[Manipuri pony]] ([[Meitei horse]]). < ref name= " :1 "  / >  The [[Leithak Leikharol]] describes Samaton as a primordial horse associated with Meitei cosmology. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  It is created by [[Sanamahi]] to attack on [[Pakhangba]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  Later, it became the mounting creature of God [[Marjing]]. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  It is also called '' " Shamadon Ayangba " '' ({{lang-mni|ꯁꯃꯗꯣꯟ ꯑꯌꯥꯡꯕ}}) or '' " Shamaton Ayangpa " '' ({{lang-omp|ꯁꯃꯇꯣꯟ ꯑꯌꯥꯡꯄ}}) due to free variation of words in [[Meitei language]] ([[Manipuri language]]). 
 
 Samaton is praised in the ritual song of [[Ougri]] sung during the [[Lai Haraoba]] festival. Samaton is considered very important to [[Meitei culture]] because it is created from the body of God [[Sanamahi]]. During the deification of Meitei kings, Samaton became associated with kings as it was a divine being. So, Samaton became the insignia of royalty. < ref name= " :0 "  / >
