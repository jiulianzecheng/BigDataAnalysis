 A '''hanamachi''' (花街) is a [[Japan]]ese word used to call a [[neighborhood]] where [[courtesan]]s and [[geisha]] lived and worked. The word's [[literal translation|literal meaning]] is '' " flower town " ''. These neighborhoods used to have many ''okiya'' (''geisha houses'') and ''ochaya'' (''tea houses'', where geishas [[entertainment|entertained]] guests). The [[term]] ''hanamachi'' is used in modern Japan to call the areas where modern-day geisha are still living and working. Today, ''hanamachi'' are rare outside of [[Kyoto]].
