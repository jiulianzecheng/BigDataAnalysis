 The '''Sutlej River''' (sometimes spelled '''Satluj River''') is a [[Tributary|branch]] of the [[Indus River]]. It is the longest of the five major rivers that flow through the region of [[Punjab region|Punjab]] in northern [[India]] and [[Pakistan]]. < ref name= " brit " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/place/Sutlej-River|title=Sutlej River|encyclopedia=Encyclopaedia Britannica|accessdate=5 February 2016}} < /ref >  It is north of the [[Vindhya Range]], south of the [[Hindu Kush]], and east of the Central Makran range in Pakistan. 
 
 It is the easternmost branch of the Indus River. The source of the river is at [[Lake Rakshastal]] in [[Tibet]], < ref name= " brit " / >  near [[Mount Kailas]]. It flows generally west and southwest through the region of Punjab. The region to its south and east is dry, and is known as the [[Thar Desert]]. 
 
 == Name == 
 In [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]], the river's name is written {{lang|pa|ਸਤਲੁਜ}}. In [[Sanskrit]], it is {{lang|sa|शतद्रु or सुतुद्री}} → {{IAST|Suṭudri}}. In [[Urdu]], it is written {{lang-ur|ستلج}}, and {{lang-hi|सतलुज}}. 
 
 In the ''[[Mahabharata]]'', the river is named the '''Satadree'''. < ref > {{cite journal|author=[[Alexander Cunningham]]|title=Journal of a trip through Kulu and Lahul|journal=Journal of the Asiatic Society of Bengal|volume=17|year=1848|publisher=Asiatic Society of Bengal|page=210, paragraph two|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Tes2AQAAMAAJ & pg=PA210}} < /ref >  It is also known as the Red River.  
 
 == Tributaries == 
 [[File:Beas river and mountains as seen from Van Vihar, Manali.jpg|thumb|River Beas and mountains, as seen from Van Vihar, Manali, India]] 
 The Beas River ({{lang-pa|ਬਿਆਸ}}) is  the second easternmost of the rivers of the Punjab region. The river rises in the [[Himalayas]] in central  [[Himachal Pradesh]], India, and flows for some 290 miles (470 & nbsp;km) to the Sutlej River in the west of the [[Punjab (India)|Indian Punjab state]]. 
 
 == Indus Water Treaty == 
 The waters of the Beas and Sutlej rivers as well as of the [[Ravi River]] are allocated to India under the [[Indus Waters Treaty]] between India and Pakistan. < ref name= " brit " / >
