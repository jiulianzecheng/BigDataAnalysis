 '''Puppis''' is a constellation that can be seen in the southern sky. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.topastronomer.com/StarCharts/Constellations/Puppis.php|title=Puppis Constellation on Top Astronomer|website=www.topastronomer.com|access-date=2014-09-16|archive-date=2012-03-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120306213956/http://www.topastronomer.com/StarCharts/Constellations/Puppis.php|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It is one of the 88 modern constellations recognized by the [[International Astronomical Union]]. 
 
 ==Notable features== 
 [[Image:PuppisCC.jpg|thumb|left|200px|The constellation Puppis as it can be seen by the naked eye.]] 
 Argo Navis was sub-divided in 1752 by the French astronomer [[Nicolas Louis de Lacaille]], forming Puppis.
