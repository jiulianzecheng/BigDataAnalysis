{{nihongo|'''Genroku'''|元禄}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Jōkyō]]'' and before ''[[Hōei]].'' This period started in September 1688 and ended in March 1704. < ref name= " Nussbaum 2002 239 " > {{cite book| last = Nussbaum | first = Louis-Frédéric| title = Japan Encyclopedia| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA239| year = 2002| publisher = Harvard University Press| isbn = 978-0-674-01753-5| page = 239 }} < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Higashiyama|Higashiyama]]''-tennō''|東山天皇}}. < ref > {{cite book| last = Nussbaum | first = Louis-Frédéric| title = Japan Encyclopedia| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA310| year = 2002| publisher = Harvard University Press| isbn = 978-0-674-01753-5| page = 310 }} < /ref > 
 
 [[File:1696 Genroku 9 (early Edo) Japanese Map of Kyoto, Japan - Geographicus - Kyoto-genroku9-1696.jpg|thumb|right|Map of Kyoto published in the Genroku era.]] 
 The years of ''Genroku'' are generally considered to be the Golden Age of the [[Edo period]]. A century of peace and seclusion created good economic conditions < ref name= " Nussbaum 2002 239 " / >  and cultural growth. < ref name= " Nussbaum 2002 239 " / > 
 
 The ''[[nengō]]'' ''Genroku'' means  " Origin of Good Fortune " < ref > Jenkins, Donald. (1971). ''Ukiyo-e Prints and Paintings: the Primitive Period, 1680-1745,'' p. 21. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Genroku'' era== 
 [[File:Oishi Yoshio Gishi Seppuku No Zu Painting.png|thumb|right|In the 16th day of ''Genroku'', [[Ōishi Yoshio]] killed himself in a public [[ceremony]].]] 
 * '''1688''' (''Genroku 1''):  [[Tokugawa shogunate]] published a code of conduct for funerals (''Fuku-kiju-ryō'') and for mourning. < ref > Smith, Robert ''et al.'' (2004). ''Japanese Culture: Its Development And Characteristics'', p. 28. < /ref > 
 * '''16 September 1689''' (''Genroku 2, 3rd day of the 7th month'') < !-- NengoCalc: 
 元禄二年八月三日 -- > : [[Engelbert Kaempfer]] arrived at [[Dejima]]. < ref > [[Timon Screech|Screech, Timon]]. (2006). ''Secret Memoirs of the Shoguns: Isaac Titsingh and Japan, 1779-1822'', p. 73. < /ref > 
 * '''1693''' (''Genroku 6, 12th month''):  [[Arai Hakuseki]] became the tutor of [[Tokugawa Ienobu]]. 
 * '''1693''' (''Genroku 6''): The code of conduct for funerals and mourning was changed. < ref > Smith, p. 28. < /ref > 
 * '''1695''' (''Genroku 8, 8th month''): The shogunate placed the Japanese character ''gen'' (元) on copper coins. < ref name =  " titsingh415 " > {{cite book| last = Klaproth| first = Julius von| title = Nipon o dai itsi ran: ou Annales des empereurs du Japon| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ| year = 1834| publisher = Oriental Translation Fund| isbn = | page = 415 }} < /ref > 
 * '''4 December 1696''': Former-[[Empress Meishō]] died at age 74. < ref > {{cite book| last = Meyer| first = Eva-Maria| title = Japans Kaiserhof in der Edo-Zeit: unter besonderer Berucksichtigung der Jahre 1846 bis 1867| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=6wEvo4wBojcC & pg=PA186| year = 1999| publisher = Lit| isbn = 978-3-8258-3939-0| page = 186 }} < /ref > 
 * '''1697''' (''Genroku 10''): The 4th official map of Japan was made. < ref > Traganeou, Jilly. (2004). ''The Tokaido Road: Traveling and Representation in Edo and Meiji Japan'', p. 230. < /ref > 
 * '''1697''' (''Genroku 10''): Great fire in Edo. < ref name= " titsingh415 "  / > 
 * '''1698''' (''Genroku 11''): Another great fire in Edo. < ref name= " titsingh415 "  / > 
 * '''1703''' (''Genroku 16, 3rd month''): [[Ōishi Yoshio]] commits [[seppuku]]. < ref > {{cite book| last = Nussbaum | first = Louis-Frédéric| title = Japan Encyclopedia| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA742| year = 2002| publisher = Harvard University Press| isbn = 978-0-674-01753-5| page = 742 }} < /ref > 
 * '''1703''' (''Genroku 16, 5th month''): First performance of [[Chikamatsu Monzaemon]]'s play ''The Love Suicides at Sonezaki'' (''Sonezaki shinjū''). < ref > {{cite book| last = Nussbaum | first = Louis-Frédéric| title = Japan Encyclopedia| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA112| year = 2002| publisher = Harvard University Press| isbn = 978-0-674-01753-5| page = 112 }} < /ref > 
 * '''31 December 1703''' (''Genroku 16, 23rd day of the 11th month'') < !-- NengoCalc: 元禄十六年十一月二十三日 -- > : The [[1703 Genroku earthquake|Great Genroku Earthquake]] shook Edo. < ref name =  " hammer63 " > {{cite book| last = Hammer| first = Joshua| title = Yokohama Burning: The Deadly 1923 Earthquake and Fire that Helped Forge the Path to World War II| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=6O8VyhDbUPgC| year = 2006| publisher = Simon and Schuster| isbn = 978-0-7432-6465-5| page = 63 }} < /ref >   Fire spread in the city. < ref name= " titsingh415 "  / >   The coast of Honshū was hit by tsunami. < ref name= " hammer63 " / >
