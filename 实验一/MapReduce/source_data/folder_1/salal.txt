 '''Salal''' is the common [[English language|English]] name of a [[plant]] in the [[family (biology)|family]] [[Ericaceae]]. The [[Lewis and Clark Expedition]] found it in [[North America]]. The expedition took place from 1804 to 1806. [[Frederick Traugott Pursh]] first described it. The official [[Botany|botanical]] name is ''Gaultheria shallon''. It is also known as '''shallon''' or '''Gaultheria''' in [[English language|English]]. The plant was introduced to [[Great Britain]] in 1828. Today, it is often used for [[flower arrangement]]s. It was also used as a cover for [[pheasant]]s. The plant also has [[medicine|medicinal]] properties. It is used for [[treatment]] of [[inflammation]] and [[cramp]]s. The plant is called  " Shallon-Scheinbeere "  in [[German language]]. The young leaves and [[berries]] can be eaten.{{biology-stub}}
