 '''Murten''' ([[French language|French]]: ''Morat'') is a [[city]] in the [[Cantons of Switzerland|canton]] of [[Fribourg (canton)|Fribourg]] in [[Switzerland]]. It is a historic city on the south side of [[Lake Morat]]. The name of the city first appears in 515 as ''Muratum''. The name is probably related to [[Celtic languages|Celtic]] ''Moridunum''. This is made from ''Mori'' (lake), and ''dunum'' ([[castle]], [[fortification]]). The city used to be part of the [[duchy]] of [[Burgundy]]. It got city rights in 1337. 
 
 Today, about 6,000 people live there. Almost all of the old parts of the city still exist.  In 2000, about 77% of the people spoke [[German language|German]], 13% spoke [[French language|French]], and about 2% spoke [[Italian language|Italian]]. < ref name=webmurten > {{cite web|url=http://www.murten-morat.ch/de/portrait/zahlenfakten/|title=Website of Murten, data|language=German|access-date=2008-10-11|archive-date=2013-12-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203155146/http://www.murten-morat.ch/de/portrait/zahlenfakten/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  About 19% of the people living there are [[foreigner]]s. < ref name=webmurten / > 
 
 On 1 January 1975 the former municipality of [[Burg bei Murten]] merged into the municipality of Murten. < ref name=Mutation > [http://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/portal/de/index/infothek/nomenklaturen/blank/blank/gem_liste/02.html Nomenklaturen – Amtliches Gemeindeverzeichnis der Schweiz] {{in lang|de}} accessed 9 February 2013 < /ref >  It was created on 1 January 1991 by the former municipality of [[Altavilla]] and on 1 January 2013 by the former municipality of [[Büchslen]]. On 1 January 2016 the former municipalities of [[Courlevon]], [[Jeuss]], [[Lurtigen]] and [[Salvenach]] merged into Murten. < ref > [http://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/portal/de/index/infothek/nomenklaturen/blank/blank/gem_liste/02.html Nomenklaturen – Amtliches Gemeindeverzeichnis der Schweiz] {{in lang|de}} accessed 3 February 2016 < /ref >
