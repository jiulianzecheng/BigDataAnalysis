 '''''Salicaceae''''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[flowering plant]]s. Each plant has either [[staminate]] or [[pistillate]] [[catkin]]s. The [[flowers]] have no [[petal]]s or [[sepal]]s. Each has a small [[scale]]-like [[bract]] below it. These developing catkins on [[willow]]s form the well-known  " pussy-willow "  branches. 
 
 Except for two [[species]] of ''[[Populas]]'' ([[Cottonwood]]), all the members of ''Salicaceae'' in the [[Bay Area]] are of the [[Genus|genera]] ''[[Salix]]'' ([[Willow]]s).
