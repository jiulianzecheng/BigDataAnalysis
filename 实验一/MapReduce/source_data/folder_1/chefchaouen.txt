 '''Chefchaouen''' or '''Chaouen''' (Berber: ⵛⴰⵡⴻⵏ/ⵛⴼⵛⴰⵡⴻⵏ, Arabic: شفشاون/الشاون) is a small important [[historical]] and [[Tourism|tourist]] city in the [[north]] of [[Maghreb]]. Chefchaouen is located in the [[Rif]] [[Mountain]]s of northwestern [[Morocco]]. < ref > Tom Masters, ''Europe on a Shoestring'' (Oakland, CA: Lonely Planet, 2009), p. 815 < /ref >  Many [[Morisco]]s and [[Jew]]s settled in this city after the [[Iberian Peninsula|Spanish]] [[Reconquista]] in the [[Middle Ages#Late Middle Ages|Late Middle Ages]]. The city is famous for its blue houses and alleys.
