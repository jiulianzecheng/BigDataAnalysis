 '''Cedarosaurus''' is a [[genus]] of [[sauropod]] [[dinosaur]] from the [[Lower Cretaceous]] [[epoch (geology)|epoch]]. < ref > Michael P. Taylor; Mathew J. Wedel; Richard L. Cifelli, 'A new sauropod dinosaur from the Lower Cretaceous Cedar Mountain Formation, Utah, USA', ''Acta Palaeontologica Polonica'', Vol. 56, No.1 (2011), p. 75 < /ref >  It was a [[herbivore]] of unknown size. < ref name=prehistwl > {{cite web |url=http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/species/c/cedarosaurus.html |title=Cedarosaurus |publisher=Prehistoric-Wildlife.com |access-date=February 6, 2017}} < /ref >  Cedarosaurus was named for the [[Cedar Mountain Formation]] in eastern [[Utah]], [[USA]]. < ref name=prehistwl/ >  It was a [[Brachiosaur|brachiosaurid sauropod]] that ate tall [[vegetation]] from the [[tree]] [[canopy (biology)|canopy]]. < ref name=prehistwl/ >  The skeleton was found in the Cedar Mountains the late 1990s by a team of [[paleontologist]]s from the [[Denver Museum of Nature and Science]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/IOTD/view.php?id=5494 |title=Images |website=The Earth Observatory |publisher= EOS Project Science Office, NASA Goddard Space Flight Center |access-date=February 6, 2017}} < /ref >  The skeleton was missing the [[neck]], [[head]] and other parts. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.dinochecker.com/dinosaurs/CEDAROSAURUS |title=Cedarosaurus |publisher=Dinochecker.com |access-date=February 6, 2017}} < /ref >
