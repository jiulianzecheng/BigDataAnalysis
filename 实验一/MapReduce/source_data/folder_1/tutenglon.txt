 ''' " Tutenglon " ''' ({{lang-mni|ꯇꯨꯇꯦꯡꯂꯣꯟ}}) is an ancient [[Puya (Meitei texts)|Meitei text]], based on the saga of the two Meitei princes, [[Yoimongba]] and [[Taothingmang]], who took upon themselves the superhuman task of dredging the channels of the biggest and the longest rivers in [[Manipur]], the [[Iril River|Iril]] and the [[Imphal River|Imphal]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yiBkAAAAMAAJ & q=tutenglon|title=A History of Manipuri Literature|last=Singh|first=Ch Manihar|date=1996|publisher=Sahitya Akademi|isbn=978-81-260-0086-9|language=en}} < /ref >  It also describes about the flood that happened during the reign of king [[Ngangoi Yoimongba]] in [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=r8EJAQAAMAAJ & q=tutenglon|title=Journal of the Indian Musicological Society|last=Society|first=Indian Musicological|date=1971|publisher=Indian Musicological Society|language=en}} < /ref >  The text also mentions that [[Yoimongba]] and [[Taothingmang]] are the two sons and [[Lairoklembi]] is a daughter of Meitei king [[Khuyoi Tompok]]. Princess Lairoklembi was married to the chief of [[Koupalu|Koubru]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GmZuAAAAMAAJ & q=tutenglon|title=Genesis of Indian Tribes: An Approach to the History of Meiteis and Thais|last=Tensuba|first=Keerti Chand|date=1993|publisher=Inter-India Publications|isbn=978-81-210-0308-7|language=en}} < /ref >
