 '''Shri''' ({{IPAc-en|ʃ|r|iː}}; < ref name= " oxford " > {{Cite web|title=Shri|url=https://www.lexico.com/en/definition/shri|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191030060633/https://www.lexico.com/en/definition/shri|archive-date=October 30, 2019|access-date=30 October 2019|website=Lexico|publisher=Oxford English Dictionary}} < /ref >  {{Lang-sa|श्री|translit=Śrī}}, {{IPA-sa|ɕriː|pron}}{{IPA-sa|ɕriː|pron}}{{IPA-sa|ɕriː|pron}}) is a Sanskrit [[term]] that means wealth and [[Prosperity theology|prosperity]]. It is mainly used as an [[honorific]]. < ref name= " oxford "  / > 
 
 == Places == 
 Lots of places use the honorific. Some of them are: 
 
 * [[Sriharikota]], India 
 * Srimangal, Bangladesh 
 * [[Srisailam]], Andhra Pradesh, a [[Shiva|Siva]] temple. 
 * Srikakulam, a town in northern Andhra Pradesh. 
 * Sri City, an integrated township located on the Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu border. 
 * Shri Kshetra, name of the Puri [[Jagannath]] Dham, [[Odisha]]. One of the four Dhams in the Hindu religion. 
 * Sree Mandira is a famous [[Mandir|Hindu temple]] built for [[Jagannath]] ([[Krishna]]). It is in the coastal town of Puri in Odisha. 
 * [[Sri Lanka]], an [[island country]] under [[India]]. 
 * Sri Perumbudur, a town in the state of [[Tamil Nadu]] 
 * [[Srirangam|Sri Rangam]], an island zone in the city of [[Tiruchirappalli|Tiruchirapalli]], in [[Tamil Nadu]]. 
 * [[Srinagar]], ''nagar'' meaning  " city " , is the capital of the [[Union territory]] of [[Jammu and Kashmir (state)|Jammu and Kashmir]] 
 * [[Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte|Sri Jayawardenapura Kotte]], the administrative capital of [[Sri Lanka]]. 
 * [[Bodhi Tree|Sri Maha Bodhi]], a sacred fig tree in the Mahamewna Gardens, Anuradhapura, [[Sri Lanka]]. 
 * Srivijaya, a former kingdom on Sumatra, [[Indonesia]]. 
 * Sriwijaya University, located in [[South Sumatra]], [[Indonesia]] 
 * Sri ({{Lang|th|ศรี}}), pronounced and usually transliterated ''Si'' in [[Thailand]] place names
