 '''''Anubias''''' is a genus of tropical plants in the [[Family (biology)|family]] [[Araceae]]. < ref name= " Crusio " > {{cite journal |last=Crusio |first=W. |author-link=Wim Crusio |year=1979 |title=A revision of Anubias Schott (Araceae). (Primitiae Africanae XII) |journal=Mededelingen Landbouwhogeschool Wageningen |volume=79 |issue=14 |pages=1–48 |url=http://edepot.wur.nl/165228 |access-date=2010-12-26}} < /ref >  Members of this [[genus]] are popular as [[aquarium]] plants. Because of the often shady places where the plants grow, the genus was named after the [[Anubis|Egyptian god Anubis]], the god of the afterlife. < ref name= " Crusio " / >  The genus was first described in 1857 by [[Heinrich Wilhelm Schott]], with ''[[Anubias afzelii|A. afzelii]]'' as its [[type species]]. < ref > {{cite journal |last=Schott |first=H. |date=December 1857 |title=Aroideen Skizzen |journal=[[Österreichisches Botanisches Wochenblatt]] |volume=7 |issue=50 |pages=398–399 |doi=10.1007/BF02071618 |language=de, la}} < /ref >  Besides A. afzelii, the genus has the following [[species]]:  ''[[Anubias barteri|A. barteri]], [[Anubias gigantea|A. gigantea]], [[Anubias gilletii|A. gilletii]], [[Anubias gracilis|A. gracilis]], [[Anubias hastifolia|A. hastifolia]], [[Anubias heterophylla|A. heterophylla]]'', and ''[[Anubias pynaertii|A. pynaertii]]''. < ref name= " Anubias " > {{cite journal |author=Crusio WE |title=Die Gattung ''Anubias'' SCHOTT (Araceae) |journal=[[Aqua Planta]] |volume=Sonderheft |issue=1 |pages=1–44 |year=1987 |language=de}} < /ref >
