 The '''Bloodhound''' is a large [[breed]] of [[dog]] [[famous]] for its [[wikt:tracking|tracking]] ability by [[scent]]. It stands 23–27 [[inch]]es (58–69 [[cm]]) tall and weighs 80–110 [[Pound (mass)|pounds]] (36–50 & nbsp;[[kg]]). It has a long nose, large ears with [[Wrinkle (skin)|wrinkles]] on its face. Its [[fur]] is short and its [[coat (dog)|coat]] is usually a brown and black color. Bloodhounds are known to be a gentle and affectionate breed. < ref name= " Britannica " / >  This breed lives an average of six years and nine months. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.thekennelclub.org.uk/download/1518/hsbloodhound.pdf|title=Report from the Kennel Club/ British Small Animal Veterinary Association Scientific Committee|publisher=The Kennel Club|access-date=2010-08-22|archive-date=2011-01-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110101095448/http://www.thekennelclub.org.uk/download/1518/hsbloodhound.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Bloodhounds are often used by [[police]] because of their tracking ability. < ref name= " Britannica " > {{cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/69930/bloodhound|title=Bloodhound|publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica Online|access-date=2010-08-22}} < /ref >
