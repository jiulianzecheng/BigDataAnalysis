 '''''Pithecopus''''' is a genus of [[frog]]s in the family [[Phyllomedusidae]], < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > < ref name= " Andrade et al. 2020 " / >  but some scientists say it is in the [[subfamily]] Phyllomedusinae in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  These frogs live in tropical South America east of the [[Andes]], from southern Venezuela to northern Argentina. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > < ref name= " Andrade et al. 2020 " / >  For a while, scientists said this was another name for ''[[Phyllomedusa]]'', but, in 2016, they changed it to ''[[Pithecopus hypochondrialis|Phyllomedusa hypochondrialis]]'' group. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / >  Its [[sister group]] is ''[[Callimedusa]]''. < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Name== 
 The name of this genus comes from the Greek ''pithekodes'', which means  " ape-like. " < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 ''Pithecopus'' frogs are medium-sized frogs measuring about {{convert|4.5|cm|in|1|abbr=on}} in [[snout–vent length]], which means from nose to rear end. On their feet, Toe I is much longer than toe II, and is opposable to it like a thumb. These frogs do not have [[vomerine teeth]]. The [[tadpole]]s have a moderately small oral disc on the bottoms of their bodies, near the front. < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / >
