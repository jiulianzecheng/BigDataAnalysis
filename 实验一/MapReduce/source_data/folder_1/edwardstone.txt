 == History == 
 The name Edwardstone means Farmstead of a man called Edweard. < ref > {{cite book| last = Mills| first = A.D.| title = A Dictionary of British Place-Names| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=br8xcW1f_a8C & pg=PA482| year = 2003| publisher = Oxford University Press| isbn = 978-0-19-157847-2| page = 482 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| last = Rye| first = James| title = Popular Guide to Suffolk Place Names| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=x-PvnAOvLPAC & pg=PA20| year = 1997| publisher = Larks Press| isbn = 978-0-948400-55-1| page = 20 }} < /ref >  Edwardstone was listed in the [[Domesday Book]] as ''Eduardestuna''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/documentsonline/details-result.asp?Edoc_Id=7591733 & queryType=1 & resultcount=1 |title=Place name: Edwardstone, Suffolk Folio: 304r Little Domesday Book Domesday... |publisher=The National Archives |accessdate=2013-10-31}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.domesdaybook.co.uk/suffolk2.html#edwardstone |title=The Domesday Book Online - Suffolk C-E |publisher=Domesdaybook.co.uk |accessdate=2013-10-31}} < /ref >  Edwardstone was once in the [[Babergh hundred]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://essex1841.com/SuffolkHundreds/Babergh_hundred.shtml |title=Babergh in Suffolk 1841 |publisher=Essex1841.com |date=2012-04-07 |accessdate=2013-10-31 |archive-date=2011-08-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110827200825/http://essex1841.com/SuffolkHundreds/Babergh_hundred.shtml |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 Edwardstone Hall was a country house located in the village. < ref > {{cite web |author=Edwardstone Hall |url=http://www.dicamillocompanion.com/Houses_detail.asp?ID=3024 |title=Database: House Details |publisher=The DiCamillo Companion |accessdate=2013-10-31 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It was demolished in 1952, < ref > {{cite book| last = Roberts| first = William Morys| title = Lost Country Houses of Suffolk| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=uUA3AQAAIAAJ| year = 2010| publisher = Boydell  &  Brewer Incorporated| isbn = 978-1-84383-523-3| page = 68 }} < /ref >  but there are several other buildings still located on its land, including two [[listed building]]s described below: the Church of St Mary the Virgin and Temple Bar. < ref name= " church " / > < ref name= " temple " > {{cite web|author=Good Stuff IT Services |url=http://www.britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/en-276446-temple-bar-edwardstone-suffolk |title=Temple Bar - Edwardstone - Suffolk - England |publisher=British Listed Buildings |accessdate=2013-10-31}} < /ref >

 The [[forest]] surrounding the village is called Edwardstone Woods. It is mainly broadleaved, mixed and yew woodland.
