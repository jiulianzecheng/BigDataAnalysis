 The term '''avocado''' refers to a type of [[berry]]. It has medium dark green or dark green bumpy or smooth skin depending on the variety. The flesh of an avocado is deep [[chartreuse green]] in color near the skin and pale [[Chartreuse (color)|chartreuse green]] near the core. It has a creamy, rich [[texture]]. The word, Avocado came from an [[aztec]] word meaning [[testicle]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Zajac|first=Stephanie|title='Avocado' actually comes from a word meaning 'testicle'|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/avocado-history-word-testicle-2018-6|access-date=2021-06-02|website=Business Insider|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Avocado trees come from [[Central America]] and [[Mexico]]. They can grow in many places, as long as it is not too cold. 
 
 Avocados have much more fat than most of the other fruits, but its fat is healthy to eat ([[monounsaturated fat]]). Avocados have lots of [[potassium]], B [[vitamin]]s, and [[vitamin E]] and [[Vitamin K|K]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Everything You Need to Know About Avocados|url=https://www.webmd.com/food-recipes/all-about-avocados|access-date=2021-05-14|website=WebMD|language=en}} < /ref >  The Mexican food called [[guacamole]] is made of avocados. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Myrick|first=Richard|date=2012-11-14|title=Guacamole Fun Facts {{!}} Mobile Cuisine|url=https://mobile-cuisine.com/did-you-know/guacamole-fun-facts/|access-date=2021-05-14|website=Mobile Cuisine {{!}} Food Truck, Pop Up  &  Street Food Coverage|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Many other foods are also made from avocado. Avocado is [[poison]]ous to some animals. Many animals will get very sick or die if they eat avocado. Avocado grows there where the climate is a little windy.
