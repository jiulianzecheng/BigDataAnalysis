 }}'''Reuters''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|r|ɔɪ|t|ər|z|audio=Reuters (spoken word).ogg}} {{respell|ROY|terz}}) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[news agency]] owned by [[Thomson Reuters Corporation]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Thomson Reuters|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Thomson-Reuters|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181107003736/https://www.britannica.com/topic/Thomson-Reuters|archive-date=7 November 2018|access-date=17 June 2022|website=Britannica}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=About us|url=https://agency.reuters.com/en/about-us.html|access-date=14 January 2019|website=Reuters}} < /ref >  It [[Employer|employs]] around 2,500 [[journalists]] and 600 [[Photojournalist|photojournalists]] in about 200 locations [[worldwide]]. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite web|title=Home - Reuters News - The Real World in Real Time|url=https://www.reutersagency.com/en/|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Reuters News Agency|language=en-US}} < /ref > < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite web|title=Careers|url=https://www.reuters.tv/careers|access-date=14 January 2019|website=www.reuters.tv|archive-date=2 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191202001145/http://www.reuters.tv/careers|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Reuters is one of the largest news agencies in the world. < ref name= " britannica-two " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/news-agency#ref203528|title=News agency|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=23 August 2002|access-date=18 February 2017}} < /ref > < ref name= " :5 " > {{cite web|last=Stephen Brook|date=30 May 2006|title=Reuters recruits 100 journalists|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2006/may/30/reuters.pressandpublishing|access-date=5 November 2021|work=[[The Guardian]]}} < /ref >

 The agency was [[wikt:establish|established]] in [[London]] in 1851 by the [[Germans|German]]-born [[Paul Reuter]]. It was bought by the [[Thomson Corporation]] of [[Canada]] in 2008. < ref name= " :5 "  / > 
 
 == History == 
 
 === 19th century === 
 [[Paul Reuter]] worked at a book-[[publishing]] firm in [[Berlin]]. He gave out radical [[pamphlets]] at the beginning of the [[Revolutions in 1848]]. In 1850, Reuter developed a news service in [[Aachen]] using [[homing pigeons]] and electric [[telegraphy]] from 1851 on. < ref name= " britannica-r " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/500259/Reuters|title=Reuters (news agency)|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=26 March 2010|access-date=3 November 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 Reuter moved to [[London]] in 1851 and established a [[News agency|news wire agency]] at the [[London Royal Exchange]]. Reuter's company covered topics like [[Commerce|commercial]] news, serving banks, brokerage houses, and [[business]] firms. < ref name= " britannica-r2 " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/500259/Reuters|title=Reuters (news agency)|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=26 March 2010|access-date=3 November 2012}} < /ref >  Reuter's company started becoming famous for reporting on important topics first many times. < ref name= " britannica-r3 " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/500259/Reuters|title=Reuters (news agency)|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=26 March 2010|access-date=3 November 2012}} < /ref >  For example, it was the first in [[Europe]] to report [[Abraham Lincoln]]'s [[Assassination of Abraham Lincoln|assassination]] in 1865. < ref name= " britannica-r4 " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/500259/Reuters|title=Reuters (news agency)|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=26 March 2010|access-date=3 November 2012}} < /ref > < ref name= " guardian " > {{cite news|last=Allen|first=Katie|date=4 May 2017|title=Reuters: a brief history|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2007/may/04/reuters.pressandpublishing|url-status=live|access-date=19 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171011004608/https://www.theguardian.com/media/2007/may/04/reuters.pressandpublishing|archive-date=11 October 2017}} < /ref >   
 
 In 1872, Reuter's expanded into the [[Far East]], followed by [[South America]] in 1874. < ref name= " guardian2 " > {{cite news|last=Allen|first=Katie|date=4 May 2017|title=Reuters: a brief history|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2007/may/04/reuters.pressandpublishing|url-status=live|access-date=19 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171011004608/https://www.theguardian.com/media/2007/may/04/reuters.pressandpublishing|archive-date=11 October 2017}} < /ref >  In 1878, Reuter [[Retirement|retired]] as [[Managing Director|managing director]]. He was replaced by his eldest son, [[Herbert de Reuter]]. < ref name= " funding-universe " > {{cite web|title=History of Reuters Group PLC|url=http://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/reuters-group-plc-history/|access-date=8 May 2019|website=Funding Universe}} < /ref >  In 1883, Reuter's began transmitting messages electrically to London [[Newspaper|newspapers]]. < ref name= " guardian2 "  / > 
 
 === 20th century === 
 Reuter's son Herbert de Reuter continued as general manager until his death by [[suicide]] in 1915. The company returned to private ownership in 1916 and the company was renamed to  " Reuters Limited " , removing the [[apostrophe]]. < ref name= " funding-universe2 " > {{cite web|title=History of Reuters Group PLC|url=http://www.fundinguniverse.com/company-histories/reuters-group-plc-history/|access-date=8 May 2019|website=Funding Universe}} < /ref >  In 1925, the [[Press Association]] (PA) of [[Great Britain]] became very interested in Reuters and some years later got full ownership. < ref name= " britannica-r5 " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/500259/Reuters|title=Reuters (news agency)|encyclopedia=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]|date=26 March 2010|access-date=3 November 2012}} < /ref >  In 1945, Reuters was the first broadcasting company to broadcast news of [[Heinrich Himmler]]'s attempts to [[negotiate]] with the western allies through [[Count Bernadotte]], a [[Swedish people|Swedish]] [[nobleman]].
