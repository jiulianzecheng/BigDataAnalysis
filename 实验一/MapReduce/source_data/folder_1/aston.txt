 '''Aston''' is an inner city area of [[Birmingham]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Aston was first mentioned in the [[Domesday Book]] in 1086. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Council|first=Birmingham City|title=Aston local history|url=https://www.birmingham.gov.uk/info/50170/local_history/1644/aston_local_history/2|access-date=2023-05-09|website=www.birmingham.gov.uk|language=en}} < /ref >  It was originally governed by its own council until it part of Birmingham in 1911. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Council|first=Birmingham City|title=Aston local history|url=https://www.birmingham.gov.uk/info/50170/local_history/1644/aston_local_history|access-date=2023-05-09|website=www.birmingham.gov.uk|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Old buildings such as the Barton Arms and Aston Hippodrome became well known.{{Citation needed|date=May 2023}}
