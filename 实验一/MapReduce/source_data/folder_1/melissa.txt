'''Melissa''' [ m''uh''-'''lis'''-''uh'' ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Melissa {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/melissa|access-date=2023-04-03|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a name given for [[female]]s. The name is [[Greek language|Greek]] and it means ''honey bee''. It also refers to the plant [[lemon balm]]. Melissa is an exquisite name that has enjoyed popularity for centuries. It has a fascinating history and profound significance. The name Melissa originates from the Greek language and is derived from the word  " melissa, "  which translates to honeybee. In Greek mythology, Melissa was a nymph who nurtured and looked after Zeus during his infancy. She nourished him with honey and concealed him from others. Zeus was immensely grateful to Melissa for her care and transformed her into a bee, hence the name Melissa became synonymous with bees and honey. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Melissa name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Melissa-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-03|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The name ''Melissa'' was not popular in the [[US]] until around the 1950s; it was popular between the years 1960 and 1991. During the year 2010, fewer than 2,500 girls in the USA were given the name. Famous celebrities with this name include [[Melissa Joan Hart]]. This name was ranked #372 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-04-03|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >
