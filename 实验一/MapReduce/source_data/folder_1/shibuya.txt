 {{nihongo|'''Shibuya'''|渋谷|Shibuya-ku}} is a [[special wards of Tokyo|special ward]] of [[Tokyo]], [[Japan]]. It is a very important area for [[commerce]] and [[business]]. It also has the two busiest [[railway station]]s in the world, [[Shinjuku Station]] and [[Shibuya Station]]. As of 2020, it has an [[population]] of about 231,043, and a [[population density]] of 15,290.7 people per km < sup > 2 < /sup >  (39,603/sq mi). The total area is 15.11 & nbsp;km < sup > 2 < /sup >  (5.83 sq mi). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.city.shibuya.tokyo.jp.e.mu.hp.transer.com/kusei/tokei/jinko/jumin.html|title=Resident registration population|date=10 April 2020|website=Shibuya City|access-date=12 April 2020|archive-date=15 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200815170653/http://www.city.shibuya.tokyo.jp.e.mu.hp.transer.com/kusei/tokei/jinko/jumin.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Shibuya is famous for its [[pedestrian scramble|scramble crossing]], which is called [[Shibuya Crossing]].
