 The '''Vaucluse''' ({{lang-oc|Vauclusa}}) is a [[departments of France|department]] in the southeast of [[France]] in the [[Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur]] [[Regions of France|region]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] is [[Avignon]]. 
 
 It is named after a famous [[Spring (water)|spring]], in the ''[[commune]]'' [[Fontaine-de-Vaucluse]]. The name Vaucluse comes from the [[Latin Language|Latin]] ''Vallis Clausa'' ( " closed valley " ) as the [[valley]] here ends in a [[cliff]] from which rises a spring. 
 
 ==History== 
 Vaucluse was not one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created on 12 August 1793 with  four [[district]]s: Apt, Orange, Ouvèze (Carpentras), Vaucluse (Avignon); its [[capital city|capital]] was Avignon. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/84his.html |title=Historique de Vaucluse |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=8 February 2017 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the districts were changed into ''arrondissements'': Avignon, Apt, Carpentras and Orange. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 After the [[Battle of Waterloo]] (18 June 1815), the department was occupied by the [[Austria]]n [[army]] from June 1815 to November 1818. 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Orange was eliminated and the subprefecture of Apt was moved to Cavaillon but went back to Apt in 1933. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Vaucluse is part of the [[Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur]] [[Regions of France|region]], in southeast France, with an area of {{Convert|3567.3|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. The department has an [[exclave]] to the north, the [[canton of Valréas]], surrounded completely by Drôme department. 
 
 The department borders with 3 regions and 6 other departments: < ref name=splaf / >

 ** [[Gard]] (west) 
 * [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] region 
 ** [[Drôme]] (north) 
 ** [[Ardèche]] (northwest) 
 * [[Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur]] region 
 ** [[Alpes-de-Haute-Provence]] (east) 
 ** [[Var (department)|Var]] (southeast) ( a short distance) 
 ** [[Bouches-du-Rhône]] (south)

 The are several [[Mountain chain|chains of mountains]] in the Vaucluse department, mainly in the eastern half; from northeast to southeast, they are: 
 # '''Dentelles de Montmirail''', a small chain of small mountains; the highest point is ''Crête de Saint-Amand'', {{Convert|730|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. 
 # '''Vaucluse Mountains''' ({{lang-fr|Monts de Vaucluse}}). The highest [[peak]] in the Vaucluse department is ''Signal de Saint-Pierre'', {{Convert|1256|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. 
 # '''Luberon Massif''', in the souhteast of the department; the highest point is ''Mourre Nègre'', {{Convert|1125|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. The chain is in the Regional Natural Park of Luberon ({{lang-fr|Parc naturel régional du Luberon}}). 
 
 The highest mountain in the department is ''[[Mont Ventoux]]'' ({{Coord|44|10|26|N|5|16|43|E|name=Mont Ventoux |display=inline |region:ID_type:mountain }}) that is {{Convert|1911|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} high. < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9913 |title=Mont Ventoux |publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=8 February 2017 |date= }} < /ref >  It stands alone to the north of the Luberon [[Mountain range|range]], separated by the Vaucluse Mountains, and just to the eastern base of the Dentelles de Montmirail. 
 
 There are several [[valley]]s and [[plain]] in the department. The main important plain is the plain of Comtat where is most of the population of the department. It is to the south and the west of the Mont Ventoux and has the [[Rhône]] [[river]] to the west, the [[Durance]] river to the south and the Vaucluse Mountains to the east. 
 
 The main [[river]]s are the [[Rhône]] and the [[Durance]], [[tributary]] of the Rhône. Other rivers are [[Tributary|tributaries]] of those two rivers. 
 
 === Climate === 
 The climate in [[Avignon]], at an [[altitude]] of {{Convert|56|m|ft|abbr=on }}, is of the subtype '''Csa''' ([[Mediterranean climate]]) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. 
 
 The [[average]] temperature for the year in Avignon is {{Convert|14.4|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|23.3|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|5|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Avignon is {{Convert|617.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is October with {{Convert|83.8|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is January with an average of {{Convert|22.9|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Avignon, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=164541 & cityname=Avignon%2C+Provence-Alpes-Cote+d%27Azur%2C+France & units=|language= |accessdate=8 February 2017 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Vaucluse is part of the region of [[Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur]]. The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Vaucluse'' in [[Avignon]].

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Vaucluse are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Vauclusiens'' (women: ''Vauclusiennes''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/vaucluse-84 |title=Vaucluse |publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=8 February 2017 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Vaucluse has a population, in 2014, of 554,374, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|554374|3567.3|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Avignon, with 296,303 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Carpentras and Apt, have respectively 129,623 and 128,448 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / > 
 
 The city with more people living in it is the [[Capital city|capital]], [[Avignon]] (92,209). The subprefectures of [[Carpentras]] and [[Apt, Vaucluse|Apt]] have, respectively, 28,447 and 11,885 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >

 == Economy == 
 Vaucluse has been always a [[Rural area|rural]] department where [[farming]] is the main [[Economy|economic]] activity. The most important [[produce]]s in the department are [[fruit]]s ([[melon]]s, [[Cherry|cherries]], [[strawberry]]s, [[apple]]s, [[grape]]s and others) and [[vegetables]] ([[tomato]]es). 
 
 Another important product of the region is [[wine]], mainly those wines known as ''Côtes-du-Rhône''.
