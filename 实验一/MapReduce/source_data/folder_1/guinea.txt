 '''Guinea''', or the '''Republic of Guinea''' ({{lang-fr|République de Guinée}}), is an independent nation in Western [[Africa]]. Guinea borders 6 countries: [[Guinea-Bissau]], [[Senegal]], [[Mali]], [[Ivory Coast]], [[Liberia]], [[Sierra Leone]]. The [[national language]] is [[French language|French]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Guinea {{!}} History, Map, Flag, Language, People,  &  Facts|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Guinea|access-date=2021-05-01|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}} < /ref >   < ref > {{Cite web|title=Guinea Maps  &  Facts|url=https://www.worldatlas.com/maps/guinea|access-date=2021-05-01|website=WorldAtlas|date=24 February 2021|language=en-US}} < /ref >  It is a member of the Economic Community of West African States. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Member States {{!}} Economic Community of West African States(ECOWAS)|url=https://www.ecowas.int/member-states/|access-date=2021-05-01|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 Before the Europeans arrived to Africa, Guinea was part of a couple of empires. In the 1890s, [[France]] colonized Guinea.  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Guinea — History and Culture|url=https://www.iexplore.com/articles/travel-guides/africa/guinea/history-and-culture|access-date=2021-05-02|website=www.iexplore.com}} < /ref >  In 1958, Guinea becomes independent. < ref > {{Cite news|date=2018-05-14|title=Guinea profile - Timeline|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-13443183|access-date=2021-05-02}} < /ref >

 == Religion == 
 About 89% of people are [[Islam|Muslim]], most of them are [[Sunni Islam|Sunni]]. 6.8% of people are [[Christian]], most of them are [[Catholicism|Catholic]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Guinea - People|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Guinea|access-date=2021-05-02|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}} < /ref >
