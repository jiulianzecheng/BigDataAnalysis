 The '''Canebrake tree frogs''' ('''Aplastodiscus''') are a group of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]]. They live in southeastern [[Brazil]] near the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web |url=https://www.iucnredlist.org/search?taxonomies=119293 & searchType=species |title=IUCN Red List of Threatened Species |access-date=May 29, 2022 |website=IUCN Red List}} < /ref >  One frog, [[Aplastodiscus perviridis|Aplastodiscus pervirdis]], also lives in [[Argentina]], and it might live in [[Paraguay]]. < ref > {{Cite web |title=Aplastodiscus perviridis |url=https://www.iucnredlist.org/species/55301/11285655 |access-date=May 29, 2022 |website=IUCN Red List}} < /ref > 
 
 At first, ''Aplastodiscus'' had two frog species in it.  In 2005, frog scientists looked at Hylidae again and decided that 12 more frogs should go into ''Aplastodiscus''.  They moved them into ''Aplastodiscus'' from ''[[Hyla]]''. < ref > {{Cite journal |last1=Faivovich |first1=Julián |last2=Haddad |first2=Celio F. B. |last3=Garcia |first3=Paulo C.  == Threats == 
 Currently, none of the species are [[Threatened species|in danger of dying out]]. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite journal |date=2008-01-01 |title=Arthroleptis nlonakoensis: IUCN SSC Amphibian Specialist Group |url=http://dx.doi.org/10.2305/iucn.uk.2008.rlts.t135994a4223700.en |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=IUCN Red List of Threatened Species|doi=10.2305/iucn.uk.2008.rlts.t135994a4223700.en }} < /ref >  But there are fewer of some frogs than others.  Human beings change the [[habitat|places where they live]] to build houses, farms, and towns. Pollution, climate change, and disease can also hurt these frogs.  Some [[invasive species]] can kill this frog. < ref > {{Cite book |last=N. |first=Stuart, S. |url=http://worldcat.org/oclc/474280211 |title=Threatened amphibians of the world |date=2008 |publisher=Lynx |isbn=978-84-96553-41-5 |oclc=474280211}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 "  / >  Many of the species are [[data deficient]]. That means that scientists do not know enough about them to know if they are in danger or not. < ref name= " :0 "  / >
