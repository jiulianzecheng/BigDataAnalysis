 '''Yuendumu''' is a town in the [[Northern Territory]] of [[Australia]]. < ref > [http://www.bushtel.nt.gov.au/northern_territory/community_search_display?comm_num=198 DLGH - Bushtel  & #124; Community Search Display] < /ref >  It is one of the larger [[wikt:remote|remote]] [[Community|communities]] in [[central Australia]]. It is 293 & nbsp;km northwest of [[Alice Springs]]. There are about 817 people living at Yuendumu; most are [[Warlpiri]] and [[Anmatyerre]] Aboriginal people. < ref > [http://www.abs.gov.au/AUSSTATS/abs@.nsf/Previousproducts/710405000Population/People12002-2006?opendocument & tabname=Summary & prodno=710405000 & issue=2002-2006 & num= & view= National Regional Profile : Yuendumu (CGC) (Statistical Local Area)] < /ref >  The town is within the Yuendumu Aboriginal Lands Trust area on traditional [[homeland]]s and includes many [[outstation]]s. < ref > [http://www.bushtel.nt.gov.au/northern_territory/community_search_display?comm_num=123 DLGH - Bushtel  & #124; Community Search Display] < /ref > < ref > [http://www.bushtel.nt.gov.au/northern_territory/community_search_display?comm_num=750 DLGH - Bushtel  & #124; Community Search Display] < /ref > 
 
 Yuendumu was established in 1946. In 1947, a [[Baptist]] [[Mission (Christianity)|mission]] was started there. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.bwa-baptist-heritage.org/bap-ab.htm |title=Baptists in Australia |access-date=2013-03-21 |archive-date=2012-03-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120321052757/http://www.bwa-baptist-heritage.org/bap-ab.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  By 1955, many Warlpiri families had settled in the town. In 2006, Yuendumu has three community stores, a school, < ref > [http://www.yuendumuschool.nt.edu.au Yuendumu CEC] < /ref >  airstrip, swimming pool, < ref > [http://www.crikey.com.au/2008/10/28/macklin-gets-out-the-scissors-as-yuendumu-pool-opens/ Macklin gets out the scissors as Yuendumu pool opens  & #124; Crikey] < /ref >  a large community of [[Aboriginal art|Aboriginal artists]], a media organisation, a church, an old people's centre, women's centre and [[safe house]].
