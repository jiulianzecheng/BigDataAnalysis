 '''Saltaire''' is a [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site]] in the [[City of Bradford]], [[West Yorkshire]], England. < ref name= " heritage " > UNESCO, [http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1028  " Saltaire " ]; retrieved 2012-4-21. < /ref >  The site is a complete and well-preserved industrial village of the second half of the 19th century. It has textile mills, public buildings and workers' housing. < ref name= " heritage " / > 
 
 == History == 
 Construction of Saltaire was started in 1851 by [[Titus Salt|Sir Titus Salt]]. The name of the village is a combination of the founder's last name and the name of the [[Aire River]] which runs through it. < ref name= " heritage " / > 
 
 Salt owned several textile mills [[Yorkshire]]. In 1853, he moved his businesses and his workers to Saltaire. < ref > Turner, Joseph Horsfall. (1888).  [https://books.google.com/books?id=8PQvAAAAMAAJ & dq=saltaire+yorkshire & pg=PA224  ''Yorkshire notes and queries,'' Vol. I, p. 224]. < /ref > 
 
 In time, the new village grew to over 800 dwellings. < ref name= " heritage " / > 
 
 In 1933, the village was sold the Bradford Property Trust. The mill closed down in 1986. < ref name= " heritage " / > 
 
 In 2001, Saltaire was named a World Heritage Site. < ref name= " heritage " / >
