 '''Dobruja''' or '''Dobrudja''' ({{IPAc-en|US|ˈ|d|oʊ|b|r|ʊ|dʒ|ə}}; < ref > {{cite web|title=Dobruja|url=https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/dobruja|access-date=21 July 2019|work=[[Collins English Dictionary]]|publisher=[[HarperCollins]]}} < /ref >  {{lang-bg|Добруджа|Dobrudzha}} or ''Dobrudža''; {{lang-ro|Dobrogea}}, {{IPA-ro|ˈdobrodʒe̯a|pron|Ro-Dobrogea.ogg}}  < small > or < /small >  {{IPA-ro|doˈbrodʒe̯a|}}; {{lang-tr|Dobruca}}) is a [[region]] between [[Bulgaria]] and [[Romania]]. It is be found between the [[Danube]] and the [[Black Sea]]. It is divided between [[Northern Dobruja]] in Romania and [[Southern Dobruja]] in Bulgaria.   
 
 The Romanian part of Dobruja, '''Dobrogea''', is made up of the counties of [[Constanţa County|Constanţa]] and [[Tulcea]]. The Bulgarian part of Dobruja, '''Dobrudzha''', is made up of the [[Provinces of Bulgaria|provinces]] of [[Dobrich Province|Dobrich]] and [[Silistra Province|Silistra]]. 
 
 Major [[City|cities]] in Dobruja include [[Constanța]], [[Tulcea]], [[Medgidia]] and [[Mangalia]] in the Romanian part and [[Dobrich]] and [[Silistra]] in the Bulgarian part. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Dobruja is a mainly [[Hill|hilly]] area except for the [[Marsh|marshy]] [[Danube Delta]]. It has an average [[altitude]] of about 200–300 & nbsp; [[Metre|metres]]. Its highest point is the Țuțuiatu (Greci) Peak in the [[Măcin Mountains]], with a height of 467 & nbsp;m. The Romanian part of Dobruja is covered mostly by the [[Dobruja Plateau]]. The Bulgarian part has the [[Ludogorie Plateau]]. [[Lake Razelm]] is one of the most important [[Lake|lakes]] in Northern Dobruja. Dobruja has a [[continental climate]]. 
 
 Dobruja is a [[Wind|windy]] region known for its [[Windmill|windmills]]. There is wind during about 85–90% of all days. It usually comes from the north or northeast. The average wind speed is about twice higher than the average in Bulgaria.
