 '''Dordogne''' ({{lang-oc|Dordonha}}) is a [[departments of France|department]] in the southwest of [[France]]. It is named after the [[Dordogne (river)|Dordogne river]] that runs through the department. 
 
 The department is in the [[Regions of France|region]] of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]]. It is between the valley of the [[Loire River|Loire]] river and the [[Pyrenees]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] ([[Capital city|capital]]) is [[Périgueux]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Dordogne was formed during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with parts of the old [[province]] of [[Périgord]]. It was divided into nine [[district]]s: Belvès, Bergerac, Excideuil, Montignac, Montpon, Nontron, Périgueux, Ribérac and Sarlat. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/24his.html |title=Historique de la Dordogne |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=28 March 2015 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 [[Bergerac, Dordogne|Bergerac]], [[Périgueux]] and [[Sarlat-la-Canéda]] were alternatively the [[Capital (city)|capital]] of the department but later it was decided that only Périgueux would be the capital. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Dordogne is part of the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] region. It is the third largest department of [[Metropolitan France]], after [[Gironde]] and [[Landes]]. It has an area of {{Convert|9060|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / >

 The main river in the department is the [[Dordogne (river)|Dordogne]] that gives its name to the department. It runs from east to west through the department. Other rivers are the [[Isle (river)|Isle]] and the [[Vézère]], both [[Tributary|tributaries]] of the Dordogne. 
 
 The highest point in the department is the southwestern [[summit]] of the ''Forêt de Vieillecour'' ({{Coord|45|34|41|N|1|1|55|E|name=Forêt de Vieillecour - Sommet Sud-ouest|display=inline|region:ID_type:mountain}}), in northwestern [[Massif Central]] near the border with the [[Haute-Vienne]] department, with an [[altitude]] of {{Convert|475|m|ft|0|abbr=on}}. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9692 |title=Forêt de Vieillecour - Sommet Sud-ouest, France|publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=8 March 2017 |date= }} < /ref >  The lowest point is in the valley of the Dordogne river and is {{Convert|16|m|ft|abbr=on}} high. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.dordogne-perigord-tourisme.fr/fr/presentation-de-la-dordogne-96.htm |title=Présentation de la Dordogne |work=Comité Départamental du Tourisme de la Dordogne |language=French |accessdate=28 March 2015 |date= |archive-date=24 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150324122054/http://www.dordogne-perigord-tourisme.fr/fr/presentation-de-la-dordogne-96.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Dordogne is part of the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]]. It is managed by the ''Departamental Council of Dordogne'' in [[Périgueux]]. 
 
 ===Administrative divisions=== 
 There are 4 [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] ([[district]]s), 25 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']] and 520 [[Communes in France|''communes'']] ([[Municipality|municipalities]]) in Dordogne. < ref name=insee1 > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/departement/DEP24-dordogne |title=Département de la Dordogne (24) |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=French |accessdate=8 March 2017 |date= }} < /ref >

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Dordogne are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Dordognais'' (women: ''Dordognaises''). < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_departement_dordogne_24.html |title=Dordogne (24)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=28 March 2015 |date= }} < /ref >  They are also known as ''Périgordins'' or ''Périgoudins''. 
 
 in 2014, the department of Dordogne had a population of 416,350, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|416350|9060|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Périgueux, with 189,301 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other three, Bergerac, Nontron and Sarlat-la-Canéda, have respectively 111,875, 40,508 and 74,666 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
