A '''namesake''' is a person named after another person. < ref name= " rhd " > {{cite web |title=Namesake |url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/namesake |website=Dictionary.com Unabridged |publisher=Random House |access-date=1 April 2019}} < /ref > < ref name= " mw " > {{cite web |url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/namesake |title=Namesake |work=Merriam-Webster Online Dictionary |access-date=1 April 2019}} < /ref > < ref name= " ahd " > {{cite web |title=Namesake |url=https://ahdictionary.com/word/search.html?q=namesake |work=American Heritage Dictionary |access-date=1 April 2019}} < /ref >  In general, a thing (such as a company, place, [[ship]], building, or idea) named after a person or thing that first had the name. < ref name= " mw "  / > < ref name= " collins " > {{cite web |title=Namesake |url=https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/namesake |website=Collins English Dictionary |publisher=HarperCollins |access-date=1 April 2019}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Family== 
 Naming a child after a relative, friend, or well-known person is common in the English-speaking world. When a son is named for his father, they add  " Jr. " / " II " ,  " III' " , or another [[suffix]] to the name of the son. 
 
 ==Concepts== 
 Buildings, such as the [[Trump Tower]] (named after [[Donald Trump]], and companies, like the [[Ford Motor Company]] (named after [[Henry Ford]], are often named for their founders or owners. A city may be named after someone. For example, [[Lawrence, Kansas]] is named after [[Amos Adams Lawrence]]. < ref name= " lawrenceci " > {{cite web|url=http://www.ci.lawrence.ks.us/about_us |title=About Us  & #124; City of Lawrence, KS |website=Ci.Lawrence.KS.us |date=November 21, 1996 |access-date=May 21, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120505033308/http://www.ci.lawrence.ks.us/about_us |archive-date=May 5, 2012 }} < /ref >
