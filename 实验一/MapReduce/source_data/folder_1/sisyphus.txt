 '''Sisyphus''' ({{lang|grc|Σίσυφος}}, ''Sísyphos'') was a legendary person in [[Greek mythology]]. < ref > [http://oed.com/view/Entry/180455?redirectedFrom=sisyphean#eid   " Sisyphean, " ] ''Oxford English Dictionary,'' 3rd ed., 2001; retrieved 2012-3-29. < /ref >   
 
 According to the ancient story, Sisyphus was forced to roll a big rock up a hill, but it rolled back down and the task was never finished. < ref > Littell, Robert S. (1849). [https://books.google.com/books?id=5A02Hobl7HIC & pg=PA53 & dq= ''The Living Age,''  Vol. 20, p. 53]. < /ref >   He was forced to repeat this over and over again forever. < ref > Mythweb.com, [http://www.mythweb.com/encyc/entries/sisyphus.html  " Sisyphus " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210329222436/http://www.mythweb.com/encyc/entries/sisyphus.html |date=2021-03-29 }}; retrieved 2012-3-29. < /ref > The word  " Sisyphean " , which relates to a task which is undertaken with great effort but is ultimately pointless, is derived from the myth.
