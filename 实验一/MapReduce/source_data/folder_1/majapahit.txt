 '''Majapahit''' ({{lang-jv|ꦩꦗꦥꦲꦶꦠ꧀}}; {{IPA-jv|madʒapaɪt}}), also known as '''Wilwatikta'''{{efn|group=note|Some Javanese court literatures uses this [[Old Javanese language|Old Javanese]] name, which bears the same meaning as  " Majapahit " , for example in {{lang|jv|[[Nagarakretagama]]}} canto 1 stanza 2 and {{lang|jv|Kidung Harsawijaya}}. It is sometimes also written backwards as Tiktawilwa, for example in {{lang|jv|Nagarakretagama}} canto 18 stanza 4. However it is still more widely known by its Javanese name, as recorded in the ''hikayat''s of Aceh, Banjar, Malay, Palembang, etc.}} ({{lang-jv|ꦮꦶꦭ꧀ꦮꦠꦶꦏ꧀ꦠ}}; {{IPA-jv|wɪlwatɪkta}}), was a [[Javanese people|Javanese]] [[Hinduism|Hindu]]-[[Buddhism|Buddhist]] [[Thalassocracy|thalassocratic]] [[empire]] in [[Southeast Asia]]. It was from the island of [[Java]] in modern-day [[Indonesia]]. It existed from 1293 to around 1527. It was strongest during the [[era]] of [[Hayam Wuruk]]. He took over many places in Southeast Asia during his [[reign]] from 1350 to 1389. According to the {{lang|jv|[[Nagarakretagama]]}} ({{lang|jv|Desawarñana}}) written in 1365, Majapahit was an empire that had land from [[Sumatra]] to [[New Guinea]].{{rp|page=87}} It had territory in present-day [[Indonesia]], [[Singapore]], [[Malaysia]], [[Brunei]], southern [[Thailand]], [[East Timor]], and southwestern [[Philippines]] (the [[Sulu Archipelago]]). 
 
 Majapahit was one of the last big [[Hinduism|Hindu]]-[[Buddhism|Buddhist]] empires of the region. It is said to be one of the greatest and most powerful empires in the history of Indonesia and Southeast Asia. Its [[influence]] went outside the modern territory of Indonesia and has been studied a lot.
