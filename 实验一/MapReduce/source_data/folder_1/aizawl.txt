'''Aizawl''' is the [[capital city]] of the [[Indian state|state]] of [[Mizoram]] in [[India]]. It has a population of 293,416 according to the census of 2011. < ref > {{cite web|title=Aizawl City Population|url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/185-aizawl.html|publisher=census2011.co.in|access-date=15 August 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131028025523/http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/185-aizawl.html|archive-date=28 October 2013|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A & state5=999|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040616075334/http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A & state5=999|archive-date=2004-06-16|title= Census of India 2001: Data from the 2001 Census, including cities, villages and towns  (Provisional)|access-date=2008-11-01|publisher= Census Commission of India}} < /ref >  It is the largest city in the state. The population of Aizawl is mostly ethnic [[Mizo people]]. The people are mostly [[Christianity|Christian]]. < ref > {{cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2010/aug/31/letter-from-india-mizoram-politics | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Paul | last=Grogan | title=India: economic growth contrasts with political unrest in a forgotten enclave | date=31 August 2010| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120606135405/http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2010/aug/31/letter-from-india-mizoram-politics | archive-date=6 June 2012 }} < /ref > 
 
 Aizawl is located in the northern part of Mizoram. It is 1,132 & nbsp;metres (3715 & nbsp;ft) above sea level. In the summer the temperatures range from {{convert|20|-|30|C}}, and in the winter {{convert|11|-|21|C}}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://mizoram.nic.in/more/aizawl.htm |title=Aizawl - the State Capital |publisher=Mizoram |access-date=9 November 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131007035702/http://mizoram.nic.in/more/aizawl.htm |archive-date=7 October 2013 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
