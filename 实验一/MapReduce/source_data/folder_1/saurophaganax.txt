 '''''Saurophaganax''''' ( " lizard-eating master " ) is a [[genus]] of [[allosaurid]] [[dinosaur]] from the [[Morrison Formation]] of [[Upper Jurassic]] in [[Oklahoma]]. It lived about 151 million years ago). < ref name=turner1999 > Turner C.E. and Peterson F. 1999. Biostratigraphy of dinosaurs in the Upper Jurassic Morrison Formation of the Western Interior, U.S.A. Pp. 77–114 in Gillette D.D. (ed) ''Vertebrate Paleontology in Utah''. Utah Geological Survey Miscellaneous Publication 99-1. < /ref >   
 
 ''Saurophaganax'' was a very large Morrison allosaurid.  The maximum size of ''S. maximus'' has been estimated at anywhere from {{convert|10.5|m|ft}} < ref name=G.S.Paul2010 > Paul G.S. 2010. ''The Princeton Field Guide to Dinosaurs''. Princeton University Press p. 112 < /ref >  to {{convert|13|m|ft|abbr=on}} in length, < ref name= " Holtz2008 " > Holtz, Thomas R. Jr. 2011. ''Dinosaurs: the most complete, up-to-date encyclopedia for dinosaur lovers of all ages,'' [http://www.geol.umd.edu/~tholtz/dinoappendix/HoltzappendixWinter2010.pdf Winter 2010 Appendix.] < /ref >  and around {{convert|3|t}} in weight. < ref name=G.S.Paul2010/ >  This means it was perhaps similar in size to ''[[Tyrannosaurus]]'' or ''[[Carcharodontosaurus]]''. 
 
 Some paleontologists think it is a [[species]] of ''[[Allosaurus]]'' (''Allosaurus maximus''). It has also been described as a separate [[genus]]. < ref name=DKS98 > {{cite journal |doi=10.1080/02724634.1998.10011039 |last=Smith, David K. 1998 |title=A morphometric analysis of ''Allosaurus'' |journal=Journal of Vertebrate Paleontology |year=1998 |volume=18 |issue=1 |pages=126–142}} < /ref >  The most recent review of basal [[tetanuran]]s accepted ''Saurophaganax'' as a distinct genus. < ref name= " HMC04 " > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/154697781|title=The dinosauria|date=2007|publisher=University of California Press|isbn=978-0-520-25408-4|edition=2nd|location=Berkeley, Calif.|oclc=154697781}} < /ref >  New possible ''Saurophaganax'' material from New Mexico may clear up the status of the genus.
