 '''Leptodactylidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[frog]]s. They are believed to have been on [[Earth]] since the [[Cenozoic]] era or at the end of the [[Mesozoic]]. < ref name=Heinicke > {{cite journal | author=Heinicke, M.P., W.E. Duellman  &  S.B. Hedges | year=2007 | title=Major Caribbean and Central American frog faunas originated by ancient oceanic dispersal |journal = [[PNAS|Proc. Nat. Acad. Sci.]] | volume=104 | issue=24 | pages=10092–7 | doi=10.1073/pnas.0611051104 | pmid=17548823 | pmc=1891260| doi-access=free }} < /ref >  There are 1,100 [[species]] in 50 [[genera]]. The ''[[Eleutherodactylus]]'' is the largest [[genus]] with over 700 different species. Leptodactylidae frogs lives in [[Mexico]], the [[Caribbean]], and [[Central America|Central]] and [[South America]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://amphibiaweb.org/lists/Leptodactylidae.shtml | title=AmphibiaWeb: Information on Amphibian Biology and Conservation | accessdate=2007-06-17 | publisher=University of California, Berkeley, CA}} < /ref >  The family includes [[terrestrial]], [[burrow]]ing, [[aquatic]], and [[wikt:arboreal|arboreal]] frogs. < ref name=EoR > {{cite book |editor=Cogger, H.G.  &  Zweifel, R.G.|author= Zweifel, Richard G.|year=1998|title=Encyclopedia of Reptiles and Amphibians|url=https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0|publisher= Academic Press|location=San Diego|pages= [https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0/page/89 89]–90|isbn= 0-12-178560-2}} < /ref > 
 
 Some of the frogs lay their eggs in [[foam|foam nest]]s. They will lay their eggs in [[crevices]], on the surface of [[water]], or on the [[forest]] floors. For [[tadpole]]s that are laid on the forest floors will remain there until they become frogs. They will not eat anything. In the genus ''Elutherodactylus'', the eggs will automatically become small frogs. They will not go through the tadpole stage. < ref name=EoR/ >  Leptodactylids can be found in [[fossil]] [[record]]s. One specimen, was found [[wikt:preserve|preserved]] and is thought to be 37 [[million]] years old. < ref name=EoR/ >
