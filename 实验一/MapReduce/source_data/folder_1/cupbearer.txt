 A '''cupbearer''' is [[tradition]]ally the person who fills the [[cup]]s and serves the [[wine]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/cupbearer |title=cupbearer |publisher=Merriam-Webster, Incorporated |access-date=January 27, 2017}} < /ref >  In a [[medieval]] [[court (royal)]], the cupbearer brought his [[lord]]'s [[ale]] or [[wine]] and tasted it to make sure it was not [[poison]]ed. < ref name=Adamson163 > Melitta Weiss Adamson, ''Food in Medieval Times'' (Westport, CT: Greenwood Press, 2004), p. 163 < /ref >  The cupbearer was often a person of high rank. It was a distinct honor to be a cupbearer to a king. < ref name=Fitzpatrick-McKinley186 > Anne Fitzpatrick-McKinley, ''Empire, Power and Indigenous Elites: A Case Study of the Nehemiah Memoir'' (Leiden: Koninklijke Brill NV, 2015), p. 186 < /ref >  In the [[Bible]], [[Nehemiah]] was cupbearer to the [[Artaxerxes I of Persia]]. < ref > Katherine Harris, ''Nelson's Foundational Bible Dictionary'' (Nashville, TN: Thomas Nelson, 2004), p. 511 < /ref >
