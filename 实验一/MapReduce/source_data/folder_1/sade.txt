 '''Sade''' (Helen Folasade Adu, [[OBE]], born 16 January 1959) is a [[British people|British]]-[[Nigeria]]n [[singer-songwriter]] who has many hit songs. Among them are ''No Ordinary Love'', ''The Sweetest Taboo'' and ''Paradise'', all of them getting high marks on [[United States]] music charts. 
 
 Sade was born  in Nigeria to an [[English people|English]] mother and Nigerian father. She moved to [[Essex]] at age four. In 2002, Adu was awarded an [[Officer of the Order of the British Empire|OBE]] for services to music, and stated her award was  " a great gesture to me and all black women in England " . < ref name= " Actress and singer collect OBEs " > {{cite news|title=Actress and singer collect OBEs |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/1934634.stm|accessdate=14 June 2012|work=BBC News|date=17 April 2002}} < /ref >
