'''Lopburi''' < ref name= " Lopburi Website " > [http://www.relaxzy.com/province/lopburi/ Lopburi Website] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081218110730/http://www.relaxzy.com/province/lopburi/ |date=2008-12-18 }}, Lopburi Website. < /ref >  is a [[province]] in the center of [[Thailand]]. It is good farming land. Lopburi has a long history. It was first called La-vo. Lopburi was settled during the [[Bronze Age]]. The Khmer expanded their power in the area during the [[10th century]] and ruled from the town of La-vo. 
 
 The administration of Lopburi is divided into 11 districts < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.tourismthailand.org/destination-guide/lopburi-16-1-1.html |title=Tourism Thailand : Destination Guide -  >  lopburi |access-date=2009-10-16 |publisher=Tourism Authority of Thailand |archive-date=2009-05-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090505194104/http://www.tourismthailand.org/destination-guide/lopburi-16-1-1.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  and 124 sub-districts. The tourist attractions are Narainivech Palace, Narai National Museum, Pralarn shrine (famous for the monkeys that come out of the jungle), Pasakchonlasit dam, Mahatad temple, Lopburi's zoo, [[India]]n pagoda's shrine, Lai temple, Vichayenn's house -it's the place where the royal government welcome the foreign envoys, and Vangkanleung's [[waterfall]].
