[[Image:Tampon with applicator.jpg|thumb|Menstrual tampon with applicator]] 
 A '''tampon''' is a mass made of absorbent material, usually [[cotton]], that are put into a body cavity to absorb body fluid. The most common type is put in a woman's [[vagina]] to absorb [[Menstruation|menstrual fluid]]. 
 
 Tampons were first used as medical devices in the 18th century.
