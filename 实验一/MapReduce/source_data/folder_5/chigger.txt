 A '''chigger''' is a small, [[parasitic]] [[mite]] which lives in tall [[grass]]. < ref > {{cite journal |author= Smith G.A. et al. 1998 |title=The summer penile syndrome: seasonal acute hypersensitivity reaction caused by chigger bites on the penis |journal=Pediatric Emergency Care |year=1998 |volume=14 |issue=2 |pages=116–118 |pmid=9583392 |doi=10.1097/00006565-199804000-00007|s2cid=37926004 }} < /ref > < ref > [http://www.merriam-webster.com/medical/chigger Medical Definition from Merriam-Webster, Chigger], 2;  " ...and causes intense irritation "  Retrieved March 7, 2014 < /ref >  It is a member of a larger [[family (biology)|family]] known as the '''Trombiculidae'''.  
 
 The [[species]] of Trombiculidae which bite in their [[larva]]l stage cause  " intense [[irritation]] " , < ref > [http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/chigger Merriam-Webster's Online Dictionary, chiggers] cause intense irritation < /ref >  or  " a [[wheal response|wheal]], usually with severe itching and [[dermatitis]] " . < ref > [http://medical-dictionary.thefreedictionary.com/chigger chigger: Medical dictionary:]  " produces a wheal, usually with severe itching and dermatitis " < /ref > < ref > [http://education.yahoo.com/reference/dictionary/entry/chigger Chigger:] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120926104621/http://education.yahoo.com/reference/dictionary/entry/chigger |date=2012-09-26 }} American Heritage Dictionary < /ref >   
 
 Their color can range from bright red to brown. Chiggers are almost too small to see with the naked eye, so checking grass for them is near impossible. Chigger bites appear 2-3 hours after a host is bitten, and can last for a few weeks. They bite in hot, moist places, such as the armpits, under the knees, and the [[genitals]]. 
 
 Trombiculidae live in [[forests]] and [[grasslands]] and in low, damp areas such as [[woodland]]s, [[shrub|berry bush]]es, [[orchard]]s, along lakes and streams. It does live in drier places, such as [[lawn]]s, [[golf course]]s, and [[park]]s. < ref > {{cite book |author= Ballantine, Todd |title=Tideland treasure: the naturalist's guide to the beaches and salt marshes of Hilton Head Island and the southeastern coast |url= https://archive.org/details/tidelandtreasure00ball |publisher= University of South Carolina Press |location=Columbia, South Carolina |year=1991 |pages=[https://archive.org/details/tidelandtreasure00ball/page/1 1]–4 |isbn=0-87249-795-X }} < /ref >  They are most numerous in early summer when grass, [[weed]]s, and other vegetation are heaviest.  
 
 In their larval stage they attach to various animals and feed on skin, often causing itching. < ref > {{cite book |last=Mandell, Gerald L; Bennett J.E.  &  Dolin R 2005 |title=Principles and practice of infectious diseases.|publisher= 6th ed, Elsevier Churchill Livingstone|location=Philadelphia|isbn=978-0-443-08686-1}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book|last=Goldman, Lee  &  Ausiello, Dennis Arthur 2007 |title=Cecil Medicine |publisher=Elsevier Health Sciences, p1032}} < /ref >
