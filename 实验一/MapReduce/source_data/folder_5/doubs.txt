 '''Doubs''' is a [[Departments of France|department]] in eastern [[France]]. It is in the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It is named after the [[Doubs (river)|Doubs]] [[river]]. 
 
 The largest city in the department, and its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] ([[Capital (city)|capital]]), is the city of [[Besançon]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Doubs is one of the original 83 departments created during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with Besançon as its capital. < ref name= " splaf " > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/25his.html |title=Historique du Doubs |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=28 June 2017 |date= }} < /ref >  It was formed from part of the old [[province]] of ''Franche-Comté''. 
 
 It was divided in six [[district]]s: Besançon, Baume-les-Dames, Saint-Hippolyte, Pontarlier, Quingey and Ornans. In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the six districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Besançon, Baume-les-Dames, Pontarlier and Saint-Hippolyte. In 1816, the [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of Saint-Hippolyte is moved to Montbéliard. < ref name= " splaf "  / > 
 
 After the [[Battle of Waterloo]] (18 June 1815), the department was occupied by the [[Austria]]n [[army]] from June 1815 to November 1818. 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Baume-les-Dames was eliminated. < ref name= " splaf "  / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Doubs is part of the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|5232.6|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 The department borders with 3 departments of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region ([[Jura (department)|Jura]], [[Haute-Saône]] and [[Territoire de Belfort]]) and 3 [[Cantons of Switzerland|cantons]] of [[Switzerland]] ([[Vaud]], [[Neuchâtel (canton)|Neuchâtel]] and [[Jura (canton)|Jura]]). 
 
 The Doubs department is partially on the [[Jura Mountains]], mainly the eastern part of the department on the border with Switzerland. The highest point in the department is [[Mont d'Or (Jura Mountains)|Mont d'Or]] ({{Coord|46|44|0|N|6|21|24|E|name=Mont d'Or|display=inline|region:FR_type:mountain}}), {{Convert|1461|m|ft|abbr=on }} high; < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9636 |title=Mont d'Or, France |publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=17 August 2017 |date= }} < /ref >  it is on the border between the ''communes'' [[Jougne]] and [[Longevilles-Mont-d'Or]] at about {{Convert|20|km|mi|abbr=on }} to the south of the city of [[Pontarlier]]. The lowest point is where the [[Ognon (Bourgogne-Franche-Comté)|Ognon]] leaves the department, {{Convert|200|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. 
 
 The main rivers in the department are the Doubs, that gives its name to the department, the Ognon and the Loue (tributary of the Doubs). 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at [[Besançon]] is an  " [[Oceanic climate]] "  (also known as Marine West Coast Climate) and of the subtype '''Cfb'''. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Besançon, France - Climate Summary |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=598813 & cityname=Besan%E7on%2C+Franche-Comte%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=28 June 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] temperature for the year in Besançon is {{Convert|11|°C|°F}}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|19.9|°C|°F}}. The coolest month, on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|2.3|°C|°F}}. 
 
 The average amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Besançon is {{Convert|934.7|mm|in|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is September with {{Convert|106.7|mm|in|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is February with an average of {{Convert|50.8|mm|in|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Doubs is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Doubs'' in [[Besançon]]. The department is part of the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] region.

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Doubs are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Doubistes'', or ''Doubiens'' (women: ''Doubiennes''). < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.habitants.fr/doubs-25 |title=Doubs (25) |publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=28 June 2017 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Doubs had a population, in 2014, of 534,710, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|534710|5232.6|km2|prec=1 |disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Besançon, with 248,199 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with more inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
