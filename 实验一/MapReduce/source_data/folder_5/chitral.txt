 '''Chitral''' ({{lang-ur|{{Nastaliq|چترال}}}}, {{lang-khw|{{Nastaliq|چھترار}}}}) is the [[Capital city|capital]] city of the [[Chitral District]] in [[Pakistan]]. It is on the western bank of the [[Chitral River]]. It has a population of 20,000. 
 
 == Languages == 
 Chitral is place of great language diversity.  The official language are [[Urdu]] and [[English language|English]], though [[Khowar]] is the most spoken language.  Most languages in Chitral are either [[Dardic languages]], [[Iranic languages]], or [[Nuristani languages]].  Around 24 languages are spoken in Chitral < ref > https://lowerchitral.kp.gov.pk/page/about < /ref >  including [[Central Asian Arabic]], [[Balti language|Balti]], [[Burushaski]], [[Dameli language|Dameli]], [[Domaaki language|Domaaki]], [[Dari|Dari Farsi]], [[Dangariwar dialect|Dangariwar]], [[Gawar-Bati]], [[Kalasha-mun]], [[Kalkoti language|Kalkoti]], [[Kamviri]], [[Indus Kohistani|Kohistani]], [[Khowar]], [[Kyrgyz language|Kyrgyz]], [[Madaklashti]], [[Munji language|Munji]], [[Palula language|Palula]], [[Shekhani dialect|Shekhani]], [[Shina language|Shina]], [[Torwali language|Torwali]], [[Turkmen language|Turkmen]], [[Ushoji language|Ushoji]], [[Uzbek language|Uzbek]], [[Waigali language|Waigali]], [[Wakhi language|Wakhi]], and [[Yidgha language|Yidgha]]. 
 
 Norwegian linguist [[Georg Morgenstierne]] wrote that Chitral is the area of the greatest linguistic diversity in the world. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BQz4tgEACAAJ | title=Notes on Phalura: An Unknown Dardic Language of Chitral | last1=Morgenstierne | first1=Georg | year=1941 }} < /ref >
