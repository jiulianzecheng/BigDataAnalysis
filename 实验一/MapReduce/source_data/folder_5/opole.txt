 '''Opole''' is the biggest city in [[Opole Voivodeship]] in the southwest part of [[Poland]]. The [[German language|German]] name of the city is '''Oppeln''', and the [[Czech language|Czech]] name is '''Opolí'''. The [[Oder River]] goes through the city. It has about 128,000 people living in it and is 149 [[km]]² in [[area]]. The mayor of the city is Arkadiusz Wiśniewski. 
 
 == History == 
 Opole is over 1000 years old.  It was originally a [[Slavic]] town. In 990 it became a Polish city. During the [[Middle Ages]] it became a [[Germany|German]] city. It was called Oppeln for a long time. After the [[Second World War]] the city once again became Polish, and the German citizens were forced to leave. It has been called by its Polish name Opole ever since.
