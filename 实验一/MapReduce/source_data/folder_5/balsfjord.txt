 {{coord|69|18|17|N|19|12|13|E|display=title}} '''Balsfjord''' is a [[municipality]] in [[Troms og Finnmark|Troms og Finnmark county]] in [[Norway]]. Its [[administrative centre]] is the village of [[Storsteinnes]] and it has a [[population]] of 5,576, with a [[population density]] of 3.9 [[Population|inhabitants]] per [[square kilometre]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=06913: Population 1 January and population changes during the calendar year (M) 1951 - 2023. Statbank Norway|url=https://www.ssb.no/en/system/|access-date=2023-03-30|website=SSB|language=en}} < /ref >  The [[municipality]] covers an [[area]] of {{convert|1497|km2|adj=off}} and is the 58th largest [[municipality]] in [[Norway]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=09280: Area of land and fresh water (km²) (M) 2007 - 2023. Statbank Norway|url=https://www.ssb.no/en/system/|access-date=2023-03-30|website=SSB|language=en}} < /ref >  The [[area]] is known for its two [[Fjord|fjords]], Malangen and Balsfjorden, and is surrounded by [[Fertility|fertile]] farmland and many peaks, including the southern end of the Lyngen Alps. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Lyngen Alps – a world-class nature paradise|url=https://www.visit-lyngenfjord.com/attractions/nature/lyngen-alps|access-date=2023-03-30|website=Visit Lyngenfjord|language=en}} < /ref >  The [[municipality]] was separated from the Tromsøe landdistrikt [[municipality]] in 1860 and has undergone several changes over the years, including a merger with neighboring Malangen [[municipality]] in 1964. The municipality's [[coat of arms]] features a red field with a [[Plow|plough]], symbolizing the importance of [[Farming|agriculture]] to the [[area]]. The local [[Population|inhabitants]] are descendants of a mixture of [[Norwegian language|Norwegian]], [[Sámi]], and [[Kven people|Kven]] people, with a history of trappers, people who hunt and trap wild animals for their [[fur]], [[meat]], or other parts, from Balsfjord being active in the [[Arctic]] from the [[18th century|18th]] to the [[20th century]]. Balsfjord also played a role in the mid-19th century mindekirken movement in the Troms region, which split from the state [[Church (building)|church parish]] in [[Tromsø]].
