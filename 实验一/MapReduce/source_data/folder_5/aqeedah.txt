'''Aqidah''' (Arabic: عقيدة‎, romanized: ʿaqīdah, plural عقائد ʿaqāʾid, also ʿaqīda, aqeeda etc.) is an [[Islamic]] term of [[Arabic language|Arabic]] origin. It means [[creed]] (Arabic pronunciation: [ʕɑˈqiːdæ, ʕɑˈqɑːʔɪd]). 
 
 Different Islamic [[theologian]]s have different views on aqidah. The term has a significant technical usage in Islamic theology: it means those matters over which Muslims hold conviction. It is a branch of Islamic studies describing the beliefs of Islam.
