 '''Vicenza''' is a ''[[comune]]'' and city in the [[Veneto]] [[Regions of Italy|region]], in [[Northeast Italy]]. Vicenza is the [[Capital city|capital]] of the [[Province of Vicenza|province of the same name]]. The city is about {{Convert|60|km|mi|abbr=on }} west of [[Venice]], the capital of the region, and {{Convert|200|km|mi|abbr=on }} east of [[Milan]]. 
 
 Vicenza is a [[Multiculturalism|cosmopolitan]] city, with a rich [[history]] and [[culture]], and many [[museum]]s, [[art gallery|art galleries]], [[Town square|piazza]]s, [[villa]]s, [[church (building)|church]]es and elegant [[Renaissance]] ''[[Palace|palazzi]]''. With the  " Palladian Villas of the Veneto "  in the surrounding area, and his renowned ''Teatro Olimpico'' (Olympic Theater), Vicenza has been listed as an [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site]] since 1994. 
 
 The city is also known as the  " City of Palladio "  after [[Andrea Palladio]], an [[architect]] that designed and built several important buildings in Vicenza and other cities. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Vicenza is at the confluence of the ''Bacchiglione'' [[river]] with its [[tributary]] ''Retrone''.The ''comune'' of Vicenza has an area of {{Convert|80.57|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} to the north of the ''Berici [[Hill]]s''. Its [[average]] [[altitude]] is {{Convert|39|m|ft|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " area " > {{cite web|url=http://www.tuttitalia.it/veneto/29-vicenza/ |title=Comune di Vicenza |publisher=Tuttitalia.it |language=Italian |accessdate=20 May 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 The southern part of the city is flat with an [[elevation]] of {{Convert|26|m|ft|abbr=on }} [[above sea level]]. The [[History|historical]] centre is among the [[meander]]s of the Bacchiglione river and of its [[Tributary|tributaries]] with an elevation of {{Convert|33|-|40|m|ft|abbr=on }} above sea level. To the south there is also an area with hills belonging to the Berici Hills with a top elevation of {{Convert|183|m|ft|abbr=on }} above sea level 
 
 Vicenza borders with the following ''[[Comune|comuni]]''ː [[Altavilla Vicentina]], [[Arcugnano]], [[Bolzano Vicentino]], [[Caldogno]], [[Costabissara]], [[Creazzo]], [[Dueville]], [[Longare]], [[Monteviale]], [[Monticello Conte Otto]], [[Quinto Vicentino]] and [[Torri di Quartesolo]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.tuttitalia.it/veneto/29-vicenza/94-comuni-limitrofi/ |title=Comuni limitrofi a Vicenza |publisher=Tuttitalia.it |language=Italian |accessdate=21 May 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 ===''Frazioni''=== 
 The following ''[[Frazione|frazioni]]'' ([[Hamlet (place)|hamlet]]s) are part of Vicenza: Anconetta, Bertesina, Bertesinella, Bugano, Campedello, Casale, Debba, Longara, Maddalene, Ospedaletto, Polegge, San Pietro Intrigogna, Santa Croce Bigolina, Tormeno. 
 
 === Climate === 
 The climate of Vicenza is an [[oceanic climate]] with template summers, '''Cfb''' (Marine West Coast Climate) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Vicenza, Italy - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=49061 & cityname=Vicenza%2C+Veneto%2C+Italy & units= |language= |accessdate=21 May 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Vicenza is {{Convert|12.8|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|23.1|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|2.2|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Vicenza is {{Convert|1087.1|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is November with {{Convert|109.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is July with an average of {{Convert|73.7|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}.
