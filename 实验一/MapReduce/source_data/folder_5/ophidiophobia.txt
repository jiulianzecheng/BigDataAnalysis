 '''Ophidiophobia''' or '''ophiophobia''' is the abnormal [[fear]] of [[snake]]s. It is sometimes called herpetophobia, which is a fear of [[reptile]]s or [[amphibian]]s. The word comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] words  " ophis "  ({{lang|grc|ὄφις}}) meaning snake, and  " phobia "  ({{lang|grc|φοβία}}) meaning fear. < ref > {{Cite news|url=http://phobiafor.com/2016/08/fear-of-snakes-phobia-ophidiophobia.html/|title=Fear of Snakes Phobia – Ophidiophobia|date=2016-08-22|newspaper=FearOf|language=en-US|access-date=2016-12-02|archive-date=2016-11-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161128075459/https://phobiafor.com/2016/08/fear-of-snakes-phobia-ophidiophobia.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 This is a [[phobia]], so it does not include people who do not like snakes or fear them for their [[Venom (poison)|venom]]. A person with ophidiophobia (called an ophidiophobe) not only fears them when in live contact, but is scared to think about them or even see them in video or still pictures. < ref > Murrie, Matthew  &  Steven. ''[https://books.google.com/books?id=IZKPhA3RmeIC & pg=PA11 & dq The First Book of Seconds]{{Dead link|date=September 2021|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}}''. [[F+W Media]], 2010. p.11. < /ref > 
 
 About a third of adult humans are ophidiophobic, making this the most common reported phobia. < ref > Lynne Isbell,  " The Fruit, the Tree, and the Serpent - Why We See So Well "  (Harvard University Press, 2009) < /ref >  Scientists think that [[mammals]] may have an [[innate]] fear of snakes. This was vital for their survival as it allowed such dangerous threats to be identified immediately. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2001/10/1004_snakefears.html|title=Fear of Snakes, Spiders Rooted in Evolution, Study Finds|website=news.nationalgeographic.com}} < /ref >
