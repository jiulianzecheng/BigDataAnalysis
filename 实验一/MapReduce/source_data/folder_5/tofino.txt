 '''Tofino''' is a [[district]] in the [[Canada|Canadian]] [[Provinces and territories of Canada|province]] of [[British Columbia]]. Tofino is found on the West Coast of [[Vancouver Island]]. It is at the western [[terminus]] of [[British Columbia Highway 4|Highway 4]], it is on the tip of the Esowista Peninsula, and is at the southern edge of [[Clayoquot Sound]]. The population of Tofino was 1,876 in 2011. < ref name=bcstats > {{cite web|url=http://www.bcstats.gov.bc.ca/StatisticsBySubject/Census/2011Census/PopulationHousing/MunicipalitiesByRegionalDistrict.aspx|accessdate=2014-06-18|title=2011 Census Total Population Results: Municipalities by Regional District|publisher=BCStats|archive-date=2014-06-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140617161230/http://www.bcstats.gov.bc.ca/StatisticsBySubject/Census/2011Census/PopulationHousing/MunicipalitiesByRegionalDistrict.aspx|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The mayor of Tofino is Josie Osborne. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://tofino.ca/content/contact-us|accessdate=2013-03-21|title=Contact Us: Mayor and Council|publisher=Tofino|archive-date=2013-03-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130303005211/http://www.tofino.ca/content/contact-us|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Tofino is a popular spot for [[Surfing|surfers]], [[Camping|campers]], [[Fishing|fishermen]], nature lovers, and [[whale]] watchers during the Summer. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.vancouverisland.com/recreation/?id=173|accessdate=2014-06-18|title=Surfing - Tofino and Jordan River, Vancouver Island, B.C.|publisher=Vancouver Island}} < /ref >  Tofino is named in association with the Tofino Inlet. In 1792, the Tofino Inlet was named by the Spanish commanders [[Dionisio Alcalá Galiano|Galiano]] and [[Cayetano Valdés y Flores|Valdés]] to honour Admiral Vicente Tofiño de San Miguel y Wanderiales. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://apps.gov.bc.ca/pub/bcgnws/names/20921.html|accessdate=2014-06-18|title=Tofino Inlet|publisher=GeoBC}} < /ref >
