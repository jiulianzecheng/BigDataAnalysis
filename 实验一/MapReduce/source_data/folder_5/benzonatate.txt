 '''Benzonatate''' (sold with other names, such as '''Tessalon''') is a medicine used to help with [[Cough|coughing]] and [[hiccups]]. < ref name= " AHFS2019 " > {{cite web|title=Benzonatate Monograph for Professionals|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/benzonatate.html|access-date=23 March 2019|website=Drugs.com|publisher=American Society of Health-System Pharmacists}} < /ref > < ref name= " Bec2010 " > {{cite journal|last1=Becker|first1=DE|date=2010|title=Nausea, vomiting, and hiccups: a review of mechanisms and treatment.|journal=Anesthesia Progress|volume=57|issue=4|pages=150–6; quiz 157|doi=10.2344/0003-3006-57.4.150|pmc=3006663|pmid=21174569}} < /ref >  It is taken by mouth. People under ten years old should not use it. < ref name= " ML2018 " > {{cite journal|date=17 December 2018|title=Drugs for cough.|journal=The Medical Letter on Drugs and Therapeutics|volume=60|issue=1562|pages=206–208|pmid=30625123}} < /ref >  It starts working after around twenty minutes, and can last eight hours. < ref name= " Tessalon label " > {{cite web|date=20 November 2019|title=Tessalon - benzonatate capsule|url=https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=8edd07a0-5f5b-9000-bafb-dbf8ff7609d4|access-date=21 April 2020|website=DailyMed}} < /ref > 
 
 It can cause [[Adverse effect (medicine)|side effects]] such as being tired or [[Dizziness|dizzy]], having stomach aches, or having [[Hallucination|hallucinations]]. It can also cause [[Allergy|allergic reactions]]. Having too much benzonatate can cause [[Seizure|seizures]], heartbeats [[Arrhythmia|that are not normal]], or even death. Chewing benzonatate capsules instead of just [[swallowing]] them can cause [[Bronchospasm|spasms]] in the [[Lung|lungs]] and [[Shock (circulatory)|shock]]. < ref name= " AHFS2019 "  / >  It is not known if people who are [[Pregnancy|pregnant]] or [[breastfeeding]] can use the drug. < ref name= " Preg2020 " > {{cite web|date=10 October 2019|title=Benzonatate Use During Pregnancy|url=https://www.drugs.com/pregnancy/benzonatate.html|access-date=20 February 2020|website=Drugs.com}} < /ref >  It works by numbing some nerves in the lungs. < ref name= " AHFS2019 "  / > 
 
 Benzonatate was allowed to be used as a medicine in the United States in 1958. < ref name= " AHFS2019 "  / >  In 2020, there were over 3 million [[Prescription|prescriptions]] for the drug. < ref name= " Clin2020b " > {{cite web|title=Benzonatate - Drug Usage Statistics|url=https://clincalc.com/DrugStats/Drugs/Benzonatate|access-date=7 October 2022|website=ClinCalc}} < /ref >
