 '''Mestrius Plutarchus''' ({{lang-el|Πλούταρχος}}; ''c.'' AD 46 – after AD 119), better known in English as '''Plutarch''', was a [[Ancient Greece|Greek]] [[historian]], and writer of [[biographer|biographies]] and [[essayist|essays]]. 
 
 Plutarch was born into a well-known family in ''Chaeronea'', ''[[Boeotia]]'' [[Greece]], a town about twenty miles east of [[Delphi, Greece|Delphi]]. His works are ''[[Parallel Lives]]'' and ''[[Moralia]]''.
