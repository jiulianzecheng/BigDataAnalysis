 '''Oakum''' is a type of [[rope]] made of [[Tar|tarred]] [[fibre]]. It is normally used to fill gaps. < ref name= " EB1911 " > {{cite EB1911|wstitle=Oakum|volume=19|page=935}} < /ref >  The main [[Custom|traditional]] use of oakum was in [[shipbuilding]]. It was used for [[caulking]], It was used to fill areas between [[Lumber|timbers]] in wooden [[Ship|vessels]] and the [[Deck (ship)|deck]] planking of [[iron]] and [[steel]] ships. < ref name= " EB1911 "  / >   Oakum was also used in [[plumbing]] to seal  connections in [[cast iron pipe]] and in [[Log cabin|log cabins]] for [[chinking]]. In ship caulking, it was put into the seams using a [[hammer]] and a caulking iron, then sealed into place with hot [[Pitch (resin)|pitch]]. < ref name= " Kemp1979 " > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8VmnPwAACAAJ|title=The Oxford Companion to Ships  &  the Sea|last=Kemp|first=Peter|publisher=Oxford University Press|year=1979|isbn=978-0-586-08308-6|page=807}} < /ref >
