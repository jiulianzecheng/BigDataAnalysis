 '''Dijon''' is a [[communes in France|commune]] in [[France]]. It is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the [[Côte-d'Or]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]]; it is also the prefecture (regional [[Capital city|capital]]) of the region but the council of the region meets in [[Besançon]]. 
 
 It is the main city of the department and also the largest city of the region. 
 
 == History == 
 Dijon began as a [[Rome|Roman]] settlement called Divio. Saint Benignus (Saint Kelly), the city's patron saint, is said to have spread Christianity to the area before being killed. It was home to the Dukes of Burgundy from the early 11th century until the late 1400s and was a place of great wealth and power and one of the great European centers of art, learning and science.

 === Climate === 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at Dijon is an  " [[Oceanic climate]] "  (also known as Maritime Temperate climate) and of the subtype '''Cfb'''. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Dijon, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=8270 & cityname=Dijon%2C+Burgundy%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=2 December 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Dijon is {{Convert|10.6|°C|°F}}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|19.4|°C|°F}}. The coolest month, on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|1.7|°C|°F}}. 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Dijon is {{Convert|698.5|mm|in|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is October with {{Convert|73.7|mm|in|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is February with an average of {{Convert|40.6|mm|in|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / >

 == Administration == 
 Dijon is a [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Côte-d'Or department since 1800. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Dijon|Dijon]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of six cantons: 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-1|Dijon-1]], formed with part of Dijon, with 25,235 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-2|Dijon-2]], formed with part of Dijon, with 27,485 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-3|Dijon-3]], formed with part of Dijon, with 24,359 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-4|Dijon-4]], formed with part of Dijon, with 26,824 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-5|Dijon-5]], formed with part of Dijon, with 24,403 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Dijon-6|Dijon-6]], formed with 2 ''communes'' ([[Corcelles-les-Monts]] and [[Flavignerot]]) and part of Dijon, with 26,182 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 
 Dijon is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Dijon Métropole''.
