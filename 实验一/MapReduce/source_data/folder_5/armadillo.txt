 A '''armadillo''' is in the '''Cingulata''', an [[Order (biology)|order]] of [[New World]] [[placenta]]l [[mammal]]s. The living ones have a [[leather]]y [[armor]]ed shell. The word ''armadillo'' is [[Spanish language|Spanish]] for  " little armored one " . 
 
 There are about 10 [[Extant taxon|living]] [[genera]] and about 20 [[species]] of armadillo. Their average length is about 75 [[centimeter]]s (30 in), including the tail. The [[giant armadillo]] grows up to 100 cm (39 in) and weigh 30 kg (66lbs). All species live in the [[Americas]]. Most of the 20 species are found in open areas, such as [[grassland]]s, but some also live in [[forest]]s. < ref >

 == Embryology == 
 The nine-banded armadillo has an unusual [[reproduction|reproductive]] system, in which [[genetics|genetically]] identical [[Multiple birth|quadruplets]] are born in each litter. < ref name= " ufl " > {{cite web|url=http://edis.ifas.ufl.edu/UW082 |title=The Nine-banded Armadillo (Dasypus novemcinctus) |publisher=Edis.ifas.ufl.edu |access-date=2009-12-17}} < /ref > < ref name= " valdosta " > {{citation |date=May–June 1998 |author=Loughry, W.J; Prodohl, Paulo A; McDonough, Colleen M;  &  Avise, John C |title=Polyembryony in Armadillos |journal=American Scientist |volume=86 |issue=3 |pages=274–279 |doi=10.1511/1998.3.274 |bibcode=1998AmSci..86..274L |s2cid=196608283 |url=http://www.valdosta.edu/~jloughry/Reprints/AmSci.pdf |access-date=2012-02-21 |archive-date=2012-02-13 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120213082811/http://www.valdosta.edu/~jloughry/Reprints/AmSci.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " Hamlett1933 " > {{citation |date=September 1933 |author=Hamlett, G.W.D |title=Polyembryony in the Armadillo: genetic or physiological? |journal=The Quarterly Review of Biology |volume=8 |issue=3 |pages=348–358 |doi=10.1086/394444 |jstor=2808431|s2cid=86435985 }} < /ref > 
 
 Because they are always [[genetics|genetically]] identical [[clone]]s, the group of four young is a good subject for scientific, behavioural or medical tests. They provide the same biological and genetic makeup, and then get different treatment. This is the only regular case of '[[polyembryony]]' in the class [[Mammalia]], where one fertilised egg splits into four embryos. It only happens in the genus '''''Dasypus''''', and not in all armadillos. Other animals which have this reproductive method are [[parasitoid]] [[wasp]]s, certain [[flatworm]]s and various aquatic [[invertebrate]]s. < ref name= " valdosta "  / >
