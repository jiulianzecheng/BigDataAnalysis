 '''Balranald''' is a town in [[New South Wales]], [[Australia]]. It is built on the banks of the [[Murrumbidgee River]]. In 2006 there were 1216 people living in the town and another 1500 live in the [[Shire of Balranald]]. < ref name= " balranald " > {{cite web | url=http://www.balranald.com/about.htm | title=About Balranald | publisher=Shire of Balranald | access-date=December 20, 2012}} < /ref >  The town is the centre of the Shire of Balranald, and is the largest town in the district. The main industry in the area is farming: grains, sheep, cattle and grapes. < ref name= " balranald " / >  It is named after Balranald in the [[Outer Hebrides]] of [[Scotland]]. < ref name= " balranald " / > 
 
 ==History== 
 Captain [[Charles Sturt]] was the first European [[explorer]] to visit the Balranald area. This was in January 1803 as he was going down the Murrumbidgee River in a boat. < ref name= " balranald " / >  In 1836, the explorer Major [[Thomas Mitchell]] camped on the site of Balranald during his trip to explore the south of Australia. < ref name= " balranald " / >  The first person to begin farming was [[Benjamin Boyd]], a [[squatter]], in 1842. < ref name= " balranald " / >  George Hobler settled in what became the town area in 1845. < ref name= " balranald " / >  The town itself began when the police set up a camp at a spot where the river was easy to cross in 1848. They were there to support the Commissioner for Crown Lands, George James McDonald, who named the area after the town in Scotland where he was born. < ref name= " balranald " / > 
 
 A shop and hotel opened in 1848 and the town became an official settlement in 1851. < ref name= " balranald " / >  The first 35 blocks of land were sold by [[wikt:auction|auction]] on 14 January 1845. < ref name= " balranald " / >  In 1858 the first steamboat arrived and Balranald became an important inland port. < ref name= " balranald " / >  The explorers [[Burke and Wills]] camped outside the hotel in 1860 on their journey north to the [[Gulf of Carpentaria]]. < ref name= " balranald " / > 
 
 ==Environment== 
 Baranald is on the flood plains of the Murrumbidgee. These plains have many swampy areas which have redgum and blackbox forests. It is also a [[habitat]] for the [[Southern Bell Frog]], or [[Growling Grass Frog]], which is on the NSW list of [[endangered species]]. < ref name= " sbf " > {{cite web | url=http://www.murrayriver.com.au/balranald/balranald-southern-bell-frog/ | title=Southern Bell Frogs around Balranald are Endangered Species | work=Discover Murray: Australia's greatest river | date=2012 | access-date=December 21, 2012}} < /ref >
