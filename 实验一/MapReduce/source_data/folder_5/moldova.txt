 '''Moldova''' is a country in [[Eastern Europe]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.oxforddictionaries.com/search/?direct=1 & multi=1 & dictCode=english & q=Moldova|title=Moldova|work=Oxford Dictionaries|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|accessdate=30 January 2016|archive-date=27 November 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161127041406/https://www.oxforddictionaries.com/search/?direct=1 & multi=1 & dictCode=english & q=Moldova|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/{{urlencode:Moldova}}|title=Moldova|work=Dictionary.com Unabridged|publisher=[[Random House]]|accessdate=30 January 2016}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > {{refn|The Free Dictionary: [http://www.thefreedictionary.com/Moldova Moldova]}} Its full name is the '''Republic of Moldova''' ({{lang-ro|Republica Moldova}}, {{Audio|Ro-Moldova.ogg|listen}} < !-- {{IPA-ro|reˈpublika molˈdova|}} -- > .) It used to be called '''Moldavia.''' It borders [[Ukraine]] to the east and [[Romania]] to the west. 
 
 The official language in Moldova is the [[Romanian language]]. The [[capital (city)|capital]] of Moldova is [[Chișinău]]. 
 
 The [[Russian Empire]] took almost half of the [[Moldavia|Principality of Moldova]] in 1812 and called it [[Bessarabia]]. Between 1918{{ndash}}1940 and 1941{{ndash}}1944 it was part of the [[Kingdom of Romania]]. It became independent from the [[Soviet Union]] after the latter country's [[Soviet Union#Dissolution|dissolution]].
