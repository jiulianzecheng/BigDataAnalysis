 '''Falaise''' is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in the [[Calvados]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Basse-Normandie]] [[Regions of France|region]] in northwestern France.{{efn|A commune in France is similar to a [[:wikt:village|village]] or town elsewhere. A French department is similar to a [[:wikt:county|county]]. A [[:wikt:region|region]] is an area.}}  
 
 ==Geography== 
 Falaise is located on the river Ante near [[Caen]] in France. The Ante river flows into the larger Dives river. Falaise is in the [[Normandy]] region of France.   
 
 ==History== 
 ===Medieval Normandy=== 
 [[Image:Chateau-falaise-calvados.jpg|right|thumb|Castle of William the Conqueror]] 
 [[Image:Falaise.jpg|right|thumb|{{center|Liberation of Falaise, 1944}}]] 
 Faliase is where [[William I of England|William the Conqueror]] was born. < ref > David C. Douglas, ''William The Conqueror'' (Berkeley; Los Angeles: University of California Press, 1964), p. 15 < /ref >   William became [[Duke of Normandy]] when he was just a boy. < ref > David Crouch, ''The Normans; The History of a Dynasty'' (London; New York: Hambledon Continuum, 2007), p. 59 < /ref >  He went on to become the first Norman [[Kings|king of England]] in 1066. < ref > François Neveux, ''The Normans; The Conquests that Changed the Face of Europe'', trans: Howard Curtis (London: Constable  &  Robinson, 2008), pp. 36-38 < /ref >  Falaise was where many of the dukes of Normandy resided before William. It is where the Treaty of Falaise was signed in December 1174. It was a [[treaty]] between William I of Scotland, [[King of Scots]], and the  King of England [[Henry II of England|Henry II]] Plantagenet. 
 
 The town is also the place where Rabbi Yom Tov of Falaise, grandchild of [[Rashi]] held his court. 
 
 ===In World War II=== 
 In modern times it is known for the battle of the  " Falaise Pocket "  during [[Operation Overlord]] in August 1944. Two German armies were [[:wikt:surrounded|surrounded]] by the [[Allies of World War II|Allied]] armies. About 10,000 German soldiers were killed. Over 50,000 were taken prisoner. 
 
 Much of Falaise was destroyed by Allied [[:wikt:bombs|bombs]]. It was then captured by [[Canada|Canadian]] and [[Poland|Polish]] solders. Falaise was restored after the war.
