'''Martha''' [ '''mahr'''-th''uh'' ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Martha {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/martha|access-date=2023-04-07|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a female [[given name]]. Martha is an ancient name with a fascinating background and significance. Its origins can be traced back to the Aramaic language, where it is derived from the word  " marta, "  which means  " lady "  or  " mistress. "  In the Bible, Martha is recognized as the sister of Lazarus and Mary, and she is celebrated for her hospitality and dedication to serving Jesus. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Martha name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Martha-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-07|website=babys.name}} < /ref >   The name has been around through many centuries. Martha of [[Bethany]] is a very notable person who has the name. She is described in the [[Gospel of John]] and the [[Gospel of Luke]]. There are other people with this female name, including [[Martha Stewart]], [[Martha McSally]] and [[Martha Coakley]]. The name was used for an [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] [[tropical cyclone|hurricane]] in 1969. This name was ranked #728 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-04-07|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >
