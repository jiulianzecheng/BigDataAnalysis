An''' excise''' or '''excise tax''' is an [[indirect tax]] charged on certain [[goods]]. < ref name=Investopedia > {{cite web |url=http://www.investopedia.com/terms/e/excisetax.asp |title=Excise Tax |publisher=Investopedia, LLC |access-date=6 March 2016}} < /ref >  As an indirect tax, they are charged by the producer or the merchant who then has to pay the [[government]]. < ref name=Investopedia/ >  For the same reason they are also not considered a [[sales tax]]. Excise taxes are commonly part of the [[retail]] price of a [[product (business)|product]] such as [[gasoline]], [[Alcoholic drink|alcohol]] or [[cigarette]]s. < ref name=InvAnsw > {{cite web |url=http://www.investinganswers.com/financial-dictionary/tax-center/excise-tax-1495 |title=Excise Tax |publisher=InvestingAnswers, Inc |access-date=6 March 2016 }}{{Dead link|date=October 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  Excise taxes may be charged by both [[US states|state]] and [[Federal government of the United States|federal governments]]. < ref name=InvAnsw/ > 
 
 == Excise vs sales tax == 
 Excise taxes are different from sales tax in two important ways. < ref name=AccTx > {{cite web |url=https://www.accuratetax.com/blog/what-is-excise-tax-and-how-does-it-differ-from-sales-tax/ |title=What is Excise Tax and How Does it Differ from Sales Tax? |publisher=AccurateTax.com |access-date=6 March 2016}} < /ref >  First, excise is charged only on specific goods. < ref name=AccTx/ >  Sales tax can apply to most things a person might buy although certain [[jurisdiction]]s may exempt food or clothing. Excise taxes are usually applied to [[wikt:luxury|luxury]] goods or products linked to [[health]] issues such as [[wikt:tan|tan]]ning salons or cigarettes. < ref name=AccTx/ >  Secondly, sales tax is charged as a [[percentage]] of the purchase price. < ref name=AccTx/ >  An excise tax is usually a fixed amount added to the price. < ref name=AccTx/ >  It can be hard to tell how much excise tax a person pays because it is part of the purchase price. < ref name=AccTx/ >  Sales tax is added to the price and is usually shown on the [[receipt]]. 
 
 == US excise taxes == 
 In the [[United States]], excise taxes make up about 4 percent of all federal revenues. < ref name=AccTx/ >  This is lower than many other countries. < ref name=AccTx/ >  The [[Constitution of the United States|Constitution]] refers to excise tax as an indirect tax. < ref name=EW > {{cite web |url=http://www.economywatch.com/tax/united-states/excise.html |title=Excise in the United States (USA) |date=13 October 2010 |publisher=Economywatch.com |access-date=6 March 2016 |archive-date=24 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424004625/http://www.economywatch.com/tax/united-states/excise.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Some states in the United States have different names for excise taxes. For example, in the state of [[Massachusetts]] the excise tax charged on [[automobile]]s is called an  " ad valorem tax " . < ref name=EW/ >
