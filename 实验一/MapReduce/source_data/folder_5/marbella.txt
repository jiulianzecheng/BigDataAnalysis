 '''Marbella''' is a city and municipality in southern [[Spain]], belonging to the [[province of Málaga]] in the autonomous community of [[Andalusia]]. It is part of the [[Costa del Sol]] and is the headquarters of the Association of Municipalities of the region; it is also the head of the judicial district that bears its name. 
 
 ==Background== 
 Marbella is near the [[Mediterranean Sea]], between [[Málaga]] and the [[Strait of Gibraltar]], in the foothills of the [[Sierra Blanca (Andalusia)|Sierra Blanca]]. The municipality covers an area of {{convert|117|km²|0|abbr=out}} crossed by highways on the coast, which are its main entrances. 
 
 In 2012 the population of the city was 140,473 inhabitants, < ref name= " La población andaluza " > {{cite book|title=La población andaluza|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=muCvMwEACAAJ|access-date=28 January 2013|year=2011|publisher=Instituto de Estadística y Cartografía de Andalucía}} < /ref >  making it the second most populous city in the [[province of Málaga]] and the eighth in Andalusia. It is one of the most important tourist cities of the Costa del Sol and throughout most of the year is an international tourist attraction, due mainly to its climate and tourist infrastructure.

 ==Climate== 
 Marbella is protected on its northern side by the coastal mountains of the Cordillera Penibética and so enjoys a climate with an average annual temperature above {{convert|18|°C|0|abbr=on}}. The highest peaks of the mountains are occasionally covered with snow, which usually melts in a day or two. Average rainfall is 628 l/m², while hours of sunshine average 2,900 annually. < ref > {{cite web|title=Marbella Weather|url=http://www.worldweatheronline.com/Marbella-weather-averages/Andalucia/ES.aspx|publisher=World Weather Online|access-date=29 January 2013}} < /ref >
