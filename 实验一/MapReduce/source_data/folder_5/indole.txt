 '''Indole''' (a blending of the terms ''[[indigo]]'' and ''[[oleum]]'') is an [[organic compound]]. The chemical can be found in many places in nature. It can be produced by [[bacteria]]. At [[room temperature]], indole is a [[solid]]. 
 
 Indole is found in some [[vegetable]]s. They include [[broccoli]], [[cauliflower]] and [[cabbage]]. < ref > {{cite document|title=Indole as Defined|work=The National Cancer Institutes|url=https://www.cancer.gov/publications/dictionaries/cancer-terms/def/indole|access-date=December 26, 2021}} < /ref >  It is also in human [[feces]]. It has a strong fecal [[odor]]. In low concentration indole has a flowery smell. Indole is in [[perfume]]s. < ref > {{cite web|title=Olefactory Perception with Humans|publisher=The National Centers for Biotechnology Information|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK11032|access-date=December 26, 2021}} < /ref >
