{{nihongo|'''Eiroku'''|永禄}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,| ''nengō'',| lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Kōji (Muromachi period)|Kōji]]'' and before ''[[Genki]].''  This period started in February 1558 and ended in April 1570. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA172    " ''Eiroku'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 172. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Ogimachi of Japan|Ōgimachi]]''-tennō''|正親町天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA739  " Ōgimachi Tennō, " ] p. 739; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA382  ''Annales des empereurs du Japon'',  p. 382-388.] < /ref > 
 
 == Events of the ''Eiroku'' era == 
 [[File:Gifujyou11.jpg|thumb|right|Gifu Castle was captured by Oda Nobunaga in the 7th year of  ''Eiroku'']] 
 * '''1560''' (''Eiroku 3, 1st month''): Ōgimachi was formally established as emperor. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA383  p. 383]; Varley, p. 44; a distinct act of ''senso'' is unrecognized prior to [[Emperor Tenji]]; and all sovereigns except [[Empress Jitō|Jitō]],  [[Emperor Yōzei|Yōzei]], [[Emperor Go-Toba|Go-Toba]], and [[Emperor Fushimi|Fushimi]] have ''senso'' and ''sokui'' in the same year until the reign of [[Emperor Go-Murakami]]. Compare ''Kunaichō'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-about/seido/seido06-ph.html  Ceremony of Accession (''Sokui-no-Rei'')]; retrieved 2012-6-29. < /ref > 
 
 * '''12 June 1560''' (''Eiroku 3, 19th day of the 5th month''):  at the [[Battle of Okehazama]], the forces of [[Imagawa Yoshimoto]] were defeated by [[Oda Nobunaga]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA745   " ''Okehazama Tatakai'' " ] at p. 745. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1564''' (''Eiroku 7''): Nobunaga took control of {{nihongo|Inabayama Castle|稲葉山城|Inabayama-jō}}, also known as [[Gifu Castle]]. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA385  p. 385.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1567''' (''Eiroku 10''): [[Todai-ji]] burned in battle led by [[Matsunaga Hisahide]]. < ref > Naracity Tourist Association, [http://narashikanko.jp/en/isan/index.html  World Heritage] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120112030949/http://narashikanko.jp/en/isan/index.html |date=2012-01-12 }}; retrieved 2011-12-8. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1568''' (''Eiroku 11''): [[Oda Nobunaga]] gives land in [[Heian-kyō]] for building a Christian church < ref > Stead, Alfred. (1906). [https://books.google.com/books?id=C58CAAAAMAAJ & dq=eiroku-ji & pg=PA93  ''Great Japan: a Study of National Efficiency,'' pp. 93-94.] < /ref >   which was first called [[Eiroku-ji]] < ref > Röpke, Ian Martin. (1999). [https://books.google.com/books?id=VNPUUisf5ykC & dq=eiroku-ji & pg=PA204   ''Historical dictionary of Osaka and Kyoto,'' p. 204.] < /ref >  then called [[Namban-ji]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA172    " ''Namban-ji'' " ] in at p. 694. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1568''' (''Eiroku 11, 2nd month''): [[Ashikaga Yoshihide]] became shogun. < ref name= " titsingh386 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA386    p. 386.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1568''' (''Eiroku 11, 9th month''): Shogun Yoshihide died from disease. < ref name= " titsingh386 " / > 
 
 * '''1569''' (''Eiroku 12''): First Christian church opened in Nagasaki. < ref > Catholic Bishops Conference of Japan, [http://www.cbcj.catholic.jp/eng/ehistory/table01.htm   " An Overview of the History of the History of the Catholic Church in Japan " ]; retrieved 2012-4-27. < /ref >
