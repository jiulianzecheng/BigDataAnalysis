 '''Glassdoor''' is a website where employees review their current or previous company. While reviewing their identity is not disclosed to other people by Glassdoor. The website was launched in 2008. < ref name= " At Glassdoor, Find Out How Much People Really Make At Google, Microsoft, Yahoo, And Everywhere Else. – TechCrunch " > Erik Schonfeld, ''[[TechCrunch]]'', June 10, 2008, [https://techcrunch.com/2008/06/10/at-glassdoor-find-out-how-much-people-really-make-at-google-microsoft-yahoo-and-everywhere-else/ At Glassdoor, Find Out How Much People Really Make At Google, Microsoft, Yahoo, And Everywhere Else.] < !--versions of article found at Internet Archive from 2008 through 2014-- > < /ref > 
 
 In 2018, the company was bought by the Japanese firm, [[Recruit (company)|Recruit Holdings]], for US$1.2 billion. < ref > {{Cite news|url=https://www.cnet.com/news/glassdoor-to-be-acquired-for-1-2b-by-japanese-hr-company/#ftag=CAD590a51e|title=Glassdoor to be acquired for $1.2B by Japanese HR company|last=Musil|first=Steven|date=May 8, 2018|work=[[CNET]]|access-date=May 8, 2018}} < /ref >  The company is headquartered in [[Mill Valley, California|Mill Valley]], [[California]], with more offices in cities around the world, including [[Chicago]], [[Dublin]], [[London]] and [[São Paulo]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Glassdoor was founded in 2007 by Tim Besse and Robert Hohman. < ref name= " Gage " > {{cite news|author=Deborah Gage|title=Google Capital Leads $70 Million Round for Job Review Site Glassdoor|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2015/01/06/glassdoor-raises-70-million-with-a-valuation-near-1-billion/|access-date=26 January 2021|work=WSJ|date=6 January 2015 }} < /ref >   
 
 Glassdoor launched its site in June 2008 < ref name= " At Glassdoor, Find Out How Much People Really Make At Google, Microsoft, Yahoo, And Everywhere Else. – TechCrunch "  / >  with rankings of companies and information about salaries in different firms. The service also allows to upload photos of offices and other  potentially interesting information about the company to help employees make a decision. < ref name= " Dishman " > {{cite web|date=July 17, 2015|title=What Glassdoor Has Learned From Seven Years Of Studying Other Companies|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3048590/lessons-learned/what-glassdoor-has-learned-from-seven-years-of-studying-other-companies|access-date=January 26, 2021|work=Fast Company}} < /ref >   
 
 In 2014, the company invited Adam Spiegel to become its [[Chief financial officer|CFO]] to help the company to prepare for an [[Initial public offering|IPO]]. < ref name= " Gage2 " > {{cite news|author=Deborah Gage|title=Google Capital Leads $70 Million Round for Job Review Site Glassdoor|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2015/01/06/glassdoor-raises-70-million-with-a-valuation-near-1-billion/|access-date=January 26, 2021|work=WSJ|date=6 January 2015 }} < /ref >
