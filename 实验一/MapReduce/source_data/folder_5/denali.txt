 '''Denali''', also formerly known as '''Mount McKinley''', is the highest [[mountain]] peak in both the [[United States]] and [[North America]]. It is exactly 20,310 [[Foot (unit of length)|feet]] (6,190.5 [[metres|m]]) high. < ref > [http://erg.usgs.gov/isb/pubs/booklets/elvadist/elvadist.html#Highest Elevations and Distances in the United States] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081006105354/http://egsc.usgs.gov/isb/pubs/booklets/elvadist/elvadist.html#Highest |date=2008-10-06 }} Retrieved on [[July 25]], [[2007]] < /ref >  It is in the highest part of the [[Alaska Range]]. Denali means  " the high one "  in the Athabaskan language. 
 
 == Location == 
 Denali is in the middle of the Alaska Range. It is about 130 [[mile]]s (209 [[km]]) [[north]]-[[northwest]] of [[Anchorage, Alaska|Anchorage]] and 155 miles (249 & nbsp;km) southwest of [[Fairbanks, Alaska|Fairbanks]]. The summit is about 35 miles (56 & nbsp;km) from the nearest major road, the George Parks Highway.
