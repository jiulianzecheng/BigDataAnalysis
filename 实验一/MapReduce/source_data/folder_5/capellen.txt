 '''Capellen''' ({{lang-lb|Capellen}}, {{lang-de|Capellen}}) is a town in the [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] of [[Mamer]], in south-western [[Luxembourg]], {{Convert|12|km|mi|abbr=on }} west of [[Luxembourg City]]. 
 
 Despite not having a ''commune'' named after it, Capellen gives its name to the [[Capellen (canton)|canton of Capellen]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Capellen lies on the old [[Roman roads|Roman road]], today's ''Kiemwee'', from [[Trier]] in [[Germany]] to [[Reims]] and [[Paris]]. 
 
 When Luxembourg was divided into cantons in 1840, as a result of disputes between [[Mamer]] and [[Koerich]], it was decided that the canton should be named Capellen and that Capellen should become the [[Capital city|capital]] of the canton.
