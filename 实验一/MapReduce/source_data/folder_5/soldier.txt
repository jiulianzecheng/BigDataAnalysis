 A '''soldier''' is a person who is a part of an [[army]]. A soldier can be [[Conscription|forced to join the army]] or [[Volunteering|volunteer]] to join. < ref name= " mw " > {{cite dictionary|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/conscription|title=Conscription|dictionary=Merriam-Webster Online}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Names and types== 
 There are many different types of soldiers: [[Trooper (rank)|trooper]]s, [[tank]]ers, [[commando]]s, [[dragoon]]s, [[infantryman|infantrymen]], [[United States Space Force|guardian]]s, [[artilleryman|artillerymen]], [[paratrooper]]s, [[grenadier]]s, [[Ranger (disambiguation)#Military|ranger]]s, [[sniper]]s, [[Combat Engineer|engineer]]s, [[sapper]]s, [[Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers|craftsmen]], [[signaller]]s, [[Combat medic|medic]]s, and [[Gunner (rank)|gunner]]s.{{Citation needed|date=September 2022}} 
 
 In many countries there are soldiers that have special names for other reasons. For example, [[military police]] personnel in the [[British Army]] are called  " red caps "  because their [[Cap|caps]] are red. < ref > {{Cite book|url=http://archive.org/details/ospreyredcapsbri00chap|title=Redcaps : Britain's provost troops and military police|last=Chappell|first=Mike|date=1997|publisher=London : Osprey|others=Library Genesis|isbn=978-1-85532-670-5}} < /ref >
