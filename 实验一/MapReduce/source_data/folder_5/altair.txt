'''Altair''', also called '''α & nbsp;Aquilae''', '''α & nbsp;Aql''', is the brightest [[star]] in the [[constellation]] of [[Aquila (constellation)|Aquila]]. 
 
 It is the [[list of brightest stars|twelfth brightest star]] in the night sky. It is in the [[G-cloud]]—a fog of gas and dust known as an [[interstellar cloud]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://interstellar.jpl.nasa.gov/interstellar/probe/introduction/neighborhood.html|title=Our Local Galactic Neighborhood|last=|first=|website=|publisher=[[NASA]]|access-date=|archive-date=2013-11-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131121061128/http://interstellar.jpl.nasa.gov/interstellar/probe/introduction/neighborhood.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite news|url=http://www.centauri-dreams.org/?p=14203|title=Into the Interstellar Void|last=Gilster|first=Paul|date=2010-09-01|work=Centauri Dreams|access-date=2017-03-26|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Altair is an [[stellar classification|A-type]] [[main sequence star]] with an apparent [[visual magnitude]] of 0.77. It is one of the vertices of the  [[Summer Triangle]] (the other two vertices are [[Deneb]] and [[Vega]]). < ref name=darlingsummer > [http://www.daviddarling.info/encyclopedia/S/Summer_Triangle.html Summer Triangle], entry, ''The Internet Encyclopedia of Science'', David Darling.
