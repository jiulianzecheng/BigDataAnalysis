 A '''dewlap''' is a flap of [[skin]] that hangs beneath the [[Neck (body part)|neck]] of many [[Animal|animals]].  
 It can also be used to mean other things such as a [[double chin]] on a [[human|person]] or the [[vocal sac]] of a [[frog]]. The term dewlap can also be used to mean a [[Caruncle (bird anatomy)|''caruncle'']], defined as  " a small, fleshy excrescence that is a normal part of an animal's anatomy " . < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.lexic.us/definition-of/caruncle|title=Definition of caruncle|author=Lexic.us|access-date=April 20, 2013}} < /ref >  At times the appearance of a dewlap indicates a stage in an animal's development. For [[example]], the appearance of a dewlap on a female [[rabbit]] indicates she is of [[Reproduction|breeding]] age. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://animals.mom.me/purpose-dewlap-rabbit-6264.html |title=What Is The Purpose of A Dewlap On A Rabbit? |author=Jen Davis |publisher=WHALEROCK DIGITAL MEDIA, LLC |access-date=1 February 2016}} < /ref >
