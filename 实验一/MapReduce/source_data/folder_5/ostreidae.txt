 '''Ostreidae''' are the '''true oysters'''. The family includes most [[species]] eaten under the name ''[[oyster]]''. [[Pearl oyster]]s are not true oysters because they belong to a different order, the [[Pterioida]]. 
 
 Like [[scallop]]s, true oysters have a central [[adductor muscle]]. This causes a [[scar]] in the middle of the [[Animal shell|shell]]. It is the point of attachment. The shell is often irregular because oysters attach to [[substrate]]s.
