 The [[phylum]] '''''Verrucomicrobia''''' is made up of six groups of [[gram-negative]], spherical or [[Bacillus (shape)|rod-shaped]] bacteria that consume other plants or animals for energy and nutrients ([[heterotrophic]]). They are distributed throughout freshwater, marine, and soil habitats, as well as vertebrae digestive tracts including those of humans. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Bergmann|first1=Gaddy T.|last2=Bates|first2=Scott T.|last3=Eilers|first3=Kathryn G.|last4=Lauber|first4=Christian L.|last5=Caporaso|first5=J.  == Planctobacteria Superphylum == 
 Members of the ''Verrucomicrobia'' phylum (group) are a part of the Planctobacteria (PVC) superphylum, along with its “[[Sister group|sister groups]]”; ''[[Chlamydiae]]'' and ''Lentisphaerae.'' < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Cho|first1=Jang-Cheon|last2=Vergin|first2=Kevin L.|last3=Morris|first3=Robert M.|last4=Giovannoni|first4=Stephen J.|date=June 2004|title=Lentisphaera araneosa gen. nov., sp. nov, a transparent exopolymer producing marine bacterium, and the description of a novel bacterial phylum, Lentisphaerae|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1462-2920.2004.00614.x|journal=Environmental Microbiology|language=en|volume=6|issue=6|pages=611–621|doi=10.1111/j.1462-2920.2004.00614.x|pmid=15142250 |issn=1462-2912}} < /ref >  Verrucomicrobia is distinguished from these other groups by a unique genetic change.
