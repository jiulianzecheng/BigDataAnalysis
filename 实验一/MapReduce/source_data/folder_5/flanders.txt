 '''Flanders''' ({{lang-nl|Vlaanderen}}, {{lang-fr|Anvers}}, {{lang-de|Flandern}}, {{lang-li|Vlaondere}}, {{lang-vls|Vloandern}}) or '''Flemish Region''' (Dutch: ''Vlaams Gewest''), is the [[Dutch language|Dutch]]-speaking northern part of [[Belgium]]. < ref > ({{lang-nl|Vlaanderen}} {{IPA-nl|ˈvlaːndərə(n)||Nl-Vlaanderen.ogg}}, {{lang-fr|Flandre}} {{IPA-fr|flɑ̃dʁ|}}, {{lang-de|Flandern}}, {{IPA-de|flɑndɛɹn|}} < /ref > 
 
 Their version of the Dutch language is usually called '''''Flemish''''' (Dutch: ''Vlaams''). < ref > Actually, Flemish is only the dialect of the provinces of West and East Flanders. < /ref >  Its [[vocabulary]] and [[pronunciation]] is not identical with Dutch as spoken in [[Holland]]. 
 
 Flanders has five provinces and 300 municipalities. About seven million people live there. Its capital is [[Brussels]], which is also the capital of Belgium.. 
 
 There are some other differences from the rest of Belgium. related to [[culture]], [[politics]] and [[history]].
