 '''Kulithalai''' is a town in [[Karur district]] in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[Tamil Nadu]]. The recorded history of Kulithalai is known from medieval Chola period of the 9th century and has been ruled, at different times, by the Medieval Cholas, Later Cholas, Later Pandyas, [[Vijayanagar Empire]] and the British Raj. [[Karunanidhi|M. Karunanidhi]], five time Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu and leader of [[Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam]] won his first legislative elections from Kulithatlai constituency in 1962. < ref > {{cite web|title=Brand Karunanidhi turns 84 Sunday|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-1281143691.html|location=Chennai|publisher=Hindustan Times|date=2 June 2007|access-date=28 April 2015|archive-date=17 November 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181117071202/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-1281143691.html|url-status=dead|via=HighBeam}} < /ref >   
 
 The town is administered by the Kulithalai municipality, which covers an area of {{convert|11.16|sqkm|sqmi|abbr=on}}. As of 2011, the town had a population of 27,910.  < ref name= " dashboard " > {{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}} < /ref >   The town is a part of the fertile [[Cauvery Delta|Cauvery delta region]] and agriculture is the major occupation. Roadways are the major mode of transportation to Kulithalai and the town also has rail connectivity.
