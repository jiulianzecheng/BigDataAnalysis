 In [[Hinduism|Hindu mythology]], '''Hiranyakashipu''' was a powerful [[Asura|demon]] king who ruled the [[Daitya|daityas]]. He was very angry when his brother [[Hiranyaksha]] was killed by [[Vishnu]] in his [[Varaha]] [[avatar]]. So he decided to become invincible by performing a [[penance]] to [[Brahma]]. < ref > Nurturing Child and Adolescent Spirituality: Perspectives from the World's Religious Traditions, p. 20, Karen-Marie Yust, Aostre N. Johnson, Sandy Eisenberg Sasso, Rowman  &  Littlefield < /ref >  Brahma granted Hiranyakashipu a boon that he could not be killed by any man, animal, or weapon. He also could not be killed inside or outside, during the day or night. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0IE8DwAAQBAJ & pg=PA185|title=The Body of God: An Emperor's Palace for Krishna in Eighth-Century Kanchipuram|last=Hudson|first=D. Dennis|date=2008-09-25|publisher=Oxford University Press, USA|isbn=978-0-19-536922-9|pages=185|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Hiranyakashipu became very arrogant and started to terrorize the three worlds (Trailokya). < ref name= " :0 "  / >  He forced everyone to worship him as the supreme god.Vishnu, who is the preserver of the universe, took pity on the people. He appeared in his [[Narasimha]] avatar, which is a half-man, half-lion form. Narasimha killed Hiranyakashipu by tearing him apart with his bare hands. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/indiathroughages00mada|title=India through the ages|last=Gopal|first=Madan|publisher=Publication Division, Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, Government of India|year=1990|editor=K.S.
