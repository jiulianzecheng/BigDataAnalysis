 '''Grevenmacher''' ({{lang-lb|Gréiwemaacher}}) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] with [[List of cities in Luxembourg|city status]] in eastern [[Luxembourg]], near the border with [[Germany]]. 
 
 Grevenmacher is the [[capital city]] of the [[Grevenmacher (canton)|Grevenmacher canton]] and of the [[Grevenmacher (district)|district of Grevenmacher]]. The city is on the left bank of the [[Moselle]] river. 
 
 ==Name== 
 The name Grevenmacher is formed by two words:  " Greven "  and  " Macher " .  " Macher "  comes from the [[Latin language|Latin]] word ''maceria'' ( " very old walls " );  " Greven "  comes from the old [[German language|German]] word ''Grafen'' (modern German: ''Graf'') that means  " [[count]], [[earl]] " . The town  " Macher of the Count "  was, since 1346, different from other  " Machern "  found in the region: Königsmacher, Kleinmacher, Rodemacher, Mecher, Mézières etc. The present name of Grevenmacher has been in use since 1769. < ref name= " commune " > {{cite web |url=http://www.grevenmacher.lu/la-commune-se-presente |title=Histoire |language=French |publisher=Ville de Grevenmacher |accessdate=3 May 2014 |date= |archive-date=11 September 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140911040110/http://www.grevenmacher.lu/la-commune-se-presente |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 The valley of the [[Moselle]], which owes its wealth to the fact that it is close to the water and it has [[Fertility|fertile]] land, was settled long before the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] [[:wikt:era|era]]. As for Grevenmacher, the first town was behind the Holy Cross Chapel at the place called  " Buerggruef " , not far from the Roman road to the city of Luxembourg. 
 
 A few centuries later, the Counts of Luxembourg chose a safe place to protect their land against the powerful [[archbishop]]s of [[Trier]]. They found it in Grevenmacher, that was the place where the road going from the city of Luxembourg to Trier meet with the one going from Trier to [[Thionville]]. Here a strong [[fortress]] was built, probably on the orders of Count Henry V. In 1252, this same count made Grevenmacher a city. < ref name= " commune "  / >

 ==Sights== 
 Famous sights in Grevenmacher are, for exampleː 
 *  " Huelen Zant " , remains from an old [[castle]]; 
 * [[Chapel]]  " Saint-Croix "  (this is French and means  " holy cross " ); 
 * The  " Kelsbach " , a beautiful piece of nature; 
 * The  " Jardin des papillons "  (this is French again and means  " garden of butterflies " ), a building which contains a lot of [[Butterfly|butterflies]] and other [[insect]]s; 
 * The  " Kulturhuef "  which is a [[museum]] and a [[cinema]]. 
 
 ==Sport== 
 The city has three [[soccer]] places, two in  " Op Floor "  (there is also the stadium from the CSG, the soccer club from Grevenmacher) and one on the bank from the Mousel. The city has also six [[tennis]] fields, three in a hall and the other three on the bank of the Mousel. Next to the tennis fields is an outdoor swimming pool. Grevenmacher has also two sports halls.
