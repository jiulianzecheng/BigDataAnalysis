 '''Coquitlam''' is a large [[city]] in the [[Canada|Canadian]] [[Provinces and territories of Canada|province]] of [[British Columbia]]. It is part of the [[Metro Vancouver]] area. As of 2016, 139,284 people lived there. In 2011, the population was 126,804. Between 2011 and 2016 the population grew by 9.8%. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www12.statcan.gc.ca/census-recensement/2016/dp-pd/prof/details/page.cfm?Lang=E & Geo1=CSD & Code1=5915034 & Geo2=CD & Code2=5915 & Data=Count & SearchText=coquitlam & SearchType=Begins & SearchPR=01 & B1=All & TABID=1|title=Census Profile, 2016 Census - Coquitlam, City [Census subdivision], British Columbia and Greater Vancouver, Regional district [Census division], British Columbia|last=Canada|first=Government of Canada, Statistics|website=www12.statcan.gc.ca|language=en|access-date=2018-03-26}} < /ref >  The mayor of Coquitlam is Richard Stewart. 
 
 The Coast Salish people occupied the territory for at least 9,000 years before Simon Fraser came through the region in 1808. Europeans settled in Coquitlam during 1859. < ref name= " Cohis " > {{cite web|url=http://www.coquitlam.ca/why-coquitlam/heritage.aspx|access-date=2012-12-08|title=Coquitlam - Heritage  &  History|publisher=Coquitlam.ca|archive-date=2012-05-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120504002343/http://coquitlam.ca/why-coquitlam/heritage.aspx|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 The city of Coquitlam was named a ''Cultural Capital of Canada'' in 2009 by the Department of Canadian Heritage. < ref name= " PoCoH " > {{cite web|url=http://www.pch.gc.ca/eng/1289309513550/1289309513553|access-date=2012-12-08|title=2009 Cultural Capitals of Canada|publisher=Canadian Heritage}} < /ref >
