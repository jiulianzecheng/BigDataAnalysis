 '''Spinach''' is a green, leafy [[vegetable]]. It comes from southwestern and central [[Asia]]. Its [[flower]]s are small and [[yellow]]. Spinach is rich in [[vitamin K]], [[vitamin A]], [[vitamin C]], and [[folic acid|folate]], < ref name=Nutridata > {{cite web |title=Nutrition Facts for Spinach, raw, one NLEA serving, 30 g |publisher=Nutritiondata.com |url=http://nutritiondata.self.com/facts/vegetables-and-vegetable-products/2626/2 |accessdate=February 22, 2016}} < /ref >  making it healthy. Spinach, along with other green, leafy vegetables, < ref name= " usda " > {{cite web|url=http://ndb.nal.usda.gov/ndb/foods/show/3202?fg= & man= & lfacet= & format= & count= & max=25 & offset= & sort= & qlookup=spinach|title=Basic Report from the USDA National Nutrient Database: Spinach, raw, 100 g|publisher=US Department of Agriculture, National Nutrient Database for Standard Reference Release 27|date=2014|accessdate=1 February 2015|archive-date=16 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150416133228/http://ndb.nal.usda.gov/ndb/foods/show/3202?fg= & man= & lfacet= & format= & count= & max=25 & offset= & sort= & qlookup=spinach|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  is rich in [[iron]].
