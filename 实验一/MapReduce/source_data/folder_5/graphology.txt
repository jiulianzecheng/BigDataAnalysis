'''Graphology''' is the study of a person's [[manuscript|handwriting]], and what it reveals about them. It has been controversial since its beginning, and many think it is a [[pseudoscience]]. < ref > " Barry Beyerstein Q & A " . Ask the Scientists. Scientific American Frontiers. Retrieved 2008-02-22.  " they simply interpret the way we form these various features on the page in much the same way ancient oracles interpreted the entrails of oxen or smoke in the air. i.e., it's a kind of magical divination or fortune telling where 'like begets like' " . [https://www.pbs.org/safarchive/3_ask/archive/qna/3282_bbeyerstein.html] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070220080111/https://www.pbs.org/safarchive/3_ask/archive/qna/3282_bbeyerstein.html |date=2007-02-20 }} < /ref > < ref > {{Scientific American Frontiers|season=8 |episode=2}} < /ref > 
 
 Graphology in [[linguistics]] has a different meaning. There it means the [[writing]] system of a [[language]], a [[parallel]] term to [[phonology]].
