 '''Attignat''' is a [[Communes of France|commune]]. It is found in the [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] [[Regions of France|region]] in the [[Ain]] [[Departments of France|department]]  in the east of [[France]]. In 2009, 2,850 people lived in the commune. The [[inhabitant]]s are the ''Attignatis''. The local [[economy]] is mostly based around [[commerce]]. The most important [[monument]]s in the commune are the [[Saint-Loup d'Attignat]] [[church]], originally from the 12th century, and the ruins of the ''[[Château de Jalamonde]]'', a [[feudal]] [[castle]]. Famous people from Attignat include [[Antoine Galléry]], a French [[poet]] from the 17th century who was born in the commune.
