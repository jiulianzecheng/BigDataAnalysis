 A '''ramekin''' or {{lang-fr|'''ramequin'''}} is a small glazed [[ceramic]] or glass serving bowl used for preparating and serving various [[dish (food)|food dishes]]. The word is from French (as ''ramequin''), and before that Middle Dutch and Middle Low German. 
   
 == Details == 
 
 Ramekins usually have a volume of {{convert|50|-|250|ml|impoz|abbr=on}}, < ref > [http://www.surlatable.com/category/kitchenbakeware/ramekins+%26+souffles.do Sur La Table Ramekins] < /ref >  and are dishes commonly used for serving a variety of dishes such as [[crème brûlée]], [[French onion soup]], [[Molten chocolate cake]], [[moimoi]], [[List of cheese dishes|cheese]] or [[List of egg dishes|egg dishes]], [[poi (food)|poi]], [[potted shrimps]], [[ice cream]], [[soufflé]], baked  " cocottes " , crumbles, or scallops or used to serve side [[garnish (food)|garnishes]] and [[condiments]] alongside an [[entree]]. 
 
 Traditionally circular with a fluted exterior, ramekins can also be found in novelty shapes, such as flowers, hearts or stars. 
 
 Ramekins are built to withstand high temperatures, as they are often used in ovens, or in the case of [[crème brûlée]], exposed to the flame of a [[cooking torch]].
