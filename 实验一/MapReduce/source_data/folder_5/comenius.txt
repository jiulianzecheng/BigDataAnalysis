 '''Comenius'''  (28 March 1592 – 15 November 1670) was a 17th-century [[Czechs|Czech]] [[bishop]], and an [[pedagogy|innovator in education]]. < ref name=crit/ >  His name is spelt differently in almost every language. < ref > {{lang-cs|Jan Amos Komenský}}; {{lang-sk|Ján Amos Komenský}}; {{lang-de|Johann Amos Comenius}}; 
 {{lang-pl|Jan Amos Komeński}}; {{lang-hu|Comenius Ámos János}}, in other sources: {{lang-hu|Comenius-Szeges János}}; [[Latin language|Latinized]]: ''Iohannes Amos Comenius'' < /ref >  In English it is John Amos Comenius, but he is usually called just 'Comenius'. 
 
 Comenius is famous for arguing for universal [[literacy]], and inventing the idea of a modern [[textbook]] which used pictures as well as prose to get its message across. < ref name= " crit " > {{cite book| last = Murphy| first = Daniel| title = Comenius: A Critical Re-Assessment of His Life and Work| url = https://archive.org/details/comeniuscritical0000murp| year = 1995| isbn = 978-0-7165-2537-0| page = [https://archive.org/details/comeniuscritical0000murp/page/27 27] }} < /ref > < ref name= " Sadler " > Sadler, John 1969. ''Comenius''. London: Collier Macmillan. {{ISBN|978-0-02-975920-2}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| last = Donohue| first = William A.| title = The New Realities| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=C8s3DwAAQBAJ| date = 2017| publisher = Routledge| isbn = 978-1-351-47850-2 | quote =  " ... a Czech, John Amos Comenius – the first person to advocate universal literacy – invented the textbook and the primer. "  }} < /ref >  Comenius also tried to design a language in which false statements could not be written. < ref > {{cite book| last = Hofstadter| first = Douglas R.| title = Godel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid| url = https://archive.org/details/godelescherbache00hofs_486| year = 2000| publisher = Penguin Group(CA)| isbn = 978-0-14-028920-6| page = [https://archive.org/details/godelescherbache00hofs_486/page/635 635] }} < /ref > 
 
 == Some books by Comenius == 
 There are about 16 works, with many translations and versions. < ref name=Sadler/ > 
 * 1633–1638. ''Didactica magna'' ( " The Great Didactic " ). 
 * 1642. ''A reformation of schools, designed in two excellent treatises.'' This is an English excerpt from the above. It explains his ideas for reform. 
 * 1658. ''Orbis sensualium pictus: quadrilingus''. It is something like an illustrated dictionary, in two languages,  [[Latin]], and [[German language|German]] 
 *: 1659. English edition. 
 *: 1685 in four languages: [[Latin]], [[Czech language|Czech]], [[German language|German]] and [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] ([[Magyar]]). 
 *: ''Orbis Pictus'' had a long-lasting influence on [[Pedagogy|children's education]]. It was a precursor of both audio-visual techniques and the lexical approach in language learning. < ref name= " Learning from Comenius " > {{cite web | url=http://www.teflideas.com/2011/05/14/learning-comenius/ | title = Learning from Comenius - the pedagogical underpinnings of the Orbis Pictus}} < /ref >  There is a [[Kindle]] version in English.
