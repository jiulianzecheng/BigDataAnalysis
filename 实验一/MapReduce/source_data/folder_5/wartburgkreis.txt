 '''Wartburgkreis''' is a ''Landkreis'' ([[Rural Districts of Germany|rural district]]) in the west of [[Thuringia]], [[Germany]]. The city of [[Eisenach]] is nearly completely surrounded by Wartburgbreis. 
 
 == History == 
 The district was set up in [[1994]] by joining the old districts of Eisenach and Bad Salzungen, and a few municipalities from the district Bad Langensalza. The city of [[Eisenach]] left the district in [[1998]] and became a district-free city. 
 
 The district is named after the [[Wartburg castle|Wartburg]], a castle near Eisenach most famous as [[Martin Luther]]'s [[refuge]] in [[1521]].
