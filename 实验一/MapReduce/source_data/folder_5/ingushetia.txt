 '''Ingushetia''' ({{IPAc-en|ɪ|ŋ|g|ʊ|ˈ|ʃ|ɛ|t|i|ə|}}; {{lang-inh|ГӀалгӏайче|Ghalghajče}}; {{lang-ru|Ингуше́тия}}), also spelled '''Ingushetiya''',{{sfn|Encyclopædia Britannica}} officially the '''Republic of Ingushetia''',{{efn|{{lang-inh|Гӏалгӏай Мохк|Ghalghaj Moxk}}; {{lang-ru|Респу́блика Ингуше́тия|Respúblika Ingushétiya}}}} is a [[Republics of Russia|republic]] of [[Russia]]. It is in the [[North Caucasus]] [[region]] of [[Eurasia]]. The republic is in the [[North Caucasian Federal District]]. It [[Border|borders]] the country of [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] to the south, and the [[Federal subjects of Russia|Russian federal subjects]] of [[North Ossetia–Alania]] to the west, [[Chechnya]] to the east, and [[Stavropol Krai]] to the north. 
 
 Its [[Capital city|capital]] is the [[town]] of [[Magas]]. Its largest city is [[Nazran]]. At 3,600 square km, the republic has the smallest area of Russia's non-[[Federal cities of Russia|city]] federal subjects. It was made on June & nbsp;4, 1992. This was after the [[Checheno-Ingush Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic]] was split in two. The republic is home to the [[Indigenous peoples of Russia|indigenous]] [[Ingush people|Ingush]] people. The [[Russian Census (2021)|2021 Census]] said its population was [[Estimation|estimated]] to be & nbsp;509,541. < ref name= " 2021 pop by const "  / > 
 
 Ingushetia is one of the [[Poverty|poorest]] and most unstable regions of Russia. This is mostly because of the [[insurgency in the North Caucasus]]. The [[violence]] has slowed down in recent years. However, the [[insurgency]] in [[Chechnya]] has sometimes came over the border into Ingushetia. According to [[Human Rights Watch]] in 2008, the republic has lots of [[corruption]], [[Crime in Russia|crime]], anti-government [[Protest|protests]], [[Assault|attacks]] on [[Soldier|soldiers]] and [[Officer|officers]], and low [[Human rights in Russia|human rights]]. 
 
 == Etymology == 
 The name ''Ingushetia'' comes from the [[Russian language|Russian]] name of the region's people (Ingush) and the [[Georgian language|Georgian]] [[suffix]] -éti. ''Ingushetia'' literally means  " the place where the Ingush live " . Other names like  " Dzurdzuketi " ,{{sfn|Волкова|1973|page=136}}  " Kistetia " ,{{sfn|Волкова|1973|page=136}}{{sfn|Finley|1827|page=[https://books.google.com/books?id=XEcBAAAAYAAJ & q=Kistes+Kistia+Kistetia & pg=PA310 310]}}  " Gligveti " ,{{sfn|Волкова|1973|page=136}}  " Gelia " ,  " Galga " ,{{sfn|Энгельгардт, Паррот|1814|page=26}} and  " Ingushiya " {{sfn|Яковлев|1925}}{{sfn|Мартиросиан|1928}}{{sfn|Мартиросиан|1933}}{{sfn|Крупнов|1939}} have also been used for the region.
