 '''Bignoniaceae''' or the '''Trumpet creeper''' or catalpa family of the [[Lamiales|mint order]] of [[flowering plant]]s. It contains about 112 genera and more than 725 species of trees, shrubs, and, most commonly, vines, chiefly of tropical America, tropical Africa, and the Indo-Malayan region. They form an important part of the tropical forest vegetation because of the numerous climbing vines. A few are found in temperate regions, notably the [[Catalpa|catalpa tree]] (''Catalpa''), the [[Campsis|trumpet creeper]] (''Campsis''), and the [[Bignonia|cross vine]] (''Bignonia'').
