 '''Eleutherodactylus''' is a [[genus]] of [[frog]]s in the [[Eleutherodactylidae]] [[family (biology)|family]]. < ref name=Hedges2008 > Hedges S.B; W.E. Duellman and M.P. Heinicke 2008. New World direct-developing frogs (Anura: Terrarana): molecular phylogeny, classification, biogeography, and conservation. Zootaxa 1737: 1-182. < /ref >  There are 185 [[species]] of ''Eleutherodactylus''. Many of them are commonly called  " rain frogs "  or  " robber frogs " . This is because of their [[sharp]], high-[[pitch]]ed, and [[insect]]-like calls. < ref name=amphibiaweb > {{cite web |url=http://research.amnh.org/vz/herpetology/amphibia/?action=references & id=8839 | title=Amphibian Species of the World | accessdate=2011-11-23}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[common coquí]] (''Eleutherodactylus coqui'') is the best known species. They are a [[symbol|national symbol]] of [[Puerto Rico]]. They are also an [[invasive species]] in the [[US state]] of [[Hawaii]]. Two ''Eleutherodactylus'' species, ''E. limbatus'' and ''E. iberia'', belong to the smallest known frogs. They measure only 8½ mm in length. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.cfrar.com/amphibianfacts.htm |title=The Center for Reptile and Amphibian Research: Interesting Facts About Amphibians < !-- Bot generated title -- >  |access-date=2012-06-09 |archive-date=2007-02-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070208170635/http://cfrar.com/amphibianfacts.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
