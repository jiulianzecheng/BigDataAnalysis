{{About|the deities|religious denomination|Apokpa Marup}} 
 An '''Apokpa''' or an '''Apokpi''' is an ancestral deity belonging to one particular clan or family, often prevailed in the [[Meitei religion]] ([[Sanamahism]]). < ref name=IJ > {{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hYrYfD_xZsoC & q=apokpa|title=Indian Anthropologist: Journal of the Indian Anthropological Association |year=2008 |volume=38-39}} < /ref >  Almost all the clans and the families of the [[Meitei ethnicity]] worship their ancestors having separate pantheons dedicated to them. < ref name=Val > {{cite book|title=Valley Society of Manipur: A Cultural Frontier of Indian Civilization|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1lpuAAAAMAAJ & q=apokpa|author=Ranajit Kumar Saha|year=1994|isbn = 9788185094823}} < /ref > < ref name=Rel > {{cite book|title=The Religion of Manipur: Beliefs, Rituals, and Historical Development|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WEQqAAAAYAAJ & q=apokpa|author=Saroj Nalini Parratt|year=1980|publisher=Firma KLM|isbn = 9780836405941}} < /ref > < ref name=J > {{cite journal|title=Journal of the Indian Anthropological Society|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ON-AAAAAMAAJ & q=apokpa|journal=Journal of the Indian Anthropological Society|publisher=The Society|volume=18|year=1983}} < /ref > < ref name=Read > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FkyAAAAAMAAJ & q=apokpa|title=Anthropology for North-East India, a Reader: Indian Anthropological Congress Commemorative Volume|author1=Arabinda Basu|author2= Biman Kumar Das Gupta|author3= Jayanta Sarkar|year=2004|publisher=Indian National Confederation and Academy of Anthropologists, Indian Anthropological Society, National Museum of Mankind|isbn=9788185525044}} < /ref >
