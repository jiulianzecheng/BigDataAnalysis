 '''Bathinda''' is a city and [[municipal corporation]] in [[Punjab, India|Punjab]], India. The city is the administrative headquarters of [[Bathinda District]]. It is located in northwestern India in the [[Malwa (Punjab)|Malwa]] Region, {{cvt|227|km}} west of the capital city of [[Chandigarh]] and is the fifth largest city of [[Punjab, India|Punjab]]. 
 
 Bathinda is home to the [[Maharaja Ranjit Singh Punjab Technical University]], [[Central University of Punjab]] and [[AIIMS Bathinda]]. The city is also home to two modern thermal power plants, [[Guru Nanak Dev Thermal Plant]] and [[Guru Hargobind Thermal Plant]] at Lehra Mohabbat. < ref name= " bathinda.nic.in " > {{cite web|url=http://www.bathinda.nic.in/html/places_to_visit.html#N10007|title=Places to Visit|access-date=25 November 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091126030332/http://www.bathinda.nic.in/html/places_to_visit.html#N10007|archive-date=26 November 2009|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >  Also located in the city is a [[fertilizer]] plant, < ref > {{cite web|url=http://bathinda.nic.in/html/industry.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050607235802/http://www.bathinda.nic.in/html/industry.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2005-06-07|title=Industry}} < /ref >  two cement plants ([[Ambuja Cements]] and [[UltraTech Cement|UltraTech Cement Limited]]), a large army cantonment, an air force station, a zoo, < ref name= " bathinda.nic.in " / >  and a historic [[Qila Mubarak]] fort. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.north-india.in/punjab/districts/bhatinda.htm |title=Bhatinda - district of Punjab - North India Online |access-date=2008-07-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719142724/http://www.north-india.in/punjab/districts/bhatinda.htm |archive-date=2011-07-19 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:Qila Mubarak in Bathinda.jpg|thumb|left|a iconical Qila Mubarak which was built by King [[Kanishka]] in 2nd CE century and is the location where the first [[Empress]] of India [[Razia Sultana]] was held captive < ref name= " fort " > {{cite web |title=Raziya Sultan |url=http://www.indiasite.com/delhi/history/raziasultan.html |access-date=7 May 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120331130034/http://www.indiasite.com/delhi/history/raziasultan.html |archive-date=31 March 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }} < /ref > ]]Bhatinda was changed to Bathinda to conform to the phonetical expression as locally pronounced. < ref name= " ReferenceA " > {{cite web|url=http://bathinda.nic.in/html/district_at_a_glance.html#N10024|title=District at A glance|access-date=3 January 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110110050936/http://bathinda.nic.in/html/district_at_a_glance.html#N10024#N10024|archive-date=10 January 2011|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >  According to [[Henry George Raverty]], Bathinda was known as ''Tabar-i-Hind'' (Labb-ut-Twarikh) or ''Tabarhindh'', which roughly translates as ‘Gateway to India’. The earliest mention of Tabar-i-Hind occurs in the ''Jami-Ul-Hakayat'' written about 607 Hijri or 1211 AD. < ref name= " GoP " > {{Cite web|author=|url=https://bathinda.nic.in/history/|title=Bhatinda History|publisher=Government of Punjab}} < /ref > 
 
 
 In 1004, [[Mahmud of Ghazni]] besieged and captured the local fort, which was located on the route from the northwest into the rich [[Ganges]] valley.

 In 1190, [[Muhammad of Ghor ]] attacked and occupied the fort of Bathinda. [[Prithviraj Chauhan]], the ruler of this region, managed to recover possession of the fort thirteen months later in 1191 after the [[First Battle of Tarain]]. However, Prithviraj Chauhan was killed in the [[Second Battle of Tarain]] and the fort of Bathinda once again came under the control of [[Muhammad Ghori]]. 
 
 In 14th century present region of bathinda was ruled by [[Bhati]] and [[Bhanot]] rulers of that time. 
 
 [[Bhanot|1488]], Bathinda was conquered by [[Rao Bika]], son of [[Jodha of Mandore]] (founder of [[Jodhpur State|Jodhpur]]) and became part of [[Bikaner State|Bikaner]] princely state. < ref > {{Cite book|last=Gupta|first=Jugal Kishore|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=G0Z457ZOR44C & dq=Rao+Bika+bhatinda & pg=PA24|title=History of Sirsa Town|date=1991|publisher=Atlantic Publishers  &  Distri|language=en}} < /ref >   
 
 Bathinda was an important fort in the area from [[Delhi]] to [[Lahore]] during the time of the [[Delhi Sultanate]]. 
 
 In 1634, a battle named [[Battle of Lahira]] (at Lahira in Bathinda) was fought between [[Guru Hargobind]] and [[Mughal Empire|Mughals]]. 
 
 The town had become an important trade and commercial centre under [[Mughal Empire|Mughal]] rule and the [[Mughal Empire|Mughal Emperors]] such as [[Akbar]] and [[Aurangzeb]] undertook many projects to enhance the fort of Bathinda. 
 
 In circa 1754, the town was conquered by Maharaja Ala Singh, the [[Maharaja of Patiala]] and since then it followed the history of erstwhile princely state of [[Patiala]]. With the dawn of independence and merger of Patiala and East Punjab States into a division called [[Patiala and East Punjab States Union]] (PEPSU), Bathinda became a full-fledged district with headquarters at Bathinda city. < ref name= " ReferenceA " / > 
 
 ==Demographics== 
 {{see also|List of cities in Punjab and Chandigarh}} 
 
 ===Population=== 
 As per provisional reports of Census India, population of Bathinda city in 2011 is 285,813; male and female are 151,782 and 134,031 respectively. The sex ratio of Bathinda city is 868 females per 1000 males. The number of literate people in Bathinda city are 211,318 of which 118,888 are males while 92,430 are females, average literacy rate is 82.84 percent of which male and female literacy is 87.86 and 77.16 percent respectively. Total children (ages 0 to 6) in Bathinda city are 30,713: 16,472 boys and 14,241 girls. Child sex ratio of girls is 865 per 1000 boys. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/city/16-bathinda.html|title=Census 2011}} < /ref > 
 
 ===Religion=== 
 [[Hinduism]] is the majority religion in Bathinda city with 62.61% people following the faith. [[Sikhism]] is the second most popular religion in the city which is followed by 35.04% of the people. Other religions include Islam, Christianity, Buddhism and Jainism. [[Sikhs]] count for 70.89% of the population in [[Bathinda District]] on a whole in spite of not being a majority in the city. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.census2011.co.in/census/district/599-bathinda.html|title=Bathinda District Population Census 2011, Punjab literacy sex ratio and density}} < /ref >
