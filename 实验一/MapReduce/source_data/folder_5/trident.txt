 A '''trident''' {{IPAc-en|ˈ|t|r|aɪ|d|ə|n|t}} is a three-[[tine (structural)|pronged]] [[spear]]. It is used for [[spearfishing]]. < ref > S.L. Hamilton, ''Spearfishing'' (Minneapolis, MN: Abdo Publishing, 2015), p. 8 < /ref >  Historically it was used as a [[polearm]]. [[Gladiator]]s used them in [[Ancient Rome]] after they cast a [[Fishing net|net]] to trap their opponent. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.wisegeek.com/what-is-a-trident.htm |title=What is a Trident? |website=wiseGEEK |publisher=Conjecture Corporation |access-date=8 November 2016}} < /ref >  The trident is the [[weapon]] of [[Poseidon]], or [[Neptune (mythology)|Neptune]], the god of the sea in [[classical mythology]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.greekmythology.com/Olympians/Poseidon/poseidon.html |title=Poseidon |publisher=GreekMythology.com |access-date=8 November 2016}} < /ref >  In [[Hindu mythology]] it is the weapon of [[Shiva]], known as ''[[trishula]]'' (Sanskrit for  " triple-spear " ). < ref > {{cite web |url=https://western-hindu.org/2009/10/14/shaivite-hindu-symbols-the-trishula/ |title=Shaivite Hindu Symbols: The Trishula |publisher=Western Hindu |access-date=8 November 2016}} < /ref >  The word  " trident "  comes from the [[French language|French]] word ''trident'', which in turn comes from the [[Latin]] word ''tridens''. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.thefreedictionary.com/trident |title=trident |website=The Free Dictionary |publisher=Farlex |access-date=8 November 2016}} < /ref >
