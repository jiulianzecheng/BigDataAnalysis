 '''Filozoa''' is a [[Cladistics|clade]] (a group of plants or animals with a common ancestor) that is in the [[Opisthokont|Opisthokonta]] clade. It includes animals, and their close [[Microorganism|single-celled]] relatives. These relatives are more closely related to animals than they are to [[Fungus|fungi]], < ref name= " pmid18461162 " > {{cite journal|author=Shalchian-Tabrizi K.|author2=Minge M.A.|author3=Espelund M.|date=7 May 2008|editor1-last=Aramayo|editor1-first=Rodolfo|title=Multigene phylogeny of choanozoa and the origin of animals|journal=PLOS ONE|volume=3|issue=5|pages=e2098|doi=10.1371/journal.pone.0002098|pmc=2346548|pmid=18461162|bibcode=2008PLoSO...3.2098S|doi-access=free}} {{open access}} < /ref >  and other Opisthokonts. 
 
 Three groups are in Filozoa: [[Filasterea]], [[Choanoflagellate|Choanoflagellatea]], and Kingdom [[Animal|Animalia]]. Animalia is the most important one, which has all proper animals in it.
