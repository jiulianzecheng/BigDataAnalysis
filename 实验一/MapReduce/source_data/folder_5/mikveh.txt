 A '''mikveh''' or '''mikvah''' ({{Hebrew Name|מִקְוֶה / מקווה|mikve|miqweh}}, {{abbr|pl.|Plural}} & nbsp;''mikva'ot'', ''mikvoth'', ''mikvot'', or ([[Yiddish]]) ''mikves'', < ref > {{cite book|last=Sivan|first=Reuven|author2=Edward A Levenston|title=The New Bantam-Megiddo Hebrew  &  English dictionary|url=https://archive.org/details/newbantammegiddo008800|url-access=registration|publisher=Bantam Books|location=Toronto; New York|year=1975|isbn=0-553-26387-0}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book|last=Lauden|first=Edna|title=Multi Dictionary|url=https://archive.org/details/multidictionaryb0036ainb|publisher=Ad Publications|location=Tel Aviv|year=2006|isbn=965-390-003-X|page=[https://archive.org/details/multidictionaryb0036ainb/page/397 397]}} < /ref >  lit.,  " a collection " ) is a [[Judaism|Jewish]] [[Ritual purification|ritual]] [[bath]]. It is used to cleanse ritual impurity. Immersion in a mikveh is also a part of the traditional process for converting to Judaism.
