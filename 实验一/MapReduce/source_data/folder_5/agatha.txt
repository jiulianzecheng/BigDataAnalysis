'''Agatha''' is a [[female]] [[given name]].The pronunciation of the name is[ag-uh-thuh]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Agatha {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/agatha|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  It comes from the Ancient [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''agathos'', meaning  " good " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=Agatha name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Agatha-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-10|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The name last ranked in the Top 1000 girls names in the [[United States]] in the 1930s. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref > But in the last few years it has fallen out of fashion. 
 
 Saint Agatha of [[Sicily]] was a 3rd-century [[saint]]. There was a famous crime writer named [[Agatha Christie]].  
 
 There have been several [[tropical cyclone]]s in the Eastern Pacific Region named ''Agatha''. [[Tropical Storm Agatha (2010)|Tropical Storm Agatha]] in 2010 caused over $1 billion in damage and over three hundred deaths across [[Central America]]. 
 
 There are different versions of the name, for example Agata. 
 
 ==People named Agatha== 
 * Saint [[Agatha of Sicily]] (died 251), Christian saint 
 * [[Agatha Barbara]] (1923 & ndash;2002), only female [[President of Malta]] 
 * [[Agatha Christie]], British novelist 
 * [[Agatha Sangma]] (born 1980), member of the Parliament of India 
 *[[Agatha Harkness]], a fictional character and anti-heroine appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics
