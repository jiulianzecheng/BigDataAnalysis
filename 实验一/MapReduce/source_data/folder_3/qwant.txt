 '''Qwant''' is a privacy-oriented [[search engine]], originated and hosted in [[France]], and is available in 13 languages. < ref name= " lang "  / >  It has over  50 million total visits worldwide. < ref > {{Cite web|title=qwant.com Traffic Statistics|url=http://similarweb.com/fr/website/qwant.com/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=SimilarWeb|language=fr}} < /ref > 
 
 == Search engine == 
 Qwant users can create an account, which allowed them to post on boards using Qwant boards (discontinued). < ref > {{Cite web|title=Qwant Boards|url=https://boards.qwant.com/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Qwant Boards|language=en}} < /ref >  It also allows them to save their preferences. 
 
 Qwant was first [[Beta testing|beta tested]] on 16 February 2013. < ref > {{Cite web|title=About Qwant|url=https://about.qwant.com/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=About Qwant|language=en-US}} < /ref >  The stable version was released on 4 July 2013, < ref > {{Cite web|title=Qwant : le nouveau moteur de recherche français est officiellement lancé|url=https://www.atlantico.fr/pepite/778409/qwant--le-nouveau-moteur-de-recherche-francais-est-officiellement-lance|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Atlantico.fr|language=fr|archive-date=2021-01-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210107203247/https://www.atlantico.fr/pepite/778409/qwant--le-nouveau-moteur-de-recherche-francais-est-officiellement-lance|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  with an updated version being released on 17 April 2015. < ref > {{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2015-04-17|title=Welcome to the new Qwant! {{!}} Blog Qwant|url=https://blog.qwant.com/en/welcome-to-the-new-qwant-2/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150522045821/https://blog.qwant.com/en/welcome-to-the-new-qwant-2/|archive-date=2015-05-22|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Qwant Blog}} < /ref > 
 
 On 10 December 2014, child-friendly version of Qwant, called Qwant Junior was [[Beta testing|beta tested]] in cooperation with the French Ministry of Education. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2014-12-01|title=Qwant Junior|url=https://blog.qwant.com/qwant-junior-en/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Blog Qwant|language=en-US}} < /ref >  A stable version was later released. 
 
 On 5 October 2015, Qwant Lite was released. Qwant Lite is a version of Qwant, specifically made for legacy [[Web browser|web browsers]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2015-05-22|title=Qwant Lite est spécifiquement adapté aux utilisateurs d'anciennes versions de navigateurs|url=http://archives.nicematin.com/derniere-minute/qwant-lite-est-specifiquement-adapte-aux-utilisateurs-danciennes-versions-de-navigat.2358540.html|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-06}} < /ref > 
 
 When the [[Brave (web browser)|Brave web browser]] was released in 2017, it featured Qwant as one of the default search engines. 
 
 In 2018, the [[Government of France|French government]] declared that all government [[Search engine|web searches]] are going to be made with Qwant. < ref > {{Cite magazine|last=Goujard|first=Clothilde|date=2018-11-20|title=France is ditching Google to reclaim its online independence|language=en-GB|magazine=Wired UK|url=https://www.wired.co.uk/article/google-france-silicon-valley|access-date=2021-01-06|issn=1357-0978}} < /ref > 
 
 == Parent company == 
 The self titled company, Qwant [[Société par actions simplifiée|SAS]] was founded in February 2011 by Jean-Manuel Rozan,  Éric Leandri and Patrick Constan. 
 
 It has over 160 employees in [[Paris]], [[Nice]], [[Ajaccio]] and [[Épinal]]. It also has offices in [[Germany]] and [[Italy]]. < ref > [https://about.qwant.com/download/11492/DP_QWANT_UK_2019.pdf  " Qwant press kit " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210108195414/https://about.qwant.com/download/11492/DP_QWANT_UK_2019.pdf |date=2021-01-08 }} (2019). < /ref > 
 
 From 2011 to 2014, Qwant SAS acquired a total of [[Euro|€3.5 million]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2014-12-31|title=Qwant wants to be alternative to Google|url=https://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/12/31/qwant-wants-to-be-alternative-to-google/?_r=0|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2016, the [[European Investment Bank]] invested [[Euro|€25 million]] in Qwant. < ref > {{Cite web|title=France: EIB provides EUR 25 million for start-up Qwant: Innovative financing for a European search engine with strong growth potential|url=https://www.eib.org/en/press/all/2015-243-eib-provides-eur-25-million-for-start-up-qwant|access-date=2021-01-06|website=European Investment Bank|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 == Privacy == 
 Qwant offers protections based on the [[General Data Protection Regulation|GDPR]]. 
 
 Qwant's Privacy Policy says that  " ''as a principle, Qwant does not collect data about its users when they search'' " . It claims not to use any cookie nor any other tracking technology. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Privacy Policy|url=https://about.qwant.com/legal/privacy/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=About Qwant|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 == Reception == 
 [[The New York Times]] compared Qwant to Quaero, another [[search engine]] made to rival [[Google Search|Google]]. Quaero shut down in 2013. 
 
 The service received public support from [[Emmanuel Macron]], then [[Ministry of the Economy and Finance (France)|Minister of the Economy and Finance]]  and the future President of France. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Macron fait la pub du « Google français » : vous ne connaissiez pas ?|url=https://www.nouvelobs.com/rue89/rue89-tech/20150414.RUE8667/macron-fait-la-pub-du-google-francais-vous-ne-connaissiez-pas.html|access-date=2021-01-06|website=L'Obs|language=fr}} < /ref >
