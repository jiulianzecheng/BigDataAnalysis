 '''Tula Paulinea Contostavlos''' (born 13 July 1988), < ref name=AMbio > {{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tulisa-mn0002849006|title=Tulisa|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=19 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161013223252/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tulisa-mn0002849006|archive-date=13 October 2016|url-status=live}} < /ref >  known as '''Tulisa''', is a [[United Kingdom|British]] singer, songwriter and actress of [[Greece|Greek]] and [[Republic of Ireland|Irish]] ancestry. She was a member of the [[London Borough of Camden|Camden]]-based [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[N-Dubz]]. Tulisa was also a judge on [[The X Factor (British TV series)|''The X Factor'']].  
 
 == Background == 
 
 Tula Paulinea Contostavlos was born on 13 July 1988 < ref name=AMbio/ >  in [[Camden Town|Camden Town, London]]. When Contostavlos was five, her mother, who has [[bipolar disorder]] and [[schizoaffective disorder]], was [[Involuntary commitment|sectioned]] under the [[Mental Health Act 1983|Mental Health Act]]. < ref name= " Mum " > {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/iplayer/episode/b00tf1zt/Tulisa_My_Mum_and_Me/?from=r & id=e066b176-8183-43ad-ac02-604faa6fd6b4.0|title=Three Programmes – Tulisa: My Mum and Me, 10 August 2010|publisher=BBC|access-date=23 September 2010}} < /ref >  The 2010 BBC programme, ''Tulisa: My Mum and Me'', described Contostavlos' life looking after her mother before joining N-Dubz. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://channelhopping.onthebox.com/2010/08/10/tulisa-%E2%80%93-my-mum-me-review/ |title=Tulisa – My Mum  &  Me Review: I Need You |work=Channelhopping.onthebox.com |date=10 August 2010 |access-date=23 September 2010 |archive-url=https://archive.today/2012.07.09-062246/http://channelhopping.onthebox.com/2010/08/10/tulisa-%E2%80%93-my-mum-me-review/ |archive-date=9 July 2012 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >

 == Career == 
 Tulisa was in the band [[N-Dubz]] with her cousin [[Dappy]] and their friend [[Fazer (rapper)|Fazer]]. The group became very popular. Tulisa was a celebrity judge on ''[[The X Factor (UK)|The X Factor]]'' in 2011 and 2012. She [[mentor]]ed the  " Groups "  category in 2011. They included [[Little Mix]], the first group to win the competition. Tulisa returned to the show in 2014 because [[Mel B]] was ill. 
 
 As a singer, Contostavlos has recorded three studio albums with N-Dubz, ''[[Uncle B]]'' (2008), ''[[Against All Odds (N-Dubz album)|Against All Odds]]'' (2009) and ''[[Love.Live.Life]]'' (2010). In 2011, she started work on a solo album. In 2012 it was released. ''[[The Female Boss]]'' got a lot of bad reviews and didn't chart well. Three singles were released from the album. The first single,  " Young " , entered the UK Singles Chart at number one. 
 
 In 2022, N-Dubz went on a reunion tour. They have also appeared on [[Gogglebox|''Celebrity'' ''Gogglebox'']] together since [[2023]]. 
 
 == Drug trial== 
 In June 2013, Costostavlos, along with rapper Mike GLC were arrested on suspicion of supplying [[Controlled drugs in the United Kingdom|class A drugs]]. < ref > [https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/tulisa-arrest Tulisa arrested] < /ref > < ref > [https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/tulisa-arrested-mike-glc-insists-1932155 Tulisa arrested: Friend Mike GLC insists star has  " not done anything "  and  " truth will come out " ] < /ref >  In July 2014, Contostavlos' trial for supplying [[cocaine]] ended because it was discovered that the journalist who accused her, Mazher Mahmood, had lied to the court. 
 
 In July 2014, she made a documentary about her time during the trial named ''Tulisa: The Price of Fame,'' which premiered on [[BBC Three]] to a moderate success, attracting 615,000 viewers (3.8% of the audience within its time slot). < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/tv/tv-news/tulisa-price-fame---615000-3929428#.U-Z8aBajxdg|title=Tulisa: The Price of Fame - 615,000 viewers watch intimate documentary|work=Daily Mirror|date=29 July 2014|access-date=9 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811184033/http://www.mirror.co.uk/tv/tv-news/tulisa-price-fame---615000-3929428#.U-Z8aBajxdg|archive-date=11 August 2014|url-status=live}} < /ref >   In April 2015, Justice [[Nicholas Kearns]], President of the Irish [[High Court (Ireland)|High Court]], described her as having been  " cruelly deceived in a shabby sting operation " , but dismissed her attempt to sue the ''[[Irish Sun]]'' for [[defamation]]. < ref name= " RTE2015-04-15a " > {{Cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/news/2015/0415/694374-tulisa/|title=Tulisa loses High Court defamation bid against the Irish Sun|publisher=[[Raidió Teilifís Éireann]]|date=15 April 2015|access-date=16 April 2015|quote=The High Court President, Mr Justice Nicholas Kearns said Tulisa Contostavlos, had been  " cruelly deceived in a shabby sting operation "  and there was no doubt it had caused her grave upset. But he said he was astounded that a more rapid reaction to initiate legal proceedings did not take place. He said he was satisfied that there should be no extension of time for the case. It is understood the singer, who was not present in court, is now considering appealing the High Court decision.|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150416060347/http://www.rte.ie/news/2015/0415/694374-tulisa/|archive-date=16 April 2015|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 After the collapse of her drug trial, ''[[The Guardian]]''{{'s}} [[Suzanne Moore]] wrote an article on Contostavlos image and reputation and wrote that  " Tulisa is famously a working class girl made good, though of course made good is never what she is allowed to be " . < ref name= " Moore " > {{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2014/jul/28/tulisa-constostavlos-middle-class-media-chavl|title=If Tulisa Contostavlos were middle class, she wouldn't face such scorn|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=28 July 2014|first=Suzanne|last=Moore}} < /ref >  In Moore's opinion,  " Tulisa has been branded by the media as a [[chav]] and therefore almost anything can be said about her " . < ref name= " Moore "  / >
