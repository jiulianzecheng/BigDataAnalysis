 '''''Adactylidium''''' is a [[genus]] of [[mites]]. It is known for its short and unusual  [[life cycle]]. < ref > {{cite book |author=Stephen Jay Gould|year=1980 |chapter=Death before birth, or a mite's ''nunc dimittis'' | pages=[https://archive.org/details/pandasthumbmorer00goul_659/page/n67 69]–75 |title=The panda's thumb: more reflections in natural history |url=https://archive.org/details/pandasthumbmorer00goul_659|publisher= Norton |isbn=9780393013801}} < /ref >  The [[pregnant]] [[female]] mite attaches herself to a single [[egg (biology)|egg]] of a [[Thrips|thrip]]. < ref name=Gould206 > Gould, Stephen Jay 1980. The throwaway male. ''New Scientist''. '''86''', (1204), Apr 24, p. 206. < /ref >  She grows from five to eight female [[wikt:offspring|offspring]] and one [[male]] in her body. The offspring begin to eat their mother from the inside out. < ref name= " pmid7042533 " > {{cite journal |author=Kirkwood T.B.  |author2=Cremer T. |title=Cytogerontology since 1881: a reappraisal of August Weismann and a review of modern progress |journal=Human Genetics |volume=60 |issue=2 |pages=101–121 |year=1982 |pmid=7042533 |doi=10.1007/BF00569695 |s2cid=25744635 |url=http://epub.ub.uni-muenchen.de/9265/1/cremer_thomas_9265.pdf }} < /ref >  The single male mite [[Sexual reproduction|mates]] with all his [[Sibling|sisters]] when they are still in the mother. < ref name=Gould206/ >  The females, now pregnant, cut holes in their mother's body. This is so that they can get out to find new thrips eggs. < ref name=Gould206/ >  The male gets out too. But having served his [[Reproduction|biological function]] he dies after a few hours. < ref name=Gould206/ >  The females die at the age of four days, when their own offspring eat them alive from the inside.
