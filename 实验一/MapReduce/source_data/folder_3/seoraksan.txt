 '''Seoraksan''' ({{lang-ko|설악산}}) is a [[mountain]] in the north-eastern [[Gangwon Province, South Korea|Gangwon-do]], [[South Korea]]. The mountain is about 1,708 meters (5,604 feet) high. < ref name= " ghsinmun " > {{cite news |title=1억 년 세월이 조각한 대자연의 걸작, ‘국민 관광지’ 설악산 |url=https://www.khan.co.kr/life/travel/article/200902231145192 |accessdate=10 August 2021 |publisher=경향신문 |language=Korean}} < /ref >  The highest peak of the mountain is ''Daecheongbong Peak'' (대청봉). < ref name= " ghsinmun "  / >  Seoraksan became the fifth [[national park]] in South Korea in 1970. < ref > {{cite web |title=공원안내 |url=http://www.knps.or.kr/front/portal/visit/visitCourseSubMain.do?parkId=120400 & parkNavGb=park & menuNo=7020093 |website=설악산국립공원 |accessdate=9 August 2021 |language=Korean}} < /ref > 
 
 == Geography == 
 ''Gongnyong Ridge'' (공룡능선) is a mountain ridge in Seoraksan. It divides the mountain into Oaeseorak (Outer Seorak) and Naeseorak (Inner Seorak). Oaeseorak is the north-eastern area of Seoraksan, and Naeseorak is the north-western area of Seoraksan. The southern area of the mountain is called Namseorak. < ref > {{cite news |title=“설악 비경을 다 감상할 수 있어요!” |url=http://www.outdoornews.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=3670 |accessdate=27 October 2021 |work=아웃도어뉴스 |date=1 January 2011 |language=ko}} < /ref > 
 
 == Animals and plants == 
 [[File:Adult male amur goral standing on rock at National Institute of Ecology, Korea.jpg|130px|thumb|long-tailed goral]] 
 About 1,562 species of animals are living in Seoraksan. < ref name= " gangwondominilbo " > {{cite news |title=[문화재, 사람에게 말을걸다] 17. 설악산 |url=http://www.kado.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=497699 |accessdate=10 August 2021 |publisher=강원도민일보 |language=Korean}} < /ref >  [[Long-tailed goral]] is the animal representing the mountain. About 250 long-tailed gorals are living in Seoraksan, and the Korea National Park Service has restored this species since 2007. < ref name= " knps " > {{cite web |title=깃대종 |url=https://www.knps.or.kr/portal/main/contents.do?menuNo=7020053#ntab05 |website=국립공원공단 |accessdate=10 August 2021 |language=Korean}} < /ref > 
 
 Also, there are about 1,013 species of plants in Seoraksan. < ref name= " gangwondominilbo "  / >  [[Siberian dwarf pine]] is the flagship species of Seoraksan, < ref name= " UNESCO " > {{cite web |title=Mount Sorak |url=http://www.unesco.org/new/en/natural-sciences/environment/ecological-sciences/biosphere-reserves/asia-and-the-pacific/republic-of-korea/mount-sorak |website=UNESCO |accessdate=10 August 2021}} < /ref >  and they mainly live near ''Daecheongbong Peak''. < ref name= " knps "  / >  To protect the species, the area near ''Daecheongbong Peak'' has been special protected area since 2007. < ref name= " knps "  / >  The Korea National Park Service has restored the species since 2012. < ref > {{cite web |title=설악산 대청봉일대 국내 유일 눈잣나무 서식지 복원 |url=http://me.go.kr/home/web/board/read.do;jsessionid=ABmZj-hqNysKR4lEe361Yac6.mehome1?pagerOffset=4950 & maxPageItems=10 & maxIndexPages=10 & searchKey= & searchValue= & menuId=286 & orgCd= & boardId=355406 & boardMasterId=1 & boardCategoryId= & decorator= |website=Ministry of Environment |accessdate=10 August 2021 |language=Korean}} < /ref > 
 
 == Famous sights == 
 === Ridges === 
 [[File:Seoraksan, nice rocks.jpg|thumb|250px|''Yongajangseong'']] 
 ==== Gongnyong Ridge ==== 
 ''Gongnyong Ridge'' (Dinosaur Ridge, 공룡능선) is a mountain ridge located between ''Madeungryeong'' (마등령) and ''Muneomigogae'' (무너미고개). The name means that it looks like a dinosaur's back. < ref > {{cite news |title=설악산 공룡능선 무박 종주 산행 |url=http://www.outdoornews.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=32021 |accessdate=27 October 2021 |work=아웃도어뉴스 |date=1 September 2020 |language=ko}} < /ref > 
 
 ==== Yongajangseong ==== 
 ''Yongajangseong'' (용아장성) is located in Naeseorak. 'Yonga' means 'dragon's teeth' in [[Korean language|Korean]], and rocks at ''Yongajangseong'' look like them. < ref > {{cite news |title=출입 금지 절벽 오르다 2명 추락사...단풍철 설악산 사고 급증 |url=https://www.ytn.co.kr/_ln/0115_202110031902096094 |accessdate=27 October 2021 |work=YTN |date=3 October 2021 |language=ko}} < /ref >  It is illegal to hike along this ridge. < ref > {{cite web |title=설악산 ‘용아장성’은 어떻게 죽음의 능선이 되었나? |url=http://san.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2021/10/05/2021100501898.html?utm_source=naver & utm_medium=referral & utm_campaign=san |website=월간산 |accessdate=27 October 2021 |language=ko |date=5 October 2021}} < /ref >
