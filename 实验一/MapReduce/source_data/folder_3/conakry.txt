 '''Conakry''' or '''Konakry''' is the [[capital (city)|capital]] and largest city of [[Guinea]]. The city is a [[Harbour|port]] on the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. The [[population]] of Conakry is about 2 million people. < ref > [http://www.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/2824.htm  " Background Note: Guinea " ]. Bureau of African Affairs, U.S. Department of State, January 2007. Accessed February 24, 2007. < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 Conakry was first [[settler|settled]] on [[Tombo Island]]. It later spread to the [[Kaloum Peninsula]]. The city was founded after [[United Kingdom|Britain]] gave the [[island]] to [[France]] in [[1887]]. It became the capital of [[French Guinea]] in about [[1904]]. 
 
 == Economy == 
 Conakry is Guinea's largest city and its [[administration|administrative]], [[communication]]s, and [[economy|economic]] center. The main part of the city's [[economy]] deals with the port. The port has up to date [[facility|facilities]] for handling and storing [[cargo]]. It is used for [[transport]]ing [[alumina]] and [[banana]]s.  Items created in Conakry include [[food|food products]] and [[material]]s used for building [[house]]s. 
 
 == Things to see == 
 [[File:Conakry-palaisdupeuple.JPG|thumb|left|People's Palace]] 
 Things to see in the city include the [[Guinea National Museum]], several [[market]]s, the [[Guinea Palais du Peuple]] (the People's [[Palace]]), [[Conakry Grand Mosque]] and the nearby [[Iles de Los]]. 
 
 The city is noted for its [[botany|botanical]] [[garden]].  The [[Polytechnical Institute of Conakry]] is also located in Conakry.
