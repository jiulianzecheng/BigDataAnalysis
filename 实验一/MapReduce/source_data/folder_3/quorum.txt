 A '''quorum''' is the [[wikt:minimum|minimum]] number of [[wikt:member|member]]s of an [[organization]] who must be present in order for their meeting to be [[legal]] or [[official]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.duhaime.org/LegalDictionary/Q/Quorum.aspx |title=Quorum Definition: |publisher=Duhaime.org |access-date=9 June 2016 |archive-date=7 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160407194917/http://www.duhaime.org/LegalDictionary/Q/Quorum.aspx |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The word is often used in [[legislature|legislative assemblies]], [[corporation]]s and [[Society|societies]] who make [[wikt:official|official]] decisions. < ref name=uslegal > {{cite web |url=http://definitions.uslegal.com/q/quorum/ |title=quorum |publisher=US Legal, Inc |access-date=9 June 2016}} < /ref >  The [[by-law]]s of an organization will usually say how many members make up a quorum. < ref name=IBBI > {{cite web |url=http://ibbi.seadeo.net/docs/rulesord/quorum.htm |title=Procedures Used in Meetings: Quorum of Members |publisher=Internet Band Boosters International |access-date=9 June 2016 |archive-date=9 June 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160609022113/http://ibbi.seadeo.net/docs/rulesord/quorum.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >   
 
 In the case of [[legislature]]s and government bodies, the requirements for a quorum are often set by [[statute]] or in their [[constitution]]. Some bodies use a fixed number for a quorum while others use a [[percent]]age of the members. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.dummies.com/how-to/content/roberts-rules-for-defining-a-quorum.html |title=Robert's Rules for Defining a Quorum |author=C. Allan Jennings |website=For Dummies |publisher=John Wiley  &  Sons |access-date=9 June 2016}} < /ref >  It is usually the responsibility of the [[chairperson]] to make sure there is a quorum present. < ref name=robert'sBART > {{cite web |url=http://www.bartleby.com/176/64.html |title=Henry M. Robert (1837–1923).  Robert’s Rules of Order Revised.  1915. 64 A Quorum |publisher=Bartleby.com |access-date=9 June 2016}} < /ref >  If there is not a quorum at a meeting, then in most cases the only business that can be discussed is taking steps to obtain a quorum, to decide on a time when to [[wikt:adjourn|adjourn]] the meeting and to adjourn the meeting. < ref name=robert'sBART/ >   
 
 The [[noun]] quorum is the [[plural]] of the [[Latin]] word ''qui'', meaning  " of whom. " < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.vocabulary.com/dictionary/quorum |title=quorum |publisher=Vocabulary.com |access-date=9 June 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 A similar term,  " quorum-busting " , is a [[wikt:tactic|tactic]] used by members of a group when they know they will lose a [[vote]]. < ref name=smithBG > {{cite web |url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/ideas/2012/06/16/five-obscure-tactics-snarl-congress/AqJRxpZ0Dz7tn35JYwOhWI/story.html |title=Five obscure tactics to snarl Congress |author=Jordan Michael Smith |date=16 June 2012 |publisher=The Boston Globe |access-date=9 June 2016}} < /ref >  If enough members do not show up for a meeting, a vote cannot take place if there is no quorum. < ref name=smithBG/ >  It is a [[wikt:delay|delay]]ing tactic similar to a [[filibuster]]. Both are used in the hopes that if a vote is delayed long enough, it may not take place at all. < ref name=smithBG/ >
