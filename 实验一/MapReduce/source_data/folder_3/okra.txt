 '''Okra''' (''Abelmoschus esculentus'') are known in many [[English language|English]]-speaking countries as '''lady's fingers''' or '''gumbo''') is a [[flowering plant]] in the [[Malvaceae|mallow family]], related to [[cotton]] and [[hibiscus]]. < ref name= " Lost Crops " > {{cite book|author=National Research Council|title=Lost Crops of Africa: Volume II: Vegetables|url=http://books.nap.edu/openbook.php?record_id=11763|accessdate=2008-07-15|series=Lost Crops of Africa|volume=2|date=2006-10-27|publisher=National Academies Press|isbn=978-0-309-10333-6|chapter=Okra|chapter-url=http://books.nap.edu/openbook.php?record_id=11763 & page=287}} < /ref >  It is valued for its edible green seed pods. It is grown for the pods, which are harvested in the immature stage and used in salads, soups and dishes. < ref > {{cite web|title=9 Amazing Health Benefits of Okra|date=25 November 2015 |url=http://www.nutritionfox.com/9-amazing-health-benefits-of-okra/|publisher=www.nutritionfox.com|accessdate=25 January 2016}} < /ref >   It is the national vegetable of Pakistan. Some of the ways it is eaten are fried, grilled, or in soup.  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Okra has an interesting food history|url=https://dailyprogress.com/okra-has-an-interesting-food-history/article_b49d5bf3-30d6-5ae2-aa15-3de9450ae1be.html|access-date=2022-03-18|website=The Daily Progress|language=en}} < /ref >
