 {{nihongo|'''Kinako'''|黄粉 or きなこ}} is toasted [[soybean]] [[flour]].  It is commonly used in [[Japanese cuisine]]. < ref > Andoh, Elizabeth. (2012). [https://books.google.com/books?id=lziyA6pHdxEC & dq=Kinako & pg=PA14 ''Washoku: Recipes from the Japanese Home Kitchen,'' p. 14]. < /ref > 
 
 Kinako itself is not sweet, but people often add [[sugar]] into kinako and coat the mixed-one to ''[[wagashi]]'' (Japanese sweets), for example [[Warabimochi]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 The use of ''kinako'' was recorded during the [[Yayoi period]] from 300 BC to 300 AD. < ref > Shurtleff, William and Akiko Aoyagi  (2009). [https://books.google.com/books?id=ahgFlXnDZ54C & dq=Kinako & pg=PA772  ''History of Miso'', p. 772]. < /ref >
