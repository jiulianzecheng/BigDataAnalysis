 '''Chukwaki Ekweani''' (born 16 July 1995) is a Nigerian singer and songwriter. He is also known as '''CKay'''. CKay is his [[stage name]]. 
 
 Ekweani was born in [[Kaduna|Kaduna, Nigeria]]. His father worked with a choir at a church. His father taught him how to play the piano. < ref name= " Drum " > {{cite web|last1=Mbendeni|first1=Alutho|title=Nigerian star CKay speaks to us about being 'Africa's boyfriend' and new music|url=https://www.news24.com/drum/celebs/news/nigerian-star-ckay-speaks-to-us-about-being-africas-boyfriend-and-new-music-20210917|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211001213201/https://www.news24.com/drum/celebs/news/nigerian-star-ckay-speaks-to-us-about-being-africas-boyfriend-and-new-music-20210917|archive-date=1 October 2021|access-date=1 October 2021|website=Drum}} < /ref >  After this, he joined a band. There were two other people in it. < ref name= " DJBooth " > {{cite web|title=Ckay Wants to Be Your Boyfriend: Interview|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2021-04-07-ckay-interview-audiomack|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211002112004/https://djbooth.net/features/2021-04-07-ckay-interview-audiomack|archive-date=2 October 2021|access-date=2 October 2021|website=DJBooth|language=en}} < /ref >  He left the band. He started making music by himself, and used the name CKay. 
 
 CKay made the song  " [[Love Nwantiti]] " . It is also known as  " Love Nwantiti (Ah Ah Ahh) " . The song was released in 2019. It was the first number one song on the [[Billboard U.S. Afrobeats Songs|''Billboard'' Afrobeats chart]]. < ref > {{cite magazine|last1=Frankenberg|first1=Eric|date=29 March 2022|title=CeeKay's 'Love Nwantiti' Is No. 1 on First Billboard U.S. Afrobeats Songs Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/ckays-love-nwantiti-tops-first-billboard-us-afrobeats-songs-chart-1235050658/|url-status=live|magazine=Billboard|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220406032031/https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/ckays-love-nwantiti-tops-first-billboard-us-afrobeats-songs-chart-1235050658/|archive-date=6 April 2022|access-date=5 April 2022}} < /ref >  It went on the chart in 2022. On 9 May 2022, it became the first African song to be listened to over one billion times on [[Spotify]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Courage|first=Onyema|date=10 May 2022|title=CKay's 'Love Nwantiti' surpasses 1 billion streams on Spotify across all versions|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/ckays-love-nwantiti-surpasses-1-billion-streams-on-spotify-across-all-versions/19r6xm6|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220511192639/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/ckays-love-nwantiti-surpasses-1-billion-streams-on-spotify-across-all-versions/19r6xm6|archive-date=11 May 2022|access-date=21 May 2022|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en}} < /ref >
