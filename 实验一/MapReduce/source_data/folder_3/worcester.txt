 '''Worcester''' (pronounced ''Wuster'') is a [[City status in the United Kingdom|cathedral city]] and district in the [[West Midlands (region)|West Midlands]] of [[England]]. It is the [[county town]] of [[Worcestershire]], a county of England. In 2015, it had 101,328 residents. < ref > {{cite web |title=Worcester |url=https://www.visitworcestershire.org/worcester/ |website=Visit Worcestershire}} < /ref > 
 
 It is about 30 miles (48 km) southwest of [[Birmingham]] and 29 miles (47 km) north of [[Gloucester]].  
 
 The [[River Severn]] runs through the middle of the city. [[Worcester Cathedral]] overlooks the river.  
 
 The [[University of Worcester]] is a high ranking [[university]] located in the city. 
 
 Famous composer [[Edward Elgar]] was from Worcester. It is also where [[Worcestershire sauce]] originated. 
 
 [[John, King of England|King John of England]]'s tomb is in Worcester, in [[Worcester Cathedral]]. 
 
 == Transport == 
 
 === Road === 
 The A38, A44, A422, A449, A4103, A4440 and A4538 roads pass through Worcester. 
 
 The [[M5 motorway|M5]] bypasses Worcester.
