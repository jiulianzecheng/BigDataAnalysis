'''Albaston''' ({{lang-kw|Trevalba}}) is a [[Hamlet (place)|hamlet]] in [[Cornwall]], England, [[United Kingdom]]. It is in the [[civil parish]] of [[Calstock]]. 
 ==Geography== 
 Albaston is about 1 & nbsp;mile (2 & nbsp;kilometres) from the centre of [[Gunnislake]] and half-a-mile (0.7 & nbsp;kilometres) from [[Gunnislake railway station]], the junction of the [[Tamar Valley Line]] from [[Plymouth]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 In the past, Albaston was closely connected with the nearby mine at [[Drakewalls]]. The success of the Drakewalls mine in the late eighteenth and early nineteenth century led to the growth of the village of Albaston, including the building of a Methodist chapel and several shops, homes and businesses. < ref name= " Gamble " > {{cite book| last = Gamble| first = Barry| title = Cornish Mines: Gwennap to the Tamar| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=1yhxoRzAspAC & pg=PA77| year = 2011| publisher = Alison Hodge Publishers| isbn = 978-0-906720-82-0| page = 77 }} < /ref >  A brewery, Edward Bowhay  &  Brothers, was established by 1877; this brewery had closed by 1930. < ref > {{cite book| last1 = Todd| first1 = Arthur Cecil| last2 = Laws| first2 = Peter| title = The Industrial Archaeology of Cornwall| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=9vsaAAAAMAAJ| year = 1972| publisher = David and Charles| page = 215 | isbn = 9780715355909}} < /ref > < ref name= " Barber1994 " > {{cite book| last = Barber| first = Norman| title = A Century of British Brewers, 1890-1990| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=5-ogAQAAIAAJ| year = 1994| publisher = Brewery History Society| isbn = 978-1-873966-04-4| page = 12 }} < /ref >

 ==Notable residents== 
 Christian [[Pentecostal]] leader [[Thomas Ball Barratt]] was born in Albaston in 1862, the son of a mining engineer. He moved to Norway when his father began working for a mine there and in later life became the founder of the Norwegian Pentecostal movement. < ref name= " Letson " > {{cite book|last=Letson|first=Harry|title=Catalysts of the Spirit: An Introduction to Pentecostal History|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BASbWIacZGIC & pg=PA73|year=2007|publisher=AuthorHouse|isbn=978-1-4259-6273-9|page=73}} < /ref >
