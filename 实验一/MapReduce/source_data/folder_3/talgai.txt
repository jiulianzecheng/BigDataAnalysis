 '''Talgai''' is an [[archaeological site]], near [[Allora, Queensland|Allora]] on the [[Darling Downs]], south east of [[Brisbane]], [[Queensland]], [[Australia]]. < ref name= " talgai " > {{cite web |url=http://www-personal.une.edu.au/~pbrown3/Talgai.html |title=Talgai |first= |last= |work=www-personal.une.edu.au |year=2012 |accessdate=13 April 2013 |archive-date=27 April 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130427231338/http://www-personal.une.edu.au/~pbrown3/Talgai.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In 1884 a [[Pleistocene]] era skull was found in Dalrymple Creek after a big flood. < ref name= " monroe " / >  It was found by William Naish, a shearer, who was building a fence at the East Talgai [[Station (agriculture)|station]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.archaeologybulletin.org/article/view/bha.20202 |title=The Curious History of the Talgai Skull |first=Jim |last=Allen |work=Bulletin of the History of Archaeology |year=2010 |accessdate=13 April 2013 }}{{Dead link|date=June 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  The skull was that of a 14 year old boy who had lived about 11,000 years ago. < ref name= " talgai " / >  Naish gave the skull to the owners of the station. It was purchased by the [[University of Sydney]] in 1914. < ref name= " monroe " / >  It was the first fossil found which showed the early human occupation of Australia. < ref name= " talgai " / > < ref name= " monroe " / > 
 
 The skull was heavily encrusted with calcium carbonate and the shape had been distorted during long time buried in the ground. < ref name= " monroe " > {{cite web |url= http://austhrutime.com/talgai_skull.htm |title=Talgai Skull |first=M. H. |last=Monroe|work=Australia: The Land Where Time Began|year=2011 |accessdate=13 April 2013}} < /ref >  At first scientists thought it was a primitive skull with ape like teeth. < ref > {{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article15601633 |title=Talgai Skull |newspaper=[[Sydney_morning_herald|The Sydney Morning Herald (NSW : 1842 - 1954)]] |location=NSW |date=19 June 1915 |accessdate=13 April 2013 |page=14 |publisher=National Library of Australia}} < /ref >  Later study showed that the skull was a modern [[Homo sapiens|Homo sapien]], the teeth were normal, but heavily worn. < ref name= " talgai " / >  The boy had a massive injury to the side of his skull which was probably what had killed him. < ref name= " monroe " / > 
 
 In 1968 a Tom Haydon made a [[documentary]] movie, ''The Talgai Skull: An Investigation Into the Origin of the Australian Aborigines''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://miff.com.au/60_years_of_miff/film_archive?movieid=16181|title=The Talgai Skull|first=|last=|work=Melbourne International Film Festival (MIFF)|year=2013|accessdate=13 April 2013}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It won the [[Australian Film Institute]] award for Best Documentary. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.afi.org.au/Content/NavigationMenu/Archive/2010Awards/2010AwardsPDF/Non-Feature_Award_Winners_1958-2010.pdf|title=AFI AWARD WINNERS - Non-Feature Award Winners 1958-2010|first=|last=|work=pdf.js|year=2011|accessdate=13 April 2013|archive-date=17 July 2011|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/60ETb1bQB?url=http://www.afi.org.au/Content/NavigationMenu/Archive/2010Awards/2010AwardsPDF/Non-Feature_Award_Winners_1958-2010.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
