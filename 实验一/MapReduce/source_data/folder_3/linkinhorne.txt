 '''Linkinhorne''' is a parish and village in southeast [[Cornwall]], [[United Kingdom]]. The village itself is at {{gbmapping|SX 320 736}} and is approximately four miles (6.5 & nbsp;km) northwest of Callington and seven miles (11 & nbsp;km) south of [[Launceston]]. < ref name= " ReferenceA " > Ordnance Survey: Landranger map sheet 201 ''Plymouth  &  Launceston'' {{ISBN|9780319231463}} < /ref > 
 
 < !-- IN ALPHA ORDER -- > As well as Linkinhorne itself, other settlements in the parish include Bray Shop, Caradon Town, Downgate, Henwood, Ley Mill, Minions, and Upton Cross. < ref name= " ReferenceA " / > 
 
 The area is bordered by the River Inny in the north-east and [[Bodmin Moor]] to the west. The valley of the [[River Lynher]] runs through the parish. < ref name= " ReferenceA " / >  The parish is largely rural but west of the B3254 road from Launceston to [[Liskeard]] it includes part of [[Caradon Hill]], once an industrialised mining area. 
 
 The parish church of St Melor is built of granite and dates from the 15th century.
