{{Greek alphabet|letter=sigma}} 
 '''Sigma''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Σ σ ς''') is the 18th letter of the [[Greek alphabet]]. It is used to represent the  " s "  sound in [[Ancient Greek|Ancient]] and [[Modern]] [[Greek language|Greek]]. In the system of [[Greek numerals]], it has a value of 200. Letters that came from it include the [[Roman alphabet|Roman]] [[S]] and [[Cyrillic alphabet|Cyrillic]] [[С]]. 
 == Mathematics == 
 The uppercase Σ is the shorthand symbol for [[summation]] (the symbol is also known as the summation operator). < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|date=2020-03-20|title=Greek/Hebrew/Latin-based Symbols in Mathematics|url=https://mathvault.ca/hub/higher-math/math-symbols/greek-hebrew-latin-symbols/|access-date=2020-10-07|website=Math Vault|language=en-US}} < /ref >  It is often used to condense [[Taylor series]] and to write out [[trigonometry|trigonometric]] functions such as [[sine]], [[cosine]], and [[tangent]] as [[series]]. 
 
 The lowercase σ is used to represent population [[standard deviation]] in [[statistics]], and [[permutation]] function in [[abstract algebra]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Sigma Symbol in Greek Alphabet|url=https://www.greeksymbols.net/sigma-symbol|access-date=2020-10-07|website=www.greeksymbols.net|language=en}} < /ref >
