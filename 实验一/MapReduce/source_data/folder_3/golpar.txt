 '''Golpar''' (''Heracleum persicum''), also known as '''Persian hogweed''', is a flowering [[plant]]. It grows wild in [[mountain]]s. 
 
 == Uses == 
 Golpar [[seed]]s are used as a [[spice]] in [[Persia|Persian]] cooking. These seedpods are slightly [[wikt:bitter|bitter]]. They are usually sold in powder and are often sold as  " Angelica Seeds. "  The powder is sprinkled over [[bean]]s and [[potato]]es. Golpar is also used in soups and stews. It is often sprinkled over [[pomegranate]] seeds. Golpar is also mixed with [[vinegar]] into which [[lettuce]] leaves are dipped before eating.
