 '''Monstercat''' (also known as Monstercat Media) is a [[Canada|Canadian]]-based independent [[electronic dance music]] (EDM) [[record label]] in [[Vancouver, British Columbia]]. < ref name= " djmag canada2 " > {{cite web|url=https://issuu.com/djmagcanada/docs/dj_20mag_20canada_20issue_20feb_fin_c35b700c37e354|title=Mike Darlington: Channeling his inner Monstercat|date=February 2014|access-date=26 May 2014|author=Line Abrahamian|work=DJ Magazine Canada}} < /ref >  Monstercat was formed in July 2005 by Mike Darlington [[chief executive officer|CEO]], < ref name= " the frontliner2 " > {{cite web|url=http://thefrontliner.com/interviews/labels/interview-monstercat-ceo-mike-darlington-37214|title=Monstercat CEO â€“ Mike Darlington|date=8 November 2013|access-date=26 May 2014|work=The FrontLiner}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.onlythebeat.com/interviews/2013/04/11/behind-the-beat-hellberg-interview/|title=Behind the Beat: Hellberg Interview|date=11 April 2013|access-date=26 May 2014|work=Only the Beat|archive-date=27 May 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140527215313/http://www.onlythebeat.com/interviews/2013/04/11/behind-the-beat-hellberg-interview/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and Ari Paunonen, [[chief operating officer|COO]]. < ref name= " djmag canada2 "  / > 
 
 The label signs artists on a non-exclusive single-track basis, allowing artists to freely move between other labels and brands. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://thedjlist.com/news/monstercat-breed-record-label-digital-music-age/|title=Monstercat: A New Breed of Record Label in the Digital Music Age|website=The DJ List|access-date=2016-05-29|archive-date=2016-06-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160608031342/http://thedjlist.com/news/monstercat-breed-record-label-digital-music-age|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Monstercat follows a simple release schedule, where new music is released three times a week on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays, < ref > {{Citation|last=Monstercat|title=[Drum  &  Bass] - Feint - Atlas [Monstercat Release]|date=2011-07-09|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEhEUiEY_Qg|quote=New videos every Monday/Wednesday/Friday!|access-date=2016-05-30}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://play.spotify.com/user/monstercatmedia/playlist/4kw9kdjzx1UmyWvpysl0y2|title=Monstercat New Releases playlist on Spotify|website=play.spotify.com|access-date=2016-05-30|quote=fresh tunes updated 3 times a week!}} < /ref >  in addition to an hour-long [[podcast]] that is live streamed every Tuesday on their [[Twitch.tv|Twitch]] account, known as ''Call Of The Wild''. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.monstercat.com/podcast/|title=Monstercat Podcast|website=www.monstercat.com|access-date=2016-05-30|quote=Weekly radio show featuring 1 hour of dance music. "   " New episodes every Tuesday 1PM PDT / 4PM EDT / 9PM BST|archive-date=2018-02-11|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180211222104/https://www.monstercat.com/podcast/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Once Monstercat has released thirty tracks consecutively, a new compilation album is created containing these tracks and two continuous mixes of the album. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.magneticmag.com/2014/05/monstercat-drops-newest-compilation-monstercat-017-ascension/|title=Monstercat Drops Newest Compilation - Monstercat 017: Ascension|first=Matthew|last=Meadow|date=2014-05-22|website=magneticmag.com|publisher=Magnetic Magazine|access-date=2016-05-30|quote=Featuring 30 tracks and two unique hour-long mixes,}} < /ref >  At several points in time, Monstercat has released a track outside of their regular release schedule for various reasons. 
 
 == History == 
 In July 2005, Monstercat's current CEO - Mike Darlington - along with Ari Pauonen, Monstercat's current COO, created the Monstercat YouTube channel as a way for their friends to promote themselves and share music with each other. < ref name= " djmag canada2 "  / > < ref name= " the frontliner2 "  / > < ref name= " noise porn2 " > {{cite web|url=http://www.noiseporn.com/2014/03/noiseporn-interviews-monstercat-ceo-mike-darlington/|title=Monstercat CEO Mike Darlington|date=10 March 2014|access-date=26 May 2014|author=Chelsea Perry|work=NoisePorn|archive-date=27 May 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140527221037/http://www.noiseporn.com/2014/03/noiseporn-interviews-monstercat-ceo-mike-darlington/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The two created compilation albums to provide artists with an easy method of releasing their music, as well as lessening the competitiveness between the artists. < ref name= " djmag canada2 "  / > < ref name= " waterloo chronicle2 " > {{cite web|url=http://www.waterloochronicle.ca/news-story/5892463-thinking-of-the-possibilities/|title=Thinking of the possibilities|date=17 September 2013|access-date=26 May 2014|author=Bob Vrbanac|work=Waterloo Chronicle}} < /ref >   Monstercat's first compilation album, Launch Week, featured seven artists: Feint, Halo Nova (currently Varien / Koinu), Stephen Walking, Ephixa, Neilio, Arion and Going Quantum. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/album/monstercat-001-launch-week/id450533017|title=Monstercat 001 - Launch Week by Various Artists on iTunes|website=iTunes|date=12 July 2011 |access-date=2016-05-30}} < /ref > 
 
 === Krewella Signs === 
 In December 2005, Monstercat signed the very notable musical group [[Krewella]], < ref name= " djmag canada2 "  / > < ref name= " the frontliner2 "  / > < ref name= " noise porn2 "  / >  an American electronic dance music duo (at the time, a trio) from [[Chicago, Illinois]].  " Killin' It "  was Krewella's first original song released by a label. < ref name= " the frontliner2 "  / > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-11-12/entertainment/chi-interview-krewella-returning-home-feeling-alive-20131112_1_krewella-trindl-band-members|title=Krewella returning home feeling 'Alive'|date=12 November 2013|access-date=26 May 2014|author=Luis Gomez|work=Chicago Tribune}} < /ref >  Monstercat promoted Krewella using many social media campaigns and partnered with many EDM [[YouTube]] promotional channels for a larger platform. < ref name= " djmag canada2 "  / > 
 
 === 2014 - 2017 === 
 During the first few months of 2014, Monstercat partnered with the electric family in aid of the Humane Society of Canada. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://edm.com/articles/2014/3/19/Electric-Family-Monstercat-Humane-Society-Canada|title=Electric Family and Monstercat Team Up For Humane Society Of Canada Bracelet|website=EDM.com|language=en-US|access-date=2016-05-30|archive-date=2016-09-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160913161545/http://edm.com/articles/2014/3/19/Electric-Family-Monstercat-Humane-Society-Canada|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  In December 2014, Monstercat launched a 24-hour digital radio station, hosted on Twitch. < ref name= " TwitchLaunch2 " > {{cite web|url=http://inthemix.com/features/behind-the-scenes-of-monstercat-the-label-born-out-of-youtube/31418/2|title=Behind The Scenes of Monstercat the Label Born Out of YouTube|first=Krystal|last=Rodriguez|date=18 Dec 2014|publisher=InTheMix|access-date=29 Dec 2014}} < /ref >  Monstercat continued to release compilation albums containing thirty songs and two mixes each until 2017. < ref name= " waterloo chronicle2 "  / > 
 
 === 2018 - Present === 
 Near the beginning of 2018, Monstercat announced that it would seperate into two brands: Monstercat Uncaged and Monstercat Instinct. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2018/01/02/monstercat-uncaged-instinct/|title=Monstercat Announces Huge Change for the New Year|last=Reis|first=Sean|date=2018-01-03|website=EDM Sauce|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-18}} < /ref >
