'''''Burkholderia''''' is a genus of ''Pseudomonadota''. These bacteria are [[gram-negative]] and they are [[Aerobic organism|aerobic]] (require oxygen to grow). They are rod-shaped and are [[motile]], moving using a single or many [[Flagellum|flagella]] (tails), except for ''[[:en:Burkholderia_mallei|Burkholderia mallei]]'', which is not motile. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Paul|first=Sulav Indra|last2=Rahman|first2=Md.  Some bacteria in this genus cause disease in humans and animals. ''[[:en:Burkholderia_mallei|Burkholderia mallei]]'' causes [[:en:Glanders|glanders]], a disease mostly found in [[horse]]s. ''[[:en:Burkholderia_mallei|B. malle]]'' must infect a host [[mammal]] to live and it is transmitted from one host to another. [[:en:Burkholderia_cepacia_complex|''Burkholderia cepacia'']], typically found in soil and water, is a [[pathogen]] that may cause pulmonary infections in people with [[cystic fibrosis]]. ''[[:en:Burkholderia_pseudomallei|Burkholderia pseudomalle]]'' mainly lives in soil and it can cause [[:en:Melioidosis|melioidosis]] in humans through contact with contaminated soil or water.

 == Microbiology == 
 ''Burkholderia'' species are [[gram-negative]] bacteria. They are [[Aerobic organism|aerobic]] so require [[oxygen]] to grow. 
 
 Antibiotic resistance is a problem when treating ''[[:en:Burkholderia_mallei|B. mallei]]'' and ''[[:en:Burkholderia_pseudomallei|B. pseudomallei]]'' infections.
