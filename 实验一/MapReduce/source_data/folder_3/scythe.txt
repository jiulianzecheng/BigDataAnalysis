 A '''scythe''' ({{PronEng|ˈsaɪð}}, from [[Old English language|Old English]] ''siðe''. < ref name= " OED " > ''Oxford English Dictionary'' 1933 < /ref > ) is an [[agriculture|agricultural]] tool for mowing [[grass]] or [[Harvest|reaping]] [[agriculture|crops]].  It was replaced by horse-drawn machines and then [[tractor]]s, but is still used in some areas of [[Europe]] and [[Asia]].
