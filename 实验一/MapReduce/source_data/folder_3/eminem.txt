 '''Marshall Bruce Mathers III''' (born October 17, 1972), known professionally as '''Eminem''', is an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, and actor. < ref name= " allmusicbio2 " > {{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p347307|pure_url=yes}}|title=Eminem – Biography|last=Ankeny|first=Jason|author2=Torreano, Bradley|year=2006|publisher=AllMusic|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201101624/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/eminem-mn0000157676|archive-date=December 1, 2017|access-date=January 30, 2008}} < /ref >  He became popular in 1999 with the release of the album ''[[The Slim Shady LP]]'', which won the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album]] that year. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1439679/20010222/eminem.jhtml|title=Eminem, Elton's 'Stan' Duet Proves Anticlimactic|last=Basham|first=David|date=February 22, 2001|publisher=MTV News|access-date=December 30, 2009}} < /ref >  His next work, ''[[The Marshall Mathers LP]]'', became the best-selling solo album in U.S. history. < ref name= " MMLP2 " > {{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/5923810/eminem_bounces_britney_from_top_spot|title=Eminem Bounces Britney From Top Spot|magazine=Rolling Stone|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080401120550/http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/5923810/eminem_bounces_britney_from_top_spot |archive-date=April 1, 2008 |url-status=dead |access-date= April 23, 2008}} < /ref >  This made him known around the world. He helped publicize his record label, [[Shady Records]], and the group [[D12]]. 
 
 ''The Marshall Mathers LP'' and his fourth album, ''[[The Eminem Show]]'', also won the [[Grammy Award]]s. This made him the first artist to win the Best Rap Album of the Year award three times in a row. In 2003, he won an [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Oscar for Best Original Song]] with  " [[Lose Yourself]]. "  That song was featured in a movie called ''[[8 Mile]]''.  " Lose Yourself "  would become the single that for the longest time occupied the top spot of the [[hip hop]] charts. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.lefalaja.com/worldrecords1.html|title=ACTUAL ARTICLE TITLE BELONGS HERE! |publisher=Lefalaja.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100829012226/http://www.lefalaja.com/worldrecords1.html|archive-date=August 29, 2010|access-date=May 24, 2010}} < /ref >  In 2004, [[rumor]]s about the end of his career were announced after the release of ''[[Encore (album)|Encore]].''  == Career == 
 === 1992–1996: Early career and ''Infinite'' === 
 One of Mathers' early mentors as a rapper was the local rapper [[Champtown]], who gave Mathers his first time in a studio. Mathers also starred in his first song in Champtown's  " Do-Da-Dipity "  from 1992. Mathers and Champtown later had relationship problems and their friendship ended in 1995. Champtown said Eminem falsely accused him of trying to get with his wife at the time, Kim. < ref > {{cite web|last=Smith|first=Brian|date=December 15, 2004|title=Champ's Town|url=http://www2.metrotimes.com/editorial/story.asp?id=7099|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050104112511/http://www2.metrotimes.com/editorial/story.asp?id=7099|archive-date=January 4, 2005|access-date=December 10, 2020|website=Metro Times}} < /ref >  Before he began rapping, Mathers also had a few [[minimum wage]] jobs. Some of these were at a [[restaurant]] called Gilbert's Lodge and at [[Little Caesars]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite book|last = Bozza| first = Anthony|title = Whatever You Say I Am|year = 2010|publisher = Crown Archetype|isbn = 978-0-307-54920-4|page = 16}} < /ref > 
 
 Mathers was first signed to FBT Productions in 1992, run by brothers Jeff and Mark Bass. In 1996, his debut album ''[[Infinite (Eminem album)|Infinite]]'', which was recorded at Bassmint, a recording studio owned by the [[Bass Brothers]], was released on the independent company [[Web Entertainment]]. < ref > {{harvnb|Bozza|2003|p=15}} < /ref >  Eminem recalls  " Obviously, I was young and influenced by other artists, and I remember several comments saying that I looked like [[Nas]] and [[AZ (rapper)|AZ]]. ''Infinite'' served me to try to figure out what my rap style was, how my voice would sound on the mic and live. It was a growth phase. I felt ' infinite ' as a demo album that ended up being pressed to become studio. " < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.eminem.com/bio/bio.aspx|title=Eminem biography|work=Eminem.com|publisher=Interscope Records|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080213185359/http://www.eminem.com/bio/bio.aspx|archive-date=February 13, 2008|access-date=2008-04-23}} < /ref >  Subjects covered in ''Infinite'' included his problems in raising  his newborn daughter, Hailie Jade Mathers, in a decent way while he had little money. < ref > {{harvnb|Bozza|2003|p=16}} < /ref >  Early in his career, Eminem formed a hip hop duo with another Detroit rapper, [[Royce da 5'9 " ]], called [[Bad Meets Evil]]. < ref > {{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p469895|pure_url=yes}}|title=Royce da 5'9 – Biography|last=Anziri|first=Jon|year=2002|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=2008-01-30}} < /ref >  After the release of Infinite, Eminem's personal problems and abuse of drugs and alcohol were too much and he attempted suicide. < ref name= " allmusicbio " > {{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p347307|pure_url=yes}}|title=Eminem – Biography |last=Ankeny|first=Jason|year=2006 |publisher= AllMusic|access-date= January 30, 2008|author2= Torreano, Bradley}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Jimmy Iovine]], co-founder of [[Interscope Records]], asked for a demo from Eminem after he finished second in the 1997 [[Rap Olympics]]. Eminem had also won ''Wake Up show's Freestyle Performer of The Year'', which helped him to land a recording contract. < ref > Anish, Chandy. Eminem Biography. http://www.buzzle.com/editorials/4-28-2005-69211.asp {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110920151433/http://www.buzzle.com/editorials/4-28-2005-69211.asp |date=2011-09-20}} < /ref >  Iovine played the demo for hip hop producer [[Dr. Dre]], the owner of [[Aftermath Entertainment]]. The two began recording songs for Eminem's ''[[The Slim Shady LP]]'' and Eminem had a cameo appearance on the album ''[[Devil Without a Cause]]'' by [[Kid Rock]]. < ref name= " allmusicbio "  / >  Hip-Hop magazine ''The Source'' had Eminem in its  " Unsigned Hype "  article in March 1998. < ref name= " Bozza 813 " > {{harvnb|Bozza|2003|p=81}} < /ref > 
 
 === 1997–1999: ''The Slim Shady LP'' === 
 Eminem signed with Interscope Records and Aftermath Entertainment in 1998. He released his major-label debut album, ''[[The Slim Shady LP]]'', a year later in February 1999. ''Billboard'' praised the album as  " light years ahead of the material he had written before " . < ref name= " billbard " > {{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/bio/index.jsp?pid=315925|title=Eminem|magazine=Billboard|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080418142233/http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/bio/index.jsp?pid=315925|archive-date=April 18, 2008|access-date=2008-04-23}} < /ref >  It went on to be one of the most popular albums of 1999, and went 3x platinum in the United States before the end of the year. < ref name= " rotn " > {{cite web|url=http://www.rockonthenet.com/artists-e/eminem_main.htm|title=Timeline|publisher=Rock on the Net|access-date=2008-04-23}} < /ref >  The album's popularity also brought controversy around much of the albums lyrics. In '97 Bonnie and Clyde "  he describes a trip with his newborn daughter, Hailie, when he discards his wife's body, and in another song called  " [[Guilty Conscience]] " , it ends with persuading a man to kill his wife and his lover.{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}  " Guilty Conscience "  marked the beginning of the friendship and musical bond between Dr. Dre and Eminem. The two would later collaborate on a lot of hit songs, including  " [[Forgot About Dre]] "  and  " What's the Difference "  from Dr. Dre's album ''[[2001 (Dr. Dre album)|2001]]'',  " [[Bitch Please II]] "  from ''[[The Marshall Mathers LP]]'',  " Say What You Say "  from ''[[The Eminem Show]]'',  " [[Old Time's Sake]] "  and  " [[Crack a Bottle]] "  from ''[[Relapse (album)|Relapse]]'' and  " [[I Need a Doctor]] "  and  " Die Hard "  from Dr. Dre's ''[[Compton (album)|Detox]]''. Dr. Dre would appear to have at least one guest appearance on all of Eminem's studio albums for Aftermath Entertainment. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.sptimes.com/2006/01/16/Artsandentertainment/Eminem_and_his_ex_wif.shtml|title=Eminem and his ex-wife remarry|publisher=[[St. Petersburg Times]]|access-date=2008-04-23|archive-date=2014-12-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141221103326/http://www.sptimes.com/2006/01/16/Artsandentertainment/Eminem_and_his_ex_wif.shtml|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The album has now been certified 5x platinum in the US. With this release, Eminem was accused of imitating the style and word choice of underground rapper [[Cage (rapper)|Cage]]. < ref name= " autogeneret12 " > {{cite news|url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-11923595_ITM|title=Interview: His name is not Slim Shady|last=Dearborn|first=Matt|date=December 1, 2005|access-date=2008-09-09|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120723010903/http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-11923595_ITM|archive-date=July 23, 2012|publisher=University Wire|author2=Duke|url-status=live}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news|url= http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-417506.html|title= Smut Peddlers: Split-Level Raunch|last= Drumming |first= Neil|date= February 14, 2001 |newspaper= The Washington Post |access-date= 2008-09-09|archive-url= https://archive.today/20120526211609/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-417506.html|archive-date= May 26, 2012 |url-status= dead}} < /ref >

 === 2002–2003: ''The Eminem Show'' === 
 Eminem's third-major album, ''[[The Eminem Show]]'', was released in the summer of 2002 and turned out to be another big hit for the rapper. The album peaked at the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], selling well over a million copies in its first week. < ref name= " rotn " / >  The album's first single was the song  " [[Without Me]] " , in which Eminem made depressive comments about [[Limp Bizkit]], [[Moby]] and [[Lynne Cheney]], among others. Other singles from the album include  " [[Cleanin' Out My Closet]] "  and  " [[Sing for the Moment]] " . The album has been certified eight times platinum by the RIAA. The album speaks on topics such as his way to fame, his relationship with his wife and daughter, and his status in the hip-hop community. He also addresses his [[Indictment|charge]] of assaulting a [[bouncer]] who kissed his wife in 2000.{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}} Stephen Thomas Erlewine of AllMusic felt that even though anger was present on several of the album's tracks, the album was not as provocative as ''The Marshall Mathers LP''. < ref name= " eminem show " > {{cite web|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|year=2002|title= " The Eminem Show "  — Overview|url=http://www.allmusic.com/cg/amg.dll?p=amg & sql=Aiypzefrk2gf6|access-date=2008-02-01|publisher=AllMusic|archive-date=2005-01-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050130100844/http://www.allmusic.com/cg/amg.dll?p=amg & sql=Aiypzefrk2gf6|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  However, [[L. The first single,  " [[We Made You]] " , was released in April. < ref > {{cite web|url= http://www.rapbasement.com/eminem/030509-eminem-will-release-two-new-albums-this-year-details-and-dates-included-read-here.html|title= Eminem Relapses Twice; 2 NEW Albums Coming|date= March 5, 2009|website= Rapbasement.com |access-date= May 24, 2010|archive-date= December 8, 2015|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20151208113023/http://www.rapbasement.com/eminem/030509-eminem-will-release-two-new-albums-this-year-details-and-dates-included-read-here.html|url-status= dead}} < /ref >  ''[[Relapse (album)|Relapse]]'', the first of the announced Records, was officially released on May 19, but did not achieve the sale success as in the previous four albums. < ref > {{cite web|url= http://musica.uol.com.br/ultnot/2009/05/20/ult89u10653.jhtm|title= Eminem lança disco  " Relapse "  com show gratuito em Detroit - 20/05/2009 - UOL Música |website= musica.uol.com.br|access-date= November 14, 2010}} < /ref >  However, Eminem still received positive reviews for his return to hip hop. On November 19, he announced the release of ''[[Relapse: Refill]]'' on December 21. It was a relaunch of ''Relapse'' with the addition of seven bonus tracks, including  " [[Forever (Drake song)|Forever]] "  and  " Taking My Ball " . 
 
 On April 14, 2010, Eminem said there was no ''Relapse 2''. Although his followers thought he was not releasing an album, he had changed its title to ''[[Recovery]].'' “I had originally planned for Relapse 2 to come out last year, but as I kept recording and working with new producers, the idea of a sequel to Relapse started to make less and less sense to me, and I wanted to make a completely new album. The music on Recovery came out very different from Relapse, and I think it deserves its own title. " < ref > {{cite web|date=April 14, 2010|title=EMINEM SPEAKS ON NEW ALBUM RECOVERY|url=http://rapradar.com/2010/04/14/eminem-speaks-on-new-album-recovery/|website=RapRadar.com}} < /ref > 
 
 ''Recovery'' was released on June 18, 2010. It sold 741,000 copies during its first week in the US, topping the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. The album was also at the top of the charts in several other countries. ''Recovery'' remained atop the ''Billboard'' 200 chart for five weeks out of seven total. 
 
 Its first single,  " Not Afraid " , was released on April 29, and its music video was released on June 4.  " [[Not Afraid]] "  was followed by  " [[Love the Way You Lie]] " . ''Recovery'' was the best-selling album worldwide in 2010, with over 3.4 million copies sold.

 === Health issues === 
 Eminem has spoken publicly about his addiction to prescription drugs, including [[Acetaminophen|Vicodin]], [[Zolpidem|Ambien]], and [[Diazepam|Valium]]. In 2001, he said he believed to have been suffering from [[Tourette syndrome|Tourette's syndrome]]. < ref > {{cite web|date=2001-06-20|title=EMINEM SWEARS HE HAS RARE ILLNESS|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/eminem-405-1383121|access-date=2020-06-03|website=NME.COM|language=en-GB}} < /ref >  He revealed that he was taking up to 60 Valium and 30 Vicodin pills every day, and that doctors told him he was two hours away from dying at one point. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/eminem-128-1264060|title=Eminem discusses drug addiction and overdose in new documentary|date=2013-06-30|website=NME.COM|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-06-03}} < /ref >  In December 2007, Eminem overdosed on [[methadone]]. He fell on his bathroom floor while urinating and needed to be hospitalized. Due to this, he couldn't spend Christmas break with his kids. He was sent home a few days later, but was readmitted to the hospital again with a torn [[Meniscus (liquid)|meniscus]] that required [[surgery]]. He got surgery in the following days and was released. A few days later, he was readmitted to the hospital again, this time with a [[seizure]]. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  Speaking about his addiction, he said,  " In seven months I was able to accomplish more than I could accomplish in three or four years of doing drugs. " < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/24/arts/music/24pare.html|title=Get Clean, Come Back: Eminem's Return|last=Pareles|first=Jon|date=2009-05-21|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-06-03|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  During his heavy drug use period, he revealed that he gained 80 & nbsp;pounds, and was going to [[McDonald's]] and [[Taco Bell]] every day. He said,  " I remember being somewhere and hearing these kids talking. One of them said, 'That’s Eminem,' and the other said, 'No, it’s not, man – Eminem ain’t fat.' I was like, Motherfucker. That’s when I knew I was getting heavy. "  He also said that [[Elton John]] was the person who helped him the most with his recovery and that Elton John called him once a week to check on him. < ref name= " :1 " / >
