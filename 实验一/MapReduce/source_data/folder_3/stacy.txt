'''Stacy''' (also '''Stacey''') [ '''stey'''-see ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Stacy {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/stacy|access-date=2023-04-04|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a mostly [[female]] [[given name]], but is used sometimes on [[male]]s. Several celebrities use this name. They include [[Stacy Ferguson]], [[Stacey Dash]] and Stacy London. Fictional people include Stacey Colbert, a character in the television show ''Ned  &  Stacey''. The meanings of this name are disputed. For [[girl]]s, ''Stacy'' means  " princess "  in [[Greek language|Greek]]. Stacy is a moniker with an extensive lineage, boasting a deep-rooted history and significance. The appellation Stacy is derived from the Greek name Eustace, signifying  " fruitful "  or  " productive.
