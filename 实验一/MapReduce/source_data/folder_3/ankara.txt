 '''Ankara''' is the [[Capital (city)|capital city]] of the [[country]] of [[Turkey]]. It is in the center of [[Anatolia]]. Ankara is the second largest city after [[Istanbul]]. The city has a [[population]] of 4,319,167 (2005) (Province 5,153,000), and an elevation of 938 meters (3080 & nbsp;feet). < ref > [http://www.fallingrain.com/world/TU/68/Ankara.html Ankara, Turkey: Latitude, Longitude and Altitude] < /ref >  It was formerly known as Engürü the city is also the capital of [[Ankara Province]] 
 
 Ankara is a very modern city. Ankara was made the capital of Turkey in 1923. Ankara is known for its performing arts, home to the State Opera and Ballet, the Presidential Symphony Orchestra and several national theater companies. In Ankara there's a place called Anitkabir, which is a big hilltop mausoleum that was built to celebrate [[Kemal Atatürk]] who was the first president. 
 
 == History == 
 Ankara is a city that has a lot of history. Ankara was invaded many times. In 546 BCE, [[Achaemenid]] [[Persians]] was in control of Ankara (approximated date). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/80014324|title=Cities of the Middle East and North Africa : a historical encyclopedia|date=2007|publisher=ABC-CLIO|others=Michael Dumper, Bruce E. Stanley|isbn=978-1-57607-920-1|location=Santa Barbara, Calif.|oclc=80014324}} < /ref >  Ankara was then invaded by the [[Roman Empire|Romans]] in 25 BCE. At the time, Ankara was controlled by [[Augustus]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/862077105|title=Capital cities around the world : an encyclopedia of geography, history, and culture|last=Cybriwsky|first=Roman A.|date=2013|isbn=978-1-61069-248-9|location=Santa Barbara, California|oclc=862077105}} < /ref >  Ankara castle was captured by the [[Crusaders]] in 1101. Ankara castle was captured again by the [[Seljuk]]s in 1227. Ankara was controlled again by the [[Ottomans]] in 1403. On October 13, 1923 Ankara became the capital of Turkey. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/308130674|title=Historic cities of the Islamic world|date=2007|publisher=Brill|others=Clifford Edmund Bosworth|isbn=978-90-474-2383-6|location=Leiden|oclc=308130674}} < /ref >
