 '''Brentor''' is a small [[village]] about {{convert|4|mi}} north of [[Tavistock]] within [[West Devon]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.visitdartmoor.co.uk/explore-dartmoor/brentor-p868973|title=Brentor, Devon|publisher=Visit Dartmoor|access-date=5 September 2015}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  The village is [[wikt:famous|famous]] for it's [[church]],  " Church of St Michel de Rupe "  (Saint Michael of the Rock). The church which dates back to the [[13th century]] sits high upon a [[tor]] overlooking the village. It can be seen from [[mile]]s around and is visited by many people each year. The rock it sits on is what is left of a [[volcano]] and is {{convert|1,130|ft|m}} high. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.devon.gov.uk/historicbrentor|title=Brentor|publisher=Devon County Council|access-date=5 September 2015|archive-date=10 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150910123521/http://www.devon.gov.uk/historicbrentor|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It was once used as a [[beacon]] [[tower]] to warn people of approaching enemy ships. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.britainexplorer.com/articles/item/285-brentor-church|title=Brentor Church - St. Michael de Rupe|publisher=www.britainexplorer.com |access-date=6 September 2015}} < /ref >
