 '''Ithaca''' ('''Ithaka''' or '''Ithaki'''; {{lang-el|Ιθάκη}}) is one of the [[Ionian Islands]] on the west [[coast]] of [[Greece]]. It is about {{convert|20|km|mi|0|lk=on}} long and {{convert|6|km|mi|1|abbr=on}} wide. In 2001 there were about 3,000 people living there. 
 
 [[Odysseus]] was said to be the [[king]] of Ithaca in the [[Epic poetry|epic]] [[poem]] the ''[[Odyssey]]'', which was written in the [[Ancient Greek language]]. It is possible that this [[Ancient history|ancient]] island is in a place different from the modern island.
