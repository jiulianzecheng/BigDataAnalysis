 '''Sardinia''' ({{lang-it|Sardegna}}, [[Sardinian language|Sardinian]]: ''Sardigna'') is the second largest island in the [[Mediterranean Sea]]. It is an [[Regions of Italy|autonomous region]] of [[Italy]]. It was formerly the [[Kingdom of Sardinia]]. 
 
 The official name is, in [[Italian language|Italian]], '''''Regione Autonoma della Sardegna'''''.The [[Capital city|capital]] and largest city is [[Cagliari]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The island of Sardinia has an area of {{convert|24090|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}} and, after [[Sicily]], is the second largest island in the [[Mediterranean Sea]]. It is {{convert|270|km|mi|abbr=on}} long from North to South, and {{convert|145|km|mi|abbr=on}} wide from West to East. It lies between 38° 51' and 41° 15' [[latitude]] north and 8° 8' and 9° 50' [[longitude]] east. 
 
 The island is on the western Mediterranean Sea, between the island of [[Corsica]] to the north and [[Tunisia]] to the south. The [[Tyrrhenian Sea]] part of the Mediterranean is directly east of Sardinia, between the Sardinian east coast and the west coast of the Italian [[mainland]] [[peninsula]]. 
 
 The [[Strait of Bonifacio]] ({{lang-co|Bucchi di Bunifaziu}}, {{lang-fr|Bouches de Bonifacio}}, [[Gallurese language|Gallurese]]: ''Bocchi di Bunifaciu'', {{lang-it|Bocche di Bonifacio}}) is the [[strait]] between [[Corsica]] and Sardinia, named after the Corsican town [[Bonifacio, Corse-du-Sud|Bonifacio]]. It is {{convert|11|km|mi|abbr=on}}  wide  < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.esa.int/esaEO/SEM7FTZ57NG_index_0.html |title= European Space Agency Observing the Earth |work=web page |publisher= European Space Agency |accessdate =4 October 2011}} < /ref >  and divides the [[Tyrrhenian Sea]] from the western [[Mediterranean Sea]]. 
 
 ===Rivers=== 
 Sardinia has few major rivers, the largest being the [[Tirso (river)|Tirso]], {{convert|151|km|mi|abbr=on}} long, which flows into the western Mediterranean Sea, the [[Coghinas]] ({{convert|115|km|mi|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}) and the [[Flumendosa]] ({{convert|127|km|mi|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}). 
 
 ===Mountains and valleys=== 
 The highest [[peak]] is [[Punta La Marmora]] ({{convert|1834|m|ft|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}), part of the [[Gennargentu]] group of mountains in the centre of the island. Other [[mountain chain]]s are [[Monte Limbara]] ({{convert|1362|m|ft|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}) in the northeast, the [[Monte Rasu|Chain of Marghine and Goceano]] ({{convert|1259|m|ft|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}) running crosswise for {{convert|40|km|abbr=off}} towards the north, the [[Monte Albo]] ({{convert|1057|m|ft|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}), the Sette Fratelli massif in the southeast, and the [[Sulcis Mountains]] and the Monte Linas ({{convert|1236|m|ft|0|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}). 
 
 The mountain chains of the island are separated by wide [[valley]]s; the main ones are [[Campidano]] in the southwest between [[Oristano]] and [[Cagliari]], and the [[Nurra]] in the northwest. 
 
 ===Islands=== 
 There are many small islands around the main island of Sardinia. Some of them are: 
 * [[Maddalena Archipelago]], to the northeast 
 * [[Asinara]], to the northwest 
 * [[San Pietro Island]], to the southwest 
 * [[Sant'Antioco]], to the southwest
