 '''Ares''' is a [[List of Greek gods and goddesses|god]] in [[Greek mythology]]. He is the god of savage [[war]] and [[bloodlust]], and represents the untamed, wild aspects of [[conflict]]. He is one of the [[Twelve Olympians]]. His parents are [[Zeus]] and [[Hera]]. He had a twin sister called [[Eris]]. Neither parent liked him. He is considered murderous and bloody. In the [[Trojan War]], he fought on the side of the [[Troy|Trojans]]. He had six children with [[Aphrodite]]. His homeland is [[Thrace]]. 
 
 ==Etymology== 
 Ares' name is believed to be derived from the [[Greek language|Greek]] word ἀρή (''arē''), the [[Ionic Greek|Ionic]] form of the [[Doric Greek|Doric]] ἀρά (''ara''), which translates to  " bane "  or  " ruin " . < ref > {{OEtymD|Ares}} < /ref > < ref > [http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.04.0073%3Aentry%3Da%29rh%2F ἀρή], Georg Autenrieth, ''A Homeric Dictionary''. {{LSJ|a)ra/|ἀρή|ref}}. < /ref >
