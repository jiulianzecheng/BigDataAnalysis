 The '''Ceratophryinae''', known as '''common horned frogs''', are a subfamily of the [[Leptodactylidae|leptodactylid]] [[frog]]s. All species live in [[South America]].   
 
 Fossils of the giant ''[[Beelzebufo]]'' from [[Cretaceous]] [[Madagascar]] suggest that the subfamily may have once ranged throughout the prehistoric [[supercontinent]] of [[Gondwana]]. < ref name= " nsf " > {{cite web |url= http://www.nsf.gov/news/news_summ.jsp?cntn_id=111119 & org=NSF & from=news |title= Scientists Discover 'Giant Fossil Frog from Hell' |publisher= [[National Science Foundation]] |last= Dybas |first= Cheryl |work= Press Release 08-025 |date= 2008-02-18 |access-date= 2008-02-19 }} < /ref >  Not all species in the subfamily have the horn-like growth at the eyes. All are [[carnivorous]], [[Terrestrial animal|terrestrial]] or semi-[[Aquatic animal|aquatic]].  They have large heads and mouths.  
 
 Some people enjoy keeping these frogs as pets.
