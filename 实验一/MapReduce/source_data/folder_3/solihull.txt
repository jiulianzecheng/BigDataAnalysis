 '''Solihull''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|ɒ|l|i|h|ʌ|l}}, {{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|oʊ|l|-}} or {{IPAc-en|ˌ|s|oʊ|l|i|ˈ|h|ʌ|l}}) is a large [[town]] in the [[West Midlands (county)|West Midlands]], [[England]] with a population of 123,187 in the [[United Kingdom Census 2011|2011 Census]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadKeyFigures.do?a=7 & b=6275235 & c=Solihull & d=13 & e=62 & g=6366884 & i=1001x1003x1032x1004 & o=362 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1450191598875 & enc=1|title=Local Authority population 2011|access-date=15 December 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151223122757/http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadKeyFigures.do?a=7 & b=6275235 & c=Solihull & d=13 & e=62 & g=6366884 & i=1001x1003x1032x1004 & o=362 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1450191598875 & enc=1|archive-date=23 December 2015|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >  [[Historic counties of England|Historically]] in [[Warwickshire]], it is a part of the West Midlands [[conurbation]]. It is the largest town in, and administrative centre of, the  [[Metropolitan Borough of Solihull]], which has a population of 209,890 people. Solihull is located {{convert|7.5|mi|km}} southeast of [[Birmingham]], {{convert|18|mi|km}} northwest of [[Warwick]] and {{convert|110|mi|km}} northwest of [[London]]. 
 
 Solihull is the most wealthy town of the West Midlands.
