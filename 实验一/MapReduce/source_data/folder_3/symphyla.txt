 '''Symphyla''' are [[soil]]-dwelling [[arthropod]]s in the [[subphylum]] [[Myriapoda]].  
 
 They are relatives of [[centipede]]s. Symphylans look like [[centipede]]s, but are smaller and translucent. They move through the pores between [[soil]] particles. They are found from the surface down to a depth of about 50 cm. They eat decaying vegetation, and can do harm in [[agriculture]] by eating seeds, roots, and [[root hair]]s in cultivated soil. 
 
 They feed on decayed vegetation.
