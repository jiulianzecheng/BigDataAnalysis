 {{Nihongo|'''Kotoshironushi'''|事代主神}}, is a deity worshipped in the Shinto religion. The deity is also known as {{Nihongo|'''Yae Kotoshironushi no kami'''|八重言代主神}}. 
 
 According to the ''[[Kojiki]]'', Kotoshironushi is the son of Ōkuninushi, who is the earthly deity of [[Izumo Province|Izumo province]] in Japan. When the heavenly deities sent Takemikazuchi to conquer Izumo, < ref name= " Kidder2007 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7pEOicqsjv0C & pg=PA116|title=Himiko and Japan's Elusive Chiefdom of Yamatai: Archaeology, History, and Mythology|last=Jonathan Edward Kidder|publisher=University of Hawaii Press|year=2007|isbn=978-0-8248-3035-9|page=116}} < /ref >  Ōkuninushi left the decision of whether to resist to his two sons. Kotoshironushi, who was fishing at the time of Takemikizuchi's arrival, agreed to accept the rule of the heavenly gods, surrendered his spear, and left Izumo. On the other hand, his brother Takeminakata fought against Takemikazuchi and was defeated. < ref name= " Hackin1932 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HAZrFhvqnTkC & pg=PA395|title=Asiatic Mythology: A Detailed Description and Explanation of the Mythologies of All the Great Nations of Asia|last=J. Hackin|publisher=Asian Educational Services|year=1932|isbn=978-81-206-0920-4|page=395}} < /ref > 
 
 
 Kotoshironushi is considered the main deity of the Asuka Shrine < ref name= " Ponsonby-Fane2014 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BY23AwAAQBAJ & pg=PA499|title=Studies In Shinto  &  Shrines|last=R. A. B. Ponsonby-Fane|date=3 June 2014|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-136-89294-3|pages=499–500}} < /ref >  and is linked to [[Ebisu]], < ref name= " HavensInoue2006 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=a_YQAQAAIAAJ|title=An Encyclopedia of Shinto (Shinto Jiten): Kami|last=Norman Havens|last2=Nobutaka Inoue|publisher=Institute for Japanese Culture and Classics Kokugakuin University|year=2006|isbn=978-4-905853-08-4|page=88}} < /ref >  another Shinto god. In Japanese mythology, Kotoshironushi served as an advisor to [[Empress Jingū]] during her invasion of Korea. He was also one of the eight deities responsible for protecting the Imperial Court. < ref name= " Aston2013 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pOZRAgAAQBAJ & pg=PT172|title=Shinto (The Way of the Gods)|last=William Aston|date=11 December 2013|publisher=Adegi Graphics LLC|isbn=978-0-543-01408-5|page=172}} < /ref >  He is also known as the father of [[Himetataraisuzu-hime]], who became the wife of [[Emperor Jimmu]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Atsushi|first=Kadoya|title=Kotoshironushi|url=http://eos.kokugakuin.ac.jp/modules/xwords/entry.php?entryID=95|access-date=14 September 2015|publisher=Encyclopedia of Shinto}} < /ref >
