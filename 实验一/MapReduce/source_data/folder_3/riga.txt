 '''Riga''' is the [[Capital (city)|capital city]] of the [[Europe]]an [[country]] of [[Latvia]]. Riga is on [[river]] [[Daugava River|Daugava]] near the [[Baltic Sea]].  The historical center of Riga is in the [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site|World Cultural Heritage List]] and is notable for its [[Art Nouveau]] architecture, which, according to UNESCO, has no equal in the world.  < ref > {{Cite web|last=Centre|first=UNESCO World Heritage|title=Historic Centre of Riga|url=https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/852/|access-date=2023-08-22|website=UNESCO World Heritage Centre|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[mayor]] of Riga was [[Mārtiņš Staķis]].  He resigned in July 2023.  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Staķis paziņo par atkāpšanos no Rīgas mēra amata|url=https://www.lsm.lv/raksts/zinas/latvija/03.07.2023-stakis-pazino-par-atkapsanos-no-rigas-mera-amata.a515143/|access-date=2023-08-22|website=www.lsm.lv|language=lv}} < /ref > 
 
 The current mayor of Riga is [[Vilnis Ķirsis]]. He became the mayor on July 5, 2023. 
 
 == History == 
 Riga was founded in 1201. It was a castle of the [[Teutonic Order]]. 
 
 In 1710 during the [[Great Northern War]] the city was incorporated to [[Russian Empire|Russia]]. Then it became a center of Courland province. 
 
 Since 1918 Riga has been the [[Capital (city)|capital]] city of Latvia, including [[Latvian Soviet Socialist Republic|Soviet Latvia]] in 1940-1991. 
 
 On June 30, 1941 German troops captured Riga. The city was liberated on October 15, 1944. 
 
 == People == 
 The Riga inhabitants are named “Rīdzinieki” on Latvian and “рижане”(rizhani) on Russian. 
 
 Most of the people by ethnic origin are Latvian (45%) and 40% are Russian. Historically the city had large [[Baltic Germans|German population]]. Among other ethnic groups there are [[Byelorussia|Byelorussian]] < nowiki/ > s, [[Poles]] and [[Jews.]] 
 
 In the 16th century, Riga was one of the largest cities on the [[Baltic Sea]] coast, with a population of about 16,000. The population fell to about 6000 in 1720, but grew rapidly later, reaching 517,000 in 1913. The world wars reduced the population. It reached its peak in 1990 — 909,135 people.  
 
 == Economy == 
 During the Soviet period the Riga wagon building factory made a very big number of local trains. 
 
 The [[Latvian National Theatre]] is in Riga. 
 
 ==Transportation == 
 The are 8 [[tram]] and 18 [[Trolleybus|trolley]] routes run by [[Rīgas Satiksme]].
