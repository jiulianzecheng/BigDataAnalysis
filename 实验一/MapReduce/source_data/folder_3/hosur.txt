'''Hosur''' is an [[industrial]] [[city]] in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[Tamil Nadu]]. It is the largest city in the [[Krishnagiri district|Krishnagiri District]]. Hosur is also a [[satellite town]] of the city of [[Bangalore|Bengaluru]].  [[Hosur (State Assembly Constituency)|Hosur assembly constituency]] is part of [[Krishnagiri (Lok Sabha constituency)|Krishnagiri Lok Sabha constituency]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://archive.eci.gov.in/se2001/background/S22/TN_ACPC.pdf |title=List of Parliamentary and Assembly Constituencies |access-date=2008-10-09 |publisher=Election Commission of India |format=PDF |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081031131000/http://archive.eci.gov.in/se2001/background/S22/TN_ACPC.pdf |archive-date=2008-10-31 }} < /ref >  It is also known as  " The Little England of India " . < ref > {{cite web|url=https://theculturetrip.com/asia/india/articles/hosur-the-little-england-of-india/|title=Hosur: 'The Little England' Of India|first=Anjali|last=Warrier|website=Culture Trip}} < /ref > 
 ==Population== 
 According to the [[2011 census of India|2011 census]], Hosur had a population of 116,821. The sex-ratio of the city is 968 females for every 1,000 males. This is much above the national average of 929. < ref name= " dashboard " > {{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}} < /ref >  The average [[literacy]] of the city was 76.69%. < ref name= " dashboard " / >
