 The '''Choctaw''' are a [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] people originally from the southeastern [[United States]], also called the [[Indigenous peoples of the Southeastern Woodlands|Southeastern Woodlands]]. This is today in [[Mississippi]] and [[Alabama]]. Their language is the Choctaw language. It is part of the [[Western Muskogean language|Muskogean]] family. It is also similar to [[Chickasaw]]. Three tribes are recognized today. They are the [[Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma]], [[Mississippi Band of Choctaw Indians]], and [[Jena Band of Choctaw Indians]] in Louisiana. < ref > " Indian Entities Recognized by and Eligible To Receive Services From the United States Bureau of Indian Affairs " . ''Federal Register''. US Department of the Interior. January 29, 2021. pp. 7554–58. < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 The people were originally [[mound builders]]. Early Choctaw lived in thatched-roof cabins of logs in communities. Agriculture was the main supply of food, such as the [[Three Sisters (agriculture)|Three Sisters]]. There was also fishing and hunting. Sports were also important and often replaced war.  
 
 The tribe supported the Americans during the [[American Revolutionary War|American Revolution]], the [[War of 1812]] and the [[Creek War]]. Europeans thought the tribe was [[Civilization|civilized]]. It was part of the so-called   " [[Five Civilized Tribes]] "  of the Southeast. < ref > Zinn, Howard (2003).  " As Long as Grass Grows or Water Runs " . ''A People's History of the United States: 1492–Present''. HarperCollins. p. 126. [[ISBN (identifier)|ISBN]] [[Special:BookSources/0-06-052842-7| < bdi > 0-06-052842-7 < /bdi > ]]. < /ref >   
 
 Most of the tribe was forced out of their land from 1831 to 1833. This was part of the [[Indian Removal Act|Indian Removal]]. The remaining Choctaw became [[Citizenship|citizens]]. The tribe supported the [[Confederate States of America|Confederates]] in the [[American Civil War|U.S. Civil War]]. Several chiefs of the Choctaw were at the US [[Bureau of Indian Affairs]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last1=Tupelo|first1=Mailing Address: 2680 Natchez Trace Parkway|last2=Thanksgiving|first2=MS 38804 Phone: 800 305-7417 The Visitor Center is open during normal business hours seven days a week The visitor center is closed|last3=December 25th|last4=Us|first4=January 1st Contact|title=Early Choctaw History - Natchez Trace Parkway (U.S. National Park Service)|url=https://www.nps.gov/natr/learn/historyculture/choctaw.htm|access-date=2022-07-28|website=www.nps.gov|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Choctaw worked as [[Code talker|codetalkers]] during [[World War I]]. They used their own Choctaw language.  The Choctaw reorganized after the [[Indian Reorganization Act]].
