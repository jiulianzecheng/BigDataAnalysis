 '''Assassination''' is the [[murder]] of an important or popular person, usually, a [[politics|political]] leader, like the head of a [[country]] or of a [[political party]]. Assassinations are usually done for political reasons or [[contract killing|for payment]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://thelawdictionary.org/assassination/ |title=What is Assassination? |author= < !--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-- >  |website=The Law Dictionary |date=4 November 2011 |publisher=Black’s Law Dictionary Free 2nd Ed. |access-date=March 9, 2016 }} < /ref >   A person who '''assassinates''' someone is called an '''assassin'''. 
 
 Throughout history, assassinations have happened for many different reasons. Sometimes, assassinations have been used to [[coup d'etat|take over governments]]. Other times, assassinations have been used to kill [[military]] leaders during [[war]]s, or for [[religious]] reasons. Some assassins want [[revenge]], or just want to be [[wikt:famous|famous]].

 ==[[Statistics]]== 
 One [[research]] study looked at assassinations of national leaders (leaders of countries, like presidents or prime ministers).  The study showed that: < ref name=hitormiss > {{Cite report |author=Jones, Benjamin F.; Olken, Benjamin A. |date=May 2007 |title=Hit or Miss? The Effect of Assassinations on Institutions and War |url=http://www.nber.org/papers/w13102.pdf |publisher=National Bureau of Economic Research |pages=1–2 |access-date=March 9, 2016 }} < /ref > 
 * Since 1895, assassins have tried to kill national leaders 298 different times.  59 of those world leaders were killed. 
 * Since 1950, a national leader has been assassinated in nearly two out of every three years. 
 
 Another study looked at all assassinations between 1946 and 2013.  It included not just national leaders, but also other members of government (including [[local government]]s), and people who were against the government.  This study found that between 1946 and 2013, a total of 954 people were assassinated in 758 different attacks. < ref name=westpt/ > 
 
 This study also looked at who was most likely to be assassinated. Of the 954 people who were assassinated: < ref name=westpt/ > 
 * 21% were members of [[parliament]] 
 * 18% were opposition leaders (leaders of [[political party|political parties]] or groups that spoke out or fought against the government) 
 * 17% were national leaders 
 * 14% were government [[Minister (government)|ministers]] 
 * 10% were [[diplomat]]s 
 * 5% were local [[politician]]s, like [[governor]]s or [[mayor|mayors]]
