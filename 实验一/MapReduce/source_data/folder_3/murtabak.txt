'''Murtabak''' or '''motabbaq''' ({{lang-ar|مطبق}}) is a kind of [[pancake]]. It is filled with ingredients, such as chopped meat and vegetables. < ref name= " Kruger-Balinese Food " > {{cite book|author=Vivienne Kruger|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WNHZAwAAQBAJ & q=Martabak+pancake & pg=PT70|title=Balinese Food: The Traditional Cuisine  &  Food Culture of Bali|publisher=Tuttle Publishing|year=2014|page=62|isbn=9781462914234}} < /ref >  It is a [[street food]] in [[Yemen]], [[Indonesia]], [[Malaysia]], [[Thailand]], and [[Singapore]]. < ref name= " LP-Murtabak " > {{cite book | title = The World's Best Street Food: Where to Find it  &  How to Make it | author = Lonely Planet Food| publisher = Lonely Planet | year = 2012 | isbn = 9781743216644| page = 108 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Wat9BAAAQBAJ & q=Murtabak & pg=PT109}} < /ref > < ref name= " Von Holzen " > {{cite book | title = A New Approach to Indonesian Cooking | author = Heinz Von Holzen | publisher = Marshall Cavendish International Asia Pte Ltd | year = 2014 | isbn = 9789814634953| page = 129 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=pRuiBQAAQBAJ & pg=PA129}} < /ref > 
 
 Murtabak was first made by [[India|Indians]] in Yemen. It was brought to [[Southeast Asia]] by Indian traders. < ref name= " Von Holzen " / >
