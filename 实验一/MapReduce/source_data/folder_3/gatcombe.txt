 '''Gatcombe''' is a village and [[civil parish]] < ref > [http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20060215045147/http://www.statistics.gov.uk/geography/geographic_area_listings/downloads/EnglishParishes & WelshCommunities_N & C_2004.xls English Parishes  &  Welsh Communities N & C 2004] < /ref >  on the [[Isle of Wight]]. It is four kilometres from [[Newport, Isle of Wight|Newport]] in the centre of the island. [[Saint Olaf|St. Olave's]] Church is a Gatcombe church dedicated in 1292, serving as chapel to [[Gatcombe Manor]]. The Gatcombe parish was established in 1560. 
 
 A carved wooden effigy of [[Edward Estur]], a local knight who fought in the Crusades, is in the church.
