 The '''Charango''' is an instrument somewhat resembling a [[guitar]], or a [[lute]]. It came from the Altiplano in South America, in the 16th century. It may have come from the city of [[Potosi, Bolivia|Potosi]], in [[Bolivia]]. 
 Usually, it has a length of {{Convert|23|cm}}, which makes it smaller than an [[Ukulele|Ukulele]]. Larger versions have been made too. It usually has four chords, and is plucked like a [[mandolin]].
