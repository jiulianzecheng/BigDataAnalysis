 '''Mexicans''' are citizens of [[Mexico]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/mexican|title= 
 Mexican definition and meaning}} < /ref > 
 
 There is a large Mexican diaspora in the [[United States]]. More than 38.5 million Mexican Americans and Mexican-born immigrants live in the United States. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.migrationpolicy.org/article/mexican-immigrants-united-states-2019|title=Mexican Immigrants in the United States}} < /ref >  There is also a significant Mexican diaspora in [[Canada]], [[Spain]], [[Germany]], the [[United Kingdom]], [[Bolivia]], [[Argentina]], [[Switzerland]], the [[Netherlands]] and [[Costa Rica]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.gob.mx/sre/articulos/do-you-know-how-many-mexicans-live-abroad|title=Do you know how many Mexicans live abroad?}} < /ref > 
 
 Most Mexicans are racially [[mestizo]], a mixture of [[Spaniards|Spanish]] and [[Native Americans|Native American]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://minorityrights.org/country/mexico/|title= 
 Mexico - World Directory of Minorities  &  Indigenous Peoples}} < /ref > 
 
 Mexicans of European descent, including those who immigrated during the 20th century, are concentrated in Mexico City, and in the West. Mexicans of European descent and other lighter-skinned Mexicans dominate the wealthiest echelons of Mexican society. < ref > [https://www.britannica.com/place/Mexico/Ethnic-groups Mexico - Ethnic groups] < /ref >  Most white Mexicans are of Spanish ancestry. 
 
 Mexico has the largest [[Native American|indigenous]] population in Latin America. < ref > [https://minorityrights.org/minorities/indigenous-peoples-4/ Indigenous peoples] < /ref >   1.38 million Mexicans are Afro-Mexicans. The majority of Afro-Mexicans live in Veracruz and the Costa Chica region. Afro-Mexicans were brought to Mexico as slaves from Africa during the Spanish colonial era. < ref > [https://minorityrights.org/minorities/afro-mexicans/ Afro-Mexicans] < /ref > 
 ==Culture== 
 Mexican culture is mainly influenced by Spanish and Amerindian cultures. Mexico is famous for it’s [[Mexican food|Mexican cuisine]]. Mexican cuisine is influenced by Spanish cuisine and indigenous cuisine. The indigenous people of Mexico have introduced [[vanilla]], [[corn]], [[chocolate]], [[avocado]] and other important crops to the world. Mexico is also known for its [[tequila]]. [[Day of the Dead]] is a holiday observed by Mexicans. The most practiced religion by Mexicans is [[Christianity]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.livescience.com/38647-mexican-culture.html|title=Mexican culture: Customs and traditions}} < /ref >
