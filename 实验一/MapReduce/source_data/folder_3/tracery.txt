'''Tracery''' is a [[device]] used in [[architecture]] by which windows (or [[Screen|screens]], panels, and vaults) are divided into different parts by [[stone]] bars. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/907380369|title=The Oxford dictionary of architecture|last=Curl|first=James Stevens|date=2015|others=Susan Wilson|isbn=978-0-19-967498-5|edition=Third|location=New York|oclc=907380369}} < /ref >  It most commonly refers to the stoneworks that support the [[glass]] in a [[window]]. Tracery can also be found on the inside and outside of [[Building|buildings]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/941907107|title=A world history of art : a History|last=Honour|first=Hugh|date=2009|others=John Fleming|isbn=978-1-78067-117-8|edition=Revised seventh|location=London|oclc=941907107}} < /ref >
