 '''Allier''' is a [[Departments of France|department]] in the centre of [[France]], in the [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It is named after the [[river]] [[Allier (river)|Allier]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Loire (river)|Loire]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] is [[Moulins, Allier|Moulins]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:Allier.jpg|left|thumb|220px|Map of Allier.]] 
 Allier is one of the original 83 departments created during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with Moulins as its [[Capital (city)|capital]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/03his.html |title=Historique de l'Allier |work=Le SPLAF |language=fr |access-date=22 December 2014 }} < /ref >  It was formed from most of all the former [[province]] of ''Bourbonnais'' (the [[Duchy]] of Bourbonnais); [[Saint-Amand-Montrond]], in the [[Cher (department)|Cher]] department, was the only part of the old province that was not made part of Allier. 
 
 It was divided in seven [[district]]s: Cérilly, Moulins, Le Donjon, Cusset, Gannat, Montmarault and Montluçon. 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the seven districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Moulins, Gannat, Lapalisse and Montluçon. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Gannat was eliminated and in 1941 Vichy became the capital of one ''arrondissement'' of the department instead of Lapalisse. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 In 1940, during [[World War II]], the city of [[Vichy]] was the [[Capital (city)|capital]] of [[Vichy France]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Allier is part of the [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|7340.1|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 It is {{Convert|130|km|mi|abbr=on }} long from east to west and {{Convert|90|km|mi|abbr=on }} wide from north to south. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.allier.fr/1416-geographie-du-departement-de-l-allier.htm |title=Géographie du département de l'Allier |work=Allier.fr |language=fr |access-date=22 December 2014 }} < /ref > 
 
 The department borders with 6 other departments in 4 regions: 
 * [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] region 
 ** [[Nièvre]] (north) 
 ** [[Saône-et-Loire]] (east) 
 * [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] region 
 ** [[Loire]] (southeast) 
 ** [[Puy-de-Dôme]] (south) 
 * [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] region 
 ** [[Creuse]] (west) 
 * [[Centre-Val de Loire]] region 
 ** [[Cher (department)|Cher]] (northwest)

 There are four main [[Geography|geographical]] zones in the department: 
 # The '''Bourbonnais [[Bocage]]''' covers about one third of the departement, covering most of the western and central parts of the department.  
 # The '''Bourbonnais Sologne''' is to the east of the department, between the valley of the river [[Allier (river)|Allier]] and the borders of [[Nièvre]] and [[Saône-et-Loire]] departments in [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]]. 
 # The '''Bourbonnais Mountains''' are in the southeast of the department. In these mountains, where the departments Allier, [[Puy-de-Dome]] and [[Loire (department)|Loire]] meet, is the highest point of the department, [[Puy de Montoncel]] ({{Coord|45|55|51|N|3|41|39|E|name=Puy de Montoncel|display=inline|region:ID_type:mountain}}); it is {{Convert|1287|m|ft|0|abbr=on }} high. < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9702 |title=Puy de Montoncel, France|publisher=Peakbagger.com |access-date=18 September 2017 }} < /ref > 
 # The '''Bourbonnais Limagne''', commonly called as ''Val d'Allier'' ( " Valley of the Allier " ), found on both sides of the river between [[Vichy]] and [[Saint-Pourçain-sur-Sioule]]. 
 
 The main rivers in the department are: 
 * [[Cher (river)|Cher]] in the western part of the department. 
 * [[Allier (river)|Allier]] and its tributary the [[Sioule]], in the centre. 
 * [[Loire River|Loire]] and its tributary the [[Besbre]], in the east. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at [[Nevers]] is an  " Oceanic climate "  (also known as Maritime Temperate climate) and of the subtype  " Cfb " . < ref > {{cite web|publisher=Annuaire-Mairie.fr |title=Le département de l'Allier |url=http://www.annuaire-mairie.fr/info-departement-allier.html |access-date=22 December 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 There are two periods of maximum precipitation in June and October and a minimum in January and February with average of {{Convert|694|mm|in|abbr=on }} [[Montluçon]] ([[altitude]]: 207 m), {{Convert|763|mm|in|abbr=on }} in [[Moulins, Allier|Moulins]] (245 m) {{Convert|778|mm|in|abbr=on }} in [[Vichy]] (251 m); and {{Convert|791|mm|in|abbr=on }} in [[Lapalisse]] (285 m).

 ==Administration== 
 Allier is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Allier'' in [[Moulins, Allier|Moulins]]. The department is part of the [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] region.

 ==Demographics== 
 There is not a traditional [[demonym]] to name the inhabitants of the Allier department. Some names used are ''Alliérins'', ''Bourbonnais'', ''Elavérins'', or simply ''habitants de l'Allier'' ( " inhabitants of Allier " ). 
 
 Of these names, the most used is ''Bourbonnais'' followed by ''habitants de l'Allier''. 
 
 Allier had a population, in 2014, of 343,062, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|343062|7340.1|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Vichy, with 122,267 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with more inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
