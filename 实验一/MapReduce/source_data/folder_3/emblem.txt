 An '''emblem''' is a pictorial [[image]] either [[abstract art|abstract]] or representational. It represents a [[Armorial of sovereign states]] [[concept]] — for example, a [[moral]] [[truth]], or an [[allegory]] — or a person, such as a [[king]] or [[saint]]. < ref name= " OED " > {{Cite book|url=http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/symbol|title=Oxford English Dictionary|last1=Simpson|first1=John|last2=Weiner|first2=Edmund|publisher=Oxford University Press|year=1989|isbn=0-19-861186-2|place=United Kingdom|author-link=|author2-link=|access-date=2011-08-16|archive-date=2007-02-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070223041647/http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/symbol|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  For example, the [[scallop]] [[Animal shell|shell]] is the traditional emblem of [[James, son of Zebedee]]. Pilgrims wore it on their hat or clothes to show that they were travelling to the shrine at [[Santiago de Compostela]].
