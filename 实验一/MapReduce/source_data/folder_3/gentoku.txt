{{nihongo|'''''Gentoku'''''|元徳}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号|nengō|lit. year name}} after ''[[Karyaku]]''. < ref name= " gentoku " > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA507 & dq= " ''Gentoku'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia,'' p. 507. < /ref >    
 
 Gentoku was a ''nengō'' of the [[Northern Court]] during the pre-[[Nanboku-chō period]] after Karyaku and before [[Shōkyō]]. This period started in August 1329 < ref name= " gentoku " / >  and ended in April 1332. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA883 & dq= " ''Shokei'' (or ''Shōkyō'') " ] at p. 883. < /ref >   
 
 In the Southern Court, but Gentoku lasted until only August 1331 when [[Genkō (era)|''Genkō'']] started. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA883 & dq= " ''Genkō'' " ] at p. 238. < /ref >   After 1331, the [[pretender]] in Kyoto was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Kōgon]]|光厳天皇|''Kōgon-tennō''}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA543 & dq=  " Kōgon Tennō " ] at p. 543. < /ref >   Kōgon's [[Southern Court]] rival in [[Yoshino, Nara|Yoshino]] during this time was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Daigo]]|後醍醐天皇|Go-Diago-tennō}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA883 & dq= " Go-Daigo Tennō " ] at p. 251. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Gentoku'' era== 
 * '''27 March 1330''' (''Gentoku 2, 8th day of the 3rd month'') < !-- NengoCalc 元徳二年三月八日  -- > : Go-Daigo visited [[Tōdai-ji]] and [[Kōfuku-ji]] in [[Nara, Nara|Nara]]. < ref > [[Helen Craig McCullough|McCullough, Helen Craig]]. (2004). ''The Taiheiki'', p.28. < /ref >
