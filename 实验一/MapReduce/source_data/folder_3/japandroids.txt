 '''Japandroids''' is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[Rock music|rock]] [[band]] from [[Vancouver]], [[British Columbia]].   
 
 ==Career== 
 The band was formed in 2006 by Brian King and David Prowse. King and Prowse met each other in 2000 while they were attending the [[University of Victoria]] in [[Victoria (Australian state)|Victoria]]. < ref name= " exclaim " > {{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/japandroids-no_fun_city_rockers|access-date=2015-09-01|title=No Fun City Rockers|publisher=[[Exclaim!]]}} < /ref >  They started going to live shows together regularly in Victoria and Vancouver after they found out that they had a mutual interest in music. In 2003, Prowse moved to [[Vancouver]] and transferred to [[Simon Fraser University]]. After King graduated in 2005, he moved to Vancouver to form the band with Prowse. The band was influenced by [[The Sonics]] and they started writing music in 2006. < ref name= " exclaim " / >  The band got their name from a combination of two other band name ideas: Japanese Scream and Pleasure Droids. < ref name= " exclaim " / > 
 
 The band self-released two [[Extended play|EP]]s: ''All Lies'' in 2007 and ''Lullaby Death Jams'' in 2008. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/japandroids_jpndrds-all_lies|access-date=2015-09-01|title=All Lies Review|publisher=Exclaim!}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/japandroids_jpndrds-lullaby_death_jams|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Lullaby Death Jams Review|publisher=Exclaim!}} < /ref >  The EPs were only limited to 500 copies each but were re-released as a compilation titled ''[[No Singles]]'' in 2010. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/14228-no-singles/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Japandroids: No Singles|publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]}} < /ref > 
 
 Their first [[studio album]] titled ''[[Post-Nothing]]'' was released on April 28, 2009 through Unfamiliar Records and re-released on August 4, 2009 on [[Polyvinyl Record Co.]] In March 2009, the website [[Pitchfork Media]] named the song  " Young Hearts Spark Fire "  as a 'Best New Track'. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/11067-young-hearts-spark-fire/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Single Review: Young Hearts Spark Fire|publisher=Pitchfork|archive-date=2009-03-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090312072123/http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/11067-young-hearts-spark-fire|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  This helped the band gain more recognition outside of Canada. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.straight.com/article-220263/local-boys-make-good|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Vancouver's own Japandroids make good|publisher=[[The Georgia Straight]]}} < /ref > 
 
 The band's second studio album ''[[Celebration Rock]]'' was released on June 5, 2012. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/45701-japandroids-talk-new-album/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Japandroids Talk New Album|publisher=Pitchfork}} < /ref >  The album's first single  " [[The House That Heaven Built]] "  was released on May 15, 2012. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/45908-japandroids-share-new-song-tour-dates/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Japandroids Share New Song, Tour Dates|publisher=Pitchfork Media}} < /ref > 
 
 The album received positive reviews. Ian Cohen of [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] said,  " Japandroids have gone from having almost none at all [lyrics] to packing their songs with an astonishing command of legend and literalism that all but dares you to feel ''something''. " < ref > {{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/16564-japandroids-celebration-rock/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Celebration Rock Review|publisher=Pitchfork Media}} < /ref >  [[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] named Japandroids 2012's ''Band of the Year'' and rated the album a 9 out of 10. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/articles/japandroids-band-year-2012-celebration-rock/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Band of the Year: Japandroids|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/reviews/japandroids-celebration-rock-polyvinyl/|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Japandroids - Celebration Rock (Polyvinyl)|publisher=Spin}} < /ref >  [[Rolling Stone]] rated it a 4 out of 5 and listed the album as #10 on their ''The 10 Coolest Summer Albums of All Time'' list. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/celebration-rock-20120605|access-date=2015-09-01|title=Japandroids Celebration Rock Album Review|publisher=[[Rolling Stone]]|archive-date=2015-06-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150628100838/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/celebration-rock-20120605|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/the-10-coolest-summer-albums-of-all-time-20120718/japandroids-celebration-rock-2012-20120717|access-date=2015-09-01|title=The 10 Coolest Summer Albums of All Time|publisher=Rolling Stone|archive-date=2017-08-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170816180321/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/the-10-coolest-summer-albums-of-all-time-20120718/japandroids-celebration-rock-2012-20120717|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
