'''Djedi''' was an Egyptian [[prince]] who lived during the 4th dynasty of Egypt. He was a son of [[Rahotep|Prince Rahotep]] and [[Nofret]] and had two brothers and three sisters. < ref > [[W. M. Flinders Petrie]]: ''Medum'', London 1892, plate XV (online: [https://archive.org/details/cu31924028670465]) < /ref >  He is depicted in the tomb chapels of his parents and has the title of  " King's Acquaintance " . < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/completeroyalfam0000dods_f9x1?view=theater|title=The Complete Royal Families Of Ancient Egypt With Over 300 Illustrations 90 In Color|last=Dodson|first=Aidan|pages=53-61}} < /ref > 
 
 In an ancient Egyptian tale,  " Khufu and the Magicians " , mention a magician called Djedi or Dedi, and it is possible that this person called Dedi was inspired by the real prince Djedi, [[Khufu|Khufu's]] nephew. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.thekeep.org/~kunoichi/kunoichi/themestream/egypt_magic.html |title=Tales of Magic in Ancient Egypt |access-date=2023-12-03 |archive-date=2010-06-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100612181359/http://www.thekeep.org/~kunoichi/kunoichi/themestream/egypt_magic.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
