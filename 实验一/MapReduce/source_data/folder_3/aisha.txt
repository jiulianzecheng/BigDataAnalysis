 '''Aisha bint Abu Bakr''' (604-678; {{lang-ar|عائشة بنت أبوبکر}}) was the third wife of [[Muhammad]]. Aisha is given honour in [[Islam]], especially in the [[Sunni]] branch. She is often called by Muslims ''Umm-al-mu'minin'' which means  " the Mother of the Believers " . 
 
 Aisha was the daughter of [[Abu Bakr]] of [[Mecca]].  Aisha was six years into puberty when she was betrothed to Muhammad. (Marriage under 21 years into puberty was common worldwide at the time) < ref name= " Spellberg " > [[Denise Spellberg|D. A. Spellberg]], ''Politics, Gender, and the Islamic Past: the Legacy of A'isha bint Abi Bakr'', [[Columbia University Press]], 1994, p. 40 < /ref > < ref name= " Armstrong " > Karen Armstrong, ''Muhammad: A Biography of the Prophet'', Harper San Franscisco, 1992, p. 157. < /ref >  She was not Muhammad's only wife. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.nndb.com/people/673/000044541/|title=Prophet Muhammad|website=www.nndb.com}} < /ref >   
 
 Her ten-year marriage to Muhammad ended when he died.  Aisha lived fifty more years in and around [[Medina]].   Much of her time was spent learning the [[Quran]], and the [[Sunnah]] of Muhammad.
