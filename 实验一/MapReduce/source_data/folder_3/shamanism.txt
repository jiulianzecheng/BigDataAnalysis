 '''Shamanism''' is a practice where a practitioner reaches [[altered state of consciousness|altered states of consciousness]]. Supposedly,  the [[shaman]]  perceives and interacts with a spirit world, and channels these  energies into this world. < ref > Hoppál, Mihály 2007. Is shamanism a folk religion?. ''Shamans and Traditions'', vol 13. ''Bibliotheca Shamanistica''. Budapest: Akadémiai Kiadó. {{ISBN|978-963-05-8521-7}} < /ref > 
 
 A shaman is a person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of good and bad spirits. They usually enter into a [[trance|trance state]] during a [[ritual]], and do [[divination]] and [[healing]]. < ref > [http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/shaman?view=uk Oxford Dictionary Online]{{Dead link|date=September 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}. < /ref >  To become a shaman, a person has to have had a [[near death experience]] (a  " ''[[shamanic illness]]'' " ). < ref name= " natgeo "  / > 
 
 The name  " shaman "  comes from the [[Evenki]] people in [[Siberia]]. < ref name= " natgeo " > {{cite web|last=Stern|first=David|title=Shamans: Masters of Ecstasy|url=http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2012/12/shamans/stern-text|publisher=National Geographic|accessdate=25 February 2016|archive-date=17 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160317162626/http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2012/12/shamans/stern-text|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 '''Shamans''' play a central role in these movements, as they can be in both worlds, this one, and the world of [[spirit]]s. 
 
 ==Beliefs== 
 
 * Spirits are real and can do things in the real world. 
 * There are [[good]] and [[evil]] spirits. 
 * [[Heal]]ing can be done in the spirit and real world. 
 * A person's spirit can leave their body and go into the world of the spirits. 
 * Animal shapes are used to [[interact]] with the spirits. 
 
 Shamanism is different from [[animism]].
