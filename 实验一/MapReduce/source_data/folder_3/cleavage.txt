 '''Cleavage''' is the low-cut in women's [[clothing]], partially revealing the women's [[Breast|breasts]]. It is also used with an [[Eroticism|erotic]] purpose.    
 
 In 1805, in [[geology]] and [[mineralogy]], cleavage meant  " tendency (of rocks or [[Jewel|gems]]) to break cleanly along natural fissures. "  General meaning  " action or state of cleaving or being cleft "  is from 1867. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=Cleavage|url=https://www.etymonline.com/word/cleavage|access-date=2021-07-27|website=Etymology Online|language=en}} < /ref >     
 
 The sense of  " cleft between a woman's breasts in low-cut clothing "  is first recorded 1946, defined in a  " [[Time (magazine)|Time]] "  magazine article. < ref name= " :0 "  / >
