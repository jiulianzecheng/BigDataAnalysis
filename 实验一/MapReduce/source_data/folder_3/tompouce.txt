 A '''tompouce''' or '''tompoes''' is a [[pastry]] often eaten in [[Netherlands|the Netherlands]] or [[Belgium]]. It is named after Admiraal Tom Pouce, the [[stage name]] of a [[Friesland|Frisian]] actor with [[dwarfism]] called Jan Hannema. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=geschiedenis van de tompouce - HEMA|url=https://www.hema.nl/on/demandware.store/Sites-HemaNL-Site/nl_NL/Search-ShowContent?fdid=NL_folder_geschiedenis_tompouce|access-date=2023-05-08|website=www.hema.nl|language=nl-NL}} < /ref >  The tompoes is a different form of a [[mille-feuille]], otherwise known as a Napoleon. 
 
 == History == 
 
 === Name === 
 The first tompouce was made by a baker in [[Amsterdam]] in 1858. The actor it was named after had gotten his name from another actor, Charles Sherwood Stratton. Stratton called himself Tom Thumb, the literal translation of Tom Pouce. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  [[Tom Thumb]] is a [[fairy tale]] about a small man. 
 
 == What it's known for == 
 
 === Colour === 
 [[File:Oranje tompouce.jpg|thumb|An orange tompouce, a special colour for a special occasion.]] 
 The tompouce is often associated with the day the people of the Netherlands celebrate the birthday of their [[monarch]] (In Dutch: ''Koningsdag''; King's Day). The colour of the icing on top of the pastry is changed for this event. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Waarom eten we op Koningsdag een tompouce?|url=https://www.npo3.nl/best-bites/waarom-eten-we-op-koningsdag-een-tompouce|access-date=2023-05-08|website=npo3.nl}} < /ref >  The icing is normally pink, but it is changed to orange. Orange is the national colour of the Netherlands. The orange tompouce is also eaten during large events the Netherlands takes part in, such as the [[FIFA World Cup]].  
 
 === Eating === 
 The tompouce is known for being difficult to eat. < ref > {{cite web|last1=Schravesande|first1=Freek|date=30 August 2007|title=Hoe eet je een tompoes?|trans-title=How do you eat a tompouce?|url=http://vorige.nrc.nl/krant/article1833184.ece|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407085347/http://vorige.nrc.nl/krant/article1833184.ece|archive-date=7 April 2014|website=NRC|language=nl}} < /ref >  When you try to put a fork in it, the cream on the inside tends to spill out. Because of this, the Dutch have come up with many different ways to eat the pastry.
