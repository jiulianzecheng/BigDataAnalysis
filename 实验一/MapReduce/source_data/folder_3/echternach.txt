 '''Echternach''' ({{lang-lb|Iechternach}}) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] in eastern [[Luxembourg]], [[Capital city|capital]] of the [[Echternach (canton)|Echternach]] canton.  It is the oldest town with [[Town privileges|city rights]] in Luxembourg. 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:St. Willibrord Basilika, Echternach-3711.jpg|left|thumb|[[Basilica]] of Echternach]] 
 The town grew around the walls of the [[Monastery|Abbey]] of Echternach, which was founded in  698  by [[Willibrord|St. Willibrord]], an English [[monk]] from [[Ripon]], [[Northumbria]] (in present-day [[North Yorkshire]], [[England]]), who became the first [[bishop]] of [[Utrecht]] and worked to [[Christianization|Christianize]] the [[Frisia]]ns. 
 
 The abbey were closed during the [[French Revolution|French revolution]] in 1797. Echternach was given city rights in the year 1236. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Echternach is in the east of the country near the border with [[Germany]], on the banks of the [[Saar River]]. 
 
 The ''commune'' has an area of {{Convert|20.5|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name=stats / >  The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|154|-|393|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. At the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude  is {{Convert|230|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |accessdate= 10 June 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Echternach is surrounded by four ''communes'' of Luxembourg: [[Rosport]] to the east and south, [[Bech]] to the southwest, and [[Berdorf]] and [[Consdorf]] to the west. To the north, the ''commune'' borders the [[Echternacherbrück]] [[municipality]] of the  [[Bitburg-Prüm]] district in the [[Germany|German]] [[States of Germany|state]] of [[Rhineland-Palatinate]]; and to the northwest the [[Bollendorf]] municipality, also in the Bitburg-Prüm district of the Rhineland-Palatinate.
