 '''Dundalk''' ({{lang-ga|Dún Dealgan}}) is a town in [[County Louth]], [[Republic of Ireland]], near the border with [[Northern Ireland]]. It takes its name from {{lang|ga|Dún Dealgan}},  " Dalga's [[Fortification|fort]] "  and officially became a town in 1189. It is located at a [[ford]] on the [[Castletown River]]. The town's crest reads  " Mé do rug Cú Chulainn Cróga "   (I gave birth to brave Cú Chulainn) 
 Dundalk is twinned with the town of [[Rezé]] in France. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://dundalkreze.com/index.htm |title=Dundalk - Reze twinning page |access-date=2008-09-30 |archive-date=2007-09-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070930081703/http://dundalkreze.com/index.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 Dundalk has a large population of 39,004 inhabitants.[http://www.cso.ie/census/documents/census2006_volume_1_pop_classified_by_area.pdf]  In 2003, Dundalk was amongst nine cities and towns to be given  " Gateway status "  in the Irish Government's  " National Spatial Strategy " .  " Gateway towns "  will be the first to get funding for improving their towns. < ref > [http://www.irishspatialstrategy.ie/ Irish Spatial Strategy] < /ref >
