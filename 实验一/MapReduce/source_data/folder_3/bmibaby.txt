 '''Bmibaby Limited''' known as '''bmibaby.com''' was a British [[low-cost airline]]. It flew to places in the [[United Kingdom|UK]] and [[Europe]] from its bases at [[George Best Belfast City Airport|Belfast City]], [[Birmingham Airport, England|Birmingham]] and [[East Midlands Airport|East Midlands]] airports. It was owned by [[British Midland International]], which itself is owned was [[Lufthansa]]. In April 2012, BMI was bought by the [[International Airlines Group]] and the airline, along with bmibaby, stopped flying.  < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-17940539|title=BMI Baby to be grounded, says IAG|work=BBC News |date=3 May 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 Bmibaby had a [[Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)|United Kingdom Civil Aviation Authority]] Type A Operating Licence. It was allowed to carry passengers, cargo and mail on aircraft with 20 or more seats. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Operating licences {{!}} Civil Aviation Authority|url=https://www.caa.co.uk/commercial-industry/airlines/licensing/licence-types/operating-licences/#:~:text=A%20Type%20A%20Operating%20Licence,with%2019%20or%20fewer%20seats.|access-date=2022-06-13|website=www.caa.co.uk}} < /ref >
