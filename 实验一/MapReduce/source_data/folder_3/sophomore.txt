'''Sophomore''' is a term used in the [[United States]] to describe a [[student]] in the second year of study (generally referring to high school or university study). < ref > {{cite web|title=Concise Oxford English Dictionary|url=http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/sophomore?view=uk|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|access-date=2023-01-24|archive-date=2007-09-29|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929083414/http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/sophomore?view=uk|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=Merriam-Webster online|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sophomore%5B1%5D|publisher=[[Merriam-Webster]]}} < /ref >   The word is also sometimes used in the USA as [[jargon]] for the second album released by a musician or group, the second movie of a director, or the second season of a professional athlete. < ref > {{cite web|title=Merriam-Webster online|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sophomore%5B2%5D|publisher=[[Merriam-Webster]]}} < /ref > 
 
 The word comes  from [[Greek language|Greek]], ''sophisma'' ‘clever device’ from ''sophizesthai'' ‘to devise, become wise’, and the original English spelling was  " Sophumer " .  < ref name= " oxed " > " Your Argument..is so sillie, as that not some exq[u]isite Sophister, but any punie Sophumer may at first sight discover the feebleness of it. "  {{cite book|url=http://dictionary.oed.com/cgi/entry/50231045?query_type=word & queryword=sophomore & first=1 & max_to_show=10 & single=1 & sort_type=alpha|title=Oxford English Dictionary|date=1989|publisher=Oxford University Press|edition=2nd|chapter=sophumer}} < /ref >  It was not until 1726 that the spelling was established in America as  " sophomore.  == Education == 
 
 === High school === 
 In the United States, the 10th grade is usually the second year of a student's high school period and is referred to as sophomore year. High school sophomores are expected to begin preparing for the [[college]] application process, including reducing and focusing their [[Extracurricular activity|extracurricular activities]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.princetonreview.com/college/research/articles/prepare/extracurriculars.asp |title=Sophomore Year: Time to Narrow Down Your Extracurricular Activities |access-date=2023-01-24 |archive-date=2008-06-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080617225055/http://www.princetonreview.com/college/research/articles/prepare/extracurriculars.asp |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Most students reach the age of 16 while they are sophomores. The year is considered a watermark for [[independence]], closely connected with getting a [[driver's license]] in most states. The [[PSAT]], a college preparatory exam taken across the U.S., is now most commonly taken during a student's sophomore year. Students at this level are also considered to be developing greater ability for abstract thinking. < ref > [http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/02/06/AR2006020601581.html Sophomore Year: Between Lark and a Hard Place < !-- Bot generated title -- > ] < /ref > 
 
 === College === 
 In the U.S., colleges generally require students to declare an [[academic major]] by the end of their sophomore year. < ref > {{cite web|title=Do I Need to Declare a Major on My College Application?|url=http://www.princetonreview.com/college/research/articles/majors/MajorsBook3.asp|publisher=[[Princeton Review]]|accessdate=2007-10-22}} < /ref >  College sophomores are advised to begin thinking about [[career]] options and to get involved in [[volunteering]] or social organizations on or near [[campus]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.yale.edu/visvi/students/year_guide/sophomore.html |title=Yale UCS - Year by Year Guide - Sophomore Year < !-- Bot generated title -- >  |access-date=2023-01-24 |archive-date=2009-06-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090621031705/http://www.yale.edu/visvi/students/year_guide/sophomore.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
