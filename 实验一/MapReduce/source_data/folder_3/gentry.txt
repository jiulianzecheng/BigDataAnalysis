'''Gentry''' means “well-born people”. The word comes from the [[latin]] word ''gentis'' which means “clan” or “extended family”. In [[England]], gentry is the [[social class]] below the [[aristocracy]]. It gets its [[income]] from large landholdings. < ref > ''Webster’s New Universal Unabridged Dictionary'' (1996) p.798 < /ref >
