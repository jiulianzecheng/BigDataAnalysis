 '''Kaskinen''' ({{lang-sv|Kaskö}}) is a [[town]] in western [[Ostrobothnia (region)|Ostrobothnia]], [[Finland]]. There were 1,341 people lived there in January 2014. It is the smallest city in Finland by population. Only nearby municipality is [[Närpes]]. The people of Kaskinen speak two languages, Finnish and Swedish. 
 
 Kaskinen is buildt on an island. The island is also called Kaskinen. 
 
 Kaskinen was established in 1785.
