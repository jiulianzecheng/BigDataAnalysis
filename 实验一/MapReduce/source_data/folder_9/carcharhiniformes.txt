 '''Carcharhiniformes''' is the largest [[Order (biology)|order]] of [[shark]]s. Also known as '''Ground sharks''' or '''Whaler sharks''', this group contains over 270 [[species]] of [[shark]]. It includes some common ones like the [[Blue shark]], [[Bull shark]], [[Tiger shark]] and the [[hammerhead shark]]s. 
 
 Ground sharks have a special [[membrane]] which protects their eyes when needed. They have two [[dorsal fin]]s, an [[anal fin]], and five [[gill]] slits. 
 
 Ground sharks are found in open [[ocean]]s, [[sea]]s, [[estuary|estuaries]], and [[brackish water|brackish]] waters.

 There are eight [[Family (biology)|families]] in this order: 
 * [[Carcharhinidae]] (Requiem sharks) 
 * [[Hemigaleidae]] (Weasel sharks) 
 * [[Leptochariidae]] (Barbeled houndsharks) 
 * [[Proscylliidae]] (Finback cat sharks) 
 * [[Pseudotriakidae]] (False cat sharks) 
 * [[Scyliorhinidae]] (Cat sharks) 
 * [[Hammerhead shark|Sphyrnidae]] (Hammerhead sharks) 
 * [[Triakidae]] (Hound sharks)
