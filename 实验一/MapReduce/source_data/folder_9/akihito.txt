 {{nihongo|'''Emperor Akihito'''|明仁天皇|Akihito-tennō|born 23 December 1933}} (known simply as Akihito) is the former [[Emperor of Japan|emperor]] of [[Japan]]. He reigned from 1989 to 2019. He was the 125th emperor of his line according to Japan's [[list of Emperors of Japan|traditional order of succession]]. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric et al. (2002).   " Traditional order of Tennō "  in ''Japan encyclopedia,'' pp. 962-963. < /ref >  He is head of the [[Imperial House of Japan]].  
 
 Akihito became emperor after the death of his father in 1989, who was [[Emperor Shōwa]]. The current emperor's official [[Japanese era name|reign name]] is ''Heisei'' (establishing [[peace]]). < ref > Nussbaum,   " Akihito "  at p. 19. < /ref > 
 
 ==Abdication== 
 The Japanese government announced in December 2017 that Akihito would [[abdicate]] on 30 April 2019. < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite news |last=Enjoji |first=Kaori |date=December 1, 2017 |title=Japan Emperor Akihito to abdicate on April 30, 2019 |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2017/11/30/asia/japan-emperor-akihito-abdication-intl/index.html |work=[[CNN]] |location=Tokyo |access-date=December 1, 2017 }} < /ref >  He then did so in a brief ceremony on that date. < ref > {{cite news |last1=McCurry |first1=Justin |title=Japan's emperor Akihito abdicates with message of love |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2019/apr/30/japan-emperor-akihito-abdicate |accessdate=30 April 2019 |work=The Guardian |date=30 April 2019}} < /ref > 
 
 ==What the emperor does== 
 The emperor has many duties and responsibilities.  For example, he regularly 
 * takes part in affairs of according to the Constitution of Japan. < ref > [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p01.html  " Affairs of State " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 () < /ref > 
 * attends various events and ceremonies held at the Imperial Palace. < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p02.html  " Ceremonies in Imperial Palace " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * attends ceremonies in Tokyo. < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p03.html  " Progress in Tokyo " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * makes appearances at a variety of ceremonies in various parts in Japan < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p04.html  " Progress around Japan " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * visits welfare facilities. < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p05.html  " Visits to Welfare facilities " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * visits regions where disasters hit < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p06.html  " Visits to disaster-affected areas " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * visits other countries < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p07.html  " Overseas visits " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 * helps transmit the traditional culture of Japan < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p08.html  " Transmitting traditional cultures to posterity " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 
 As a personal interest, Akihito studies fish. < ref > ''Kunaicho'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-kids/work_e/p09.html  " Studies " ]; retrieved 2011-10-18 (). < /ref > 
 
 ===Television broadcast=== 
 Akihito directly addressed his subjects in a television broadcast after the [[2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami]].  This was the first time any emperor used television in this way. < ref > Chancellor, Alexander. [http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2011/mar/18/japanese-emperors-lesson-for-monarchy   " The Japanese emperor's lesson for the British monarchy, " ] ''The Guardian'' (UK). 17 March 2011; retrieved 2011-10-17. < /ref >
