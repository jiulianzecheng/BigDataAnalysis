 '''Sbarro, LLC''' is a [[restaurant]] chain. Sbarro is known for their [[New York style pizza]]. The restaurant was created by both Gennaro and Carmela Sbarro in 1956. 
 
 They opened their first Salumeria, an Italian grocery store, at 1701 65th Street and 17th Avenue in Bensonhurst, Brooklyn, New York. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://sbarro.com/aboutUs/aboutUs.php|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=About Us|publisher=Sbarro|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110422151555/http://sbarro.com/aboutUs/aboutUs.php|archive-date=2011-04-22}} < /ref >  One of their busiest Sbarro restaurants was located in the [[World Trade Center (1973â€“2001)|World Trade Center]] mall. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/global/2001/1015/041.html|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Business as usual|work=[[Forbes]]}} < /ref >   The headquarters for Sbarro is located in [[Melville, New York|Melville]], [[New York]]. 
 
 On August 9, 2001, there was a suicide bombing at a Sbarro restaurant in downtown [[Jerusalem]], [[Israel]]. The bombings were carried out by [[Hamas]]. 15 people were killed, which included 7 children, and a pregnant woman. It also wounded 130 people. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.jpost.com/Israel/Nine-years-after-the-Sbarro-massacre|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Nine years after the Sbarro massacre|publisher=[[The Jerusalem Post]]}} < /ref > 
 
 On March 10, 2014, Sbarro filed for [[Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code|chapter 11]] [[bankruptcy]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/briansolomon/2014/03/10/burned-to-a-crisp-sbarro-files-for-bankruptcy-again/|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Burned To A Crisp: Sbarro Files For Bankruptcy (Again)|work=Forbes}} < /ref >
