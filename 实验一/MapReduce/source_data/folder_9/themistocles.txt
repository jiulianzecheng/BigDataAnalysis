 '''Themistocles''' (?524 BC-?460 BC < ref name= " Collins " > {{cite book |editor1-last=Treffry |editor1-first=Diana |title=Collins English Dictionary |date=1998 |publisher=HarperCollins |isbn=0 00 470453-3 |page=1587 |edition=Fourth}} < /ref > ) was an [[Ancient Greek]] [[Athens|Athenian]] < ref name= " Collins " / >  politician and general. He fought against the Persians at the [[Battle of Marathon]] as a young man{{Citation needed|date=June 2020|reason=Aged ?34 in 490 BC}}. He later persuaded [[Athens]] to build a [[navy]] < ref name= " Smiths " > {{cite book |editor1-last=Smith |editor1-first=William |title=A New Classical Dictionary of Biography. Mythology, and Geography |url=https://archive.org/details/cu31924091208342 |date=1853 |publisher=John Murray |page=[https://archive.org/details/cu31924091208342/page/n773 758] |edition=Second}} < /ref >  which he commanded in the engagement against the [[Persia]]ns at the [[Battle of Salamis]] in 480 BC. < ref name= " Collins " / > 
 
 After the war he became unpopular and had to leave. He went to work for the [[Achaemenid Empire|Persian Empire]].
