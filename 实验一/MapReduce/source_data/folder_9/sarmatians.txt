 The '''Sarmatians''' ({{IPAc-en|s|ɑr|ˈ|m|eɪ|ʃ|i|ə|n|z}}; {{lang-la|Sarmatae, Sauromatae}}) were groups of Iranic peoples that lived in a region called Sarmatia that is the present day [[Ukraine]] near the [[Black Sea]] shore. They were flourishing from 5th century BC to the 4-5th century AD. < ref > Tanner, Arno, ed. The Forgotten Minorities of Eastern Europe: The history and today of selected ethnic groups in five countries. East-West Books, 2004. < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 At their greatest development, near 1st century AD, Sarmatian tribes covered the territories from the [[Vistula]]  to the [[Volga River|Volga]]. Sarmatians started to battle with [[Roman Empire]] forces together with [[Germanic peoples|Germanic tribes]] around 1st century AD. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Sarmatian  & #124; people|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Sarmatian|website=Encyclopedia Britannica}} < /ref > 
 
 In the 3-5 century AD Sarmatians were conquered by [[Goths]] and later by [[Huns]]. Later on the Sarmatians were assimilated by [[Slavs]] of Eastern Europe. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Tarasov|first=Илья Тарасов / Ilia|title=Балты в миграциях Великого переселения народов. Галинды // Исторический формат, № 3-4, 2017. С. 95-124.|url=https://www.academia.edu/37147068|journal=Балты в миграциях Великого переселения народов. Галинды|date=January 2017|via=www.academia.edu}} < /ref >
