 }}'''Kiwix''' is a [[Free and open-source software|free and open-source]] offline [[web browser]] created by Emmanuel Engelhart and Renaud Gaudin in 2007. < ref name= " sutherland2014 "  / >  It was first launched to allow [[offline]] access to [[Wikipedia]]. But it has expanded to include other projects from the [[Wikimedia Foundation]] as well as [[public domain]] texts from [[Project Gutenberg]]. It is available in more than 100 languages. Kiwix has been included in several high-profile projects, from smuggling operations in [[North Korea]] < ref > {{cite news|title=The plot to free North Korea with smuggled episodes of  " Friends " |work=[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]]|url=https://www.wired.com/2015/03/north-korea/|access-date=27 April 2016|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >  and encyclopedic access in [[Cuba]] < ref > {{cite news|title=¿Cómo utilizar Kiwix como servidor local?|language=es|url=http://ecured.cubava.cu/2017/06/12/como-utilizar-kiwix-como-servidor-local/|access-date=3 July 2017|df=dmy-all|archive-date=4 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170704034754/http://ecured.cubava.cu/2017/06/12/como-utilizar-kiwix-como-servidor-local/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  to Google Impact Challenge's recipient [[Bibliothèques Sans Frontières]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Les Lauréats du Google Impact Challenge|url=http://www.tntv.pf/Les-laureats-du-Google-Impact-Challenge_a8169.html|access-date=27 April 2016|df=dmy-all|archive-date=2 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160602184827/http://www.tntv.pf/Les-laureats-du-Google-Impact-Challenge_a8169.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 ===Background=== 
 Founder Emmanuel Engelhart sees [[Wikipedia]] as a [[common good]], saying  " The contents of Wikipedia should be available for everyone! Even without [[Internet access]]. This is why I have launched the Kiwix project. " < ref name= " sutherland2014 " > Sutherland, Joe. ''[https://blog.wikimedia.org/2014/09/12/emmanuel-engelhart-inventor-of-kiwix/ Emmanuel Engelhart, Inventor of Kiwix: the Offline Wikipedia Browser]''. In: [[Wikimedia Blog]]. 12 September 2014. Accessed on 26 November 2014. < /ref > 
 
 After becoming a Wikipedia editor in 2004, Engelhart became interested in developing [[offline]] versions of Wikipedia. A [[wikipedia:Version_1.0_Editorial_Team|project to make a Wikipedia CD]], initiated in 2003, was a trigger for the project. < ref name= " sutherland2014 "  / >

 == Description == 
 The software is designed as an [[offline reader]] for a web content. It can be used on computers without an [[internet connection]], computers with a slow or expensive connection, or to avoid [[censorship]]. It can also be used while [[Travel|travelling]] (e.g. on a [[Airliner|passenger plane]] or a [[Passenger train|train]]). 
 ===Download=== 
 Users first download Kiwix. Then they download content for offline viewing with Kiwix. [[Data compression|Compression]] saves [[disk space]] and [[Bandwidth (computing)|bandwidth]]. All of English-language Wikipedia, with pictures, fits on a large [[USB stick]] or external media  (82 & nbsp;GB as of march 2021, or 43 GB with no pictures). < ref name= " Slashdot.org "  / > < ref name= " Content in all languages - Kiwix " > {{Cite web|title=Content in all languages - Kiwix|url=https://wiki.kiwix.org/Content_in_all_languages|access-date=2016-08-08|website=wiki.kiwix.org|df=dmy-all}} < /ref > 
 ===Files=== 
 All content files are compressed in [[ZIM (file format)|ZIM format]]. It makes them smaller, but leaves them easy to index, search, and selectively decompress. 
 
 The ZIM files are then opened with Kiwix, which looks and behaves like a [[web browser]]. Kiwix offers full [[text search]], [[Tab (GUI)|tabbed navigation]], and the option to export articles to [[PDF]] and [[HTML]]. < ref name= " sourceforge " > {{cite web|title=Kiwix|url=http://sourceforge.net/projects/kiwix/|access-date=22 March 2012|publisher=[[SourceForge]]|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >
