 The '''Matterhorn''' (in [[German language|German]]; {{lang-it|Cervino}}, {{lang-fr|Mont Cervin}} or ''Le Cervin'') is ranked, by height, 12th  [[mountain]] in the European [[Alps]]. < ref > Even though the Matterhorn is very famous, both because of its beauty, and because it is hard to climb, it is not one of the 100 tallest mountains in the Alps. Several mountains near it, including [[Monte Rosa]], the [[Dom (mountain)|Dom]], [[Lyskamm|Liskamm]] and the [[Weisshorn]], are taller. < /ref >  Its height is 4,478 [[metre]]s. < ref > NHK, [http://pf.nhk-ep.co.jp/detail/1446  " Matterhorn: Majestic Peak that Pierces the Sky -- Switzerland, 4,478 m " ]; retrieved 2012-5-24. < /ref > 
 
 The mountain is on the border between [[Switzerland]] and [[Italy]], it towers over the Swiss village of [[Zermatt]] and the Italian village [[Cervinia|Breuil-Cervinia]] in the Val Tournanche. The name of the mountain comes from the [[German language|German]] words ''Matte'', meaning ''valley'' or ''meadow'', and ''Horn'', which means ''peak''. < ref > [http://www.swissworld.org/en/switzerland/swiss_specials/swiss_mountains/names/ Swiss Mountains - Names] www.swissworld.org Retrieved [[26 November]] 2007. < /ref >
