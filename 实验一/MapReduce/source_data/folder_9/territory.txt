A '''territory''' (plural: '''territories''', from the word ''terra'', which means 'land') is an [[area]] which belongs to a [[person]], [[organization]], [[institution]], [[animal]], [[nation]] or [[state]]. In [[international]] [[law]], a  " territory "  is an area of land which is outside the borders of a nation, but owned by that nation. [[Example]]s are: [[Puerto Rico]] as a [[United States|U.S.]] territory, or [[Bermuda]] as a territory of the [[UK]]. 
 
 General examples of territories are states controlled by a [[federation|federated government]] (such as [[Germany]] or the [[counties]] of a state within the [[U.S. state|States of the United States]]), a [[unitary state]] such as [[France]], an [[occupied territory]] (land which has been [[invasion|invaded]] by the [[military]] of another country) or a [[Territorial dispute|disputed territory]] (such as [[Kashmir]], which both [[India]]Â and [[Pakistan]] believe is theirs).
