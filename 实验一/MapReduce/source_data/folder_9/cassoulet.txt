 '''Cassoulet''' is a [[casserole]] made particularly in the south of [[France]]. It is called after its traditional cooking vessel, the ''cassole'', a deep, round, [[earthenware]] pot with slanting sides. < ref name=David > {{cite book|last=David|first=Elizabeth|title=French Provincial Cooking|year=1999|publisher=Penguin Books|location=New York|ISBN=0141181532|pages=59 and 61|edition=7th printing}} < /ref >  The name comes from the [[Occitan language|Occitan]] ''caçolet'' (or ''cassolet).'' It contains meat (usually [[pork]] [[sausage]]s, [[goose]], [[duck]] and sometimes [[Lamb and mutton|mutton]]), [[pork]] skin (''couennes'') and white beans (haricots blancs). It may be baked with an open or a closed top. 
 
 The homeland of cassoulet is the province of [[Languedoc]], especially the towns of [[Toulouse]], [[Carcassonne]], and [[Castelnaudary]]. < ref name=Dryansky > {{cite book|last=Dryansky|first=G.Y.|title=Coquilles, Calva,  &  Crème: Exploring France's Culinary Heritage|url=https://archive.org/details/coquillescalvacr0000drya|year=2012|publisher=Pegasus Books|location=New York|ISBN=978-1-60598-329-5|edition=1st Pegasus Books|author2=Dryansky, Joanne|chapter=The Southwest Trinity}} < /ref >  The brotherhood of Cassoulet  " La Grande Confrérie du Cassoulet de Castelnaudary "  organizes competitions and fairs about Cassoulet. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.confrerieducassoulet.com/|title=Grande Confrérie du Cassoulet de Castelnaudary|website=www.confrerieducassoulet.com}} < /ref >
