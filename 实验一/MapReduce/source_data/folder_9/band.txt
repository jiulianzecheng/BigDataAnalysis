 In [[music]], a '''band''' is a group of people (usually [[musician]]s) that get together to sing [[song]]s, or perform music. There are different kinds of bands that use different groups of instruments. < ref name= " types_of_bands " > {{cite web|last=Estrella|first=Espie|title=Types of Bands|url=http://musiced.about.com/od/lessonsandtips/a/bandtypes.htm|publisher=About Education|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=14 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160314045158/http://musiced.about.com/od/lessonsandtips/a/bandtypes.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Types of bands== 
 *A [[rock band]] is usually a [[group]] of 4 [[people]] with 1 or 2 [[electric guitars]], a [[bass guitar]], a [[drum kit]] and a [[singer]]. Some rock bands have only 3 members, like [[Rush (band)|Rush]]. < ref > {{cite web|last=Lineberger|first=Jason|title=Instruments in Rock Music|url=http://study.com/academy/lesson/instruments-in-rock-music.html|publisher=Study.com|accessdate=19 February 2016}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|last=Powell|first=Wade|title=How to Start a Band|url=http://iml.jou.ufl.edu/projects/Fall08/PowellW/instruments.html|publisher=ufl.edu|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=20 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160320112224/http://iml.jou.ufl.edu/projects/Fall08/PowellW/instruments.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 *A [[brass band]] is a group of about 20 - 30 people with [[brass instrument]]s and [[drum]]s. < ref > {{cite web|last=Schramm|first=Robert|title=Brass Band Instrumentation|url=http://brasscrest.com/instru001.html|publisher=The Brass Crest|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=4 February 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160204225626/http://brasscrest.com/instru001.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=What is a Brass Band?|url=http://www.nzbrass.com/index.php/about-dalewool/what-is-a-brass-band|publisher=Dalewool Auckland Brass|accessdate=19 February 2016}} < /ref > 
 *A [[concert band]] is a large group of 20 - 60 people with [[woodwind instrument]]s, brass instruments, drums and [[percussion]] instruments. < ref > {{cite web|title=Instrumentation|url=http://www.lipscomb.edu/windbandhistory/rhodeswindband_11_instrumentation.htm|publisher=lipscomb.edu|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=6 February 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160206084124/http://www.lipscomb.edu/windbandhistory/rhodeswindband_11_instrumentation.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 *A [[marching band]] has similar instruments to the concert band. Instead of sitting down to play, they march in [[parade]]s or at [[American football]] matches. < ref name= " types_of_bands "  / > 
 *A [[folk band]] uses traditional styles of music from around the world. It can include a [[guitar]], [[violin]], [[tin whistle]], [[flute]], [[accordion]], lagerphone and [[tea]] chest bass, and many other makeshift instruments. A lagerphone is a percussion instrument which uses bottle tops to make a sound. A tea chest bass is a one string instrument that uses a large wooden tea box to make the sound louder. < ref > {{cite web|last=MB|title=The History of Folk Music|url=http://www.music-folk.com/the-history-of-folk-music/|publisher=music-folk.com|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=19 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160319032353/http://www.music-folk.com/the-history-of-folk-music/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=The History of American Folk Music|url=http://folkmusic.about.com/od/historyoffolk/a/Folk_History.htm|publisher=About Entertainment|accessdate=19 February 2016|archive-date=17 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160317013756/http://folkmusic.about.com/od/historyoffolk/a/Folk_History.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
