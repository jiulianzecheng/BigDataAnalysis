 '''Korpiklaani''' ([[Finnish language|Finnish]]: {{lang|fi|The Backwoods Clan}}) < ref name= " High Korpi2 " > {{cite web|author=Morton, Kenneth|title=Interview with Jarkko Aaltonen of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.highwiredaze.com/korpiklaani.htm|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080225172928/http://highwiredaze.com/korpiklaani.htm|archive-date=25 February 2008|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Highwiredaze.com}} < /ref >  is a Finnish [[folk metal]] band. The band was made in [[Lahti]] as the '''Shamaani Duo''' in 1993. It was also named '''Shaman'''. [[Jonne Järvelä]] is the only person who has stayed in the band since it was made. He is the singer and [[guitar]] player for the band. 
 
 Korpiklaani started as a [[folk music]] group named the Shamaani Duo. It was made by Järvelä and singer Maaren Aikio. When Aikio left, Järvelä renamed the group to Shaman. He started making folk metal instead of folk music. They made two [[Album|albums]], then the band's name changed again to Korpiklaani. They joined [[Napalm Records]] (a [[record label]]) and made more music. 
 
 The band's first album to go onto many national [[Record chart|music charts]] was ''Tervaskanto''. It was released in 2007. All of the band's albums since ''Tervaskanto'' have been onto many national music charts, such as charts in Finland, Germany, and Switzerland. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Die Offizielle Schweizer Hitparade - hitparade.ch|url=https://hitparade.ch/search.asp?search=Korpiklaani & cat=a|access-date=2023-02-01|website=hitparade.ch}} < /ref > < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=finnishcharts.com - Finnish charts portal|url=https://finnishcharts.com/search.asp?search=Korpiklaani & cat=a|access-date=2023-02-01|website=finnishcharts.com}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Offizielle Deutsche Charts - Offizielle Deutsche Charts|url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/album-details-471196|access-date=2023-02-01|website=www.offiziellecharts.de}} < /ref >  The band's most successful album is ''[[Jylhä]].'' It was released in 2021. The band's current members are Järvelä, guitar player Kalle Savijärvi, [[Bass guitar|bass]] player Jarkko Aaltonen, [[accordion]] player Sami Perttula, and drummer Samuli Mikkonen. 
 
 The band mixes folk music and [[Heavy metal music|metal music]] by using metal music [[Musical instrument|instruments]], such as the [[electric guitar]] and [[bass guitar]], with folk music instruments, such as the [[lute]], accordion, and [[torupill]]. The band also uses singing that sounds similar to [[Joik|joiking]]. Joiking is a type of Finnish folk singing. The band's lyrics are often about old Finnish [[Religion|religions]]. 
 
 == History == 
 
 === Shamaani Duo and ''Hunka Lunka'' (1993–1997) === 
 Korpiklaani started in 1993, with the name Shamaani Duo. < ref > {{cite web|author=Van Horn Jr., Ray|title=Interview with Jarkko Aaltonen of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.maelstrom.nu/ezine/interview_iss37_193.php|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080320233707/http://www.maelstrom.nu/ezine/interview_iss37_193.php|archive-date=20 March 2008|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Maelstrom.nu}} < /ref >  It was made by Jonne Järvelä. Shamaani Duo made folk music, with no [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]]. < ref > {{cite web|author=Dicks, Britton|title=Interview with Jarkko Aaltonen of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.metalcoven.com/interview_korp_britton.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080516220347/http://www.metalcoven.com/interview_korp_britton.html|archive-date=16 May 2008|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Metalcoven.com}} < /ref >  The group was inspired by folk music made by the [[Sámi]]. They played most of their music in a restaurant in the [[Finnish Lapland|Lapland]]. The restaurant was named Hullu Poro. < ref > {{cite web|author=Sharpe-Young, Garry|title=MusicMight Shama|url=http://www.musicmight.com/artist/finland/lahti/shaman|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110811173110/http://www.musicmight.com/artist/finland/lahti/shaman|archive-date=11 August 2011|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=MusicMight.com}} < /ref >  In 1996, they released an album named ''Hunka Lunka''. This album had folk music on it. It used a type of folk singing called [[Yoik|joiking]], which the Sámi used. 
 
 After making the album, one of the members of Shamaani Duo left the band. Her name was Maaren Aikio. She was one of the singers. After she left, Järvelä changed the band's name to Shaman. < ref name= " Voice of Ramos " > {{cite web|author=Ramos Jr, Octavio|title=Voice Of The Forest|url=http://www.soundchecks.co.uk/articles/korpikla.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080112032531/http://www.soundchecks.co.uk/articles/korpikla.html|archive-date=12 January 2008|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Soundchecks.co.uk}} < /ref > 
 
 === Shaman (1997–2003) === 
 Shaman was made in 1997. It made music that was [[Heavy metal music|heavier]] than the music Shamaani Duo made. < ref name= " High Korpi " > {{cite web|author=Morton, Kenneth|title=Interview with Jarkko Aaltonen of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.highwiredaze.com/korpiklaani.htm|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080225172928/http://highwiredaze.com/korpiklaani.htm|archive-date=25 February 2008|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Highwiredaze.com}} < /ref >  The band's music had lyrics that were spoken in the [[Sámi]] language. Many things from folk music were still used in Shaman's music, such as joiking. 
 
 The band made a [[Demo (music)|demo song]] in 1999. It was named  " Ođđa máilbmi "  (''New World'' in Sami). The song was put on the band's first album. The album was named ''[[Idja]]''. It was released on January 8, 1999. The band made a second album in 2002. It was named ''Shamániac.'' < ref name= " Voice of Ramos "  / > 
 
 === Korpiklaani (2003–present) === 
 [[File:PaganfestEurope_2008_Munich_Korpiklaani_01.jpg|thumb|336x336px|Korpiklaani playing music in 2008. From left to right: Jonne Järvelä, Kalle  " Cane "  Savijärvi, and Jaakko  " Hittavainen "  Lemmetty.]] 
 The band changed again in 2003. The band's new name was Korpiklaani. The only members of the band that were from Shaman were Järvelä and drummer Samu Ruotsalainen. The band's music changed to sound more similar to [[folk metal]] and [[thrash metal]]. The band stopped singing in the Sami language. They also started using real folk [[Musical instrument|instruments]]. < ref name= " Yoik " > {{cite web|author=Lehtinen, Arto|title=Interview with Jonne Järvelä of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.metal-rules.com/interviews/Korpiklaani-March2004.htm|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Metal-rules.com}} < /ref >  Järvelä has said that the band started making more heavy metal music because he worked with [[Finntroll]]. < ref name= " Tartare Korpi " > {{cite web|author=Fjordi|title=Interview with Jonne Järvelä of Korpiklaani|url=http://www.tartareandesire.com/interviews/korpiklaani.html|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Tartareandesire.com|archive-date=27 March 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080327134649/http://www.tartareandesire.com/interviews/korpiklaani.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Finntroll is another folk metal band. 
 
 Korpiklaani joined a [[record label]] named [[Napalm Records]]. In Korpiklaani's first albums, the lyrics were mostly in English. Some of the music had lyrics in Finnish. However, their later albums have more music in Finnish than English. < ref > {{cite web|date=27 October 2014|title=Perisuomalaista black metalia kalevalamitassa – Hiidenhaudalta tuore musiikkivideo!|url=http://www.inferno.fi/uutiset/perisuomalaista-black-metalia-katso-uusi-hiidenhauta-musiikkivideo/|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Inferno.fi}} < /ref >  Members of Korpiklaani and Finntroll have worked together many times. Ruotsalainen has played the drums with both bands. Järvelä has also done joiking for some of Finntroll's music. 
 
 The band's first album to go onto Finland's national music chart was ''Voice of Wilderness''. It was the band's second album. It was released in 2005. After ''Voice of Wilderness'', all of the band's albums have been on national music charts. In 2008, Korpiklaani joined [[Nuclear Blast]]. This was a different record label. Their first album with Nuclear Blast was named ''[[Korven Kuningas]]'' (2008). 
 
 In 2011, the band released ''Ukon Wacka''. This album went to the number 9 place on Finland's music chart. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  Later in 2011, the band said Jaakko Lemmetty was leaving Korpiklaani. This was because he was having health problems. Lemmetty played many folk instruments for the band. Tuomas Rounakari replaced him. < ref > {{cite web|title=Tuomas Rounakari - Encyclopaedia Metallum|url=http://www.metal-archives.com/artists/Tuomas_Rounakari/398804|access-date=1 February 2023|publisher=Metal-archives.com}} < /ref >
