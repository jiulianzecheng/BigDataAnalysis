 '''Ahmedabad''' is the largest city in the [[Gujarat]] state, in Western [[India]]. The city is the administrative centre of Ahmedabad district, and was the capital of Gujarat from 1960 to 1970. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.incredibleindia.org/content/incredibleindia/en/destinations/states/gujarat.html|title=Incredible India {{!}} Gujarat|website=www.incredibleindia.org|access-date=2019-08-24|archive-date=2019-08-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190824145332/https://www.incredibleindia.org/content/incredibleindia/en/destinations/states/gujarat.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The city is located on the banks of the Sabarmati River, < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Kumar|first1=Rita. N.|last2=Solanki|first2=Rajal|last3=J.I|first3=Nirmal Kumar|year=2011|title=AN ASSESSMENT OF SEASONAL VARIATION AND WATER QUALITY INDEX OF SABARMATI RIVER AND KHARICUT CANAL AT AHMEDABAD, GUJARAT.|url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/286893088|journal=EJEAFChe|volume=10|issue=5|issn=1579-4377|eissn=2248-2261}} < /ref >  and it shares proximity with the [[Gandhinagar]], the newly built state capital. 
 
 The city's area is approximately 8,087 sq.k.m and it's total population of District is 7,486,573 (2014). < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|url=https://ahmedabad.gujarat.gov.in/about-ahmedabad|title=About Ahmedabad {{!}} About Us {{!}} Collectorate - District Ahmedabad|website=ahmedabad.gujarat.gov.in|access-date=2019-08-24|archive-date=2019-08-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190824113141/https://ahmedabad.gujarat.gov.in/about-ahmedabad|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 
 The city was founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah in 1411 A.D. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xgU9DAAAQBAJ & dq=Ahmedabad++Founder & pg=PA279|title=Neo-Liberal Strategies of Governing India|last=Samaddar|first=Ranabir|date=2016-05-26|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9781317199694|pages=279|language=en}} < /ref >  But, the area around the city has been inhabited since the 11th century when it was known as ''Ashaval''. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=un4WcfEASZwC & dq=Ahmedabad+Ashaval & pg=PA37|title=Grove Encyclopedia of Islamic Art  &  Architecture: Three-Volume Set|last1=Bloom|first1=Jonathan|last2=Blair|first2=Sheila S.|last3=Blair|first3=Sheila|date=2009-05-14|publisher=OUP USA|isbn=9780195309911|pages=37|language=en}} < /ref >  The city was built on an elevated plain known as ''Bhadra'' on the eastern bank of Sabarmati River. 
 
 In 1487, the city was fortified with an outer wall 10 & nbsp;km (6.2 & nbsp;mi) in circumference by Mahmud Begada, the grandson of Sultan. The fortification consisted of twelve gates, 189 bastions and over 6,000 battlements. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AvggAAAAMAAJ & q=ahmedabad+189+bastions+and+over+6,000+battlements.|title=India through the ages: history, art, culture, and religion|last=Kuppuram|first=G.|date=1988|publisher=Sundeep Prakashan|isbn=9788185067094|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1573, the city was annexed by the [[Mughal Empire]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.nationalgeographic.com/travel/destinations/asia/india/things-to-do-ahmadabad-unesco-world-heritage-site/|title=See Stunning Architecture in India's First World Heritage City|date=2018-04-25|website=Travel|access-date=2019-08-24}} < /ref >  But, in the year 1758, Mughals surrendered the city to [[Marathas]] after the brief joint-rule from 1739 to 1744. < ref name= " :1 "  / > 
 
 == Governance == 
 Since July 1950, the city is governed by [[Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation]] (AMC) in urban area and Ahmedabad Urban Development Authority (AUDA) in suburbs area. Both, provides a number of urban services such as water supply, roads construction, sanitation, primary education, city transport etc. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://shodhganga.inflibnet.ac.in/bitstream/10603/89194/10/10chapter%201.pdf|title=Chapter 1, Introduction to Road Transport and Ahmedabad Municipal Transport Service|website=Sodhganga, INFLIBNET|access-date=August 25, 2019}} < /ref >  In totality, the administration pf the city is divided into 14 talukas which includes 556 villages, 1 corporation, 1 cantonment area, and 7 municipalities. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 == Economy == 
 For a long time, the city has been known for its textile mills and have been dubbed as '' " Manchester of the East " .'' < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/9781118822531.ch38|title=Forty Ways To Think About Architecture|last=Perry|first=Victoria|date=2015|publisher=John Wiley  &  Sons, Ltd|isbn=9781118822531|pages=249–253|language=en|doi=10.1002/9781118822531.ch38}} < /ref >  In 1861, the first textile mill was opened and gradually, the whole sector grew through city's own traditional culture and passion for [[entrepreneurship]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Spodek|first=Howard|date=1969|title=Traditional Culture and Entrepreneurship: A Case Study of Ahmedabad|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/40737346|journal=Economic and Political Weekly|volume=4|issue=8|pages=M27–M31|issn=0012-9976|jstor=40737346}} < /ref >  Besides textile, Ahmedabad is also known for being [[automotive]] < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/ahmedabad/Rise-of-an-Auto-hub/articleshow/50791613.cms|title=Rise of an Auto hub {{!}} Ahmedabad News - Times of India|author=Piyush Mishra|date=January 31, 2016|website=The Times of India|language=en|access-date=2019-08-24}} < /ref >  and [[pharmaceutical]] manufacturing hub. < ref > {{Cite news|url=https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/healthcare/biotech/pharmaceuticals/gujarats-contribution-surged-in-pharma-to-become-global-hub-assocham/articleshow/19802815.cms?from=mdr|title=Gujarat's contribution surged in pharma, to become global hub: ASSOCHAM|last=Dutta|first=Vishal|date=2013-04-30|work=The Economic Times|access-date=2019-08-24}} < /ref > 
 
 == Transportation == 
 
 === Railway === 
 The city is connected with pan-India railway network. It has three stations - Kalupur Station, Maninagar Station and Sabarmati Station. The Kalupur station is in the center of the city which will be convergence point for Ahmedabad Metro, Ahmedabad BRTS, and upcoming high-speed rail system. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/ahmedabad/next-stop-for-heritage-makeover-is-kalupur-railway-station/articleshow/65554965.cms|title=World Heritage Site: Next stop for heritage makeover is Kalupur railway station {{!}} Ahmedabad News - Times of India|date=August 27, 2018|author=Himanshu Kaushik|website=The Times of India|language=en|access-date=2019-08-24}} < /ref > 
 
 === Roads === 
 The city is well connected to the major cities in the state and the country through several national and state highways. 
 
 === Air === 
 The city is served by Sardar Vallabbhai Patel International Airport. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.aai.aero/en/airports/ahmedabad|title=Sardar Vallabbhai Patel International Airport, AAI|website=Airport Authority of India|access-date=2019-08-24|archive-date=2019-12-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191214010405/https://www.aai.aero/en/airports/ahmedabad|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The airport is located 9 & nbsp;km from city center and also caters the state capital [[Gandhinagar]]. It was started in the year 1937 with the inaugural flight Bombay-Ahmedabad-Karachi, piloted by [[J. R. D. Tata]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://qz.com/india/323808/a-short-history-of-tatas-long-tryst-with-indias-aviation/|title=A short history of Tata's long tryst with Indian aviation|last=Dutta|first=Manu Balachandran and Saptarishi|website=Quartz India|date=9 January 2015 |language=en|access-date=2019-08-24}} < /ref >
