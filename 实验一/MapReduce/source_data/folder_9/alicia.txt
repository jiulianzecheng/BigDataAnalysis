'''Alicia''' [ uh-lish-uh, uh-lish-ee-uh, uh-lee-shuh, -shee-uh ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Alicia {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/alicia|access-date=2023-03-13|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref > is a female given name. It is related to the name ''Alice''. The name is very popular in certain countries which speak [[English language|English]].In 2021, the U.S. national ranking for newborn prevalence is 415. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/|access-date=2023-03-13|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref > Alicia is thought to signify  " noble "  or  " belonging to nobility. "  Additionally, it is linked with alicium, a Latin word that means  " truth. " < ref > {{Cite web|title=Alicia name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Alicia-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-13|website=babys.name}} < /ref > 
 
 It was also used for a [[tropical cyclone|hurricane]] in 1983, [[Hurricane Alicia]].
