 {{Nihongo|'''Yamagata'''|山形市|Yamagata-shi}}  is a [[city]] in the [[Tōhoku region]] of [[Japan]]. It is the [[capital city]] of [[Yamagata Prefecture]]. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA1038  " Yamagata " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 1038; [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA970  " Tōhoku " ] at p. 970. < /ref >  It is on the island of [[Honshu]]. It has been [[wikt:recognized|recognized]] as a [[Special cities of Japan|special city]] since 2001. < ref > Jacobs, A.J. [http://www.hindawi.com/journals/usr/2011/692764/  " Japan's Evolving Nested Municipal Hierarchy: The Race for Local Power in the 2000s, " ] ''Urban Studies Research'' (2011), [http://www.hindawi.com/journals/usr/2011/692764/tab3/ Table 3]. Retrieved 2012-12-18. < /ref > 
 
 The sightseeing spot of Yamagata are the peaks of the Zaou, Rissyaku Temple and Yamagata castle. This place is site of Hanagaza festival. This festival is held in August. 
 
 Yamagata is hot in summer and cold in winter.
