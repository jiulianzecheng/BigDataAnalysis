 '''Ayubia''' is a protected area located in [[Abbottabad District]] in [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]], [[Pakistan]]. It is also known as '''Ayubia National Park''' and was called a national park in 1984. It is a small natural park 26 km from the [[Murree]] hill station. Ayubia was named after [[Ayub Khan]], the second [[president]] of Pakistan. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.tourism.gov.pk/ayubia_national_park.html|title=New Page 1|website=www.tourism.gov.pk|access-date=2018-01-19|archive-date=2011-10-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111015235703/http://www.tourism.gov.pk/ayubia_national_park.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1384649|title=Footprints: TOURIST TRAIL TO AYUBIA|first=Qasim A.|last=Moini|date=23 January 2018|website=DAWN.COM}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.samaa.tv/tag/ayubia/|title=ayubia|website=www.samaa.tv}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  it is managed by the Wildlife and Parks Department of Government of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Ayubia is spread around an area of {{convert|8184|acre|km2|0}}. It is surrounded by forests, villages and three towns of [[Khanaspur]], [[Nathia Gali]] and [[Thandiani]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dawn.com/news/119842|title=Development of Galyat planned, says official|date=13 October 2003|website=DAWN.COM}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Wildlife== 
 The park has 104 species of plants. Some of the main species are [[Cedrus deodara]], [[taxus|yew]], [[horse chestnut]] and [[oak]]. 
 The park harbors up to 203 species of birds. including the [[golden eagle]], the [[vulture]]. It has 31 species of mammals. It also has animals like the Asiatic [[leopard]].
