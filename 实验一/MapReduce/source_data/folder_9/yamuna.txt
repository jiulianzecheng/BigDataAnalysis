 The '''Yamuna''' ({{lang-sa|यमुना}}), sometimes called '''Jamuna''' ({{lang-bn|যমুনা}}, {{lang-hi|जमुना}}, {{lang-ur|جمنا}}) or '''Jumna''', is a major river in North India. It is the largest branch of a bigger river called the [[Ganges]]. It is 1370 [[kilometer|km]] long. It begins at the Yamunotri, which is in the [[Himalayas]]. It goes through the Indian [[state]]s of [[Delhi]], [[Haryana]], and [[Uttar Pradesh]]. At the end, it flows into the Ganges. Rivers that come out of the Yamuna include the [[Tons]] (the largest of the ones that come out of it), [[Chambal]], [[Betwa]], and [[Ken]]
