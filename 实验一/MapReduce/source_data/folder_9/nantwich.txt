 '''Nantwich''' is a market [[town]] and [[civil parish]] in the [[ceremonial counties of England|ceremonial county]] of [[Cheshire]], [[England]].  It is known for having one of the highest numbers of [[listed building]]s in England, with [[Tudor]] and [[Georgian]] [[architecture]]. In 2011, the town had a [[population]] of 17,424. 
 
 The town is around {{convert|4|mi}} south-west of the railway town of [[Crewe]] and 20 miles south-east of the city of [[Chester]]. It is on the [[Cheshire Plain]], on the banks of the [[River Weaver]]. The [[Shropshire Union Canal]] runs to the west of the town. 
 
 The origins of the settlement date to Roman times, when [[salt]] from Nantwich was used by the Roman garrisons at [[Chester]] and [[Stoke-on-Trent]] as a [[preservative]] and a [[condiment]].
