 '''Sherborne''' is a [[market town]] in northwest [[Dorset]], in [[South West England]]. It is on the [[River Yeo]], {{convert|6|mi|km}} east of [[Yeovil]]. The A30 road, which goes from [[London]] to [[Penzance]], runs through the town.

 ==Places== 
 Sherborne is best known for Sherborne Abbey, a historical building. It was a [[Anglo-Saxons|Saxon]] [[cathedral]] from the years 705–1075. It was a [[Benedictine]] abbey from the years 998–1539. Now it is a local [[church]].  
 
 Sherborne also has two [[castle]]s. One is [[ruins]] of a [[12th century]] [[fortification|fortified]] [[palace]].The second is a [[16th century]] [[mansion]] named Sherborne Castle. It was built by [[Sir Walter Raleigh]]. Much of the old town is built from [[ochre]]-coloured [[stone]]. These include the abbey and many [[medieval]] and Georgian [[era]] buildings.
