 '''Saurischia''' is one of the two [[Order (biology)|order]]s of [[dinosaur]]s. In 1888, [[Harry Seeley]] [[Scientific classification|classified]] dinosaurs into two main orders. < ref name= " seeley1888 " > Seeley, H.G. (1888).  " On the classification of the fossil animals commonly named Dinosauria. "  ''Proceedings of the Royal Society of London'', '''43''': 165-171. < /ref >  Their [[hip]] [[structure]] was why they were put into these orders. Saurischians ('lizard-hipped') and the [[Ornithischia|ornithischians]] ('bird-hipped') have differences in the ways bones in the hip are put together. 
 
 All [[carnivore|carnivorous]] dinosaurs (the [[Theropoda|theropod]]s) are saurischians, and so are the [[sauropods]] like ''[[Diplodocus]]'' and ''[[Brachiosaurus]]''. 
 
 [[Bird]]s are direct descendants of a group of theropod dinosaurs, < ref name= " padian2004 " > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/801843269|title=The dinosauria|date=2004|publisher=University of California Press|isbn=978-0-520-94143-4|edition=2nd|location=Berkeley, Calif.|oclc=801843269}} < /ref >  so they are a sub-[[clade]] of saurischian dinosaurs in modern classification.
