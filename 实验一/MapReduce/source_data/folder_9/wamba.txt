'''Wamba''' is a small [[town]] in [[Kenya]]. It is in the [[Samburu district]]. The Wamba [[administrative division]] is there. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://peerwater.org/en/organizations/103|title=Organization Info - The Samburu Project - Peer Water Exchange|website=peerwater.org}} < /ref >  The town has a big [[Catholic]] [[hospital]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.ehealth.or.ke/facilities/facility.aspx?fas=15769 |title=Catholic Hospital Wamba  & #124; eHealth Kenya |access-date=2014-01-05 |archive-date=2014-02-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140203115221/http://www.ehealth.or.ke/facilities/facility.aspx?fas=15769 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The hospital is considered important to the Samburu district. < ref > Paolo Tablino, ''Christianity Among the Nomads'', Volume 1: The Catholic Church in Northern Kenya (Nairobi: Consolata Fathers, 2006), p. 280 < /ref >  The town plays a role in the [[tourism]] industry in Kenya. It has strong ties with [[Prince William, Duke of Cambridge|Prince William]] of Britain due to his efforts to promote [[conservation]] and ties with Samburu communities. < ref > http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/africa/2013-11/22/c_132908014.htm < /ref >
