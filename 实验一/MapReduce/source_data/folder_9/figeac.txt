 '''Figeac''' ({{lang-oc|Fijac}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in southern [[France]] in the [[Regions of France|region]] of [[Occitanie]]. It is the [[Prefectures in France|''sous-prefecture'']] ([[Capital city|capital]]) of the ''[[Arrondissements of France|arrondissement]]'' of the same name in the [[Lot (department)|Lot]] [[Departments of France|department]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 In the late ninth century, the King [[Pepin I of Aquitaine]] founded a [[monastery]] in Figeac; the town developed around the monastery as an important city on the [[pilgrimage]] routes. In the 14th century, the walls of Figeac were built. 
 
 Figeac is on the [[via Podiensis]], a major [[Middle Ages|medieval]] pilgrimage [[trail]] which is part of the [[Way of St. James]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Figeac is on the southwestern edge of the central [[plateau]] (''[[Massif Central]]''), north of the valley of the [[Lot (river)|Lot]] [[river]], on the right bank of the [[Célé]] river, a left tributary of the Lot. 
 
 The ''commune'' has an area of {{Convert|35.16|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|311|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|200|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Figeac-46100/ |title=Figeac |publisher=Map-France.com |language= |accessdate=28 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref >

 == Administration == 
 Figeac is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of the Lot department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Figeac|Figeac]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the [[Canton of Figeac-1|Figeac-1]] and [[Canton of Figeac-2|Figeac-2]] cantons. 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Le Grand Figeac'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté de communes du Grand Figeac}}). 
 
 ==People from or related to Figeac== 
 * [[Jean-François Champollion]], the first translator of [[Egyptian hieroglyphs]], was born in Figeac, where there is a [[Champollion Museum]]. On the '' " Place des écritures " '' ( " Square of the Writings " ), there is a giant copy of the [[Rosetta Stone]], by [[Joseph Kosuth]]. 
 * The [[actor]] [[Charles Boyer]] was also born in Figeac.
