 '''Canterbury''' is a city in [[Kent]], [[England]]. It is about {{convert|60|mi}} from [[London]]. It was made famous by a book called ''[[The Canterbury Tales]]''. It is also famous for [[Canterbury Cathedral]]. [[Thomas Becket]] was [[murder|assassinated]] by knights who came from Sandlings. 
 ==Transportation== 
 There are two [[railway station]]s in the city; Canterbury East and Canterbury West. The [[A2 road]] passes near the city. It goes west to [[London]] and south-east to [[Dover, Kent|Dover]]. 
 ==Education== 
 The city has an estimated 31,000 students.  == History == 
 The Canterbury area has been lived in since [[prehistoric Britain|prehistoric times]]. [[Paleolithic]] stone axes, and [[Neolithic]] and [[Bronze Age]] pots have been found in the area. < ref name=Lyle > Lyle, Marjorie 2002. ''Canterbury: 2000 years of history''. Tempus, 16-43. {{ISBN|0-7524-1948-X}} 16 < /ref >  Canterbury was once the main settlement of the [[Celtic]] tribe of the [[Cantiaci]], which lived in most of modern-day [[Kent]]. In the 1st century AD, the [[Roman Empire|Romans]] captured the settlement and named it [[Durovernum Cantiacorum]]. < ref name=Lyle/ >  The Romans rebuilt the city, with new streets in a grid pattern, a theatre, a temple, a forum, and public baths. < ref name=Lyle/ >  Its position on [[Watling Street]] on route to the major Kentish ports of Rutupiae (now [[Richborough]]), Dubrae (now [[Dover]]), and Lemanae (now [[Lymne]]) made it important.
