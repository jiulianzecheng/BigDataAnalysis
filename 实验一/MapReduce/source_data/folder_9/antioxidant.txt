An '''antioxidant''' is a [[molecule]] that can slow or stop the [[Redox|oxidation]], or electron transfer, of other molecules.  
 
 Antioxidants can be found in food. 
 
 Antioxidants are molecules that relieve oxidative stress by preventing the formation and oxidation of free radicals. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  Antioxidants donate one of their electrons or hydrogen to free radicals, stopping their chain reaction. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  Found in our diet (for example, in vitamins) or formed inside our body (like enzymes), antioxidants can protect us from the damaging effects of free radicals. < ref name= " :2 "  / > 
 
 The best way to combat free radicals is to have a diet of fresh fruits and vegetables, red wine, and green tea. < ref name= " :1 "  / > < ref name= " :3 "  / >  These functional foods are rich in [[phytochemical]]s with antioxidant properties. Health supplements enriched with antioxidants are also now widely available.
