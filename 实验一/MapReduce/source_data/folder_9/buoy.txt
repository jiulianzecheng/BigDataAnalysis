A '''buoy''' is a [[Buoyancy|float]]ing device that can have many uses. It is usually [[anchor]]ed in a certain place. The word is most commonly said ({{IPA|[bɔɪ]}}) (as in [[buoyancy|''buoyant'']]),  but in American English it is often pronounced {{IPA|[ˈbu.i]}}. 
 [[File:NOAA-NDBC-discus-buoy.jpg|thumb|right|Weather buoy]] 
 
 ==Types== 
 
 ===Navigational aids=== 
 Buoys are used by ships to navigate or sail safely from place to place. They are used to mark a safe [[channel]] on a river, lake or harbour. They are used to mark dangerous rocks and other items which ships have to keep clear of. < ref > http://www.iala-aism.org/web/pages/publications/docpdf/mbsenpdf/MBS1to3.pdf Maritime Buoyage System {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061008142707/http://www.iala-aism.org/web/pages/publications/docpdf/mbsenpdf/MBS1to3.pdf |date=2006-10-08 }} < /ref >  They can also be used to mark a shipwreck, pipelines or swimming areas.

 ===Other uses=== 
 Buoys are used as [[wikt:marker|marker]] for fishing, moorings for boats, yacht or dinghy racing, diving < ref name=Davies > {{cite journal |author=Davies, D |title=Diver location devices |journal=Journal of the [[South Pacific Underwater Medicine Society]] |volume=28 |issue=3 |year=1998 |url=http://archive.rubicon-foundation.org/5968 |accessdate=2013-04-16 |archive-date=2009-05-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090519022556/http://archive.rubicon-foundation.org/5968 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  or weather stations < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.iala-aism.org/wiki/dictionary/index.php/ODAS_buoy|title=International Dictionary of Marine Aids to Navigation – ODAS buoy|last=IALA|date=2008|website=|publisher=|access-date=10 December 2016}} < /ref >  and may be marked on [[chart]]s as  " Superbuoys " .
