'''Outlook''' is a free [[webmail|email]], address book and calendar software, included with [[Microsoft Office]]. It is owned by [[Microsoft]]. It was started in 1996 as '''Hotmail'''. [[Sabeer Bhatia]] and [[Jack Smith]] created it. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.stanfordalumni.org/news/magazine/1999/sepoct/articles/bhatia.html|title=What's the Big Idea?|date=1999|last=Bronson|first=Po|website=Stanford Magazine|accessdate=10 November 2018|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060411193202/http://www.stanfordalumni.org/news/magazine/1999/sepoct/articles/bhatia.html|archivedate=11 April 2006}} < /ref >  In 1997, Microsoft bought Hotmail for about [[USD|$]]400 million. They renamed it twice, to MSN Hotmail and [[Windows Live]] Hotmail. In 2013, Hotmail became Outlook.
