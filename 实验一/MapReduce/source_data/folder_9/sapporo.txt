 {{Nihongo|'''Sapporo'''|[[wikt:札幌|札幌]][[wikt:市|市]]|Sapporo-shi}}  is the [[Capital (city)|capital city]] of [[Hokkaidō Prefecture]] in [[Japan]]. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA823 & dq=  " Sapporo " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', pp. 823-824. < /ref >  The [[port]] city is on the southwest part of the island of [[Hokkaidō]] and it is the island's largest city. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA343 & dq=  " ''Hokkaido'' " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 343. < /ref > 
 
 The city has a famous [[snow]] [[festival]] in winter, it has a growing [[population]] of about 1,800,000 [[wikt:people|people]].  Its area is {{convert|110,000|km2|mi2}}. There are a lot of fish markets in Sapporo. A [[beer]] brand named  " Sapporo "  was named after this city. [[Ramen]] is a very popular food in Sapporo. The city has a subway system. Some famous landmarks are the [[Sapporo TV Tower]], and the [[Sapporo Clock Tower|clock tower]] that is in the center of the city. There are several universities here, including [[Hokkaido University]]. The nearby airport is called [[New Chitose Airport]].
