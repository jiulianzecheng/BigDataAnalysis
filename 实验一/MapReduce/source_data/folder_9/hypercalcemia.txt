'''Hypercalcemia''' is too much [[calcium]] in the body. It is usually caused by either [[cancer]] or [[parathyroid]] disease. If hypercalcemia is severe, it may cause changes in mental status. Other symptoms can include [[nausea]], [[abdominal pain]], [[constipation]], and thirst. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/hypercalcemia/symptoms-causes/syc-20355523|title=Hypercalcemia - Symptoms and causes|website=Mayo Clinic}} < /ref > 
 Hypercalcemia can be asscociated with [[Sarcoidosis]] < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.uptodate.com/contents/hypercalcemia-in-granulomatous-diseases|title=UpToDate|website=www.uptodate.com}} < /ref >  or [[Tuberculosis]].
