 '''Moradabad''' ({{audio|Moradabad.ogg|pronunciation}}) is a city, commissionary, and a [[municipal corporation]] in [[Moradabad district]] of [[India]]n state of [[Uttar Pradesh]]. It was established in 1625A.D. by Rustam Khan and is named after prince Murad Baksh, the son of the [[Mughal Empire|Mughal]] Emperor [[Shah Jahan]]. 
 
 Moradabad is situated on the banks of the [[Ramganga]] river, at a distance of {{convert|167|km|mi|abbr=on}} from the national capital, [[New Delhi]] and 344 & nbsp;km north-west of the state capital [[Lucknow]]. The city is known as ''Pital Nagri'', ( " Brass City " ) for its famous [[brass]] handicrafts industry. < ref name= " Husain " > {{cite book | author=Majid Husain | title=Understanding: Geographical: Map Entries: for Civil Services Examinations: Second Edition | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_9VGKfQ-sQsC & pg=RA7-PA16 | access-date=6 October 2012 | publisher=Tata McGraw-Hill Education | isbn=978-0-07-070288-2 | page=7}} < /ref >  It is also the divisional headquarters of Northern Railway (NR). < ref name= " NR " > {{cite web|title=Northern Railway: Moradabad Division|url=https://nr.indianrailways.gov.in/cris/view_section.jsp?lang=0 & id=0,5,317|access-date=2015-07-29|website=Northern Railway|publisher=nr.indianrailways.gov.in}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://moradabad.nic.in |title=Moradabad (UP), India  & #124; Official Website |publisher=Moradabad.nic.in |access-date=2015-07-29}} < /ref >
