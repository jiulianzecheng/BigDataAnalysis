 {{nihongo|'''Manzai'''|漫才|}} is a [[tradition]]al kind of [[Japan]]ese [[comedy]]. < ref name= " nussbaum " > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2002).  " Manzai "  in Japan Encyclopedia, p. 608. < /ref >  ''Manzai'' is a special kind of [[stand-up comedy]]. < ref name= " torikai " > Torikai, Kumiko. (2009). [https://books.google.com/books?id=I1nu69nmlxgC & pg=PA116  ''Voices of the Invisible Presence: Diplomatic Interpreters in Post-World War II Japan,'' p. 116 n66.] < /ref >   It is both [[dialogue]] and performance. 
 
 A comedy team brings together a straight man (''[[tsukkomi]]'') who feeds punchlines to a funny man (''boke''). < ref > [http://www.japanfortheuninvited.com/articles/manzai.html   " ''Manzai'' (Double-act comedy) " ]; retrieved 2011-11-21. < /ref >   A pair of comedians with a special back-and-forth style is called ''kakeai manzai''. < ref name= " torikai " / > 
  	 
 == History == 
 The history of ''manzai'' began in the [[Nara period]]. < ref > Kodansha. (1985).  " Mansai, "   ''Encyclopedia of Japan,'' Vol. 5, p. 111. < /ref > 
 
 In the [[Edo period]], the traditional [[dialogue]] was performed on stage. < ref name= " nussbaum " / > 
 
 In modern times, ''manzai'' performances are televised in Japan. < ref name= " nussbaum " / > 
 
 ===New Year's tradition=== 
 In Japan's past, traditional ''manzai'' performers would go from house to house in the New Year's season.  They would sing songs < ref > Yamaguchi, Kenkichi ''et al.'' (1964). [https://books.google.com/books?id=NbGZAAAAIAAJ & q=manzai  ''We Japanese,'' p. 5.] < /ref >  and perform good-luck dances in return for a small amount of [[rice]] or money. < ref > Ueda, Makoto. (1995). [https://books.google.com/books?id=kmdWOrsYBgMC & pg=PA309  ''Bashō and His interpreters: Selected Hokku with Commentary,'' p. 309.] < /ref >
