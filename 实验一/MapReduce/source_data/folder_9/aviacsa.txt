 '''Aviacsa''' was a [[Mexico|Mexican]] [[airline]]. It started operations in 1990. < ref name=AI > {{cite web |url=http://www.airlines-inform.com/world_airlines/aviacsa.html |title=Aviacsa |publisher=www.airlines-inform.com |access-date=7 June 2015}} < /ref >  Aviacsa was a [[low-cost carrier]] based in [[Mexico City]]. < ref name=AI/ >  In 2009 the Mexican [[government]] refused to allow Aviacsa to fly because of [[safety]] concerns. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/travel/flights/2009-07-07-aviacsa-grounded_N.htm|title=Mexico's Aviacsa airline grounded for third time in a month - USATODAY.com|work=usatoday.com|access-date=7 June 2015}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  Aviacsa appealed the decision in court. Meanwhile, the [[FAA]] removed Aviacia's [[airworthiness certificate]] in the [[United States]] until it could resolve the safety issues in Mexico. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.flightglobal.com/news/articles/faa-blocks-mexico39s-aviacsa-from-operating-into-the-328603/ |title=FAA blocks Mexico's Aviacsa from operating into the US |author=Brendan Sobie |date=20 June 2009 |publisher=FlightGlobal |access-date=7 June 2015}} < /ref >  Aviacsa ceased operations in July 2009. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.airfleets.net/flottecie/Aviacsa.htmhttp://www.airfleets.net/flottecie/Aviacsa.htm|title=Aviacsa fleet details|publisher=Airfleets.net |access-date=7 June 2015}} < /ref >  It was sold to the Madero Group in 2010. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://aerolineasmexicanas.mx/lista-de-aerolineas-mexicanas/aviacsa-historia-y-datos |title=Aviacsa, historia y datos |publisher=Aerolíneas Mexicanas |access-date=7 June 2015}} < /ref >  In 2011, Aviacsa was going to start flying again. However, it didn't happen because the airline owed a lot of money to the Mexican Government. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.routesonline.com/news/38/airlineroute/105668/mexicos-aviacsa-to-resume-operation-from-may-2011/|title=Mexico’s Aviacsa to resume operation from May 2011|first=UBM (UK)|last=Ltd. 2019|website=Routesonline}} < /ref >  Aviacsa stopped all operations for good on May 4, 2011. < ref > http://www.travelation.com/airlines/6A-Aviacsa-Airlines{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  [[Interjet]] took over many of Aviacsa's old routes. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.flightglobal.com/news/articles/mexicos-interjet-adds-two-domestic-destinations-334348/|title=Mexico's Interjet adds two domestic destinations|first=Brendan|last=Sobie|date=3 November 2009|website=Flightglobal.com}} < /ref >
