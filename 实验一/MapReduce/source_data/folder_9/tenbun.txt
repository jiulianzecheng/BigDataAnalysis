{{nihongo|'''Tenbun'''|天文}}, also known as '''Tembun''' or '''Temmon,''' was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,| ''nengō'',| lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Kyoroku|Kyōroku]]'' and before ''[[Kōji (Muromachi period)|Kōji]].''  This period started in July 1532 and ended in October 1555. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA956   " ''Tembun'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 956. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Nara of Japan|Go-Nara]]''-tennō''|後奈良天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA257  " Go-Nara Tennō, " ] p. 257; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA372  ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 372-382.] < /ref >  or {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Nara of Japan|Go-Heizei]]''-tennō''|後平城天皇}} < ref > Titsingh, Isaac. (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & dq=nipon+o+dai+itsi+ran & pg=PP9 ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 372]–382. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Tenbun'' era== 
 [[File:Ashikaga Yoshiteru.jpg|thumb|right|Ashikaga Yoshiteru was made shōgun in the 15th year of ''Tenbun'']] 
 * '''1536''' (''Tenbun 5, 26th day of the 2nd month''): Go-Nara was formally established as emperor. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA374  p. 374]; Varley, p. 44; a distinct act of ''senso'' is unrecognized prior to [[Emperor Tenji]]; and all sovereigns except [[Empress Jitō|Jitō]],  [[Emperor Yōzei|Yōzei]], [[Emperor Go-Toba|Go-Toba]], and [[Emperor Fushimi|Fushimi]] have ''senso'' and ''sokui'' in the same year until the reign of [[Emperor Go-Murakami]]. Compare ''Kunaichō'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/e-about/seido/seido06-ph.html  Ceremony of Accession (''Sokui-no-Rei'')]; retrieved 2012-6-29. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1542''' (''Tenbun 11, 25th day of the 8th month''): Imagawa Yoshimoto of [[Suruga Province]] lost battle with army of [[Oda Nobuhide]]. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA376   p. 376.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1543''' (''Tenbun 12, 25th day of the 8th month''):  [[Portugal|Portuguese]] ship landed at [[Tanegashima]] bringing the first [[gun]] into Japan. < ref > [http://www3.pref.kagoshima.jp/foreign/english/profile/gaiyou/history_main.html  History of Kagoshima] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111108095224/http://www3.pref.kagoshima.jp/foreign/english/profile/gaiyou/history_main.html |date=2011-11-08 }}; retrieved 2011-12-6. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1543''' (''Tenbun 13, 7th month''): Flooding in Kyoto and nearby areas. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA377   p. 377.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1546''' (''Tenbun 15, 20th day of the 12th month''):  [[Ashikaga Yoshiteru]] became 13th Shōgun of the ''[[Ashikaga shogunate]]''. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA378  p. 378.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1547''' (''Tenbun 11''): [[Joseon]] and  " Treaty of Tenbun "  limited trade to Korean port of [[Pusan]]. < ref > [[John Whitney Hall|Hall, John Whitney.]] (1997). [https://books.google.com/books?id=6RBXXJixf-sC & pg=PA249  ''The Cambridge History of Japan: Early Modern Japan'', p. 249.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''15 August 1549''' (''Tenbun 18, 22nd day of the 7th month'') < !--  nengoCalc 天文十八年七月二十二日 -- > :  [[Jesuit]] [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] priest [[Francis Xavier]] arrived in Japan < ref name= " catholic " > Catholic Bishops Conference of Japan, [http://www.cbcj.catholic.jp/eng/ehistory/table01.html  " An Overview of the History of the History of the Catholic Church in Japan " ]; retrieved 2012-4-27. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1551''' (''Tenbun 20''): The first mechanical [[clock]] was brought to Japan by Xavier; and he gave it to [[Ouichi Yoshitaka]]. < ref > Oosterling, Henk. (1996). [https://books.google.com/books?id=s_nfFaHjkBwC & pg=PA96  ''Time and Temporality in Intercultural Perspective,'' p. 96.] < /ref >
