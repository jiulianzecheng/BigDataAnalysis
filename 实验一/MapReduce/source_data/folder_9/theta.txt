 '''Theta''' (uppercase '''Θ''' / lowercase '''θ'''), is a letter in the [[Greek alphabet]]. It represents the  " th "  sound (as in  " think " ) in [[Ancient Greek|Ancient]] and [[Modern Greek language|Modern]] Greek. In the system of [[Greek numerals]], it has a value of 9. The [[Cyrillic alphabet|Cyrillic]] letter [[Ѳ]] came from theta. 
 
 In [[mathematics]], the lowercase θ is used as a variable to represent an [[angle]], and the uppercase Θ is used in big-theta notation (a variant of [[Big O notation|big-O notation]]). < ref > {{Cite web|date=2020-03-20|title=Greek/Hebrew/Latin-based Symbols in Mathematics|url=https://mathvault.ca/hub/higher-math/math-symbols/greek-hebrew-latin-symbols/|access-date=2020-10-06|website=Math Vault|language=en-US}} < /ref >  The lowercase θ is also used to represent the potential temperature in [[meteorology]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Theta Symbol in Greek Alphabet|url=https://www.greeksymbols.net/theta-symbol|access-date=2020-10-06|website=www.greeksymbols.net|language=en}} < /ref >
