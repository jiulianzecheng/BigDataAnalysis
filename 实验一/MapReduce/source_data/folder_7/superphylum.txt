
 A '''superphylum''' is an unofficial [[evolutionary grade|grade]] in taxonomy, above the phylum and below the subregnum, used to describe a group of phyla which probably evolved from a common ancestor. < ref > Introduction to Conservation Genetics, p. 371; Richard Frankham, David A. Briscoe, Jonathan D. Ballou, Cambridge University Press, 2002 < /ref > 
 
 It is used especially in the [[kingdom (biology)|animal kingdom]]. Examples: 
 
 *[[Ecdysozoa]] 
 *[[Lophotrochozoa]] 
 *[[Radiata]] 
 *[[Deuterostomia]] 
 *[[Bilateria]]
