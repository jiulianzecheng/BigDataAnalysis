'''Kleksography''' is the art of making images of various figures from [[Ink|inkblots]]. [[Justinus Kerner]] was the first who included kleksography in the book of poetry. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=toArWzNbBUAC & pg=PA134|title=Howard Andrew Knox: Pioneer of Intelligence Testing at Ellis Island|last=Richardson|first=John T. E.|date=2011-11-29|publisher=Columbia University Press|isbn=978-0-231-51211-4|language=en}} < /ref >   
 
 [[Victor Hugo]] and [[George Sand]] used inkblots in their [[manuscript|manuscripts]].   
 [[File:KernerKlecksographie.png|thumb|A klecksograph by [[Justinus Kerner,]] published 1879]] 
 Since 1890, kleksography was used as the tool for work of [[unconsciousness]]. The most popular example is [[Rorschach test]].
