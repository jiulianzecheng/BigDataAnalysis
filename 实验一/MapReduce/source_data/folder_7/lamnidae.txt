 The '''Lamnidae''' are the [[Family (biology)|family]] of [[Lamniformes|mackerel sharks]] known as '''white sharks'''. < ref name= " McEachran Fechhelm 1998 " > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lAgyDQAAQBAJ|title=Fishes of the Gulf of Mexico, Vol. 1: Myxiniformes to Gasterosteiformes|last1=McEachran|first1=J.|last2=Fechhelm|first2=J.D.|publisher=University of Texas Press|year=1998|isbn=978-0-292-75206-1|series=Fishes of the Gulf of Mexico|publication-place=Austin|page=53|oclc=38468784|access-date=13 July 2021}} < /ref >  They are large, fast-swimming [[predatory fish]] found in oceans worldwide.  
 
 The name of the family is formed from the [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''lamna'', which means  " fish of prey " , and was derived from the [[Greek mythology|Greek]] [[legendary creature]], the [[Lamia]]. < ref > {{ISBN|9781258302863}}: ''A source-book of biological names and terms'', 1944, Edmund Carroll Jaeger < /ref >
