'''Graffiti''' ([[plural]]; [[singular]]: '''graffiti''' or '''graffito''', however ''graffito'' is rarely used except in [[archeology]]) is marks, scratching or drawings made on a surface on a [[public]] [[:wikt:place|place]]. It is often created with [[paint]] or [[:wikt:spray|spray]] paint (paint that is sprayed from a [[can]]). A single mark could be called a '''graffito''' but the word graffiti is usually used, meaning that there is more than one mark. The word comes from [[Greek language|Greek]] ''{{lang|el|γράφειν}}'' (''graphein'')  meaning  " to [[write]] " . < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.etymonline.com/word/graffiti|title=graffiti {{!}} Origin and meaning of graffiti by Online Etymology Dictionary|website=www.etymonline.com|language=en|access-date=19 November 2023}} < /ref > 
 
 Graffiti can take the form of [[art]], [[draw]]ings or [[word]]s. When done without a property owner's permission it can be considered [[vandalism]]. Sometimes it is just a person's name or a [[profanity|rude word]]. Sometimes it is a public [[political]] [[protest]] because graffiti is illegal without permission. 
 
 == Types == 
 There are lots of different types of graffiti, and some people call some things graffiti that other people would not. 
 
 [[File:2009 010 CES utka.JPG|thumb|230px|Beast FAHM[[Ces53]]|alt=]] 
 
 In some kinds of graffiti, people write their name in different ways. It is usually not their real name. Sometimes people write the name of their crew, which is a group of friends who write together. < ref > {{Cite journal |last1=Avramidis |first1=Konstantinos |last2=Drakopoulou |first2=Konstantina |date=2012-04-01 |title=Graffiti Crews' Potential Pedagogical Role |url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/268410433 |journal=Journal for Critical Education Policy Studies |volume=10 |pages=327–340}} < /ref >  Small writing like a signature is called a '''tag'''. < ref > {{Cite book|url=http://archive.org/details/worldatlasofstre0000scha|title=The world atlas of street art and graffiti|last=Schacter|first=Rafael|date=2013|publisher=New Haven : Yale University Press|others=Internet Archive|isbn=978-0-300-19942-0}} < /ref >  Bigger writing that is very round and has an outline is called a '''throw up'''. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Whitehead|first=Jessie L.|date=November 2004|title=Graffiti: The Use of the Familiar1|url=https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00043125.2004.11653573|journal=Art Education|language=en|volume=57|issue=6|pages=25–32|doi=10.1080/00043125.2004.11653573|issn=0004-3125}} < /ref >  A graffiti writing with lots of detail is called a '''piece''', which is short for masterpiece. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FJUUCgAAQBAJ|title=Graffiti Lives: Beyond the Tag in New York's Urban Underground|last=Snyder|first=Gregory J.|date=2011-04-15|publisher=NYU Press|isbn=978-0-8147-4046-0|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Some graffit is made of pictures. This can be a small picture or a large [[mural]]. 
 
 Graffiti can be [[Law|legal or illegal]]. Some towns have walls that anyone is allowed to paint on. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=McAuliffe|first=Cameron|date=February 2013|title=Legal Walls and Professional Paths: The Mobilities of Graffiti Writers in Sydney|url=http://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0042098012468894|journal=Urban Studies|language=en|volume=50|issue=3|pages=518–537|doi=10.1177/0042098012468894|issn=0042-0980}} < /ref >  Sometimes the government or a business will pay someone to paint, to attract tourists or advertise something. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kGKpCwAAQBAJ|title=Routledge Handbook of Graffiti and Street Art|last=Ross|first=Jeffrey Ian|date=2016-03-02|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-317-64586-3|language=en}} < /ref >  Some graffiti artists like [[Keith Haring]] became very famous. 
 
 == History == 
 [[File:Klottersanering i Stockholm.jpg|alt=|left|thumb|A person cleaning up graffiti in [[Stockholm]]]] 
 Paintings were done on cave walls over 40,000 years ago in Australia. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZnGCDwAAQBAJ|title=The Popular History of Graffiti: From the Ancient World to the Present|last=McDonald|first=Fiona|date=2013-06-13|publisher=Simon and Schuster|isbn=978-1-62636-291-8|language=en}} < /ref >  The oldest graffiti that used [[writing]] was in the [[Roman Empire]], people wrote messages on walls. It wasn't considered vandalism, instead people saw it as just a way to talk to one another. < ref > {{Cite web|last1=Magazine|first1=Smithsonian|last2=Griggs|first2=Mary Beth|title=Archaeologists in Greece Find Some of the World's Oldest Erotic Graffiti|url=https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/erotic-graffiti-found-greece-180951979/|access-date=2023-09-13|website=Smithsonian Magazine|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 In the 1700s and 1800s in the [[United States]], some people began to write  " monikers "  on trains. Monikers were pictures and fake names. One famous moniker was Bozo Texino. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Who Is Bozo Texino?|url=http://billdaniel.squarespace.com/who-is-bozo-texino|access-date=2023-09-13|website=Bill Daniel|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 Modern graffiti was made famous in [[New York City]] in the early 1960s and 1970s by the children of the working class. They called it  " writing "  and called themselves  " writers. " < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ozuHIgPK_cAC|title=Subway Art|last1=Cooper|first1=Martha|last2=Chalfant|first2=Henry|date=1984|publisher=Macmillan|isbn=978-0-8050-0678-0|language=en}} < /ref >  At first they wrote on trains, but when the New York Subway started to make it harder, writers moved into the city to write on walls instead. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Snyder|first=Gregory J.|date=April 2006|title=Graffiti media and the perpetuation of an illegal subculture|url=http://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1741659006061716|journal=Crime, Media, Culture: An International Journal|language=en|volume=2|issue=1|pages=93–101|doi=10.1177/1741659006061716|issn=1741-6590}} < /ref > 
 
 In a 1971 New York Times article, Norman Mailer first used the term  " graffiti "  was first used to refer to the new kind of art people were painting on trains. < ref > {{Cite web|title=The history of graffiti|url=https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/skills/reading/b2-reading/history-graffiti|access-date=2023-09-13|website=learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org|language=en}} < /ref >  This period of the early 1970s is known as the  " Original School. "  Later in the 1980s, the next generation of graffiti artists is known as  " Old School. "
