 '''Chinon''' is a [[France|French]] [[Communes of France|commune]]. It is in the west of the [[Indre-et-Loire]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Centre-Val de Loire]] [[Regions of France|region]], to the southwest of [[Paris]], and is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] in the department. It is also the [[Capital city|capital]] of the ''arrrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Chinon|Chinon]] and the [[administrative centre]] of the [[Canton of Chinon|Chinon]] canton. 
 
 Chinon is in the [[valley]] of the [[Loire River|Loire]] [[river]], registered as a [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site]] since 2000. 
 
 ==History== 
 The historic town of Chinon is on the banks of the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]] river about {{convert|10|km|0}} from where it joins the [[Loire River|Loire]].

 == Administration == 
 Chinon is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of the Indre-et-Loire department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Chinon|Chinon]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the [[Cantons of France|canton]] of [[Canton of Chinon|Chinon]], with 34,893 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Chinon, Vienne et Loire'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté de communes Chinon, Vienne et Loire}}).
