 '''Nevers''' is a [[Communes of France|''commune'']] in the [[Nièvre]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]] in central France. It is the ''[[Prefectures in France|préfecture]]'' ([[Capital city|capital]]) of the department. 
 
 It is the main city of the department and one of the largest city of the region, after [[Dijon]] and [[Chalon-sur-Saône]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 The name of the city comes from the name of the ''[[Nièvre (Loire)|Nièvre]]'' river. [[Julius Caesar]] named the town as '''''Noviodunum''''' but later was named as '''''Nebirnum''''', after the name of the river. 
 
 Nevers was the capital of the old [[province]] of ''Nivernais'' and became the capital of Nièvres on 4 March 1790 with the creation of the departments in France.

 Nevers is at about {{Convert|260|km|mi|abbr=on }} to the southeast of [[Paris]], on the [[confluence]] of the [[Loire (river)|Loire]] and the [[Nièvre (Loire)|Nièvre]] [[river]]s.

 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for Nevers is an  " [[Oceanic climate]] "  (also known as Maritime Temperate climate) and of the subtype '''Cfb'''. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Nevers, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=598834 & cityname=Nevers%2C+Bourgogne%2C+France & units= & set=metric |language= |accessdate=8 October 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Nevers is {{Convert|10.9|°C|°F}}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|19.2|°C|°F}}. The coolest month, on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|3.5|°C|°F}}. 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Nevers is {{Convert|607.1|mm|in|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is May with {{Convert|73.7|mm|in|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is February with an average of {{Convert|27.9|mm|in|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / >

 The inhabitants of Nevers are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Nivernais'' (women: ''Nivernaises'') but they are also known as ''Neversois'' (women: ''Neversoises''), leaving Nivernais for the people of the department. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_nevers_58194.html |title=Nevers (58000) |publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=8 October 2014 |date= }} < /ref >

 ==Administration== 
 Nevers is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Nièvre department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Nevers|Nevers]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of four cantons: 
 * [[Canton of Nevers-1|Nevers-1]] formed by the ''commune'' [[Coulanges-lès-Nevers]] and part of Nevers, with 11,738 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Nevers-2|Nevers-2]] formed by 3 ''communes'' and part of Nevers, with 11,570 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Nevers-3|Nevers-3]] formed by 3 ''communes'' and part of Nevers, with 11,874 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 * [[Canton of Nevers-4|Nevers-4]] formed by a part of Nevers, with 10,448 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Nevers Agglomération'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération de Nevers}}).

 ==Places of interest== 
 Some interesting places in Nevers are: 
 * The '''Nevers Cathedral''' (''Cathédrale Saint-Cyr-et-Sainte-Julitte'') is a [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholic]] [[cathedral]], dedicated to [[Quiricus and Julietta|Saints Cyricus and Julitta]]. 
 * The '''Palais Ducal''' ( " Ducal Palace " , now occupied by the courts of justice and an important [[ceramic]] [[museum]]) was built in the 15th and 16th centuries. 
 * The '''Porte du Croux''', a square [[tower]] dating from the end of the 14th century.
