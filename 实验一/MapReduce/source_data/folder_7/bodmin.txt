'''Bodmin''' is a town on the [[A30 road]], in [[Cornwall]], United Kingdom. It is in the centre of the county southwest of [[Bodmin Moor]]. < ref > Ordnance Survey: Landranger map sheet 200 ''Newquay  &  Bodmin'' {{ISBN|978-0-319-22938-5}} < /ref > 
 
 The neighbouring parishes are Cardinham, Lanhydrock, Lanivet and Helland. < ref > [http://mapping.cornwall.gov.uk/website/ccmap/ Cornwall Council online mapping] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927050041/http://mapping.cornwall.gov.uk/website/ccmap/ |date=2013-09-27 }}. Retrieved May 2010 < /ref > 
 
 In 2001 there were 12,881 people living in Bodmin. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=3 & b=792818 & c=bodmin & d=16 & e=15 & g=430597 & i=1001x1003x1004 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1304161013812 & enc=1 & dsFamilyId=779 |title=2001 population |access-date=2011-04-30 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304232020/http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=3 & b=792818 & c=bodmin & d=16 & e=15 & g=430597 & i=1001x1003x1004 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1304161013812 & enc=1 & dsFamilyId=779 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 In the Middle Ages Bodmin was one of the most important towns in Cornwall and the site of a monastery founded by Saint Petroc. The parish church of Bodmin was built in the 15th century and is the second largest church in the county after Truro Cathedral.
