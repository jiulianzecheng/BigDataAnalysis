 '''Chemotherapy''', or 'chemo', is the use of [[chemical substance]]s to treat [[disease]]s. The word  " chemotherapy "  is often used for a type of medicine used to treat [[cancer]]. The drugs are [[cytotoxic]], which means they are [[toxic]] to the body's [[cell]]s. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/types/chemotherapy|accessdate=29 May 2018|title=Chemotherapy to Treat Cancer - National Cancer Institute|date=29 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 There are many types of chemotherapy. and doctors chose the medication that will treat each types of cancer in the best way. Often, chemotherapy drugs are combined with each other and with other drugs when a person is treated for cancer. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/understanding/what-is-cancer|accessdate=29 May 2018|title=What is Cancer? - National Cancer Institute|date=17 September 2007}} < /ref > 
 
 When a person has surgery to remove a tumor, chemotherapy may be given before or after.  
 
 Chemotherapy can also be used to treat other diseases, such as [[rheumatoid arthritis]].  
 
 Chemotherapy often has bad [[Adverse effect (medicine)|side effects]]: 
 * Loss or damage of hair and [[fingernail|nails]] 
 * Weakened immune system 
 * [[Nausea]] and [[vomiting]]
