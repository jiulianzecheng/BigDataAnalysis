 In the [[United States]], a '''plat''' ({{IPAc-en|p|l|æ|t}} < ref > ''American Heritage Dictionary'', 3rd ed., s.v.  " plat. " < /ref >  or {{IPAc-en|p|l|ɑː|t}}) < ref > from a letter published in The Gentleman's Magazine, Volume 72, Part 1, London, 1802 [https://books.google.com/books?id=Nc6JTJZxH4EC & pg=PA212]:  " ... many attorneys ought to be whipt for not knowing how to spell; that ''plot'' a conspiracy and ''plat'' a piece of ground were pronounced exactly alike ... " < /ref >  ('''plan''') is a map that shows the parts of a piece of land for where houses and buildings will be built.
