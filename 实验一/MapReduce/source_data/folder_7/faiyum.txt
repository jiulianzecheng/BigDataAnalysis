 '''Faiyum''' < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.lonelyplanet.com/egypt/medinat-al-fayoum|title=Al Fayoum travel - Lonely Planet|last=Planet|first=Lonely|website=Lonely Planet|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171205200103/https://www.lonelyplanet.com/egypt/medinat-al-fayoum|archive-date=5 December 2017|url-status=live|access-date=3 May 2018}} < /ref >  ({{Lang-ar|الفيوم}}; {{Lang-cop| & nbsp; & #x300;Ⲫⲓⲟⲙ or Ⲫⲓⲱⲙ}} & nbsp;̀Ⲫⲓⲟⲙ or Ⲫⲓⲱⲙ) is a city in the Middle of Egypt. It is {{Convert|100|km|0}} away from [[Cairo]]. The city is located in the Faiyum Oasis, and it is the capital of Faiyum Governorate. The city was called '''Shedet''' in Ancient Egypt. When the Greeks took Egypt, they called it '''Crocodilopolis''' or '''Krocodilopolis.''' the Romans called it '''Arsinoë'''. It is one of Egypt's oldest cities, and it has a strategic location. < ref name=Riggs > {{cite book|author1=Paola Davoli|editor1-last=Riggs|editor1-first=Christina|title=The Oxford Handbook of Roman Egypt|date=2012|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=9780199571451|pages=152-153|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZOLuaRusoCgC & pg=PA152|chapter=The Archaeology of the Fayum}} < /ref >

 == Main sights == 
 
 * Hawara, archeological site {{Convert|27|km|0}} from the city 
 * Lahun Pyramids, {{Convert|4|km|0}} outside the city 
 * Qaitbay Mosque, in the city. the wife of the [[Mamluk]] [[Sultan]] [[Qaitbay]] built it. 
 * [[Qasr Qarun]], {{Convert|44|km|0}} from the city 
 * [[Wadi Elrayan]] or Wadi Rayan, the largest waterfalls in Egypt, around {{Convert|50|km|0}} from the city 
 * Wadi Al-Hitan or Valley of whales, It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
