 '''''Ugni''''' is a [[genus]] of plants of the [[myrtle]] [[Family (biology)|family]] ([[Myrtaceae]]). Four [[species]] belong to this genus, all from [[western]] [[America]]. 
 
 ==Description== 
 
 They are small [[evergreen]] [[shrub]]s. The [[Leaf|leaves]] are simple, entire, opposite, [[Ellipse|elliptical]]; they are 1–2 & nbsp;cm long and 0.2-2.5 & nbsp;cm broad, dark green, and with a [[Spice|spicy]] [[Odor|scent]] if broken into many small pieces. < ref name= " eflora " > {{cite web |url=http://www.efloras.org/florataxon.aspx?flora_id=201 & taxon_id=134091|format = |title='''Ugni''' Turcz.|author=Carmen Ulloa Ulloa  &  Peter Møller Jørgensen|work=Árboles y arbustos de los Andes del Ecuador |publisher=eFloras.org |language=Spanish |accessdate=24 July 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 The solitary [[flowers]] are usually hanging; they are 1–2 & nbsp;cm [[diameter]] with four or five white or pale pink [[petal]]s and many short [[stamen]]s. The [[fruit]] is a small red or purple [[berry]], 1 & nbsp;cm in diameter, with many [[seed]]s. < ref name= " eflora "  / > 
 
 ==Name== 
 The name comes from ''Uñi'' with which the [[Mapuche]]s ([[Native American|native people]] from south-central Chile and southwestern Argentina) name the [[fruit]]s of the best known species of the genus, ''Ugni molinae''. 
 
 The genus was formerly often included in either ''[[Myrtus]]'' or ''[[Eugenia]]''; it is distinguished from these two [[Genus|genera]] by the hanging flowers with stamens shorter than the petals. 
 
 The [[Russia]]n [[botanist]] [[Nicolai Stepanowitsch Turczaninow]] created the genus ''Ugni'' in 1849. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/34378135 |format = |title= Bulletin de la Société impériale des naturalistes de Moscou, xxi, No. 1|date=1848 |author=Nicolai Stepanowitsch Turczaninow |page=579 |language=Latin |accessdate=24 July 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Where it grows== 
 All species of this genus are found in [[west]]ern [[South America|South]] and [[Central America]], native from southern [[Mexico]] to central southern [[Chile]] and [[Argentina]]. 
 
 ==Species== 
 There are four species in this genus:' < ref name = Kew > {{cite web |url=http://apps.kew.org/wcsp/qsearch.do?page=quickSearch & plantName=Ugni |format =  |title=Ugni|author=[[Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew]] |work= World Checklist of Selected Plant Families |language= |accessdate=24 July 2013}} < /ref > < ref name =plantlist > {{cite web |url=http://www.theplantlist.org/tpl/search?q=Ugni |format =  |title=Search results - Ugni |author= |work=The Plant List |language= |accessdate=24 July 2013}} < /ref > 
 * ''[[Ugni candollei]]'' (Barnéoud) O.Berg: Chile 
 * ''[[Ugni molinae]]''[[Nicolai Stepanowitsch Turczaninow|Turcz.]]: Chile and Argentina 
 * ''[[Ugni myricoides]]'' (Kunth) O.Berg: Southern Mexico, Central America to [[Bolivia]], [[Venezuela]] and northern [[Brazil]] 
 * ''[[Ugni selkirkii]]'' (Hook.  &  Arn.) O.Berg: [[Endemism|Endemic]] species of the [[Juan Fernández Islands]], Chile 
 
 ==Uses== 
 ''[[Ugni molinae]]'' is grown as an [[ornamental plant]] and for its edible fruits; these fruits are also used to make [[jam]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.nap.edu/openbook.php?record_id=1398 & page=219 |format =  |title=Lost Crops of the Incas: |author= |publisher=The National Academies Press |language= |accessdate=24 July 2013}} < /ref >
