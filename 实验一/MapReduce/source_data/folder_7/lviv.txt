 '''Lviv''' ({{lang-uk|Львів}}) is the [[capital city]] of [[Lviv Oblast]] in [[Ukraine]]. 860,000 people live in Lviv. < ref > [http://www.auc.org.ua/city/?sid=5 & ln=en] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928001631/http://www.auc.org.ua/city/?sid=5 & ln=en |date=2007-09-28 }} 2001 estimate. URL accessed on June 20, 2006 < /ref >  88% of the people living there are [[Ukrainians|Ukrainian]], 8% are [[Russians|Russian]], and 1% are [[Poles|Polish]]. An extra 200,000 people [[commuting|commute]] to Lviv every day for work. 
 
 During the city's history, it was ruled by many countries. When part of [[Poland]] it was called '''Lwów''' (pronounced and sometimes spelled ''Lvov''). In [[German language|German]], it was known as '''Lemberg''', that was part of the [[Austria]]n and [[Austro-Hungarian Empire]]s and again under [[Nazism|Nazi German]] occupation. From 1350 until 1772 it belonged to the Kingdom of Poland and from 1918 to 1939 to the Republic of Poland.
