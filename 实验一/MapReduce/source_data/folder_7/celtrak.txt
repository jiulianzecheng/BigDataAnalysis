 }}'''Celtrak Ltd.''' is an [[Information technology|IT]] and [[electronic engineering]] company in [[Galway]], Ireland. Celtrak works across [[Europe]], [[North America]], and the [[Middle East]]. The company makes GPS products for [[civil engineering]] and utilities in [[Ireland]], the [[United Kingdom|UK]] and [[Europe]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Celtrak Limited was founded in 2000. 
 
 The company also undertook wireless technology research in partnership with NUIG.  < ref > {{cite web|title=SFI Awards Major Research Centre to NUI Galway|url=http://www.nuigalway.ie/press/research_matters/documents/research_matters_may2003.pdf|url-status=dead|accessdate=2008-09-17|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060820060733/http://www.nuigalway.ie/press/research_matters/documents/research_matters_may2003.pdf|archivedate=2006-08-20}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://ee.nuigalway.ie/research/collaborator/9|title=Celtrak|accessdate=2008-09-17|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071128034240/http://www.ee.nuigalway.ie/research/collaborator/9|archivedate=2007-11-28}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://car.nuigalway.ie/|title=Image, Signal and Communication Processing for the Automotive Environment|publisher=Car.nuigalway.ie|date=2009-05-12|accessdate=2016-09-21}} < /ref >  The company has been involved in developing technology to help drivers improve fuel efficiency. 
 
 The company now focuses on delivering GPS solutions to the civil engineering and utilities space in [[Ireland]], the [[United Kingdom|UK]] and [[Europe]]. Oil distribution companies also use the technology for fleet management.
