 The '''latitude''' of the [[Earth]] gives the distance north or south of the [[equator]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.satsig.net/lat_long.htm 
  |title=Explanation of Latitude and Longitude 
  |author= |website= 
  |publisher=Satellite Signals Limited |accessdate=27 June 2016}} < /ref >  It is measured in degrees. Latitude is represented by the Greek letter [[phi (letter)|phi]],  < math > \phi\,\! < /math > . It is usually used along with a measurement of [[longitude]] in order to pinpoint a location on Earth. 
 
 The equator is numbered 0 degrees. Everything north or south of the equator is designated either as '''north latitude''' or '''south latitude'''. The [[north pole]] is 90 degrees north, and the [[south pole]] is 90 degrees south. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://nationalgeographic.org/encyclopedia/latitude/ 
  |title=latitude |author= |website= 
  |date=6 November 2012 |publisher=National Geographic Society |accessdate=27 June 2016}} < /ref >  The [[Antarctic Circle]], [[Tropic of Capricorn]], [[Tropic of Cancer]], and [[Arctic Circle]] are all circles of latitude.
