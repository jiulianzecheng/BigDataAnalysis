 '''Junipers''' is the name for a number of [[species]] of [[conifer]]ous [[plant]]s in the [[genus]] ''Juniperus''. They are related to the  [[family (biology)|family]] ''Cupressaceae'' (the [[cypress]]). There are between 50 and 67 species of juniper. Junipers grow in the [[northern hemisphere]].  
 
 Junipers are [[evergreen]]. They vary in size from low [[shrub]]s to tall [[tree]]s, which grow to between twenty and forty metres tall.  
 
 == Juniper berries == 
 The [[female]] trees have [[seed cone]]s. These look like like a [[berry]], and are called [[juniper berry|juniper berries]]. They take from six to eighteen months to grow after [[pollination]]. They are often [[aromatic]], and can be used as a [[spice]]. 
 
 Unfortunately, the berries of some species, such as ''[[Juniperus sabina]]'', are [[toxic]]. Eating them them is not advised. < ref name= " grieve " > {{cite book | author=Grieve, M. | title=A Modern Herbal | publisher=Penguin | year=1984 | isbn=978-0-14-046440-5 }} < /ref >  ''Juniper'' has a number of species, and you have to know what you are doing before you eat the berries.
