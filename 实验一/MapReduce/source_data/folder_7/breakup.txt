 The word  " '''breakup''' "  is an [[idiom]]. It is when an [[intimate relationship]] ends. The term is not used for relationships that end because of [[death]]. [[Marriage]]s which end are not normally called breakups. 
 
 ==Reasons== 
 A breakup can happen when one person is unhappy in their relationship. If a person ends a relationship, that person  " [[wikt:dump|dumps]] "  their partner. < ref > {{cite web|url= http://onlineslangdictionary.com/thesaurus/words+meaning+to+break+up+with.html|title=Dumping|publisher=The Online Slang Dictionary  
 |access-date=2012-05-28}} < /ref > 
 
 Some relationships end because both people are unhappy. The breakup is then  " [[wikt:mutual|mutual]] " . 
 
 People in a relationship may be unhappy because of differences in: < ref > {{cite journal|last=Hill|first=Charles T.|author2=Rubin Zick|author3=Peplau Letita Anne|title=Breakups Before Marriage: The End of 103 Affairs|journal=Journal of Social Issues|year=1976|volume=32|pages=147–168|doi=10.1111/j.1540-4560.1976.tb02485.x|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1540-4560.1976.tb02485.x/abstract|access-date=26 September 2012}} < /ref > 
 * [[Ageing|age]] 
 * [[intelligence]] 
 * [[physical attractiveness]] 
 
 ==Grief== 
 After a breakup, people may feel grief or unhappiness. Their [[personality]] or  " sense of [[social value]] "  may change. < ref > Steve Duck et al, ''The Basics of Communication'' (2011) p. 151 Table 6.2 < /ref > 
 
 People may need  " time to [[wikt:heal|heal]] "  after a breakup. This time can depend on the length of the relationship. 
 
 ==Statistics== 
 eNation did a [[survey]] on breakups in 2008. Almost one in three [[Americans]] were in a breakup within the last ten years. Young people were more likely to have been in more than one breakup. < ref > Fetto, John (2003) – ''Love Stinks: Statistics on Failed Relationships''.
