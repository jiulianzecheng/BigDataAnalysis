'''Alnmouth''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|æ|l|(|ə|)|n|m|aʊ|θ}}) is a [[village]] on the [[coast]] of [[Northumberland]], England. It is 4 miles (6 km) south east of the [[town]] of [[Alnwick]]. The population of Alnmouth in 2001 was 562. However at the 2011 census the population was less at 445. < ref > {{cite web|title=Civil Parish population 2011|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadKeyFigures.do?a=7 & b=11119952 & c=alnmouth & d=16 & e=62 & g=6452726 & i=1001x1003x1032x1004 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1453976478832 & enc=1|access-date=28 January 2016|archive-date=11 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160311102334/http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadKeyFigures.do?a=7 & b=11119952 & c=alnmouth & d=16 & e=62 & g=6452726 & i=1001x1003x1032x1004 & m=0 & r=1 & s=1453976478832 & enc=1|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Alnmouth is at the end of the [[River Aln]] (the [[Mouth (river)|mouth]]). The village had a [[port]] supporting a small [[fishing]] [[industry]]. Fishing and trade decreased at the end of the [[19th century]]. Alnmouth is a now a [[tourist attraction]]. It is within the {{ill|Northumberland Coast|lt=Northumberland Coast|en}} [[Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty]].
