 '''''Triprion''''' or '''shovel-headed tree frogs''' is a genus of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]].  They live in [[Mexico]] and [[Guatemala]].  They live on the Pacific side of the mountains (west side), the [[Yucatán Peninsula]], and Guatemala. These frogs hide in tree-holes and put their bony heads against the opening of the hole to close it. 
 
 ==Species== 
 Scientists say there are three species in this genus: 
 * ''[[Triprion petasatus]]'' {{small|(Cope, 1865)}} - Yucatán shovel-headed tree frog 
 * ''[[Triprion spatulatus]]'' {{small|(Günther, 1882)}} - Mexican shovel-headed tree frog 
 * ''[[Triprion spinosus]]'' {{small|(Steindachner, 1864)}} - Spiny-headed tree frog
