 '''Abigail''' is a feminine [[given name]]. The name is from the [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] name ''Avigail'', which indicates  " father rejoice " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=Abigail name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Abigail-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-26|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  This a popular name, especially in 2005. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Top 5 Names in Each of the Last 100 Years|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/top5names.html|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref > The pronunciation of this name is [ ab-i-geyl ]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of abigail {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/abigail|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref > The name can be shortened to '''Abbie''' or '''Abby'''. 
 
 The shorthand ''Abby'' was used for several Atlantic hurricanes in the 1960s. 
 
 This name was ranked #17 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-26|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >   
 
 People with the name include [[Abigail Breslin]], [[Abigail Adams]] and [[Abbie Cornish]].

 * [[Abigail Breslin]] (born 1996), American actress 
 * [[Abbie Cornish]] (born 1982), Australian actress 
 * [[Abigail Fillmore]] (1798–1853), wife of U.S. President [[Millard Fillmore]] 
 * [[Abigail and Brittany Hensel]], (born 1990), American conjoined twins 
 * [[Abigail (actress)|Abigail]] (born as Abigail Rogan) (born 1946), Australian actress 
 * [[Abigail Kubeka]] (born 1945), South African jazz musician and actress 
 * [[Abigail Spears]] (born 1981), American tennis player 
 * [[Abigail Goldman]], American journalist 
 * [[Abigail Spencer]], American actress
