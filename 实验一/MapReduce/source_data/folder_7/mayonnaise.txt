 '''Mayonnaise''' (also known as '''Mayo''') is a thick [[sauce]]. It is an [[emulsion]] of [[vegetable oil]] and [[Egg (food)|egg]] yolk, with either [[lemon]] juice or [[vinegar]]. < ref > Mayonnaise is an emulsion of oil droplets suspended in a base composed of egg yolk, lemon juice or vinegar, which provides both flavor and stabilizing particles and carbohydrates. "  On Food and Cooking, Harold McGee, Scribner, New York, 2004 page 633. < /ref >  It is often used as a [[condiment]] for food such as [[salad]]s, [[sandwich]]es and [[french fries]].
