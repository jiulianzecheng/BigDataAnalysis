The name '''Chloe''' [ '''kloh'''-ee ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Chloe {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/chloe|access-date=2023-04-05|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[female]] [[given name]]. It is especially popular in [[United Kingdom]]. The name comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''khloē'', one of many names of the Greek goddess Demeter. Chloe is a name that has been popular for baby girls for centuries and is renowned for its beauty. The name's Greek roots translate to  " blooming "  or  " young green shoot, "  evoking imagery of growth and vitality. It is widely associated with elegance, charm, and feminine qualities. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Chloe name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Chloe-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-05|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The name refers to young, green foliage. The name appears in [[New Testament]]. In [[Northern Ireland]], ''Chloe'' was among the most popular names for babies between 1997 and 2002.  
 
 People named ''Chloe'' include [[Chloë Moretz]], [[Chloë Sevigny]] and [[Khloé Kardashian]]. Fictional Chloes include Chloe Sweeney, played by [[Amanda Seyfried]] in the 2009 movie  " Chloe "  , Chloe Pain and Chloe Ive.
