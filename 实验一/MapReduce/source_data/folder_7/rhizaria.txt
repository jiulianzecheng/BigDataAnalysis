 The '''Rhizaria''' are a supergroup of mostly [[unicellular]] [[eukaryote]]s. < ref > {{cite web | url = http://www.palaeos.com/Eukarya/Units/Rhizaria/Rhizaria.html | year = 2004 | author = Christopher Taylor | title = Rhizaria | access-date = 2014-07-04 | archive-date = 2009-04-20 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090420034036/http://www.palaeos.com/Eukarya/Units/Rhizaria/Rhizaria.html | url-status = dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " pmid15148395 " > {{cite journal |author=Nikolaev S.I. | display-authors = etal |title=The twilight of Heliozoa and rise of Rhizaria, an emerging supergroup of amoeboid eukaryotes |journal=Proc.  This supergroup was proposed by [[Thomas Cavalier-Smith|Cavalier-Smith]] in 2002. < ref > {{cite journal | first = Thomas| last = Cavalier-Smith| title = The phagotrophic origin of eukaryotes and phylogenetic classification of Protozoa| journal = International Journal of Systematic and Evolutionary Microbiology| year = 2002 | volume = 52 | issue = 2 | pages = 297–354| doi = 10.1099/00207713-52-2-297| issn = 1466-5026| url = http://ijs.sgmjournals.org/cgi/content/abstract/52/2/297| accessdate = 2007-06-08| pmid = 11931142}} < /ref >  It has many species. They vary considerably in form, but for the most part they are [[amoeba]]-like with  [[pseudopod]]s. Many produce shells or skeletons, which may be quite complex in structure. They make up the vast majority of protozoan fossils. Nearly all have [[mitochondrion|mitochondria]] with folds. 
 
 == Groups == 
 There are three main groups of Rhizaria: < ref name= " pmid17174576 " > {{cite journal |author=Moreira D. | display-authors = etal |title=Global eukaryote phylogeny: combined small- and large-subunit ribosomal DNA trees support monophyly of Rhizaria, Retaria and Excavata |journal=Mol. Phylogenet. Evol. |volume=44 |issue=1 |pages=255–66 |date=2007 |pmid=17174576 |doi=10.1016/j.ympev.2006.11.001 |url=http://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S1055-7903(06)00433-7}} < /ref > 
 
 * [[Cercozoa]] – Various amoebae and flagellates, usually with [[pseudopod]]s and common in soil 
 * [[Foraminifera]] – Amoeboids with reticulose pseudopods, common as marine [[benthos]] 
 * [[Radiolaria]] – Amoeboids with skeletons, common as marine [[plankton]]
