 '''Saiph''' is the sixth-brightest [[star]] in the [[Constellation|constellation]] of [[Orion (constellation)|Orion]]. It is the star at the South-Eastern corner of Orion. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Ian|date=2020-02-04|title=Saiph (Îº Orionis) {{!}} Facts, Information, History  &  Definition|url=https://nineplanets.org/saiph-%CE%BA-orionis/|access-date=2023-12-11|website=The Nine Planets|language=en-US}} < /ref >  When viewing Orion from the [[Northern Hemisphere|northern hemisphere]], Saiph would be found in the lower left of Orion. A [[southern hemisphere]] viewer would find Saiph in the upper right section of the constellation. [[Parallax]] measurements have found that Saiph is about 650 [[Light year|light years]] away from the sun, which is about the same as [[Betelgeuse]]. Although being smaller and less bright, Saiph has a hotter surface than [[Rigel]].
