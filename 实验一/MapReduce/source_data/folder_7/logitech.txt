 '''Logitech''' (sometimes shortened to '''Logi)''' is a Swiss [[company]] that makes [[electronics]]. They are best known for making [[peripheral equipment]] for computers, including [[webcam]]s, [[Keyboard (computer)|keyboards]] and [[computer mouse|computer mice]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Logitech was founded in [[Apples, Vaud|Apples, Switzerland]], in 1981 by graduates of [[Stanford University]] Daniel Borel and Pierluigi Zappacosta, and former Olivetti engineer Giacomo Marini. < ref > Peter Day, BBC .  In 2001, Logitech acquired Labtec for $150 million to expand its range of computer peripherals.

 In April 2016, Logitech agreed to pay {{US$|7.5 & nbsp;million}} penalty related to accusations that it and some former executives improperly inflated the company's results for its 2011 fiscal year to meet guidance and other accounting violations.  
 
 On 12 April 2016, Logitech  announced that they had agreed to acquire [[:en:Jaybird_(company)|Jaybird]], a leader in wireless audio wearables for sports and active lifestyles, for {{US$|50 & nbsp;million}}, with an additional earnout of up to {{US$|45 & nbsp;million}} based on achievement of growth targets. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2016/04/12/logitech-snaps-up-wireless-headset-maker-jaybird-for-50m/?guccounter=1 & guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8 & guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAMD_FWOeHIJBhT89gcnlu8kLbqxLwjRKHjv8dSso2db2W9Po4SVboKZWoUnDf7YRmThIrxNKjTvPqjotFReUOq8sYj7AnlRoXeDWSldmo1JibYKk6NHOjiVXQnNMC8z2kgGNnBqNSqUSYrrnUmzbfmca0eUJaZUOXZ-HTOZ0PwAr|title=Logitech snaps up wireless earbuds maker JayBird for $50M|date=13 April 2016|publisher=Tech Crunch}} < /ref > 
 
 On 15 September 2016, Logitech announced that they had purchased the [[Saitek]] brand and assets from [[Mad Catz]] for {{US$|13 & nbsp;million}}. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.gamesindustry.biz/articles/2016-09-16-mad-catz-sells-saitek-for-usd13-million|title=Logitech buys Saitek from Mad Catz|publisher=Games Industry Biz|date=16 September 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 On 26 September 2019, Logitech acquired [[Streamlabs]], producer of software and tools for live-streaming, for approximately $89 million. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2019/09/26/logitech-acquires-streamlabs-to-embed-itself-more-into-game-broadcasting/|title=Logitech acquires Streamlabs to get deeper into game broadcasting|first=Jeff|last=Grubb|date=September 26, 2019|access-date=September 26, 2019|work=[[Venture Beat]]}} < /ref >

 == Mice == 
 The first [[mice]] were made in [[Switzerland]]. Now Logitech makes its items all over the world. Selling computer mice made Logitech a big company. In 2008, Logitech made its billionth mouse. < ref > {{cite news | url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/technology/7751627.stm | title=Firm makes one billionth mouse | date=3 December 2008 | publisher=[[BBC News]] | accessdate=2008-12-03}} < /ref > 
 
 == Product lines == 
 As well as [[webcam]]s, [[Keyboard (computer)|keyboards]] and mice, Logitech also makes other products such as: 
 * [[Loudspeaker]]s 
 * Gamepads 
 * Phone accessories 
 * Special TV remotes 
 *Headphones 
 *Programmable remote controls 
 
 Logitech has also made two computer games: ''[[Jelly Jumper]]'' and ''[[Jelly Battle]]''.
