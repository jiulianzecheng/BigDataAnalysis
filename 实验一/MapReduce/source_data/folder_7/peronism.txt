 '''Peronism'''{{efn|{{lang-es|peronismo}}}} or '''justicialism'''{{efn|{{lang-es|justicialismo}}. The [[Justicialist Party]] is the main Peronist party in Argentina, it derives its name from the concept of [[social justice]].|name=|group=}} is an [[Argentina|Argentine]] political movement based on the ideas and legacy of [[President of Argentina|Argentine president]] [[Juan Perón]] (1895–1974). < ref name= " britannica " > {{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Peronist|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Peronist|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2019|website=[[Encyclopedia Britannica]]|language=en}} < /ref >  It has been an influential movement in Argentine politics. < ref name= " britannica "  / >  Since 1946 Peronists have won 10 out of the 13 < !-- Economist source says 11, but is from before the 2019 elections -- >  presidential elections in which they have been allowed to run. < ref name= " economist " > {{Cite news|url=https://www.economist.com/the-americas/2015/10/15/the-persistence-of-peronism|title=The persistence of Peronism|last=|first=|date=15 October 2015|newspaper=[[The Economist]]|access-date=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190718174646/https://www.economist.com/the-americas/2015/10/15/the-persistence-of-peronism|archive-date=2019-07-18|url-status=live|url-access=subscription}} < /ref >  The main Peronist party is the [[Justicialist Party]]. < ref name= " economist "  / >  The ideas of each president were different, < ref name= " economist "  / >  but the general ideology has been described as  " a vague blend of [[nationalism]] and [[labourism]] " < ref name= " economist "  / >  or [[populism]]. < ref name= " britannica "  / > 
 
 Juan Perón became Argentina's minister of labour after participating in the [[1943 Argentine coup d'état|1943 military coup]] and was elected [[president of Argentina]] in 1946. < ref name= " britannica "  / > < ref > {{cite book| last = Dougherty| first = Terri| title = Argentina| url = https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781590181089/page/35| year = 2003| isbn = 978-1-59018-108-9| page = 35| via = [[Internet Archive]] }} < /ref >  He introduced social programs that benefited the [[working class]],{{sfn|Dougherty|2003|p=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781590181089/page/36 36]}} supported [[Labor union|labor unions]], and called for additional involvement of the state in the economy. < ref name= " britannica "  / >  In addition he helped industrialists. < ref name= " economist "  / >  Perón was very popular and gained even more admiration through his wife, [[Eva Perón|Eva]], who championed for the rights of migrant workers and was loved by the people.{{sfn|Dougherty|2003|p=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781590181089/page/37 37]}} Due to rising [[inflation]] and other economic problems, the military [[Revolución Libertadora|overthrew Perón in 1955]].{{sfn|Dougherty|2003|p=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781590181089/page/39 39]}} The Peronist party was banned{{sfn|Dougherty|2003|p=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9781590181089/page/39 39]}} and it wasn't until [[September 1973 Argentine general election|1973]] that open elections were held again. In the 1973 election, Perón was again elected president. < ref name= " britannica "  / >  He died the next year and his widow, [[Isabel Peron|Isabel]], took his place as president. < ref name= " britannica "  / >  Perón's death had led to disunity and the military [[1976 Argentine coup d'état|overthrew Isabel in 1976]]. < ref name= " britannica "  / > 
 
 Peronist [[Carlos Menem]] was president from 1989 to1999. His policies were very different from previous Peronists, < ref name= " economist "  / >  he focused on [[privatization]], < ref name= " economist "  / >  [[Free market|free-market]] policies, < ref name= " britannica "  / >  and international relations. < ref name= " economist "  / >  Peronist [[Néstor Kirchner]] was president from 2003 to 2007, and his wife [[Cristina Fernández de Kirchner]] was president from 2007 to 2015. < ref name= " britannica "  / >  According to the Economist, the Kirchners returned to  " [[economic nationalism]] and near-[[autarky]] " . < ref name= " economist "  / >  The ideology of the Kirchners is known as [[Kirchnerism]].
