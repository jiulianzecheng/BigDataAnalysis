 '''Bridgnorth''' is a town in [[Shropshire]], [[England]]. It is on the [[River Severn]]. 
 
 == History == 
 [[File:Bridgnorth_castle_ruins.jpg|left|thumb|The ruins of [[Bridgnorth Castle]]]] 
 Bridgnorth is named after a bridge over the River Severn. < ref name= " ShropshireGuide " > {{Cite book|title=A Guide to Shropshire|last=Raven|first=Michael|page=32|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Txby6_Lrm0cC|year=2005|isbn=0-906114-34-9|publisher=Michael Raven|access-date=14 May 2008}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > < ref name= " Routes " > {{cite web|url=http://www3.shropshire-cc.gov.uk/roots/places/bridgnorth/bridgnorth.htm|title=Bridgnorth|work=Shropshire Routes to Roots|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080908013227/http://www3.shropshire-cc.gov.uk/roots/places/bridgnorth/bridgnorth.htm|archive-date=8 September 2008|url-status=dead|access-date=14 May 2008|df=dmy}} < /ref >  A [[Saxons|Saxon]] defencive mound existed in Bridgnorth as early as 912. < ref name= " ShropshireGuide "  / >  Earliest names for Bridgnorth include Brigge, Brug and Bruges, all referring to its position on the Severn. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/SAL/Bridgnorth/|title=Bridgnorth, Shropshire|work=The National Gazetteer of Great Britain and Ireland 1868 transcribed by Colin Hinson ©2012|publisher=GENUKI UK and Ireland Genealogy|access-date=27 June 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1101, the town was founded when Robert of Bellême, 3rd Earl of Shrewsbury built a [[Bridgnorth Castle|castle]] and a [[church]] here. < ref name= " ShropshireGuide "  / >  The town was attacked and burnt in 1322. < ref name= " Parl Writs II Digest " > {{Citation|title=Parliamentary Writs Alphabetical Digest|volume=II|year=1834|location=London|publisher=National Archives}} < /ref > 
 
 During the [[English Civil War]], in 1646, [[Oliver Cromwell]] and his [[cavaliers]] attacked [[Roundheads|roundhead]] Bridgnorth. By the time the castle was in ruins, the entire town was on fire. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.aboutbridgnorth.com/lavingtons-hole/|title=AboutBridgnorth: Lavington's Hole|date=February 23, 2019|website=AboutBridgnorth}} < /ref >  After a three-week siege, Cromwell was successful and he ordered that the castle be demolished in 1647. < ref > [http://www.shropshiretourism.co.uk/castles/bridgnorth/ Bridgnorth Castle] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180930043704/https://www.shropshiretourism.co.uk/castles/bridgnorth/ |date=2018-09-30 }}, ShropshireTourism.co.uk, accessed May 2010 < /ref >
