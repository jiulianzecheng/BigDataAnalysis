'''Christine''' is a [[female]] given name. It is pronounced [kri-steen]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Christine {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/christine|access-date=2023-03-27|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  It comes from the word ''Christ''. This name means ''follower of Christ''. Christine is an exquisite name that has enjoyed popularity for centuries. Its origins can be traced back to the Greek name Christina, which means  " one who follows Christ. "  Throughout history, this name has taken on different forms and has been linked with numerous noteworthy individuals. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Christine name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Christine-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-27|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  In 1968, it was the 14th most frequently-given name for girls in the [[United States]]. < ref name=Campbell > Campbell, Mike.  ==People== 
 * [[Saint Christina of Bolsena]], remembered on the 24th of July 
 * [[Saint Christine of Persia]], remembered on the 13th of March 
 * [[Saint Christine of Ancyre]], remembered on the 18th of May 
 * [[Christine Adjobi]], an Ivorian politician and physician 
 * [[Christine Amertil]] (born 1979), a Bahamian athlete competing mainly in the 400 metres

 * [[Christine Bottomley]] (born 1979), a British actress

 * [[Christine Sinclair]] (born 1983), a Canadian football player who is currently a member of the [[Canadian women's national soccer team]] 
 * [[Christine Truman]] (born 1941), a British tennis player and French Open Champion.
