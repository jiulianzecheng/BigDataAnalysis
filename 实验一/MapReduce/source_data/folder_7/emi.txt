 '''EMI''' was a [[music industry|music company]] founded in [[London]]. It is in charge of a [[record label]], EMI Music. EMI is one of the  " big four "  record labels; the labels not part of the  " big four "  are called [[:Category:Independent record labels|independent]]. It started in 1931, when it was called  " Electric and Music Industries " . In 1957, it took over [[Capitol Records]], an [[United States|American]] record company. 
 
 In 1962, [[The Beatles]] signed a contract with EMI's [[Parlophone Records]], and they became the company's most successful musicians. The Beatles made nearly all their recordings at [[Abbey Road Studios]], EMI's main [[recording studio]]s in [[London]]. 
 
 In 1992, EMI bought [[Virgin Records]], which had been owned by [[Richard Branson]]. It almost bought [[Universal Music Group|Universal Records]] in 1998, also. In 2007, EMI was taken over by a group called Terra Firma, after it said no to a [[bid]] from [[Warner Bros.|Warner Music Group]]. 
 
 Other famous musicians that were with EMI include [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Pink Floyd]], [[Spice Girls]], and [[Coldplay]]. < ref > [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7188861.stm Profile: British music giant EMI] (15 January 2008). [[BBC]]. Accessed 23 August 2008. < /ref > 
 
 In 2012, [[Universal Music Group]] bought EMI, but the [[European Commission]] told Universal to sell some of EMI's record labels. [[Parlophone]], the oldest EMI label, was sold to [[Warner Music Group]]; while [[BMG Rights Management|BMG]] bought [[Mute Records]]. EMI's music publishing business was sold to a group that included Sony/ATV Music Publishing.
