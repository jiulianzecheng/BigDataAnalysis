 '''Gamma''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Γ γ'''), is the third letter of the [[Greek alphabet]], used to represent the  " g "  sound in [[Ancient Greek|Ancient]] and [[Modern Greek language|Modern Greek]]. In the system of [[Greek numerals]], it has a value of 3. Letters that came from it include the Roman [[C]] and Cyrillic [[Г]]. 
 
 In [[mathematics]], the lowercase γ is used to represent [[Euler–Mascheroni constant]], and the uppercase Γ is used to represent [[gamma function]] and [[gamma distribution]]. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2020-03-20|title=Greek/Hebrew/Latin-based Symbols in Mathematics|url=https://mathvault.ca/hub/higher-math/math-symbols/greek-hebrew-latin-symbols/|access-date=2020-10-05|website=Math Vault|language=en-US}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Gamma Symbol in Greek Alphabet|url=https://www.greeksymbols.net/gamma-symbol|access-date=2020-10-05|website=www.greeksymbols.net|language=en}} < /ref >   
 
 In physics, [[gamma rays]] (γ-rays) are a kind of radiation that result from [[radioactive decay|nuclear decay]]. In the [[physics]] and [[engineering]], gamma is used to represent many different things, depending on the subject.
