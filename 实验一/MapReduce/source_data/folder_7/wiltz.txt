 '''Wiltz''' ({{lang-lb|Wolz}}) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] with [[List of cities in Luxembourg|city status]] in north-western [[Luxembourg]], capital of the [[Wiltz (canton)|canton Wiltz]]. Wiltz is situated along the sides of the river [[Wiltz (river)|Wiltz]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 During World War II, Luxembourg was occupied 10 May 1940 (the first day of the [[Battle of France]]).  
 On 31 August 1942, a [[Strike|general strike]] began in Wiltz that spread throughout the country. 
 
 On 1 January 2015, Wiltz absorbed the neighboring commune of [[Eschweiler, Wiltz|Eschweiler]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The ''commune'' is in the [[Oesling]], a region within the greater [[Ardennes (forest)|Ardennes]] area. It has an area of {{Convert|39.3|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name=stats / >  The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|285|-|506|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. At the [[castle]], the altitude  is {{Convert|464|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |access-date= 10 June 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 Wiltz is surrounded by six ''communes'': [[Wincrange]] to the north and northwest, [[Clervaux]] to the northeast, [[Kiischpelt]] to the east, [[Goesdorf]] to the southeast, [[Lac de la Haute-Sûre]] to the south and southwest, and [[Winseler]] to the west.

 ==Population== 
 There were 6,730 persons living in the ''commune'' in January 2016, < ref name=stats2 / >  for a [[population density]] of {{Pop density|6730|39.3|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km². 
 
 Other towns, besides the town of Wiltz, within the ''commune'' are Roullingen and Weidingen.

 ==Places of interest== 
 One of the main features of the town is [[Wiltz Castle]] ({{lang-fr|Château de Wiltz}}, [[German language|German]]: ''Schloss Wiltz'', {{lang-lb|Schlass Wolz}}) which belonged to the former [[Count]]s of Wiltz.  This castle, which is situated on {{Convert|600|acre|km2}} of lawns and gardens, contains hundreds of rooms.  It was completed in 1727, and the final Count died in 1793.
