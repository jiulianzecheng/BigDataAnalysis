 '''Ptolemaida''', was named at the Ottoman period '''KayÄ±lar''', is a [[Greece|Greek]] town in the [[Kozani prefecture]] in the region of [[Macedonia (Greece)|Macedonia]]. The city is best known for its coal mines of lignite and power station. It has been also called by Greek people ''The Socket of [[Greece]]'' because it has very strong power stations. < ref > [http://wikimapia.org/1750234/Ptolemaida Power stations and coal mines in Ptolemaida] < /ref >  Its population is 28.679 inhabitants according to the 2001 [[census]]. Ptolemaida is the biggest industrial, trade, and rural city of the prefecture and one of the most developed cities in industry in Greece. Once The [[Turkish people|Turkish]] rural population was mainly of [[Konariotes]] shepherds.
