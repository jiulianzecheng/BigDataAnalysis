 A '''storyboard''' is a way of planning [[animation|animated films]], [[movie]]s, or [[Television programme|television shows]]. A storyboard shows [[example]]s of what the [[artist]] wants to make before they are [[Animation|animated]]. It also lets artists [[organize]] their stories before they start to make the animation. Storyboarding, in the form it is known today, was developed at [[Walt Disney Animation Studios|Walt Disney Productions]]. < ref name=ThornEL > {{cite web |url=http://elearnmag.acm.org/featured.cfm?aid=2024072 |title=The Art of Storyboarding |author=Kevin Thorn |date=August 2013 |publisher=eLearn Magazine |access-date=3 January 2016}} < /ref >  It was first used in the 1928 animated [[short film]], ''[[Plane Crazy]]''. " < ref name=ThornEL/ >  The 1939 movie ''[[Gone with the Wind (movie)|Gone with the Wind]]'' was the first to be completely storyboarded. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.instructionaldesign.org/storyboarding.html |title=Storyboarding |author=Richard Culatta |publisher=Instructional Design.org |access-date=3 January 2016}} < /ref >
