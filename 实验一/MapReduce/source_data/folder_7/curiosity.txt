 '''Curiosity''' is an [[emotion]] that shows a [[want]] or [[need]] to [[Knowledge|know]] new things. It is shown in [[behavior]] such as [[exploration]], [[wikt:investigation|investigation]], and [[learning]]. It is an [[innate behaviour]] of [[human]]s and many [[animal]] species. Curiosity is a major cause and force behind scientific [[research]] and other studies.  
 
 Curiosity is common to [[human]]s at all ages starting from [[infancy]], < ref name= " pmid10207585 " > {{Cite journal| author = Ofer G, Durban J. | title = Curiosity: reflections on its nature and functions. | url = https://archive.org/details/sim_american-journal-of-psychotherapy_winter-1999_53_1/page/35 | journal = Am J Psychother. | volume = 53 | issue = 1 | pages = 35–51 | year = 1999 | doi = 10.1176/appi.psychotherapy.1999.53.1.35 | pmid = 10207585 }} < /ref >  through [[adulthood]]. < ref name= " pmid13190171 " > {{Cite journal| author = Berlyne DE. | title = A theory of human curiosity. | journal = Br J Psychol. | volume = 45 | issue = 3 | pages = 180–91 | year = 1954 | doi = 10.1111/j.2044-8295.1954.tb01243.x | pmid = 13190171 }} < /ref >  It is easy to see in many other animal species, such as [[ape]]s, [[cat]]s, [[rodent]]s, < ref name= " pmid13252149 " > {{Cite journal| author = Berlyne DE. | title = The arousal and satiation of perceptual curiosity in the rat. | journal = J Comp Physiol Psychol. | volume = 48 | issue = 4 | pages = 238–46 | year = 1955 | doi = 10.1037/h0042968 | pmid = 13252149 }} < /ref >  [[fish]], [[reptile]]s, and [[insect]]s. Although it is an innate behaviour of many living beings, it is not thought of as an [[instinct]] because it is not a [[fixed action pattern]]. 
 
 A ''morbid curiosity'' is an [[Addiction|addictive]] curiosity with [[death]], [[violence]], or something else that may hurt you physically or emotionally.
