'''Colonoscopy''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|k|ɒ|l|ə|ˈ|n|ɒ|s|k|ə|p|i}}) or '''coloscopy''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ə|ˈ|l|ɒ|s|k|ə|p|i}}) < ref > {{Citation | url = http://medical-dictionary.thefreedictionary.com/colonoscopy | title = American Heritage Medical Dictionary}} < /ref >  is the [[endoscopy|endoscopic]] test of the [[Large intestine|large bowel]] and parts the [[ileum|small bowel]]. Tests use a [[CCD camera]] or a [[Optical fiber|fiber optic]] camera on a flexible tube passed through the [[anus]]. It can provide a visual diagnosis and allow [[biopsy]] or removal of suspected [[colorectal cancer]] lesions.
