 A '''slurry''' is, in general, a thick [[Suspension (chemistry)|suspension]] of solids in a liquid. 
 
 ==Uses== 
 There are many kinds of slurries, including 
 * A [[wikt:mixture|mixture]] of [[cement]] and water to form [[concrete]] < ref > Ibis World, [http://www.ibisworld.com.au/industry/default.aspx?indid=215   " Concrete Slurry Manufacturing Market Research Report, " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120423000702/http://www.ibisworld.com.au/industry/default.aspx?indid=215 |date=2012-04-23 }} Nov 2011; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of [[thickening agent#weapon use|thickening agent]], oxidizers, and water used to form a [[Water gel explosive|gel explosive]] 
 * A mixture of pyroclastic rock and water known as a [[lahar]] < ref > Cronin1, Shane J. ''et al.'' [http://geology.gsapubs.org/content/24/12/1107.abstract   " Unusual 'snow slurry' lahars from Ruapehu volcano, New Zealand, " ] ''Geology.'' September 1995; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of [[bentonite]] and water used to make [[slurry wall]]s < ref > HowStuffWorks.com, [http://science.howstuffworks.com/engineering/structural/wtc3.htm   " The World Trade Center " ]; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of coal waste and water known as [[coal slurry]] < ref > SlugeSafety.org, [http://www.sludgesafety.org/what-coal-slurry  What is coal slurry? " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120424102749/http://www.sludgesafety.org/what-coal-slurry |date=2012-04-24 }}; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of wood pulp and water used to make [[paper]] < ref > PaperRecyclingCoalition.com, [http://www.paperrecyclingcoalition.com/recycled/index.php/faqs/how-is-100-recycled-paperboard-made/   " How 100% Recycled Paper is Made " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100107161729/http://www.paperrecyclingcoalition.com/recycled/index.php/faqs/how-is-100-recycled-paperboard-made/ |date=2010-01-07 }}; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of animal waste and water used as [[fertilizer]] < ref > SurryTech.co.za, [http://www.slurrytech.co.za/why-use-slurry/   " Why use slurry as a fertilizer? " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131223153001/http://www.slurrytech.co.za/why-use-slurry/ |date=2013-12-23 }}; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of finely ground meat and water, centrifugally dewatered into [[meat slurry]], a food product 
 * An abrasive substance used in [[chemical-mechanical polishing]] 
 * A mixture of ice crystals, freezing point depressant, and water called [[slurry ice]] < ref > HitachiZosen.co, [http://www.hitachizosen.co.jp/english/products/products034.html   " Slurry Ice Plants " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120511172817/http://www.hitachizosen.co.jp/english/products/products034.html |date=2012-05-11 }}; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref > 
 * A mixture of raw materials and water involved in the [[rawmill]] manufacture of [[Portland cement]] 
 * A mixture of minerals, water, and additives used in the manufacture of [[ceramic]]s < ref > EngTips.com, [http://www.eng-tips.com/viewthread.cfm?qid=222745  " Creating a Ceramic Slurry " ]; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref >    
 * A mixture of water and food starch used to thicken [[pudding]]s, [[sauce]]s, and [[gravy]]s. < ref > FoodNetwork.com, [http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/slurry/index.html   " Slurry " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120120225051/http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/slurry/index.html |date=2012-01-20 }}; retrieved 2012-4-25. < /ref >
