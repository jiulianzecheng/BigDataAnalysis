 '''Lille''' is a city in the northern part of [[France]] with a [[population]] of about 226,800 [[inhabitant]]s. It is the capital of the region [[Hauts-de-France]] and the [[Nord (department)|Nord]] 
 
 [[Nord (department)|department]]., and is not far from the border with [[Belgium]], where in [[Dutch language|Dutch]] it is called {{lang|nl|Rijsel}}. About two million people live in the urban area (''conurbation'') shared with [[Kortrijk]] and [[Tournai]], which is the fifth largest in France behind [[Paris]], [[Lyon]], [[Marseille]], and [[Toulouse]].

 == Transport == 
 There are two big [[railway station]]s in Lille with many trains to and from different places around France and Belgium. Most of these serve {{lang|fr|Lille-Flandres}} station and are operated by [[SNCF]], including the [[TGV]] high-speed service. The other big station, {{lang|fr|Lille-Europe}}, has [[Eurostar]] trains to and from [[Brussels]], [[Paris]], and [[St Pancras station]] in [[London]] via the [[Channel Tunnel]] to the [[United Kingdom]]. 
 
 [[Public transport]] in Lille is made up of [[bus]]es, two [[rapid transit|metro]] lines, and two [[tram]] lines to the nearby towns of [[Tourcoing]] and [[Roubaix]]. The metro system is one of the first in the world that does not have drivers.
