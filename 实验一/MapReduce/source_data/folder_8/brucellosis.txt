 '''Brucellosis''' is a [[disease]] that is caused by [[bacteria]] in the ''[[Brucella]]'' [[genus]]. It is also called '''Bang's disease''', '''Crimean fever''', '''Gibraltar fever''', '''Malta fever''', '''Maltese fever''', '''Mediterranean fever''', '''rock fever''', and '''undulant fever'''. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://education.yahoo.com/reference/dictionary/entry/brucellosis |title= " Brucellosis "  in the American Heritage Dictionary |access-date=2011-07-07 |archive-date=2011-06-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606062045/http://education.yahoo.com/reference/dictionary/entry/brucellosis |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > [http://www.wrongdiagnosis.com/medical/maltese_fever.htm Maltese Fever] ''by wrongdiagnosis.com, last Update: 25 February 2009 (12:01)'', retrieved 2009-02-26 < /ref >  It is a very [[contagious]] disease (can be spread very easily from one person to another.)  
 
 Brucellosis is a [[zoonosis]], a disease that is spread from [[animal]]s to [[human]]s. Humans get the disease by [[drinki]]ng [[milk]] that is [[Sterilization (microbiology)|unsterilized]], or by [[eat]]ing [[meat]] from sick animals. Sometimes, but not very often, the disease can be spread from human to human. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dhsspsni.gov.uk/brucellosis-pathway.pdf |title=Diagnosis and Management of Acute Brucellosis in Primary Care |date= August 2004 |publisher= Brucella Subgroup of the Northern Ireland Regional Zoonoses Group}} < /ref > 
 
 The disease was first called  " Malta fever. "  [[Britain|British]] [[doctors]] in the [[military]] first discovered it in [[Malta]] during the [[Crimean War]] (1850s). Dr. [[David Bruce]] was the first person to discover the [[bacteria]] that caused brucellosis. < ref > {{cite book|title=The Cambridge World History of Human Disease|author=Wilkinson, Lise|publisher=[[Cambridge University Press]])|year=1993|editor=Kiple, Kenneth F.|chapter= " Brucellosis " }} < /ref > < ref > [http://www.whonamedit.com/synd.cfm/819.html ''Brucellosis''] named after ''[http://www.whonamedit.com/doctor.cfm/871.html Sir David Bruce]'' at Who Named It? < /ref >
