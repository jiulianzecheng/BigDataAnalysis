 '''''The Tragedy of Cymbeline'''''  is a play by [[William Shakespeare]]. It is based on a story in a book called ''[[Holinshed's Chronicles]]'' by [[Raphael Holinshed]]. Shakespeare also used ''[[History of the Kings of Britain]]'' by [[Geoffrey of Monmouth]] about the real-life British king Cunobelinus. Shakespeare also used a story in a book by [[Giovanni Boccaccio]] called ''[[Decameron]]'' for some details. < ref > F. D. Hoeniger,  " Two Notes on Cymbeline, "  ''Shakespeare Quarterly'', Vol. 8, No. 1 (Winter, 1957), p. 133, < /ref > < ref > Nosworthy, J. M. (1955) ''Preface'' in [https://books.google.com/books?id=mxGM3uMrNZ8C & pg=PR24 ''Cymbeline: Second Series''] p.xxiv. < /ref >  The first known performance was in April 1611. It was first printed in 1623 in the [[First Folio]], a collection of all of Shakespeare's plays.
