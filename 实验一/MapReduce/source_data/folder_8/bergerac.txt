 '''Bergerac''' ({{Lang-oc|Brageirac}}) < ref > {{cite web |url=http://communes-oc.cg24.fr/cantons/bergerac/BERGERAC.htm |title=Bergerac |publisher=Le nom occitan des communes du Périgord |language=fr |access-date=1 April 2015 |archive-date=3 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303183117/http://communes-oc.cg24.fr/cantons/bergerac/BERGERAC.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  is a [[Communes of France|commune]] and a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of the [[Dordogne]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] [[Regions of France|region]], southwestern [[France]]. 
 
 It is the second biggest city of the department, after [[Périgueux]]. 
 
 Despite the similarities in their names, there is no connection to [[Cyrano de Bergerac]], a French writer from [[Paris]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Bergerac is in the south of the Dordogne department, in the region named as ''Périgord Pourpre''; the [[Dordogne (river)|Dordogne]] river flows through the city. 
 
 The city is at about {{Convert|48|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Périgueux]], {{Convert|90|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Agen]], {{Convert|93|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Bordeaux]] and {{Convert|110|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Cahors]]. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|56.10|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|79|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|39|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Bergerac-24100/ |title=City of Bergerac |publisher=Map-France.com |access-date=1 April 2015 }} < /ref >

 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Bergerac is a [[Mediterranean climate]] (Marine West Coast Climate), '''Cfb''' in the [[Köppen climate classification]], with mild winters and warm summers. < ref name= " annuaire " > {{cite web |work=Annuaire-Mairie.fr |title=Ville de Bergerac |url=http://www.annuaire-mairie.fr/ville-bergerac.html |language=fr |access-date=1 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Population== 
 The inhabitants of Bergerac are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Bergeracois'' (women: ''Bergeracoises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_bergerac_24037.html |title=Bergerac (24100) |publisher=habitants.fr |language=fr |access-date=1 April 2015 }} < /ref > 
 
 With a population of 27,764 (2014), < ref name=insee > {{cite web |url=http://www.insee.fr/fr/statistiques/fichier/2525755/dep24.pdf |title=Régions, départements, arrondissements, cantons et communes |work= Populations légales 2014 |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |format=PDF |language=fr |access-date=11 March 2017}} < /ref >  Bergerac has a [[population density]] of {{Pop density|27764|56.1|km2|prec=0|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > .

 ==Administration== 
 Bergerac is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of the [[Dordogne]] department since 1790, and the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Bergerac|Bergerac]]. 
 
 The ''commune'' is the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of two [[Cantons of France|cantons]]: 
 # '''Bergerac-1''', formed with a part of Bergerac; < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/canton/CANTON2401-bergerac-1 |title=Canton de Bergerac-1 (2401) |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=fr |access-date=11 March 2017 }} < /ref >  and 
 # '''Bergerac-2''', formed with the remaining part of Bergerac and 9 other ''communes''. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/canton/CANTON2402-bergerac-2 |title=Canton de Bergerac-2 (2402) |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=fr |access-date=11 March 2017 }} < /ref > 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Bergeracoise'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération bergeracoise}}).

 ==Economy== 
 Bergerac is known for its [[wine]]s and its [[tobacco]]. It has 12 recognized wine kinds that are ''[[Appellation d'origine contrôlée]]'' (AOC).
