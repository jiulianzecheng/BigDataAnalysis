 '''Monoceros''' ({{lang-el|Μονόκερως}}) is a [[constellation]] on the [[celestial equator]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.topastronomer.com/StarCharts/Constellations/Monoceros.php|title=Monoceros Constellation on Top Astronomer|website=www.topastronomer.com|access-date=2014-09-16|archive-date=2014-12-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141218115350/http://www.topastronomer.com/StarCharts/Constellations/Monoceros.php|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Its name is Greek for [[unicorn]]. It was described by 17th-century Dutch [[cartographer]] [[Petrus Plancius]]. [[Orion (constellation)|Orion]] is to the west of Monceros. [[Gemini (constellation)|Gemini]] is to the north. [[Canis Major]] is to the south. [[Hydra (constellation)|Hydra]] is to the east. Other constellations next to Monoceros are [[Canis Minor]], [[Lepus (constellation)|Lepus]] and [[Puppis]]. 
 
 == Notable features == 
 [[Image:MonocerosCC.jpg|thumb|left|200px|The constellation Monoceros as it can be seen by the naked eye.]] 
 
 ===Stars=== 
 Monoceros is hard to see with the naked eye. Its brightest star, [[Alpha Monocerotis]], has a [[visual magnitude]] of 3.93. It is brighter than [[Gamma Monocerotis]] at 3.98. 
 
 Monoceros does have some interesting features to look at with a small [[telescope]]. [[Beta Monocerotis]] is a [[triple star]] system. The three stars form a [[triangle]] which looks to be in one spot. [[William Herschel]] commented that it is  " one of the most beautiful sights in the heavens " . < ref > http://90millimeter.org/2013/02/17/monocerotis/{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 Monoceros also has [[Plaskett's Star]]. It is a big [[binary star|binary system]]. Its whole [[mass]] is said to be that of almost 100 [[Sun]]s put together.
