 '''Kohala''' is an extinct [[shield volcano]] in [[Hawaii]]. It is the oldest of five volcanoes on the [[Hawaii (island)|Big Island]], < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.soest.hawaii.edu/soestwp/announce/news/a-closer-look-at-kohala-mountain-the-big-islands-oldest-above-water-volcano/|title=A closer look at Kohala Mountain: The Big Island’s oldest above-water volcano|language=en-US|access-date=2020-08-07}} < /ref >  forming its northwestern part. 
 
 == History == 
 Around a million years ago, Kohala started erupting. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|url=https://pubs.usgs.gov/of/2007/1089/Hawaii_expl_pamphlet.pdf|title=Geologic Map of the State of Hawai‘i|website=United States Geological Survey|access-date=August 7, 2020}} < /ref >  Its last eruption was 120,000 years ago, meaning it's likely extinct. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.soest.hawaii.edu/GG/HCV/haw_activity.html|title=Hawaii Center for Volcanology {{!}} Active Hawaiian Volcanoes|website=www.soest.hawaii.edu|access-date=2020-08-07}} < /ref >  The other volcanoes on the Big Island are either active or dormant. 250,000 to 300,000 years ago, a massive landslide destroyed much of the mountain. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 [[Kamehameha I]], the first ruler of the [[Kingdom of Hawaii]], was born near Kohala in 1758. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.biography.com/political-figure/kamehameha-i|title=Kamehameha the Great|website=Biography|language=en-us|access-date=2020-08-07|archive-date=2020-08-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200808153638/https://www.biography.com/political-figure/kamehameha-i|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
