 '''Ugarit''' ({{IPAc-en|j|uː|ˈ|g|ɑː|r|ɪ|t|,_|uː|-}}; < ref > {{MerriamWebsterDictionary|Ugarit}} < /ref >  {{lang-uga|𐎜𐎂𐎗𐎚}}, ''ʼUgart''; {{lang-ar|أُوغَارِيت}} ''Ūġārīt'' or {{lang|ar|أُوجَارِيت}} ''Ūǧārīt'') was an ancient [[Port|port city]] in northern [[Syria]]. It is in the outskirts of modern [[Latakia]]. It was discovered by accident in 1928 together with the [[Ugaritic texts]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IfHQx5FUZW8C & q=The+site+of+Ugarit+and+its+indigenous+language+were+discovered+by+accident+in+1928%E2%80%931929. & pg=PA1|title=An Introduction to Ugaritic|last=Huehnergard|first=John|date=2012|publisher=Hendrickson Publishers|isbn=978-1-59856-820-2|language=en}} < /ref >  Its ruins are often called '''Ras Shamra''' < ref > Sometimes written  " Ras Shamrah " ; {{lang-ar|رأس شمرة}}, literally  " Cape Fennel " ). See [https://books.google.com/books?id=t9LHVdWLc7gC & q=%22Ras+shamra%22 & pg=PA293]. < /ref >  due to the [[Headlands and bays|headland]] on which they are found. 
 
 Ugarit had close connections to the [[Hittite Empire]]. It also sent tribute to [[Ancient Egypt|Egypt]] at times and maintained trade and diplomatic connections with [[Cyprus]] (then called [[Alashiya]]). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=V-LVAAAAMAAJ & q=diplomatic+relations+between+Cyprus+and+Ugarit|title = Mycenaean Cult Buildings: A Study of Their Architecture and Function in the Context of the Aegean and the Eastern Mediterranean|isbn = 9788291626031|last1 = Whittaker|first1 = Helène|last2 = Hofsten|first2 = Helène Whittaker von|year = 1997}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|date=2017-03-23|title=Searching for the Lost City of Copper|url=https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/magazine/2017/03-04/enkomi-cyprus-ancient-city-copper-bronze/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=History Magazine|language=en}} < /ref >  This is documented in the archives recovered from the site. It is confirmed by [[Mycenaean culture|Mycenaean]] and Cypriot [[pottery]] found there. The [[polity]] was at its height from c. 1450 BCE until its destruction in c. 1200 BCE. This destruction was possibly caused by the mysterious [[Sea Peoples]] or internal struggle. The kingdom would be one of the many that fell apart during the [[Bronze Age collapse|Bronze Age Collapse]].
