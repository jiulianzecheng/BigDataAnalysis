'''Dey''' (Arabic: داي), from the [[Turkish people|Turkish]] honorific title  ''dayı'', literally meaning uncle, was the title given to the rulers of the [[Regency of Algiers]] ([[Algeria]]), [[Tripoli]], and [[Tunis]] under the [[Ottoman Empire]] from 1671 onwards.
