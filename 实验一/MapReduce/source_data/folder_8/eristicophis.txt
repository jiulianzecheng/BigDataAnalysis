 '''Eristicophis''' is a [[monotypic]] [[genus]] made for the [[Venom (poison)|venom]]ous [[viper]] species  " ''E. macmahonii'' " . It is found in the [[desert]] areas near the borders of [[Iran]], [[Pakistan]], and [[Afghanistan]]. There are currently no subspecies recognized. The E. macmahonii is also known as the  " ''McMahon's Viper'' " , the  " ''Asian Sand Viper'' " , the  " ''Leaf-nosed Viper'' " , and the  " ''Whiskered Viper'' " .  
 
 == Description== 
 The McMahon's Viper is a small species of [[snake]], growing up to less than one meter. Males are smaller than females; males can grow up to the length of 22-40 cm, and females can grow up to the length of 28-72 cm. They are reddish-brown or yellowish brown in color. 
 
 == Where they live== 
 The McMahon's Viper is found in the [[desert]] regions of [[Balochistan (region)|Balochistan]], near the borders of [[Iran]], [[Pakistan]], and [[Afghanistan]].   
 
 == Behavior== 
 The McMahon's Viper is said to be [[nocturnal]], but some say it is crepuscular, meaning it is active during the early morning or late evening. It is able to sidewind on loose sand, and sometimes climbs into [[Shrub|bushes]]. It is a aggressive [[snake]], hissing very loudly when disturbed or annoyed, and sometimes it raising its head of the ground and starts striking aggressively. It is able to sink into the sand by moving in a rocking motion. When it sinks into the sand it leaves its eyes and snout sticking out of the sand.  
 
 == Feeding== 
 The McMahon's Viper eats small [[lizard]]s, small [[rodent]]s like [[mice]], and sometimes [[bird]]s. 
 
 == Reproduction== 
 The McMahon's Viper is [[oviparous]], meaning it lays eggs. It lays around a dozen eggs at a time, and these eggs take around 6-8 weeks to hatch. When the eggs hatch the young are about 6 inches (15 cm) long. 
 
 == Venom== 
 Not much is known about the McMahon's Viper [[venom (poison)|venom]], but some say it is similar to the venom of the [[Echis]].
