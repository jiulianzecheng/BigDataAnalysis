 '''Foix''' ({{lang-oc|Fois}}; {{lang-ca|Foix}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]], the former capital of the [[Comté de Foix|County of Foix]]. Today it is the [[Prefectures in France|préfecture]] of the [[Ariège (department)|Ariège]] [[Departments of France|department]] in southwestern France in the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]]. 
 
 It is to south of [[Toulouse]], close to the border with Spain and [[Andorra]]. The city is only the second city of the department after [[Pamiers]]. Of all [[France|French]] ''préfectures'', Foix is the one with fewest people living in it. 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:foix france castle.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Château des Comtes de Foix]] 
 The [[Ancient Rome|Romans]] built a [[Fortification|fort]] on the [[Rock (geology)|rock]]y [[hill]] where the Château de Foix [[castle]] now [[:en:wikt:overlook|overlooks]] the town. The town of Foix probably owes its origin to an [[oratory]] founded by [[Charlemagne]], which afterwards became the [[Abbey]] of [[Volusianus of Tours|Saint Volusianus]] in 849. The current area of the department was part of the old [[County of Foix]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The city of Foix lies in the foothills of the [[Pyrénées]] mountains, in southern France. It is on the [[confluence]] of the [[Ariège (river)|Ariège]] and [[Arget (river)|Arget]] rivers. Foix is at {{Convert|83|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the south of [[Toulouse]].

 == Administration == 
 Foix is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Ariège department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Foix|Foix]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the [[Cantons of France|canton]] of [[Canton of Foix|Foix]] (with 13,700 inhabitants in 2014). 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Pays Foix-Varilhes'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération Pays Foix-Varilhes}}). 
 
 === Twinned and partner towns === 
 Foix is [[Twin town|twinned]] with: 
 * {{Flagicon|Spain}} [[Lleida]], [[Spain]] 
 * {{Flagicon|Andorra}} [[Andorra la Vella]], [[Andorra]] 
 
 ==Sites of interest== 
 * The main [[History|historic]] building is the '''[[Castle]] of the Counts of Foix''' or, simply, '''Castle of Foix'''; it is on the top of a rocky [[hill]] that [[:en:wikt:overlook|overlooks]] the city. 
 * The ''old town'', with narrow streets and houses of traditional [[architecture]]. 
 * '''Saint-Volusien [[Abbey]]''' 
 * The '''''Villote''''' (small town), in the [[Middle Ages]], was a space outside the walls surrounding the city built in 1330.
