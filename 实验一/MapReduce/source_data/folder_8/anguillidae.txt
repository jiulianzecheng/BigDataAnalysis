 '''Anguillidae''', the '''freshwater eels''', are a family of [[Eel|eels]]. They are in the suborder [[Anguilloidei]]. [[European eel|''Anguilla anguilla'']] is the [[type species]]. Most of them are [[extant]]. Aside from the living species, there are also [[Prehistoric eel|prehistoric eels]].  
 
 They spend their adult lives in freshwater, but migrate to the ocean to [[Spawn (biology)|spawn]]. Eels are an important food fish. Some species are now farm-raised, but not bred in captivity. Many populations in the wild are now threatened. Seafood Watch recommends people avoid eating anguillid eels. < ref > Halpin, Patricia 2007. Seafood Watch: Unagi (PDF). [https://web.archive.org/web/20100706014923/http://www.montereybayaquarium.org/cr/cr_seafoodwatch/content/media/MBA_SeafoodWatch_UnagiReport.pdf] Monterey Bay Aquarium. < /ref > 
 
 The first fossils of eels is found at the start of the [[Palaeocene]], just after the [[K/T extinction event|Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event]] 66 mya. If prehistoric eels like ''[[Urosphen]]'' were still alive, they would have replaced the extant eels.
