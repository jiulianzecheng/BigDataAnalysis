 '''Glaucetas''', or '''Glauketas''' ({{lang-el|Γλαυκέτας}}; flourished 315–300 BC), was a [[Greeks|Greek]] [[pirate]] mostly active in the [[Aegean Sea]] during the 4th century BC. Little is known about his life. Glaucetas is mentioned in ancient Greek inscriptions, or stone markings, describing how the [[navy]] of [[Athens]] led by Thymochares attacked his base on the island of Kythnos. < ref name=Ormerod > {{harvnb|Ormerod|1924|pp=115–116, 124}}. < /ref > < ref name=Inscription > ''I.G.'', II, 331. < /ref >  In the end, the Athenian navy removed Glaucetas from Kythnos thus  " making the sea safe for those that sailed thereon. " < ref name=Ormerod/ > < ref name=Inscription/ >
