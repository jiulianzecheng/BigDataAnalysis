'''Alison''' [al-uh-suhn] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Alison {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/alison|access-date=2023-03-13|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a female [[given name]] that has many kinds of spellings. It was originally in medieval Norman. It was derived from the name ''Alice''.  
 
 The name was first recorded in [[Scotland]] in the 12th century. The variant spelling ''Allison'' ranked 228 for female names for the [[United States]] in 1990, and was the 45th most popular for girls between 2005 and early 2010s. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/|access-date=2023-03-13|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref >  Alison has its roots in the English language and is believed to be a variation of the French name Alis. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Alison name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Alison-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-13|website=babys.name}} < /ref > 
 
 As ''Allison'', the name was used for [[tropical cyclone|tropical storms]] for [[Tropical Storm Allison (1989)|1989]], 1995 and [[Tropical Storm Allison|2001]]. The 2001 tropical storm was incredibly damaging, which in turn caused the name to be retired the following year.
