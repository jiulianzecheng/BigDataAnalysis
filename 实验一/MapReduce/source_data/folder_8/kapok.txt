 '''''Ceiba pentandra''''' or the '''Kapok''' is a [[species]] of tropical [[tree]]s of the order [[Malvales]] and the family [[Malvaceae]] (previously separated in the family [[Bombacaceae]]), native to [[Mexico]], [[Central America]] and the [[Caribbean]], northern [[South America]], and (as the variety ''Ceiba pentandra'' var. ''guineensis'') to tropical west [[Africa]].The normal colours are white and pink flowers it takes 4 weeks to grow a bud. 
 [[File:Kapok tree-pod.jpg|left|thumb|Kapok pod showing kapok fibres inside]] 
 
 The word is also used for the fibre obtained from its seed pods. The tree is also known as the Java cotton, Java kapok, or ceiba. It is a sacred symbol in [[Maya mythology]]. 
 
 The tree of life grows to {{convert|60|-|70|m|ft|abbr=off}} tall. It has a very substantial trunk up to {{convert|3|m|abbr=off}} in diameter with buttresses.
