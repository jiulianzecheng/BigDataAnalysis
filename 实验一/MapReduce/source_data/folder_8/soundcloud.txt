 '''SoundCloud''' is a Swedish-founded online audio distribution platform and [[music sharing]] website. It is based in [[Berlin]], Germany.  
 ==Services== 
 It enables its users to upload, promote, and share audio, as well as a DSP enabling listeners to stream audio. Started in 2007 by Alexander Ljung and [[Eric Wahlforss]], SoundCloud has grown to be one of the largest music streaming services. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://expandedramblings.com/index.php/soundcloud-statistics/|title=16 Amazing SoundCloud Statistics|date=2016-06-24|publisher=DMR|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-04}} < /ref >  SoundCloud offers both free and paid membership on the platform. It is available for desktop and mobile devices. 
 ==Influence== 
 SoundCloud has influenced the music industry through the success of many artists who have used the service to launch or advance their careers. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Battan|first=Carrie|title=The Messy Story of How SoundCloud Rap Took Over Everything|url=https://www.gq.com/story/soundcloud-rap-boom-times|access-date=2020-11-24|website=GQ|language=en-us}} < /ref >  SoundCloud got support from many investors and other media platforms such as [[Twitter]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=https://www.vox.com/2017/3/10/14887806/soundcloud-sale-investment |title=SoundCloud needs more money, or it may sell at a fire-sale price |last=Kafka|first=Peter|date=2017-03-10|website=Vox|access-date=2019-08-04}} < /ref >  Although the streaming platform itself has unsolved funding issues and has dismissed many employees in order to remain profitable. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Music streaming pioneer SoundCloud raises $75M from Pandora owner SiriusXM|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2020/02/11/music-streaming-pioneer-soundcloud-raises-75m-from-pandora-owner-siriusxm/|access-date=2020-11-24|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}{{Dead link|date=June 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >
