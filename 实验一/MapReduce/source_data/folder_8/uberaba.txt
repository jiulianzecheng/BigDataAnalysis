 '''Uberaba''' ({{IPA-pt|ubeˈɾabɐ}}) is a city in the state of [[Minas Gerais]], [[Southeast Region, Brazil|southeast Brazil]]. < ref > {{Cite web |title=Uberaba {{!}} Brazil {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Uberaba |access-date=2022-03-07 |website=www.britannica.com |language=en}} < /ref >   
 
 It was founded in 1820 and the city status was granted in 1856.  
 
 In [[2021]] about 340,277 people lived in Uberaba. < ref > {{Cite web |last=giftnily.com |date=2022-03-07 |title=There is tupi in Portuguese spoken in Brazil |url=https://www.opovo.com.br/noticias/brasil/2022/03/07/curitiba-ubatuba-pipoca-quotchorar-as-pitangas-quot-o-tupi-presente-no-portugues-falado-no-brasil.html |access-date=2022-03-07 |website=Giftnily |language=en-US}} < /ref >  The name of the city comes from the [[Tupi language]], and means  < nowiki > ''bright water'' < /nowiki > . 
 
 It covers an area of 4,529.7 [[Square kilometre|km < sup > 2 < /sup > ]] (1,748.9 [[Square mile|sq mi]]).
