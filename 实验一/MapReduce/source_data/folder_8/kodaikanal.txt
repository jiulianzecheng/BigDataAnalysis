'''[[Kodaikanal]]''' is a  " hill station "  city in the hills of the [[Dindigul district]] in the state of [[Tamil Nadu]], India. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://tamilnadu.com/destinations/tamilnadu-kodaikanal.htmlhtml|title=Aringnar Anna Zoological Park|publisher=Tamilnadu.com|date=8 February 2013|access-date=31 May 2017|archive-date=13 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191213114913/http://tamilnadu.com/destinations/tamilnadu-kodaikanal.htmlhtml|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Its name in the [[Tamil language]] means  " The Gift of the Forest " . < ref name= " about " > {{cite web|url=http://www.municipality.tn.gov.in/kodaikanal/abcity_city.htm|title=About City|publisher=Kodaikanal Department Of Municipal Administration And Water Supply|work=[[Government of Tamil Nadu]]|access-date=23 November 2009|archive-date=16 March 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100316080804/http://www.municipality.tn.gov.in/kodaikanal/abcity_city.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Kodaikanal is referred to as the  " Princess of Hill stations "  and has a long history as a popular tourist destination. 
 
 Kodaikanal was established in 1845 as a resort town where people would come to find refuge from the high temperatures and tropical diseases of the plains. < ref name= " books.google.com " > Mitchell Nora, ''Indian Hill Station: Kodaikanal'', University of Chicago, Dept. of Geography, [https://books.google.com/books?id=1yZjAAAAIAAJ & q=tropical+disease ch 2, Rational for Tropical Hill Stations, pp13-15], 1972.  Original from the University of California Digitized 28 Jan 2008 < /ref >  Much of the local economy is  based on the hospitality industry serving tourism. As of 2011, the city had a population of 36,501. < ref name= " dashboard " > {{cite web|title=Census Info 2011 Final population totals|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/censusinfodashboard/index.html|publisher=Office of The Registrar General and Census Commissioner, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|year=2013|access-date=26 January 2014}} < /ref >

 The town's infrastructure changes every year in preparation for the peak tourist season. Due to the rapid development of nearby cities such as [[Madurai]] and [[Coimbatore]], the town is learning to deal with year-round tourism. Popular tourist souvenirs include handcrafts, home-made chocolates, postcards and [[eucalyptus]] oil. 
 
 ===Agriculture=== 
 [[Terrace farming|Terrace farms]] in surrounding villages grow plums, pears, chile peppers, carrots, cauliflower, cabbage, garlic and onions. Most of the produce is transported by truck to other parts of India, while some are sold in the local market.other exotic fruits like dragon fruit,pears,apples, are also grown here
