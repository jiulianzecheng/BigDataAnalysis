 '''Camelopardalis''' is a [[constellation]] in the northern sky.  It is hard to see because it is not very bright.  It is also very large.  It was created and named by the Dutch astronomer named Petrus Plancius in 1612.  It represents a [[giraffe]]. < ref name=startales > {{cite web |url=http://www.ianridpath.com/startales/camelopardalis.htm |title=Camelopardalis: the Giraffe |last1=Ridpath |first1=Ian |work=Star Tales |access-date=27 January 2013}} < /ref >   Its brightest star, [[Beta Camelopardalis|β Cam]], has a [[Apparent magnitude|magnitude]] of only 4.03.  This means that Camelopardalis is very dark.  People who live in cities cannot see Camelopardalis.
