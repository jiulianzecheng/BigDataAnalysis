 '''Spikethumb frogs''' ('''''Plectrohyla''''') are a genus (group of species) of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]]. They live in Central America. They live as far north as southern Mexico through Guatemala and northern El Salvador to central and northern Honduras. The last time scientists reexamined the family Hylidae, they moved 21 species into this genus from ''[[Hyla]]''. < ref > {{aut|Faivovich, J.; Haddad, C.F.B.; Garcia, P.C.A.; Frost, D.R.; Campbell, J.A.; Wheeler, W.C.}}, 2005: Systematic Review of the Frog Family Hylidae, with Special Reference to Hylinae: Phylogenetic Analysis and Taxonomic Revision. ''Bulletin of the American Museum of Natural History'', Num. '''294''', pp.1-240. [http://digitallibrary.amnh.org/dspace/bitstream/2246/462/1/B294.pdf] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070221131729/http://digitallibrary.amnh.org/dspace/bitstream/2246/462/1/B294.pdf |date=2007-02-21 }} < /ref >  They are called spikethumb because of the spike on their thumbs. The spike is called a prepollex. The name  " ''Plectrohyla'' "  comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''plēktron'', which means  " [[spur]], " < ref > {{cite book|last=Borror|first=Donald J.|title=Dictionary of word roots and combining forms : compiled from the Greek, Latin, and other languages, with special reference to biological terms and scientific names|year=1988|publisher=Mayfield Pub. Co.|location=Mountain View, Calif.|isbn=0874840538|edition=11. print.|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/dictionaryofword00mayf}} < /ref >   and ''hyla'', the name of the group in which many of its frogs used to be.
