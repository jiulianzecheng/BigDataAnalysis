'''''Inshallah''''' ([[Arabic language|Arabic]]: إِنْ شَاءَ ٱللَّٰهُ) is an Arabic phrase that means  " if God is willing " . It is used by Arabic speakers to talk about events they hope will happen. 
 
 In many religions, such as [[Islam]], it is believed that things do not happen unless [[Allah]] (God in Arabic) lets them happen. < ref > {{Cite web|last=EIFD|date=2018-10-11|title=Insha Allah (أَن يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ): A Critical, Contextual, and Comparative Understanding|url=https://fullerstudio.fuller.edu/insha-allah-أَن-يَشَاءَ-اللَّهُ-a-critical-contextual-and-comparative-understanding/|access-date=2021-04-04|website=Fuller Studio|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Therefore this phrase is a hope that God will let the event happen. 
 
 
 " Inshallah "  is also used in many other languages, especially where there are many Muslims or where people were introduced to the Arabic language.
