 {{nihongo|'''Meowth'''|ニャース|Nyāsu|'''Nyarth''' in original [[Japanese language]] versions}} is a Pokémon species in [[Nintendo]]'s ''[[Pokémon]]'' series. Meowth first shows in the video games [[Pokémon Red and Blue|''Pokémon Red'' and ''Blue'']]. Meowth is one of the most popular Pokémon, because of an individual Meowth's role in the [[Pokémon (anime)|''Pokémon'' anime]] series. This Meowth, owned by [[Team Rocket]], is one of the few Pokémon who can speak in human language. 
 
 ==Design== 
 Meowth has a cat-like design, with cream-colored fur, and has brown paws and tail tip. It has whiskers, black-and-brown ears, and a gold coin on its head. Meowth is spends most of the daytime sleeping and walks the city streets at night. < ref name= " meowthfirered " > {{pokedex|firered|All it does is sleep during the daytime.  ==Appearances== 
 ===In the video games=== 
 Meowth appears as a wild Pokémon in many versions of the games. Meowth is one of the characters in the [[Pokémon Mystery Dungeon]] games. Meowth is also seen in the [[Nintendo 64]] game [[Pokémon Snap]], the [[Nintendo GameCube]] game ''[[Pokémon Channel]]'', and many others. Meowth is seen in the [[Super Smash Bros. (series)|Super Smash Bros. series]] as a Pokémon used with a [[Poké Ball]] item, and uses its move  " Pay Day "  and throws coins at players. 
 
 ===In the anime=== 
 Meowth has made an appearance in almost every episode in the anime because one is owned by [[Team Rocket]] agents [[Pokémon crime organizations#In the anime|Jessie and James]], the show's main antagonists. He first lived in the city, and tried to impress a female Meowth named Meowzie by learning to walk on two legs and speak human language.  ===In other media=== 
 In the [[Pokémon Adventures]] manga, Meowth's first appearance is a cameo in the first chapter as one of the Pokémon that escapes from Professor Oak's Laboratory. Meowth has appeared in the [[Pokémon Trading Card Game]] first in the Jungle series. A special Meowth card was given when buying the [[Pokémon Trading Card|Pokémon Trading Card Video Game]]. In an open forum interview with [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC News]], Pokémon anime director Masakazu Kubo noted Meowth as his favorite Pokémon, saying that while not a major Pokémon in the games, a Meowth is in the anime. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://gameboy.ign.com/articles/074/074973p1.html |title=ABC News Pokémon Chat Transcript |author=Staff |date=2000-02-09 |publisher=IGN |accessdate=2009-06-09 |archive-date=2012-08-12 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/69s7R7mJO?url=http://gameboy.ign.com/articles/074/074973p1.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
