 '''Hohenlinden''' (meaning  " high [[linden trees]] " ;{{efn|German ''hohe'' meaning  " high " , ''Linden'' meaning  " linden trees "  (singular: ''Linde'')}} colloquially: ''Linden''; in the [[Bavarian dialect]]: ''Hea-lin'') is a community in the [[Upper Bavaria]]n district of [[Ebersberg (district)|Ebersberg]]. The city of [[Lynden, Washington|Lynden]], [[Washington (state)|Washington]] is named after it, as is [[Linden, Alabama|Linden]], [[Alabama]]. Hohenlinden is also the subject of an [[s:Hohenlinden|eponymous poem]] by [[Thomas Campbell (poet)|Thomas Campbell]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Hohenlinden is in the Munich Planning Region roughly 35 & nbsp;km east of [[Munich]]. 
 
 Hohenlinden has the following traditional rural land units (''Gemarkungen'' in German): Ebersberger Forst and Hohenlinden.
