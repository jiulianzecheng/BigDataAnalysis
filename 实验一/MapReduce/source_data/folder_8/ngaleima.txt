 == Etymology == 
 The [[Meitei language|Meitei]] female given name  " Ngaleima "  (ꯉꯥꯂꯩꯃ) is made up of two words. The two words are  " Nga "  (ꯉꯥ) and  " Leima "  (ꯂꯩꯃ).  " Nga "  (ꯉꯥ) has multiple meanings. It may mean ''to [[:en:wikt:incline|incline]]'' or ''to [[:en:wikt:lean|lean]]'' in verbal form. It means [[fish]] in noun form. Here, the word  " Nga "  (ꯉꯥ)  
 refers to  " fish " . < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%89%EA%AF%A5 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Nga|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref >  The word  " Leima "  (ꯂꯩꯃ) means queen, mistress or lady. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%82%EA%AF%A9%EA%AF%83%EA%AF%A5 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Leima|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref > 
 
 == Myths == 
 [[Menstruation|Menstrual blood]], charoo ([[hay]], dried paddy stalks), hentak (edible fish paste), sumjit ([[broom]]) are considered [[profanity|unholy]] to Goddess Ngaleima. < ref name= " :4 " > {{Cite book|url=http://archive.org/details/aclcpl000001283a551/page/384/mode/2up?q=Ngaleima|title=ACL-CPL 00128 Man In India Vol.50 1970 Oct-Dec}} < /ref >  So, if there is an adverse entry of fish into the fishing nets, the presence of menstruating woman is highly suspected at the spot, especially on the floating dam. < ref name= " :4 "  / >  Other suspicious reasons are the dropping down of the charoo (hay, dried paddy stalks) or hentak (edible fish paste) or sumjit (broom) by people out of jealousy into the place. < ref name= " :4 "  / > 
 
 == Association with other deities == 
 {{See|Incarnations of Leimarel Sidabi}} 
 Goddess Ngaleima (Ngareima) is considered to be one of the divine manifestations of [[Leimarel Sidabi|Leimarel (Leimalel)]], the supreme mother earth goddess. It is said that Leimalel becomes Ngaleima when she is in the fish yard. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.461915/page/n73/mode/2up?q=Ngareima+Leimalel+manifests+fish+yard|title=A Critical Study Of The Religious Philosophy|last=Singh|first=L. Bhagyachandra|date=1991|pages=73}} < /ref >
