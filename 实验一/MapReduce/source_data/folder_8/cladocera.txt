 '''Cladocera''' is an [[order (biology)|order]] of small [[crustacean]]s commonly called '''water fleas'''. Around 620 species have been recognised so far, with many more undescribed. < ref name= " Forró " > {{cite book |author=L. Forró N.M. Korovchinsky, A.A. Kotov  &  A. Petrusek |year=2008 |chapter=Global diversity of cladocerans (Cladocera; Crustacea) in freshwater |journal=Hydrobiologia |volume=595 |issue=1 |pages=177–184 |doi=10.1007/s10750-007-9013-5 |series=Developments in Hydrobiology 198 |title=Freshwater animal diversity assessment |isbn=978-1-4020-8259-7 |s2cid=45363782 |editor=Estelle V. Balian, Christian Lévêque, Hendrik Segers  &  Koen Martens |chapter-url=http://decapoda.nhm.org/pdfs/27704/27704.pdf }} {{doi|10.1007/978-1-4020-8259-7_19}} < /ref >   
 
 They are everywhere in inland [[aquatic]] [[habitat]]s, but rare in the oceans. < ref name= " Belk " > {{cite book |editor=Sol Felty Light  &  James T. Carlton |year=2007 |title=The Light and Smith Manual: intertidal invertebrates from Central California to Oregon |edition=4th |publisher=University of California Press |isbn=9780520239395|chapter=Branchiopoda |author=Denton Belk |pages=414–417 |chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=64jgZ1CfmB8C & pg=PA416}} < /ref >  Most are {{convert|0.2|-|6.0|mm|2|abbr=on}} long, with a down-turned head, and a carapace covering the thorax and abdomen. < ref name= " Pennak_Cladocera " > {{cite book |editor=Douglas Grant Smith |chapter=Cladoceran Branchiopoda (water fleas) |pages=453 488 |author=Douglas Grant Smith  &  Kirstern Work |year=2001 |title=Pennak's freshwater invertebrates of the United States: Porifera to Crustacea |edition=4th |publisher=Wiley |isbn=9780471358374 |chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GqIctb8IqPoC & pg=PA468}} < /ref >  There is a single median [[compound eye]]. < ref name= " Belk " / >  They swim with jerking [[flea]]-like movements.   
 
 Most species show cyclical [[parthenogenesis]], where [[asexual reproduction]] is occasionally supplemented by [[sexual reproduction]]. The sexual reproduction produces resting eggs that allow the species to survive harsh conditions and disperse to distant habitats. < ref name= " Decaestecker " > {{cite book |editor=Isa Schön, Koen Martens  &  Peter van Dijk |year=2009 |title=Lost sex: the evolutionary biology of parthenogenesis |publisher=Springer |isbn=9789048127696 |chapter=Cyclical parthenogeness in ''Daphnia'': sexual versus asexual reproduction |author=Ellen Decaestecker, Luc De Meester  &  Joachim Mergaey |pages=295–316 |chapter-url=https://lirias.kuleuven.be/handle/123456789/208129 |doi=10.1007/978-90-481-2770-2_15|s2cid=82949264 }} < /ref >
