 '''Blaenavon''' ({{lang-cy|Blaenafon}}) is a [[town]] in southeastern [[Wales]]. It is on the south of [[Afon Lwyd]] and north of [[Pontypool]]. Parts of the town and surrounding country form the [[Blaenavon Industrial Landscape]] became part of the [[World Heritage Site]] by [[UNESCO]] in [[2000]]. There are about 6,057 people living there. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.visitblaenavon.co.uk/en/WorldHeritageSite/LookingAfterBlaenavon/Regeneration.aspx|title=Regeneration - Blaenavon World Heritage Site|website=www.visitblaenavon.co.uk|access-date=2020-08-10|archive-date=2020-09-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200923060133/https://www.visitblaenavon.co.uk/en/WorldHeritageSite/LookingAfterBlaenavon/Regeneration.aspx|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.torfaenpublicservicesboard.co.uk/en/Documents/Assessment-of-Well-being/Part-2-Well-being-of-Blaenavon-and-its-communities.pdf|title=Blaenavon Wellbeing Infographics|access-date=2020-08-10|archive-date=2021-01-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210126092039/http://www.torfaenpublicservicesboard.co.uk/en/Documents/Assessment-of-Well-being/Part-2-Well-being-of-Blaenavon-and-its-communities.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 ''Blaenavon'' literally means  " front of the river "  or loosely translated as  " river's source "  in the [[Welsh language]].  
 
 == Government == 
 Blaenavon is a [[Community (Wales)|community]] represented by Blaenavon Town Council, and [[Electoral division|electoral ward]] of [[Torfaen County Borough Council]]. Blaenavon is often 
 
 [[Town twinning|twinned]] with [[Coutras]] in France. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.torfaen.gov.uk/en/LeisureParksEvents/TourismTravel/Towntwinning/Town-Twinning.aspx|title=Town Twinning|publisher=[[Torfaen County Borough Council]]|access-date=20 September 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 == Geography == 
 The town is near the source of the [[Afon Lwyd]] river. It is in the north of [[Cwmbran]]. 
 
 == Landmarks == 
 Parts of the town and surrounding country form the [[Blaenavon Industrial Landscape]] were recognised as a [[World Heritage Site]] by [[UNESCO]] in 2000. Some of the attractions in the town include:  
 
 * [[Big Pit National Coal Museum]]  
 * [[Blaenavon Ironworks]]  < ref > [http://www.world-heritage-blaenavon.org.uk/whs-info/nomination/iron/ironworks.htm Blaenavon Ironworks] < /ref >   
 * [[Pontypool and Blaenavon Railway]]  
 * Blaenavon Male Voice Choir  
 * Blaenavon Town Band  
 
 == Education == 
 After [[Samuel Hopkins]] death in 1815, his sister Sarah Hopkins of Rugeley, erected Blaenavon Endowed School in his memory. 
 
 == Religious sites == 
 St Peter's Church was constructed in 1804. It was gifted to the parish by Thomas Hill and Samuel Hopkins. 
 
 == Sports == 
 It had a Blaenavon Golf Club which was founded in 1906. The club closed in 1937. < ref > [http://www.golfsmissinglinks.co.uk/index.php/wales-64/303-blaenavon-golf-club-forge-side-torfaen “Blaenavon Golf Club”], “Golf’s Missing Links”. < /ref >
