'''Arbaeen''' ({{lang-ar|'''الأربعين'''}},  " forty " ), '''Chehelom''' ({{lang-fa|'''چهلم'''}}, {{lang-ur|'''چہلم'''}},  " the fortieth [day] " ) or '''Qirkhi''', ''' Imamin Qirkhi''' ({{lang-az|'''İmamın qırxı''', '''امامین قیرخی'''}},  " the fortieth of Imam " ) is a [[Shia Islam|Shia Muslim]] religious observance that occurs [[40 (number)|forty]] days after the [[Day of Ashura]]. It honors the [[martyrdom]] of [[Husayn ibn Ali]], the grandson of [[Muhammad]]. < ref name=BBC > {{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-30462820 |title=Shia pilgrims flock to Karbala for Arbaeen climax |author= < !--Staff writer(s)-- >  |date=13 December 2014 |publisher=BBC News |access-date=19 December 2014}} < /ref >  [[Imam]] Husayn ibn Ali and 72 [[companion]]s were killed by [[Yazid I]]'s army in the [[Battle of Karbala]] in 61 & nbsp;AH (680 CE). < ref name=BBC/ >  Arbaeen or forty days is also the usual length of [[mourning]] in many Muslim [[tradition]]s. Arbaeen is one of the largest [[pilgrimage]] gatherings on Earth. [[Million]]s of people go to the city of [[Karbala]] in [[Iraq]]. The pilgrims include Shia, Sunnis, Christians, Yazidi and other faiths. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/20-million-shia-muslims-brave-isis-by-making-pilgrimage-karbala-arbaeen-1476618 |title=20 Million Shia Muslims Brave Isis by Making Pilgrimage to Karbala for Arbaeen |author=Mark Piggott|date=25 November 2014 |publisher= IBTimes Co., Ltd |access-date=19 December 2014}} < /ref >
