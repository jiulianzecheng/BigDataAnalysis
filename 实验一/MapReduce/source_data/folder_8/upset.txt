An '''upset''' happens in a [[competition]], mainly in [[Election|electoral]] [[politics]] or [[sports]], when the party popularly expected to win (the favorite), either loses to or draws/ties a game with an [[Underdog (term)|underdog]] whom the majority expects to lose. It is often used in reference to beating the betting odds in sports, or beating the opinion polls in electoral politics. < ref > [http://www.wordorigins.org/index.php/upset  " Upset " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120624093600/http://www.wordorigins.org/index.php/upset/ |date=2012-06-24 }} at Wordorigins.org.  Retrieved April 17, 2013. < /ref > 
 
 Examples include: [[Donald Trump]] beating [[Hillary Clinton]] in the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 presidential election]] or [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez]] beating Representative [[Joe Crowley]] in the 2018 midterms.
