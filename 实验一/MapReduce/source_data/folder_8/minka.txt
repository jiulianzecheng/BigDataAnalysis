 {{nihongo|'''Minka'''|(民家|}} is general [[Japan]]ese term for all rural and traditional buildings  except for palaces, temples and [[wikt:structure|structure]]s which have been influenced by Western [[architecture]]. < ref name= " nussbaum637 " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA637 & dq=  " ''Minka'' " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 637. < /ref > 
 
 Minka are traditional houses and private residences of farmers, artisans, and merchants 
 
 ==Types== 
 [[File:Gassho-zukuri farmhouse-02.jpg|thumb|right|Repairing a Minka roof]] 
 There are several types or styles of ''minka'', including 
 * Semi-buried huts (''tate-ana jūkyo'') < ref name= " nussbaum637 " / > 
 * Wooden houses with roofs of thatching (''kayabunki''), wood shingles (''ishitori'') or tiles (''kawarabuki'') < ref name= " nussbaum637 " / > 
 * Shops and warehouses (''dozō, kura'') < ref name= " nussbaum637 " / >
