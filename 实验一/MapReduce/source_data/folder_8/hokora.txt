 {{Nihongo|'''''Hokora''''' or '''''hokura'''''|祠 or 神庫}} is a small [[Shinto shrine]]. Sometimes they are built near larger shrines. Other times they are built near streets. < ref name= " eos " > {{Cite web|title=Shinto Portal - IJCC, Kokugakuin University|url=https://www2.kokugakuin.ac.jp/e-shinto/|access-date=2022-10-15|website=www2.kokugakuin.ac.jp|language=en}} < /ref >  Sometimes they are built to host minor kami who protect travelers. < ref name= " eos "  / > 
 
 The term ''hokora''  is an old word for Shinto shrine, it came from {{Nihongo|''hokura''|神庫}}, literally meaning  " kami repository " . This means the first shrines may have been huts to house [[yorishiro]].  < ref group= " note " > [[Yorishiro]] are objects Kami are invited to live in such as trees or rocks. < /ref > < ref name= " Tamura20 " > {{Cite book|title=Japanese Buddhism - A Cultural History|url=https://archive.org/details/japanesebuddhism0000tamu|last=Tamura|first=Yoshiro|publisher=Kosei Publishing Company|year=2000|isbn=4-333-01684-3|edition=First|location=Tokyo|page=[https://archive.org/details/japanesebuddhism0000tamu/page/232 232] pages|chapter=The Birth of the Japanese nation}} < /ref >
