 '''Lombardy''' ([[Help:IPA/English|/ˈlɒmbərdi, ˈlʌm-/]] [[Help:Pronunciation respelling key|''LOM-bər-dee, LUM-'']]; [[Italian language|Italian]]: ''Lombardia'' [[Help:IPA/Italian|[lombarˈdiːa]]]; [[Lombard language|Lombard]]: ''Lombardia'',  < small > Western Lombard: < /small >  [[Help:IPA/Lombard|[lũbɐ̞rˈdiːɑ]]],  < small > Eastern Lombard: < /small >  [[Help:IPA/Lombard|[lombɐ̞rˈdiːɑ, -ˈdeːɑ]]]) is a [[Regions of Italy|region]] in the northern part of [[Italy]]. It is the most populated region of the country, and has almost 9.4 million people. The capital is [[Milan]]. Lombardy has [[province]]s within it.  
 
 To the north of Lombardy is the country of [[Switzerland]] (Canton Ticino and Grigioni). To the west is the region of [[Piedmont]]. To the east are the regions of [[Veneto]] and [[Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol|Trentino - Alto Adige]]. To the south is the region of [[Emilia-Romagna]]. 
 [[File:Porta di Prada, Grigna - panoramio.jpg|thumb|left|A natural formation (or [[geological]] [[feature]]) in Lombardy]] 
 
 == Name == 
 In the Italian language, Lombardy is called ''Lombardia''. This name comes from an old German word: ''Langbardland'' and means  " country of the [[Lombards]] " . 
 
 == Geography == 
 In the north of Lombardy there are very high mountains, (40% of the territory is mountains) the highest mountains are almost 4.000 meters in the [[Bernina Range]]. 
 
 In the south of the region flows the [[Po river]], which is the longest river in Italy. The most important rivers are the [[Adda river]], the [[Mincio]] and the [[Ticino (river)|Ticino river]], all of them end in the Po river. 
 
 There are also a lot of lakes, the largest are the [[lake Maggiore]], the [[Lake of Varese]], the [[lake Como]], and the [[lake Garda]].
