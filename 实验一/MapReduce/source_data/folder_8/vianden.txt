 '''Vianden''' ({{lang-lb|Veianen}}) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] with [[List of cities in Luxembourg|city status]] in north-eastern [[Luxembourg]], capital of the [[Vianden (canton)|canton Vianden]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 The name of the town of Vianden is derived from the [[Gaulish language|Gallic]] word '''''vien''''' which means  " [[Rock (geology)|rocky]] " . Already in the year 1256, Vianden is cited as a city, but it is only since 1308 that legally holds the title of city. < ref name=commune > {{cite web |url=http://www.vianden.lu/fr-FR/la-commune-se-presente |title=Présentation |publisher=Vianden - La commune |language=fr |access-date=29 May 2016 }}{{Dead link|date=February 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The ''commune'' is in the [[Oesling]], a region within the greater [[Ardennes (forest)|Ardennes]] area. Vianden is in the [[valley]] of the river [[Our (river)|Our]], a  [[tributary]] of the [[Sauer]] river, near the border between Luxembourg and [[Germany]]. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|9.7|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name=stats / >  The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|198|-|515|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. At the [[church]], the altitude  is {{Convert|224|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |access-date= 10 June 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 Vianden is surrounded by two ''communes'' of Luxembourg: [[Tandel]] to the south and west; and [[Putscheid]] to the northwest. To the north, west and southwest, Vianden borders with four [[Municipality|municipalities]] of the [[Eifelkreis Bitburg-Prüm]] district of the [[Germany|German]] [[States of Germany|state]] of [[Rhineland-Palatinate]]ː [[Waldhof-Falkenstein]] and [[Bauler, Bitburg-Prüm|Bauler]] to the north, [[Körperich]] to the east and [[Roth an der Our]] to the southeast.

 ==Places of interest== 
 Some [[History|historic]] [[building]]s in the ''commune'' areː < ref name=commune / > 
 * The '''Vianden Castle''' ({{lang-fr|Château de Vianden}}, [[German language|German]]: ''Burg Vianden'', {{lang-lb|Buerg Veianen}}) is one of the largest [[Fortification|fortified]] [[castle]]s west of the [[Rhine]] river. With origins dating from the 10th century, the castle was built in the [[Romanesque architecture|Romanesque style]] from the 11th to 14th centuries. 
 * The '''Church of the Trinitarians''', built in 1248 with its [[Gothic architecture|Gothic]] [[cloister]]. 
 * The '''City Hall''', built in 1679. 
 * The '''St. Nicolas Church''', built in the thirteenth century. 
 
 ==People from or related to Vianden== 
 * [[Yolanda of Vianden]], (1231–83), youngest daughter of [[Count]] Henry I, who joined the [[Convent]] of Marienthal. 
 * [[Victor Hugo]] (1802–85), the famous French [[author]], who stayed in Vianden on several occasions between 1862 and 1871. 
 * [[Edmond de la Fontaine]] (1823–91) otherwise known as ''Dicks'', Luxembourg's national [[poet]], who also wrote Vianden's first travel guide. 
 * [[René Engelmann]] (1880–1915), a [[Linguistics|linguist]] and [[novel]]ist, who established the basic [[grammar]] of the [[Luxembourgish]] dialect, later to become a national language.
