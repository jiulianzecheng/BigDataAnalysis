In [[Middle Ages|medieval history]], '''Ifriqiya''' or '''Ifriqiyah''' ({{lang-ar|إفريقية}}) was the area of the coastal regions of what are today western [[Libya]], [[Tunisia]], and eastern [[Algeria]]. This area included what had been the [[Roman province]] of [[Africa Province|Africa]]. 
   
 In modern Arabic, the term simply means  " [[Africa]] " . It comes from the [[Latin]] term ''Africa''.
