'''Statin''' drugs, also known as '''HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors''', are lipid-lowering drugs that reduce [[disease|illness]] in people who have a high risk of [[cardiovascular disease]]. They are the most common [[cholesterol]]-lowering drugs. < ref > {{cite web|title=Cholesterol Drugs|publisher=American Heart Association|url=https://watchlearnlive.heart.org/?moduleSelect=chldrg|access-date=May 24, 2021}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Low-density lipoprotein]] (LDL) carriers of cholesterol play key roles in [[arteriosclerosis]], [[stroke]] and [[coronary heart disease]]. < ref > {{cite document|title=Lipid Modification|year=2014 |publisher=National Institutes of Health|pmid=25340243 |url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK248067|access-date=May 24, 2021|author1=National Clinical Guideline Centre (UK) }} < /ref > 
 
 The common side effects of statins are [[diabetes mellitus]], [[nausea]], [[hormonal imbalance]], and muscle pains. < ref > {{cite document|title=Comparative Tolerability/Harms of Individual Statins|publisher=London School of Economics|url=http://eprints.lse.ac.uk/53591/1/__lse.ac.uk_storage_LIBRARY_Secondary_libfile_shared_repository_Content_Naci%2C%20H_Naci_Comparative_%20tolerability_%20harms_2013_Naci_Comparative_%20tolerability_%20harms_2013.pdf|access-date=May 24, 2021}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Schooling|first1=C.
