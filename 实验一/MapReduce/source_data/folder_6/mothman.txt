 The '''Mothman''' is a [[legendary creature]] in American [[folklore]]. [[Point Pleasant, West Virginia|Point Pleasant]], [[West Virginia]], [[United States]] saw Mothman from  November 15, 1966 to December 15, 1967. 
 
 The first newspaper publication of November 16, 1966 was titled  " Couples See Man-Sized Bird...Creature...Something. " < ref name=Point > Mark Turner (November 16, 1966).  " Couples See Man-Sized Bird...Creature...Something. "  ''Point Pleasant Register''. cited in [https://web.archive.org/web/20071011230219/http://www.westva.net/mothman/1966-11-16.htm  WestVA.Net]. < /ref >   
 
 The people who saw Mothman saw a dark shape with very large glowing red eyes. 
 
 Mothman may have been a [[barred owl]].  Barred owls's eyes [[eyeshine|shine bright red]] in the dark. At night, the eyes look much bigger than they really are.  The people may have seen a barn owl flying very close to them and thought it was a larger animal further away. < ref name=Aud > {{cite web|url=https://www.audubon.org/news/is-mothman-west-virginia-owl|publisher=[[Audubon Society]]|accessdate=August 8, 2021|author=Asher Elbein|title=Is the Mothman of West Virginia an Owl? |date=October 26, 2018}} < /ref >

 == In popular culture == 
 
 * The Mothman became popular when American journalist and [[Ufology|UFOlogist]] [[John Keel]] wrote the book ''[[The Mothman Prophecies]]'' and published it in 1975. The book was adapted into a  [[The Mothman Prophecies (film)|2002 movie]].  
 
 * Also the Mothman appeared in the video game [[Fallout 76]].
