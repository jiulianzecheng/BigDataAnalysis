 '''Hydrox''' is a [[cream]]-filled [[cookie]] [[sandwich]] made by [[Leaf Brands]]. It originally launched in 1908, and was made by [[Sunshine Biscuits]] for over 90 years. < ref name=post > {{cite news|title=The Big O: The Chelsea-born Oreo cookie celebrates its 100th birthday|author=Eber, H.|publisher=[[New York Post]]|date=February 26, 2012|pages=44â€“45}} < /ref >  Hydrox cookies have a sweet filling and a crunchy cookie shell. < ref name=Dead > {{cite news | author=Christopher Rhoads| title=The Hydrox Cookie Is Dead, and Fans Won't Get Over It | url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB120069573721101481 | work=The Wall Street Journal | date=January 19, 2008 | access-date=November 29, 2009}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[Oreo]] cookie, introduced in 1912, was inspired by the Hydrox. The Oreo eventually became more popular than Hydrox which resulted in Hydrox being thought of as a copy of Oreo. < ref name=Lukas > {{cite news | author=Paul Lukas| title=Oreos to Hydrox: Resistance Is Futile | url=http://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/fortune_archive/1999/03/15/256478/index.htm | work=Fortune | date=March 15, 1999 | access-date=November 29, 2009}} < /ref >  Hydrox was mostly closed in 1999 after Sunshine was bought by [[Keebler Company|Keebler]], which was later bought by [[Kellogg's]]. < ref name= " Lukas "  / > 
 
 In 2008, fans of Hydrox made thousands of phone calls and an online [[wikt:petition|petition]] asking Kellogg's to make Hydrox again for its 100th [[wikt:anniversary|anniversary]]. < ref > {{cite news | author=Christopher Rhoads| title=Hydrox Redux: Cookie Duels Oreo, Again | url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB121193695783324733 | work=The Wall Street Journal | date=May 28, 2008}} < /ref >  Kellogg's started making Hydrox again for less than one year. In September 2015, Leaf Brands began making Hydrox again with a new recipe. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.foodbusinessnews.net/articles/6751-leaf-brands-begins-production-of-hydrox-cookies | title=Leaf Brands production of Hydrox cookies | last1=Schroeder | first1=Eric | date=September 4, 2015 | publisher=Food Business News}} < /ref >  Leaf Brands filed a report with the US [[Federal Trade Commission]] in 2019. Leaf Brands claimed that the maker of Oreos, [[Mondelez International]], unfairly put Hydrox products behind Oreo products on [[supermarket]] shelves. < ref > {{cite news |last1=Gasparro |first1=Annie |title=Oreo Competitor Makes Federal Case Out of 'Cutthroat' Cookie Rivalry |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/food-fight-in-the-cookie-aislehydrox-vs-oreo-turns-cutthroat-11547740235 |accessdate=October 30, 2021 |work=Wall Street Journal |date=January 17, 2019}} < /ref >
