 '''CNET''' (written as '''c|net''') is formerly known as  '''Computer Network'''. Founded in 1994 by Halsey Minor and Shelby Bonnie, it was the flagship brand of CNET Networks and became a brand of CBS Interactive through that unit's acquisition of CNET < ref > {{Cite web|title=CNET|url=https://www.prfire.co.uk/cnet-launches-data-centre-fundamentals-program-as-a-1-day-instructor-led-program/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=FREE UK Press Release Distribution|language=en-GB}}{{Dead link|date=October 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  Networks in 2008, which was the previous owner prior to October 30, 2020. Red Ventures owned CNET since 2020. CNET originally produced content for radio and television in addition to its website and now uses new media distribution methods through its Internet television network, CNET Video, and its podcast and blog networks. 
 
 It is an [[United States|American]] media website that publishes reviews, news, articles, blogs, podcasts and videos on global technology and [[consumer electronics]].  CNET has the highest readership among web news sites. It has over 200 million readers per month.
