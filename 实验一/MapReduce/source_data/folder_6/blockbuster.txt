 '''Blockbuster''' was an American provider of home video ([[DVD]], [[VHS]]), and [[video game]] rental services that was founded in 1985 and disestablished in 2013. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/news/ni0228635/|title=Blockbuster opening Mexico multiplex|work=IMDb}} < /ref >  In 2004, Blockbuster had 84,300 employees < ref > {{cite web|title=The Sad End Of Blockbuster Video: The Onetime $5 Billion Company Is Being Liquidated As Competition From Online Giants Netflix And Hulu Prove All Too Much For The Iconic Brand|url=http://www.ibtimes.com/sad-end-blockbuster-video-onetime-5-billion-company-being-liquidated-competition-1496962|website=International Business Times|date=5 December 2013}} < /ref >  and over 8,000 stores. < ref > {{cite news |title=15 Companies That Might Not Survive 2009 |url=http://money.usnews.com/money/blogs/flowchart/2009/2/6/15-companies-that-might-not-survive-2009.html |author=Rick Newman |date=February 6, 2009 |access-date=December 9, 2010 |publisher=US News |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101205145626/http://money.usnews.com/money/blogs/flowchart/2009/2/6/15-companies-that-might-not-survive-2009.html |archive-date=December 5, 2010 |df=mdy }} < /ref > < ref name= nyt09 > {{cite news|title=Other Retailers Find Ex-Blockbuster Stores Just Right|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/09/business/09blockbuster.html|work=The New York Times|first=Stephanie|last=Clifford|date=April 8, 2011}} < /ref > 
 
 Blockbuster was founded in 1985 by David Cook, a technician. In 1994 it was bought by media giant [[Viacom]] and in 1997 John Antioco was named CEO. In 2004, they launched the  " Blockbuster By Mail "  DVD service to compete with [[Netflix]] and In 2007, James Keyes, a [[7-Eleven]] executive, replaced Antioco as CEO. Blockbuster also started  " Blockbuster On Demand "  as an online-streaming service. Blockbuster filed for [[bankruptcy]] on September 23, 2010. < ref > {{cite web|title=Blockbuster LLC Chapter 11 Petition|url=https://www.pacermonitor.com/view/PXDYCPY/Blockbuster_Inc.__nysbke-10-14997__0001.0.pdf|website=PacerMonitor|publisher=PacerMonitor|access-date=9 May 2016}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite press release |title=Blockbuster Reaches Agreement on Plan to Recapitalize Balance Sheet and Substantially Reduce its Indebtedness |publisher=Blockbuster |date=September 23, 2010 |url=http://investor.blockbuster.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=99383 & p=irol-newsArticle & ID=1474126 |access-date=September 23, 2010 |archive-date=October 3, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181003181215/http://investor.blockbuster.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=99383 & p=irol-newsArticle & ID=1474126}} < /ref >  On April 6, 2011, the company and its remaining 1,700 stores were bought by the satellite television company [[Dish Network]] and Michael Kelly of Dish was named President of Blockbuster. < ref name= " Fritz " > {{cite news |url=http://articles.latimes.com/2011/apr/07/business/la-fi-ct-dish-blockbuster-20110407 |title=Dish Network wins bidding for assets of bankrupt Blockbuster |work=Los Angeles Times |access-date= May 2, 2011 |first=Ben |last=Fritz |date=April 7, 2011}} < /ref > < ref name= " DishNetwork.MediaRoom.com " > {{cite web|title=DISH Network Completes Acquisition of Blockbuster Assets|url=http://about.dish.com/press-release/financial/dish-network-completes-acquisition-blockbuster-assets| date= April 26, 2011|publisher=DishNetwork.MediaRoom.com|access-date=June 16, 2011}} < /ref >  Stores remained open until Dish closed all company-owned locations in 2013. 
 
 In 2015, Blockbuster replaced  " Blockbuster On Demand "  with Sling TV, an over-the-top television service. The Blockbuster Fan Page originally tracked the franchise-owned stores, but as of 2019 there is only remaining in operation in Bend, [[Oregon]]. < ref name=IsntDead > {{cite web |title=The Blockbuster Fan Page |url=http://cybernight.elementfx.com/buster.html |access-date=2016-09-11 |archive-date=2016-10-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161024000222/http://cybernight.elementfx.com/buster.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
