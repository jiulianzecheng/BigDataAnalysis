 ''' " Rockabye " ''' is a [[house music|house]] and [[pop music|pop]] song from the [[electronic dance music|electronic dance]] group [[Clean Bandit]]. The song also has [[Jamaica|Jamaican]] singer [[Sean Paul]] and [[Britain|British]] singer [[Anne-Marie (singer)|Anne-Marie]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.warnermusic.ca/press-release/2524/uk-trio-clean-bandit-release-their-new-single-rockabye-feat-sean-paul-and-anne-marie|title=UK Trio Release Their New Single 'Rockabye'|publisher=Warner Music Canada|access-date=2017-01-17|archive-date=2016-12-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220222414/http://www.warnermusic.ca/press-release/2524/uk-trio-clean-bandit-release-their-new-single-rockabye-feat-sean-paul-and-anne-marie/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The song is about how hard it is to be a single mother. The song refers to the [[nursery rhyme]]  " Rock-a-bye Baby. " 
 
 On 28 October 2016, the song entered the [[UK Singles Chart]] at #7. The following week, it went to #3. In its third week, it reached #1. In the [[United Kingdom]], the song became their second #1, after  " Rather Be "  in 2014.  The song also topped charts in Australia, Germany, Italy, Switzerland, Denmark, Austria, Belgium, Italy, Sweden, Finland, and New Zealand. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Clean+Bandit+feat.+Sean+Paul+ & +Anne-Marie & titel=Rockabye & cat=s|title=australian-charts.com - Australian charts portal|last=Hung|first=Steffen|website=australian-charts.com|access-date=2018-01-17|archive-date=2017-10-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171004131202/http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Clean+Bandit+Feat%2E+Sean+Paul+%26+Anne%2Dmarie & titel=Rockabye & cat=s|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 In the [[United States]],  " Rockabye "  began at #100 on [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] during the week of 24 December. It was Clean Bandit's first entry on the chart since  " Rather Be "  with Jess Glynne. The song reached number nine on the US Hot 100, becoming their second US top-ten hit after  " Rather Be " . < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2016-12-24|title=Hot 100|publisher=Prometheus Media LLC|access-date=2017-01-17}} < /ref >
