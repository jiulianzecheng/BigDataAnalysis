 '''Taothingmang''' was a [[Meetei people|Meetei]] ruler of [[Ningthouja dynasty]] of [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). He is a son and the successor of Emperor [[Khuyoi Tompok]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & dq=Taothingmang & pg=PA82|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wLzDLsEYZGQC & dq=Taothingmang & pg=PA22|title=Cultural History of Manipur: Sija Laioibi and the Maharas|last=Devi|first=Jamini|date=2010|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-8324-342-1|language=en}} < /ref > 
 He is one of the nine kings associated with the different designs in a historic flag of the kingdom. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5_Sd30LF2fUC & q=Taothingmang|title=Anvīkshā|date=2004|publisher=Jadavpur University|language=en}} < /ref >  Other than the [[Cheitharol Kumbaba]], the [[Ningthourol Lambuba]] and the [[Chada Laihui]], Taothingmang and his elder brother [[Yoimongba]] are also especially mentioned in the [[Toreirol Lambuba]] and the [[Tutenglon]].
