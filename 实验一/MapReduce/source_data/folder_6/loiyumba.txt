 '''Meidingu Loyumba''' ({{lang-omp|Meitingu Loyumpa}}) or '''Ningthou Loiyumba''' ({{lang-omp|Ningthou Loiyumpa}}) (1074 AD-1122 AD) was a ruler of [[Manipur]] ([[Manipur|Kangleipak]]). He is best known for introducing [[Loiyumpa Silyel]], the first written [[constitution]] to the kingdom in 1110 AD. He was a good ruler and there were peace during his time. He assigned a particular duty to every family ({{lang-mni|[[yumnak]]}}) in the kingdom. < ref name= " Paniker 1997 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KYLpvaKJIMEC & dq=loiyumba+Shinyen & pg=PA326|title=Medieval Indian Literature: Surveys and selections|last=Paniker|first=K. Ayyappa|date=1997|publisher=Sahitya Akademi|isbn=978-81-260-0365-5|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " Behera " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kZJCEAAAQBAJ & dq=Loiyumba & pg=PA262|title=Tribe-British Relations in India: Revisiting Text, Perspective and Approach|last=Behera|first=Maguni Charan|date=2021-09-11|publisher=Springer Nature|isbn=978-981-16-3424-6|language=en}} < /ref >  He divided his kingdom into 6 panas or [[district]]s (circles for administrative convenience). According to many historians, the coronation of King Loyumba marks the end of [[Ancient Manipur]] and the beginning of [[Medieval Manipur]] in the [[history of Manipur]] kingdom. < ref name= " Paniker 1997 " / > < ref name= " Behera " / > 
 
 He introduced [[Lallup system]], the self imposed compulsory military education system into the kingdom in 1074 AD. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fNXkDQAAQBAJ & dq=Loiyumba+self+imposed+compulsory+military+education+1074 & pg=PA52|title=ATTITUDES OF POSTGRADUATE STUDENTS TOWARDS LIFELONG LEARNING|last=Singh|first=Dr Rajkumar Suresh|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=978-1-365-47887-1|language=en}} < /ref >  He initially introduced the [[Shammi Lanmi Phi]] shawl, crafted by the [[Khoisnam]] family.
