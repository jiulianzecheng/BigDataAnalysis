'''Fiqh''' or '''Islamic jurisprudence''' ({{lang-ar|فقه}}) is an expansion of [[Sharia]] law and is meant to be used with [[fatwa]]s by [[Islam]]ic clerics (known as 'Ulema' in Arabic) to help Muslims not break Sharia law. Fiqh is a section of [[Islamic law]] which deals with acts of Muslim, that includes both worship and daily life actions.  
 In [[Sunni Islam]] there are four main schools of thought, they are: 
 
 *[[Hanafi]] 
 *[[Maliki]] 
 *[[Shafi'i]] 
 *[[Hanbali]] 
 
 < nowiki > ** < /nowiki > '''Islamic jurisprudence or [https://www.al-feqh.com/en Feqh] illustrates Islamic Law for Acts of Worship such as Prayer, Zakat, Fasting, Hajj, and Purification.''' 
 
 The different schools of thought are not different beliefs but different views . 
 
 In [[Shia Islam]] there is one main school of thought, it is called [[Jaferi|Ja'fari]]
