 '''Onoway''' is a town in [[central Alberta]], [[Canada]]. It is west of [[Edmonton, Alberta|Edmonton]] at the junction of [[Alberta Highway 37|Highway 37]] and [[Alberta Highway 43|Highway 43]].  
 
 The [[Canadian National Railway]] tracks run through the town, which is east of [[Lac Ste. Anne (Alberta)|Lac Ste. Anne]] and south of the [[Sturgeon River (Alberta)|Sturgeon River]]. Onoway built and opened a new elementary school, but the fate of the existing school has not yet been confirmed. Every June, Onoway has a fair weekend, called  " Heritage Days " . 
 
 == Demographics == 
 In the [[Canada 2011 Census|2011 Census]], the Town of Onoway had a population of 1,039. This was an 18.7% increase from its 2006 population of 875. About 375 of its 403 total houses were occupied. In the 2010 [[Census#Alberta|municipal census]], there was a population of 1,021. < ref name=2010pop > {{cite web | url=http://www.municipalaffairs.alberta.ca/documents/msb/2010pop.pdf | title=2010 Official Population List | publisher=Alberta Municipal Affairs | date=2010-09-15 | accessdate=2011-01-19}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2006, Onoway had a population of 875 living in 355 houses, a 3.3% increase from 2001.
