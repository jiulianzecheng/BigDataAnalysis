 '''Amblypygi''' is an [[Order (biology)|order]] of [[Arachnid|Arachnids]] also known as '''whip spiders''' or tailless '''whip scorpions'''. < ref > not to be confused with whip scorpions that belong to the arachnid order [[Thelyphonida]] < /ref >  The name 'amblypygid' means  " blunt rump " , a reference to a lack of the telson (tail). They are harmless to [[human]]s. 
 
 The Amblypygs are in the subphylum [[Chelicerata]] of the [[phylum]] [[Arthropod|Arthropoda]]. The order has 5 families, 17 genera and about 155 species. They are found in [[tropics|tropical]] and [[subtropics|subtropical]] regions worldwide. Some species are subterranean; many are [[nocturnal]]. During the day, they may hide under logs, bark, stones, or leaves. They prefer a [[Humidity|humid]] [[environment]].
