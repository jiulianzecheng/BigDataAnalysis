 A '''relic''' is an object, especially a piece of the [[body]] or a personal item of someone of [[Religion|religious]] importance, that was carefully [[Preservation|preserved]] with an air of [[veneration]] as a memorial that you can touch. Relics are an important aspect of [[Buddhism]], some [[Religious denomination|denominations]] of [[Christianity]], [[Hinduism]], [[shamanism]], and many other personal belief systems. 
 
 The word '''relic''' comes from the [[Latin]] ''reliquiae'' ('remains'). A [[reliquary]] is a [[shrine]] that houses one or more relics. 
 
 Some well-known religious relics include the Tooth of [[Buddha]], the [[Holy Grail]], the Crown of Thorns, the [[True Cross]] of Jesus, the Staff of [[Moses]].
