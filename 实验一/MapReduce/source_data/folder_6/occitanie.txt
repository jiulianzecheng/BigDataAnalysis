 '''Occitanie''' ({{lang-oc|Occitània}}, {{lang-ca|Occitània}}) is an administrative [[regions of France|region of France]]. It was created on 1 January 2016 from the former French regions [[Languedoc-Roussillon]] and [[Midi-Pyrénées]].  
 
 The name in [[French language|French]], Occitanie, was approved as the new name of the region on 28 September 2016. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/affichTexte.do?cidTexte=JORFTEXT000033161437 & dateTexte= & categorieLien=id |title= Décret n° 2016-1264 du 28 septembre 2016 portant fixation du nom et du chef-lieu de le région Occitanie |publisher=Légifrance.gouv.fr |language=fr |access-date=11 October 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 The new Occitanie Region is the main part of a wider cultural entity known as '''Occitania'''. This wider region once included part of [[Spain]] (Aran Valley), and [[Monaco]], and parts of [[Italy]] (the Occitan Valleys and Guardia Piemontese). This wider area used to speak [[Occitan language|Occitan]] as their first language. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The Occitanie region is the third largest region of France, after [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] and [[French Guiana]], and the second of [[Metropolitan France]] with an area of {{Convert|72723.5|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. It is in southern France (the ''Midi'') and borders to the south with [[Spain]] and [[Andorra]]; it also borders three French regions: [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] to the west, [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] to the north, [[Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur]] to the east. The [[Mediterranean Sea]] is to the southeast. 
 
 Its [[Capital city|capital]], [[Toulouse]], is at {{Convert|590|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the southwest of  [[Paris]], the national capital, at {{Convert|405|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the west of [[Marseille]], at {{Convert|245|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the southeast of [[Bordeaux]] and at {{Convert|395|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the north of [[Barcelona]] (Spain).
