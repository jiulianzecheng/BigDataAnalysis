'''Crocin''' is a [[chemical compound]] that produces a type of natural [[pigment]]. It is found in the flowers of [[crocus]] and [[gardenia]] plants. It has a deep red colour. Crocin is what is mainly responsible for the colour of [[saffron]]. It is also called '''saffronin''' or '''polychroite'''. < ref > {{cite book|title=Introduction to Food Chemistry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6hvsj1dbAj8C & pg=PA154|page=154|author=Richard Owusu-Apenten|publisher=CRC Press|year=2004|isbn=9780849317248}} < /ref > 
 
 Crocin was used as a [[dye]] for [[textile]]s. Because it is unstable it is no longer used for that purpose.. < ref > ''Bulletin of Pharmacy'', Volume 14 (Detroit: William M. Warren Medical publishing, 1900), p. 115 < /ref >
