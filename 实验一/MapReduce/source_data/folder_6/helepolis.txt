 '''Helepolis''' ({{lang-el|ἑλέπολις}}, [[English language|English]]:  " Taker of Cities " ) was an [[Ancient Greece|ancient]] [[siege engine]]. It was used in the unsuccessful [[siege of Rhodes (305 & nbsp;BC)|siege of Rhodes]] in 305 [[Anno Domini|BC]]. It is based on an earlier, smaller design used against [[Battle of Salamis (306 BC)|Salamis]] in 306 BC. The name was taken from the [[Ancient Greek language|Greek]] '' " ελείν πόλεις " '', meaning destroyer of ''cities''. < ref > Diodorus Siculus, Book 20. 48 
 [http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/Diodorus_Siculus/20C*.html#48 online] < /ref >   < !-- Dioeclides of Abdera ??  -- >  [[Vitruvius]], [[Plutarch]], and in the Athenaeus Mechanicus. It was the largest and most powerful [[siege tower]] ever built. < ref > Paul Bentley Kern, ''Ancient Siege Warfare'' (Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 1999), p. 243 < /ref >
