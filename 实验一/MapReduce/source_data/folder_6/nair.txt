 '''Nair''', also known as '''Nayar''', is a [[Hindu]] [[caste]] of the Indian state of [[Kerala]] < ref name= " britannica " > {{cite encyclopedia|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/407129/Nayar|title=Nāyar|encyclopedia=Encyclopædia Britannica|date=2011|accessdate=2011-11-15}} < /ref >  on the Malabar Coast.  
 
 ==History== 
 
 According to some theories, the Nair have Indo-Scythian ancestry. < ref > Tyagi, Vidya Prakash. (2009). [https://books.google.com/books?id=vRwS6FmS2g0C & dq=nair+scythian & pg=PA282  ''Martial Races of Undivided India,'' p. 282]; India, Office of the Registrar General. (1965). [https://books.google.com/books?id=k9ccAQAAMAAJ & q=nair+scythian  ''Census of India, 1961,'' Vol. 7, p. 16]. < /ref > < ref name= " britannica " / >  During British rule, Nair were known as Nayarkutti and were important in [[politics]], [[government]], medicine, [[education]], and [[law]]. < ref name= " britannica " / >
