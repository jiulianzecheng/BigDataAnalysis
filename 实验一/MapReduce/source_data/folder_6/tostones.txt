 '''Tostones''' are a dish of sliced and fried [[Plantain|plantains]].  == Preparation == 
 Plantains that are green and not ripe are used. It is sliced into small flat pieces and then it is [[Frying|fried]] two times. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2013-12-13|title=Garlic Tostones: Puerto Rican Fried Plantains with Rice and Beans|url=https://pinchofyum.com/garlic-tostones-puerto-rican-fried-plantains|access-date=2021-08-03|website=Pinch of Yum|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 == Origin == 
 It is unknown where tostones came from. Many Caribbean and Latin American countries say they made tostones. The recipe is known under different names. They are called ''patacones'' in [[Panama]], [[Colombia]], [[Ecuador]], [[Costa Rica]] and [[Peru]], < ref name= " :0 "  / >  and they are called ''tostones'' in [[Cuba]], [[Nicaragua]], [[Puerto Rico]], and [[Dominican Republic]]. In the Dominican Republic, they also go by the name ''platano frito'' or ''fritos verdes'', and in [[Haiti]] they are also called ''bannann peze''.
