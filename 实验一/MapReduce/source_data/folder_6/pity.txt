{{Emotion}} 
 '''Pity''' means [[Emotion|feeling]] for others, particularly feelings of [[sadness]] or [[Sorrow (emotion)|sorrow]]. In a positive sense it means  " [[sympathy]] "  and  " [[empathy]] " . More commonly Pity is a negative [[judgement]] of others and their situation. < ref name=ZeevPT > {{cite web |url=https://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/in-the-name-love/201008/do-not-pity-me|title=Do Not Pity Me|author=Aaron Ben-Zeév Ph.D.14 August 2010|website= |publisher=Psychology Today|accessdate=17 September 2015}} < /ref >  An [[example]] of pity is how most people feel about the [[homeless]]. < ref name=ZeevPT/ >  Pity and [[compassion]] are closely related words but have subtle differences. The difference is that compassion usually involves some [[wikt:commitment|commitment]] to help. Pity does not usually require any personal [[wikt:involve|involve]]ment. < ref > David Konstan, ''Pity Transformed'' (London: Duckworth, 2001), pp. 21–22 < /ref >  Sometimes pity can also have feelings of contempt and dislike that go with it. < ref name=DB > {{cite web |url=http://www.differencebetween.net/science/nature/difference-between-pity-and-compassion/|title=Difference Between Pity and Compassion|author= |website= |publisher= Difference Between|accessdate=17 September 2015}} < /ref >  In a positive sense, and especially as practiced in many [[religion]]s, it can involve feelings the person does not deserve what has happened to him or her. < ref name=DB/ >  In this case the person feeling pity feels sorry it happened.
