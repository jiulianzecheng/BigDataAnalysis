'''Primogeniture''' is the [[system]] of inheritance by the [[wikt:firstborn|firstborn]], usually the eldest son. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.dictionary.com/browse/primogeniture |title=primogeniture |publisher=Dictionary.com |access-date=January 20, 2017}} < /ref >  In [[feudal]] [[England]] and other legal systems, the [[Legitimacy|legitimate]] first-born son gets the first right to [[inherit]] property. < ref name=LII > {{cite web |url=https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/primogeniture |title=primogeniture |website=Legal Information Institute |publisher=Cornell University Law School |access-date=January 20, 2017}} < /ref >  His claim is stronger than all daughters, younger sons and even elder [[illegitimate]] sons. The rule is that the eldest will always have the first claim. If there is no son, each of the daughters inherit an equal share of the [[estate (law)|estate]]. < ref name=LII/ >  If there are no children, the property is often inherited by the eldest brother. Among siblings, sons inherit before daughters and so on. Primogeniture is [[Latin]] for  " first born " .
