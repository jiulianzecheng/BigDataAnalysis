The '''Charyapada''' is a collection of 8th-12th century [[Vajrayana]] Buddhist ''[[caryagiti]]''.  They are mystical poems from the [[tantra|tantric]] tradition in eastern [[India]]. The Charyapada were meant to be sung.  
 
 A manuscript of this anthology was found in the early 20th century. It shows early examples of the [[Assamese language|Assamese]], [[Oriya language|Oriya]], [[Maithili language|Maithili]] and [[Bengali language|Bengali]] languages. The writers of the Charyapada belonged to the various regions of [[Assam]], [[Bengal]], [[Orissa]] and [[Bihar]].
