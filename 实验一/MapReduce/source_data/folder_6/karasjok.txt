 {{audio-nohelp|Karasjok.ogg|'''Karasjok'''}} ({{lang-se|Kárášjohka}} {{IPA-se|ˈkʰaːraːʃˌjohka|}}; < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.kartverket.no/kart/stedsnavn/stadnamn-og-rettskriving/|title=Stadnamn og rettskriving|language=no|access-date=2018-07-13|publisher=[[Kartverket]]|archive-date=2018-07-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180701054759/https://kartverket.no/kart/stedsnavn/stadnamn-og-rettskriving/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  {{lang-fkv|Kaarasjoki}}) is a [[List of municipalities of Norway|municipality]] in the [[Finnmark]] area of [[Troms og Finnmark]] [[Counties of Norway|county]], [[Norway]].  
 
 The municipality (of which the urban area is a part,) has Sami (or Sámi) and Norwegian as official languages. 
 
 The municipality is one of two in Norway where the majority of inhabitants speak Sami and have it as their main language. < ref name=ÁVJOVÁRRIurfolksregion > [http://norut.no/sites/default/files/norut_rapport_9-2016.pdf ÁVJOVÁRRI URFOLKSREGION I ET SAMISK PERSPEKTIV]{{Dead link|date=August 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}. page 19 < /ref >

 ===World War II=== 
 During World War II a [[Nazi]] [[concentration camp]] was built in Karasjok: ''[[Lager IV Karasjok]]'' (German for  " Karasjok Camp No.4 " , {{lang-no|Karasjok fangeleir}}). < ref name=StyrvoldMasterThesisp14 > {{citation |url=https://www.duo.uio.no/bitstream/handle/10852/23776/Styrvold.pdf |language=Norwegian|page=14 |title=Heil og Sæl. ''Krieg und Verbrechen: Situation und Intention: Fallbeispiele''. 2006. p. 114 < /ref >  The camp was run by [[SS]], and it was among < ref name=StyrvoldMasterThesisp14qInitialFour > {{citation |url=https://www.duo.uio.no/bitstream/handle/10852/23776/Styrvold.pdf |language=Norwegian|page=14 |title=Heil og Sæl. Jeg er utdannet morder! "  - Den norske SS Vaktbataljon 1942 – 45 |last=Sigurd Bakke Styrvold |work=MA in history - University of Oslo |quote= Det fantes på forskjellige tidspunkter flere serberleire i Norge, men de norske vaktene gjorde bare tjeneste i de fire første; Lager I Beisfjord, Lager II Elsfjord, Lager III Rognan (også kjent som Botn) og Lager IV Karasjok}} < /ref >  the first four [[Nazi concentration camp]]s in [[Northern Norway]]. 
 
 In July 1943, 374 [[political prisoner]]s and [[POW]]s prisoners [mostly Yugoslav,] were brought to the concentration camp. They were tasked with widening the road to [[Karigasniemi]], [[Finland]]. < ref name=Prisoncamp > [http://www.nrk.no/nordnytt/blodveien-i-karasjok-1.12028795 Disse fangene bygget «blodveien» – og så ble de henrettet - De jugoslaviske fangene som var i Karasjok under andre verdenskrig skulle bare bygge veien til Finland, og deretter skulle de ikke eksistere mer.] 
 [These prisoners built 'the blood road' - and then they were executed - The Yugoslavian prisoners that were in Karasjok during World War Two, were only to build a road to Finland, and thereafter they were not to exist anymore] < /ref >  After four or five months, only 111 of these prisoners were still alive. < ref name=Prisoncamp/ >  At the end of the prisoners' stay in Karasjok, before transportation out of Karasjok, 45 prisoners were massacred by the firing of small arms. < ref name=Prisoncamp/ >  At least one former prisoner is (as of 2013) still alive. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nrk.no/sapmi/folelsesladet-mote-med-karasjok-1.11041097|title=Følelsesladet møte med Karasjok|first=Dan Robert|last=Larsen|date=24 May 2013|website=NRK}} < /ref > 
 
 ===Later=== 
 In 2015 the second edition of [[Sapmi Pride]], the LGBT pride festival, was held in Karasjok. < ref name= " nrk.no " > {{cite web|url=https://www.nrk.no/sapmi/flytter-sapmi-pride-til-kautokeino-1.12979294|title=Flytter Sápmi Pride til Kautokeino|first=Dan Robert|last=Larsen|date=2 June 2016|website=NRK}} < /ref >  To protest homosexuals attending the [[Karasjok Church]], and to protest that a female priest held the services, [[Norges Samemisjon]] cancelled one of their radio broadcasts.

 The Sami [[secondary school]], is one of two in Norway (as of 2014); < ref name=FactsAbout > {{cite web|url=https://www.regjeringen.no/no/tema/urfolk-og-minoriteter/samepolitikk/samiske-sprak/fakta-om-samiske-sprak/id633131/|title=Fakta om samiske språk|first=administrasjons-og kirkedepartementet|last=Fornyings-|date=20 August 2018|website=Regjeringen.no}} < /ref >  73% of the schools students say that Sami is their main language; 27% say that Sami is their second language (as of the school year that started in [August]  2009 and ended in [June] 2010); < ref name=IndigenousPeoplesInNumbers > {{cite web|url=https://www.ssb.no/befolkning/artikler-og-publikasjoner/urbefolkningen-i-tall-samisk-statistikk|title=Urbefolkningen i tall - samisk statistikk|website=ssb.no}} < /ref >  That school is run by the government.
