 '''''Nectophrynoides''''' is a [[genus]] of [[Bufonidae|true toads]]. They live in [[Eastern Arc]] forests and wetlands in [[Tanzania]]. < ref name=Channing > Channing and Howell. (2006). ''Amphibians of East Africa.'' Pp. 104-117. {{ISBN|3-930612-53-4}} < /ref >  All but ''[[Nectophrynoides tornieri|N. tornieri]]'' are [[Threatened species|threatened]]. Species of the genus are [[Ovoviviparity|ovoviviparous]]: fertilization is internal, and the females give birth to fully developed, small toadlets. < ref name=Channing/ >  Together with the West Africa ''[[Nimbaphrynoides]]'', they are the only frogs/toads in the world that do not lay eggs. < ref name=Wells > Wells (2007). ''The Ecology and Behavior of Amphibians.'' Pp. 486-487. {{ISBN|978-0-226-89334-1}} < /ref >
