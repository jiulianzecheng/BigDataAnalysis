 '''Mentorship''' is when a person who is more experienced in a subject [[Tutor|tutors]] another person to help them learn more about the subject. < ref name= " MasteryWorks " > {{cite web|last1=Farren Ph.D.|first1=Caela|title=Eight Types of Mentor: Which Ones Do You Need?|url=http://www.masteryworks.com/newsite/downloads/Article3_EightTypesofMentors-WhichOnesdoyouNeed.pdf|publisher=MasteryWorks|access-date=2018-02-17|archive-date=2015-07-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150701201614/http://www.masteryworks.com/newsite/downloads/Article3_EightTypesofMentors-WhichOnesdoyouNeed.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It is the process of guiding and influencing a personal and professional growth in an [[organization]].  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Why Mentors Matter: A Summary of 30 Years of Research|url=https://www.sap.com/insights/hr/why-mentors-matter.html|access-date=2021-05-08|website=SAP|language=English|archive-date=2021-02-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210215073132/https://www.sap.com/insights/hr/why-mentors-matter.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Mentorship requires the mentor to have more experience in the subject they're tutoring in than the person teaching, also called a mentee. Mentorship is complete when the mentee has the same level of knowledge about the subject as the mentor.
