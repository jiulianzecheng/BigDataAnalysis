 The '''Azores''' ({{lang-pt|Açores}}) is an [[archipelago]] of nine [[Volcano|volcanic]] [[island]]s in the North [[Atlantic Ocean]]; they are about {{convert|1500|km|mi|abbr=on}} west of [[Lisbon]] and about {{convert|1900|km|mi|abbr=on}} southeast of [[Newfoundland (island)|Newfoundland]]. The islands, and their [[Exclusive Economic Zone]], form the '''Autonomous Region of the Azores''', one of the two [[Autonomous area|autonomous regions]] of Portugal. 
 
 There are nine major Azores islands and several very small islands ([[islet]]s), in three main groups. They extend for more than {{convert|600|km|mi|abbr=on}} and lie in a northwest-southeast direction. 
 
 The archipelago is part of the [[Macaronesia]] islands. 
 
 ==History== 
 
 Officially, the islands were discovered in the 15th century (in 1431) by [[Gonçalo Velho|Gonçalo Velho Cabral]] a Captain in the service of [[Henry the Navigator|Infante Dom Henrique]], although credit is also given to the explorer [[Diogo de Silves]] (in 1427). 
 
 The archipelago was settled over the centuries, mostly from mainland Portugal. Portuguese settlers came from the provinces of [[Algarve]], [[Minho Province|Minho]], [[Alentejo Province|Alentejo]] and [[Ribatejo Province|Ribatejo]], as well as [[Madeira]]. 
 
 In 1976, the Azores became the Autonomous Region of the Azores ({{lang-pt|Região Autónoma dos Açores}}), an [[Autonomous area|autonomous region]] of Portugal. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:Azores-map.png|400px|thumbnail|left|Map of the Azores Islands.]] 
 The archipelago is in the [[Atlantic Ocean]] between 36º and 43º latitude North and between 25º and 31º [[longitude]] West. They have a very [[Humidity|humid]] [[ocean]]ic [[climate]] with relatively small annual variations. 
 
 The nine islands are in three natural geographic groups, each group separated by more than {{convert|160|km|mi|abbr=on}} of water. These groups are: 
 * The [[east]]ern group, with the islands of [[São Miguel Island|São Miguel]] and [[Santa Maria (Azores)|Santa Maria]]; 
 * The central group, with the islands of [[Faial Island|Faial]], [[Terceira]], [[Graciosa (island)|Graciosa]], [[São Jorge (island)|São Jorge]], and [[Pico (island)|Pico]]; 
 * The [[west]]ern group, with the islands of [[Flores (Azores)|Flores]] and [[Corvo (Azores)|Corvo]]. 
 
 The eastern group also has, to the northeast of Santa Maria, a group of very small islands ([[islet]]s) and [[reef]]s that are named ''[[Ilhéus das Formigas]]'' ([[Islet]]s of the [[Ant]]s), or just ''Formigas'' ( " [[Ant]]s " ); these smalls islands, together with the ''Dollabarat'' reef, form the '''Reserva Natural do Ilhéu das Formigas'''. 
 
 The nine islands have a total area of {{convert|2333|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}}. Their individual areas vary between {{convert|759|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}} of the largest island (São Miguel) to {{convert|17|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}} of the smallest (Corvo).

 All the islands have [[Volcano|volcanic]] origins, although some, such as Santa Maria, have had no recorded activity since the islands were settled. The last volcano to [[Volcanic eruption|erupt]] in the archipelago was the Capelinhos Volcano ({{lang-pt|Vulcão dos Capelinhos}}) in 1957, in the western part of the island of Faial. < ref name= " netbiome " > {{cite web |url=http://netbiome.org/index.php?option=com_content & view=article & id=65%3Aazores & catid=51%3Amonographies & showall=1 |title=Azores |publisher=netbiome |year=2013 |access-date=April 24, 2013 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  [[Earthquake]]s are common on most of the islands. 
 
 The Azores are on place where three of the world's large [[Plate tectonics|tectonic plates]] (the [[North American Plate]], the [[Eurasian Plate]] and the [[African Plate]]) meet. < ref > {{cite journal|url=http://e-terra.geopor.pt/artigos/cong_geoq/machado.pdf|last1=Machado|first1=Adriane|last2=Azevedo|first2=José M. M.|last3=Alemeida|last4=Delia P.M.|last5=Chemale Jr.|first5=Farid|title=Geochemistry of Volcanic Rocks from Faial Island (Azores)|journal=E-Terra : Revista Electrónica de Ciências da Terra|publisher=e-Terra, GEOTIC – Sociedade Geológica de Portugal|location=Lisbon|volume=5,1|year=2008|access-date=April 24, 2013|issn=1645-0388|archive-date=May 11, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110511135248/http://e-terra.geopor.pt/artigos/cong_geoq/machado.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The westernmost islands of the archipelago, (Corvo and Flores), are in the North American Plate, while the remaining islands are located within the region where the Eurasian plate is separated from the African Plates. < ref name= " morton " > {{cite book|last1=Morton |first1=B. |last2=Britton |first2=J.C. |last3=Frias Martins |first3=A.M. |title=Ecologia Costeira dos Açores |language=pt |year=1998 |publisher=Sociedade Afonso Chaves |location=São Miguel, Açores, Portugal |isbn=972-97774-1-1}} < /ref > 
 
 The volcano [[Mount Pico]], on the Pico island, is the highest point in Portugal, at {{convert|2351|m|ft|0|abbr=on}}. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 Daily maximum temperatures usually range between {{convert|15|°C|0|abbr=on}} and {{convert|25|°C|0|abbr=on}}. The average annual rainfall increases from east to west, and it ranges from 700 to 1600 annual millimetres (27.6–63 & nbsp;in) on average, reaching {{convert|6300|mm|in|-1}} on [[Mount Pico]]. < ref name= " Climate of the Azores islands " > {{cite web |url=http://www.azoresweather.com |title=Climate of the Azores islands |publisher=Azores Weather |access-date=May 5, 2009 |archive-date=May 14, 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090514035733/http://www.azoresweather.com/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 The sea around the Azores is warmed by the [[Gulf Stream]] and sea water temperature varies from {{convert|14|°C|0|abbr=on}} to {{convert|22|°C|0|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " netbiome "  / >

 ===Hurricanes=== 
 A total of 11 [[Tropical cyclone|tropical]] or [[subtropical cyclone]]s have affected the region in history. Most of them were either [[Extratropical cyclone|extratropical]] or [[Tropical cyclone|tropical storms]] when they impacted the region, although several [[hurricane]]s of Category 1 have reached the Azores. 
 
 ==Administration== 
 When the Azores became an Autonomous Region of Portugal, the cities of [[Ponta Delgada (Azores)|Ponta Delgada]], [[Angra (Azores)|Angra]] and [[Horta (Azores)|Horta]] were considered [[Capital (city)|capital/administrative cities]] to the regional government: homes to the President (Ponta Delgada), the Judiciary (Angra) and the Regional Assembly (Horta).

 ==Population== 
 According to the 2011 Census, the total population in the Azores was 246,746: 121,533 men and 125,213 women. The density for the whole Archipelago is 106 persons/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . < ref name= " ine " > {{cite web |url=http://www.ine.pt/xportal/xmain?xpid=INE & xpgid=ine_indicadores & indOcorrCod=0005889 & contexto=bd & selTab=tab2 |title=Census 2011 |publisher=Statistics Portugal |date=November 20, 2012 |access-date=April 24, 2013 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Economy== 
 The Azores economy is based mainly on [[agriculture]], [[Fishery|fisheries]] and [[tourism]]. < ref name= " netbiome "  / > 
 
 In the agriculture sector, [[cattle]]-raising is very important. Some of the most important [[crop]]s in the Azores are [[pineapple]]s, [[grape]]s, [[potato]]es, [[tobacco]] and [[tea]]. Fisheries are an important economic activity on all islands. Fishing methods are highly traditional among Azorean fishermen. [[Industry]] in the Azores is based mainly on the production of [[dairy]] products ([[milk]], [[cheese]]) and transformation of fisheries products, such as [[tuna]]. < ref name= " netbiome "  / > 
 
 It is tourism, however, that has been growing more in recent years. It is far less developed than those industries of its [[Macaronesia]]n neighbours, [[Madeira]] and [[Canary Islands]]. < ref name= " netbiome "  / >
