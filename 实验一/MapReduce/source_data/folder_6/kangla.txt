The '''Palace of Kangla''' ({{lang-omp|ꯀꯪꯂꯥ|kang-laa|dry land}}) was the earliest known [[fort|fortified]] [[capital city]] of [[Ancient Manipur]]. < ref > {{cite web | url=http://www.e-pao.net/epSubPageExtractor.asp?src=manipur.Kangla.Kangla_The_ancient_Capital_of_Manipur | title=Kangla the ancient Capital of Manipur }} < /ref >  It served as the seat of administration of the rulers of many dynasties, right from the reign of Emperor [[Tangja Leela Pakhangba]] in 1445 BC (15th century BC), < ref > {{cite web | url=https://themanipurpage.tripod.com/history/meiteikings.html | title=Meitei Kings }} < /ref >  though significant advanced developments belonged to the post periods of [[Nongda Lairen Pakhangba]] (33 AD - 153 AD). < ref > {{cite web | url=http://books.e-pao.net/Heritage_Manipur/epShowChapter.asp?src=kangla/origin | title=E-Pao! Books :: Complete e-platform for Manipuris }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web | url=http://books.e-pao.net/Heritage_Manipur/epShowChapter.asp?src=kangla/extirpation | title=E-Pao! Books :: Complete e-platform for Manipuris }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web | url=http://books.e-pao.net/Heritage_Manipur/epShowChapter.asp?src=kangla/Pakhangba | title=E-Pao! Books :: Complete e-platform for Manipuris }} < /ref >
