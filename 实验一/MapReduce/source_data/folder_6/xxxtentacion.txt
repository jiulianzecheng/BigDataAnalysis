 He released his first album, ''[[17 (XXXTentacion album)|17]]'', on August 25, 2017. Onfroy's second album, [[? (XXXTentacion album)|''?'']], was released on March 16, 2018. It debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200. Its singles  " [[Sad!]] " ,  " [[Changes (XXXTentacion song)|Changes]] "  and  " [[Moonlight (song)|Moonlight]] "  all reached the top 20 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].

 ==Career== 
 He made [[Members Only (hip hop collective)|Members Only]] when he was 16 after leaving [[Ski Mask the Slump God]]'s group Very Rare. Ski later joined Members Only as well. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/04/the-break-presents-ski-mask-slump-god/|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=The Break Presents: Ski Mask The Slump God|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2013, he recorded the song  " [[Vice City|Vice City]] "  which was the first song he released on [[SoundCloud]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2022/music/news/xxxtentacion-soundcloud-reissues-documentary-1235166483/|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=XXXTentacion’s Manager and Producer Talk SoundCloud Reissues and the Long-Delayed Documentary|publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Personal life== 
 He was diagnosed with [[bipolar disorder]] when he was 13 and was re-diagnosed later on in his life. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.gq.com/story/xxxtentacion-look-at-me-documentary-hulu-cleopatra-bernard|accessdate=August 1, 2023|title=“Tensions Ran High But We Wanted to Tell the Whole Story”: Inside the Complicated XXXTentacion Documentary|publisher=[[GQ]]}} < /ref >  One of his best friends was fellow rapper [[Ski Mask the Slump God]]. He met Ski while in [[Youth detention center|juvenile detention]] for gun possession. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/videos/id.26195/title.ski-mask-the-slump-god-explains-how-he-met-xxxtentacion|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Talks Meeting XXXTentacion|publisher=[[HipHopDX]]}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2016, he was charged with [[domestic violence]] against his ex-girlfriend Geneva Ayala. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.42893/title.xxxtentacion-freed-from-jail|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=XXXTentacion Freed From Jail|publisher=HipHopDX}} < /ref >  Ayala later wanted the charges to be dropped. She did not want to testify in court and did not want Onfroy to be sent to prison. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8054950/xxxtentacions-ex-drop-domestic-abuse-case-prosecution|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=XXXTentacion's Ex Allegedly Wants to Drop Domestic Abuse Case, But Prosecutors Plan to Continue|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/xxxtentacions-family-says-they-believe-his-abuse-victim-geneva-ayala-in-new-hulu-documentary|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=XXXTentacion's Family: We Believe His Abuse Victim|publisher=[[The Daily Beast]]}} < /ref >  He pled not guilty and was put on [[house arrest]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.46286/title.judge-lets-xxxtentacion-off-house-arrest-to-support-album|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=Judge Lets XXXTentacion Off House Arrest To Support  " ? "  Album|publisher=HipHopDX}} < /ref >  The charges were [[Nolle prosequi|voluntarily dropped]] after he died. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/a/victoria-johnson/xxxtentacion-domestic-abuse-charges-dropped-after-his-death|accessdate=August 7, 2023|title=XXXTentacion's Domestic Abuse Charges Officially Dropped After His Death|publisher=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]}} < /ref > 
 
 Seven months after his death, his son, Gekyume Onfroy was born with Jenesis Sanchez on January 26, 2019. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxxtentacion-son-gekyume-is-born/|accessdate=August 1, 2023|title=XXXTentacion’s Son Gekyume Onfroy is Born|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}} < /ref >

 ==Robbery and murder==

 On June 18, 2018, Onfroy went to a bank to withdraw some money before heading to a [[motor sports]] dealership at Riva Motorsports. As he was leaving the store, a black [[SUV]] blocked his car from leaving the parking lot. Two men stepped out of the SUV and started fighting Onfroy. The men stole a [[Louis Vuitton]] bag from Onfroy's car. Then they shot him a few times in the neck. He was dead on arrival at hospital. 
 
 Michael Boatwright, Dedrick Williams, Robert Allen and Trayvon Newsome were arrested and charged with first-degree murder in Onfroy's killing. On August 12, 2022, Allen pled guilty of second-degree murder in exchange for testimony against the other three. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/local/plea-deal-struck-in-2018-murder-of-rapper-xxxtentacion-in-pompano-beach/2834456/|accessdate=June 2, 2023|title=Plea Deal Struck in 2018 Murder of Rapper XXXTentacion in Pompano Beach|publisher=NBC Miami}} < /ref >  Allen was sentenced to 7 years in prison with credit for time served, as well as 20 years probation when he is released. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/local/suspect-who-took-plea-deal-in-2018-murder-of-rapper-xxxtentacion-sentenced/3036408/|accessdate=June 2, 2023|title=Suspect Who Took Plea Deal in 2018 Murder of Rapper XXXTentacion Sentenced|publisher=NBC Miami}} < /ref >  The other three were found guilty on all counts. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://ktla.com/news/nexstar-media-wire/nationworld/headlines/jury-convicts-3-of-murder-in-death-of-rapper-xxxtentacion/|accessdate=June 2, 2023|title=Jury convicts 3 of murder in death of rapper XXXTentacion|publisher=[[KTLA]]}} < /ref >  Newsome and Williams were sentenced to life in prison without parole. Boatwright was sentenced to two life sentences without parole, one for first-degree murder and one for armed robbery, and was sentenced to another 30 years for other charges. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/xxxtentacion-murder-trial-florida-sentencing-75dd907a5d1b274347661bb805bc8d08|accessdate=June 2, 2023|title=XXXTentacion’s convicted killers sentenced to life in prison|publisher=[[Associated Press|AP News]]}} < /ref >
