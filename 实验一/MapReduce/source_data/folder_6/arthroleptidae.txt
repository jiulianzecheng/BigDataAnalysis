 The '''Arthroleptidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[frog]]s found in [[sub-Saharan Africa]]. They are known as '''squeakers''' because of their high-pitched call. They are small, less than {{convert|4|cm|in}} in length.  They live on land mostly in leaf litter on the forest floor. They do not have [[tadpole]]s. They lay their eggs on the ground.  
 
 Some species hatch already completely [[metamorphosis|metamorphosed]] into the adult form, others still have [[tails]] when they hatch. < ref name=EoR > {{cite book |editor=Cogger H.G.  &  Zweifel R.G.|author= Zweifel, Robert G.|year=1998|title=Encyclopedia of Reptiles and Amphibians|url=https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0|publisher= Academic Press|location=San Diego|isbn= 0-12-178560-2|page= [https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0/page/101 101]}} < /ref > 
 
 == Taxonomy == 
 Arthroleptidae is separated into two sub-families: Arthroleptinae and Leptopelinae. Some think these are separate families.
