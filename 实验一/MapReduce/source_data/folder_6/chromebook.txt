 A '''Chromebook''' is a [[laptop]] that uses the [[Linux]]-based [[Chrome OS]] as its [[operating system]].  
 
 It uses the [[internet]]. Most applications and data are in the  " [[Cloud computing|cloud]] " . A Chromebook is an example of a [[thin client]]. It is a lightweight computer which uses remote access to a [[server]].  In March 2018, [[Acer Inc.|Acer]] and [[Google]] announced the creation of the first Chromebook tablet: the Chromebook Tab 10. This device was to compete with the market for discount Apple [[iPad]] tablets in the education market. The Tab 10 screen (9.7 inches, 2048 x 1536 resolution) was identical to that of the iPad. The device includes a [[Pen|stylus]]. Neither device included a [[Keyboard (computer)|keyboard]]. 
 
 In March 2020, following the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], many schools used Chromebooks to do school online, and put apps onto it like [[Google Meet]] and [[Zoom (software)|Zoom]]. 30 million Chromebooks were bought by schools in 2020 alone because of it. Some schools used [[IPad|iPads]], but they cost too much money for other schools, so they use Chromebooks.{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}}
