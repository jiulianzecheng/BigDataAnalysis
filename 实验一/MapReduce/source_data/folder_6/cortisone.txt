 '''Cortisone''' is a 21-carbon [[steroid]] [[hormone]]. Its chemical name is 17-hydroxy-11-dehydrocorticosterone. It is one of the main hormones released by the [[adrenal gland]] in response to [[stress (biology)|stress]].  
 
 In chemical structure, it is closely related to [[cortisol]]. It is used to treat a variety of ailments and can be administered in various ways. Cortisone suppresses the [[immune system]]. This reduces inflammation, pain and swelling at the site of the injury. Long-term use of cortisone has risks. < ref name=mayo > {{cite web|url=http://www.mayoclinic.com/health/cortisone-shots/MY00268 |title=Cortisone shots |publisher=MayoClinic.com |date=2010-11-16 |access-date=July 31, 2013}} < /ref > < ref name=mayorisk2 > {{cite web|url=http://www.mayoclinic.com/health/steroids/HQ01431 |title=Prednisone and other corticosteroids: Balance the risks and benefits |publisher=MayoClinic.com |date=2010-06-05 |access-date=2011-09-03}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Tadeus Reichstein]], [[E.C. Kendall]] and [[P.S. Hench]] were awarded the [[Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine]] in 1950 for their discovery of cortisone.
