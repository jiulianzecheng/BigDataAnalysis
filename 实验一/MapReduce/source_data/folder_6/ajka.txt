 '''Ajka''' is a [[city]] in [[Hungary]], in [[Veszprém (county)|Veszprém county]]. Its population is 31,407. 
 
 == Location == 
 Ajka is located south of the [[Road 8 (Hungary)|Road 8]], in the [[Transdanubian Mountains]]. 
 
 === Connections === 
 The [[Road 8 (Hungary)|Road 8]] bypass Ajka in North what is connect Ajka with [[Devecser]] and [[Városlőd]]. 
 
 A minor road is also going through Ajka, what connects Ajka with Road 8 and with [[Road 77 (Hungary)|Road 77]]. 
 
 == History == 
 The city was already populated at the [[Stone Age]]. Around [[1000 BC|1000 B.C.]], [[Celts]] settled down here, before the [[Roman Empire|Romans]] invaded the territory. After that, the [[Magyars]] came here. 
 
 The town was borned because the 8 villages what was there submerged together. 
 
 The village didn't grow very well until the [[19th century]], when [[coal]] was found southeast of the territory. The [[Catholic Church|Catholic church]] was build beetween [[1933]] and [[1934]]. The oldest [[krypton]]-factory in the world were settled here, the building is still there. After that, [[bauxite]] was found too. Because of the ores were found, Ajka turned into one of [[Hungary]]'s most important industrial city. In the [[1970s]], the city center got remaked, and several buildings, such as [[Hotel|hotels]] and [[Mall|shopping centres]] got placed there. The city got a [[Joseph Hild reward]] in [[1987]]. The [[End of socialism in Hungary]] hurt the industries a little bit, but it doesn't effect the city very well. Even the [[Great Recession]] or the [[Ajka alumina plant accident]] didn't do anything serious with the city.
