 '''Tajadas''' are a dish of fried [[Plantain|plantains]] that are sliced long. < ref > [http://southamericanfood.about.com/od/saladssidedishes/r/Fried-Ripe-Plantain-Strips.htm Tajada] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170101161303/http://southamericanfood.about.com/od/saladssidedishes/r/Fried-Ripe-Plantain-Strips.htm |date=2017-01-01 }} About.com < /ref >  It is a food of [[Caribbean]] countries as well as [[Nicaragua]], [[Colombia]], [[Honduras]], [[Panama]], and [[Venezuela]]. 
 
 == Preparation == 
 The plantains used are usually [[Ripening|ripe]]. 
 
 == Variations == 
 
 === Colombia === 
 Tajadas is a very common dish that is usually served with [[meat]], [[white rice]], and [[Bean|beans]]. 
 
 === Costa Rica === 
 Tajadas are served with [[gallo pinto]] or [[casados]]. It can also be eaten with [[natilla]] and [[queso blanco]] on top. 
 
 === Venezuala === 
 [[File:Pabellon criollo.jpg|thumb|[[Pabellon criollo]] with [[Kidney bean|caraotas fritas]], tajadas, cheese, and [[rice]] with a fried [[Egg (food)|egg]] on top.]] 
 Tajadas is served in a traditional dish known as pabellon criollo.
