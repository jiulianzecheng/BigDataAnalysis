 '''Grenada''' ({{IPAc-en|audio=En-us-Grenada.ogg|ɡ|r|ə|ˈ|n|eɪ|d|ə}} {{Respell|grə|NAY|də}}; [[Grenadian Creole French]]: ''Gwenad'') is an island [[country]] in the [[Caribbean Sea]] that received its [[independence]] from the [[United Kingdom]] in 1974. Its capital is [[St. George's, Grenada|St. George's]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Before it was discovered, Grenada was inhabited by indigenous people. In [[1649]], it was colonised by [[France]]. Over a century later in [[1763]], Grenada was given to the [[United Kingdom]]. 
 
 In [[1974]], Grenada became an independent country led by [[List of heads of government of Grenada|Prime Minister]] [[Eric Gairy]]. In [[1983]], Grenada was invaded by the [[United States]] in an effort to stop [[communism]] on the island.  
 
 In [[2004]], a hurricane named [[Hurricane Ivan|Ivan]] destroyed most of the island's resources. Grenada is called the '''[[Spice]] Island''' on account of its most famous export, the [[nutmeg]]. 
 
 == Politics == 
 Grenada is a [[monarchy]] with [[Charles III]] as [[king]]. Grenada has a [[parliament]] made up of a [[senate]] (13 seats) and a [[House of Representatives]] (15 seats). The senators are appointed by the representatives, who are elected by the people.
