 '''Paignton''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|p|eɪ|n|t|ən}} {{respell|PAYN|tən}}) is a [[town]] in [[Devon]], [[England]]. It is on the [[coast]] of [[Tor Bay]].  Together with [[Torquay]] and [[Brixham]], it forms the [[unitary authority|borough]] of [[Torbay]]. The borough was created in 1968. The Torbay area is a [[holiday]] destination. It is called the English [[French Riviera|Riviera]]. Paignton began as a [[Celt]]ic [[Urban-type settlement|settlement]]. It was first mentioned in 1086. The town grew as a small [[Fishing industry|fishing village]]. A  [[Harbor|harbour]] was built in 1847. A [[Railway track|railway line]] was opened to passengers in 1859. This created  links to Torquay and [[London]]. As more people started to live there, it joined with the [[Village|villages]] of [[Goodrington]] and [[Preston, Devon|Preston]]. Paignton is about {{Convert|25|mi}} north east of [[Plymouth]] and {{Convert|20|mi}} south of [[Exeter]].
