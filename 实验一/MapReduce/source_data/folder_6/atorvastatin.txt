'''Atorvastatin''', sold by the brand name '''Lipitor''' is a [[statin]] drug used for preventing [[cardiovascular disease]] and treating [[dyslipidemia|abnormal lipid levels]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Atorvastatin|publisher=Drugs.com|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/atorvastatin.html|access-date=May 24, 2021}} < /ref >   
 
 Common [[side effect]]s are [[nausea]], [[diarrhea]], muscle pains and joint pain. Serious side effects may include [[type 2 diabetes]] and [[liver]] problems. 
 
 Atorvastatin was patented in 1986 by [[Pfizer]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Atorvastatin Monograph for Professionals|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/atorvastatin.html|access-date=2021-05-24|website=Drugs.com|language=en}} < /ref >  It was approved for medical use in the [[United States]] ten years later. < ref > {{cite book|title=Analogue Based Drug Discovery|isbn=9783527607495|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FjKfqkaKkAAC & pg=PA473|access-date=May 24, 2021|last1=Fischer|first1=JÂ¿nos|last2=Robin Ganellin|first2=C.|date=13 December 2006}} < /ref >  The medication is available as a [[generic drug]]. < ref > {{cite book|title=Top 100 Drugs|date=30 March 2014 |publisher=Elsevier Health Services|isbn=9780702055157 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oeYjAwAAQBAJ & dq=Atorvastatin+inexpensive & pg=PA197|access-date=May 24, 2021}} < /ref >
