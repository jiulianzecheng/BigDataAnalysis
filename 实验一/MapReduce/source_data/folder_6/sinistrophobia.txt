'''Sinistrophobia''' is a fear of objects to the left side of bodies or left-handed people. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://common-phobias.com/sinistro/phobia.htm|title=Sinistrophobia - left side of the body fear, left-handed fear, left side of the body phobia, left-handed phobia, fear of left-handed people, fear of things at the left side of the body, phobia of left-handed people, phobia of things at the left side of the body, Dextrophobia, Levophobia|website=common-phobias.com|access-date=2019-06-10|archive-date=2019-06-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190630024245/http://common-phobias.com/sinistro/phobia.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 The word sinistrophobia is derived from the Latin word sinistro (meaning left) and the Greek word phobia (meaning fear). 
 
 Person(s) diagnosed with sinistrophobia often suffer an irrational fear of any object located to the left of a body, < ref > {{cite journal|title=sinistrophobia|url=https://medical-dictionary.thefreedictionary.com/sinistrophobia|journal=The Free Dictionary}} < /ref >  objects or person(s) to their left and those who use their left hand, symptoms can include but are not limited to, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, dry-mouth, shaking, irregular heartbeat or any of the common symptoms associated with a [[panic attack]]. Those suffering from sinistrophobia may also refuse to use their left hand, touch another persons left hand or any objects located to their left.
