 '''Deira''' ([[Common Brittonic|Brittonic]]: ''Dubro'' or ''dyfr''{{efn|Meaning 'waters' referring to the abundance of water around the [[River Humber]] [[estuary]]. < ref name=Marsden27 > John Marsden, ''Northanhymbre Saga; the History of the Anglo-Saxon Kings of Northumbria'' (London: Kyle Cathie Limited, 1992), p. 27 < /ref > }}) was a kingdom in [[England|Northern England]]. The Anglian people living there were known as the ''Dere''. Deira occupied the area that was later called the [[East Riding of Yorkshire]]. Deira was one of two early Anglian kingdoms north of the [[River Humber]]; the other being [[Bernicia]], their northern neighbor. In the early [[7th century]] they combined to become the kingdom of [[Northumbria]].   
 
 
   
 == Roman and early Anglian Deira == 
 
 In [[Roman Britain]] Deira{{efn|The name of the kingdom was Deira; the collective name for the Anglian people of Diera was the Dere. < ref > Frank Stenton, ''Anglo-Saxon England'' (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1971), p. 74 < /ref > }} was separated from Bernicia. The two had a broad area between them where no one lived. The Romans had small defensive works in this area to protect the route to [[Hadrian's Wall]]. < ref name=Foster565 > Brian Foster, 'The Boundary between Deira and Bernicia', ''The Modern Language Review'', Vol. 51, No. 4 (Oct., 19560, p. 565 < /ref >  It was a kind of [[no man's land]] after the [[Angles]] arrived. < ref name=Foster565/ >  Deira first came under the control of the Angles in 560 under their leader [[Ella]]. < ref > Thomas Keightley, ''The history of England'', Volume 1, ed. Joshua Toulmin Smith (Boston: Hilliard, Gray, and Co., 1840), P. 10 < /ref >  He conquered the Britons in Deira in 581. Deira was still blocked from northward [[:wikt:expansion|expansion]] by Bernicia. To the west they were blocked by the last remaining British kingdom of any size, [[Elmet]]. < ref name=Snyder209 > Christopher A. Snyder, ''The Britons'' (Malden, MA: Blackwell Publishing, 2003), p. 209 < /ref >  King [[Athelfrith of Bernicia|Athelfrith]] of Bernicia united the kingdoms of Deira and Bernicia into Northumbria. < ref name=Yorke74 > Barbara Yorke, ''Kings and Kingdoms of Early Anglo-Saxon England'' (London; New York: Routledge, 1990), p. 74 < /ref >  In 617 Edwin of Deira defeated Elmet and expelled their king, Cerdic. < ref name=Snyder209/ >  Deira and Bernicia were separated again briefly after the death of King [[Oswald of Bernicia]]. < ref name=Foster565/ >  His brother [[Oswiu of Northumbria]] inherited the kingship of Bernicia but did not control Deira until 651. From that time until 867, Deira was treated as a [[:en:wikt:subkingdom|subkingdom]] of Northumbria. In 867 the [[Vikings]] took control of Deira. < ref > Mike Ashley, ''The Mammoth Book of British Kings and Queens'' (New york: Carroll  &  Graf, 1999), p. 272 < /ref >
