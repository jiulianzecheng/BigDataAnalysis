 '''Buchloe''' ({{IPA-de|buːxˈloːə}}) is a community raised to town status in 1954, being in [[Ostallgäu]] in [[Bavaria]]. Together with the neighbouring communities of Jengen, Lamerdingen and Waal, Buchloe belongs to the ''[[Amt (subnational entity)|Administrative Community]]'' of Buchloe. 
 
 ==Geography and transport== 
 Buchloe is right on ''Bundes[[autobahn]]'' 96 ([[Munich]]–Buchloe–[[Memmingen]]–[[Lindau]]) with interchanges with ''Bundesstraße'' ( " Federal Highway " ) 12 (Lindau by way of Munich and [[Passau]] to [[Philippsreut]]) and is known as the  " Gateway to the [[Allgäu]] " . [[Buchloe station]] is an important railway hub for traffic on the Munich–[[Kempten]]–Lindau route on the [[Munich–Buchloe railway|Munich–Buchloe]] and the [[Buchloe–Lindau railway|Buchloe–Lindau]] lines (KBS 970) and on the [[Augsburg–Buchloe railway|Augsburg–Buchloe]] and the [[Buchloe–Memmingen railway|Buchloe–Memmingen lines]] with their [[InterCity|IC]] services and direct services into [[Switzerland]] by [[EuroCity-Express]].
