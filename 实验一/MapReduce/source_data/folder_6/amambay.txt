 The '''Amambay department''' ({{lang-es|Departamento de Amambay}}) is a [[Departments of Paraguay|department]] of [[Paraguay]]. The [[Capital city|capital]] is the [[city]] of [[Pedro Juan Caballero, Paraguay|Pedro Juan Caballero]]. Its [[ISO 3166-2:PY|ISO 3166-2]] code  is '''PY-13'''. 
 
 The name comes from the name of a part of the Caaguazú Cordillera, the  " Amambay Cordillera "  ({{lang-es|Cordillera de Amambay}}). < ref name= " ubicacion " > {{cite web |url=http://amambay.gov.py/index.php/2014-04-24-19-00-38/ubicacion |title=Amambay |publisher=Gobernación de Concepción |language=es |access-date=25 August 2016 |archive-date=2016-10-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161012191804/http://amambay.gov.py/index.php/2014-04-24-19-00-38/ubicacion |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  ''Amambay'' (or ''amambái'') means  " [[fern]] "  in [[Guarani|Guaraní language]]; ferns are common in the [[forest]]s of the region. 
 
 ==History== 
 The town of [[Pedro Juan Caballero, Paraguay|Pedro Juan Caballero]] was found in 1893, and [[Bella Vista, Amambay|Bella Vista]], in 1902. The department of Amambay was created in 1945 as the No. 10 department of the country.  After some reorganization it was declared as the No. 13 department., < ref name= " ubicacion "  / > 
 
 Pedro Juan Caballero, the capital of the department, is still known by its old name, ''Punta Porá'', because is near the ''Punta Porá'' Hill; that name was used by the people that transported [[yerba mate]] from [[Mato Grosso]] to [[Concepción Department, Paraguay|Concepción]]. The [[Brazil]]Ian [[municipality]] across the [[border]], [[Ponta Porã]] ([[Mato Grosso do Sul]]), keeps the name. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The Amambay department is in the north part of the ''Oriental'' region. It has an area of {{Convert|12933|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}, with a population of 125,611 for a [[Population density]] of {{Pop density|125611|12933|km2|prec=1|disp=num}}. < ref name= " proyeccion "  / > 
 
 Amambay is the highest of the Paraguayan departments, {{Convert|300|-|400|m|ft|0|abbr=on }} high, and the ''Punta Porá'' Hill, {{Convert|700|m|ft|0|abbr=on }} high, < ref name= " ubicacion "  / >  is one the highest point in the country. 
 
 ===Limits=== 
 * To the north: [[Brazil]], separated by the Apa river and the Amambay Cordillera. 
 * To the east: [[Brazil]], separated by the Amambay Cordillera. 
 * To the south: [[Canindeyú Department|Canindeyú]] department. 
 * To the west: [[Concepción Department, Paraguay|Concepción]] and [[San Pedro Department, Paraguay|San Pedro]] departments.

 ===Rivers=== 
 The main [[river]]s in the department are the [[Apa (river)|Apa]] in the north, and the [[Ypané (river)|Ypané]] and the [[Aquidabán River|Aquidabán]] rivers in the central part of the department. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 {{As of|2012}}, the total amount of [[precipitation]] for the year 2012 in the city of [[Pedro Juan Caballero, Paraguay|Pedro Juan Caballero]] was {{Convert|1800.7|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation was February with {{Convert|253.6|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation was May with {{Convert|2.1|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " clima " > {{cite web |url=http://www.dgeec.gov.py/Publicaciones/Biblioteca/ambiental2012/compendio%20ambiental%202012.pdf |title=Compendio Estadístico Ambiental del Paraguay 2002-2012 |publisher=Dirección General de Estadísticas, Encuestas y Censos |format= PDF |language=es |access-date=25 August 2016 |date= December 2013 }} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] temperature for the year 2012 in Pedro Juan Caballero was {{Convert|22.5|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, was December with an average temperature of {{Convert|31.8|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is July, with an average temperature of {{Convert|17.1|°C|°F }}. < ref name= " clima "  / >
