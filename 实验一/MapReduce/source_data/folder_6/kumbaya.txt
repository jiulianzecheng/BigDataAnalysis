 " '''Kum ba yah''' "  ( " '''Come by Here''' " ) is an [[African American]] [[Spiritual (music)|spiritual]] [[song]] of unknown origin, but is known to be sung by the [[Gullah]] people of the [[Sea Islands|islands off South Carolina and Georgia]], who are [[descendant]]s of former [[slave]]s from [[West Africa]]. The song is thought to have spread from the islands to other [[Southern United States|Southern U.S. states]] and to [[Northern United States|Northern U.S. states]], as well as other places in the world. The first known [[recording]], of someone known only as H. Wylie, who sang in the [[Gullah language|Gullah dialect]], was recorded by the [[folk music]] enthusiast Robert Winslow Gordon in 1926. It is a popular [[hymn]] in African American [[Church (building)|churches]]. It later became a popular campfire song in [[Scouting]] and summer [[Camping|camps]].
