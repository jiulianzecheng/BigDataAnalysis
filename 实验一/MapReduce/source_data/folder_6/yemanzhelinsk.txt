 '''Yemanzhelinsk''' ([[Russian language|Russian]]: Еманжели́нск) is a town and [[administrative centre]] in the [[Yemanzhelinsky District]] in [[Chelyabinsk Oblast]], [[Russia]]. It is located near the [[Kazakhstan]] border, and is on the eastern slopes of the Southern [[Ural Mountains]]. Yemanzhelinsk was founded in 1770 by the [[Cossacks]]. The population was 30,216 at the 2010 census. < ref > http://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200419232529/https://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm |date=2020-04-19 }} '''(Russian)''' < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 
 Yemanzhelinsk was founded in 1770 as a [[Cossacks|Cossack]] village and  was named '''Yemanzhelinskaya''' ({{lang|ru|Еманжелинская}}) since 1866. It became a [[coal mining]] settlement in the early 1930s, and was granted town status on September 25, 1951. The [[chelyabinsk meteor]] landed close to Yemanzhelinsk.
