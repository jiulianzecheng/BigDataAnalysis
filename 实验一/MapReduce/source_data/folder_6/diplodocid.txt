 With their peg-like teeth they could strip leaves from branches, and leave the grinding to [[gastrolit]]hs. They bacteria in their vast stomachs would break down the [[cellulose]] in the leaves, and the product would be absorbed further on in the [[alimentary canal]]. 
 
 The diplodocids have two sub-families: 
 #Diplodocinae: ''Diplodocus'' and other long, slender forms. 
 #Apatosaurinae: ''[[Apatosaurus]]'' and other stockier types. 
 
 Their cousins the [[brachiosaur]]s are put into a sister family. The much later [[titanosaur]]s were the last group of sauropods on Earth.
