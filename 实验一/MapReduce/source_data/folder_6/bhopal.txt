 '''Bhopal''' is the [[capital (city)|capital]] of [[Madhya Pradesh]], [[India]], and the second largest city of the state, after [[Indore]]. Bhopal is also called the City of Lakes. < ref > {{cite book |title=The Geography of India: Sacred and Historic Places |date=2010 |publisher=The Rosen Publishing Group, Inc |isbn=978-1-61530-142-3 |page=174 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mjr0X-8jrLAC & q=bhopal+lakes |access-date=11 August 2020 |language=en}} < /ref >  It is the headquarters of Bhopal District. It is in the central part of India. 
 
 The city is believed to have first been called Bhojpal, named after the King of Bhoj. According to this theory, it was later changed to Bhopal. 
 
 Bhopal has an average elevation of 499 metres (1637 & nbsp;ft).  
 
 == Bhopal Disaster == 
 {{main|Bhopal disaster}} 
 In 1984 a poisoned gas was leaked from a private chemical factory at night when a worker was cleaning out a clogged pipe connected with a [[wikt:tank|tank]] containing gas. The tank had 42 tons of methyl isocyanate (MIC) that was leaked at midnight and leaving more than 5,000 people and animals dead. Many people also became sick and they are still suffering from deadly diseases. This has been called as  " World's worst industrial disaster " .
