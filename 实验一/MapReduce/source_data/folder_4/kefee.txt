 '''Kefee Obareki Don Momoh''' (5 February 1980 - 12 June 2014), < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-27840948|title=Nigerian gospel singer Kefee dies|date=13 June 2014|accessdate=20 October 2022|publisher=[[BBC]]|archive-date=19 October 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221019104402/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-27840948|url-status=bot: unknown}} < /ref >  commonly referred to as '''Kefee''' was a female gospel musician in [[Nigeria]]. 
 ==Early life== 
 She was born in [[Sapele, Delta]] on 5 February 1980 < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/entertainment/64358-kefee-to-be-buried-july-11.html|title=Kefee to be buried July 11|date=3 July 2014|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=Premium Times}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.nigeriafilms.com/news/31413/46/kefee-comes-alive-today-at-her-first-memorial-birt.html|title=KEFEE COMES ALIVE TODAY AT HER FIRST MEMORIAL BIRTHDAY CONCERT|last=Ezeh|first=Maryjane|date=5 February 2015|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=NigeriaFilms.com|archive-date=7 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150707171116/http://www.nigeriafilms.com/news/31413/46/kefee-comes-alive-today-at-her-first-memorial-birt.html|url-status=bot: unknown}} < /ref >  to the family of Andrew Obareki who were at a time Deacons at a church founded by the parents of her ex-husband Alec Godwin. 
 
 Kefee graduated from [[University of Benin]] with a degree in Business Administration. Growing up as teen, she actively engaged herself with church activities especially singing in the choir. < ref name= " kefee " > {{cite web|url=http://sunnewsonline.com/new/music-star-kefee-died|title=How music star, Kefee died|last1=Erhariefe|first1=Tony Ogaga|last2=Chima|first2=Nkechi|date=14 June 2014|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=The Sun|archive-date=9 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150909110208/http://sunnewsonline.com/new/music-star-kefee-died|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 ==Career== 
 As her passion for music kept growing bigger, she started writing and composing songs. In 2000 she released an album titled  " Trip "  and that made way for her into the Nigerian music scene as a Gospel artist. 
 
 In 2003, she got signed to Alec's Entertainment, a record label founded by her former choir director and she released her debut studio album  " Branama "  shortly after that. 
 
 The Branama album brought her into the spot light as a fulfilled Gospel artist with sales both national and international. Branama sold nine thousand cassettes in three weeks and over two million CD/VCDs in a month. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://afrobios.com/-Kefee|title=Kefee|publisher=Afrobios.com}} < /ref >  It served as a starting part to her successful career as Nigerian Gospel artist. The late singer's best known hits are  " Branama "  and  " Kokoroko " . < ref name= " kefee "  / > 
 ==Awards== 
 She was awarded the International Young Ambassador for Peace Award in 2009. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.thenigerianvoice.com/movie/5858/kefee-becomes-un-peace-ambassador.html|title=Kefee becomes UN Peace Ambassador|date=14 February 2009|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=The Nigerian Voice}} < /ref >  Kefee won the 2010 Headies Awards for Best Collaboration with Timaya for  " Kokoroko " .

 ==Death and burial== 
 Though the cause of death was rumoured to be pre-eclampsia, Kefee Obareki Don Momoh died of lung failure at a hospital in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] on June 12, 2014. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.thenigerianvoice.com/news/149521/1/gospel-singer-kefee-is-dead.html|title=Gospel singer, Kefee is Dead|date=13 June 2014|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=The Nigerian Voice}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://pmnewsnigeria.com/2014/06/13/gospel-singer-kefee-is-dead/|title=Gospel Singer Kefee Is Dead|last=Arogundade|first=Funsho|date=13 June 2014|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=PM News}} < /ref > < ref name= " dies " > {{cite web|url=http://thenet.ng/2014/06/kefee-dies-after-15-days-in-coma/|title=Kefee dies after 15 days in coma|last=Alonge|first=Osagie|date=13 June 2014|accessdate=21 October 2022|publisher=Thenet.ng|archive-date=5 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305082250/http://thenet.ng/2014/06/kefee-dies-after-15-days-in-coma/|url-status=bot: unknown}} < /ref >  She had been in a coma for fifteen days. < ref name= " dies "  / >
