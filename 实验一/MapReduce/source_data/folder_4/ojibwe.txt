 The '''Ojibwe''', '''Ojibwa''', '''Chippewa''', or '''Saulteaux''' are an [[Anishinaabe]] people of Canada and the United States. They are one of the biggest [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|indigenous peoples]] north of the [[Rio Grande]]. In Canada, they are the second-biggest [[First Nations]] group. The only group bigger are the [[Cree]]. In the United States, they have the fifth-biggest number of people among [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] peoples. The only groups bigger are the [[Navajo Nation|Navajo]], [[Cherokee]], [[Choctaw]] and [[Sioux]].  
 
 Most of the Ojibwe people live in the United States. There are 77,940 mainline Ojibwe; 76,760 Saulteaux; and 8,770 Mississauga, organized in 125 bands. They live from western [[Quebec]] to eastern [[British Columbia]]. {{as of|2010}}, the US census says that there are 170,742 Ojibwe people. < ref name=oppva / >
