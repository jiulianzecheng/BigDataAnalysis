
 '''Neoconservatism''' (often shortened as '''neocon''') is a form of [[Conservatism in the United States|American conservatism]] that emphasizes an [[Aggression|aggressive]] [[Foreign policy of the United States|American foreign policy]], usually criticized as that of [[American imperialism]]. < ref name= " britannica " > {{Cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/topic/neoconservatism|title=Neoconservatism|last=Dagger|first=Richard|website=Encyclopædia Britannica|access-date=16 May 2016}} < /ref > < ref name= " merriam-webster " > {{Cite web|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/neoconservative|title=Neoconservative|website=Merriam-Webster Dictionary|access-date=11 November 2012}} < /ref >  It started in the [[United States]] during the 1960s. Neocons supported the [[Vietnam War|Vietnam war]], but disliked the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic party]], [[Great Society]], and the [[New Left]]. 
 
 Neoconservative sometimes refers to people who started as anti-[[Stalinism|Stalinists]] and then became [[Conservatives in the United States|American conservatives]] during the 1960s and 1970s. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Neoconservatism: The biography of a movement|last=Vaïsse, Justin|date=2010|publisher=Harvard University Press|pages=6–11}} < /ref > 
 
 == Examples == 
 Some neocons are [[Republican Party (United States)|Republicans]], like the presidents between from the 1970s to the 2000s. For example, [[George W. Bush]] started the [[2003 invasion of Iraq]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7hOlgQUq7FYC & pg=PT47|title=Wanting War: Why the Bush Administration Invaded Iraq|last=Record, Jeffrey|publisher=Potomac Books, Inc.|year=2010|isbn=9781597975902|pages=47–50|access-date=2016-06-12}} < /ref >  His neocon friends include [[Paul Wolfowitz]], Elliott Abrams, Richard Perle, and [[Paul Bremer]]. [[Dick Cheney]] and [[Donald Rumsfeld]] also listened to these neocons. Together they supported the defense of [[Israel]] and the American invasion of the [[Middle East]]. Very few of those people actually called themselves Neoconservatives though. Some people who call themselves Neoconservatives are [[Douglas Murray]] and [[Reihan Salaam]]. 
 
 == Where did it come from? == 
 Neocon started in the 60s with [[Jew|Jewish]] magazine ''Commentary'', edited by [[Norman Podhoretz]] and published by the [[American Jewish Committee]]. < ref > Mm{{Cite book|title=The neoconservative revolution: Jewish intellectuals and the shaping of public policy|last=Friedman, Murray|date=2005|publisher=Cambridge University Press}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite news|title=Running Commentary: The Contentious Magazine that Transformed the Jewish Left Into the Neoconservative Right|last=Balint, Benjamin|date=2010|work=PublicAffairs}} < /ref >  They opposed the [[New Left]] and thus started the neocon movement. < ref > {{Cite news|title=The Neoconservatism Persuasion|last=Beckerman, Gal|date=6 January 2006|work=The Forward}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|title=The Neoconservative Revolution Jewish Intellectuals and the Shaping of Public Policy|last=Friedman|first=Murray|publisher=Cambridge University Press|year=2005|location=Cambridge, UK}} < /ref >
