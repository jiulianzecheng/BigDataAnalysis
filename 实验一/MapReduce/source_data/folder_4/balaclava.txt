 A '''balaclava''' (or '''ski mask''') is a type of [[clothing]] worn over the [[head]]. It has holes for [[eyes]], the [[mouth]] and the [[nose]]. < ref name= " Maitra2007 " > {{cite book|author=K. K. Maitra|title=Encyclopaedic Dictionary of Clothing and Textiles|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BAcAtLtdjh0C & pg=PA402|access-date=10 September 2013|date=15 November 2007|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-8324-205-9|pages=402}} < /ref >  They are normally worn to protect from cold weather, and is often worn by [[skiing|skiers]]. 
 
 The name  " balaclava "  comes from the town of [[Balaklava]], near [[Sevastopol]] in [[Crimea]], [[Ukraine]].
