 '''Ngarutjaranya''', also known as '''Mount Woodroffe,''' is [[South Australia]]'s highest peak. It is {{convert|1435|m}} above sea level. The word ''Ngarutjaranya'' comes from the [[Pitjantjatjara]] language. < ref > {{Cite web

   }} < /ref >  In their [[Australian Aboriginal mythology|Dreaming]], the mountain represents the spirit being [[Ngintaka]]. 
 
 Ngarutjaranya is in the far northwest of [[South Australia]], in the [[Musgrave Ranges]]. The mountain range rises about 700â€“800 metres above the plains around it. The rock is mostly [[granite]] and [[gneiss]]. 
 
 Ngarutjaranya was given its European name by [[William Gosse]], who first saw it on 20 July 1873. He named it after [[George Woodroffe Goyder]], another early explorer of South Australia and the [[Northern Territory]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.placenames.sa.gov.au/pno/pnores.phtml?recno=SA0072655 |title=Mount Woodroffe, South Australia |work=Place Names Online |publisher=Government of South Australia |accessdate=2006-07-30|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070930152339/http://www.placenames.sa.gov.au/pno/pnores.phtml?recno=SA0072655|archivedate=2007-09-30}} < /ref >  Access to the mountain is restricted.
