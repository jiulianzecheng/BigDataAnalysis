 The '''jaguarundi''' (''Puma yagouaroundi'') is a small wild [[felidae|cat]] native to [[Central America|Central]] and [[South America]]. It is a smaller relative of the better-known [[cougar]], and has many local names. < ref > Ferreira A.B.H. 1986. ''Novo Dicionário da Língua Portuguesa. Segunda edição. Rio de Janeiro: Nova Fronteira.'' P. 980 < /ref > 
 
 The cat is still widespread throughout the [[Amazon Basin]], and is listed as [[least concern]] on the [[IUCN Red List]] since 2002. < ref name=iucn > {{IUCN |assessor=Caso A. |assessor2=de Oliveira T. |assessor3=Carvajal S.V. |year=2015 |title=Puma yagouaroundi |id=9948 |version=2016.2}} < /ref >  It is sometimes found in [[Mexico]] and southern [[Texas]], and as far south as northern [[Argentina]] and these cats have been introduced to the state of [[Florida]] and state of [[Alabama]] in [[1980's]]. 
 
 Jaguarundis are mostly daytime animals. They are comfortable in trees, but hunt on the ground. 
 
 According to a 2006 [[sequence analysis|genomic]] study of Felidae, an ancestor of today's  ''[[Leopardus]]'', ''[[Lynx]]'', ''[[Puma]]'', ''[[Prionailurus]]'', and ''[[Felis]]'' lines migrated across the [[Beringia|Bering land bridge]] into the [[Americas]] about 8.0 to 8.5 million years ago. < ref name= " Johnson2006 " > {{cite journal |last=Johnson W.E.
