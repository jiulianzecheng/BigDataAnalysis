 '''Herning''' is a town in Denmark. It is in the [[Central Denmark Region]] on the Jutland peninsula. It is the main town and the administrative center of Herning Municipality. The town has a population of 50,565 people, including the surrounding areas Tjørring, Snejbjerg, Lind, Birk, Hammerum, and Gjellerup. It is the 11th most populous urban area in Denmark. < ref > {{Cite web|title=The Mobile Statbank - Population 1. January by urban and rural areas, population, area and population density and time|url=https://m.statbank.dk/TableInfo/BY3?lang=en|access-date=2023-06-12|website=m.statbank.dk}} < /ref >

 == History == 
 Herning was founded at the beginning of the 1790s as a commercial centre providing goods and services to the farmers in the area. A textile industry later developed in and around the town. Today, the town has a more diverse industrial base. In 1913, Herning became a market town. It was twice awarded the title of Danish City of the Year.

 == Attractions == 
 The town is home to Messecenter Herning, the largest exhibition center in [[Scandinavia]]. It hosts various trade fairs. Herning also has several notable attractions, including the Carl-Henning Pedersen and Else Alfelt's Museum of Art and three buildings designed by architect Jørn Utzon. 
 
 [[Carl-Henning Pedersen]] and [[Else Alfelt|Else Alfelt's]] Museum of Art (also known as [[Herning Art Museum]]) is located in the city. 
 
 Three buildings in the city were designed by the architect [[Jørn Utzon]]. One is owned by the public, and two are privately owned. 
 
 There is a monumental artwork called [[Elia (sculpture)|Elia]] by sculptor [[Ingvar Cronhammar]]. The sculpture is located near the Herning Art Museum. 
 
 The old Herningsholm Estate in Herning is open to the public for tours. The Classensborg Estate, now called Skarrildhus, is situated 25 km south of the town. It is closed to the public because it is a private hotel and educational facility. However, the grounds can be visited and are known for their beautiful displays of flowering plants [[Rhododendron|rhododendrons]] during the spring season. 
 
 The Herning Museum displays a history of Herning, as well as the development of moorland agriculture and ancient textile production. < ref > [http://www.sologstrand.com/holiday-denmark/museums/herning-museum.htm  " Herning Museum " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170202001445/http://www.sologstrand.com/holiday-denmark/museums/herning-museum.htm|date=2 February 2017}}, ''Sol Og Strand'', < /ref > < ref > [http://www.euro-t-guide.com/See_Coun/Denmark/Jut-M/DK_See_Herning_Museum_1-1.htm  " Denmark – Herning Museum " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170202030356/http://www.euro-t-guide.com/See_Coun/Denmark/Jut-M/DK_See_Herning_Museum_1-1.htm |date=2017-02-02 }}.  == Sports == 
 [[Herning Blue Fox]] is a professional [[ice hockey]] team from Denmark. They play in the top Danish ice hockey league called the [[Metal Ligaen|Oddset Ligaen]]. They have won 16 championships and 29 medals. The team has greatest number of victories in the history of professional ice hockey in Denmark. 
 
 FC Midtjylland is a football team that plays in the [[Danish Superliga]]. It was formed in 1999 when Herning Fremad and Ikast FS merged. In 2015, they won the national championship of Denmark for the first time after being the runner-up twice. FC Midtjylland'splay their home matches at [[MCH Arena]], which is located next to the largest sports and concert venue in Denmark, Jyske Bank Boxen. 
 
 Herning is also known for its cycling. The GP Herning is an annual professional bicycle race held in the city. The 2012 [[Giro d'Italia]] started in Herning. Bjarne Riis, the first Danish cyclist to win the [[Tour de France]], was born in Herning. Fourteen years later, Riis admitted to using illegal drugs during the competition. However, he kept the Trikot because the time limit for punishment had already passed before he made his admission. 
 
 The final match of the 2019 World Men's Handball Championship took place in Jyske Bank Boxen.

 == Transport == 
 
 === Road and rail === 
 [[File:Herning_Station_2019_01.jpg|link=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Herning_Station_2019_01.jpg|thumb|Herning railway station.]] 
 Herning is an important transportation hub for both roads and trains in central Jutland. The town's main railway station, Herning railway station, connects various rail lines that cross the [[peninsula]]. These lines provide connections to [[Vejle]], [[Aarhus|Århus]], [[Esbjerg]], [[Holstebro]]. There are also several daily trains to [[Copenhagen]]. Additionally, there are [[railway halts]] at Birk Centerpark and Herning Messecenter. 
 
 Herning is located at the meeting point of three major roads. Route 18 runs from southeast to northwest across the [[Jutland Peninsula]]. Route 15 goes from Aarhus in the east to Ringkobing in the west. Route 12 connects [[Esbjerg]] in the southwest to Viborg in the northeast. 
 
 == Airport == 
 There is Karup Airport in the town. It is located 25 km (16 mi) northeast of Herning. There are several daily flights connecting Karup Airport to [[Copenhagen Airport]].
