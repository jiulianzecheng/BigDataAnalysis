 '''Banbury''' is a [[market town]] on the River Cherwell in northern [[Oxfordshire]], [[England]].  < ref > {{Cite web|title=The history of Banbury, Oxfordshire|url=https://www.information-britain.co.uk/history/town/Banbury11/|access-date=2020-08-20|website=www.information-britain.co.uk|archive-date=2017-07-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170709071628/http://information-britain.co.uk/history/town/Banbury11/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It had a population of 43,867 at the 2001 [[census]]. Although this figure has increased greatly in recent years. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.ons.gov.uk/|title=Home - Office for National Statistics|website=www.ons.gov.uk}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Banbury Community profile of Health and Wellbeing evidence|url=https://insight.oxfordshire.gov.uk/cms/system/files/documents/BanburyJSNAprofileNov19.pdf|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=20 August 2020|website=Oxfordshire City Council}} < /ref >   Banbury is part of the Cherwell district. The [[Member of Parliament]] for Banbury is Victoria Prentis. Banbury played an important part in the [[English Civil War]] prior to the [[Battle of Edge Hill]], as shown by [[The Reindeer inn]] (December 2005). 
 
 Heavy [[clay]] and [[Ironstone]] deposits surround Banbury.  
 
 The [[Oxfordshire Ironstone Railway]] (the O.I.R.) was the local railway line from Banbury to the nearby [[ironstone]] [[quarry]] at [[Wroxton]]. It was open from [[1917]] to [[1967]].
