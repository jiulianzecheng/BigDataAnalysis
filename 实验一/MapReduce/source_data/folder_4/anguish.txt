 '''Anguish''' is an [[emotion]]. It is a feeling of severe [[pain]], mental [[suffering]], [[Distress (medicine)|distress]] and [[sadness]]. Anguish is related to [[misery]], [[dread]], [[despair]], and [[Depression (mood)|depression]].

 ==Anguish in law== 
 Mental anguish is a [[wikt:concept|concept]] of [[law]]. It is sometimes written into a [[legal code]]. In [[tort law]], a person can receive [[money|compensation]] when someone hurts them. This is known as a personal injury case. This kind of legal case is handled by a [[personal injury lawyer]]. 
 
 In the [[United States]], personal injury [[lawsuit]]s account for less than 5% of all civil cases. < ref name=debunk > {{cite web| url=https://www.justice.org/what-we-do/promote-accountability-safety/debunking-myths | title = Debunking the Myths | access-date=25 April 2015}} < /ref >  That number is [[wikt:decline|declining]]. < ref name=debunk/ >  Between 1999 and 2008, there was a 25% [[wikt:decrease|decrease]] in personal injury lawsuits filed in the United States. < ref name=debunk/ > 
 
 In the [[Judiciary|judicial system]], mental anguish is also called  " [[pain]] and [[suffering]] " . This may include feelings of [[fear]], [[Distress (medicine)|distress]], [[anxiety]], [[Depression (mood)|depression]], [[Truama (medicine)|trauma]], or [[grief]]. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.nolo.com/dictionary/mental-anguish-term.html|title=Mental Anguish|access-date=18 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 When [[Jimmy Carter]] was [[President of the United States]], he issued a [[wikt:proclamation|proclamation]] about [[human rights]]. He said that we must [[wikt:defend|defend]] the [[victim]]s of anguish, not the [[culprit]]s (the people who cause it). < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/STATUTE-93/pdf/STATUTE-93-Pg1556.pdf | title=Proclamation 4705 of December 6,1979, Bill of Rights Day, Human Rights Day and Week, 1979. By the President of the United States of America. A Proclamation. | access-date=18 April 2015}} < /ref >

 ==Anguish in psychology== 
 Mental anguish can have significant as physical symptoms. < ref name=palliative > {{cite web | url=https://palliative.stanford.edu/palliative-sedation/the-problem-of-psychological-suffering/ | title=The Problem of Psychological Suffering | date=20 April 2013 | publisher=Stanford School of Medicine | access-date=18 April 2015 | archive-date=15 September 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150915090037/https://palliative.stanford.edu/palliative-sedation/the-problem-of-psychological-suffering/ | url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Psychological [[suffering]] from anguish can be just as significant as physical [[pain]]. < ref name=palliative/ >  Psychological symptoms like [[anxiety]], [[panic attack]]s and [[existential crisis]] can cause [[suffering]]. < ref name=palliative/ > 
 
 [[Sigmund Freud]] is considered the inventor of [[psychoanalysis]]. Freud said that anguish is one of the feelings that brings together and tears apart people, families, and [[society|societies]]. < ref > {{cite book|last1=Doolittle|first1=Hilda |last2=Freud |first2=Sigmund | last3=Bryher|last4=Stanford Friedman |first3=Susan| date=2005|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iZZKp4NzNL8C|title=Analyzing Freud: Letters of H.D., Bryher, and Their Circle|publisher= New Directions Publishing|page=xxxvii |isbn= 9780811216036 |access-date=18 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Anguish in philosophy== 
 Anguish is an idea in [[philosophy]]. 
 
 ;Existentialism: 
 
 Anguish is an important concept in [[existentialism]]. < ref > {{cite book|last=Olson|first=Robert G.  |date=2012|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=CxDK1NUW4GIC|title=An Introduction to Existentialism|publisher= Courier Corporation |isbn= 9780486119281 |access-date=17 April 2015}} < /ref > < ref name= " Pratt " > {{cite web|url=http://www.iep.utm.edu/nihilism/|title=Nihilism|last=Pratt|first=Alan |publisher=Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy| access-date= 20 April 2015}} < /ref >  Existentialists believe that freedom causes anguish. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://plato.stanford.edu/entries/existentialism/ | title=Existentialism |last=Crowell| first=Steven | publisher=The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy | date = 9 March 2015 | access-date=17 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 [[France|French]] philosopher [[Jean-Paul Sartre]] talked about anguish in his book ''L'Être et le Néant'' (1943) (in English: ''Being and Nothingness'') (1956). 
 
 < blockquote > 
 " It is certain that we cannot overcome anguish, for we are anguish. " < ref > {{cite book|last=Sartre|first=Jean-Paul |date=1956|title=Being and Nothingness|url=https://archive.org/details/beingnothingness0000unse|publisher= Philosophical Library |isbn= 9781453228555 |access-date=17 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 < /blockquote > 
 
 For Sartre and [[Martin Heidegger|Heidegger]] and [[Søren Kierkegaard|Kierkegaard]] anguish is an important emotion. < ref > {{cite book|last=Solomon|first=Robert C.  |date=2001|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iHM9ABHZyB0C|title=From Rationalism to Existentialism: The Existentialists and Their Nineteenth-century Backgrounds|publisher= Rowman  &  Littlefield |isbn= 9780742512412 |access-date=18 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 Another French philosopher, [[Gilles Deleuze]], said that anguish is a basic human emotion. He said that people feel anguish when they understand that they cannot escape from [[evil]] and [[pain]] in the world. < ref > {{cite book|last=Williams|first=James  |date=2011|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8C2rBgAAQBAJ|title=Gilles Deleuze's Philosophy of Time: A Critical Introduction and Guide|publisher= Oxford University Press |isbn= 9780748645428 |access-date=18 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 ;Nihilism: 
 
 Anguish is an important concept in [[nihilism]]. < ref name= " Pratt " / >  Nihilists believe that life is [[wikt:meaningless|meaningless]]. That belief is a source of anguish. < ref name= " Pratt " / > 
 
 [[Germany|German]] philosopher [[Arthur Schopenhauer]] wrote about  " anguish of conscience. "  He said that [[consciousness]] is anguish and anguish is pain. < ref > {{cite book|last=Schopenhauer |first=Arthur | title=Complete Arthur Schopenhauer Philosophy Criticism Collection | publisher=AEB Publishing}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Anguish in theology== 
 [[Søren Kierkegaard]] was a [[Danish people|Danish]] philosopher. He wrote about religion, or [[wikt:theology|theology]]. Kierkegaard talked about anguish in several of his books: ''Fear and Trembling'' (1843), ''Either/Or'' (1843), ''The Sickness Unto Death'' (1849), and ''Two Discourses of God and Man'' (1938). In the book ''Eighteen Upbuilding Discourses'' (1843), Kierkegaard talked about [[Paul the Apostle]]. Paul felt anguish because he [[Persecution of Christians|persecuted Christians]]. < ref > {{cite book|last1=Kierkegaard|first1=Søren |last2=Perkins|first2=Robert L. |date=2003|title=Eighteen Upbuilding Discourses|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VcnWzgEACAAJ|publisher=Mercer University Press|page=151|isbn= 9780865548794|access-date=17 April 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Anguish in movies== 
 Anguish is feeling common for characters in [[Woody Allen]]'s [[movie]]s. Marion feels anguish in ''Another Woman''. Alvy feels anguish in ''[[Annie Hall]]''. Mary fakes feelings of anguish in ''[[Manhattan (movie)|Manhattan]]''.
