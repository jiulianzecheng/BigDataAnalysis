 {{nihongo|'''Fujiidera'''|藤井寺市|Fujiidera-shi}} is a [[city]] in the middle region of [[Osaka Prefecture]] in [[Japan]]. 
 
 The [[Municipalities in Japan|municipality]] calls itself  " Fujidera City "  in English. < ref name= " history " > Fujidera City, [http://www.city.fujiidera.osaka.jp/7,950,86,130.html 藤井寺市の歴史 ( " History of the city Fujiidera " )] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130627224921/http://www.city.fujiidera.osaka.jp/7,950,86,130.html |date=2013-06-27 }}; retrieved 2012-4-1. < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 According to the [[Imperial Household Agency]], [[Emperor Chūai]] and [[Emperor Ingyō]] are buried in an [[wikt:earthen|earthen]] [[tumulus]] (''[[kofun]]'') at Fujiidera. < ref > Gowland, William.  " The Burial Mounds and Dolmens of the Early Emperors of Japan, "  ''The Journal of the Royal Anthropological Institute of Great Britain and Ireland,'' Vol. 37, Jan.-Jun., 1907, pp. 10-46; retrieved 2011-10-16. < /ref >  Chūai and Ingyō are [[wikt:venerate|venerated]] at a [[memorial]] [[Shinto]] [[shrine]] (''misasagi'') at this site. < ref > [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/019/index.html 允恭天皇 (19)]; retrieved 2012-4-1. < /ref > 
 
 The present city was established in 1966. < ref name= " history " / >
