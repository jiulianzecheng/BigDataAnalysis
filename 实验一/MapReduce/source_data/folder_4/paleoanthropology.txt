 '''Paleoanthropology''' (or palaeoanthropology) combines the [[wikt:study|study]] of [[paleontology]] and [[physical anthropology]]. It is the study of [[human evolution]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://humanorigins.si.edu/education/introduction-human-evolution |title=Introduction to human evolution |author= |date= |website=National Museum of Natural History |publisher=Smithsonian Institution |accessdate=17 August 2016}} < /ref >   
 
 The subject is also closely [[wikt:connected|connected]] to [[archaeology]]. < ref > Barry Lewis; Robert Jurmain  &  Lynn Kilgore 2013. ''Understanding humans: an introduction to physical anthropology and archaeology''. 11th ed, Belmont CA: Wadsworth Cengage Learning, p. 18. < /ref >  The [[evidence]] of [[handaxe|tool-making]] and other activities shows the [[wikt:subtle|subtle]] but important changes in thinking that early humans went through. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/evolution/library/07/4/l_074_01.html |title=Dexterity and early tools |author= |date= |website=PBS: Evolution |publisher=WGBH Educational Foundation; Clear Blue Sky Productions, Inc|accessdate=17 August 2016}} < /ref >
