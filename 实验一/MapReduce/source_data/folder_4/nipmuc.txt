 The '''Nipmuc''' or '''Nipmuck''' people are [[Native American|Native Americans]] originally from present-day [[Massachusetts]] and parts of [[Connecticut]] and [[Rhode Island]]. They are from the [[Indigenous peoples of the Northeastern Woodlands|Northeastern Woodlands]] culture. Their language is in the Eastern [[Algonquian languages|Algonquian]] language family. They did [[hunting]], [[fishing]] and [[farming]] (mostly corn). They moved for different seasons. Family groups lived in [[Village|villages]]. They were led by chiefs. These villages were often not allied with other Nipmuc villages. Villages allied with stronger neighbors like the [[Mohegan]] or [[Massachusett|Massachuset]]. Many Natives died from European [[Disease|diseases]]. Nipmuc supported [[Metacomet]] in [[King Philip's War]]. Many were forced to stay in [[Boston]] Harbor. Others were killed or forced into [[slavery]]. The [[Puritanism|Puritan]] [[missionary]] [[John Eliot (missionary)|John Eliot]] converted Natives and created [[praying towns]].
