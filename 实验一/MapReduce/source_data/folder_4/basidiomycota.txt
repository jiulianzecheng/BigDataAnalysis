 '''Basidiomycota''' is a group of higher [[fungi]]. It is one of two large [[phyla]] that make up the subkingdom [[Dikarya]]. < ref > Kirk P.M.; Cannon P.F.  &  Stalpers J.A. 2008. ''Dictionary of the Fungi'', 10th ed. CABI. < /ref >  The other is the [[Ascomycota]].  
 
 These are  " higher fungi " . They include jelly and shelf, or [[bracket fungi]], [[mushroom]]s, [[puffball]]s, and [[stinkhorn]]s; and the rusts and smuts. < ref > Moore R.T. 1980. Taxonomic proposals for the classification of marine yeasts and other yeast-like fungi including the smuts. ''Botanica Marina'' '''23''': 371. < /ref > 
 
 == Agaricomycotina == 
 This sub-phylum includes the  " classic "  mushrooms, polypores, corals, [[chanterelle]]s, crusts, [[puffball]]s and [[stinkhorn]]s. < ref > https://cpb-us-e1.wpmucdn.com/blogs.uoregon.edu/dist/c/8944/files/2014/09/Lecture-7-Ustilaginomycets-1w4kndx.pdf < /ref >  The three classes in the Agaricomycotina are the [[Agaricomycetes]], the [[Dacrymycetes]], and the [[Tremellomycetes]]. < ref > {{harvnb|Kirk|Cannon|Stalpers|2008|p=13}} < /ref > 
 
 == Pucciniomycotina == 
 The Pucciniomycotina include the rust fungi, the insect parasitic/symbiotic genus ''[[Septobasidium]]'', a former group of smut fungi (in the [[Microbotryomycetes]], which includes mirror yeasts), and a mixture of odd, infrequently seen, or seldom recognized fungi, often parasitic on plants. 
 
 == Ustilaginomycotina == 
 The [[Ustilaginomycotina]] are most (but not all) of the former [[smut fungi]] and the [[Exobasidiales]]. The classes of the Ustilaginomycotina are the [[Exobasidiomycetes]], the Entorrhizomycetes, and the [[Ustilaginomycetes]]. < ref > {{harvnb|Kirk|Cannon|Stalpers|2008|pp=717â€“718}} < /ref >
