{{nihongo|'''''Genryaku'''''|元暦}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Juei]]'' and before ''[[Bunji (Japanese era)|Bunji]].''  This period started in April 1184 and ended in August 1185. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA343  " ''Genryaku'' " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 239. < /ref >   The reigning emperors were {{nihongo|[[Emperor Antoku|Antoku]]''-tennō''|安徳天皇}} and {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Toba|Go-Toba]]''-tennō''|後鳥羽天皇}}. < ref > Titsingh, Isaac. (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA207  ''Annales des empereurs du Japon'', pp. 207-221]; Brown, Delmer ''et al.'' (1979). [https://books.google.com/books?id=w4f5FrmIJKIC & pg=PA334 ''Gukanshō''], pp. 334-339; Varley, H. Paul. (1980). ''Jinnō Shōtōki.'' pp. 215-220. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Genryaku'' era== 
 * '''25 April 1185''' (''Genryaku 2, 24th day of the 3rd month'') < !-- [http://web.me.com/meyer.eva/www.yukikurete.de/nengo_calc.htm   NengoCalc] 元暦二年三月二十四日-- > : the [[Taira]] (also known as the [[Heike]]) and the [[Minamoto]] clashed in the [[Battle of Dan-no-ura]]; and the Heike are defeated. < ref name= " kitagawa787 " > Kitagawa, Hiroshi ''et al.'' (1975). ''The Tale of the Heike'', p. 787. < /ref >   Antoku drowned at Dan-no-ura. < ref > Kitagawa, pp. 676-677; [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/081/index.html 安徳天皇 (81)]; retrieved 2012-5-23. < /ref > 
 * '''6 August 1185''' (''Genryaku 2, 9th day of the 7th month'') < !-- NengoCalc 元暦二年七月九日-- > : Great earthquake in [[Kyoto]] and in the [[Kinai]] region. < ref name= " kitagawa787 " / >
