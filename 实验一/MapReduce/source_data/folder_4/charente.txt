 '''Charente''' ([[Saintongeais dialect|Saintongeais]]: ''Chérente'', {{lang-oc|Charanta}}) is a [[departments of France|department]] in southwestern France, in the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] [[Regions of France|region]]. 
 
 It is named after the [[Charente (river)|Charente]] [[river]], the most important river in the department. The two largest towns of the department, [[Angoulême]] and [[Cognac, Charente|Cognac]], are along this river. 
 
 ==History== 
 Charente is one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created from the old province of ''Angoumois'' with lands around [[Angoulême]]. 
 
 The new department had six [[district]]s: Ruffec, Confolens, La Rochefoucauld, Angoulême, Barbezieux and Cognac. The [[Capital city|capital]] (now [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) was [[Angoulême]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/16his.html |title=Historique de la Charente |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=12 July 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the six districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Angoulême, Barbezieux, Cognac, Confolens and Ruffec. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissements'' of Barbezieux and Ruffec were eliminated. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Charente is part of the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|5956|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 The highest point of the department is ''Rocher aux Oiseaux'', {{Convert|368|m|ft|abbr=on}} high, in the ''commune'' of [[Montrollet]]; the lowest point is in the valley of the Charente river, ''commune'' of [[Merpins]], and is {{Convert|8|m|ft|abbr=on}} high. 
 
 The department is bordered by the departments: [[Deux-Sèvres]], [[Vienne]], [[Haute-Vienne]], [[Dordogne]] and [[Charente-Maritime]].

 The main [[river]] in the department is the [[Charente (river)|Charente]] that gives its name to the department. Other rivers are the [[Dronne]] and the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]]. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Cognac is an oceanic climate with template summers, '''Cfb''' (Marine West Coast Climate) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Cognac, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=21470 & cityname=Cognac%2C+Poitou-Charentes%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=12 July 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Cognac is {{Convert|777.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is November with {{Convert|86.4|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is July with an average of {{Convert|48.3|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Cognac is {{Convert|13.3|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|21.1|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|6.1|°C|°F }}. 
 
 ==Administration== 
 The department is managed by the ''Departmental Council of the Charente'' in [[Angoulême]]. Charente is part of the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]].

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Charente are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Charentais'' (women: ''Charentaises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/charente-16 |title=Charente (16)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=12 July 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Charente has a population, in 2014, of 353,853, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|353853|5956|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Angoulême, with 197,377 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Cognac and Confolens, have respectively 92,224 and 64,252 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
