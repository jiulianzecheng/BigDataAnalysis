 '''Shrewsbury''' is the county town of [[Shropshire]], a [[county]] of [[England]]. It is located 9 miles (14 & nbsp;km) east of the [[Wales|Welsh]] border and serves as the commercial centre for [[Shropshire]] and [[mid-Wales]]. 
 
 The town is very old. It was started around [[700|700 AD]]. The town centre has almost the exact same [[medieval]] street plan and over 660 listed buildings. Many of these buildings have timber framing from the 15th and 16th centuries. There is also [[Shrewsbury Castle]], which was built  in 1074, and [[Shrewsbury Abbey]], which was built in 1083. The [[town centre]] is almost completely surrounded by the [[River Severn]]. 
 
 [[Charles Darwin]] was born in Shrewsbury and lived there for 27 years. 
 
 The [[population]] of Shrewsbury is just over 76,000, making it the second largest town in Shropshire, the biggest being [[Telford]]. 
 
 The town has a [[Shrewsbury railway station|railway station]] and a professional [[Association football|football]] club, [[Shrewsbury Town F.C.|Shrewsbury Town]], who compete in [[Football League One|League One]]. 
 
 ==Twinning== 
 Shrewsbury is twinned with {{flagicon|NED}} [[Zutphen]], [[Netherlands]].
