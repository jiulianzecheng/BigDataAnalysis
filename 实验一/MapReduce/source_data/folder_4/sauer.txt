 The '''Sauer''' ([[German language|German]], [[Luxembourgish language|Luxembourgish]]) or '''Sûre''' ([[French language|French]]) is a [[river]] in [[Belgium]], [[Luxembourg]] and [[Germany]]. It is a left [[tributary]] of the river [[Moselle River|Moselle]], and so it is part of the [[drainage basin]] of the [[Rhine river]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The Sauer river has a length of {{Convert|173|km|mi|1|abbr=on}} < ref > {{cite web |url = http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/574969/Sure-River |title = Sûre River |date = |publisher = Encyclopædia Britannica Online Academic Edition |accessdate= 28 April 2014 }} < /ref >  and a [[drainage basin]] with an area of {{Convert|4240|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. 
 
 ===Course=== 
 [[File:Sauer - Vaux-sur-Sure.jpg|thumb|left|The Sauer near its source in [[Vaux-sur-Sûre]]]] 
 The [[River source|source]] of the Sauer is, as Sûre, in the southeast of Belgium in the [[Ardennes (forest)|Ardennes]] ([[Luxembourg (province)|Luxembourg]] province), on the western part of the [[Rhenish Massif]], at an [[altitude]] of about {{Convert|510|m|ft|0|abbr=on }}. 
 
 From there, the Sauer flows to the east and crosses the border between Belgium and Luxembourg near [[Martelange]]. It forms the border between Belgium and Luxembourg for {{Convert|13|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} north of Martelange. On their way through the [[mountain]]ous north of Luxembourg (the [[Oesling]]), it passes through the Lac de la Haute Sure, an artificial [[lake]] to the west of [[Esch-sur-Sûre]]. 
 
 After flowing through [[Ettelbruck]] and [[Diekirch]], the Sauer forms the border between Luxembourg and Germany for the last {{Convert|50|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} of its course, passing [[Echternach]] before joining, as left tributary, the Moselle in [[Wasserbillig]], at about {{Convert|132|m|ft|0|abbr=on }} of altitude.
