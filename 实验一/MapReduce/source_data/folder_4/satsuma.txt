 '''Satsuma''' < ref > It is commonly called '''unshuu''' in Japan, '''satsuma''' in the UK, '''naartjie''' in Africa and '''mandarin''' or '''tangerine''' in Canada. In the United States, satsumas are most frequently marketed as tangerines. It is less commonly called '''satsuma tangerine''', '''satsuma orange''', or '''satsuma mandarin'''. < /ref >  / sat-soo-muh/ '''.n'''. is a variety of [[tangerine]] with a loose skin.  
 It is named after the former [[Satsuma Province]] of [[Japan]].  In the [[United Kingdom]] it is often associated with Christmas.
