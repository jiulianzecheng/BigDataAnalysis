 '''Cayenne''' is a [[commune in France|commune]], the [[Capital city|capital]] ([[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) of the [[French Guiana]], an [[overseas region]] and [[Overseas department|department]] of [[France]] that is in northern [[South America]]. The city is on a former [[island]] at the mouth of the [[Cayenne (river)|Cayenne]] [[river]] on the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] coast. 
 
 == History == 
 The first [[Europe]]an [[settlement]] in the region was founded on the Cayenne [[island]] in 1604 by [[French people]] sent by [[Henry IV of France|Henry IV]] but the settlement was destroyed by [[Portugal|Portuguese]] to [[:wikt:enforce|enforce]] the [[Treaty of Tordesillas]] 
 
 French [[Colony|colonists]] came back in 1643 and founded Cayenne, but they were forced to leave once more following [[Amerindian]] attacks. In 1664, France finally succeeded at establishing a permanent settlement at Cayenne. Over the next decade the colony changed hands between the French, [[Dutch people|Dutch]], and English, before being restored to France. It was captured by an Anglo-Portuguese force at the invasion of Cayenne in 1809 and administered from Brazil until 1814, when it was returned to French control. It was used as a French [[penal colony]] from 1854 to 1938. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Cayenne is a city along the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] [[coast]] on the sides of a common [[estuary]] of the Cayenne and Montsinéry rivers. It has an area of {{Convert|23.60|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|53|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Cayenne-97300/ |title=Cayenne |publisher=Map-France.com |access-date=12 February 2017 }} < /ref > 
 
 The ''commune'' is at {{Convert|268|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni]] and {{Convert|64|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Kourou]]. The distances to other cities out of French Guiana are: 
 * [[Paris]], the national capital, la capitale nationale, {{Convert|7100|km|mi|0|abbr=on }}. 
 * [[Fort-de-France]], capital of [[Martinique]], {{Convert|1500|km|mi|0|abbr=on }}. 
 * [[Paramaribo]], capital of [[Surinam]], {{Convert|342|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} to the northwest. 
 * [[Macapá]], capital of [[Amapá]] ([[Brazil]]), {{Convert|777|km|mi|0|abbr=on }}.

 === Climate === 
 Cayenne has an [[equatorial climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]]: '''Af''') bordering on a [[Tropical climate|tropical monsoon climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]]: '''Am''') with a dry season and a heavy [[monsoon]] the rest of year and no cold season. 
 
 The [[average]] temperature for the year in Cayenne is {{Convert|26.2|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is September with an average temperature of {{Convert|27.2|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|26.1|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Cayenne is {{Convert|3205.5|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is May with {{Convert|513.1|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is September with an average of {{Convert|43.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. There is an average of 201.0 days of precipitation, with the most precipitation occurring in May with 27.0 days and the least precipitation occurring in September with 5.0 days. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Cayenne, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=50418 & cityname=Cayenne%2C+Guyane%2C+French+Guiana & units= |access-date=12 February 2017 }} < /ref >
