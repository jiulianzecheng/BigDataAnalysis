 '''Memory''' is the personal record of past experiences. It is the [[brain]]'s [[wikt:ability|ability]] to store [[information]] to use later. Memory is [[Research|studied]] in sciences known as  " [[cognitive psychology]] " .  
 
 There are two types of memory: short-term and long-term memory, or known as working memory and reference memory. < ref > {{cite book|author=Grahame Hill|title=AS Level Psychology Through Diagrams|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-rYkNmp4EPcC & pg=PA6|year=2001|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-832833-9|pages=6â€“}} < /ref > 
 
 # ''[[Short-term memory]]'', the ability to keep information in mind for a short time, like remembering a phone number long enough to find a piece of paper. 
 # ''[[Long-term memory]]'', the ability to remember things long after they happen, like remembering a holiday you had last year. 
 
 The Greeks considered memory ([[Mnemosyne]]) as the mother of Muses, for without her there would be no art and no science.
