'''Kettlebaston''' is a [[village]] and [[civil parish]] in [[England]].  It is in the [[Babergh]] district of [[Suffolk]], about {{convert|3|mi|0}} east of [[Lavenham]]. There are just 28 houses in the village. The village's name comes from ''Kitelbeornastuna'', a [[Scandinavia]]n name that means  " Kitelbjorn's [[farm]]. "  Today, the name is [[wikt:pronunciation|pronounced]] like ''Kettlebarston.'' The village was mentioned for the first time in the ''[[Domesday Book]]'' in 1086.
