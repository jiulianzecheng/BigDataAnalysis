{{nihongo|'''Enryaku'''|延暦}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Ten'ō]]'' and before ''[[Daidō]].''  This period started in August 782 and ended in May 806. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA181   " ''Enryaku'' " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 181. < /ref >   During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Kammu|Kammu]]''-tennō''|桓武天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA464  " Kammu Tennō, " ] p. 464; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA86  ''Annales des empereurs du Japon'', pp. 86-95]; Brown, Delmer ''et al.'' (1979). [https://books.google.com/books?id=w4f5FrmIJKIC & pg=PA277 ''Gukanshō'', pp. 277-279]; Varley, H. Paul. ''Jinnō Shōtōki'', pp. 148-150. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Enryaku'' era== 
 [[File:Enryaku-ji Jodoin-r.jpg|thumb|right|In ''Enryaku'' 12, work building Enryaku-ji temple was started]] 
 * '''782''' (''Enryaku 1, 6th month''): [[Fujiwara no Uona]] was removed from his office. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA211   " Fujiwara no Uona " ] at p. 211. < /ref > 
 * '''783''' (''Enryaku 3, in the 3rd month''): [[Fujiwara no Tamaro]] died at the age of 62 years. < ref name= " titsingh86 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA86  p.86]; Brown, [https://books.google.com/books?id=w4f5FrmIJKIC & pg=PA278  p. 278.] < /ref > 
 * '''783''' (''Enryaku 3, in the 7th month''): [[Fujiwara no Korekimi]] < ref > Imperial Japanese Commission to the World's Columbian Exposition. (1893). [https://books.google.com/books?id=wXoLAAAAIAAJ & dq=Fujiwara+no+korekimi & pg=PA106  ''History of the Empire of Japan,'' p. 106.] < /ref >  was promoted in the [[hierarchy]] of the Imperial court. < ref name= " titsingh86 " / > 
 * '''784''' (''Enryaku 3''): The emperor moves the capital to [[Nagaoka-kyō|Nagaoka]] < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA682  " Nagaoka-kyō "  at p. 682]. < /ref > 
 * '''788''' (''Enryaku 7''): The Buddhist monk [[Saichō]] < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA805  " Saichō "  at p. 805]. < /ref >  establishes a monastery on [[Mount Hiei|Mt Hiei]] 
 * '''793''' (''Enryaku 12''): [[Construction]] began on the Buddhist temple [[wikt:complex|complex]] at [[Enryaku-ji]]. < ref  name= " brown279 " > Brown, [https://books.google.com/books?id=w4f5FrmIJKIC & pg=PA279  p. 279.] < /ref > 
 * '''17 December 794''' (''Enryaku 13, 21st day of the 10th month'') < !-- [http://web.me.com/meyer.eva/www.yukikurete.de/nengo_calc.htm  NengoCalc 延暦十三年十一月二十一日]  -- > : The Emperor moved by carriage in a grand [[wikt:parade|parade]] from [[Nara]] to [[Heian-kyō]]. < ref  name= " brown279 " / > 
 * '''796''' (''Enryaku 15''): Copper coins were minted with the legend ''Ren-hei Ei-hō''. < ref > [[Georges Appert|Appert, Georges]]. (1888). ''Ancien japon'', p. 30. < /ref > 
 * '''797''' (''Enryaku 16''): ''[[Shoku Nihongi]]'' was written. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA883  " ''Shoku Nihongi'' " ] at p. 883. < /ref > 
 * '''806''' (''Enryaku 25''): Emperor Kammu died at the age of 70. < ref > Varley, p. 150; [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/050/index.html 桓武天皇 (50)]; retrieved 2011-12-27. < /ref >
