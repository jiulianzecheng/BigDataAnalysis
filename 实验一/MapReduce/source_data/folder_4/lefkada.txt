 '''Lefkada''' is a [[Greece|Greek]] island. It is in the west of Greece in the [[Ionian Sea]]. It is the fourth largest island of the [[Ionian Sea]] after [[Kefalonia]], [[Corfu]] and [[Zakynthos]]. Its total area is 303 [[Square kilometre|square kilometers]]. Its population is 23,845 residents according to 2001 Greek census. The largest town of Lefkada is [[Lefkada (city)]]. Its population is 10,875 people. 
 
 == Sights - Tourism == 
 [[File:Egremni Beach (Lefkada, Greece).jpg|200px|right|thumb|Egremni beach in Lefkada]] 
 Lefkada is famous for its beautiful beaches. The island is surrounded by many beautiful beaches with azure-green color. The most impressive beaches are Porto Katsiki, Kathisma and Egremni. All of these beaches are in the west of the island. According to Guardian newspaper the Porto Katsiki beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in [[Europe]]. < ref > [http://www.guardian.co.uk/travel/2008/aug/08/beaches.europe.top10 guardian.co.uk] Top 10 beaches in Europe < /ref > 
 
 Every year many tourists visit Lefkada. Tourists come from western European countries, most from Italy. The most [[:en:wikt:touristic|touristic]] village on the island is [[Nidri]] which is in the east of the island. Opposite Nidri there are many small green [[islet]]s. Among them is the islet Scorpios which belonged to millionaire [[Aristotle Onassis]].

Later it passed under the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] rule and [[Byzantine Empire|Byzantine]] rule. In [[middle ages|medieval era]] many conquerors passed from the island. In the thirteenth century [[Venice]] took the island from Byzantium. The Venetians remained in island for many years. The [[Turkey|Turks]] conquered the island for a brief period but the Venetians took the island back and held it until 1797. Then the Napoleon's [[France]] took the island and after the island passed in [[England]]. In 1864, the English gave Lefkada to Greece, along with the other Ionian islands.
