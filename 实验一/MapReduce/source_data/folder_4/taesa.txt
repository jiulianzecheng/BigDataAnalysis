'''TAESA''' was a [[Mexican]] [[low-cost carrier]]. It began on [[April 27]], [[1988]].  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://thesaurus.babylon.com/TAESA? & tl=|title=TAESA? & tl= synonym by Babylon's thesaurus|work=babylon.com|accessdate=3 July 2015}} < /ref >  It was one of the first low-cost airlines in [[Mexico]].  < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1992/11/13/business/mexico-s-little-airline-that-could.html|title=Mexico's Little Airline That Could|first=Agis|last=Salpukas|date=13 November 1992|publisher=|via=NYTimes.com}} < /ref >  At times, it became Mexico's 3rd largest [[airline]], after [[Aerom√©xico]] and [[Mexicana de Aviacion]]. On [[November 9]], [[1999]], a TAESA plane crashed.  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.airdisaster.com/photos/taesa725/photo.shtml|title=AirDisaster.Com: Accident Photo: TAESA 725|work=airdisaster.com|accessdate=3 July 2015}}{{Dead link|date=February 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It was flying from Uruapan to [[Mexico City International Airport]]. The crash killed 18 people.  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/18-killed-in-mexico-plane-crash/|title=18 Killed In Mexico Plane Crash|date=10 November 1999|work=cbsnews.com|accessdate=3 July 2015}} < /ref >  That crash and other factors led to TAESA declaring [[bankruptcy]] in February 2000.  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://amarillo.com/stories/2000/02/22/bus_airlines.shtml|title=Mexican airline files bankruptcy|work=amarillo.com|accessdate=3 July 2015}} < /ref >
