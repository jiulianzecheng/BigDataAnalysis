 '''Nuneaton''' is the largest town in the [[England|English]] county of [[Warwickshire]]. Nuneaton is famous for the novelist, [[George Eliot]], who was born on a farm outside Nuneaton in 1819 and lived in Nuneaton during her early life. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.online-literature.com/george_eliot/|title=George Eliot|publisher=online-literature.com|accessdate=2009-11-22}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The town is 14.5 & nbsp;km (9 miles) north of [[Coventry]], 32 & nbsp;km (20 miles) east of [[Birmingham]] and 166 & nbsp;km (103 miles) northwest of London. The ''River Anker'' runs through the town. 
 Nuneaton had a population of 70,721 as of 2001 < ref > 2001 census < /ref >  though the 2008 estimate is 73,000. 
 
 Nuneaton also has a football team called Nuneaton Town.
