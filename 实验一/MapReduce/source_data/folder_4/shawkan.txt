 '''Mahmoud Abou Zeid''' (Arabic محمود أبو زيد) or '''Shawkan''' (Arabic شوكان) is an [[Egypt]]ian photojournalist. His photographs were [[published]] in many countries. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/get-involved/take-action/journalism-is-not-a-crime-free-shawkan/|title=Egyptian photojournalist at risk of death penalty|publisher=|access-date=2016-04-23|archive-date=2016-04-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160405233327/https://www.amnesty.org/en/get-involved/take-action/journalism-is-not-a-crime-free-shawkan/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   He has been in [[prison]] without a [[trial]] since 2013, after he took photographs of a [[protest]] in [[Egypt]]. 
 
 == Work == 
 
 Many Egyptian news organizations used Shawkan's photos.  Shawkan's photos were in many [[international]] newspapers and magazines, including [[Time (magazine)|Time magazine]], [[The Sun (United Kingdom)|The Sun]], Bild (a German newspaper) and [[Die Zeit]].  In 2010, Shawkan went to work for Demotix, a [[photographer|photo]] [[agency]] in [[London]]. During the [[Arab Spring]], Shawkan took photos of the Tahir Square [[protest]]s in [[Cairo]]. < ref name= " roygreenslade " > {{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/greenslade/2014/jun/25/egypt-journalist-safety|title=The photojournalist held in an Egyptian jail for 10 months without charge|author=Roy Greenslade|work=the Guardian|date=25 June 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2013, Shawkan took photos of protests against the Egyptian president, Mohamed Morsi in [[Cairo]].  Police [[arrest]]ed him.   < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/campaigns/voicesindanger/top-egyptian-news-photographer-in-prison-for-over-800-days-without-trial-a6712206.html|title=Shawkan: top Egyptian news photographer in prison for over 800 days without trial|author=Robert Trafford|date=28 October 2015|work=The Independent}} < /ref >  Two other photographers were arrested at the same time: Louis Jammes, a [[French people|French]] [[photographer]] and Mike Giglio, a [[United States|US]] [[reporter]]. Jammes and Giglio are free but Shawkan is still in [[prison]]. < ref name= " roygreenslade " / > 
 
 In March 2015, Shawkan wrote a letter about his [[torture]] in prison.  Shawkan's trial was scheduled for April 23, 2016. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://globalvoices.org/2016/04/15/freeshawkan-after-900-days-in-jail-egyptian-photographer-finally-faces-trial/|title=#FreeShawkan: After 900 Days in Jail, Egyptian Photographer Finally Faces Trial · Global Voices|work=Global Voices|date=15 April 2016}} < /ref >   Shawkan went to court, but the court did not free him. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.theoslotimes.com/article/court-denies-shawkan's-release?fb_action_ids=10153976274691628 & fb_action_types=og.comments|title=Court denies Shawkan's release|publisher=}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >   The trial was changed to May 5. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.madamasr.com/news/after-nearly-1000-days-prison-hearing-adjourned-photojournalist-shawkan|title=After nearly 1000 days in prison, hearing adjourned for photojournalist Shawkan|work=Mada Masr|access-date=2016-04-23|archive-date=2016-04-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424073052/http://www.madamasr.com/news/after-nearly-1000-days-prison-hearing-adjourned-photojournalist-shawkan|url-status=dead}} < /ref >

 === The Shawkan Photo Awards === 
 
 In 2015 a photojournalism [[prize]], The Shawkan Photo Awards, was named for Shawkan.  The first winner was Islam Osama, who took a photo of the death of [[activist]] [[Shaimaa Sabbagh]]. < ref name= " middleeasteye.net " / > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://egyptianchronicles.blogspot.com/2015/12/shawkan-photo-awards-for-2015-egypts.html|title=Egyptian Chronicles|author=Zeinobia|date=31 December 2015|publisher=}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dailynewsegypt.com/2016/01/24/shawkan-photo-awards-competition-concluded-first-round/|title=Shawkan Photo Awards competition concluded first round|date=24 January 2016|publisher=}} < /ref > 
 
 == Campaign for freedom == 
 
 Shawkan was arrested on August 14, 2013 during protests against the government. More than 1000 people were killed and many protesters were arrested.  Two other journalists were arrested at the same time, one from France and one from the United States. The foreign journalists were freed quickly, but Shawkan was charged with [[weapon]]s possession, illegal assembly, [[murder]] and [[attempted murder]]. One of the other journalists, Mike Giglio, said Shawkan only had a camera. < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/23/world/middleeast/jailed-by-egypt-honored-for-his-photojournalism.html|title=Jailed by Egypt, Honored for His Photojournalism|first1=Russell|last1=Goldman|first2=Nour|last2=Youssef|newspaper=The New York Times|date=23 November 2016|publisher=}} < /ref > 
 
 Many organizations want freedom for Shawkan. The Committee to Protect Journalists made a statement. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dailynewsegypt.com/2016/02/06/shawkans-trial-one-outrage-after-the-other-cpj/|title=Shawkan's trial 'one outrage after the other': CPJ|date=6 February 2016|publisher=}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=https://cpj.org/blog/2015/04/egyptian-journalist-shawkan-on-his-600-days-in-pri.php|title=Egyptian journalist Shawkan on his 600 days in prison|date=10 April 2015|publisher=}} < /ref >   
 [[Amnesty International]] said he should be free. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2015/12/egypt-continued-detention-of-photojournalist-shawkan-for-more-than-800-days-is-an-affront-to-press-freedom/|title=Egypt|date=10 December 2015|publisher=}} < /ref >   Orla Guerin from the BBC, Patrick Kingsley from the Guardian, and Peter Greste from Al-Jazeera, who was also in prison in Egypt, wrote to the Egyptian president. < ref name= " middleeasteye.net " > {{cite web|url=http://www.middleeasteye.net/news/prize-celebrates-top-egyptian-photojournalism-2015-899357850|title=Prize celebrates top Egyptian photojournalism of 2015|work=Middle East Eye}} < /ref >
