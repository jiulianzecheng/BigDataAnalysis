 '''Solána Imani Rowe''' (born November 8, 1989 < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.thefader.com/2022/11/09/sza-psa-teaser | title=SZA teases new song  " PSA "  as she celebrates her 33rd birthday }} < /ref > ) known professionally as '''SZA''', is an American [[R & B]] singer-songwriter. She was born in [[St. Louis]], [[Missouri]], and raised in [[Maplewood, New Jersey]]. She began making music in the early 2010s. When she started her music career, she made two [[Extended play|EPs]]. After that, she signed with the [[hip hop]] record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]]. Through the signing, SZA released her third studio EP, ''Z'', in 2014. < ref > {{cite magazine | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6041313/sza-talks-z-album-being-the-only-girl-in-top-dawg-entertainment | title=SZA Talks 'Z' Album  &  Being the Only Girl in Top Dawg Entertainment | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] }} < /ref >  During that same year, she co-wrote  " [[Feeling Myself]] "  with [[Nicki Minaj]] and [[Beyoncé]]. In 2016, she was featured in [[Rihanna]]'s song  " [[Consideration (song)|Consideration]] " . 
 
 In 2017, she released her first [[studio album]], [[Ctrl (SZA album)|Ctrl]]. It reached number 3 on the [[Billboard 200]]. It was certified triple [[Platinum certification|platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). It was released to international praise from critics. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/ctrl/sza | title=CTRL by SZA | website=[[Metacritic]] }} < /ref >   
 
 Also in 2017, she collaborated with [[Maroon 5]] to make the Top 10 hit single  " [[What Lovers Do]] " .  
 
 The next year, in 2018, she collaborated with [[Kendrick Lamar]] to create the Top 10 single  " [[All The Stars]] "  for the [[Black Panther]] soundtrack.  " All The Stars "  was nominated for an [[Academy Award|Oscar]] for both artists. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.essence.com/entertainment/sza-kendrick-lamar-all-the-stars-oscar-nomination/ | title=All the Stars: SZA and Kendrick Lamar Are Now Oscar-Nominated }} < /ref > 
 
 In 2020, she released the single  " [[Good Days]] " . In 2021, she released the single  " [[I Hate U]] "  and collaborated with [[Doja Cat]] to make the song  " [[Kiss Me More]] " . All three singles reached the top 10 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100]]. < ref > {{cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9512991/morgan-wallen-sza-chris-brown-young-thug-hot-100-top-10/ | title=Morgan Wallen, SZA Hit Hot 100's Top 10, Chris Brown  &  Young Thug Rule Radio Songs | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/1/20/sza-reacts-to-landing-her-first-solo-billboard-hot-100-hit-g.html |title=SZA reacts to landing her first solo 'Billboard' Hot 100 hit,  " Good Days "  - Music News - ABC News Radio |access-date=2023-01-03 |archive-date=2021-05-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210502052953/http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/1/20/sza-reacts-to-landing-her-first-solo-billboard-hot-100-hit-g.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In 2022, SZA released her second studio album,  " SOS " . SOS reached number one on the Billboard 200. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://pitchfork.com/news/sza-scores-first-no-1-album-with-sos/ | title=SZA Scores First No. 1 Album with SOS | website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] | date=19 December 2022 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Awards and nominations== 
 SZA has earned several awards including a [[Grammy Award]], a [[Billboard Music Award]], an [[American Music Award]] and two [[MTV Video Music Award]] with nominations for an [[Academy Award]] and a [[Golden Globe Award]].
