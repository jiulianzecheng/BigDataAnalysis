 '''Topaz''' is a [[silicate mineral]]. Along with [[silicate]], it includes the [[chemical element]]s [[aluminium]] and [[fluorine]] with the [[chemical formula]] [[aluminium|Al]] < sub > 2 < /sub > [[silicon|Si]][[oxygen|O]] < sub > 4 < /sub > ([[fluorine|F]],[[hydroxide|OH]]) < sub > 2 < /sub > . 
 
 ==Color and varieties== 
 Pure topaz is colorless and transparent but they usually have colors made by small impurities. The [[wikt:typical|typical]] topaz is wine, yellow, pale gray, reddish-orange, or blue brown. It can also be made white, pale green, blue, gold, or pink (rare). 
 
 ''Orange topaz'', also known as [[wikt:precious|precious]] topaz, is the [[tradition]]al November [[birthstone]], the symbol of friendship, and the [[state]] [[gemstone]] for the [[US]] state of [[Utah]]. < ref > [http://pioneer.utah.gov/research/utah_symbols/gem.html Utah State Gem – Topaz] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121114180742/http://pioneer.utah.gov/research/utah_symbols/gem.html |date=2012-11-14 }}. Pioneer.utah.gov (2010-06-16). Retrieved on 2011-10-29. < /ref > 
 
 ''Imperial topaz'' is yellow, pink (rare, if natural) or pink-orange. Some imperial topaz stones can fade if they are [[wikt:expose|expose]]d too much in the [[sunlight]]. < ref > [http://www.nhm.org/research/minsci/impgem.htm Imperial Topaz] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090513005018/http://www.nhm.org/research/minsci/impgem.htm |date=2009-05-13 }}, [[Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County]] < /ref > < ref name=Emporia > [http://www.emporia.edu/earthsci/amber/go340/topaz.htm Gemstones  &  Gemology – Topaz] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110808013549/http://www.emporia.edu/earthsci/amber/go340/topaz.htm |date=2011-08-08 }}, [[Emporia State University]] < /ref > 
 
 ''Blue topaz'' is the US state [[Texas]]' gemstone. < ref > [https://web.archive.org/web/20090312074504/http://www.tsl.state.tx.us/ref/abouttx/symbols/gem.html State Gem – Texas Blue Topaz. State Gemstone Cut – Lone Star Cut]. state.tx.us < /ref > 
 
 ''Mystic topaz'' is colorless topaz which has been [[artificial]]ly coated, making it rainbow-colored. < ref > [http://www.farlang.com/art/mystic-topaz Mystic Topaz, Consumer Information]. Farlang.com (2008-10-30). Retrieved on 2011-10-29. < /ref >
