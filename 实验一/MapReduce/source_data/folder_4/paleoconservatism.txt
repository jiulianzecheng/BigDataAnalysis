'''Paleoconservatism''' (sometimes called '''paleocon''') is a [[conservatism|conservative]] idea that stresses [[tradition]], [[limited government]] and civil society, along with [[religion|religious]], national and [[Western world|Western]] identity. The idea is mainly found in the [[United States]]. 
 
 According to scholar [[Michael Foley (academic)|Michael Foley]], paleoconservatives want more restrictions on immigration, cuts in [[Multiculturalism|multicultural]] programs, [[protectionism]] (basically meaning that foreign goods will be taxed more), [[isolationism]], and to bring back traditional societal roles. 
 
 [[Paul Gottfried]] created the term in the 1980s. < ref > Mcdonald Wesley.  " Russell Kirk and the Prospects for Conservatism " , ''Humanitas'', Vol. 12, 1999. < /ref >  It was used to refer to conservative traditional [[Catholicism|Catholics]] and [[Agrarianism|agrarian]] [[Southern United States|Southerners]] who turned against [[Communism]] during the [[Cold War]].
