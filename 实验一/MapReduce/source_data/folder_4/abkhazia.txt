 '''Abkhazia'''  < ref > {{lang-ab|Аҧсны}} ''Apsny,'' {{Lang-ka|აფხაზეთი}} ''Apkhazeti'' or ''Abkhazeti, ''{{Lang-ru|Абха́зия}} ''Abkhazia'' < /ref >  is an unrecognised country in the Caucauses. 
 
 The country fought a war with Georgia for its independence in 1991, the [[Georgian–Abkhaz conflict]]. Since its [[declaration of independence]] from [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] in 1991, it has been ruled by the partly-recognized '''Republic of Abkhazia'''. < ref > Olga Oliker, Thomas S. Szayna.  The secessionist movement of the Abkhaz minority led to the Georgian–Abkhaz conflict. The War in Abkhazia resulted in a Georgian military defeat and the mass exodus and [[ethnic cleansing]] of the [[Georgians|Georgian]] population from Abkhazia. 
 
 There was a 1994 [[ceasefire]] agreement. There is a [[United Nations|UN]]-monitored and Russian-dominated [[Commonwealth of Independent States|CIS]] peacekeeping operation. The sovereignty dispute has not been resolved. The dispute is a source of conflict between Georgia and Russia.
