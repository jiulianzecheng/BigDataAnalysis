'''Nirban''', also spelt Narban or Nirwan, is a large [[gotra]] of [[Yaduvanshi caste|Yaduvanshi Ahirs]] of [[Haryana]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1QmrSwFYe60C & q=gots+of+the+Jadubanshis & pg=PA6|title=Glossary of the Tribes and Castes of the Punjab and North West Frontier Province|last1=Ibbetson|first1=Sir Denzil|last2=Maclagan|date=1990|publisher=Asian Educational Services|isbn=978-81-206-0505-3|language=en}} < /ref >  There are [[Village|villages]] of Nirban in [[Pataudi (princely state)|Pataudi]]. They an also be found in the [[Badli]] and [[Haidurpur]] villages of [[Delhi]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Yadava|first=J. S.|date=1971|title=History and Development of a Village Settlement in North India|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/481533|journal=Ethnohistory|volume=18|issue=3|pages=239–244|doi=10.2307/481533|jstor=481533 |issn=0014-1801}} < /ref > 
 
 Nirban is also a clan of [[Rajput|Rajputs]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yQi2AAAAIAAJ & q=nirban+clan|title=Proceedings of the ... Session|last=Commission|first=Indian Historical Records|date=1981|publisher=The Commission|language=en}} < /ref >  According to [[historian]] [[Henry Miers Elliot|Sir Henry Miers Elliot]], the Nirban gotra is also found in [[Nanda|Nandvanshi Ahirs]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mX4TAAAAYAAJ & dq=nirban+clan & pg=PA4|title=Memoirs on the History, Folk-lore, and Distribution of the Races of the North Western Provinces of India: Being an Amplified Edition of the Original Supplemental Glossary of Indian Terms|last1=Elliot|first1=Henry M.|last2=Elliot|first2=Sir Henry Miers|date=1869|publisher=Trübner  &  Company|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VYnlAAAAMAAJ & q=nirban|title=Hindu Tribes and Castes|last=Sherring|first=Matthew Atmore|date=1872|publisher=Thacker, Spink  &  Company|language=en}} < /ref >
