 '''Dio''' was an American [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band. It was founded by [[Ronnie James Dio]] in 1983 after he left [[Black Sabbath]]. He left the band because of an argument with [[Tony Iommi]] and [[Geezer Butler]] over the mixing of Live Evil and took Vinny Appice with him. He also left [[Black Sabbath|Sabbath]] because he wanted to make his own music.  < ref name= " news.com.au " > {{cite news|last=Goodman|first=Dean|date=26 October 2006|title=Black Sabbath reunites without Ozzy|publisher=[[News Limited#Websites|news.com.au]]|url=http://www.news.com.au/story/0,23599,20648014-1702,00.html|url-status=dead|access-date=October 13, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091005180655/http://www.news.com.au/story/0,23599,20648014-1702,00.html|archive-date=October 5, 2009}} < /ref >   He took Vinny Appice with him. Appice stayed in the band until 1988. He and [[Ronnie James Dio|Ronnie]] joined [[Black Sabbath|Sabbath]] again in 1992. They toured with [[Black Sabbath|Sabbath]] as well. They both left the band again, and made music until 1996. 
 
 The band made 10 [[Album|studio albums]]. Dio had many different members. Ronnie James Dio was the only person to stay in the band. Some of the people who have played in the band are [[Vivian Campbell]], [[Craig Goldy]], and Doug Aldrich. The band was put on hiatus in 2006 when Dio joined [[Heaven and Hell]]. They released one song and started work on a new album.Those plans were demolished after the band officially broke up in 2010 when Dio died from stomach cancer. He was 68 years old. 
 
 Dio sold more than 20 million albums.
