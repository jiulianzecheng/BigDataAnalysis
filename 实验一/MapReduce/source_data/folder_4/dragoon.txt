 '''Dragoons''' were originally a type of mounted [[infantry]]. They used horses to move around, but got off the horses to fight on foot. They were also used as [[cavalry]] and were trained to fight with swords on horseback. < ref > {{cite book|first=W.Y.|last=Carman|page=[https://archive.org/details/dictionaryofmili0000carm/page/48 48]|title=A Dictionary of Military Uniform|year=1977|url=https://archive.org/details/dictionaryofmili0000carm|isbn=0-684-15130-8}} < /ref >  The name is taken from a type of [[firearm]], called a  " dragon " . The dragon was a [[handgun]] version of a [[blunderbuss]]. It was carried by dragoons of the [[French Army]]. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.oed.com/view/Entry/57447?isAdvanced=false & result=1 & rskey=synm9O &  |title= Dragoon |website= Oxford English Dictionary |quote=  A kind of carbine or musket.}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite journal |url= https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/1911_Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica/Dragoon |title= Dragoon |journal= Encyclopædia Britannica |year= 1911 |quote=... & nbsp;took his name from his weapon, a species of carbine or short musket called the dragon.}} < /ref >  In the late 17th and early 18th centuries, most European armies had dragoons. In modern times, some [[armoured warfare|armoured]] or ceremonial mounted [[regiment|regiments]] still call themselves dragoons.
