'''Metformin''' is a drug used to treat patients having [[Diabetes mellitus|diabetes]]. There are 2 types of diabetes, [[Type 1 diabetes|type 1]] and [[Type 2 diabetes|type 2]] and metformin is only used to treat people having [[type 2 diabetes]]. Metformin come as a tablet and taken by mouth. It is the drug of choice to lower the blood sugar level when a person is first found to have diabetes. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Qaseem|first1=Amir|last2=Humphrey|first2=Linda L.|last3=Sweet|first3=Donna E.|last4=Starkey|first4=Melissa|last5=Shekelle|first5=Paul|date=2012-02-07|title=Oral Pharmacologic Treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus: A Clinical Practice Guideline From the American College of Physicians|url=https://www.acpjournals.org/doi/10.7326/0003-4819-156-3-201202070-00011|journal=Annals of Internal Medicine|volume=156|issue=3|pages=218–231|doi=10.7326/0003-4819-156-3-201202070-00011|pmid=22312141|s2cid=3703337|issn=0003-4819}} < /ref > 
 
 == Uses == 
 * Type-II diabetes - adult onset diabetes due to lack of [[insulin]] production and resistance of body tissues to insulin 
 * [[Gestational diabetes]] - diabetes during pregnancy 
 * To treat [[obesity]] in diabetes - metformin lowers the appetite and used to treat obesity < ref > {{Cite book|date=2017-09-08|title=Clinical Obesity in Adults and Children - Google Books|isbn=9781405143660|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qGVnItPoPCYC & pg=PA262|access-date=2020-09-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908185309/https://books.google.com/books?id=qGVnItPoPCYC & pg=PA262|archive-date=2017-09-08|last1=Kopelman|first1=Peter G.|last2=Caterson|first2=Ian D.|last3=Dietz|first3=William H.}} < /ref > 
 * [[Polycystic ovary syndrome]] (PCOS) - presence of multiple ( " poly " ) fluid filled cavities ( " cystic " ) in the ovaries of females < ref > {{Cite web|date=2016-12-24|title=Metformin Hydrochloride Monograph for Professionals - Drugs.com|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/metformin-hydrochloride.html|access-date=2020-09-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161224162245/https://www.drugs.com/monograph/metformin-hydrochloride.html|archive-date=2016-12-24}} < /ref > 
 
 == Side effects == 
 * [[Nausea]] 
 * [[Diarrhea]] 
 * [[Abdominal pain|Abdominal cramps]] 
 * [[Flatulence]] 
 * [[Lactic acidosis]] 
 
 Metformin is not used in patients having severe [[Renal disease|kidney disease]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Bolen|first1=Shari|last2=Feldman|first2=Leonard|last3=Vassy|first3=Jason|last4=Wilson|first4=Lisa|last5=Yeh|first5=Hsin-Chieh|last6=Marinopoulos|first6=Spyridon|last7=Wiley|first7=Crystal|last8=Selvin|first8=Elizabeth|last9=Wilson|first9=Renee|date=2007-09-18|title=Systematic Review: Comparative Effectiveness and Safety of Oral Medications for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus|url=https://www.acpjournals.org/doi/10.7326/0003-4819-147-6-200709180-00178|journal=Annals of Internal Medicine|volume=147|issue=6|pages=386–399|doi=10.7326/0003-4819-147-6-200709180-00178|pmid=17638715|s2cid=44380397|issn=0003-4819}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 Metformin was first produced from the [[French lilac]] or goat's rue (''[[Galega officinalis]]''). Its blood sugar-lowering property was first described in 1929 by [[Slotta]] and [[Tschesche]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Hadden|first=D. R.|date=October 2005|title=Goat's rue - French lilac - Italian fitch - Spanish sainfoin: gallega officinalis and metformin: the Edinburgh connection|url=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16402501/|journal=The Journal of the Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh|volume=35|issue=3|pages=258–260|issn=1478-2715|pmid=16402501}} < /ref >  Metformin became available in [[Great Britain|Britain]] in 1958 and got approval by the [[FDA|U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)]] for type 2 diabetes in 1994. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2007-09-29|title=|website=[[Food and Drug Administration]]|url=https://www.fda.gov/bbs/topics/ANSWERS/ANS00627.html|access-date=2020-09-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929152824/https://www.fda.gov/bbs/topics/ANSWERS/ANS00627.html|archive-date=2007-09-29}} < /ref >  Today, Metformin has become the world's most widely used medication to treat type 2 diabetes. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Bailey|first1=C.
