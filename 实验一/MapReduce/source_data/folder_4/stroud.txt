 '''Stroud''' is a market town and civil parish in the centre of [[Gloucestershire]], England. It is 16 kilometres south of [[Gloucester]], 24 kilometres south-southeast of [[Cheltenham]], 21 kilometres west-northwest of [[Cirencester]] and 42 kilometres northeast of [[Bristol]]. It is known for its steep streets and caf√© culture. 
 
 ==History== 
 Stroud Parish was originally part of [[Bisely]], until the church was assigned parochial rights by the rectors of Bisely in 1304. 
 
 It was heavily involved in the [[Industrial Revolution]] as a cloth town.
