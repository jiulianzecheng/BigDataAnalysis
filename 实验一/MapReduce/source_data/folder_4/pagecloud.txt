 == History == 
 PageCloud was founded by Craig Fitzpatrick in [[2014]] and officially launched in May [[2015]] by at TechCrunch Disrupt. Fitzpatrick founded the initial software after the sale of previous startup Source Metrics, where he became so reluctant with how difficult it was to build and run a [[website]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=PageCloud Wants To Simplify Website Creation|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2015/05/04/pagecloud-wants-to-simplify-website-creation/|access-date=2021-05-23|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}{{Dead link|date=July 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 == Product == 
 PageCloud is involved in designing companies who need websites with less than 30 pages. They have templates and a drag and drop [[HTML]] website builder that such as apps, [[graphics]], image galleries, fonts, and many more. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2018-11-18|title=Wipo Publishes Patent of Pagecloud for  " Methods and Systems for WYSIWYG Web Content Generation "  (Canadian Inventor) - US Fed News Service, Including US State News {{!}} HighBeam Research|url=https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P4-1867577647.html|access-date=2021-05-23|website=web.archive.org|archive-date=2018-11-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181118014321/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P4-1867577647.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=PageCloud founder's goal is to 'render products like WordPress obsolete'|url=https://financialpost.com/entrepreneur/fp-startups/pagecloud-founders-goal-is-to-render-products-like-wordpress-obsolete|access-date=2021-05-23|website=financialpost|language=en-CA}} < /ref >
