 '''Rhyl''' ({{lang-cy|Y Rhyl}}), pronounced ''Rill'', is a seaside [[resort]] and community in [[Denbighshire]] in North [[Wales]]. It is in the historic county of [[Flintshire (historic)|Flintshire]] and the [[Preserved counties of Wales|preserved county]] of [[Clwyd]]. In 2011, the town's [[population]] was 25,149. This makes it one of the largest settlements in North Wales. 
 
 The town was once an elegant [[Victorian era|Victorian]] resort. Influences from [[Liverpool]] and [[Manchester]], both cities in [[England]], after [[World War II]] changed the town. By 1990, the town had declined. However, since then the town has had major regeneration. 
 
 It has a number of Grade II [[listed building]]s. An example is the Parish Church of St Thomas in Bath Street. It is listed as Grade II*. 
 
 Very old documents refer to a small dwelling in the area called ''Ty'n Rhyl''. There is a manor house with that name that still exists in the town.
