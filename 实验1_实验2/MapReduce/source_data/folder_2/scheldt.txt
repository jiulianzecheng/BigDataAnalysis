 The '''Scheldt''' is a 435-kilometre-long (270 mi) river that flows through northern [[France]], western [[Belgium]], and the southwestern part of the [[Netherlands]], into the [[North Sea]]. In French, the river is called ''Escaut'', in Dutch and in German, it is called ''Schelde''.  Its latin name is ''Scaldis''.The Scheldt is an important [[waterway]].  Boats can get up to [[Cambrai]].  From there they can get on to the [[Canal de Saint-Quentin]].  The [[Scheldt–Rhine Canal]] connects it to the [[Rhine River|Rhine]].
