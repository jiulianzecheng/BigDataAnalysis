''eagle''), also known as '''Oriol''', < ref > {{cite web|title=Oriol, Russia|url=https://geographic.org/geographic_names/name.php?uni=-4168283 & fid=5539 & c=russia|access-date=26 October 2020|website=Geographical Names|publisher=National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency}} < /ref >  is the largest [[city]] in and the [[administrative center]] of [[Oryol Oblast]]. It is on the [[Oka River]], around {{convert|368|km|sp=us}} south-southwest of [[Moscow]]. It is part of the [[Central Federal District]] and the [[Central Economic Region]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Oryol was a [[fortress]] town in Russia for several centuries. It was [[Looting|looted]] by Polish forces in [[1611]] and [[1615]]. It was rebuilt in 1636. The fortress was taken apart in the 18th century. 
 
 During [[WW2]], the city was almost fully destroyed during [[Operation Kutuzov]]. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Oryol is on the banks of the [[Oka River]] and its [[tributary]] the Orlik river. It is in the [[Central Russian Upland]] of the [[East European Plain]].
