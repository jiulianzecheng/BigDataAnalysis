 '''''Xenoturbella''''' is a [[genus]] of [[bilateria]]n animals; it contains two [[Ocean|marine]] [[worm]]-like [[species]]. The first known species was discovered in 1915 but was only described in 1949. < ref > Westblad 1949. ''Arkiv. Zoologi.'' '''1''':3-29 < /ref >  A 2003 [[DNA]] study has shown it is a [[primitive]] [[deuterostome]] [[phylum]]. < ref name= " B2003 " > Bourlat S.J. ''et al.'' 2003.  ''Xenoturbella'' has a very simple body plan: it has no [[brain]], no through [[Alimentary canal|gut]], no [[excretion|excretory system]], no organized [[gonad]]s (but does have [[gamete]]s), or any other [[organ (anatomy)|organs]] except for a [[statocyst]] containing [[flagella]]ted cells. It has [[cilia]] and a diffuse [[nervous system]]. The animal is up to {{convert|4|cm}} long, and has been found off the coasts of [[Sweden]], [[Scotland]] and [[Iceland]]. < ref > [http://www.wellcome.ac.uk/News/Media-office/Press-releases/2003/WTD002847.htm Enigmatic worm identified as mankind's long lost relative] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101009085224/http://www.wellcome.ac.uk/News/Media-office/Press-releases/2003/WTD002847.htm |date=2010-10-09 }} – Accessed January 3, 2008 < /ref > 
 
 The association of specimens of ''Xenoturbella'' with [[mollusc]] larva has led many to suggest that they are molluscivores. However, another idea is that the ''Xenoturbella'' [[larva]]l stage develops as an internal [[parasite]] of certain molluscs. < ref > [http://www.palaeos.com/Invertebrates/Deuterostomia/80A000Ambulacraria.html#Xenoturbella ''Xenoturbella'' & nbsp;– Back to the basics] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071210130540/http://www.palaeos.com/Invertebrates/Deuterostomia/80A000Ambulacraria.html#Xenoturbella |date=2007-12-10 }} – Accessed January 3, 2008 < /ref > 
 
 The genus ''Xenoturbella'' contains two species: 
 * ''Xenoturbella bocki'' 
 * ''Xenoturbella westbladi''
