 '''Dollarama''' is a [[dollar store]] chain. The chain was created by Larry Rossy in 1992. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dollarama.com/about_us/|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=About Us|publisher=Dollarama}} < /ref >  The first Dollarama was opened at the shopping centre  " Les promenades du St-Laurent "  in [[Matane]], [[Quebec]] in April, 1992. < ref name= " history " > {{cite web|url=http://www.dollarama.com/about_us/our_history/|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Our History|publisher=Dollarama|archive-date=2011-06-29|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110629143827/http://www.dollarama.com/about_us/our_history/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The discount retailer grew to 44 stores. < ref > {{cite web | url=https://rossy.ca/en/about-us/ | title=About Us }} < /ref > 
 
 Many items are priced at $1.00 or less. In early 2009, Dollarama began to introduce items priced up to $2.00 (including $1.25 and $1.50 price points).Dollarama has always had a No Return and No Exchange Policy. 
 
 In November 2004, 80 percent of Dollarama was sold to a [[private equity fund]], [[Bain Capital]], of [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]] for $850 million [[United States dollar|US]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.canada.com/nationalpost/financialpost/story.html?id=7dcebed2-ab61-413f-bf93-19b08cd945d9|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Dollarama undergoes major transformation|publisher=[[National Post]]|archive-date=2006-06-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060616160301/http://www.canada.com/nationalpost/financialpost/story.html?id=7dcebed2-ab61-413f-bf93-19b08cd945d9|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
