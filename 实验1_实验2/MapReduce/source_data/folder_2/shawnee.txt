 The '''Shawnee people''' are a Native American tribe that originated in [[Tennessee]]. They are part of the [[Indigenous peoples of the Northeastern Woodlands|Northeast Woodlands]].   
 
 ==History== 
 ===1800s=== 
 On November 7, 1825, a [[treaty]] was signed to move the Shawnee people from [[Ohio]] to [[Kansas Territory|Kansas]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://dc.library.okstate.edu/digital/collection/kapplers/id/26103/rec/2|title=Treaty with the Shawnee, 1825, Article 5, Page 264|date=November 7, 1825|publisher=Oklahoma State University|access-date=May 8, 2020}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Federal recognition== 
 In 2000 the  " Loyal "  or  " Cherokee "  Shawnee were [[federal recognition|recognized]] as being different from the Cherokee Nation. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/106/s3019/text|title=Text of S. 3019 (106th): Shawnee Tribe Status Act of 2000 (Introduced version)|website=GovTrack.us|publisher=Civic Impulse|date=September 7, 2000|access-date=May 8, 2020}} < /ref >  They are now known as the  " Shawnee Tribe " . 
 
 ==Language== 
 The Shawnee language, an [[Algonquian languages|Algonquian language]], was spoken by 200 people in 2002, including over 100 [[Absentee Shawnee]] and 12 [[Loyal Shawnee]] speakers. The language is written in the [[Latin script]]. It has a dictionary and parts of the [[Bible]] were translated into Shawnee. < ref > {{cite web|title=Shawnee|url=https://www.ethnologue.com/language/sjw|website=Ethnologue|access-date=28 April 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 == Famous leaders == 
 A famous leader of the Shawnee tribe is [[Tecumseh]].
