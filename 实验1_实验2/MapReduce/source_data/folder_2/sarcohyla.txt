 '''''Sarcohyla''''' is a genus of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  These frogs live in [[Mexico]] and the hills and mountains between Durango in the north and Guerrero in the south. These frogs live in habitats that humans have not changed too much, near streams in pine-oak forests between {{convert|1500|and|3100|m|abbr=on}} [[above sea level]]. < ref name=Frost/ >  The name comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] ''sarkodes'' meaning  " fleshy "  and from ''[[Hylas]]''. It is for the thick, glandular skin of most of the frogs in the genus. < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 ''Sarcohyla'' are medium sized or large frogs. They have thick skin with glands. They have large [[thumb]]s and no projecting spine. The alary process of the [[premaxilla]] does not split into two pieces like it does in some other frogs. < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / > 
 
 ==Taxonomy== 
 Scientists first invented ''Sarcohyla'' in 2016 using molecular data. The species in ''Sarcohyla'' had been in the ''[[Sarcohyla bistincta|Hyla bistincta]]'', ''[[Sarcohyla arborescandens|Hyla arborescandens]]'' < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / >  and ''Plectrohyla bistincta'' groups. < ref name= " Campbell et al. 2018 " / >  ''Sarcohyla'''s [[sister group]] is ''[[Plectrohyla]]''. < ref name= " Duellman et al. 2016 " / >  Not all species in ''Sarcohyla'' were included in the molecular analysis, so not all scientists are sure the genus is monophyletic. < ref name=Frost/ >
