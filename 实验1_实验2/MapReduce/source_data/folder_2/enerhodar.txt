 '''Enerhodar''' ({{IPAc-en|ɛ|n|ə|r|ˈ|h|oʊ|d|ɑː|r}}; {{lang-uk|Енергода́р|translit=Enerhodár}}, {{IPA-uk|enerɦoˈdɑr|pron}}, {{lit|energy's gift}}; {{lang-ru|Энергодар|translit=Energodar}}) is a [[List of cities in Ukraine|city]] and [[List of hromadas of Ukraine|municipality]] in the north-western part of [[Zaporizhzhia Oblast]] in [[Ukraine]]. The city was captured by the Russian Armed Forces during the [[battle of Enerhodar]] and is currently under their control. < ref > {{Cite web |date=2022-03-07 |title=Ukraine after 11th night of war: Mayor killed, towns taken, Moscow promises civilian corridors to Russia |url=https://bnn-news.com/ukraine-after-11th-night-of-war-mayor-killed-towns-taken-moscow-promises-civilian-corridors-to-russia-233013 |access-date=2022-05-08 |website=Baltic News Network - News from Latvia, Lithuania, Estonia |language=en-US}} < /ref >  Its population is about 52,887 as of 2021. 
 
 Enerhodar was founded on June 12, 1970, to build and maintain the [[Zaporizhzhia thermal power station]]. During the 1980s, Europe's biggest [[nuclear power plant]], the [[Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant]], was built there. Many people work at these two power stations.
