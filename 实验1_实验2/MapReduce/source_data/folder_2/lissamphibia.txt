 The [[subclass]] '''Lissamphibia''' includes all living [[amphibia]]ns. They are [[biological classification|classified]] in three [[Order (biology)|orders]]: 
 *the [[Anura]]: [[frog]]s, including [[toad]]s  
 *the [[Caudata]] or Urodela: [[salamander]]s, including [[newt]]s 
 *the [[Gymnophiona]] or Apoda: the limbless [[caecilian]]s.  
 
 Although the ancestry of each group is controversial, < ref > Carroll, Robert 2009. ''The rise of amphibians: 300 million years of evolution''. Johns Hopkins University Press. ISBN 978-0-8018-9140-3 < /ref >  all share certain common characteristics. This indicates they evolved from a common ancestor and so form a [[clade]].
