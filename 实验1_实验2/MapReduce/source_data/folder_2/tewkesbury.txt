 '''Tewkesbury''' is a town and civil parish in northern [[Gloucestershire]], near the border with [[Worcestershire]]. 
 
 The battle of Tewkesbury was fought On 4th May 1471 and was one of the decisive battles of the [[Wars of the Roses]]. 
 
 The [[River Severn]] and [[River Avon|Avon]] flow by Tewkesbury. 
 
 == Transport == 
 The [[M5 motorway|M5]] and [[M50 motorway (Great Britain)|M50]] motorways and A38 and A46 trunk roads serve Tewkesbury. There are frequent bus rides to the area. 
 
 Tewkesbury is served by [[Ashchurch for Tewkesbury railway station]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Ashchurch and Tewkesbury District Rail Promotion Group|url=http://ashchurchandtewkesburyrail.webs.com/index.htm|access-date=28 May 2018|archive-date=11 August 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110811220452/http://ashchurchandtewkesburyrail.webs.com/index.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It was the last mainline station in Gloucestershire to be reopened after [[British Rail]] was fragmented into [[Railtrack]].
