 '''Yugoslavs'''  is a national name used by a small number of [[South Slavs]] across the countries of the former [[Yugoslavia]]. Some say this name is for all people of South Slav heritage, including those of [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]], Bulgaria, [[Croatia]], Montenegro, [[Serbia]] and the presently disputed region of [[Kosovo]], Slovenia, and [[North Macedonia]]. < ref > Lenard J. Cohen. ''Broken bonds: Yugoslavia's disintegration and Balkan politics in transition''. 2nd edition. Boulder, Colorado, USA: Westview Press, 1995. Pp. 4. < /ref >
