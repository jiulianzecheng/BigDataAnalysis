'''Dyslexia''', or '''word blindness''', is a [[learning disability]] that affects reading. < ref name=ninds1 > {{cite web |url=https://www.ninds.nih.gov/Disorders/All-Disorders/Dyslexia-Information-Page |title=Dyslexia Information Page |date=2 November 2018 |publisher=National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite journal | vauthors = Siegel LS | title = Perspectives on dyslexia | journal = Paediatrics  &  Child Health | volume = 11 | issue = 9 | pages = 581–7 | date = November 2006 | pmid = 19030329 | pmc = 2528651 | doi = 10.1093/pch/11.9.581 }} < /ref >  Different people are affected to different degrees. The condition that makes it difficult to learn and understand things in the same way others do. It is a very common problem. 
 
 Dyslexia affects the way the [[brain]] understands words. The most common signs of dyslexia are [[reading]] and [[writing]] problems. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Leseyane|first=Monicca|last2=Mandende|first2=Peter|last3=Makgato|first3=Mary|last4=Cekiso|first4=Madoda|date=2018-03-05|title=Dyslexic learners’ experiences with their peers and teachers in special and mainstream primary schools in North-West Province|url=https://ajod.org/index.php/ajod/article/view/363|journal=African Journal of Disability|language=English|volume=7|issue=0|pages=7|doi=10.4102/ajod.v7i0.363|issn=2226-7220|pmc=5843944|pmid=29535918|via=AJOD}} < /ref >   
 
 Estimates are that in the [[United States]] between 5 and 9% of school children have dyslexia.  ==Characteristics== 
 A person can have dyslexia even if they are [[Intelligence|intelligent]]. Their [[education]] might be affected by their dyslexia or not. Recent studies show there are some [[small business]] owners that have dyslexia. Researchers think many dyslexic entrepreneurs are successful because they can [[delegate]] responsibilities (of writing letters) and still be good at speaking. < ref > {{cite news|author=Brent Bowers|date=December 6, 2007|title=Tracing Business Acumen to Dyslexia|work=New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/12/06/business/06dyslexia.html}} Cites a study by Julie Logan, professor of entrepreneurship at  Cass Business School in London, among other literature. < /ref >

 ==Therapy== 
 Children with dyslexia can be helped. One way teachers help dyslexic students is to break words into different sounds. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Knight|first=Cathryn|date=2018|title=What is dyslexia? An exploration of the relationship between teachers' understandings of dyslexia and their training experiences|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/dys.1593|journal=Dyslexia|language=en|volume=24|issue=3|pages=207–219|doi=10.1002/dys.1593|pmc=6099274|pmid=30019501|via=Wiley Online Library}} < /ref >  The student must learn how to write the different sounds and create words. This helps with reading and writing. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Kirby|first=John R.|last2=Silvestri|first2=Robert|last3=Allingham|first3=Beth H.|last4=Parrila|first4=Rauno|last5=La Fave|first5=Chantal B.|date=2008|title=Learning Strategies and Study Approaches of Postsecondary Students With Dyslexia|url=http://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0022219407311040|journal=Journal of Learning Disabilities|language=en|volume=41|issue=1|pages=85–96|doi=10.1177/0022219407311040|issn=0022-2194|via=SAGE Journals}} < /ref >
