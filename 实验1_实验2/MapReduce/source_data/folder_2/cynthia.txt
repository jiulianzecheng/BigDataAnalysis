'''Cynthia''' is a female [[given name]] that comes from [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''kynthia''. It is pronounced [ '''sin'''-thee-''uh'' ]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Cynthia {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/cynthia|access-date=2023-03-16|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  There are several different versions for this name. One short version is ''Cindy''. Cynthia is a Greek-origin name which translates to  " from Mount Kynthos " . It is derived from the Greek goddess of the moon, also referred to as Cynthia, and has become synonymous with the moon and the night sky. The name frequently indicates beauty, grace, and elegance. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Cynthia name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Cynthia-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-16|website=babys.name}} < /ref > This name was ranked #806 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/|access-date=2023-03-16|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 There are many well known people under the name, including [[Cynthia McKinney]] and [[Cynthia Nixon]]. The short version, ''Cindy'', is being used for [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic Basin]] [[tropical cyclone]]s.
