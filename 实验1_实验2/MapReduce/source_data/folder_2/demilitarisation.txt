'''Demilitarisation''' or '''demilitarization''' may mean the lowering of state armed forces. It is the opposite of [[militarisation]]. < ref name= " LachenmannWolfrum2017 " > {{cite book|author1=Frauke Lachenmann|author2=Rüdiger Wolfrum|title=The Law of Armed Conflict and the Use of Force: The Max Planck Encyclopedia of Public International Law|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=boWuDQAAQBAJ & pg=PA327|year=2017|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-878462-3|pages=327–}} < /ref >  For instance, the demilitarisation of Northern Ireland caused a lowering of British security and military weapons. < ref > {{Cite book|title=The State of Loyalism in Northern Ireland|url=https://archive.org/details/stateloyalismnor00spen_484|url-access=limited|last=Spencer|first=Graham|publisher=Palgrave Macmillan|year=2008|isbn=9781349542246|location=New York|pages=[https://archive.org/details/stateloyalismnor00spen_484/page/n160 148]}} < /ref >  Another example is the demilitarisation of the [[Rhineland]] after World War 1. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Rhineland {{!}} Facts, History,  &  Significance {{!}} Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Rhineland|access-date=2023-11-06|website=www.britannica.com|language=en}} < /ref >  Demilitarisation in this sense is usually the result of a [[peace treaty]] ending a war or a major conflict.
