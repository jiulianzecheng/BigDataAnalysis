 '''Philology''' considers both [[morphology (linguistics)|form]] and [[Meaning (linguistics)|meaning]] in [[writing]], such as text or [[manuscript]], and linguistic expression. It combines [[linguistics]] and [[literary studies]]. < ref > {{cite book| last = Ziolkowski| first = Jan| title = On Philology| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Z5LOraIe6pkC & pg=PA7| year = 2010| publisher = Penn State Press| isbn = 978-0-271-04462-0| page = 7 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| last = Peile| first = John| title = Philology| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=2joVAAAAYAAJ| year = 1880| publisher = Macmillan| isbn =  }} < /ref > 
 
 [[Classical philology]] is the philology of the [[Ancient Greek|Greek]], [[Latin]] and [[Sanskrit]] languages. < ref > [http://www.ucr.ac.cr/programas_grado.php University of Costa Rica] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080430202955/http://www.ucr.ac.cr/programas_grado.php |date=2008-04-30 }} (link in Spanish) has a career in Classical philology that includes Sanskrit, Latin and Greek. < /ref > 
 
 Any [[classical language]] can be studied philologically, and indeed describing a language as  " classical "  is to [[:wikt:imply|imply]] the existence of a philological tradition associated with it.
