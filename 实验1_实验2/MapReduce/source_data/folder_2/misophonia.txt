'''Misophonia''' (meaning  " hatred of sound " ) is a rarely [[Diagnosis|diagnosed]] [[mental disorder]]. It is a condition where certain [[sound]]s can cause someone to be [[angry]] or en[[rage]]d. < ref name=LearnerNYT > {{cite web |url=http://well.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/02/23/please-stop-making-that-noise/?_r=0 |title=Please Stop Making That Noise |author=Barron H. Learner |date=23 February 2015 |work=The New York Times |accessdate=28 February 2015}} < /ref >  The sounds could be as simple as someone chewing food with their mouth open. < ref name=LearnerNYT/ >  Other [[example]]s include a [[ballpoint pen]] clicking (repeatedly), [[wikt:tap|tap]]ping, [[typing]] and other common sounds. < ref name=KimWP > {{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/health-science/misophonia-is-a-newly-identified-condition-for-people-hypersensitive-to-sound/2014/12/01/7c392782-69ba-11e4-a31c-77759fc1eacc_story.html |title=Misophonia is a newly identified condition for people hypersensitive to sound |author=Meeri N. Kim |date=1 December 2014 |website= |newspaper=The Washington Post |accessdate=28 February 2015}} < /ref >  The disorder has only been [[wikt:identify|identified]] and [[name]]d in the last 20 years. < ref name=LearnerNYT/ >  According to one sufferer,  " It’s like a [[Fight or flight response|fight-or-flight response]]: Your [[muscle]]s get tense, you’re on edge, your [[heart]] races, and you feel the urge to flee " . < ref name=KimWP/ >  Misophonia is often mis[[diagnose]]d as one of several [[psychological]] disorders. < ref name=DeutschABC > {{cite web |url=http://abcnews.go.com/blogs/health/2012/05/17/do-you-have-misophonia/ |title=Do You Have Misophonia? |author=Gail Deutsch |date=17 May 2012 |website= |publisher=ABC News Internet Ventures |accessdate=28 February 2015}} < /ref >  Sufferers often keep away from trigger sounds. There is no cure but it can be treated. < ref name=DeutschABC/ > 
 
 People with misophonia have different amounts of tolerance for triggering sounds. The presence of misophonia may be determined by specific diagnostic criteria. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Dozier|first=Thomas H.|last2=Lopez|first2=Michelle|last3=Pearson|first3=Christopher|date=2017-11-14|title=Proposed Diagnostic Criteria for Misophonia: A Multisensory Conditioned Aversive Reflex Disorder|url=http://journal.frontiersin.org/article/10.3389/fpsyg.2017.01975/full|journal=Frontiers in Psychology|volume=8|doi=10.3389/fpsyg.2017.01975|issn=1664-1078|pmc=5694628|pmid=29184520}} < /ref >   They all, however, result in anger and irritability. Many also have trouble focusing on normal activities while exposed to triggering sounds. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2022-01-10|title=Misophonia: The Hatred of Sound Explained|url=https://quietliving.co.uk/misophonia/|access-date=2022-03-09|website=Quiet Living|language=en-GB}} < /ref > 
 
 The cause of misophonia is not know. Some studies say that it affects more than 5% of the population.{{cn|date=March 2022}} 
 
 == Symptoms of Misophonia == 
 
 * Repulsion to Specific Sounds 
 * Emotional Response 
 * Physical Response 
 * Avoidance
