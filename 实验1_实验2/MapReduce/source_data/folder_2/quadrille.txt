 The '''quadrille''' was a type of social dance. It was a [[sequence dance]], a lively dance by four couples arranged in the shape of a square. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.credoreference.com/entry/harvdictmusic/quadrille_fr_from_sp_cuadrilla_a_group_of_horsemen |title=Quadrille|first= |last= |work=The Harvard Dictionary of Music|year=2003 |accessdate=13 July 2011}} < /ref >  It became popular in [[France]] around 1760. It became popular in [[England]] in 1813. American square dancing is a later adaption of the quadrille. In [[Australia]], the [[bushranger]] Ned Kelly danced in a quadrille during the [[siege]] at [[Glenrowan, Victoria]]. < ref > {{cite book |last= McMenomy |first= Keith |title=Ned Kelly - The authentic illustrated story |publisher= Currey O'Neill Ross Pty Ltd |date=1984 |location=South Yarra, Victoria |page=161 |url= |isbn =085902122X }} < /ref >
