The '''Nahe''' is a [[river]] in [[Rhineland-Palatinate]], [[Germany]], a left [[tributary]] to the [[Rhine]]. It is also a famous [[wine region]] of [[Germany]]. 
 
 The name Nahe comes from the Latin word ''Nava'', which is of Celtic origin and means ''the wild river''. The Nahe separates the northern part of the [[Palatinate (region)|Palatinate]] from the [[Hunsr√ºck]].
