 '''''Sporosarcina''''' is a [[genus (biology)|genus]] of [[bacteria]]. 
 
 The cells of ''Sporosarcina'' are either shaped like a rod or like a bowl (coccoid). Most [[species]] are able to move (motile). < ref name= " Bergey " > {{cite book|last=Garrity|first=George M.  |display-authors = etal |title=Bergey's Manual of Systematic Bacteriology: the Firmicutes, Volume 3.|year=2009 }}pp. 377â€“378 < /ref >  All species of ''Sporosarcina'' are capable of [[aerobic respiration]] using [[oxygen]]. < ref > {{cite book|last=Margulis, L.|first=Chapman, M.|title=Kingdoms and Domains: An Illustrated Guide to the Phyla of Life on Earth|year=2009}} p. 92 < /ref >  Some species have the [[enzyme]] [[urease]] and can break down [[urea]]. < ref name= " Bergey "  / >  The species [[Sporosarcina ureae|''S. ureae'']] is common in the [[soil]] where [[cattle]] live.
