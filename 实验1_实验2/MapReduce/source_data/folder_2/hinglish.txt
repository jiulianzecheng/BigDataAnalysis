'''Hinglish''' (हिंगलिश) is a [[language]] that combines [[word]]s from the [[English language]] with those of [[South Asia]]. < ref name= " CoughlanBBC " > {{cite web|author=Sean Coughlan|first=BBC|title=It's Hinglish, innit?|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/magazine/6122072.stm|access-date=8 November 2006|publisher=BBC News Magazine}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}} < /ref >  The name Hinglish itself is a [[Portmanteau word|portmanteau]] of  " Hindi "  and  " English " . < ref name=Econ > {{cite web |url=http://www.economist.com/blogs/johnson/2012/08/hinglish |title=Not same, very different |publisher=The Economist Newspaper Limited |access-date=13 October 2015}} < /ref >  It is spoken on the [[Indian subcontinent]] and the [[United Kingdom]]. It borrows words from other languages such as [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]], [[Urdu]] and [[Hindi]]. < ref name=CoughlanBBC/ >  At first it was spoken only by what Indians refer to as  " ABCDs "  (American-Born Confused Desi). < ref name=BaldaufCSM > {{cite web |url=http://www.csmonitor.com/2004/1123/p01s03-wosc.html |title=A Hindi-English jumble, spoken by 350 million |author=Scott Baldauf |date=23 November 2004 |publisher=[[The Christian Science Monitor]] |access-date=13 October 2015}} < /ref >  Now it is spoken by about 350 [[million]] speakers. < ref name=BaldaufCSM/ >  It is heard on [[television]] and in [[advertisement]]s in India. [[Coca-Cola]]'s ad in Hinglish is  " Life ho to aisi "  (Life should be like this). [[Pepsi]]'s ad is  " Yeh Dil Maange More "  (the heart wants more). [[Domino's Pizza]] asks  " Hungry kya? "  (Are you hungry?). < ref name=BaldaufCSM/ >  It has become a [[hybrid]] language many Indians speak naturally and without giving it any thought. < ref name=Econ/ >   
 
 The first book written in Hinglish, ''All we need is Love'', is by Richa Devesar. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/chandigarh/Tricity-gives-birth-to-first-novel-in-Hinglish/articleshow/46569733.cms |title=Tricity gives birth to first novel in Hinglish |author=Shivalika Bhateja |date=15 March 2015 |publisher=The Times of India |access-date=13 October 2015}} < /ref >  It was published in March of 2015.
