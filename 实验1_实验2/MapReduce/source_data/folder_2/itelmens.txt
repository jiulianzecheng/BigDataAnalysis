 The '''Itelmens''' (Itelmen: Итәнмән, Russian: Ительмены) are a native population of the [[Kamchatka Peninsula]] in [[Russia]]. Itelmen call themselves ''itenme'n-itelmen'', which means  " an inhabitant of dry land " , or  " a human being. " < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=The Red Book of the Peoples of the Russian Empire: Itelmens|url=https://www.eki.ee/books/redbook/itelmens.shtml|access-date=1 March 2022|website=www.eki.ee}} < /ref >  They are also called [[Kamchadals|Kamchadal]]. They are related to the [[Chukchi people|Chukchi]] and [[Koryak people|Koryak]] peoples. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Itelmen|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Itelmen|access-date=28 February 2022|website=www.britannica.com}} < /ref > 
 
 == Language == 
 Itelmen spoke the [[Itelmen language]]. It is a [[Chukotsko-Kamchatsky languages|Chukotsko-Kamchatsky language]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Itelmen|url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/humanities/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/itelmen|access-date=28 February 2022|website=www.encyclopedia.com}} < /ref >  As of 2016, there are only about 5 native speakers of the language alive, and it is an [[endangered language]]. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite web|last=Degai|first=Tatiana S.|date=2016|title= " ITƏNMƏN " --  " The One Who Exists " : Sociolinguistic Life of the Itelmen in Kamchatka, Russia in the Context of Language Loss and Language Revitalization|url=https://repository.arizona.edu/handle/10150/621442|access-date=2022-03-01|website=repository.arizona.edu|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Most Itelmen now speak [[Russian language|Russian]]. There have been efforts to [[Language revival|bring the language back]] since the 1980s. < ref name= " :1 "  / > 
 
 == Pre-contact life == 
 Kamchadal is the general term for the native peoples of [[Kamchatka Krai]]. This includes the Itelmen. Itelmen were called Kamchadals by Russian [[Settler|settlers]] until 1927. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Murashko|first=Olga|date=1997|title=Itelmens and Kamchadals: Marriage Patterns and Ethnic History|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/40316432|journal=Arctic Anthropology|volume=34|issue=1|pages=181–193|jstor=40316432 |issn=0066-6939}} < /ref >  They were fishermen and fur hunters. They were also [[Herbal medicine|herbalists]], which means they used [[Plant|plants]] like [[Herb|herbs]] as [[medicine]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 == Russian contact == 
 Itelmens were being [[Religious conversion|converted to Christians]] as early as the 1730s. < ref name= " :2 " > {{Cite journal|last=Koester|first=David|date=2005|title=Global Movements and Local Historical Events: Itelmens of Kamchatka Appeal to the United Nations|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/3805353|journal=American Ethnologist|volume=32|issue=4|pages=642–659|doi=10.1525/ae.2005.32.4.642 |jstor=3805353 |issn=0094-0496}} < /ref > 
 
 The population of the native peoples of Kamchatka declined from 1769-1770. There were hundreds of Itelmen settlements in the 18th century, but there were only seven Itelmen-speaking [[Village|villages]] by the 20th century. < ref name= " :2 "  / >  Russian became the main language, and Russians spread [[Disease|diseases]] to the Itelmen, like [[smallpox]]. They also suffered from [[famine]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Murashko|first1=Olga|last2=Krupnik|first2=Igor I.|last3=Davis|first3=Eugenia W.|date=1994|title=A Demographic History of the Kamchadal/Itelmen of Kamchatka Peninsula: Modeling the Precontact Numbers and Postcontact Depopulation|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/40316361|journal=Arctic Anthropology|volume=31|issue=2|pages=16–30|jstor=40316361 |issn=0066-6939}} < /ref > 
 
 Kamchadal people suffered from Russian rule. [[Cossacks]] kept Itelmen as [[Slavery|slaves]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/49844675|title=Steller's history of Kamchatka: collected information concerning the history of Kamchatka, its peoples, their manners, names, lifestyle, and various customary practices|last1=Steller|first1=Georg Wilhelm|last2=Falk|first2=Marvin W|date=2003|isbn=978-1-889963-49-5|oclc=49844675}} < /ref >  Some Itelmen who fought against the Russians were [[Deportation|deported]], or had their [[Tribe|tribes]] separated. < ref name= " :0 "  / >
