 '''Sorbet''',  or '''sherbet''', is a cold [[dessert]] made with [[water]] or [[fruit juice]] instead of [[milk]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.epicurious.com/cooking/how_to/food_dictionary/entry?id=4541|title=Sherbet definition in the Food Dictionary at Epicurious.com|publisher=|access-date=2009-10-23|archive-date=2006-12-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061215121017/http://www.epicurious.com/cooking/how_to/food_dictionary/entry?id=4541|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   Most sorbets have fruit flavors. [[Lemon]] is the most common flavor, and other flavors include [[Orange (fruit)|orange]], [[lemon]], [[cherry]], [[lime]], [[wine|red wine]].  [[Rainbow]] is a mix of different-colored flavors, usually including lemon and cherry. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.fosselmans.com/Pages/flavors.html |title=Fosselman's Flavors |access-date=2009-10-23 |archive-date=2009-10-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091020185858/http://www.fosselmans.com/Pages/flavors.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://baskinrobbins.com/IceCream/classicflavors3.aspx |title=Baskin-Robbins Ice Cream Classics |access-date=2009-10-23 |archive-date=2009-10-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091008000938/http://www.baskinrobbins.com/icecream/classicflavors3.aspx |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 Sorbet is a [[French language|French]] name, but the origin of the dessert is not known for sure. It has been around for several centuries. Before [[refrigeration]] frozen ice stores were used. Claims have been made that the idea was known in ancient [[Persia]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IJCVgTGfxf0C & dq=earliest+ice+cream+persian+empire & pg=PT60|title=Book of Firsts|publisher=RW Press|isbn=9781909284296|language=en|quote=c. 550-330 BC, First mention of flavoured snow or ice : during the Persian Empire.}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7g3yC_EI310C & dq=sorbet+originated+in+persia & pg=PT255|title=The Painted Word: A Treasure Chest of Remarkable Words and Their Origins|last=Cousineau|first=Phil|date=2012-09-11|publisher=Simon and Schuster|isbn=9781936740253|language=en|quote=The ancient Persians created a delicious and cooling concoction called sharbat}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.icecreamhistory.net/frozen-dessert-history/who-invented-ice-cream/|title=Who Invented Ice Cream? - Ice Cream Inventor|website=www.icecreamhistory.net|access-date=2019-10-11|quote=History of ice creams begun around 500 B.C. in the Persian Empire where ice was used in combination with grape juices, fruits, and other flavors to produce very expensive and hard to produce summertime treats.}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://worldhistory.us/ancient-history/the-origin-and-history-of-ice-cream-explored.php|title=The Origin and History of Ice Cream, Explored|last=Bot|first=History|date=2017-06-27|website=World History|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-11|quote=Though nearly every civilization in the world aims to be credited with the invention of ice cream, earliest remnants for such a concoction appear to be of Persian nature. Other flavors were also added to the mix including fruits and spices.}} < /ref >
