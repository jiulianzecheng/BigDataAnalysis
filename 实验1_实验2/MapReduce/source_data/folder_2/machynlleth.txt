 '''Machynlleth''', sometimes known locally as just ''Mach'', is a historic [[market town]], community and [[Ward (electoral subdivision)|electoral ward]] in the county of [[Powys]] in [[Wales]]. It is inside the historic boundaries of [[Montgomeryshire]]. 
 
 Machynlleth was the seat of [[Owain Glyndŵr]]'s Welsh Parliament in 1404, and because of this, says it is the  " ancient capital of Wales " . However, it has never held any official recognition as a capital. It applied for [[City status in the United Kingdom|city status]] in [[2000]] and [[2002]], but was unsuccessful.  
 
 [[Welsh language|Welsh]] is spoken by many as well as [[English language|English]]. The 2011 Census found that 67% of the town's people had some knowledge of the Welsh language, with 39% able to read, write and speak the language.
