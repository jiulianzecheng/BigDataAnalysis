 '''Chespirito''' (born '''Roberto Gómez Bolaños''', February 21, 1929  & ndash; November 28, 2014) was a [[Mexicans|Mexican]] writer, actor, director, comedian, humorist, songwriter and poet. < ref > {{cite book |title=Mexican cinema: reflections of a society, 1896-1980 |last=Mora |first=Carl J. |page=162 |year=1989 |publisher=[[University of California Press]] |isbn=9780520043046 }} < /ref >   
 
 He was known for writing, directing, and starring in the ''[[Chespirito (TV series)|Chespirito]]'' (1968-1995), ''[[El Chavo del Ocho]]'' (1971-1980), and ''[[El Chapulín Colorado]]'' (1972-1981) television series. The character of ''El Chavo'' is one of the most [[iconic]] in the history of [[Latin American]] television. ''El Chavo del Ocho'' is still popular. It has been watched every day by 91 million people per episode. < ref name= " Forbes " > {{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/andersonantunes/2012/02/01/meet-el-chavo-the-worlds-most-famous-and-richest-orphan/ |title=Meet El Chavo, The World's Most Famous (And Richest) Orphan |work=[[Forbes]] |access-date= November 29, 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Early life== 
 Chespirito was born on February 21, 1929 in [[Mexico City]], Mexico. His parents were Francisco Gómez Linares and Elsa Bolaños Cacho Aguilar. His brother [[Horacio Gómez Bolaños|Horacio]] was also an actor. Mexican President [[Gustavo Díaz Ordaz|Gustavo Díaz Ordaz Bolaños]] was his cousin once removed. 
 
 ==Early career== 
 Before becoming an actor, Chespirito was a small-time [[boxing|boxer]]. He wanted to be a [[engineer]], so he studied at [[National Autonomous University of Mexico]]. Chespirito also wrote some plays before he started acting. His stage name,  " Chespirito " , was given to him by a producer during Chespirito's first years as a writer. The nickname means  " Little Shakespeare " . < ref > {{cite news|last1=Benedetti|first1=Ana Maria|title=10 Things You Didn't Know About 'El Chavo Del Ocho'|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/05/28/el-chavo-del-ocho-facts_n_5367671.html|access-date=28 November 2014|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|date=28 November 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Personal life== 
 Chespirito's first wife was Graciela Fernandez. They married in 1968. They had six children. They divorced in 1989. 
 
 In 2004, Chespirito married his co-star and long-time partner, actress [[Florinda Meza]].  
 
 Chespirito was a member of the [[National Action Party (Mexico)|National Action Party]] (PAN).
