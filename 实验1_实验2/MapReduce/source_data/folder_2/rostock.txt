 '''Rostock''' ( < small > German pronunciation: < /small >  [[Help:IPA/Standard German|[ˈʁɔstɔk]]]) is a city in the northeast of [[Germany]]. It is in the [[States of Germany|state]] of [[Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania]]. 
 
 Rostock has over 200,000 people (204,260 in December 2011). It has an old university (founded in 1419). The town is on the river [[Warnow]]. The [[mayor]] is Roland Methling. The city today has the name ''Hansestadt'' (Hanseatic city) in memory of its history, where it has been an important part of the [[Hanseatic League]]. The architecture of the city and the region is influenced by this historic period. 
 
 Rostock is on the [[Baltic Sea]]. It has an important [[harbor]]. A [[district]] of the city, [[Warnemünde]], is famous for its long [[beach]]es. It has a [[humid continental climate]] (''Dfb'' in the [[Koeppen climate classification]]). 
 
 The most important [[association football|football]] club of Rostock is [[F.C. Hansa Rostock]]. It is in the German [[Bundesliga]]. 
 
 Other major Hanseatic cities today are: [[Hamburg]], [[Lübeck]], [[Wismar]], [[Stralsund]], [[Bremen]], [[Greifswald]] and [[Demmin]].
