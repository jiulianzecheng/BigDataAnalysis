 ==Origin and History== 
 The etymological origin of the term Banaut is a matter of dispute but it is said that the word means converted. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref > {{Cite book |last=Singh |first=K. S. |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=diL4PB1jyQ4C & q=banaut |title=People of India: Bihar (2 pts.) |date=2008 |publisher=Anthropological Survey of India |isbn=978-81-85579-09-2 |language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Regarding the origin of Banaut, it is said that earlier they belonged to Goala or [[Ahir]]/[[Yadav|Yadava]] caste and were called Mandals, but later on they started using Singh title and associated themselves with [[Rajput|Rajputs]]. < ref > {{Cite book |last=O'Malley |first=Lewis Sydney Steward |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2xr-TP_Y4YMC & dq=banaut+rajput & pg=RA3-PA68 |title=Purnea |date=2011 |publisher=Concept Publishing Company |isbn=978-81-7268-202-6 |language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book |last1=Bihar (India) |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=CJ9hAAAAIAAJ & q=banaut+rajput |title=Bihar District Gazetteers: Purnea |last2=Choudhury |first2=Pranab Chandra Roy |date=1963 |publisher=Superintendant, Secretariat Press, Bihar |language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Culture== 
 ===Deities=== 
 Banaut have their particular village deities such as [[Hanuman]]ji, [[Rama|Ram-]][[Sita|Janaki]] and Radhakrishna. < ref > {{Cite book |last=Singh |first=Kumar Suresh |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dw8wAQAAIAAJ & q=banaut |title=People of India: Bihar, including Jharkhand (2 pts) |date=2008 |publisher=Anthropological Survey of India |isbn=978-81-7046-302-3 |language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 ===Gotra=== 
 The gotras of Banaut are Bhardwaj, Dhenu, Kashyap, Kaushik, Garg, Sandilya and Vishwamitra. < ref name= " :1 "  / >
