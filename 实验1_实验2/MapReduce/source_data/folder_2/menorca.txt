 '''Menorca''' (Spain, Catalan: ''Menorca'') is the most east of the [[Balearic Islands]]. 
 
 == Geography == 
 
 Menorca is located east of [[Mallorca]]. It has an area of {{convert|694.39|km2|abbr=on}}. About 88,000 people live there. The main town is [[Mah√≥n]], which has about 27,000 people. 
 
 == History == 
 
 Menorca was influenced by the [[Minoan]] < nowiki/ > s. Later it was ruled by [[Carthage]], and by [[Ancient Rome|Rome]] after the [[Punic Wars]]. From the 5th century AD there was a seat of a bishop on Menorca. The [[Vandals]] invaded but the Byzantine Empire recovered Menorca in 534. The [[Moors]] took over in 903. In 1287 the Christians took it back and enslaved the Muslims. 
 
 Later Menorca was ruled by the Kingdom of Mallorca. In 1334 it became part of [[Aragon]], which later became part of [[Spain]]. In 1713 the British conquered Menorca but in 1781 the Spanish recovered it in the [[American Revolutionary War]]. 
 
 == Economy == 
 
 Menorca lives mainly on tourism. There is one airport near the main city.
