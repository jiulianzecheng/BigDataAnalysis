 The '''Almohad Caliphate''' (From [[Arabic language|Arabic]] {{lang|ar|الموحدون}} ''al-Muwahhidun'', i.e.,  " [[monotheist|the monotheists]] "  or  " the Unitarians " ), was a [[Sunni Muslim]] empire that was founded in the 12th century, and conquered all of [[North Africa|northern Africa]] as far as [[Libya]], together with [[Al-Andalus]] ([[Moors|Moorish]] [[Iberian Peninsula|Iberia]]). 
 
 == History == 
 
 Between 1130 and his death in 1163, Abd al-Mu'min al-Kumi, a Berber from the Masmuda tribe, defeated the ruling [[Almoravid dynasty|Almoravids]] and became ruler over all northern Africa as far as [[Libya]]. He became [[Emir]] of [[Marrakech]] in 1149 and conquered [[Al-Andalus]], Moorish Iberia. In 1170 the Almohads transferred their capital to [[Seville]]. But by 1212 Muhammad III,  " al-Nasir "  (1199–1214) was defeated by an alliance of the four Christian princes of [[Kingdom of Castile|Castile]], [[Aragón]], Kingdom of [[Navarre]] and [[Portugal]], at the Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa in the [[Sierra Morena]]. The Almohads lost nearly all of the Moorish dominions in Iberia soon after. The great Moorish cities of [[Córdoba, Spain|Córdoba]] and [[Seville]] fell into Christian possession in the first half of the 13th century. The Almohads continued to rule in Africa for some time, but they lost a lot of their territory. The last representative of the line, Idris II, had only [[Marrakech]] left. There he was murdered by a slave in 1269.
