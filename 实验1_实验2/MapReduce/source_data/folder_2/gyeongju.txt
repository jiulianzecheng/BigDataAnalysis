 '''Gyeongju''' (경주) is a [[wikt:coast|coastal]] [[city]] in the southeastern corner of [[North Gyeongsang]] [[province]] in [[South Korea]]. < ref name= " EncyKorea Gyeongju 1 " > {{cite web |url=http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s=K & i=296984 & v=44 |title=경주시 |trans-title=Gyeongju-si |publisher=[[Nate]] / Encyclopedia of Korean Culture |language=Korean |accessdate=2009-08-02 |archive-date=2011-06-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610070044/http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s=K & i=296984 & v=44 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " Xinhua " > {{cite news|url=http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/2005-10/18/content_3640593.htm |title=S. Korean, US presidents to meet before APEC summit |publisher=Xinhua News|date=2005-10-18 |accessdate=2009-09-15}} < /ref >  It is the second largest city in the province. It is {{convert|1324|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}} large, with 269,343 people living in the city, according to the 2008 [[census]]. < ref name= " EncyKorea Gyeongju 1 "  / > < ref name= " Gyeongbuk area " > {{cite web |year=2007 |language=Korean |url=http://stat.gb.go.kr/nsiiu/view/stat.do?task=viewStatTbl & act=new & tblid=DT_A0A112 & orgid=216 & path=%C5%EB%B0%E8%C0%DB%BC%BA%20%B1%E2%B0%FC%BA%B0%20%3E%20%B0%E6%BB%F3%BA%CF%B5%B5%20%3E%20%B0%E6%BB%F3%BA%CF%B5%B5%B1%E2%BA%BB%C5%EB%B0%E8%20%3E%20%C5%E4%C1%F6%20%B9%D7%20%B1%E2%C8%C4%20%3E%20%C7%E0%C1%A4%B1%B8%BF%AA |title=Administrative divisions |publisher=The Government of North Gyeongsang province |accessdate=2009-09-15 |archive-date=2020-05-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200511103131/http://stat.gb.go.kr/nsiiu/view/stat.do?task=viewStatTbl & act=new & tblid=DT_A0A112 & orgid=216 & path=%C5%EB%B0%E8%C0%DB%BC%BA%20%B1%E2%B0%FC%BA%B0%20%3E%20%B0%E6%BB%F3%BA%CF%B5%B5%20%3E%20%B0%E6%BB%F3%BA%CF%B5%B5%B1%E2%BA%BB%C5%EB%B0%E8%20%3E%20%C5%E4%C1%F6%20%B9%D7%20%B1%E2%C8%C4%20%3E%20%C7%E0%C1%A4%B1%B8%BF%AA |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  From southeast, Gyeongju is {{convert|370|km|mi|abbr=on}} away from [[Seoul]], < ref name= " Brisbane " > {{cite web |last=Smyth |first=Terry |date=2008-11-13 |url=http://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/travel/saving-face-for-australia-20081113-63t3.html?page=-1 |title=Saving face for Australia |publisher=[[Brisbane Times]] |accessdate=2009-08-11 |archive-date=2020-02-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200222104641/https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/travel/saving-face-for-australia-20081113-63t3.html?page=-1 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  and from the east, {{convert|55|km|mi|abbr=on}} away from the provincial capital, [[Daegu]]. < ref name= " Kyŏngju " > {{cite web|year=2009 |url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/326010/Kyongju |title=Kyŏngju |publisher=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]] |accessdate=2009-09-15}} < /ref >  [[Cheongdo]] and [[Yeongcheon]] are on the west side of the city. [[Ulsan]] is to the south side, and [[Pohang]] is to the north. On the east there is the coast of the [[Sea of Japan]] (East Sea). < ref name= " EncyKorea Gyeongju 1 "  / >  Many low mountains are around the city. < ref name= " EncyKorea Nature " > {{cite web |url=http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=3008021 |title=경주시의 자연환경 |trans-title=Natural environment of Gyeongju |publisher=Nate / Encyclopedia of Korean Culture |language=Korean |accessdate=2009-08-05 |archive-date=2011-06-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610054017/http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=3008021 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  They are part of the [[Taebaek Mountains|Taebaek range]]. 
 
 Gyeongju was the capital of the ancient kingdom of [[Silla]] (57 BC – 935 AD). Silla ruled most of the [[Korean Peninsula]] between the 7th and 9th centuries, and many [[archaeology|archaeological sites]] from that time are still in the city. This is why Gyeongju is often called  " the museum without walls " . < ref > Robinson et al. 2007. p.187 < /ref > < ref name= " Travel Blackboard " > {{cite news|date=2005-03-04 |url=http://www.etravelblackboard.com/showarticle.asp?id=35779 & nav=13 & suc= & cid= & email= & news= |title=Spring into Korea's Cultural Festivals |publisher=Travel Blackboard |accessdate=2009-09-15}} < /ref > 
 For example, [[Gyeongju Historic Areas]] and [[Yangdong Folk Village]] were made [[World Heritage Site]]s by [[UNESCO]]. < ref > {{cite web|year=2009 |url=http://whc.unesco.org/en/statesparties/kr |title=Korea, Republic of |publisher=UNESCO World Heritage Centre |accessdate=2010-08-22}} < /ref > < ref name= " Yangdong 2010 " > {{cite web|title=Gyeongju Yangdong Folk Village (UNESCO World Heritage)|url=http://www.visitkorea.or.kr/enu/SI/SI_EN_3_1_1_1.jsp?cid=804281|publisher=[[Korea Tourism Organization]]|access-date=2010-10-17|archive-date=2013-10-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023060325/http://www.visitkorea.or.kr/enu/SI/SI_EN_3_1_1_1.jsp?cid=804281|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The many important historical sites have helped Gyeongju become one of the most popular places in Korea for tourists. < ref name= " Kyŏngju "  / > < ref > {{cite web|date=2009-05-05 |url=http://english.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2009/05/05/2009050500290.html |title=Insa-dong Rivals Jeju as Most Popular Tourist Spot |publisher=[[The Chosun Ilbo]] |accessdate=2009-08-04}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1995, the city of Gyeongju and Gyeongju County were put together. < ref > {{cite journal|last1=Lee, Man Hyung|last2=Lee, Jae Won|year=1997|title=Urban-Rural Integration Conflicts After 1994's Reform in Korea|url=http://kocer.re.kr/files/UrbanStudies/3th/US3-05.pdf|url-status=dead|journal=Dosi Yeongu, Korea Center for City and Environment Research|volume=3|pages=103–121|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5jSRCuERz?url=http://kocer.re.kr/files/UrbanStudies/3th/US3-05.pdf|archive-date=2009-09-01|access-date=2010-10-18}} < /ref >  There are 53 other small and medium-sized cities with about 300,000 or less people in South Korea. < ref > {{cite web|author=Yun, Daesic|author2=Hwang, Junghoon|last3=Moon, Changkeun|date=June 2008|title=A Study on Analysis of Mode Choice Characteristics and Travel Pattern in Urban-Rural Integrated City|url=http://168.126.177.50/pub/docu/kr/AD/BC/ADBC2008SAB/ADBC-2008-SAB-007.PDF|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120105231753/http://168.126.177.50/pub/docu/kr/AD/BC/ADBC2008SAB/ADBC-2008-SAB-007.PDF|archive-date=2012-01-05|publisher=Korea Research Institute For Human Settlements|page=118|language=Korean|accessdate=2009-09-15}} < /ref >  Today, Gyeongju is not only influenced by its history–it is influenced by the economic, [[demography|demographic]] and social changes in South Korean culture. Its economy is mostly based on tourism. Manufacturing has also developed because Gyeogju is near large industrial cities such as Ulsan and Pohang. It is also connected to railways and highways that are often used by tourists. < ref name= " Industry " > {{cite web |language=Korean |url=http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=3008027 |title=경주시의 산업·교통 |trans-title=Industry and Transportation of Gyeongju |publisher=Nate / Encyclopedia of Korean Culture |accessdate=2009-09-15 |archive-date=2011-06-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610060922/http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=3008027 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " Brit Transport " > {{cite web |url=http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=1010600 |title=경주시 산업과 교통 |trans-title=Industry and transport of Gyeongju |publisher=Nate / Britannica |language=Korean |accessdate=2009-08-07 |archive-date=2011-06-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610062429/http://100.nate.com/dicsearch/pentry.html?s= & i=1010600 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " Tamásy " > Tamásy  &  Taylor, (2008) p.129 < /ref >
