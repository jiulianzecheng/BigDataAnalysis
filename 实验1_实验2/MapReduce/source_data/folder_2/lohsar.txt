 ==Ethnography== 
 They are located in Indian states of Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, [[Himachal Pradesh]], Haryana, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, [[Gujarat]] < ref name= " :0 "  / >  and [[Delhi|Dehli]]. And also located in various Pakistani regions, including the [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]], Azad Kashmir, Gilgit-Baltistan, [[Balochistan]], [[Islamabad]] and Punjab (especially in Gujranwala  &  [[Gujrat District|Gujrat district]] < ref > {{Cite book |last=Punjab (India) |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IHkMAQAAMAAJ |title=Gazetteer of the Gujrat District |date=2007 |publisher=Sang-i-Meel Publications |isbn=978-969-35-2039-2 |pages=46 |language=en |quote=list of Gujjar clans:__ Kalas, '''Lohsar''', Bargar, Singal, Jagal,  Bijeran,  Anghat,  Manghat, Bhambre, Khair,  Bahrwal, Bania, Baukan, Doi, Dhakkar, and so on.}} < /ref > ).
