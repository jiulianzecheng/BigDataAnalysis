 The '''Adige''' ({{lang-de|Etsch}}; {{lang-vec|Àdexe}}; {{lang-rm|Adisch}}; {{lang-lld|Adesc}}; {{lang-la|Athesis}}; {{lang-grc|Ἄθεσις}}) is an [[Italy|Italian]] river that starts in the [[Alps]] in the province of [[South Tyrol]] near the Italian border with [[Austria]] and [[Switzerland]], flowing through most of [[Northeast Italy]] to the [[Adriatic Sea]]. 
 
 It is the second Italian river by [[length]] after the [[Po (river)|Po]], the third for its [[watershed]] after the Po and the [[Tiber River|Tiber]], and the fourth by its mean yearly [[Discharge (hydrology)|discharge]] after the Po, the [[Ticino (river)|Ticino]] and the Tiber. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The Adige river has a length of {{Convert|410|km|mi|1|abbr=on}} and a [[drainage basin]] with an area of {{Convert|12200|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " treccani " > {{cite web |url=http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/adige/ |title=Adige |publisher=Treccani Enciclopedia |language=it |access-date=26 June 2017 }} < /ref >   
 
 Its average yearly [[Discharge (hydrology)|discharge]] ([[volume]] of [[water]] which passes through a section of the river per [[Unit of measurement|unit]] of [[time]]) is {{Convert|205|m3/s|cuft/s|abbr=on }} at [[Boara Pisani]], a ''comune'' in the [[Province of Padua|Padua]] province near the mouth of the river. < ref name= " hydro " > {{cite web |url=http://193.206.192.243/simn/dati/alov_portate.php?id=27 & nome=ADIGE%20a%20Boara%20Pisani |title=ADIGE a Boara Pisani |publisher=Agenzia per la protezione dell'ambiente e per i servizi tecnici |language=it |access-date=26 June 2017 |archive-date=20 April 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170420045830/http://193.206.192.243/simn/dati/alov_portate.php?id=27 & nome=ADIGE%20a%20Boara%20Pisani |url-status=dead }} < /ref >

 ===Course=== 
 The Adige starts in the [[Reschen Pass]] in the ''comune'' of [[Graun im Vinschgau]], [[South Tyrol]] province, at an [[altitude]] of about {{Convert|1550|m|ft|0|abbr=on }} close to the borders with [[Austria]] and [[Switzerland]]. First, it flows to the south until the [[confluence]] with the Ram river and then turns to the east. At [[Merano]], the Adige is joined by the Passirio river and turns to the southeast; in [[Bolzano]], it is joined by the Isarco, its main [[tributary]]. In the confluence with the Tasso river, the Adige flows to the south and, then, from [[Badia Polesine]], it turns to the east and flows into the [[Adriatic Sea]] to the south of the mouth of the [[Brenta (river)|Brenta]] river and to the north of the mouth of the Po river. < ref name= " treccani "  / > 
 
 The [[valley]] of the Adige gets several names: ''Venosta'' valley between the [[Source (river or stream)|source]] and [[Merano]], ''Adige'' valley between Merano and [[Trento]], ''Vallagarina'' between Trento and [[Verona]] and, last, ''Padana'' valley between Verona and the mouth.
