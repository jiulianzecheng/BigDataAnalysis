 '''Yonne''' is a [[Departments of France|department]] in the centre of [[France]], in the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]]. The largest city in the department, and its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] ([[Capital city|capital]]), is [[Auxerre]]. 
 
 The department is named after the [[Yonne (river)|Yonne]] [[river]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Seine]], that flows through the department. The [[Celtic|Celts]] name of the river was ''Ica-onna'' ({{lang-lat|Icauna}}) and from this name come the name of the river and of the people living in the department: ''Icaunais''. < ref name= " histoire " > {{cite web |url=http://www.cg89.fr/L-Yonne/L-histoire-de-l-Yonne |title=L'histoire de l'Yonne |work=Conseil Général de l'Yonne |language=French |accessdate=24 November 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 During [[Roman Empire|Roman]] times, the main city in the territory of the present department was [[Sens]], the capital of one Roman [[province]]. < ref name= " histoire "  / > 
 
 Yonne is one of the original 83 departments created during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with Auxerre as its [[Capital (city)|capital]]. < ref name= " splaf " > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/89his.html |title=Historique de l'Yonne |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=24 November 2014 |date= }} < /ref >  It was formed from combined parts of the old [[province]]s of ''[[Burgundy]]'', ''[[Champagne (historical province)|Champagne]]'' and ''[[Île-de-France]]''. < ref name= " histoire "  / > 
 
 It was divided in seven [[district]]s: Auxerre, Sens, Joigny, Saint-Fargeau, Avallon, Tonnerre and Saint-Florentin. < ref name= " splaf "  / > 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the seven districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Auxerre, Avallon, Joigny, Sens and Tonnerre. On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissements'' of Joigny and Tonnerre were eliminated. < ref name= " splaf "  / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:Carte de l'Yonne.svg|right|thumb|280px|Map of Yonne.]] 
 Yonne is part of the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|7427.4|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / >  The highest point of the department is [[Bois de la Pérouse]] ({{Coord|47|18|57|N|4|1|8|E|name=Bois de la Pérouse |display=inline |region:ID_type:mountain }}), {{Convert|607|m|ft|abbr=on}} high. < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9610 |title=Bois de la Pérouse, France |publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=15 June 2017 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 The department borders with five departments in four [[Regions of France|regions]]: 
 * [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] region 
 ** [[Côte-d'Or]] (east) 
 ** [[Nièvre]] (south) 
 * [[Grand Est]] region 
 ** [[Aube]] (northeast) 
 * [[Centre-Val de Loire]] region 
 ** [[Loiret]] (west) 
 * [[Île-de-France]] region 
 ** [[Seine-et-Marne]] (northwest)

 The northern part of the department is formed by [[plain]]s that are part of the great plain of [[Paris]]. The mountains of the ''Morvan'' and the [[hill]]s of the ''Auxerrois'' (the region around the city of [[Auxerre]]) are in the southern half of the department. 
 
 The main river in the department is the [[Yonne (river)|Yonne]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Seine]] river that flows through the department from south to north. Other rivers that flow through the department are the [[Armançon]], [[Loing]], [[Ouanne (river)|Ouanne]] and [[Serein]] rivers. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at [[Auxerre]] is an  " Oceanic climate "  (also known as Maritime Temperate climate) and of the subtype  " Cfb " . < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Auxerre, France - Climate Summary |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=598859 & cityname=Auxerre%2C+Bourgogne%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=24 November 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Auxerre is {{Convert|11.5|°C|°F}}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|20.2|°C|°F}}. The coolest month, on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|3.6|°C|°F}}. 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Auxerre is {{Convert|957.6|mm|in|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is December with {{Convert|106.7|mm|in|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is April with an average of {{Convert|63.5|mm|in|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Yonne is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Yonne'' in [[Auxerre]]. The department is part of the [[Bourgogne-Franche-Comté]] region.

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Yonne are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Icaunais'' (women: ''Icaunaises''). < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_departement_yonne_89.html |title=Yonne (89) |publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=24 November 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Yonne had a population, in 2014, of 341,814, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|341814|7427.4|km2|prec=1 |disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Auxerre, with 179,922 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with more inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
