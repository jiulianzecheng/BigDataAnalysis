 {{nihongo|'''Shimonoseki'''|下関市|Shimonoseki-shi}}, also known as '''Amagaseki''' and '''Bakan''', is a [[Japan]]ese [[cities of Japan|city]] in [[Yamaguchi Prefecture]] on the island of [[Honshu]]. < ref name= " nussbaum862 " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA862  " Shimonoseki " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 862; [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA67  " Bakan, " ] p. 67. < /ref >   The city is in the [[Chūgoku region]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA127  " Chūgoku, " ]  p. 127. < /ref > 
 
 It has been [[wikt:recognized|recognized]] as a [[core city]] since 2005. < ref > Jacobs, A.J. [http://www.hindawi.com/journals/usr/2011/692764/   " Japan's Evolving Nested Municipal Hierarchy: The Race for Local Power in the 2000s, " ] ''Urban Studies Research,'' Vol. 2011 (2011), p. 8 [PDF 8 of 14]; retrieved 2012-12-29. < /ref >

 ==Geography== 
 Shimonoseki is a [[port]] on the [[Kamnon Strait]] ([[Shimonoseki Strait]]) at the Western end of Honshu. < ref name= " nussbaum862 " / >   The city is near to [[Kitakyushu, Fukuoka|Kitakyūshū]] on the island of [[Kyushu]].
