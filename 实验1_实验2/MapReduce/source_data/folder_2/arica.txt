 '''Arica''' is a [[Communes of Chile|commune]] and a [[port]] city with a population of 196,590 in the [[Arica Province]] of northern [[Chile]]'s [[Arica y Parinacota Region]]. It is Chile's most northern city; it is at only {{convert|18|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} south of the [[border]] with [[Peru]]. 
 
 The city is the [[Capital city|capital]] since 2007 of both the Arica Province and the Arica and Parinacota Region. It was founded on 25 April 1541 by Lucas Martínez Vegaso as ''San Marcos de Arica''. 
 
 ==Population== 
 {{As of|2002}} (last national [[census]]), there were 185,268 people living in the commune, giving it a [[population density]] of {{Pop density|185268|4799.4|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km². < ref name= " ineDPA "  / > 
 
 The city of Arica has an [[urban area]] of {{Convert|41.89|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and a population, in 2002, of 175,441 inhabitants. < ref name= " ineDPA "  / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:ElMorroDeArica.jpg|thumb|left|Morro of Arica]] 
 Arica is on the [[Pacific Ocean]] [[coast]] at the [[:wikt:bend|bend]] of [[South America]]'s western [[coast]] known as the Arica Bend or Arica [[Elbow]]. The city is on the foot of the '''Morro of Arica''', or just '''El Morro''', a [[:wikt:steep|steep]] and tall [[hill]], {{Convert|139|m|ft|0||abbr=on }} [[above sea level]]. 
 
 The city is at {{Convert|2051|km|mi|0|abbr=on }} from [[Santiago, Chile|Santiago]], the national capital. The [[River mouth|mouths]] of two [[river]]s: the [[Lluta River|Lluta]] and the ''San José de Azapa'' rivers, are near the city. 
 
 The commune has an area of {{Convert|4799.4|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " ineDPA "  / >  It borders with [[Peru]] ([[Tacna Province|Tacna]] province) on the north, the [[Parinacota Province|Parinacota]] province on the east, the [[Camarones, Chile|Camarones]] commune on the south and the [[Pacific Ocean]] on the west.

 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at Arica is a  " [[Desert climate|mild desert climate]] "  and of the subtype  " '''BWh/BWn''' " . < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Arica, Chile - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=60458 & cityname=Arica%2C+Arica+And+Parinacota%2C+Chile & units= |access-date=9 October 2016}} < /ref >  Arica is known as the more dry inhabited place on Earth, at least as measured by [[rainfall]]: the [[average]] amount of [[precipitation]] for the year is {{Convert|0.76|mm|in|2|abbr=on }}. 
 
 The average [[temperature]] for the year in Arica is {{Convert|67|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is January with an average temperature of {{Convert|74|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is July, with an average temperature of {{Convert|62|°C|°F }}. < ref name= " wbase "  / > 
 
 The highest [[:wikt:record|recorded]] temperature in Arica is {{Convert|35|°C|°F }}, which was recorded in March. The lowest recorded temperature in Arica is  {{Convert|3.9|°C|°F }}, which was recorded in May.
