 The '''Malacostraca''' (Greek:  " soft shell " ) are the largest subgroup of [[crustacean]]s. They include [[Decapoda|decapods]] (such as [[crab]]s,  [[lobster]]s and [[shrimp]]), stomatopods ([[mantis shrimp]]) and [[krill]]. There are 22,000 members in this group. It represents two thirds of all crustacean species. The first malacostracans appeared in the [[Cambrian]] period.{{fact|date=April 2009}} 
 
 The [[scientific classification|classification]] of crustaceans is currently being debated. Some think Malacostraca is a [[class (biology)|class]] and others think it is a [[Subclass (biology)|subclass]]. 
 
 == Morphology == 
 [[File:General malacostracan en.svg|thumb|200px|left|General malacostracan ''layout'']] 
 
 Malacostraca have heads with six segments. They have a pair of [[antenna (biology)|antennules]] and a pair of [[antenna (biology)|antennae]]. They also have mouthparts. There are appendages near the mouthparts, called [[maxilliped]]s. They have five pairs of walking legs. The first pair is formed like a pincer. There are eight thoracic segments. There are six abdominal segments. They are used for swimming. Members of Malacostraca have compound stalked or [[sessile]] eyes. They have a two-chambered [[stomach]] and a centralized [[nervous system]].
