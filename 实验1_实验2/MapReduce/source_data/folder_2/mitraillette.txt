 A '''''Mitraillette''''' ({{IPA-fr|mitʁajɛt}}, literally  " [[submachine gun]] " ), is a type of food from [[Belgium]]. < ref name= " APfries " > {{cite web |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1A1-D9DSMR100.html |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |title=Belgium's Dutch and French-speakers unite on fries |date=15 February 2010 |access-date=22 April 2015  |via=[[HighBeam Research]] |archive-date=24 September 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924041637/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1A1-D9DSMR100.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It might come from [[Brussels]], but is also popular in [[Flanders]], [[Wallonia]] and the [[Nord-Pas-de-Calais|Nord]] region of [[France]], where it is also known as an  " ''Américain'' "  (literally an  " American " ). < ref name= " NVM " > {{cite web|author=Jamel Daugherty|url=http://www.northernvirginiamag.com/gut-check/2010/07/02/the-american-sandwich/|title=The American Sandwich|publisher=[[Northern Virginia Magazine]]|date=2 July 2010|access-date=1 April 2015|archive-date=29 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110629132957/http://www.northernvirginiamag.com/gut-check/2010/07/02/the-american-sandwich/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " APImage " > {{cite web |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1A2-285e5403-1cea-42ae-9065-4f2f6a5a9c10.html |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |title=Belgium Fries Forever |date=15 February 2010 |access-date=22 April 2015 |via=[[HighBeam Research]] |archive-date=24 September 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924043726/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1A2-285e5403-1cea-42ae-9065-4f2f6a5a9c10.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Composition== 
 A typical ''mitraillette'' consists of: < ref name= " APfries " / > < ref name=Recipe1 > {{cite web|last=Malhotra|first=Saira|title=La Mitraillette (Belgian Machine Gun) Sandwich Recipe|url=http://www.marcussamuelsson.com/recipe/la-mitraillette-belgian-machine-gun-sandwich-recipe|publisher=Marcus Samuelsson|access-date=11 March 2013|archive-date=12 July 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120712212204/http://www.marcussamuelsson.com/recipe/la-mitraillette-belgian-machine-gun-sandwich-recipe|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " WP " > {{cite web |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/01/28/AR2010012803666.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150712202203/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/01/28/AR2010012803666.html |archive-date=12 July 2015 |publisher=[[Washington Post]] |title=Here's to Belgium's monks (and witches) |date=31 January 2010 |access-date=9 July 2015 |url-status=bot: unknown }} < /ref > 
 
 * A demi-[[baguette]] (or sometimes a smaller version of a [[baguette]]). 
 * Fried meat (as [[sausage]], [[Patty|burger]], [[steak]], [[frikandel]]). The type of meat will be different in each restaurant. 
 * [[French fry|Fries]] 
 * One of a variety of sauces, including [[mayonnaise]], [[ketchup]], [[sauce andalouse]], [[Aioli|garlic sauce]], [[bearnaise sauce]] and many others. 
 
 [[Crudités]] are often included (grated [[carrot]], fresh [[lettuce]], [[tomato]] slices), though different in each location. [[Cheese]] and [[cabbage]] are also sometimes included. < ref > {{cite web|title=La 1ère Mitraillette sur le Web!|url=http://www.frites.be/v4/index.cfm?context=article & ContentID=764|publisher=Frites.be|access-date=11 March 2013|archive-date=4 October 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081004131717/http://www.frites.be/v4/index.cfm?context=article & ContentID=764|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Originally ''mitraillettes'' only contained a sausage or sliced meat. Different versions quickly became available. Often, [[dürüm]]s are served instead in more multicultural areas.
