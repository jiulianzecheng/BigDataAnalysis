 '''Troyes''' is a [[Communes of France|commune of France]].  It is the [[Capital (city)|capital city]] of the [[Aube]] [[Departments of France|department]] in north-central France. < ref > {{cite book| last = Chisholm| first = Hugh| title = The Encyclopedia Britannica: A Dictionary of Arts, Sciences, Literature and General Information| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=49uUqs-3skgC & pg=PA888| year = 1910| publisher = Encyclopedia Britannica Company| page = 888 }} < /ref >  It is on the [[Seine]] river about {{convert|150|km|mi|abbr=on}} southeast of Paris. < ref > {{cite book| last = Vaughan| first = L. Brent| title = The Junior Encyclopedia Britannica: A Reference Library of General Knowledge| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=zlAPAQAAMAAJ & pg=PA591| year = 1897| publisher = E. G. Melvin  &  Company| page = 591 }} < /ref >   It was briefly the [[capital of France]] in the early 15th century. < ref name =  " britannica320 " > {{cite book| last = Chisholm| first = Hugh| title = The Encyclopaedia Britannica: A Dictionary of Arts, Sciences, Literature and General Information| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=bi8EAAAAYAAJ & pg=PA320| year = 1911| publisher = [Cambridge] University Press| page = 320 }} < /ref > 
 
 The name [[troy weight]] for gold derives from the standard of measurement which developed in Troyes. < ref > {{cite book| last = Perkins| first = George Roberts| title = The Practical Arithmetic: Designed for Such Institutions as Require a Greater Number of Examples Than are Given in the Elementary Arithmetic| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=DupHAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA111| year = 1852| publisher = D. Appleton| page = 111 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 Troyes has existed since Roman times.  It was the capital of the [[Counts of Champagne]]. < ref > {{cite book| title = Americanized Encyclopedia Britannica, Revised and Amended: A Dictionary of Arts, Sciences and Literature; to which is Added Biographies of Livings Subjects.| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=RD4hAQAAIAAJ & pg=PA5930| year = 1890| publisher = The  " Examiner " | page = 5930 }} < /ref > 
 
 The first Lord of Troyes was Gwain de Nantouillet in 884. 
 
 In 1417, the Duke of Burgandy and Isabeau of Bavaria, wife of [[Charles VI of France]], planned to establish the [[capital of France]] at Troyes.  This included a court, council, and parliament with comptroller's offices. < ref name= " catholic " > [http://www.newadvent.org/cathen/15067a.htm   " Troyes, " ] ''Catholic Encyclopedia'' (2009); retrieved 2011-11-29. < /ref > 
 
 In 1419-1425, during the [[Hundred Years' War]], Troyes was the center of the royal government. < ref name= " britannica320 " / >
