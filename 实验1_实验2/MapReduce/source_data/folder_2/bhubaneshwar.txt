 '''Bhubaneshwar''' is the capital city of the Indian state of [[Odisha]]. It is the largest city in Odisha. Bhubaneswar means  " lord of the world " . < ref name= " MohapatraMohapatra1995 " > {{cite book|author1=Amulya Mohapatra|author2=Bijaya Mohapatra|title=Hinduism: Analytical Study|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vpiU9m7T_tQC & pg=PA107|access-date=15 July 2012|date=1 December 1995|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-7099-388-9|pages=107–}} < /ref >  Many temples are present in this city. So, it is also called  " Temple city of India " . < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://nceg.gov.in/abouthost.htm |title=E-Governance |access-date=2012-07-15 |archive-date=2012-12-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121203092940/http://www.nceg.gov.in/abouthost.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is a religious centre in Orissa from the [[Middle Ages]]. < ref name= " R. Publishing Corporation, Delhi, 1986, {{ISBN|978-81-7018-346-4}} < /ref >  It is a [[planned city]] of modern India. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.studio-basel.com/assets/files/files/04_atlas_web.pdf|title=Planned Cities in India|access-date=2012-07-15|archive-date=2016-03-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304032120/http://www.studio-basel.com/assets/files/files/04_atlas_web.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1948, Bhubaneshwar became political capital of Odisha. Before this, it was Cuttack. Bhubaneshwar and Cuttack are together called  " twin-cities "  of Orissa. < ref name= " Orissa General Knowledge " > {{cite book|title=Orissa General Knowledge|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hMCbjkhA_ncC & pg=PA13|access-date=15 July 2012|publisher=Bright Publications|isbn=978-81-7199-574-5|pages=13–}} < /ref >
