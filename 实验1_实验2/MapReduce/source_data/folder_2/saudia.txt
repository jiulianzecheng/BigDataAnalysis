 '''Saudia''' ({{lang-ar|السعودية}} ''{{transliteration|ar|DIN| as-Suʿūdiyyah}}''), formerly known as '''Saudi Arabian Airlines''' ({{lang-ar|الخطوط الجوية العربية السعودية}} ''{{transliteration|ar|DIN|al-Ḫuṭūṭ al-Jawwiyyah al-ʿArabiyyah as-Suʿūdiyyah}}'') is the [[flag carrier]] and largest airline of [[Saudi Arabia]], based in [[Jeddah]]. < ref > {{cite news|last1=Hofmann |first1=Kurt |title=Saudia outlines 2017 fleet delivery plan |url=http://atwonline.com/airframes/saudia-outlines-2017-fleet-delivery-plan |publisher=[[Air Transport World]] |date=20 January 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170121143314/http://atwonline.com/airframes/saudia-outlines-2017-fleet-delivery-plan |archive-date=21 January 2017 |quote=Saudi Arabia's national carrier Saudia will take delivery of 30 aircraft this year, according to a Jan. 17 statement. |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web |url= http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=31109806 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20130605150811/http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=31109806 |url-status= dead |archive-date= 5 June 2013 |title=Saudi Arabian Airlines Ground Services Company: Private Company Information|work=Businessweek|access-date=3 September 2012}} < /ref >  It was started in 1945 with the help of the [[United States government]].  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://twdcs.org/Saudiahistorypage.html|title=Saudiahistorypage|work=twdcs.org|accessdate=7 June 2015}} < /ref >  [[TWA]] managed the airline until the [[1960s]]. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-5um9PPeoyYC & dq=twa+saudi & pg=PA130|title=Inside the Mirage|isbn=9780786742530|accessdate=7 June 2015|last1=Lippman|first1=Thomas|date=10 November 2008}} < /ref >  The airline joined [[SkyTeam]] in May 2012.
