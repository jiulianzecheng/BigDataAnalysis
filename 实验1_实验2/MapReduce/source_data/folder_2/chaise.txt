 A '''chaise''', sometimes called '''chay''' or '''shay''', is a lightweight two- or four-wheeled [[carriage]]. It is made for one or two people. It is used for travel or leisure. It has a folding hood or [[Barouche#Calash|calash]] top. < ref name=cowie > {{cite book|last=Cowie|first=L.W.|title=The Wordsworth Dictionary of British Social History|year=1996|publisher=Wordsworth Reference|isbn=1-85326-378-8|page=[https://archive.org/details/dictionaryofbrit0000cowi/page/55 55]|url=https://archive.org/details/dictionaryofbrit0000cowi/page/55}} < /ref >  The name, in use in England before 1700, came from the [[French language|French]] word [“[[chaise]]”] (meaning “[[chair]]”) through a transfer from a [[Sedan chair|sedan-chair]] to a [[wheeled vehicle]]. < ref > {{cite EB1911 |wstitle=Chaise |volume=5 |page=802}} < /ref >
