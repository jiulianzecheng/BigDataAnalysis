 An '''episode''' is a singular part of a story, [[radio]] or [[television program]]. Episodes often follow in an order. They are often 20 to 40 minutes long, and follow the same storyline. 
 
 ==Origin of the word== 
 ''Episode'' comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] word ''epeisodion'' which refers to the spoken words between two songs in a Greek tragedy. The word was first used in English in 1773 to mean an  " outstanding incident, experience. "  By the 1930s, the word was used to refer to each broadcast of a radio program that was part of a series. < ref > {{cite web|title=episode|url=http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?allowed_in_frame=0 & search=episode|work=Online etymology dictionary|access-date=11 May 2016}} < /ref >
