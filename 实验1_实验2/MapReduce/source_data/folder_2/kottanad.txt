 ''' Kottanad ''' is a village in [[Pathanamthitta district]], [[Kerala]], [[India]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.pta.kerala.gov.in/index.php?option=com_content & view=article & id=89 & Itemid=57 |title=Local Self Government |publisher=PTA |access-date=30 July 2012 |archive-date=5 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120705150554/http://www.pta.kerala.gov.in/index.php?option=com_content & view=article & id=89 & Itemid=57 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Religion== 
 
 Kottanad's major religions are [[Hinduism]], [[Christianity]], and [[Islam]].  
 
 ==Climate== 
 
 Like the other parts of the state, Kottanad has a wet and [[Sea|maritime]] [[tropical climate]]. The region receives most of the rain from the [[Southwest monsoon|South-West monsoon]] and the [[Northeast Monsoon|North-East monsoon]]. Summer is from March to May. During these times the place receives locally developed thundershowers. Due to the higher elevation, the climate is cooler towards the eastern area. 
 
 ==Economy== 
 [[File:Rubber trees in Kerala, India.jpg|thumb|Rubber Trees in a Plantation]] 
 
 The economy of Kottanad is mainly from agriculture.  Kottanad is one of the [[rubber]] producing villages in Kerala. Other major crops are [[coconut]], [[tapioca]] and [[Black pepper|pepper]].

 ==Transport== 
 The major mean of transportation of people in Kottanad is public transportation system, KSRTC as well as private bus services.  
 
 ==Administration== 
 Kottanad Village is a part of Pathanamthitta district administration. The Kottanad village office is in [[Perumpetty]].
