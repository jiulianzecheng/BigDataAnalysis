 '''Kongsberg''' is a town located in [[Viken]] county in [[Norway]]. Kongsberg has a population of 27.716 as of 2020. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Kommunefakta|url=https://www.ssb.no/kommunefakta/kommune|access-date=2020-11-04|website=SSB|language=nb}} < /ref > 
 [[File:Kirketorget Kongsberg, 22. mai 2014.jpg|thumb|Kirketorget in Kongsberg, featuring Kongsberg church.]] 
 
 == History == 
 The city of Kongsberg was founded in 1624 after the discovery of silver in the area. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Thorsnæs|first=Geir|date=2019-10-14|title=Kongsberg|url=http://snl.no/Kongsberg|journal=Store Norske Leksikon|language=nb}} < /ref >  The population grew quickly due to the mining industry, and in 1749 Kongsberg was the largest town in Norway based on population. < ref > {{Cite journal|date=2020-10-18|title=Kongsberg|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kongsberg & oldid=984145096|journal=Wikipedia|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Kongsberg was part of the county [[Buskerud]] until it merged with [[Akershus]] and [[Østfold]] to create the larger county Viken. 
 
 In 2021, five people [[Kongsberg attack|were killed]] in a [[mass stabbing]]. 
 
 == Other == 
 The [[Royal Norwegian Mint]] is located in Kongsberg, and is responsible for producing the [[Norwegian krone|Norwegian coins]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|date=2020-07-29|title=Royal Norwegian Mint|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Royal_Norwegian_Mint & oldid=970077991|journal=Wikipedia|language=en}} < /ref >
