'''WYSIWYG''' ({{pronEng|ˈwɪziwɪg}} < ref > Dictionary.com Unabridged (v 1.1). Retrieved November 09, 2007, from Dictionary.com website: [http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/wysiwyg WYSIWUG] < /ref >  or {{IPA|/ˈwɪz < s > ɪ < /s > wɪg/}} < ref > OED < /ref > ), is an [[acronym]] for '''''W'''hat '''Y'''ou '''S'''ee '''I'''s '''W'''hat '''Y'''ou '''G'''et'', used in [[computing]] to describe a system where things you edit on the on-screen editor appear almost the same to the final output, < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/wysiwyg?view=uk|title=Compact Oxford English Dictionary: WYSIWYG|publisher=Oxford University Press|access-date=2008-08-22|archive-date=2008-10-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081018220458/http://www.askoxford.com/concise_oed/wysiwyg?view=uk|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  which might be a [[Printing|printed]] document, a [[Webpage|web page]], a [[Microsoft PowerPoint|PowerPoint]] presentation or even the lighting for a [[concert]]. [[Web designers]] often use this word to talk about their work.
