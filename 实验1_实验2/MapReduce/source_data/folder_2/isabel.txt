'''Isabel''' (or '''Isabelle''') [ '''iz'''-''uh''-bel ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Isabel {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/isabel|access-date=2023-04-06|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a female [[given name]]. It comes from the [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] name ''Elisheva''. ''Elisheva'' is often translated to ''Elizabeth'', of which Isabel is a division. There are many royal figures or [[queen]]s with the name or its variants. Isabel is a name of great beauty that has enjoyed popularity for centuries. It has been embraced by diverse cultures, each giving it their own unique interpretation. The name Isabel originates from the Hebrew name Elisheba, which translates to  " God is my oath. "  Over time, this name has been adopted by numerous cultures, such as the Spanish, Portuguese, and English. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Isabel name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Isabel-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-06|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  There are also many other famous people by Isabel and its related names. As ''Isabel'', this name was used on two [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] [[tropical cyclone]]s. The [[Hurricane Isabel|2003 storm]] was so damaging that the name was later retired.
