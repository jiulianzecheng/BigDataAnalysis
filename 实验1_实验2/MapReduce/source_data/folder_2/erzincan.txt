'''Erzincan''' is the capital of [[Erzincan Province|Erzincan]] [[Provinces of Turkey|Province]] in the eastern [[Anatolia]]n region of [[Turkey]].  
 < !-- Place this is an infobox that is appropriate usually infoxbox settlement - Nearby cities include [[Erzurum]], [[Sivas]], [[Tunceli]], [[Bingöl]], [[Elazığ]], [[Malatya]], [[Gumushane]], [[Bayburt]], and [[Giresun]]. The city is at 39° 45' 12 "  North and 39° 20' 28 "  East, with an altitude of 1185 meters. The population is 90,100 ([[2009]] census). Its climate produces snowy winters and cool summers. -- > 
 
 On December 27, 1939 at 01:57 [[UTC]], the city was hit by a [[Seismic magnitude scales|magnitude]] 7.8 [[earthquake]], the [[1939 Erzincan earthquake|most powerful to hit the country]] since the [[1668 North Anatolia earthquake]], < ref name= " KOERI " > {{Cite web |url=http://www.koeri.boun.edu.tr/sismo/2/deprem-bilgileri/tarihsel-depremler/ |title=Tarihsel Depremler (Historical Earthquakes) |website=Boğaziçi University, Kandilli Observatory and Earthquake Research Institute 
 |language=tr |access-date=}} < /ref > < ref name= " USGS_world_list " > {{Cite web |url=http://earthquake.usgs.gov/regional/world/historical.php/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090825081330/http://earthquake.usgs.gov/regional/world/historical.php/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2009-08-25 |title=Historic Worldwide Earthquakes |publisher=United States Geological Survey|language=en}} < /ref >  which killed 32,700 people and injured more than 100,000. On the same day, the temperature was {{convert|-30|°C}}, which caused many survivors of the earthquake to die from [[hypothermia]]. The city was almost completely destroyed and it took many years to recover. It is the second-deadliest earthquake in Turkey after the [[2023 Turkey–Syria earthquake]] and the deadliest earthquake in Turkey in the [[20th century]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite journal|last1=Gürsoy|first1=Halil|last2=Tatar|first2=Orhan|last3=Akpınar|first3=Zafer|last4=Polat|first4=Ali|last5=Mesci|first5=Levent|last6=Tunçer|first6=Doğan|date=2013-04-01|title=New observations on the 1939 Erzincan Earthquake surface rupture on the Kelkit Valley segment of the North Anatolian Fault Zone, Turkey|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0264370712000981|journal=Journal of Geodynamics|series=SI : Tethyan Evolution and Active Tectonics in Anatolia dedicated in honour of Prof. Dr. Ali Koçyiğit’s retirement|language=en|volume=65|pages=259–271|doi=10.1016/j.jog.2012.06.002|bibcode=2013JGeo...65..259G|issn=0264-3707}} < /ref > 
 
 At 08:18 UTC on 13 March 1992, a magnitude 6.8 earthquake < ref name= " KOERI " / > < ref name= " USGS_world_list " / >  shook the city, killing 652 people and injuring more than 2,000. The city was badly damaged. 150 buildings collapsed and 8,000 buildings were damaged so badly, that they were later [[Demolition|demolished]]. < ref > {{cite journal |title=The Erzincan (Turkey) Earthquake (Ms 6.8) of March 13, 1992 and its Aftershock Sequence|first1=H. |last1=Grosser |first2=M. |last2=Baumbach |first3=H. |last3=Berckhemer |first4=B. |last4=Baier |first5=A. |last5=Karahan |first6=H. |last6=Schelle |first7=F. |last7=Krüger |first8=A. |last8=Paulat |first9=G. |last9=Michel |first10=R. |last10=Demirtas |first11=S. |last11=Gencoglu |first12=R. |last12=Yilmaz |year=1998 |journal=Pure and Applied Geophysics |pages=465–505 |volume=152 |issue=3 |bibcode=1998PApGe.152..465G |doi=10.1007/s000240050163|s2cid=129640525 |url=http://gfzpublic.gfz-potsdam.de/pubman/item/escidoc:226188 |language=en}} < /ref >
