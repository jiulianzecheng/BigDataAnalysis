 '''Taw''', '''tav''', or '''taf''' is the twenty-second and last [[Letter (alphabet)|letter]] of the [[Semitic abjads]], including [[Phoenician alphabet|Phoenician]] Tāw  [[File:Phoenician_taw.svg|12px]], [[Hebrew alphabet|Hebrew]] Tav {{script|Hebr|ת}}, [[Aramaic alphabet|Aramaic]] Taw [[File:Taw.svg| 10 px]], [[Syriac alphabet|Syriac]] Taw ܬ, and [[Arabic script|Arabic]] ت Tāʼ  (22nd in [[abjadi order]], 3rd in modern order). In Arabic, it is also gives rise to the derived letter {{lang|ar|ث}} [[Ṯāʼ]]. Its original sound value is {{IPAslink|t}}. 
 '''Tav''', or '''Taw''', is the twenty-second and last letter in many [[Semitic languages|Semitic]] [[abjad]]s, including [[Phoenician language|Phoenician]], [[Aramaic language|Aramaic]], [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] and [[Arabic alphabet]]. Its sounds like a {{IPAslink|t}}.
