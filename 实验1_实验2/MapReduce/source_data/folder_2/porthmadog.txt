 '''Porthmadog''' ({{IPAc-en|p|ɔr|θ|ˈ|m|æ|d|ɒ|ɡ}}; {{IPA-cy|pɔrθˈmadoɡ}}) is a coastal [[town]] and [[community]] in [[Gwynedd]], [[Wales]]. 
 
 The 2011 census revealed that the town has a [[population]] of 4,185. 
 
 Until [[1974]], it was also known by its [[Anglicisation|anglicised]] name of '''Portmadoc''' and was known locally as '''Port'''. The town came into existence after William Madocks built a sea wall, the ''Cob'', between [[1808]] and [[1811]].
