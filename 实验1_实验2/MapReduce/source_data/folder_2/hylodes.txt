 '''''Hylodes''''' is a [[genus]] of [[frog]]s in the [[Family (biology)|family]] [[Hylodidae]]. < ref name=frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  Scientists believe these frogs [[paraphyletic|live in the same places]] as ''[[Megaelosia]]''. These frogs live in southeastern [[Brazil]]. In English, people [[Common name|call them]] '''tree toads''' or '''[[torrent frog]]s''', even though other frogs are also called torrent frogs. < ref name=frost > {{cite web |url=http://research.amnh.org/vz/herpetology/amphibia/Amphibia/Anura/Hylodidae/Hylodes |title=''Hylodes'' Fitzinger, 1826 |author=Frost, Darrel R. |year=2015 |work=Amphibian Species of the World: an Online Reference. Version 6.0 |publisher=American Museum of Natural History |access-date=28 April 2015}} < /ref >  They are [[Diurnality|active during the day]] and usually swim in shallow mountain streams. < ref > {{Cite journal| doi = 10.1670/12-192| title = Population dynamics of ''Hylodes uai'' tadpoles (Anura, Hylodidae) in a tropical forest stream, southeastern Brazil| journal = Journal of Herpetology| volume = 49| pages = 23–27| year = 2015| last1 = Fatorelli | first1 = P. | last2 = Pereira | first2 = E. G. | last3 = Almeida-Gomes | first3 = M. | last4 = Rocha | first4 = C. F. D. | s2cid = 83735882}} < /ref >
