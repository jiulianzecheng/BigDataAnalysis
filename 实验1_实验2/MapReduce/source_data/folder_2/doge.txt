 '''Doge''' (plural '''dogi''' or '''doges''') is a word of an [[Italian language|Italian]] [[dialect]] that comes from the [[Latin]] ''dux'' (as does the English ''[[duke]]'' and the standard Italian ''duce''), that means  " leader " , especially in a military context. 
 
 The title of '''doge''' was used for the elected [[head of state]] in a number of [[Italy|Italian]]  " crowned republics " .  The two best known such [[republic]]s were [[Republic of Venice|Venice]] and [[Genoa]], which had maritime, commercial, and territorial mini-empires. Sometimes the Doge ruled for life; sometimes for a fixed number of years.
