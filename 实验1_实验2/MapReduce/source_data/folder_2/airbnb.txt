 == Overview == 
 Airbnb clients have the opportunity to give for rent their housing partially or fully to different travelers. The site provides a platform for establishing contact between the owner and the guest. Airbnb is also responsible for money payments. Airbnb offers housing in 65,000 cities in 191 countries of the world. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Airbnb: Most Innovative Company|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/company/airbnb|access-date=2021-07-14|website=Fast Company|language=en-US|archive-date=2021-06-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210624041826/https://www.fastcompany.com/company/airbnb|url-status=live}} < /ref >  From the moment of founding in August 2008 and until April 2017, more than 150 million people have found housing through the Airbnb website. 
 
 Short-term [[Renting|rentals]] are [[Regulation|regulated]] in many places.  Hosts may have to have business [[License|licenses]]. They may have to  pay hotel taxes. There may be other rules.  In [[Italy]] they should collect  21% of landlords’ rental income as tax. In [[New York City|New York]] people cannot rent their homes for less than 30 days unless they are present on the property. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Airbnb to pay €576mn settlement in end to tax dispute in Italy|url=https://www.ft.com/content/6bb671df-bcbf-454b-9068-f1df064f8673|access-date=2023-12-14|website=www.ft.com}} < /ref >
