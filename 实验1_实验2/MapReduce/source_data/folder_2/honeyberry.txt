 The '''honeyberry''' (''Lonicera caerulea'') (also known as a '''blue honeysuckle''', '''sweetberry honeysuckle''', '''fly honeysuckle''', '''blue fly honeysuckle''', '''blue-berried honeysuckle''' or '''haskap''') is a [[fruit]] that is [[edible]]. < ref name=NGA > {{cite web |url=https://garden.org/ediblelandscaping/?page=201105-edible |title=Edible of the Month: Honeyberry |publisher=National Gardening Association |access-date=January 10, 2017}} < /ref >  It is a member of the [[honeysuckle]] family. < ref name=NGA/ >  It is easy to grow and has few [[pest]]s. < ref name=NGA/ >  They are native to [[Russia]] and can tolerate very cold weather. < ref name=GrantGKH > {{cite web |url=https://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/fruits/honeyberries/how-to-grow-honeyberries.htm |title=Haskap Berry Info – How To Grow Honeyberries In The Garden |author=Bonnie L. Grant |publisher=Gardening Know How |access-date=January 10, 2017}} < /ref >  They can survive down to {{convert|-55|°F|°C}}. < ref name=GrantGKH/ >  They have been raised for [[centuries]] in [[Asia]] and [[Eastern Europe]]. < ref name=GrantGKH/ >  Honeyberry is a well-behaved plant that is not seen as being an [[Invasive species]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://uncommonfruit.cias.wisc.edu/honeyberry/ |title=Uncommon Fruit |publisher=Board of Regents, University of Wisconsin |access-date=January 10, 2017 |archive-date=January 4, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170104025705/http://uncommonfruit.cias.wisc.edu/honeyberry/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
