 '''Sirenidae''' or '''sirens''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[aquatic]] [[salamander]]s. They have very small front legs and do not have back legs. < ref name= " EoR " > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/39559811|title=Encyclopedia of Reptiles  &  Amphibians|date=1998|isbn=0-12-178560-2|edition=2nd|location=San Diego, CA|oclc=39559811|last1=Zweifel |first1=Richard George }} < /ref >  They have [[gill]]s for breathing. Sirens are found only in the southeastern [[United States]] and northern [[Mexico]]. 
 
 ==Taxonomy== 
 The siren family (Sirenidae) is subdivided into two genera, with two species each: 
 
 Family '''SIRENIDAE''' 
 * Genus ''Pseudobranchus''  < small > (Gray, 1825) < /small >   & ndash; [[Dwarf siren]]s 
 ** ''Pseudobranchus axanthus''  < small > ([[M. Graham Netting|Netting]]  &  Goin, 1942) < /small >   & ndash; [[Southern Dwarf Siren]] 
 ** ''Pseudobranchus striatus''  < small > (LeConte, 1824) < /small >   & ndash; [[Northern Dwarf Siren]] 
 * Genus ''Siren''  < small > ([[Abraham Österdam|Österdam]], 1766) < /small >   & ndash; [[Siren (genus)|Sirens]] 
 ** ''Siren intermedia''  < small > (Barnes, 1826) < /small >   & ndash; [[Lesser Siren]] 
 ** ''Siren lacertina''  < small > ([[Carl Linnaeus|Linnaeus]], 1766) < /small >   & ndash; [[Greater Siren]]
