 '''''Inception''''' is a 2010 British-American [[science fiction]] [[action movie|action]] [[heist movie]] written and directed by [[Christopher Nolan]], who also produced the movie with his wife, [[Emma Thomas]]. The movie stars [[Leonardo DiCaprio]] as a professional thief who steals information by infiltrating the [[subconscious]] of his targets. He is offered a chance to have his criminal history erased as payment for the implantation of another person's idea into a target's subconscious. The ensemble cast includes [[Ken Watanabe]], [[Joseph Gordon-Levitt]], [[Marion Cotillard]], [[Elliot Page]], [[Tom Hardy]], [[Dileep Rao]], [[Cillian Murphy]], [[Tom Berenger]], and [[Michael Caine]]. < ref name= " synop " > {{cite web| url = http://screenrant.com/inception-plot-synopsis-deatails-mikee-58124/ | title = Updated 'Inception' Synopsis Reveals More | work = Screen Rant | date = 2010-05-05 | accessdate = 2010-07-18| first = Mike | last = Eisenberg| quote = One last job could give him his life back but only if he can accomplish the impossible—inception.}} < /ref > 
 
 The idea behind ''Inception'' was first developed over nine years before it was released. But, there is also another anime film just like it called Paprika (2006 film). After the 2002 completion of ''[[Insomnia (movie)|Insomnia]]'', Nolan wrote an 80-page [[movie treatment|treatment]] for a [[horror movie]] envisioning  " dream-stealers " , presenting the idea to [[Warner Bros.]] based on ideas of [[lucid dreaming]] and [[dream incubation]]. < ref > {{cite web | date = July 16, 2010 | author = Sheila Marikar | title = Inside 'Inception': Could Christopher Nolan's Dream World Exist in Real Life? Dream Experts Say 'Inception's' Conception of the Subconscious Isn't Far From Science | work =  [[ABC News]] | publisher = [[The Walt Disney Company]]| url = http://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/inside-inception-christopher-nolans-dream-world-exist-real/story?id=11174201 }} < /ref >    
 Deciding he needed more experience before tackling a production of this magnitude and complexity, Nolan shelved the project < ref name= " Itzkoff " > {{cite news | last= Itzkoff | first= Dave | title= A Man and His Dream: Christopher Nolan and ''Inception'' | work= [[The New York Times]] | date= 2010-06-30 | url= http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/06/30/a-man-and-his-dream-christopher-nolan-and-inception/ | accessdate= 2010-07-01| quote = This is a Film I first pitched to the studio probably nine years ago, and I wasn’t really ready to finish it. I needed more experience in making a big movie. }} < /ref >  and instead worked on 2005's ''[[Batman Begins]]'', 2006's ''[[The Prestige (movie)|The Prestige]]'', and ''[[The Dark Knight (movie)|The Dark Knight]]'' in 2008. He spent six months polishing up the script for ''Inception'' before Warner Bros. purchased it in February 2009. < ref name= " first " > {{cite news | author = Michael Fleming | url = http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117999988 | title = Nolan tackles ''Inception'' for WB | work = [[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date = 2009-02-11 | accessdate = 2009-02-25}} < /ref >  Filming spanned in six countries, beginning in Tokyo on June 19 and finishing in [[Canada]] in November 22. It was officially budgeted at $160 million, a cost which was shared between [[Warner Bros.]] and [[Legendary Pictures|Legendary]]. < ref name= " budget " > {{cite news | date = July 15, 2010 | last = Fritz |first= Ben| title = Movie projector: 'Inception' headed for No. 1, 'Sorcerer's Apprentice' to open in third | url = http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/entertainmentnewsbuzz/2010/07/inception-sorcerers-box-office.html | work = [[Los Angeles Times]] | quote = It's also one of the most expensive, at $160 million, a cost that was split by Warner and Legendary Pictures. | accessdate = 2010-07-27  }} < /ref >  Nolan's success with ''The Dark Knight'' helped gain the movie $100 million to spend on advertising. < ref name= " budget "  / > 
 
 ''Inception'' was first screened in [[London]] on July 8, 2010 and was shown in standard and [[IMAX]] theatres on July 16, 2010. < ref name= " release " > {{cite web |url= http://www.boxofficemojo.com/news/?id=2860 & p=.htm | title= Weekend Briefing: 'Inception' Breaks In, 'Apprentice' Lacks Magic |publisher= [[Internet Movie Database]] | work = Box Office Mojo |accessdate=2010-07-18}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web | date = July 14, 2010 | author = Jenny Peters | title = Partying for a Cause at the  " Inception "  Premiere | url = https://news.yahoo.com/s/fwd/20100714/en_fashion_fwd/partyingforacauseattheinceptionpremiere | work = Fashion Wire Daily | publisher = [[Yahoo!]] | access-date = August 31, 2017 | archive-date = July 21, 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100721022426/http://news.yahoo.com/s/fwd/20100714/en_fashion_fwd/partyingforacauseattheinceptionpremiere | url-status = dead }} < /ref >  ''Inception'' grossed over $828 million worldwide, becoming the fourth-highest-grossing movie of 2010. Considered one of the best movies of the 2010s, ''Inception'' received critical praise for its screenplay, direction, themes, action sequences, visual effects, musical score, and ensemble cast. < ref name= " mojo " > {{cite web |url = http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=inception.htm |title = Inception(2010) | publisher = [[Internet Movie Database]] | work = [[Box Office Mojo]] | accessdate = 2010-08-23 }} < /ref >  It won four [[Academy Awards]] ([[Academy Award for Best Cinematography|Best Cinematography]], [[Academy Award for Best Sound Editing|Best Sound Editing]], [[Academy Award for Best Sound Mixing|Best Sound Mixing]], and [[Academy Award for Best Visual Effects|Best Visual Effects]]) and was nominated for four more: [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]], [[Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay|Best Original Screenplay]], [[Academy Award for Best Art Direction|Best Art Direction]], and [[Academy Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Score]].
