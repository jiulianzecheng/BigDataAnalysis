 '''Spes''' was the personification of [[wikt:hope|hope]] in [[Ancient Rome]], especially the hope to have [[Child|children]], and the hope for a good [[harvest]]. Spes also has a counterpart in [[Greek mythology|Greek Mythology]], called [[Elpis]]. Usually, Spes was portrayed as a girl, on her toes. It was depicted as wearing clothes similar   Venus. Spes was venerated in Rome, at least from the 5th century BC.
