 The '''Philistines''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|ɪ|l|ᵻ|s|t|iː|n|z}}, {{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|ɪ|l|ᵻ|s|t|aɪ|n|z}}, {{IPAc-en|f|ᵻ|ˈ|l|ɪ|s|t|ᵻ|n|z}}, or {{IPAc-en|f|ᵻ|ˈ|l|ɪ|s|t|iː|n|z}}; < ref > [http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/Philistines Dictionary.com –  " Philistines " ]. < /ref >  {{lang-he-n|פְּלִשְׁתִּים}}, ''Plištim'') were a people described in the [[Bible]]. During the [[Book of Judges|Judges period]] they were one of the [[Israelite]]'s most dangerous [[enemy|enemies]]. < ref > Riad M. Nasser, ''Palestinian Identity in Jordan and Israel'' (New York: Routledge, 2005), p. 173 < /ref >   [[Biblical studies|Biblical scholars]] have connected the Philistines to other biblical groups such as [[Caphtorim]] and the [[Cherethites and Pelethites]]. Both have been identified with [[Crete]], and leading to the tradition of an [[Aegean Sea|Aegean]] origin. < ref name= " jewishencyclopedia " > {{cite web|url=http://www.jewishencyclopedia.com/articles/4303-cherethites|title=CHERETHITES|year=1906|publisher=jewishencyclopedia.com|access-date=25 September 2014}} < /ref >
