An '''antimetabole''' is the [[wikt:identical|identical]] or near [[repetition]] of words in one [[phrase]] or clause, in [[wikt:reverse|reverse]] order in the next phrase or clause. For example, US President [[John F. Kennedy]] used an antimetabole in this famous statement:  " Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country " . < ref > {{cite web |url=http://rhetoric.byu.edu/figures/a/antimetabole.htm |title=Antimetabole |work=rhetoric.byu.edu |year=2007 |access-date=26 May 2012 |archive-date=21 June 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120621000412/http://rhetoric.byu.edu/figures/A/antimetabole.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
