 The word '''fistula''' comes from a [[Latin]] word that means [[tube]] or [[pipe]]. In [[medicine]], a fistula is an [[wikt:abnormal|abnormal]] [[connection]] between two [[hollow]] spaces. This can be [[blood vessel|blood vessels]], [[intestine]]s, or other hollow [[Organ (anatomy)|organs]]. Fistulas are usually caused by [[injury]] or [[surgery]]. But they can also result from an [[infection]] or [[inflammation]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/ency/article/002365.htm|title=Fistula|work=nih.gov|accessdate=25 March 2015}} < /ref >  Fistulas are generally a [[disease]] condition, but [[Medical doctor|doctor]]s may create fistulas by [[surgery]] for [[medical]] reasons. 
 
 == Treatment == 
 
 [[Treatment]] for fistula depends on the reason for the fistula and on how serious it is.  Doctors often treat fistula with surgery and [[antibiotic]]s. The first step in treating a fistula is usually an [[wikt:examination|examination]] by a doctor.  The doctor determines the size and  " path "  that the fistula takes in the body.  
 
 == Types of fistula == 
 
 *[[Rectovaginal fistula]] between the [[rectum]] and [[vagina]]. 
 *[[Obstetric fistula]] between either the rectum and vagina or the rectum and [[bladder]] after severe or failed [[childbirth]], when adequate medical care is not available. 
 *[[Vesicovaginal fistula]] - a type of [[urogenital]] fistula (between the vagina and bladder)
