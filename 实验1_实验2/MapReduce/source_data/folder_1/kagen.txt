{{nihongo|'''Kagen'''|嘉元}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name|Japanese era]]|年号,|''[[nengō]]'',|lit.  " [[year]] name " }} after ''[[Kengen]]'' and before ''[[Tokuji]].''  This period started in August 1303 and ended in December 1306. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA447  " ''Kagen'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 447. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Nijō|Go-Nijō]]''-[[tennō]]''|後二条天皇}}. < ref > [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA275 ''Annales des empereurs du Japon,'' pp. 275-278]; [[H. Paul Varley|Varley, H. Paul]]. ''Jinnō Shōtōki,'' p. 239. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Kagen'' era== 
 * '''1304''' (''Kagen 2, 16th day of the 7th month''):  Former [[Emperor Go-Fukakusa]] died. < ref > Perkins, George W. (1990). [https://books.google.com/books?id=quaeAAAAIAAJ & dq=kengen+1302 & pg=PA150  ''The Clear Mirror: a Chronicle of the Japanese Court during the Kamakura Period,'' p. 150]; [[Imperial Household Agency]] (''Kunaichō''), [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/089/index.html 後深草天皇 (89)]; retrieved 2012-5-16. < /ref > 
 * '''1305''' (''Kagen 3, 15th day of the 9th month''):  Former [[Emperor Kameyama]] died. < ref > Perkins, [https://books.google.com/books?id=quaeAAAAIAAJ & dq=kengen+1302 & pg=PA151  p. 151]; ''Kunaichō'', [http://www.kunaicho.go.jp/ryobo/guide/090/index.html 亀山天皇 (90)]; retrieved 2012-5-16. < /ref > 
 * '''1305''' (''Kagen 3''): [[Ashikaga Takauji]] was born.  He would grow to become the founder and first [[shogun]] of the [[Ashikaga shogunate]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA55  " Ashikaga Takauji " ] at p. 55. < /ref >
