 '''Sachem''' and '''sagamores''' are terms for [[Native Americans in the United States|North American]] Indian chiefs. It is particularly used in [[Algonquian languages|Algonquian]]-speaking tribes of the [[Indigenous peoples of the Northeastern Woodlands|Northeastern Woodlands]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of SACHEM|url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sachem|access-date=2022-08-05|website=www.merriam-webster.com|language=en}} < /ref >  The sachem was the highest level of chiefs. The sagamore was a lower-ranking chief. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of SAGAMORE|url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/sagamore|access-date=2022-08-05|website=www.merriam-webster.com|language=en}} < /ref >  Examples of sachems include [[Massasoit]], [[Metacom]], [[Uncas]] and [[Daniel Nimham]]. [[Little Turtle]] is an example of a sagamore.
