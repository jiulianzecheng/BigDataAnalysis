 '''Primus''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band. It was made in [[El Sobrante, Contra Costa County, California|El Sobrante, California]] in 1984. The people that made Primus were [[Bass guitar|bassist]] and singer [[Les Claypool]] and [[Guitar|guitarist]] Todd Huth. The band's first drummer was Vince Parker. [[Jay Lane]] replaced Parker. Lane and Huth left the band in 1989. Guitarist [[Larry LaLonde]] and drummer [[Tim Alexander]] replaced them. 
 
 As of 2022, Primus has made nine [[Album|studio albums]]. The band's most popular albums are ''[[Sailing the Seas of Cheese]]'' (1991), ''[[Pork Soda]]'' (1993), and ''[[Tales from the Punchbowl]]'' (1995). The band has had two albums [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]. < ref name= " RIAA " > {{cite web|title=Primus - Gold and Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award & ar=Primus & ti= & lab= & genre= & format= & date_option=release & from= & to= & award= & type= & category= & adv=SEARCH#search_section|access-date=26 December 2022|publisher=RIAA}} < /ref >  ''Pork Soda'' and ''Tales from the Punchbowl'' went into the top ten on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' 200]], an American [[Record chart|music chart]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Primus|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/primus/chart-history/|access-date=26 December 2022|publisher=billboard.com}} < /ref >  ''Sailing the Seas of Cheese'' was their first popular album. It had a popular [[Single (music)|single]] named  " Jerry Was a Race Car Driver " . Primus has made other popular songs, such as  " My Name Is Mud " ,  " Wynona's Big Brown Beaver " , and  " John the Fisherman " . < ref > {{cite web|title=Primus Chart History|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/primus/chart-history/mrt/|access-date=26 December 2022|publisher=Billboard}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=Australian ARIA Top 100 Singles Chart - Week ending 6th August 1995|url=https://imgur.com/YMTEwtq|access-date=26 December 2022|website=Imgur}} < /ref > 
 
 The band took a break in 2000. They started making music again in 2003. < ref name= " RoSto Naugahyde " > {{cite magazine|author=Greg Prato|date=June 10, 2011|title=Return of Drummer Jay Lane has 'Breathed Life' Back Into Primus|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/les-claypool-return-of-drummer-jay-lane-has-breathed-life-back-into-primus-20110610|url-status=live|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110612031719/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/les-claypool-return-of-drummer-jay-lane-has-breathed-life-back-into-primus-20110610|archive-date=June 12, 2011|access-date=26 December 2022}} < /ref >  They did not make another album until ''Green Naugahyde'' in 2011. < ref name= " Naugahyde press " > {{cite web|author=Press release|date=June 6, 2011|title=Primus to Release New Album in September|url=http://www.theprp.com/2011/06/06/news/primus-to-release-new-album-in-september/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110610123045/http://www.theprp.com/2011/06/06/news/primus-to-release-new-album-in-september/|archive-date=June 10, 2011|access-date=26 December 2022|publisher=theprp.com}} < /ref >  Since then, Primus has made two more albums. 
 
 == History == 
 
 === The band starts, ''Frizzle Fry'' (1984–1990) === 
 [[File:Les_Claypool_at_The_Roxy,_West_Hollywood,_Cica_1999.jpg|thumb|288x288px|Les Claypool was one of the people who made the band. He plays the [[bass guitar]].]]

 After making the demo, Primus had many different drummers. None of them stayed in the band for a long time. In 1988, [[Jay Lane]] started playing the drums for the band. < ref name= " through the years2 "  / >  The band made a demo named ''Sausage''. Later, Lane and Huth left the band. Huth wanted to be with his family more, and Lane was making other music. Primus stopped making music. Claypool joined [[Blind Illusion]]. This was a band he had already been in. The guitarist for Blind Illusion was [[Larry LaLonde]]. < ref name= " birthday " > {{cite web|author=Randy Reiss|date=September 1997|title=You Say It's Your Birthday: Les Claypool of Primus|url=http://www.ram.org/music/primus/articles/les_bday_atn.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120718203335/http://www.ram.org/music/primus/articles/les_bday_atn.html|archive-date=July 18, 2012|access-date=May 1, 2011|work=Addicted to Noise, via ram.org}} < /ref >  Claypool asked LaLonde if he wanted to join Primus. LaLonde joined Primus, and the band started again. [[Tim Alexander]] joined the band to play the drums. After a month, they made their first album. < ref name= " RoSto Naugahyde2 " > {{cite magazine|author=Greg Prato|date=June 10, 2011|title=Return of Drummer Jay Lane has 'Breathed Life' Back Into Primus|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/les-claypool-return-of-drummer-jay-lane-has-breathed-life-back-into-primus-20110610|url-status=live|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110612031719/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/les-claypool-return-of-drummer-jay-lane-has-breathed-life-back-into-primus-20110610|archive-date=June 12, 2011|access-date=June 12, 2011}} < /ref >  It was named ''Suck on This''. It was a [[live album]] with music from the band's [[Concert|concerts]]. < ref name= " animals discog " > Illustrated Discography, ''[[Animals Should Not Try to Act Like People]]'' DVD, 2003. < /ref > 
 
 In 1990, the band made ''[[Frizzle Fry]]''. It was their first [[Album|studio album]]. The band became more popular because of the album. < ref name= " hart " > {{Cite magazine|last=Hart|first=Ron|date=February 7, 2020|title=Les Claypool Looks Back on Primus' Debut 'Frizzle Fry' at 30|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8550442/les-claypool-primus-debut-frizzle-fry|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=February 11, 2020}} < /ref >  They also became more popular because they went on a [[Concert|concert tour]] with [[Jane's Addiction]]. Large [[Record label|record labels]] noticed that the band was getting popular. Primus joined [[Interscope Records]] in 1990. 
 
 === Popular albums (1991–1996) === 
 The band released their second album in 1991. It was named ''[[Sailing the Seas of Cheese]]''. Two [[Single (music)|singles]] from the album had [[Music video|music videos]] that were shown on [[MTV]]. These were  " [[Jerry Was a Race Car Driver]] "  and  " Tommy the Cat " . The album was [[RIAA certification|certified gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]. < ref name= " riaa " > {{cite web|title=RIAA - Gold  &  Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinum.php|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101210073830/http://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinum.php|archive-date=2010-12-10|access-date=2009-07-21|publisher=[[RIAA]]}} < /ref >  Primus went on tour with many bands, such as [[Rush (band)|Rush]], [[U2]], [[Fishbone]], [[Public Enemy]], and [[Anthrax (American band)|Anthrax]]. 
 
 Primus was in the movie ''[[Bill  &  Ted's Bogus Journey]].'' They played music in it. They also made a song for a [[Beavis and Butt-Head]] album. In 1992, Primus made an [[extended play]] (EP) of [[Cover version|cover songs]]. It was named ''[[Miscellaneous Debris]]''. A song from the EP named  " Making Plans for Nigel "  was played on the radio many times. It went onto the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' charts]]. < ref > {{cite magazine|title=Primus Chart History, Singles|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=primus|chart=all}}|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 1, 2007}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1993, Primus released their third album. It was named ''[[Pork Soda]]''. It went into the top 10 on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' 200]] music chart. It had a popular single named  " My Name Is Mud " . The band played music to [[Lollapalooza]] in the same year. They were the main band at Lollapalooza. They also played music at [[Woodstock '94]]. < ref > [https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6221688/exclusive-primus-les-claypool-talks-woodstock-94-20-years-later Primus' Les Claypool Talks Woodstock '94, 20 Years Later] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171022090358/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6221688/exclusive-primus-les-claypool-talks-woodstock-94-20-years-later|date=October 22, 2017}} billboard.com < /ref >  When they started playing  " My Name Is Mud "  at Woodstock, people threw mud at the band. The band stopped playing music, and Claypool said  " the song is called 'My Name Is Mud', but keep the mud to yourselves you son-of-a-bitch " . < ref > {{Cite web|date=August 8, 2014|title=Full Show Friday  & #124; Primus At Woodstock 1994|url=https://www.jambase.com/article/full-show-friday-primus-at-woodstock-1994|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190308003021/https://www.jambase.com/article/full-show-friday-primus-at-woodstock-1994|archive-date=March 8, 2019|access-date=March 7, 2019|website=JamBase}} < /ref > 
 
 Claypool, Todd Huth, and Jay Lane made a band in 1994. It was named Sausage. Its name came from Primus' first demo. They made an album named ''Riddles Are Abound Tonight''. 
 
 Primus released their fourth album in 1994. It was named ''[[Tales from the Punchbowl|Tales From the Punchbowl]]''. The album has Primus' most successful [[Single (music)|single]]. It is named  " [[Wynona's Big Brown Beaver]] " . The single was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]]. The song's music video shows the band dressed in [[cowboy]] clothes. The band played music on the [[Late Show with David Letterman]] and [[Late Night with Conan O'Brien]]. Later, Claypool made an album by himself. It was named ''[[Highball with the Devil]].'' Many other musicians helped him make the music for it. 
 
 === ''Brown Album'' and ''Antipop'' (1997–2000) === 
 Tim Alexander left the band. [[Brain (musician)|Bryan Mantia]] replaced him. < ref > [http://www.ram.org/music/primus/articles/BAMinter.html Interview with Primus taken from BAM magazine, July 11, 1997.] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140818020245/http://ram.org/music/primus/articles/BAMinter.html|date=August 18, 2014}} Retrieved October 22, 2012. < /ref >  Mantia was the new drummer. In 1996, Primus made the [[Theme (music)|theme song]] for ''[[South Park]]''. They also made other music for the show. 
 [[File:Primus_copenhagen_1998.jpg|thumb|281x281px|Primus in 1998]] 
 ''[[Brown Album]]'' was released in 1997. It had two singles. After the album was released, Primus went on the first [[SnoCore Tour|SnoCore]] concert tour. They went with bands such as [[Blink-182]] and [[the Aquabats]]. The band released an extended play named ''Rhinoplasty''. It had [[Cover version|cover songs]] and [[Concert|live songs]] on it. A video version of ''Rhinoplasty'' was also made. It was named ''Videoplasty''. 
 
 The band released their sixth album in 1999. It was named ''[[Antipop]]''. Many popular musicians helped make the music on the album. Some of these musicians were [[Tom Waits]], [[Fred Durst]], [[Tom Morello]], and [[James Hetfield]]. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/limpbizkit0000deve/page/159|title=Limp Bizkit|last1=Devenish|first1=Colin|publisher=St. Martin's|year=2000|isbn=0-312-26349-X|pages=[https://archive.org/details/limpbizkit0000deve/page/159 159–60]}} < /ref >  Some critics said the album sounded similar to [[nu metal]]. < ref > {{cite magazine|last=Schultz|first=Christopher|title=Primus, 'Green Naugahyde'|url=http://www.spin.com/reviews/primus-green-naugahyde-prawn-songato|url-status=live|magazine=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121022115637/http://www.spin.com/reviews/primus-green-naugahyde-prawn-songato|archive-date=October 22, 2012|access-date=November 24, 2012}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news|last=Uley|first=Jeremy|title=CD Review: PRIMUS Green Naugahyde|work=Metal Injection|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/reviews/primus-emgreen-naugahydeem|url-status=live|access-date=November 24, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130527214729/http://www.metalinjection.net/reviews/primus-emgreen-naugahydeem|archive-date=May 27, 2013}} < /ref >  Nu metal was popular at the time. The band went to Ozzfest (a [[music festival]]) after the album was released. They made a music video for  " Lacquer Head " . This was a song from the album. It was not shown on MTV. This was because it showed [[illegal drugs]]. The song did not support using illegal drugs. 
 
 In 2000, Primus made a cover version of  " [[N.I.B.|N.I.B]] " . This was a song made by [[Black Sabbath]]. It was put on a [[Album|tribute album]] for Black Sabbath. 
 
 === The band takes a break (2000–2009) === 
 The band did not make any music from 2000 to 2003. Claypool said that making music in Primus  " wasn't fun anymore " . < ref name= " RoSto Naugahyde2 "  / >  Tim Alexander played with many bands during the break. He played music with bands such as [[A Perfect Circle]] and the [[Blue Man Group]]. Claypool made his own band named Oysterhead. The other members of the band were [[Stewart Copeland]] and [[Trey Anastasio]]. He also made [[Colonel Claypool's Bucket of Bernie Brains]]. This was a band with Mantia, [[Buckethead]] and [[Bernie Worrell]]. 
 
 In 2003, Primus started making music again. Mantia did not play the drums for the band. Alexander joined the band again to play the drums. They made an EP with five songs on it. They also made a [[DVD]] with videos from concerts and music videos on it. The band sold the EP and DVD together. The release was named ''[[Animals Should Not Try to Act Like People]].'' The band played at concerts for two months. In 2004, they kept going to concerts. They recorded their concert in Chicago. The video was released as ''Hallucino-Genetics: Live 2004.'' 
 
 The band played at [[Lollapalooza]] in 2005. Claypool made a new music group named Les Claypool's Fancy Band. This band played music at some concerts. The song  " John the Fisherman "  was put on [[Guitar Hero II]]. < ref > {{cite web|date=2006-10-09|title=Guitar Hero II Final Tracklist Revealed|url=http://xbox360.ign.com/articles/737/737956p1.html|access-date=2009-07-21|publisher=[[IGN]]}} < /ref >
