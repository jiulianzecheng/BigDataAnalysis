 '''''Papurana''''' is a genus of frogs in the family [[Ranidae]],  " true frogs. " < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  They live in [[New Guinea]], some islands near New Guinea, and in northern [[Australia]]. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Oliver et al. 2015 " / >  ''[[Papurana daemeli]]'' is the only frog in Ranidae that lives in [[Australia]]. < ref name= " Ingram 1993 " / > 
 
 ==Taxonomy== 
 Scientists first named ''Papurana'' as a [[subgenus]] of ''[[Rana (genus)|Rana]]''. At times, they have said it was a subgenus of ''[[Hylarana]]''. In 2015, Oliver and colleagues looked at ''Hylarana'' closely and said it should have only a few species in it.  They said ''Papurana'' should be its own genus. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name= " Oliver et al. 2015 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 ''Papurana'' frogs have a mask mark behind their eyes, their body shape, strong wavy stripes on the backs of their thighs, and either no dorsolateral folds or very thin folds, with rough spots. The [[Dorsum (anatomy)|back]] is evenly roughened to warty and sometimes has small spikes. The frogs are medium to very large in size. Male frogs have paired, external [[vocal sac|organs for making sound]]s. < ref name= " Oliver et al. 2015 " / >
