 '''George Mamua Telek''' (born 1969), commonly known simply as '''Telek''', is a musician from [[Papua New Guinea]]. He is one of the country's most successful musicians, < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/gig-reviews/sing-sing/2006/05/12/1146940705442.html|title=Sing Sing|first=Jack|last=Marx|work=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=12 May 2006|accessdate=6 November 2012}} < /ref >  and his music is well known in [[Australia]] and the [[Pacific Islands]]. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.postcourier.com.pg/20050202/whatson.htm|title=ENB artist claims Telek got his song|first=Jessie|last=Lapou|newspaper=Post Courier|date=2 February 2005|accessdate=6 November 2012|archive-date=8 February 2005|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050208121014/http://www.postcourier.com.pg/20050202/whatson.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Telek was born in Raluana, a village near Rabaul in [[New Britain]]. He sings in his native language, [[Kuanua]], and also in [[Tok Pisin]]. 
 
 Telek sang with various bands in Papua New Guinea early in his career, most notably with the group [[Painim Wok]], famous in the 1980s. Telek met Australian musician [[David Bridie]] in Rabaul in the mid-1980s. Together they recorded the very successful album ''Tabaran''. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.theage.com.au/articles/2004/12/03/1101923277448.html|title=Volcanic rock|author=Staff writer|newspaper=The Age|date=3 December 2004|accessdate=6 November 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 Telek recorded his first solo album, ''Telek'', outside Papua New Guinea in 1997. This album won an [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] award for Best World Music Album. He recorded his second international album ''Serious Tam'' in 2000 at [[Peter Gabriel]]'s studios in [[London]]. The next album he released was ''Amette'', in 2004. He recorded it in Bridie's studio in Melbourne. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/Music/Magic-sounds-from-forgotten-islands/2004/11/30/1101577477942.html|title=Magic sounds from forgotten islands|first=Bruce|last=Elder|newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=1 December 2004|accessdate=6 November 2012}} < /ref >  His most recent album ''Akave'' was released through the Wantok Music-label.
