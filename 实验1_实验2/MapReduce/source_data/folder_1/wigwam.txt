 A '''wigwam''', '''wickiup''', '''wetu''' ([[Wampanoag]]), or '''wiigiwaam''' ([[Ojibwe language|Ojibwe]]) was a type of home for [[Native American|Native Americans]]. The homes were often shaped like domes and were round. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of WIGWAM|url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/wigwam|access-date=2022-07-31|website=www.merriam-webster.com|language=en}} < /ref >  It is different than a [[longhouse]] or [[tipi]]. Wigwams were common in the [[Northeastern Woodlands]]. They were also in the [[Indigenous peoples of the Southeastern Woodlands|Southeastern Woodlands]] and the [[Western United States]]. Wood was used for the frames. Materials to make roofs included bark, hides, grass, brush, rushes, mats, reeds, or cloth. The homes were good against many types of weather conditions. Wigwams were built by bending young trees and placing them along a circle. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Wigwam {{!}} The Canadian Encyclopedia|url=https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/wigwam|access-date=2022-07-31|website=www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca}} < /ref >  Different native groups had different names for the wigwam. ''Wigwam'' was used in the Northeastern Woodlands. The term ''wickiup'' was used in the Southeast and Western regions. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Wigwam - New World Encyclopedia|url=https://www.newworldencyclopedia.org/entry/wigwam|access-date=2022-07-31|website=www.newworldencyclopedia.org}} < /ref >
