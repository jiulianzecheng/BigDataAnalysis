 '''''Phantasmarana''''' is a group of [[Frog|frogs]] in the family [[Hylodidae]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web |title=Phantasmarana Vittorazzi, Augusto-Alves, Neves-da-Silva, Carvalho-e-Silva, Recco-Pimentel, Toledo, Lourenço, and Bruschi, 2021 {{!}} Amphibian Species of the World |url=https://amphibiansoftheworld.amnh.org/Amphibia/Anura/Hylodidae/Phantasmarana |access-date=2022-09-11 |website=amphibiansoftheworld.amnh.org}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |title=Hylodidae Günther, 1858 {{!}} Amphibian Species of the World |url=https://amphibiansoftheworld.amnh.org/Amphibia/Anura/Hylodidae |access-date=2022-09-11 |website=amphibiansoftheworld.amnh.org}} < /ref >  These frogs live in [[Brazil]], in the [[Atlantic Forest]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 These frogs live in fast-flowing rivers. Scientists can't catch them easily. They jump into the water. These frogs have long, fanglike teeth. This might be because they eat small [[vertebrates|animals with bones]] as well as insects and other [[invertebrate|animals without bones]]. The [[Tadpole|tadpoles]] swim in the water. ''Phantasmarana'' are large frogs. Adults can be as long as {{convert|120|mm|abbr=on}} [[snout–vent length|from nose to rear end]]. < ref name= " VittCaldwell " > {{cite book |last1=Vitt |first1=Laurie J. |title=Herpetology: An Introductory Biology of Amphibians and Reptiles |last2=Caldwell |first2=Janalee P. |publisher=Academic Press |year=2014 |edition=4th |page=486}} < /ref > 
 
 The genus has the name ''Phantasmarana'' ( " phantom frog " ) because the wild frogs are so rare and hard to catch. It also has this name because the frogs do not sing or call with their voices. Instead, they make strange noises. Members of ''Phantasmarana'' were formerly placed in ''[[Megaelosia]]''.
