'''Occident''' is a term meaning  " the west " . 
 
 Edward Said also used the term in his book [[Orientalism (book)|Orientalism]] (1978). He used it to mean Western countries that had colonized the [[Orient]]. This in particular includes France, Great Britain and later the United States. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Pedagogy of the Other: Edward Said, Postcolonial Theory, and Strategies for Critique|last=Burney|first=Shehla|publisher=Peter Lang|year=2012|pages=24|chapter=Orientalism: The Making of the Other}} < /ref >
