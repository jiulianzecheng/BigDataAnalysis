 The '''actinide''' series is the 15 [[chemical element]]s between [[actinium]] and [[lawrencium]] on the [[periodic table]]. They have the [[atomic number]]s between 89 and 103. The actinide series is named after actinium. All of the elements are [[radioactive]]. [[Uranium]] is the most common natural actinide, and [[thorium]] second. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Anonymous|date=2018-04-19|title=Properties of Actinide Materials under Extreme Conditions|url=https://ec.europa.eu/jrc/en/research-facility/properties-actinide-materials-under-extreme-conditions|access-date=2021-11-11|website=EU Science Hub - European Commission|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The term ‘'''actinide series’''' has been derived from the first element of the series, actinium. The symbol '''An''' is used while referring to any of the actinide series elements. 
 
 All actinide series elements are radioactive in nature, they release a large amount of energy on radioactive decay. Uranium and thorium are the most abundant naturally occurring actinides on earth, whereas [[plutonium]] is synthetically obtained. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Actinide - an overview {{!}} ScienceDirect Topics|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/earth-and-planetary-sciences/actinide|access-date=2021-11-11|website=www.sciencedirect.com}} < /ref >
