 '''Watarru''' is an [[Indigenous Australians|Aboriginal]] community in the [[Aṉangu Pitjantjatjara Yankunytjatjara|Aṉangu Pitjantjatjara Yankunytjatjara Lands]] in [[South Australia]]. Watarru is at the base of [[Birksgate Range|Mount Lindsay]], about {{convert|550|km}} south-west of [[Alice Springs]]. It is surrounded by the [[Great Victoria Desert]], and is one of the most [[wikt:remote|remote]] settlements in Australia. It can be accessed by road from the [[Stuart Highway]] over roughly {{convert|650|km|abbr=on}} dirt road.  
 
 The nearest neighbouring settlement is [[Pipalyatjara, South Australia|Pipalyatjara]], about {{convert|110|km|abbr=on}} to the northeast. There were 56 people living at Watarru in the 2001 census, with 82.1% of those being Indigenous Australians. The village was established as a small family [[Outstation movement|outstation]], and began to grow during the 1990s. < ref > {{cite web|title=Watarru|url=http://www.waru.org/communities/watarru/|work=Waru|publisher=PY Media|accessdate=28 October 2012|archive-date=8 April 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080408003253/http://www.waru.org/communities/watarru/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 The community owns and manages the [[Watarru Indigenous Protected Area]], which surrounds the town for more than {{convert|13948|km2}}.
