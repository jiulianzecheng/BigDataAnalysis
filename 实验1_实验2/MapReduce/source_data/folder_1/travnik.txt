 '''Travnik'''  (''Травник'') is a town in central [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]], {{convert|90|km|0|abbr=off}} west of [[Sarajevo]]. It is the [[capital city|capital]] of the [[Central Bosnia Canton]]. < ref name=OV > {{cite web |url=http://www.ovguide.com/travnik-9202a8c04000641f8000000000328e74 |title=Travnik Video, Tourism and Events |publisher=OVGuide |access-date=December 30, 2016 |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304071722/http://www.ovguide.com/travnik-9202a8c04000641f8000000000328e74 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is in the Travnik Municipality. In 2013, the town of Travnik had 16,534 residents. < ref name=OV/ >  The Travnik [[metropolitan area]] population was 57,543 people. < ref name=OV/ >  In 1699 it became the residence of the [[vizier]]s of the [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman province]] of Bosnia. < ref name=ANU > {{cite web |url=http://anu.edu.ba/travnik/ |title=Travnik |publisher=American Northwest University |access-date=December 30, 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 Travnik is close to the [[Geographical centre|geographic center]] of Bosnia and Herzegovina at {{Coord|44|14|N|17|40|E|}}. < ref name=Traveltill > {{cite web |url=http://www.traveltill.com/destination/Bosnia-and-Herzegovina/Travnik/location.php |title=Location of Travnik |publisher=Traveltill.com |access-date=December 30, 2016}} < /ref >  The river [[Lašva]] flows from west to east through the city. < ref name=Traveltill/ >  Travnik is in the [[Lašva valley]]. This valley connects the Bosna river valley to the [[Vrbas (river)|Vrbas]] river valley. < ref > Mučibabić  B., Ed. (1998): Geografski atlas Bosne i Hercegovine. Geodetski zavod BiH, Sarajevo, {{ISBN|9958-766-00-0}}., pp: 4-5 < /ref > < ref > Vojnogeografski institut, Ed. (1955): Travnik (List karte 1:100.000, Izohipse na 20 m). Vojnogeografski institut, Beograd. < /ref > 
 
 Travnik is {{convert|514|m|ft|abbr=off}} [[Above mean sea level|above sea level]]. < ref name=Traveltill/ >  The Vilenica and [[Vlašić mountain]]s are two of the geographical features found near Travnik. < ref name=Traveltill/ >  Vlašić mountain is at the very center of Bosnia and Herzegovina. < ref name=Traveltill/ >  Its highest [[peak]] is Paljenik with an [[elevation]] of {{convert|1943|m}}. < ref name=Traveltill/ >
