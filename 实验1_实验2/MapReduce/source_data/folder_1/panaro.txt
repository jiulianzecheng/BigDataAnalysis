 The '''Panaro''' ({{lang-la|Scultenna}}) is a [[river]] of northeastern [[Italy]], the last important right [[tributary]] of the [[Po (river)|Po]] [[river]]. It starts in the Northern [[Apennines]] [[mountain range]] and flows through the [[Emilia-Romagna]] [[Regions of Italy|region]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:Scoltenna tra Riolunato e Pievepelago.JPG|thumb|left|Scoltenna (Panaro) river]] 
 The Panaro river has a length of {{Convert|148|km|mi|0|abbr=on}}, and a [[drainage basin]] with an area of approximately {{Convert|2292|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " trecani " > {{cite web |url=http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/panaro/ |title=Panaro |publisher=Treccani.it - Enciclopedie on line |language=it |access-date=27 September 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 ===Course=== 
 [[File:Scoltenna a Pievepelago.JPG|thumb|The Scoltenna (Panaro) in Pievepelago]] 
 The Panaro river, with the name ''Rio delle Tagliole'', starts in the northern [[Apennines]] where Emilia-Romagna meets [[Tuscany]], in the place called ''Foce a Giovo'' near the [[Monte Cimone]]. 
 
 From here it flows to the northeast down the [[valley]]. At [[Pievepelago]] (in the [[Province of Modena|Modena]] province), its name changes to '''''Scoltenna'''''. < ref name= " trecani "  / >  Finally, the name is changed to Panaro after the [[confluence]] of the Scoltena and the ''Leo'' [[stream]] in the Montespecchio valley, [[Pavullo nel Frignano]]. < ref name= " autorita " > {{cite web |url=http://www.adbpo.it/on-multi/adbpolight/Home/Pianificazione/PianostralcioperlAssettoIdrogeologicoPAI/Pianovigente/Lineegeneralidiassettoidraulicoeidrogeologico/documento5422.html |title=Linee generali di assetto idraulico e idrogeologico nel bacino del Panaro |publisher=Autorit√† di bacino del fiume Po |language=it |format=PDF |access-date=27 September 2016 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 It borders the ''Parco regionale dei Sassi di Roccamalatina'' ( " Regional Park of the Sassi di Roccamalatina " ) and keeps flowing to the north. South of [[Modena]], in ''Ponte Guerro'', it is joined by the ''Guerro'' stream and in ''Fossalta'', a place to the east of [[Modena]], it is joined, as a left [[tributary]], by the ''Tiepido'' stream, its main tributary. < ref name= " autorita "  / > 
 
 At [[Bomporto]], the river joins the ''Naviglio de Modena'' ( " Modena [[canal]] or [[waterway]] " ) and can be [[:wikt:navigate|navigated]] until its confluence with the Po, on the border with the [[Veneto]] region and to the west of [[Ferrara]]. 
 
 The Panaro river flows through the [[city|cities]] of [[Vignola]], [[Finale Emilia]] and [[Bondeno]] and just to the east of Modena. 
 
 Of [[historical]] and [[art]]istic interest is the '''[[bridge]] of Olina''' ({{coord|44.298474|N|10.782833|E|display=inline |region:IT_type:river_source:GNS-enwiki}}), built in 1522 across the river (still called Scoltenna) near the [[town]] of the same name, in the [[commune]] of [[Pavullo nel Frignano]] 
 
 The Panaro flows mainly through the province of Modena but it also flows for a short distance in the [[Province of Ferrara|Ferrara]] province after [[Finale Emilia]]. It flows through several [[comune]]s; some of them, all in the Modena province, Emilia-Romagna region, are:

 ==Main tributaries == 
 The [[Tributary|tributaries]] of the Panaro river are small streams; the most importants are the Guerro ({{convert|25|km|mi|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}) and the Tiepido ({{convert|29.5|km|mi|abbr=on|disp=x| [|]}}); they join the Panaro from the left side.
