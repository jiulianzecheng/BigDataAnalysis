 '''Kapae-a-Lakona''' — also spelled '''Kapea-a-Lakona''' — was High Chief of [[Oahu]] in ancient [[Hawaii]]. < ref > The World Archaeological Congress, Southampton and London: ''Comparative studies in the development of complex societies'' < /ref > 
 == Family == 
 He was a son of [[Lakona of Oahu|High Chief Lakona of Oʻahu]] < ref > Ross H. Cordy (1981). ''A study of prehistoric social change: the development of complex societies in the Hawaiian Islands''. < /ref >  and his wife, Chiefess Alaʻikauakoko < ref > ''[http://www.kekoolani.org/pages/kekoolani%20genealogy%20database%20(paf)/pafg136.htm#2408 Kapaealakona (Kapea-a-Lakona)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161021192512/http://www.kekoolani.org/pages/kekoolani%20genealogy%20database%20(paf)/pafg136.htm#2408 |date=2016-10-21 }}'' < /ref >  — daughter of a man named Pokai and his wife Hineuki. < ref > Peleioholani, Solomon Lehuanui Kalaniomaiheuila (1906). ''Genealogy of the Robinson family, and ancient legends and chants of Hawaii''. [[Honolulu]] Bulletin Publishing Company, Ltd., 1908, Hawaii State Archives (Photocopy), CS 2209. R62 P45 1908. < /ref >  After his father's death, Kapae became the Chief of Oʻahu. Kapae married a woman named Wehina, whose parents are not known today. Wehina and Kapae's only known child was Chief [[Haka of Oahu]].
