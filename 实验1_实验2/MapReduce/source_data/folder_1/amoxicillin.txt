 '''Amoxicillin''' is an [[antibiotic]] used to treat some [[bacteria]]l [[infection]]s. < ref name= " AHFS2015 "  / >  It is one of the most common antibiotics given to children. < ref > {{cite book| vauthors = Kelly D |title=Diseases of the liver and biliary system in children|date=2008|publisher=Wiley-Blackwell|location=Chichester, UK|isbn=978-1-4443-0054-3|page=217|edition=3|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=z7nO3OrNXckC & pg=PA217|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908135935/https://books.google.com/books?id=z7nO3OrNXckC & pg=PA217|archive-date=8 September 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 It may be given for middle [[ear infection]], [[strep throat]], [[pneumonia]], [[cellulitis|skin infections]], and [[urinary tract infection]]s among others. < ref name=AHFS2015/ >  It is taken by mouth, or less commonly by injection. < ref name=AHFS2015 > {{cite web|title=Amoxicillin|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/amoxicillin.html|work=The American Society of Health-System Pharmacists|access-date=1 August 2015|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150905150500/http://www.drugs.com/monograph/amoxicillin.html|archive-date=5 September 2015}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=Amoxicillin Sodium for Injection|url=https://www.medicines.org.uk/emc/medicine/5359|website=EMC|access-date=26 October 2016|date=10 February 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161027054630/https://www.medicines.org.uk/emc/medicine/5359|archive-date=27 October 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 Common bad [[side effect]]s are [[nausea]] and a [[rash]]. < ref name=AHFS2015/ >  It can also make [[candidiasis|yeast infections]] more likely. When taken with [[clavulanic acid]], it can cause [[diarrhea]]. < ref name= " Common harms from amoxicillin: a sy " > {{cite journal | vauthors = Gillies M, Ranakusuma A, Hoffmann T, Thorning S, McGuire T, Glasziou P, Del Mar C | title = Common harms from amoxicillin: a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized placebo-controlled trials for any indication | journal = CMAJ | volume = 187 | issue = 1 | pages = E21–E31 | date = January 2016| pmid = 25404399 | pmc = 4284189 | doi = 10.1503/cmaj.140848 }} < /ref >  It should not be used on someone [[allergy|allergic]] to [[penicillin]].  < ref name=AHFS2015/ >  It can be used for people with [[kidney]] problems, but less should be given than normal. < ref name=AHFS2015/ >  It may be used on [[pregnant]] women or women who are [[breastfeeding]]. < ref name=AHFS2015/ >   
 
 Amoxicillin was discovered in 1958 and its use started in 1972. < ref > {{cite book| vauthors = Fischer J, Ganellin CR |title=Analogue-based Drug Discovery|date=2006|publisher=John Wiley  &  Sons|isbn=978-3-527-60749-5|page=490|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FjKfqkaKkAAC & pg=PA490|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908135935/https://books.google.com/books?id=FjKfqkaKkAAC & pg=PA490|archive-date=8 September 2017}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| vauthors = Roy J |title=An introduction to pharmaceutical sciences production, chemistry, techniques and technology|date=2012|publisher=Woodhead Pub.|location=Cambridge|isbn=978-1-908818-04-1|page=239|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0IdmAgAAQBAJ & pg=PA239|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908135935/https://books.google.com/books?id=0IdmAgAAQBAJ & pg=PA239|archive-date=8 September 2017}} < /ref >  It is on the (WHO) [[WHO Model List of Essential Medicines|World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines]]. < ref name= " WHO21st " > {{cite book | vauthors = ((World Health Organization)) | title = World Health Organization model list of essential medicines: 21st list 2019 | year = 2019 | hdl = 10665/325771 | author-link = World Health Organization | publisher = World Health Organization | location = Geneva | id = WHO/MVP/EMP/IAU/2019.06.
