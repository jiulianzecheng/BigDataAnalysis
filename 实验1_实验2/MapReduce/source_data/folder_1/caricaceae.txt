 '''Caricaceae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[flowering plant]]s in the order [[Brassicales]], found in [[tropical]] regions of [[Central America|Central]] and [[South America]] and [[Africa]]. They are short-lived [[evergreen]] [[pachycaul]] [[shrub]]s or small [[tree]]s growing to 5–10 m tall; many bear edible [[fruit]]. 
 
 The family comprises six genera and about 34-35 species: 
 * ''[[Carica]]'' – one species ''[[Papaya|Carica papaya]]'' ([[Papaya]]), Americas 
 * ''[[Cylicomorpha]]'' – two species, Africa 
 * ''[[Horovitzia]]'' – one species, Mexico 
 * ''[[Jacaratia]]'' – eight species, Americas 
 * ''[[Jarilla]]'' – three species, Americas 
 * ''[[Vasconcellea]]'' – 20-21 species, Americas
