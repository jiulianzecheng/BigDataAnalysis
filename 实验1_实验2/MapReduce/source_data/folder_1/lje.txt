 The [[Cyrillic alphabet|Cyrillic]] letter '''lje''' (Љ, љ) is a combination of [[El (Cyrillic)|Л]] and [[Soft sign|Ь]] and is used in [[Serbian language|Serbian]] and [[Macedonian language|Macedonian]]. It is used to denote the [[palatal lateral approximant]] {{IPA|/ʎ/}}, a sound similar (but not equal) to the palatalized alveolar lateral {{IPA|/lʲ/}} represented by the digraph Ль. It was created by [[Vuk Karadžić]] and is same to the [[Digraph (orthography)|Latin digraph]] [[Lj (letter)|{{Unicode|ǈ}}]] in [[Serbian language]] and [[Croatian language]]. It is romanized using the letter L with caron (Ľ ľ). It also takes the form of Ль.
