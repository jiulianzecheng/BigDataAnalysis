 {{nihongo|'''Suisei'''|すいせい||}} is the name of a [[Japan]]ese  [[spacecraft]]. < ref > Suisei was identical in construction and shape to [[Sakigake]], but carried a different payload. < /ref >   This was Japan's first interplanetary mission. < ref > {{cite book| last1 = Harvey| first1 = Brian| last2 = Smid| first2 = Henk H.F.| last3 = Pirard| first3 = Theo| title = Emerging Space Powers: The New Space Programs of Asia, the Middle East and South-America| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=XD1ZaYbiWwMC & pg=PA54| year = 2011| publisher = Springer Science  &  Business Media| isbn = 978-1-4419-0874-2| page = 54 }} < /ref > 
 
 The launch was a project of the [[Institute of Space and Astronautical Science]] of the [[University of Tokyo]]. 
 
 The unmanned [[space probe]] was sent into space on August 18, 1985. < ref > JAXA, [http://www.isas.ac.jp/e/enterp/missions/catalogue.shtml   " Catalogue of ISAS Missions " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141231025049/http://www.isas.ac.jp/e/enterp/missions/catalogue.shtml |date=2014-12-31 }}; retrieved 2012-4-2. < /ref >   It was designed to study [[Halley's Comet]] when it passed through the inner solar system in 1986.
