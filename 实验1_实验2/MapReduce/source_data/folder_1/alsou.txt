 '''Alsou Ralifovna Abramova''' ({{lang-ru|Алсу́ Рали́фовна Абра́мова}}; {{lang-tt|Алсу Рәлиф кызы Абрамова}}; ''née'' '''Safina''', born June 27, 1983) is a [[Bugulma]]n singer. She is known simply as '''Alsou''' ({{lang|ru|Алсу́}}). She is a [[Tatars|Tartar]] and a [[Muslim]], born in Bugulma (Republic of Tatarstan). < ref name=RA > {{cite web |url=http://music.russiansabroad.com/detail.aspx?id=pop & detail=alsou |title=Alsou Safina Biographia |author= < !--Staff writer(s)-- >  |publisher=RUSSIANSAbroad.com |access-date=31 December 2014 |archive-date=15 July 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715214957/http://music.russiansabroad.com/detail.aspx?id=pop & detail=alsou |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Alsou only speaks a few common phrases in her own language. < ref name=RA/ >  At the age of 15 she gained recognition with her first recording, ''Alsou''. < ref name=UNN > {{cite web |url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=38942#.VKP4yivF-So |title=Russian singer Alsou Abramova joins roster of UNESCO Artists for Peace |author= < !--Staff writer(s)-- >  |publisher=UN News Centre |access-date=31 December 2014}} < /ref >   
 
 She competed in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2000]] finishing in second place. < ref name=Beumers235 > Birgit Beumers, ''Pop Culture Russia!: Media, Arts, and Lifestyle'' (Santa Barbara: ABC-CLIO, 2005), p. 235 < /ref >  She also [[wikt:released|released]] her first [[English language|English]] album in 2000. < ref name=Beumers235/ >  Alsou hosted the final of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009]] held in [[Moscow]]. On 7 July 2011 Alsou was appointed as a [[UNESCO Artist for Peace]] by Director-General [[Irina Bokova]]. < ref name=UNN/ >  She is a [[wikt:founder|founder]] and [[organize]]r of the International Folk Music Festival of Peoples of the Volga River. < ref name=UNN/ >
