{{nihongo|'''Gennin'''|元仁|}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Jōō (Kamakura period)|Jōō]]'' and before ''[[Karoku]].''  This period spanned the years from November 1224 to April 1225. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA239  " ''Gennin'' " ] in  ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 239. < /ref >  The reigning emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Horikawa|Go-Horikawa]]''-tennō''|後堀河天皇}}. < ref > [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac]]. (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA238 ''Annales des empereurs du Japon'', pp. 238-241]; [[H. Paul Varley|Varley, H. Paul]]. (1980). ''Jinnō Shōtōki.'' pp. 226-227. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Gennin'' Era== 
 * '''1224''' (''Gennin 1''): ''[[Kyogyoshinsho]]'' was believed to have been composed by [[Shinran]] in 1224; and this is also considered the year in which [[Jōdo Shinshū]] was founded. < ref > Yamamoto, Kōshō. (1955). ''The Shinshu Seiten: the Holy Scripture of Shinshu,'' p. 343. < /ref >
