 '''Galactose''' (from the [[Greek (language)|Greek]] stem γάλακτ– ''galakt–'',  " milk " ) is a [[sugar]]. It has almost the same [[Chemistry|chemical structure]] as [[glucose]].  
 
 Large amounts of pure galactose do not exist in [[nature]]. Instead, galactose is usually found with glucose in [[lactose]], a sugar found in [[milk]] and other milk products. After lactose is [[digestion|digested]] and [[absorption|absorbed]], galactose arrives in the [[liver]]. There it is changed into either glucose or [[glycogen]].
