 In the United States and Canada, '''redlining''' is the process of denying services to residents of specific neighborhoods or communities, usually neighborhoods with non-[[White people|White]] people, either directly or by raising prices. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/05/03/526655831/a-forgotten-history-of-how-the-u-s-government-segregated-america|title=A 'Forgotten History' Of How The U.S. Government Segregated America|last=Gross|first=Terry|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2019-03-31}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite journal |last1=Harris |first1=Richard |last2=Forrester |first2=Doris |title=The Suburban Origins of Redlining: A Canadian Case Study, 1935-54 |journal=Urban Studies |date=2 July 2016 |volume=40 |issue=13 |pages=2661–2686 |doi=10.1080/0042098032000146830 |s2cid=154651681 }} < /ref >  It is often seen as a modern day version of [[segregation]].  
 
 The best known examples of redlining have involved denial of financial services such as [[banking]] or [[insurance]]. < ref > {{cite journal |last1=Zenou |first1=Yves |last2=Boccard |first2=Nicolas |title=Racial Discrimination and Redlining in Cities |journal=Journal of Urban Economics |date=September 2000 |volume=48 |issue=2 |pages=260–285 |doi=10.1006/juec.1999.2166 }} < /ref >  In the United States in the middle of the [[20th century|twentieth century]], banks considered it risky to give [[Mortgage|mortgages]] to people in neighborhoods where most people were in ethnic [[Minority group|minority groups]], so they made them pay more money or refused to give them mortgages at all. As a result, ethnic minorities accumulated less wealth than White people. Redlining has also occurred in when other services such as [[health care]] (see also [[Race and health]]) or even [[supermarket]]s < ref > {{cite journal |last1=Eisenhauer |first1=Elizabeth |title=In poor health: Supermarket redlining and urban nutrition |url=https://archive.org/details/sim_geojournal_2001_53_2/page/125 |journal=GeoJournal |date=2001 |volume=53 |issue=2 |pages=125–133 |doi=10.1023/A:1015772503007 |s2cid=151164815 }} < /ref >  have been denied to residents.
