 '''Cahors''' ({{lang-oc|Caors}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in southwestern [[France]]. It is in [[Occitanie]]. Cahors is the [[Prefectures in France|''prefecture'']] ([[Capital city|capital]]) of the [[Lot (department)|Lot]] [[Departments of France|department]]. It is also the capital of 3 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']]: [[Canton of Cahors-1|Cahors-1]], [[Canton of Cahors-2|Cahors-2]] and [[Canton of Cahors-3|Cahors-3]]. 
 
 Cahors was the capital of the old province of [[Quercy]] and the people of this city are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''cadurciens'' (women: ''cadurciennes''). This name comes from the old name of the city, ''Carduca''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_cahors_46042.html|title=Cahors|publisher=habitants.fr |language=fr |access-date=18 May 2014 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 Cahors is an [[Ancient history|ancient]] town. The people that inhabited the area at the time of the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] conquest were called ''Carduci'' and so the Romans named the town as Divona or ''Divona Cadurcorum'',  " Divona of the Cadurci " ; Divona was a [[River source|source]] of water, now called  " ''fontaine des Chartreux'' " . The word ''Cahors'' comes from ''Cadurcorum''. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Cahors is located in the southwest of France, in the old province of [[Quercy]], in the valley of the [[Lot (river)|Lot]] river, and is found in a [[peninsula]] ({{lang-fr|presqu'île}}) formed by an U-shaped [[meander]] of that river which gives its name to the department. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|72.48|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|219|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|122|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.map-france.com/Cahors-46000/|title=Cahors |publisher=Map-France.com |access-date=19 May 2014 }} < /ref >

 == Administration == 
 Cahors is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Lot department, the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Cahors|Cahors]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the [[Canton of Cahors-1|Cahors-1]], [[Canton of Cahors-2|Cahors-2]] and [[Canton of Cahors-3|Cahors-3]] cantons. 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Le Grand Cahors'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération du Grand Cahors}}). 
 
 ==Places of interest== 
 [[Image:Cahors cathedrale.jpg|thumb|right|175px|Cahors Cathedral]] 
 Some places of interest are: 
 * ''Pont Valentré'' (Valentré [[Bridge]], {{lang-oc|Pont de Balandras}}), a symbol of the town across the Lot river. It was built between 1308 and 1378. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://structurae.net/structures/data/index.cfm?ID=s0000635|title=Valentré Bridge (Cahors, 1350)|publisher=Structurae - International Database for Civil and Structural Engineering |language=fr |access-date=18 May 2014 }} < /ref > 
 * ''Cathédrale Saint-Étienne de Cahors'' (Cahors [[Cathedral]]), a national [[monument]] in France. 
 * ''Arc de Diane'' (Diane's Arch), remains of [[:en:wikt:thermae|Roman bath]]s.  
 * Saint-Barthélémy Church (14th century). 
 * Maison Henri IV or Hôtel de Roaldès (15th century).
