 '''Nationwide Building Society''' is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[mutual organisation|mutual]] financial institution, the seventh largest [[co-operative banking|cooperative financial institution]] < ref > {{cite report|url=https://www.ica.coop/sites/default/files/basic-page-attachments/global300-report-2011-1757377405.pdf|title=Global300 Report 2010: The world's major co-operatives and mutual businesses|publisher=International Co-operative Alliance|access-date=2021-06-04|archive-date=2021-08-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210813153010/https://www.ica.coop/sites/default/files/basic-page-attachments/global300-report-2011-1757377405.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and the largest [[building society]] in the world with over 15 million members.  Its headquarters are in [[Swindon]], England. 
 
 Nationwide is the result of over a hundred mergers, the most notable of which are its merger with [[Anglia Building Society]] in 1987 and [[Portman Building Society]] in 2007. It is now the second largest provider of household [[savings]] and [[mortgages]] in the UK and has a 7.7% market share of [[current account (banking)|current account]]s. 
 
 For the financial year 2015/2016, Nationwide had assets of around £208.9 billion < ref name= " auto " > {{cite report|url=http://www.nationwide.co.uk/~/media/MainSite/documents/about/corporate-information/results-and-accounts/prelim-results-2016.pdf|title=Preliminary Results for the year ended 4 April 2016|publisher=Nationwide Building Society|author=Mark Rennison – Group Finance Director|date=23 May 2016|access-date=27 October 2017|archive-date=1 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160701235026/http://www.nationwide.co.uk/~/media/MainSite/documents/about/corporate-information/results-and-accounts/prelim-results-2016.pdf|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  compared to £331 billion for the entire building society sector, < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.bsa.org.uk/information/publications/industry-publications/bsa-yearbook-2015-16|title=Key Stats|publisher=Building Societies Association|access-date=2021-06-04|archive-date=2016-08-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160826172419/https://www.bsa.org.uk/information/publications/industry-publications/bsa-yearbook-2015-16|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  making it larger than the remaining 44 British building societies combined.{{update after|2015|06}}
