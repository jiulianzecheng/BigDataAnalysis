 '''Roehampton''' is a small suburban village in [[Southwest]] [[London]]. Historically Roehampton was a [[wealth]]y town. Some of the great [[mansion]]s of the 1700s are now used by colleges of the [[University of Roehampton]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.information-britain.co.uk/county16/townguideRoehampton/ |title=A guide to Roehampton |author= < !--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-- >  |date=n.d. |website= |publisher=Information Britain |accessdate=9 December 2014}} < /ref >  The university was founded in 2004. In 2007 the [[National Tennis Centre (United Kingdom|National Tennis Centre]] was set up in Roehampton. It has 22 courts and is a [[training]] facility for some of Britain's top [[tennis player]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.lta.org.uk/Footer/about-us/National-tennis-centre/ |title=National Tennis Centre |author= < !--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-- >  |website= |publisher=LTA British Tennis |accessdate=9 December 2014}} < /ref >  It also hosts the [[Qualifying tournament|qualifying rounds]] for [[The Championships, Wimbledon|the Wimbledon championships]]. < ref > {{cite news |title=Roehampton qualifiers provide the final route to Wimbledon 2013 |author=James Riach |newspaper=The Guardian |date=18 June 2013 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/jun/18/roehampton-qualifiers-wimbledon-2013 |accessdate=9 December 2014}} < /ref >  The [[Priory Hospital]] at Roehampton is a [[mental health]] [[hospital]]. It is [[international]]ly known for the [[treatment]] of [[celebrity|celebrities]] with various [[addiction]]s. < ref > {{cite news |title=Ricky Hatton books into the Priory after cocaine binge |author=Niall Hickman; David Pilditch |newspaper=The Express |date=13 September 2010 |url=http://www.express.co.uk/news/uk/199159/Ricky-Hatton-books-into-the-Priory-after-cocaine-binge |accessdate=9 December 2014}} < /ref >
