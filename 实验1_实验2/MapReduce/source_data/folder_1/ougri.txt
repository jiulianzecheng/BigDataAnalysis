{{About|the ancient dance and music performance|the modern day festival|Ougri festival}} 
 The '''Ougri Hangen''' or the '''Ougri Hangken''', also known as the '''Leiroi Nongloi Esei''', is a [[Meitei culture|Meitei cultural]] ritual song with various appellations of the sun and the mythology of creation. < ref > {{cite book| last = Singh| first = Ch Manihar| title = A History of Manipuri Literature| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=yiBkAAAAMAAJ| year = 1996| publisher = Sahitya Akademi| isbn = 978-81-260-0086-9 }} < /ref >  It is often sung in the conclusion of the [[Lai Haraoba]] festival. < ref > {{cite book| author = North East India History Association. Tripura| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=JQjgAAAAMAAJ| year = 2003| publisher = Gyan Publishing House| isbn = 978-81-212-0812-3 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book| last = Bahadur| first = Mutua| title = Jewelleries of Manipur| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=CoEtAAAAMAAJ| year = 1988| publisher = Mutua Museum| isbn =  }} < /ref >  Its theme is closely associated with the [[creation myth]] of the earth itself. < ref > {{cite book| title = Fifth North Eastern Literary Conference, 8th, 9th  &  10th January 1994, Agartala, Tripura [souvenir]| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=cutjAAAAMAAJ| year = 1994| publisher = The Conference| isbn =  }} < /ref >  It is also regarded as the song of [[thanksgiving]] to the Almighty God. < ref According to the manuscript  " Laisra Pham " , a treatise on administration and social conduct,  " Ougri "  is refered to as sung at the coronation of [[Nongta Lailen Pakhangpa]] in 33 & nbsp;AD. The [[Naothingkhong Phambal Kaba]] mentions it as being taught by the sage Luwawg king to the Meitei prince as a part of the [[Meitei folklore|Meitei lore]], which according to the chronicle [[Cheitharol Kumbaba]], took place just prior to 663 & nbsp;AD. 
 In some cases, it is also refered to as the actual war songs of the [[Meitei people]]. < ref > {{cite book| author = Yanao Lungharnao Roland Shimmi| title = Comparative History of the Nagas, from Ancient Period Till 1826| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=6CBuAAAAMAAJ| year = 1988| publisher = Inter-India Publications| isbn = 978-81-210-0210-3 }} < /ref > 
 Notably, it is regarded as one of the earliest gems in the [[Meitei literature]] ([[Manipuri literature]]). < ref name= " google1984 "  / >
