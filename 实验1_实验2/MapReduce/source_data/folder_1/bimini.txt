 '''Bimini''' is the westernmost district of the [[Bahamas]]. Bimini is the closest point in the Bahamas to the mainland [[United States]].  It is about 137 miles (209 & nbsp;km) west-northwest of [[Nassau, Bahamas|Nassau]]. There are about 1600 people on the chain of islands. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://islands.thebahamian.com/bimini.html |title= " The Bahamian "  Reference Page |access-date=2012-05-15 |archive-date=2012-04-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120422194446/http://islands.thebahamian.com/bimini.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >   The capital is [[Alice Town]].  [[South Bimini Airport]] is on South Bimini island.  The ocean around the islands is said to be one of the world's top [[big-game fishing]] spots. < ref > Pinder,  Macushla N. [http://www.jonesbahamas.com/print.php?a=6986  " Bimini Landmark Destroyed " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713120021/http://www.jonesbahamas.com/print.php?a=6986 |date=2011-07-13 }}. Jones Bahamas, January 14, 2006. < /ref > 
 
 Bimini is home to several unique and threatened species.  The Bimini [[Boinae|Boa]] < ref > [https://web.archive.org/web/20091026101500/http://geocities.com/vermont_herpetology/article1.htm  Endangered Reptiles and Amphibians of the World - I. The Bimini Boa, Epicrates striatus fosteri] < /ref >  is the largest of the [[reptile]]s on Bimini.  The Bimini [[Ameiva]] is a very common, fast moving [[lizard]] on the island. The [[Smalltooth sawfish]] is one of the rarest [[fish]] in the world. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www6.miami.edu/sharklab/aboutbimini_reptiles.html |title=Reptiles of Bimini |access-date=2012-05-15 |archive-date=2010-07-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100706171338/http://www6.miami.edu/sharklab/aboutbimini_reptiles.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The [[spiny dogfish]] also can be found on Bimini. 
 
 [[Ernest Hemingway]] lived on Bimini from 1935 to 1937.
