 '''Ardabil''' {{pronunciation|Ardebil.ogg}} ([[Persian language|Persian]] and [[Azerbaijani language|Azeri]]: اردبیل, also [[Romanize]]d as '''Ardabīl''' and '''Ardebīl''' < ref > {{GEOnet3|-3053565}} < /ref > ) is a historical city in north-western [[Iran]]. The name Ardabil comes from the [[Zoroastrian]] name of  " Artawila " , meaning a holy place. < ref > [http://www.iranchamber.com/provinces/06_ardabil/06_ardabil.php Iranian Provinces: Ardabil < !-- Bot generated title -- > ] < /ref >  Ardabil is the capital of [[Ardabil Province|Ardabil]] [[Provinces of Iran|Province]].  At the 2011 census, its population was 564,365, in 156,324 families, < ref > {{IranCensus2006|24}} < /ref >  Its inhabitants are ethnic [[Azeri people|Azeris]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://looklex.com/e.o/ardabil.htm |title=Ardabil |publisher=[[Looklex Encyclopaedia]] |access-date=2013-08-13 |archive-date=2013-09-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928001733/http://looklex.com/e.o/ardabil.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/33279/Ardabil |title=Ardabīl |publisher=[[Encyclopædia Britannica]] |access-date=2013-08-13}} < /ref >  Ardabil is also known for the sanctuary and tomb of [[Shaikh]] [[Safi-ad-din Ardabili|Safî ad-Dîn]], [[eponym]] of the [[Safavid Dynasty]].
