 '''Instagram''' (sometimes shortened to '''Insta''' or '''IG''') is an online [[:wikt:photo|photo]] and [[:wikt:video|video]] sharing [[social networking service|social networking]] service. It allows users to take pictures and videos, add digital [[Photographic filter|filters]] to them and share them to their followers. They can then share them on a variety of [[Social network service|social networking services]], such as [[Facebook]], [[Twitter]], [[Tumblr]], and [[Flickr]]. < ref name=nov2010 > {{cite web|last=Frommer|first=Dan|title=Here's How To Use Instagram|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/instagram-2010-11|work=[[Business Insider]]|access-date=May 20, 2011|date=November 1, 2010}} < /ref > 
 
 Until 2015, < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.wired.com/2015/08/instagram-says-goodbye-square-photos/|title=Instagram Ends the Tyranny of the Square|last=Stinson|first=Liz|website=www.wired.com|language=en-US}} < /ref >  Instagram confined photos to a [[square]] shape, similar to Kodak [[Instamatic]] and [[Instant camera|Polaroid]] images. This was different than the 4:3 [[:en:wikt:aspect ratio|aspect ratio]] typically used by [[digital camera]]s. Users were able to share very short videos. < ref name= " instagram1 " > [http://blog.instagram.com/post/53448889009/video-on-instagram Instagram Blog] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818124109/http://blog.instagram.com/post/53448889009/video-on-instagram |date=2017-08-18 }}. Blog.instagram.com. Retrieved on 2013-08-20. < /ref >  In 2017, the length limit was raised to ten minutes. 
   
 Instagram was created by [[Kevin Systrom]] and [[Mike Krieger]]. When Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger talked to famous NY Photographer [[Spider-Man|Peter Parker]], a photographer for the [[Daily Bugle]], Systrom and Krieger were inspired to make an app just for photography. It was released on October 6, 2010.
