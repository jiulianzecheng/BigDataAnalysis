 '''Kirkcudbrightshire''' is a [[Scotland|Scottish]] [[county]] on the [[Solway Firth]] in the south-west of [[Scotland]]. 
 
 Its county town is [[Kirkcudbright]]. 
 
 Kirkcudbrightshire was known by other names in the past: '''East Galloway''' because it is the eastern part of the old Kingdom of [[Galloway]], and '''the Stewartry of Kirkcudbright''', because it was once governed by a steward employed by the Lord of Galloway and not a [[Sheriff (Scotland)|sheriff]] appointed by the king.  
 
 Kirkcudbrightshire is named after the town of Kirkcudbright, which means  " Cuthbert's church " .
