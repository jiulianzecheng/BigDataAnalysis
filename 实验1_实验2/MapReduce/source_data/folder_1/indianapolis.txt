 '''Indianapolis''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|ɪ|n|d|i|ə|ˈ|n|æ|p|əl|ᵻ|s}} {{respell|IN|dee|ə|NAP|əl|iss}}), < ref > {{cite book|last=Jones|first=Daniel|author-link=Daniel Jones (phonetician)|title=English Pronouncing Dictionary|editor=Peter Roach |editor2=James Hartmann |editor3=Jane Setter|place=Cambridge|publisher=Cambridge University Press|orig-year=1917|year=2003|isbn=3-12-539683-2}} < /ref > < ref > {{MerriamWebsterDictionary|Indianapolis}}; {{Dictionary.com|Indianapolis}} < /ref >  commonly called '''Indy''', is the [[Capital city|capital]] and [[List of U.S. states' largest cities by population|most populous city]] of the [[U.S. state]] of [[Indiana]]. It is also the [[county seat|seat]] of [[Marion County, Indiana|Marion County]] and is [[wikt:combine|combined]] with the county as a part of [[Unigov]]. The [[United States Census Bureau|U.S. Census Bureau]] said that the [[population]] of Indianapolis together with Marion County was 977,203 in 2020. < ref name= " QuickFacts2 " > {{cite web |title=QuickFacts: Marion County, Indiana |url=https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/fact/table/marioncountyindiana,indianapoliscitybalanceindiana/POP010220 |publisher=United States Census Bureau |access-date=20 August 2021}} < /ref >  The  " [[Indianapolis (balance)|balance]] "  population was 887,642. < ref name= " QuickFacts " > {{cite web |title=QuickFacts: Indianapolis city (balance), Indiana |url=https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/fact/table/indianapoliscitybalanceindiana/POP010220 |publisher=United States Census Bureau |access-date=20 August 2021}} < /ref >  This [[measurement]] leaves out some cities that are slightly [[Autonomy|autonomous]] from Unigov. It is the [[List of United States cities by population|16th-most populous]] city in the U.S., the third-most populous city in the [[Midwestern United States|Midwest]] after [[Chicago]] and [[Columbus, Ohio]], and the fourth-most populous state capital after [[Phoenix, Arizona]], [[Austin, Texas]], and Columbus. The [[Indianapolis metropolitan area]] is the [[List of Metropolitan Statistical Areas|33rd-most populous]] [[metropolitan statistical area]] in the U.S., with 2,111,040 residents. < ref name= " metropop " > {{cite web |url=https://www.census.gov |title=Cumulative Estimates of Resident Population Change and Rankings: April 1, 2010 to July 1, 2018 – United States – Metropolitan Statistical Area; and for Puerto Rico 2018 Population Estimates |date=July 2019 |publisher=U.S. Census Bureau |access-date=November 13, 2019 }} < /ref >  Its [[Combined statistical area|combined statistical area ranks 28th]], with a population of 2,431,361. < ref name= " CSA " > {{cite web|url=https://www.census.gov|title=U.S. Census website|date=July 2019|publisher=U.S. Census Bureau|access-date=November 13, 2019}} < /ref >  Indianapolis has an [[area]] of {{convert|368|sqmi|km2|}}. This makes it the [[List of United States cities by area|18th largest city by land area]] in the U.S. 
 
 [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Indigenous peoples]] lived in the area as early as 10,000 [[BC]]. < ref name= " Jones2 " > {{cite web|author1=James R. Jones III, PhD.|author2=Amy L. Johnson|date=2016|title=Early Peoples of Indiana|url=https://www.in.gov/dnr/historic/files/HP_earlypeoples.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200725090311/https://www.in.gov/dnr/historic/files/HP_earlypeoples.pdf|archive-date=July 25, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020|publisher=Indiana Department of Natural Resources Division of Historic Preservation and Archaeology}} < /ref >  In 1818, the [[Lenape]] gave their [[territory]] to the U.S. in the [[Treaty of St. Mary's]]. < ref name= " Ency " > {{cite book|last=Bodenhamer|first=David|author2=Robert Graham Barrows|author3=David Gordon Vanderstel|title=The Encyclopedia of Indianapolis|publisher=Indiana University Press|year=1994|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bg13QcMSsq8C|isbn=0-253-31222-1}} p. 1042 < /ref >  In 1821, Indianapolis was made as a [[planned city]] to be the new capital of Indiana. The old capital was [[Corydon, Indiana|Corydon]]. The city was [[Plat|platted]] by [[Alexander Ralston]] and [[Elias Pym Fordham]] on a {{convert|1|sqmi|km2|adj=on}} grid next to the [[White River (Indiana)|White River]]. The finishing of the [[National Road|National]] and [[Michigan Road|Michigan]] [[Road|roads]] and the start of [[railroad]] [[Construction|building]] made the city an important [[transport hub]]. < ref > {{cite encyclopedia|editor1-last=Bodenhamer|editor1-first=David|editor2-last=Barrows|editor2-first=Robert|title=The Encyclopedia of Indianapolis|year=1994|publisher=Indiana University Press|location=Bloomington  &  Indianapolis|pages=190}} < /ref >  Two of the nicknames for Indianapolis (the  " [[Crossroads of America]] "  and  " Railroad City " ) matches the city's history with [[transportation]]. < ref name= " Indy Chamber2 " > {{cite web|title=Metro Indianapolis Export Plan|url=http://www.indychamber.com/files/1414/2429/3865/Metro_Indy_GCI_Export_Plan_low_resolution.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161022095403/http://www.indychamber.com/files/1414/2429/3865/Metro_Indy_GCI_Export_Plan_low_resolution.pdf|archive-date=October 22, 2016|access-date=August 16, 2016|publisher=Indy Chamber}} < /ref > < ref name= " NPS2 " > {{cite web|title=Capital at the Crossroads of America–Indianapolis: A Discover Our Shared Heritage Travel Itinerary|url=http://www.nps.gov/nr/travel/indianapolis/introessay.htm|access-date=March 24, 2016|publisher=National Park Service (U.S. Dept. of the Interior)}} < /ref > < ref name= " Railroad City "  / >  In 1970, Indianapolis and Marion County were [[City-county consolidation|combined]]. This system is called Unigov. The city is led by an [[Election|elected]] 25-member [[Indianapolis City-County Council|city-county council]] led by the [[List of mayors of Indianapolis|mayor]].
