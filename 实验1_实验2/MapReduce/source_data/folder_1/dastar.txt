 A '''Dastār''' ({{lang-pa|ਦਸਤਾਰ/دستار}}, from {{lang-fa|دستار}}) which derives from ''dast-e-yār'' or  " the hand of God " , < ref > {{Cite web|title=Persian-English translation :: dast :: Dictionary|url=https://www.translatepersian.info/en/dictionary-persian-english/dast|access-date=2020-12-31|website=translatepersian.info|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Persian-English translation :: yar :: Dictionary|url=https://www.translatepersian.info/en/dictionary-persian-english/yar|access-date=2020-12-31|website=translatepersian.info|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[turban]] worn by [[Punjabis|Punjabi]] [[muslims]] and [[Sikhism|Sikhs]]. It is an important part of the [[Punjabis|Punjabi]] culture. The word comes from [[Persian language|Persian]] to [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]]. In Persian, the word ''dastār'' can describe any kind of [[turban]].
