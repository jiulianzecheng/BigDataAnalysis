 '''Autoradio''' ({{lang-be|Аўтарадыё}}) (105.1 Minsk FM) was the first private [[Radio|radio station]] in [[Belarus]]. It worked from [[Minsk]]. The radio station used the [[Frequency modulation|frequency]] 105.1 [[MHz]]. It worked from August 7, 1992 < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Плавник|first=А.|year=2011|title=Даты, события, люди|url=https://elib.bsu.by/bitstream/123456789/33207/1/Radio_2011.pdf|journal=Беларускае радыё: гісторыя, сучаснасць, перспектывы развіцця|language=ru|location=Мінск|publisher=[[Belarusian State University|БДУ]]|pages=8–27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200608143745/http://elib.bsu.by/bitstream/123456789/33207/1/Radio_2011.pdf|archive-date=2021-01-08|access-date=2021-01-08}} < /ref >  to January 12, 2011. < ref > {{Cite web|language=ru|url=https://www.dw.com/ru/%D0%B0%D0%B2%D1%82%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%BE-%D0%B2-%D0%B1%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%B0%D1%80%D1%83%D1%81%D0%B8-%D0%BB%D0%B8%D1%88%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B8-%D0%B3%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%BE%D1%81%D0%B0/a-14767585|title= " Авторадио "  в Беларуси лишили голоса|first1=Полина|last1=Птицына|date=2011-01-14|publisher=[[Deutsche Welle]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211132303/https://www.dw.com/ru/%D0%B0%D0%B2%D1%82%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%BE-%D0%B2-%D0%B1%D0%B5%D0%BB%D0%B0%D1%80%D1%83%D1%81%D0%B8-%D0%BB%D0%B8%D1%88%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B8-%D0%B3%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%BE%D1%81%D0%B0/a-14767585|archive-date=2020-12-11|access-date=2020-12-11|last2=Дорохов|first2=Владимир|url-status=live}} < /ref >   
 
 ==History== 
 From the start of the 1990s to January 1, 1995, Autoradio was heard on the frequency 67.7 OIRT FM. < ref name= " главред "  / >  Autoradio used OIRT FM (the International Radio and Television Organisation wave in the air). It was the only open range for people and organizations in Belarus at that time. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Курейчик|first=Анна|year=2016|title=Зарождение коммерческой модели радиовещания в Республике Беларусь|url=https://elib.bsu.by/bitstream/123456789/161005/1/Журналістыка-2016.pdf|journal=Журналістыка-2016: стан, праблемы і перспектывы|language=ru|publisher=БГУ|pages=146–149|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200919170452/https://elib.bsu.by/bitstream/123456789/161005/1/%D0%96%D1%83%D1%80%D0%BD%D0%B0%D0%BB%D1%96%D1%81%D1%82%D1%8B%D0%BA%D0%B0-2016.pdf|archive-date=2020-09-19|access-date=2021-04-18|url-status=live}} < /ref >  In 1995, the radio station was cut. It took four years to get back on the air. < ref name= " spring96 " > {{cite web|language=be|url=http://spring96.org/be/news/41283|title=Агляд-хроніка парушэньняў правоў чалавека ў Беларусі, студзень 2010 году|publisher=[[Viasna Human Rights Centre]]}} < /ref >  In 2001, the second wave line was started on the FM frequency 105.1 MHz (Megahertz). < ref name= " главред " > {{Cite web|language=ru|url=https://news.tut.by/society/513478.html|title=Умер основатель и главред  " Авторадио " |date=2016-09-25|publisher=[[TUT.BY]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161115063722/http://news.tut.by/society/513478.html|access-date=2021-01-08|url-status=live|archive-date=2016-11-15}} < /ref >

 === End of broadcasting === 
 At 12:40 on January 12, 2011, the Autoradio's broadcasting document was taken away and the radio station saw its end. < ref > [http://nn.by/?c=ar & i=48860 Закрытая «Аўтарадыё»] // [[Nasha Niva]] {{in lang|be}} < /ref >  The Republican Commission on Television and Radio Broadcasting led by Oleg Proleskovsky took the Autoradio's broadcasting document away after the station broadcast [[Advertising|advertisements]] for [[Candidate|candidates]] Andrei Sannikov and Uladzimir Nyaklyayew. Both were against [[Alexander Lukashenko]] in the 2010 presidential elections in Belarus. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/belarus_radio_opposition_ads_off_air/2274257.html|title=Belarus Radio Station Loses License|publisher=[[Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]]|language=en|access-date=2019-08-28}} < /ref > 
 
 Journalist Paulyuk Bykouski said that the Autoradio's [[Advertising|advertisements]] were in agreement with the Central Election Commission of Belarus (the organization that does elections). < ref name= " Bykouski "  / >  The court of the first level supported the position of Avtoradio. Its decision was changed later. < ref name= " Bykouski " > {{Cite journal|last=Bykouski|first=Paulyuk|year=2011|title=Belarusian Media in the Virtual Space|url=https://www.eesc.lt/uploads/news/id392/Bell%204%20(25)%20(2011).pdf|journal=BELL|volume=4|language=en|issue=25|pages=5|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211141328/https://www.eesc.lt/uploads/news/id392/Bell%204%20%2825%29%20%282011%29.pdf|archive-date=2020-12-11|access-date=2020-12-11}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|language=ru|url=https://news.tut.by/society/220122.html|title=Решение о закрытии  " Авторадио "  осталось в силе|first=Татьяна|last=Дорощенок|work=[[BelaPAN]]|date=2011-03-28|publisher=[[TUT.BY]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110430133852/https://news.tut.by/society/220122.html|archive-date=2011-04-30|access-date=2021-01-08|url-status=live}} < /ref >  As a result of the change, Dmitry Alexandrov, judge of the Economic Court of Belarus, who ruled to shut the radio station down, became a person whose [[European Union|EU]] accounts and moves to the European Union were iced. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://nn.by/?c=ar & i=61552|title=Поўны спіс 208 беларускіх чыноўнікаў, якім забаронены ўезд у ЕС|language=be|publisher=[[Nasha Niva]]|date=2011-10-11}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite journal|date=2011-05-24|title=COUNCIL IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) No 505/2011 of 23 May 2011implementing Regulation (EC) No 765/2006 concerning restrictive measures against President Lukashenko and certain officials of Belarus|url=http://web.valcloud.eu/SanctionCheck/regulations/505-2011.pdf|journal=[[Official Journal of the European Union]]|series=L|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211144748/http://web.valcloud.eu/SanctionCheck/regulations/505-2011.pdf|archive-date=2020-12-11|access-date=2020-12-11}} < /ref > 
 
 The government-run [[Radio|radio station]] Stalitsa ({{lang-be|Сталіца}}) was given the [[Frequency modulation|frequency]] 105.1 [[MHz]] that was taken away from Autoradio. < ref name= " baj2012 " > {{Cite journal|year=2012|title=Mass Media Week in Belarus|url=https://baj.by/sites/default/files/analytics/files/rassylka19-03_01-04_2012en.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211135808/https://baj.by/sites/default/files/analytics/files/rassylka19-03_01-04_2012en.pdf|archive-date=2012-12-11|access-date=2012-12-11|publisher=[[Belarusian Association of Journalists]]|pages=3–4|language=en|journal=Info‐posting}} < /ref >  Stalitsa began using it on April 15, 2012. < ref name= " baj2012 "  / > 
 
 Yuri Bazan, the person who made Autoradio and was its main editor, died on September 24, 2016. < ref name= " dies "  / > < ref name= " главред "  / >
