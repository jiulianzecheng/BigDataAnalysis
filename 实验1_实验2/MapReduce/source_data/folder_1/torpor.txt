'''Torpor''' is a [[physiological]] state of the body. It occurs in some small [[warm-blooded]] animals when food is scarce. 
 
 Some animals have periods of torpor every day, including [[hummingbirds]]. < ref name= " Hainsworth(1970)a " > {{cite journal| last = Hainsworth F.R.  &  Wolf L.L. 1970| title = Regulation of oxygen consumption and body temperature during torpor in a hummingbird, ''Eulampis jugularis''| journal = Science | year = 1970| volume = 168 | issue = 3929 | pages = 368–369| doi = 10.1126/science.168.3929.368 | pmid=5435893| s2cid = 30793291}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web |url=http://nationalzoo.si.edu/conservationandscience/migratorybirds/webcam/hummingbirds.cfm |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080214224153/http://nationalzoo.si.edu/conservationandscience/migratorybirds/webcam/hummingbirds.cfm |archive-date=2008-02-14 |publisher=Migratory Bird Center, Smithsonian National Zoological Park |title=Hummingbirds }} < /ref >
