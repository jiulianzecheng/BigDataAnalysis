 The '''Charminar''' is a monument and [[mosque]] in [[Hyderabad, India]]. The structure was built in 1591 [[AD]]. It is the most famous building of Hyderabad and also one of the most famous buildings in India. < ref > {{cite web|title=Richard Goslan travels to India â€“ Herald Scotland.|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/life-style/travel-outdoors/richard-goslan-travels-to-india-1.1074175|access-date=27 March 2013|archive-date=18 December 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101218111918/http://www.heraldscotland.com/life-style/travel-outdoors/richard-goslan-travels-to-india-1.1074175|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It was built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shahi to celebrate the end of a deadly [[plague]]. < ref name= " autogenerated2 " > [http://www.britannica.com/facts/11/788576/Charminar-as-discussed-in-Hyderabad Charminar: Hyderabad], Britannica Compton's Encyclopedia < /ref >  The Charminar lies near the bank of the river [[Musi]]. It is close to Laad Bazaar and Makkah Masjid. < ref name=autogenerated2 / >  Charminar is taken from two words ''Char'' and ''Minar'' which translate as ''Four Towers'' in English. < ref name=autogenerated2 / > 
 
 Some people say that Charminar was the first structure to be constructed in the newly built city of Hyderabad. < ref name= " UNESCO " > {{cite web|title=The Qutb Shahi monuments of Hyderabad-Golconda Fort, Qutb Shachi Tombs, Charminar|url=http://whc.unesco.org/en/tentativelists/5573/|publisher=UNESCO World Heritage Centre|access-date=27 March 2013}} < /ref >  It is said that Quli Qutab Shah prayed for the end of the plague and vowed to build a mosque if it was eradicated. < ref name= " I-Radio " > {{cite news|title=India: Charminar is in fact a madrasa and masjid|url=http://english.irib.ir/subcontinent/culture/history/item/85075-india-charminar-is-in-fact-a-madrasa-and-masjid|publisher=IRIB World Service|access-date=27 March 2013}} < /ref >  Mir Momin Astarabadi, the prime minister of Qutb Shah played an important role in the design and layout of Charminar and the city of Hyderabad. < ref name= " Marika " > {{cite thesis|title=Golconda through time: A mirror of the evolving Deccan|last=Sardar|first=Marika|publisher=[[New York University]]|year=2007|id=UMI Number:3269810}} < /ref >  The structure is of [[Indo-Islamic architecture]] with some Persian elements. < ref name= " Lauren & Jaffrelot " > {{cite book| last1 = Gayer| first1 = Lauren| last2 = Lynton|  Jaffrelot| title = Muslims in Indian cities: trajectories of marginalisation| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Z7J8ITgtkosC| access-date = 27 March 2013| year = 2011|
