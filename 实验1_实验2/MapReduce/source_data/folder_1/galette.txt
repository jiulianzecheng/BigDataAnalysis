 A '''galette''' is a term used in [[French cuisine]] for various types of flat, round or freeform crusty [[cake]]s. < ref > [http://www.finecooking.com/articles/how-to/galettes-rustic-tarts.aspx Galette] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812060814/http://www.finecooking.com/articles/how-to/galettes-rustic-tarts.aspx |date=2014-08-12 }} at finecooking.com < /ref > 
 
 One notable type is the ''galette des Rois'' ('King cake') eaten on the day of [[Epiphany (holiday)|Epiphany]]. In [[Quebec]], the term ''galette'' is usually applied to [[Pastry|pastries]] best described as large [[cookies]]. 
 
 Galette is also the name given in most French ''crêperies'' to [[savoury]] [[buckwheat]] flour [[pancake]]s. Those made from [[wheat]] flour, much smaller in size and mostly served with a sweet filling, are called [[crêpe]]s.
