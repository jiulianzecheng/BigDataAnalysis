 '''Marzanna''' (in [[Polish language|Polish]]), '''Marena''' (in [[Russian language|Russian]]), '''Morana''' (in [[Czech language|Czech]], [[Bulgarian languahe|Bulgarian]], [[Slovene language|Slovene]], and [[Serbo-Croatian language|Serbo-Croatian]]),  or '''Morena''' or '''Kyselica''' (in [[Slovak language|Slovak]]), '''Kiszehajtás''' (in [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]]), '''Morena''' (in [[Macedonian language|Macedonian]]), '''Maslenitsa''' (in [[Russia]]) and also '''Mara''' (in [[Belarusian language|Belarusian]] and [[Ukrainian literature|Ukrainian]]), Maržena, Moréna, Mora or Marmora, '''Morė''' (in [[Lithuanian language|Lithuanian]]) is the [[goddess]] of death and winter in [[Slavic paganism]]. In many Slavic countries, it is very popular to burn a doll, that represents Marzanna, when it's beginning of spring.
