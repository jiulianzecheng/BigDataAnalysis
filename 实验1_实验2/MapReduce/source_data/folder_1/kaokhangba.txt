 '''Meidingu Kaokhangba''' ({{lang-omp|Meitingu Kaokhangpa}}) was a ruler of [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). He is the successor of [[Pengsiba]] and the predecessor of [[Naokhamba]]. In [[Ancient Meitei language]],  " Kao "  means a wild bull and  " Khangpa "  means to prevent or to catch.  
 
 He is one of the nine kings associated with the design of a historic flag. He is mentioned in the [[Cheitharol Kumbaba]] and the [[Ningthourol Lambuba]].
