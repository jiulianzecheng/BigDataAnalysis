'''Saltash''' is a town in south east [[Cornwall]], [[United Kingdom]]. It has a population of 14,964. < ref > [http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/viewFullDataset.do?instanceSelection=03070 & productId=779 & $ph=60_61 & datasetInstanceId=3070 & startColumn=1 & numberOfColumns=8 & containerAreaId=790338 Office for National Statistics : ''Census 2001 : Parish Headcounts : Caradon''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151223123357/http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/viewFullDataset.do?instanceSelection=03070 & productId=779 & $ph=60_61 & datasetInstanceId=3070 & startColumn=1 & numberOfColumns=8 & containerAreaId=790338 |date=2015-12-23 }}. Retrieved 30 January 2010. < /ref >  It is separated from the city of [[Plymouth, Devon|Plymouth]] in Devon by the [[River Tamar]]. Saltash is the largest town within the East Cornwall area.  
 
 One of the landmarks of Saltash is the [[Royal Albert Bridge]], 1859, which carries the railway line across the River Tamar. Alongside it is the Tamar Bridge, a toll bridge carrying the [[A38 road|A38]] trunk road. Saltash railway station is close to the town centre. The station is served by a regular train service, with some direct High Speed services to and from London Paddington.
