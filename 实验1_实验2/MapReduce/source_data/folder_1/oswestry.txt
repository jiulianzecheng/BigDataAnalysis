 '''Oswestry''' ({{lang-cy|Croesoswallt}}), is one of the [[United Kingdom]]'s oldest [[border]] towns. It is the largest [[market town]] and [[Civil parishes in England|civil parish]] in [[Shropshire]], [[England]], close to the [[Wales|Welsh]] border. Oswestry is the third largest town in [[Shropshire]], following [[Telford]] and [[Shrewsbury]]. The 2001 [[Census]] recorded the population of the civil parish as 15,613, and the [[:wikt:urban|urban]] area as 16,660. In 2008 the latter figure was estimated to have increased to 17,116. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x= & men=gpro & lng=en & dat=32 & geo=-81 & srt=pnan & col=aohdq & pt=c & va=x & geo=514277697 |title=Oswestry |work=World Gazetteer |accessdate=2008-05-14 |archiveurl=https://archive.today/20121216135916/http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x= & men=gpro & lng=en & dat=32 & geo=-81 & srt=pnan & col=aohdq & pt=c & va=x & geo=514277697 |archivedate=2012-12-16 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The town is five miles (8 & nbsp;km) from the Welsh border, Oswestry has a mixed Welsh and [[English people|English]] [[:wikt:heritage|heritage]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.shropshiretourism.info/oswestry/ |title=Oswestry  &  the Welsh Borders |author=Shropshire Tourism |accessdate=2009-03-03 |archive-date=2018-10-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181029045522/http://www.shropshiretourism.info/oswestry/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is the home of the [[Shropshire Council|Shropshire libraries]]' Welsh Collection. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.shropshire.gov.uk/shropshire/library.nsf/open/D55470EF515E450B80256CD300486C60 |title=Welsh Collection at Oswestry Library |author=Shropshire Council |accessdate=2009-03-03 }}{{Dead link|date=December 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >   
 
 == Name history == 
 The [[Battle of Maserfield]] is thought to have been fought here in 642 between [[Penda of Mercia]] and [[Oswald of Northumbria]]. < ref > John Davies, ''A History of Wales'' (London: Penguin Books, 1994), p. 63 < /ref >  Oswald was killed in this [[battle]]. < ref > John Marsden, ''Northanhymbre Saga; the History of the Anglo-Saxon Kings of Northumbria'' (London: Kyle Cathie Limited, 1992), p. 130 < /ref >  According to [[legend]], one of his arms was carried to an [[ash tree]] by a [[raven]]. < ref name=Gannon97 > Anna Gannon, ''The Iconography of Early Anglo-Saxon Coinage: Sixth to Eighth Centuries'' (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2003), p. 97 < /ref >  [[Miracle]]s were then attributed to the tree (as Oswald was considered a [[saint]]). < ref name=Gannon97/ >  The Welsh who held this area at the time called it ''Croes-Oswellt'' (meaning Oswald's cross). < ref name=Cathrall3 > William Cathrall, ''The history of Oswestry'' (Oswestry: George Lewis, 1855), p. 3 < /ref >  During the [[Anglo-Saxon]] period the town was called Oswald's Tree. Later this became Oswestry. < ref name=Cathrall3/ >
