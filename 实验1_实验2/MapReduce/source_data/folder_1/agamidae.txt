 '''Agamidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of iguanian [[lizards]]. They are [[indigenous]] to [[Africa]], [[Asia]], [[Australia]], and there are a few in southern [[Europe]].  
 
 The family has about 350 [[species]] in about 50 [[genus|genera]]. Agamids usually have scaly [[Body|bodies]], well-developed legs, and a moderately long [[tail]]. The average body size ranges from 10 to 15 & nbsp;cm (4 to 6 inches), and the tail is 20 to 30 & nbsp;cm (8 to 12 inches) long, though the family varies widely.
