 {{nihongo|'''''Ichinomiya'''''|一宮, also 一の宮 or 一之宮|first shrine}} is a [[Japan]]ese term for the chief [[Shinto shrine]]s in each of the pre-modern [[Provinces of Japan|provinces]]. < ref > [http://eos.kokugakuin.ac.jp/modules/xwords/entry.php?entryID=1120   " ''Ichi no miya/Sōja,'' " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200805230404/http://eos.kokugakuin.ac.jp/modules/xwords/entry.php?entryID=1120 |date=2020-08-05 }} ''Encyclopedia of Shinto''; retrieved 2012-8-24. < /ref > 
 
 The ''ichinomiya'' are the shrines with the highest shrine [[rank]] in an area. < ref > The provincial ''ichinomiya'' sometimes became place names, such as the city of [[Ichinomiya, Aichi]]. < /ref >  Shrines of the lower rank are called ''ninomiya'' (二宮, second), ''sannomiya'' (三宮, third), ''shinomiya'' (四宮, fourth), and so forth. 
 
 ==History== 
 When [[Prefectures of Japan|prefectures]] were established in the [[Meiji period]], the old system of provincial ''ichinomiya'' was not changed.  Each new prefecture had one or more ichinomiya. < ref > [http://eos.kokugakuin.ac.jp/modules/xwords/images/uploads/EOS070712Ab.pdf  " Nationwide List of ''Ichinomiya'', "  pp. 1-3] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130517061440/http://eos.kokugakuin.ac.jp/modules/xwords/images/uploads/EOS070712Ab.pdf |date=2013-05-17 }}; retrieved 2012-8-23. < /ref >
