 '''Abdication''' is the act of resigning from an office (official job), especially from being the leader of a country. The word is normally used for [[kings]] and [[queens]] who decide to give up their position which brings an end to a [[wikt:reign|reign]]. < ref name= " britannica2011 " > [http://en.wikisource.org/wiki/1911_Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica/Abdication   " Abdication, " ] ''Encyclop√¶dia Britannica'' (1911); retrieved 2013-2-11. < /ref >   The term is also used for [[pope]]s. < ref > [http://www.newadvent.org/cathen/01031a.htm   " Abdication, " ] ''Catholic Encyclopedia''; retrieved 2013-2-11. < /ref > 
 
 A similar term for an elected or appointed official is [[resignation]]. 
 
 [[Edward VIII of the United Kingdom|King Edward VIII of the United Kingdom]] is an example of a king who abdicated. < ref > [http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/2701463.stm   " Edward VIII: Abdication timeline, " ] BBC. 29 January 2003; retrieved 2013-2-11. < /ref >
