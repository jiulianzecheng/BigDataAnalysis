 '''''Parahenodus''''' is an extinct ''[[henodontidae]]'' [[placodont]] only known from a skull, described in 2018. < ref > {{Cite journal|author1=Carlos de Miguel Chaves |author2=Francisco Ortega |author3=Adán Pérez‐García |year=2018 |title=A new placodont from the Upper Triassic of Spain provides new insights on the acquisition of the specialized skull of Henodontidae |journal=Papers in Palaeontology |volume=4 |issue= 4|pages= 567–576|doi=10.1002/spp2.1218 |s2cid=133679210 }} < /ref >  The skull, named and described as '''''Parahenodus atancensis''''', was discovered in [[Upper Triassic]] rock in [[Spain]]. It was the [[sister taxon]] to ''[[Henodus]]''.
