 '''Paderborn''' ({{lang-nds|Patterbuorn}}, ''Paterboärn'') is a city in the middle west of [[Germany]], in the [[States of Germany|state]] of [[North Rhine-Westphalia]]. The name of the city derives from the river Pader and  " born " , an old German term for the source of a river. The river Pader originates in more than 200 springs near Paderborn Cathedral. 
 It has about 150,338 [[inhabitant]]s  < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.paderborn.de/rathaus-service/stadtportrait/109010100000056020.php|title=Bevölkerung / Einwohnerzahlen|website=Stadt Paderborn|language=de-DE|access-date=2018-04-11}} < /ref >  and a university. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.uni-paderborn.de/|title=Universität Paderborn|website=www.uni-paderborn.de|language=de|access-date=2018-04-11}} < /ref > 
 
 Paderborn has a football club in the first men's league in Germany (SC Paderborn 07 < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.scp07.de/|title=SC Paderborn 07|website=www.scp07.de|language=de|access-date=2018-04-11}} < /ref > ) and a basketball team in the 2nd German league (Paderborn Baskets). < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://paderborn-baskets.de/|title=Paderborn Baskets – – offizielle Website Paderborn Baskets 91 e.V.|website=paderborn-baskets.de|language=de-DE|access-date=2018-04-11}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Pope John Paul II]] visited Paderborn in 1996 and [[Pope Leo III]] visited Paderborn in 799. 
 
 Paderborn has got a very big cathedral. It was built by [[Charlemagne]] ( " Karl der Große " ).
