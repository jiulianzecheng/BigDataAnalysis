 '''Westmorland''' (an older spelling is ''Westmoreland'', an even older spelling is ''Westmerland'') is an area of north west [[England]] and one of the 39 [[historic counties of England]]. It was an [[administrative county]] from 1889 to 1974 and now is part of [[Cumbria]].  
 
 == Early history == 
 [[Image:England_Administrative_1931.jpg|thumb|200px|right|Westmorland as shown on a 1931 administrative map of [[England]]]] 
 At the time of the [[Domesday Book]] in 1086 the county was said to form part of [[Yorkshire]].  
 The historic county borders are with [[Cumberland]] to the north, [[County Durham]] and [[Yorkshire]] to the east, and [[Lancashire]] to the south and west.  
 
 The highest point of the county is [[Helvellyn]] at 950m (3,117 ft).  In 1974, under the [[Local Government Act 1972]], the county was abolished and its former area was combined with [[Cumberland]] and parts of [[Lancashire]] and [[Yorkshire]] to form the new county of [[Cumbria]]. The former county now forms part of the districts of [[South Lakeland]] and [[Eden, Cumbria|Eden]].
