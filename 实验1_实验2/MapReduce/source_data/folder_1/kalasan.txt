 '''Kalasan''' ({{lang-id|Candi Kalasan}}) is a [[Buddhist]] temple built in the 8-century during [[Sailendra]] dynasty and located near [[Sewu]] temple in [[Yogyakarta]] region of [[Indonesia]]. Based on Kalasan inscription (dated 778 AD written in [[Sanskrit]] with Pranagari script), Kalasan temple was created by King 'Guru Sang Raja Sailendravamçatilaka' as sacred building dedicated to the goddess [[Tara (Devi)]]. < ref > {{cite book|last= Coedès|first= George|editor= Walter F. Vella|others= trans.Susan Brown Cowing|title= The Indianized States of Southeast Asia|year= 1968|publisher= University of Hawaii Press|isbn= 978-0-8248-0368-1}} < /ref >
