 '''''Acanthosphinx''''' is a monotypic(meaning it only has one subclass) [[genus]] of [[moth]]. It is in the family [[Sphingidae]]. It was erected by Per Olof Christopher Aurivillius in 1891.  
 
 It has one species, which is '''''Acanthosphinx guessfeldti''''', the '''widow sphinx'''. It was described by Hermann Dewitz in 1879. < ref > {{cite web|last=Savela|first=Markku|title=''Acanthosphinx'' Aurivillius, 1891|url=http://ftp.funet.fi/pub/sci/bio/life/insecta/lepidoptera/ditrysia/bombycoidea/sphingidae/smerinthinae/acanthosphinx/|access-date=November 15, 2017|website=Lepidoptera and Some Other Life Forms|archive-date=November 15, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171115143411/http://ftp.funet.fi/pub/sci/bio/life/insecta/lepidoptera/ditrysia/bombycoidea/sphingidae/smerinthinae/acanthosphinx/|url-status=live}} < /ref >  It is seen in [[Forest|forests]] in [[Sierra Leone]] to the [[Congo Basin|Congo]], [[Angola]], [[Zambia]], [[Malawi]], [[Tanzania]] and [[Uganda]]. < ref > {{cite journal|last=Carcasson|first=R. H.|author-link=Robert Herbert Carcasson|date=1967|title=Revised Catalogue of the African Sphingidae (Lepidoptera) with Descriptions of the East African species|url=https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/51111621#page/279/mode/1up|journal=Journal of the East Africa Natural History Society and National Museum|volume=26|issue=3|pages=1–173|via=Biodiversity Heritage Library|access-date=2022-03-01|archive-date=2021-11-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211125203633/https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/51111621#page/279/mode/1up|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 Each forewing is 57–70 mm in [[length]].
