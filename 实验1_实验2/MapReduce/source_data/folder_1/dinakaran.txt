Dinakaran is a [[Tamil language|Tamil]] [[daily newspaper]] distributed in [[India]]. It was founded by [[K. P. Kandasamy]] in 1977.  ==Attack case== 
 Dinakaran published the result of a opinion polls. The poll was about people's wish of who will become the next leader of DMK. The results said that 70% of the people support Stalin while only 2% support Alagiri. Dinakaran daily's office were bombed by Alagiri supporters. In this attack case, 3 were dead . < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.dnaindia.com/report.asp?NewsID=1095852|work= DNA India |title= Sun Network accuses Alagiri as being main culprit in Dinakaran attack|access-date=12 November 2013}} < /ref > < ref name=Survey > {{cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/news/2007/may/09azha.htm|title=TN: 2 killed as Dinakaran office set afire|publisher=[[Rediff]]|date=9 May 2007}} < /ref >  This led to the resignation of Central Minister [[Dayanidhi Maran]], brother of Kalanidhi Maran from the Union Cabinet. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.ibnlive.com/news/politics/05_2007/dmk-drama-raja-in-maran-out-40592.html|title=DMK kicks out Dayanidhi, brings in Raja as Minister|publisher=[[CNN-IBN]]|date=15 May 2007}} < /ref >
