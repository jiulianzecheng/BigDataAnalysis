 '''Baketmut''' (Ancient Egyptian: ''b3k.t-mwt'',  " Handmaid of [[Mut]] " ) was an [[ancient Egypt]]ian princess of the Nineteenth Dynasty. She was the second daughter of Pharaoh [[Ramesses II]]. < ref > Dodson, Aidan, Hilton, Dyan. The Complete Royal Families of Ancient Egypt. Thames  &  Hudson (2004). {{ISBN|0-500-05128-3}}, p.170 < /ref > 
 
 Her statue stands at the feet of one of her father's statues at the [[Abu Simbel temples|Great Temple of Abu Simbel]]. She is depicted as an adult, with an uraeus on her head. She is also depicted inside the temple, She appears as the second daughter after Bintanath. It has been proposed that her mother was Queen [[Nefertari]], but it's not confirmed. < ref > [http://mathstat.slu.edu/~bart/egyptianhtml/kings%20and%20Queens/Queen_Nefertari.html Anneke Bart: ''Queen Nefertari''] < /ref >  She is not shown at the Small Temple of Abu Simbel, which was built for Nefertari and has statues of princes and princesses who are likely to have been her children. < ref > Dodson, op.cit., p.167 < /ref > 
 
 Her tomb is not known.
