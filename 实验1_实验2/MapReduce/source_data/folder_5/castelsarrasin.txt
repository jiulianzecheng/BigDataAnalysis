 '''Castelsarrasin''' ({{lang-oc|Los Sarrasins}} < ref > {{cite web |url=http://toponimiaoc.webs.com/82tarnegarona.htm |title=Las comunas de TARN ET GARONA |work=Toponimia occitana e senhalizacion en occitan |language=oc |access-date=5 July 2014 }} < /ref > ) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in the [[Tarn-et-Garonne]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]] of [[France]]. It is the second city in Tarn-et-Garonne with more people living in it, after [[Montauban]]. 
 
 It is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] in the department and the [[Capital city|capital]] of the [[Arrondissement of Castelsarrasin|Castelsarrasin]] [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissement'']]. 
 
 ==History== 
 The oldest written example of ''Castelsarrasin'', as ''Castel Sarracenum'', is from the year 961. It was written in the [[Will (law)|will]] of [[Raymond II of Rouergue]] where he gave the ''Saint-Sauveur'' church to the [[monastery]] of [[Moissac]]. 
 
 ''Castel Sarracenum'' ( " [[Saracen]] [[Castle]] " ) was given that name because the castle was built in the Saracen < ref group= " Note " > [[Islam|Muslim]]s were known, in [[Europe]], as '''Saracens''' during the [[Middle Ages]]. < /ref >  era. 
 
 There were many wars during the early [[history]] of the city: 
 * against the [[English people|English]] until the end of the 12th century; 
 * the [[Albigensian Crusade]] during the first part of the 13th century; and 
 * the [[Shepherds' Crusade (1320)|Shepherd's Crusade]] of 1320 when many [[Jew]]s that lived in the city were killed. 
 
 Later, the [[Hundred Years' War]] affected the region. Most people that lived in the city were [[Catholicism|Catholic]]s but many [[Protestantism|Protestant]]s lived around the city. Fights were common here between those two groups during the [[French Wars of Religion]] in the 16th century. 
 
 The region was more calm during the following centuries, up to the time of the [[French Revolution]]. 
 
 From 1790 to 1795, Castelsarrasin was the capital of the [[District]] of Castelsarrasin in the [[Haute-Garonne]] department. In 1808, when the [[Tarn-et-Garonne]] department was created, the [[arrondissement of Castelsarrasin]] was made part of the new department. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.archivesdefrance.culture.gouv.fr/action-culturelle/celebrations-nationales/2008/vie-politique-et-institutions/creation-du-departement-de-tarn-et-garonne/|title=Création du département de Tarn-et-Garonne |publisher=Archives de France |access-date=5 July 2014 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Castelsarrasin is near the [[Garonne river]], which runs through the southwest of the commune. The city is at {{Convert|21|km|mi|abbr=on }} to the west of [[Montauban]], ''préfecture'' of the department. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|76.77|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|79|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|81|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Castelsarrasin-82100/ |title=Castelsarrasin |publisher=Map-France.com |access-date=5 July 2014 }} < /ref >

 ==Administration== 
 Castelsarrasin is a [[Prefectures in France|subprefecture]] of the Tarn-et-Garonne department since 1809. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Castelsarrasin|Castelsarrasin]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the canton [[Canton of Castelsarrasin|Castelsarrasin]] with 6 ''communes'' and 20,937 inhabitants in 2014. 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Terres des confluences''.

 ==Places of interest== 
 Some interesting places in Castelsarrasin are: 
 * The '''''Saint-Sauveur'' church''', mentioned since 961; it was rebuilt in 1254. 
 * The '''''Saint-Jean'' church''', mentioned since 1216; it was partially rebuilt in 1515. 
 * The '''''Notre-Dame d'Alem'' chapel'''. 
 * The '''''[[Jacques Cousteau|Jacques-Yves Cousteau]]'' [[port]]'''.
