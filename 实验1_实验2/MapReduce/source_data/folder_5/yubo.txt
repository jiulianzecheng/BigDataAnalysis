 '''Yubo''', or '''Yellow''', is a French [[Social network service|social networking]] app. < ref name= " TechCrunch " > {{Cite web|date=11 June 2019|title=Yubo is a social network about socializing|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2019/06/11/yubo-is-a-social-network-about-socializing/|access-date=2020-04-16|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}{{Dead link|date=July 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  The app has 60 million users as of 2022. < ref > {{Cite news|last=Klein|first=Alyson|date=2022-06-06|title=Yubo and Other Social Apps Popular With Kids: Facts and Cautions for Educators|language=en|work=Education Week|url=https://www.edweek.org/leadership/yubo-and-other-social-apps-popular-with-kids-facts-and-cautions-for-educators/2022/06|access-date=2022-09-07|issn=0277-4232}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 Yubo's creators consisted of three people. Back then, they were engineering students at [[CentraleSupélec]] Graduate school of the [[Paris-Saclay University]] and [[Télécom Paris]]. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2020-04-06|title=Sacha Lazimi (Yubo) : la social discovery à la française|url=https://www.planete-business.com/2020/04/06/sacha-lazimi-yubo-social-discovery/|access-date=2020-11-12|website=Planete Business|language=fr|archive-date=2020-12-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201210033318/https://www.planete-business.com/2020/04/06/sacha-lazimi-yubo-social-discovery/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It was first released in 2015. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Yubo, l'application pour ados qui inquiète les parents|url=https://www.cnews.fr/vie-numerique/2019-03-02/yubo-lapplication-pour-ados-qui-inquiete-les-parents-817940|access-date=2020-04-16|website=www.cnews.fr|language=fr}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|date=12 December 2019|title=Yubo lève 11 millions pour développer sa plateforme pour la génération Z|url=https://www.challenges.fr/club-entrepreneurs/yubo-leve-11-millions-pour-developper-son-appli-video-de-cour-de-recre-digitale_689007|access-date=2020-04-16|website=Challenges|language=fr}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[COVID-19 pandemic]] gave the app its popularity. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2021-07-07|title=Yubo adds digital currency to Gen Z social app|url=https://www.businessofapps.com/news/yubo-adds-digital-currency-to-gen-z-social-app/|access-date=2022-01-06|website=Business of Apps|language=en-US}} < /ref >  The daily signups reached 30,000 per day in mid-April.

 == Negative impact == 
 
 === Safety issues === 
 Yubo had increased media attention after the app popularized in 2019. The app was described as a  " Tinder for Teens " , < ref > {{Cite news|title=Teens using Yubo may be at risk – study|language=en|work=The Irish Times|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/education/teens-using-yubo-may-be-at-risk-study-1.3754117|access-date=2023-01-06}} < /ref >  paying attention to the swiping feature. People were suspicious of their children engaging in [[online dating]]. 
 
 Illegal actions have been reported, < ref name= " :3 " > {{Cite web|title=Man accused of sexually exploiting 12-year-old he met on Yubo app|url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/news/2019/02/14/man-accused-sexually-exploiting-12-year-old-he-met-yubo-app/2868063002/|access-date=2020-04-16|website=Cincinnati.com|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=KCBD Staff|date=July 5, 2022|title=Lubbock man accused of soliciting child pornography through French social app|url=https://www.kcbd.com/2022/07/05/lubbock-man-solicits-child-pornography-through-french-social-app/|access-date=2023-01-06|website=KCBD.com|language=en}} < /ref >  such as [[Pedophilia|predatory behavior]], [[Child grooming|grooming]], and child pornography. 
 
 Using the facial recognition technology, Yubo has deleted accounts that are under 13. 
 
 In 2018, Yubo worked to prevent [[cyberbullying]]. < ref name= " Australia " > {{Cite web|date=2018-08-14|title=Roblox and Yubo join Australian efforts to tackle cyberbullying {{!}} Office of the eSafety Commissioner|url=https://www.esafety.gov.au/about-the-office/newsroom/media-releases/roblox-and-yubo-join-australian-efforts-to-tackle-cyberbullying|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180814234844/https://www.esafety.gov.au/about-the-office/newsroom/media-releases/roblox-and-yubo-join-australian-efforts-to-tackle-cyberbullying|archive-date=2018-08-14|access-date=2020-11-15}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2020, Yubo created a program with the [[National Center for Missing  &  Exploited Children]] that scans for any bad content. The livestream is deleted when bad content is found. 
 
 In July 2021, Yubo was updated with the Muted Words feature. Users can choose to block people by muting them. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2021-07-16|title=Yubo launches ability to mute words to increase user safety|url=https://mumbrella.com.au/yubo-launches-ability-to-mute-words-to-increase-user-safety-693378|access-date=2022-01-06|website=Mumbrella|language=en-US}} < /ref >
