'''Playfire''' was a website for [[video game players]] to help gaming networking. Playfire helped users to track their in-game awards, achievements and [[gameplay]]. Kieran O'Neill, Sophia Hayes  &  Ben Phillips opened the company in December 2007. Playfire was the first website to release a [[PlayStation]] gamercard. It attracted a big number of gamers. < ref > {{cite web|last=Alexander|first=Leigh|date=July 8, 2008|title=Playfire Launches PS3 Trophies Gamercard Service|url=http://kotaku.com/5022059/playfire-launches-ps3-trophies-gamercard-service|publisher=Kotaku.com|access-date=October 6, 2021|archive-date=March 14, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170314111911/http://kotaku.com/5022059/playfire-launches-ps3-trophies-gamercard-service|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The site brought in an automatic tracking of [[Xbox 360]], [[PlayStation 3]] and [[PC gameplay]] in March 2011. < ref > {{cite web|author=Tom Cheshire|date=August 17, 2011|title=Playfire: The social network dedicated to gamers|url=https://www.wired.co.uk/magazine/archive/2011/09/start/network-gamer|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111201172718/http://www.wired.co.uk/magazine/archive/2011/09/start/network-gamer|archivedate=December 1, 2011|work=Wired}} < /ref > 
 == Investment == 
 Playfire got £1.3m in funding from investors such as [[Niklas Zennström]] (the founder of [[Skype]]), [[Michael Birch]] (the founder of [[Bebo]]), Chris Deering (the former Chairman of [[SCE]]), William Reeve (the founder of [[LOVEFiLM]]) and others. < ref > {{cite web|last=O'Hear|first=Steve|date=November 10, 2009|title=Confirmed: Gamer social network Playfire secures $2.1m Series A|url=https://techcrunch.com/2009/11/10/confirmed-gamer-social-network-playfire-secures-2-1m-series-a/|publisher=Techcrunch}} < /ref > 
 == History == 
 The idea for Playfire came from watching the gamers adding information about themselves such as their PSN ID, recently played games, and other things in their signatures under every forum post. 
 
 In September 2011 Playfire united with GameSpot to bring Playfire users profile to [[GameSpot]] through an API collaboration. 
 
 On 9 July 2012 Playfire was bought by [[Green Man Gaming]]. < ref > {{cite web|author=OMRI PETITTE|date=July 9, 2012|title=Green Man Gaming merges with gaming social network Playfire|url=https://venturebeat.com/2012/07/09/green-man-gaming-playfire-merge/|publisher=VentureBeat}} < /ref > 
 
 At the end of 2013, Green Man Gaming said that Playfire service was able to track in-game achievements/trophies and gameplay on Xbox One < ref > {{cite web|date=10 December 2013|title=Playfire now tracking Xbox One - This Is Xbox|url=http://www.thisisxbox.com/playfire-now-tracking-xbox-one/|publisher=|access-date=6 October 2021|archive-date=6 October 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211006113905/http://www.thisisxbox.com/playfire-now-tracking-xbox-one/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and [[PlayStation 4]]. < ref > {{cite web|date=2013-12-23|title=Gamasutra - The Art  &  Business of Making Games|url=http://www.gamasutra.com/view/pressreleases/207666/Playfire_now_tracking_PS4.php|publisher=Gamasutra}} < /ref > 
 
 In January 2014, Playfire started its Rewards BETA. < ref > {{cite web|author=Matthew Jarvis|date=January 30, 2014|title=Green Man Gaming starts Playfire Rewards beta|url=http://www.pcr-online.biz/news/read/green-man-gaming-launches-playfire-rewards-beta/033089|publisher=PC Retail}} < /ref >  the Rewards BETA motivated users to link their Steam account to their Playfire account in order to earn Green Man Gaming store Credit. < ref > {{cite web|date=September 10, 2014|title=Get paid to play Steam games with Playfire|url=http://www.computerandvideogames.com/476272/sponsored-get-paid-to-play-steam-games-with-playfire/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140911013950/http://www.computerandvideogames.com/476272/sponsored-get-paid-to-play-steam-games-with-playfire/|archivedate=September 11, 2014|publisher=}} < /ref >  Available Rewards are listed daily on the Playfire Rewards. Page < ref > {{cite web|title=Rewards BETA - Playfire|url=https://www.playfire.com/a/rewards|publisher=}} < /ref >  and by May 2014, over one million Rewards had been given out to BETA participants < ref > {{cite web|date=May 7, 2014|title=Playfire blazes through the ONE MILLION rewards mark!|url=http://www.mcvuk.com/press-releases/read/playfire-blazes-through-the-one-million-rewards-mark/0132119|work=MCV UK}} < /ref > 
 
 Playfire was the sponsor of the Most Played Game Award at the 32nd Golden Joystick Awards, < ref > {{cite web|date=October 21, 2014|title=Playfire announces Most Played Golden Joystick|url=http://www.pcgamer.com/playfire-announces-most-played-golden-joystick/|work=PC Gamer}} < /ref >  and sponsored the Gamers' Choice Award at the Games Media Awards 2014 < ref > {{cite web|date=September 30, 2014|title=Games Media Awards: Gamers' Choice vote is open now|url=http://www.mcvuk.com/news/read/games-media-awards-gamers-choice-vote-is-open-now/0139104|work=MCV UK}} < /ref >  in October 2014. 
 
 In blog post on 7 November 2016, [[:en:Green_Man_Gaming|Green Man Gaming]] said that Playfire is closing. It will be united with their new  " Community "  site that GMG made on the Muut platform. < ref > {{cite web|date=November 7, 2016|title=New community platform on the way|url=https://www.greenmangaming.com/blog/new-community-platform-on-the-way/}} < /ref >  Later, on 5 May 2017 [[:en:Green_Man_Gaming|Green Man Gaming]] said that it is coming to a close this year. < ref > {{cite web|date=May 5, 2017|title=Farewell, Playfire|url=https://www.greenmangaming.com/blog/playfire-to-close/}} < /ref >  As of 30 May 2017 Playfire was united with GMG Community with its website [https://www.playfire.com/ playfire.com]. Now it is redirecting to [https://www.greenmangaming.com/playfire greenmangaming.com/playfire].
