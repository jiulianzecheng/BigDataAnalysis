 {{Nihongo|'''Tajimamori'''|[[:ja:タヂマモリ|田道間守]]}} is a Japanese legendary hero of the [[Kofun period]], he is worshipped as the god of [[Confectionery|sweets]]. His name is written in [[kanji]] as 多遅麻毛理 in the ''[[Kojiki]]'', and as 田道間守 in the ''[[Nihon Shoki]]''. 
 
 
 [[Emperor Suinin]] commanded Tajimamori get a magical fruit called {{Nihongo||非時香菓|tokijiku no kagu no konomi}}. To find it he went to {{Nihongo||常世の国|[[Tokoyo no kuni]]}}, < ref name= " :4 " > {{Cite web|title=Encyclopedia of Shinto詳細|url=https://d-museum.kokugakuin.ac.jp/eos/detail/?id=8740|access-date=2023-03-10|website=國學院大學デジタルミュージアム|language=ja}} < /ref >  after ten years he returned but Suinin was dead already. Tajimamori gave half of his branches to Suinin's widow and half to his tomb and then died. 
 
 
 Tajimamori is worshipped as the god of sweets in {{Interlanguage link|Nakashima Shrine|ja|中嶋神社}} in [[Hyōgo Prefecture]]. He is mainly worshipped by people who make candy.
