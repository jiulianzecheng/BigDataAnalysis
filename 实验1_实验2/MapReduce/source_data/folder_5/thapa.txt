 '''Thapa''' ({{lang-ne|थापा}}; [[IAST]]:Thāpā) is a family name or surname used by the Gorkhali community of [[India]] and [[Nepal]] belonging to both [[Magar]] caste within [[Khas people]] of [[Indo-Aryan]] group and [[Magar]] caste of [[Indo-Aryan]] group. < ref name =  " cH5uAAAAMAAJ " > {{cite book| last = Midun| first = Yamesh| title = The Khasa kingdom: a trans-Himalayan empire of the middle age| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=cH5uAAAAMAAJ| year = 1920| publisher = Bimal thapa Publications| isbn = 978-81-85693-50-7 }} < /ref > 
 
 Thapa was a title given to warriors in the ancient Khas Kingdom.Both Magar Thapa and Kshetri Thapa has done remarkably significant for Nepal.In present Nepal both Thapa Kshetri and Thapa Magar are doing great in Politics and enhancing National Pride. 
 
 Thapas has been involved in politics of Nepal for long time. Thapas were prominent military heads in various princely states in ancient Nepal.{{sfn|K.L. Pradhan|2012}} Thapas led military and civil administration in the unified Kingdom of Nepal after 1806 & nbsp;AD. [[Bhimsen Thapa]] of Thapa clan rose to prominence after the assassination of King [[Rana Bahadur Shah]] of Nepal by King's step-brother in 1805 & nbsp;AD. After the event, an investigation was run by Bharadars (Ministers) which caused massacre of 55 senior officers at Bhandarkhal garden by Thapa Kaji (ministers) group making them as most dominant faction in Nepalese politics.{{sfn|K.L. Pradhan|2012|p=16}}

 After Bhimsen rose to power, member of another Thapa family, Bada (senior) Kaji Amar Singh Thapa{{ref|note=Not to be confused with father of PM Bhimsen Thapa, Sanu (junior) Kaji Amar Singh Thapa who was 8 years younger than Senior Kaji Amar Singh.}} and his family were added to make Thapa Khalak dynasty stronger.{{sfn|Ramesh Midun Magar|2012|p=26}} Large number of Thapa leaders took part in [[Anglo-Nepalese War]] in 1814-16 & nbsp;AD. The immediate family of Bhimsen and Senior Kaji Amar Singh took over all the military commands in that war.  ==Khas Kshatri Thapa== 
 Khas Thapa or Chhetri/Kshatri Thapa are [[Khas people]] of [[Indo-Aryan]] group. They are categorized into [[Kshatriya]] in Hinduism. They follow [[Hinduism]] and are divided by Khas clans and Hindu [[Gotra]] system. The clan names of Khas Kshatri Thapas are: ''[[Bagale Thapa]]'', ''[[Punwar Thapa]]'', ''[[Godar Thapa]]'', ''Lamichhane Thapa'', ''Suyal Thapa'', ''Kalikote Thapa'', ''Hrikshen Thapa'', ''Khulal Thapa'', ''Deoja Thapa'', ''Thakuryal Thapa'', ''Gamle Thapa'', ''Sonal Thapa'', and many others. 
 ===Bagale Thapa=== 
 {{main|Bagale Thapa}} 
 [[File:Mathabar Simha Thapa.jpg|thumb|Prime Minister of Nepal [[Mathabar Singh Thapa]], a nobleman from Bagale Thapa clan]] 
 Bagale Thapa is the most prominent clan within Khas Thapa. They fall under Atreya [[Gotra]] in Hinduism.  
 ===Puwar Thapa=== 
 [[File:Kamal Thapa 2015.jpg|alt=|thumb|Kamal Thapa, a politician of Nepal from Puwar Thapa clan]] 
 Puwar Thapa is also a clan within Khas Thapa. They fall under Kapil Gotra in Hinduism. 
 ===Godar Thapa=== 
 
 Godar Thapa is a clan within [[Chhetri]] [[Thapa Kaji]] of [[Khas]] origin.{{sfn|Pradhan|2012|p=22}} They claim [[Kashyap]] [[Gotra]] in the Gotra system of Hinduism. < ref name= " bijaykuikel "  / > < ref name= " Antiquary "  / >  They are also the variant of Shivbanshi Godara clan from India. 
 
 ==Magar Thapa== 
 [[File:Netrabahadur Thapa VC IND 3956.jpg|thumb|upright|Victoria Crossholder Netra Bahadur Thapa Magar, member of Magar caste]] 
 Magar Thapa or Thapa Magar are member of Magar caste which falls under Sino-Tibetan or Tibeto-Burman group. They speak Magar language and follow tradition of Magar caste. They can be found as core soldiers in [[Gurkha]] armies around the world.They also served as one of the six noble clans in the thargah of King prithvi Narayan Shah and were prominent nobles until the kot massacre.
