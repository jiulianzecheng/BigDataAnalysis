 '''''Atlantihyla''''' is a [[genus]] of [[Frog|frogs]] in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  These frogs [[endemic|live in]] [[Honduras]] and [[Guatemala]] in Central America. < ref name=Frost/ >  Scientists named these frogs ''Atlantihyla'' because they live on the east side of the [[isthmus|narrow piece of land]], which is the same side as the Atlantic Ocean. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / >  These frogs are named '''[[Stream frog (disambiguation)|stream frogs]]''' in English. 
 
 ==Taxonomy== 
 Scientists made the genus using [[molecular data|information about DNA]]. Julián Faivovich and colleagues looked at the subfamily [[Hylinae]] in 2018. They moved two former ''Ptychohyla'' species to ''Atlantihyla'' and four former ''Ptychohyla'' species to  ''[[Quilticohyla]].'' ''Atlantihyla'' is a [[sister group]] with ''[[Bromeliohyla]]'', ''[[Duellmanohyla]]'', and ''Quilticohyla''. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / >  A third ''Atlantihyla'' species was described in 2020. < ref name= " Townsend et al. 2020 " / > 
 
 ==Description== 
 These frogs only look alike in one way: They have a white stripe on the sides of their bodies and a ridge on their backs. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / >  Adult male frogs are {{convert|30|-|41|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} and adult female frogs are {{convert|31|-|46|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} long [[snout–vent length|from nose to rear end]]. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / > < ref name= " Townsend et al. 2020 " / >  The [[tadpole]]s have large mouth discs. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2018 " / > 
 
 == Species == 
 There are three species in this genus: < ref name=Frost/ > 
 * ''[[Atlantihyla melissa]]''  < small > Townsend, Herrera-B., Hofmann, Luque-Montes, Ross, Dudek, Krygeris, Duchamp, and Wilson, 2020 < /small > < ref name= " Townsend et al. 2020 " / > 
 * ''[[Guatemala stream frog|Atlantihyla panchoi]]''  < small > (Duellman and Campbell, 1982) < /small >  – Guatemala stream frog 
 * ''[[Ceiba stream frog|Atlantihyla spinipollex]]''  < small > (K. P. Schmidt, 1936) < /small >  – Ceiba stream frog 
 
 [[AmphibiaWeb]] says this genus is monotypic, with ''Atlantihyla melissa'' as the only species in it. < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >
