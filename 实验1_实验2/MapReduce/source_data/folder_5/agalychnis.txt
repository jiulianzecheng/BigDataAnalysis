 '''''Agalychnis''''' is a genus of [[tree frog]]s that live in forests in [[Mexico]], [[Central America]], and northwestern South America.  Scientists have also called them ''Pachymedusa''.  They are also called leaf frogs, rough leaf frogs, Mexican giant tree frogs, middle American multicolored tree frogs, and Mexican leaf frogs. < ref name=AMNH > {{cite web|publisher=[[American Museum of Natural History]]|work=[[Amphibian Species of the World 6.1, an Online Reference]]|url=https://amphibiansoftheworld.amnh.org/Amphibia/Anura/Phyllomedusidae/Agalychnis|accessdate=February 13, 2022|title=Agalychnis Cope, 1864 }} < /ref > 
 
 These frogs live on the Pacific (western) side of the mountains in [[Mexico]].  They also live in [[Colombia]], [[Ecuador]], the [[Venezuela]], and [[Peru]]. < ref name=AMNH / >
