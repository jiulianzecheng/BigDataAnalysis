 '''Charlottetown''' is the capital and the largest city of the [[Canada|Canadian]] [[Provinces and territories of Canada|province]] of [[Prince Edward Island]]. 
 
 The population of Charlottetown was 34,562 in 2011. < ref name=statcan > {{cite web|url=http://www12.statcan.gc.ca/census-recensement/2011/as-sa/fogs-spg/Facts-cma-eng.cfm?Lang=Eng & TAB=1 & GK=CMA & GC=105|access-date=2012-12-12|title=Focus on Geography Series, 2011 Census – Census agglomeration of Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island|date=8 February 2012|publisher=Statistics Canada}} < /ref >  The mayor of Charlottetown is Clifford J. Lee. 
 
 The French were the first European settlers in the area and they founded a settlement in 1720 named Port La Joye. British forces built Fort Amherst near the site of the abandoned Port La Joye settlement after they took control of the settlement and the rest of the island during the height of the French and Indian War. The town is named after [[Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz]], [[queen consort]] of the [[United Kingdom]]. < ref name=CoC > {{cite web|url=http://www.city.charlottetown.pe.ca/cityhistory.php|access-date=2012-12-08|title=City History|publisher=Charlottetown|archive-date=2011-09-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110921024557/http://www.city.charlottetown.pe.ca/cityhistory.php|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
