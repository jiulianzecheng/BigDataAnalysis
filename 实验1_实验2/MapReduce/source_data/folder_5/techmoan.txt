 '''Matthew Julius  " Mat "  Taylor''' (born January 1971), better known by his stage name '''Techmoan''', is a [[YouTuber]] and [[Blog|blogger]] who has been active since May 2009, featuring reviews of [[consumer electronics]] (or [[consumer tech]]) and  " RetroTech "  documentaries. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/techmoan/about|title=Techmoan/about|via=YouTube|access-date=24 July 2018}} < /ref >  He lives in [[Wigan]], [[Greater Manchester]].

 == Career == 
 In 2006, Taylor started a YouTube channel called  " Vectrexuk " , with videos of similar items of technology, such as installing a home cinema and controlled toasters, < ref > {{Citation|last=Techmoan|title=Techmoan - Not the 10th Anniversary Show|date=2017-07-25|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I-a25On3lPU|access-date=2018-11-12}} < /ref > < ref > {{Citation|title=Vectrexuk|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Vectrexuk|website=YouTube|access-date=2019-05-06}} < /ref >  to prove people will watch anything on YouTube. < ref > {{Cite web|url = http://www.techmoan.com/about/|title = About Techmoan}} < /ref > < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I-a25On3lPU Techmoan - Not the 10th Anniversary Show], 25 July 2017 < /ref > 
 
 The channel  " Techmoan "  was started on May 31, 2009. The first video uploaded on Techmoan was a tour of a 2009 [[Piaggio MP3]], which was filmed at [[480p]] and had a very basic sound quality. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite web|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kl38HRGcxK4|title = Youtube -Techmoan's First Video| website=[[YouTube]] }} < /ref >  In 2015, he started another channel, called  " Youtube Pedant " , to host videos not about technology. < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC6NEXZflFAvd1b7cAIe3XOw/about About the YouTube Channel  " Youtube Pedant " ] < /ref >  In 2016, in a video covering the [[D-VHS|DVHS]] format, he uncovered [[1080p]] video footage of [[New York City]] from 1993. < ref > {{Citation|title=Retro-Tech: When HD Movies came on VHS|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jiu0LPeLQPE|language=en|access-date=2019-12-04}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.techmoan.com/blog/2016/4/22/retro-tech-that-time-when-hd-came-on-vhs.html|title=Techmoan - Techmoan - Retro-tech. That time when HD came on VHS|website=www.techmoan.com|language=en|access-date=2019-12-04}} < /ref >  This footage was uploaded separately to his  " Youtube Pedant "  channel where, as of December 2019, it has received 3.8 million views, and it has also been widely shared on websites such as [[Reddit]] < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/videos/comments/4fzyrd/new_york_city_in_1993_recorded_in_high_definition/|title=r/videos - New York City in 1993 recorded in High Definition.|website=reddit|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-04}} < /ref >  and [[The Verge]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.theverge.com/2016/4/25/11503816/new-york-city-hd-footage-manhattan|title=Holy schnikes, this HD footage from 1993 NYC looks like it was filmed today|last=Plante|first=Chris|date=2016-04-25|website=The Verge|language=en|access-date=2019-12-04}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://boingboing.net/2019/08/20/new-york-city-in-1993.html|title=This footage of New York in 1993 will make you miss New York in 1993|date=2019-08-20|website=Boing Boing|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-04}} < /ref >  As of June 2019, the main channel has over 870,000 subscribers and over 180 million views. Some videos on the main channel have had over 3 million views. 
 
 === Later documentary videos === 
 Techmoan has made documentary videos that are about forgotten [[magnetic tape]] recording formats, such as the [[OMNI Entertainment System]], < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zyOKgLtUxto MB OMNI Entertainment System - The 1980s 8-Track games machine.], 6 August 2017 < /ref >  which uses [[8-track tape]] storage, and the [[HiPac]], a successor of the [[PlayTape]] and related uses of it. Other videos show some of the smallest and largest analog recording tape cartridges ever made, such as the [[Picocassette]] < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t5FqjQlFBDM The Picocassette – Smallest Analogue Cassette Tape ever made], 2 August 2015 < /ref >  for dictation machines or the [[Cantata 700]] background music system. < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1WQbJ0VFrFQ Retro Tech: This 1960s BGM Machine played the Biggest Cassettes ever made], 11 May 2016 < /ref >  Further videos show other discontinued ¼-inch-tape cartridge formats, such as the [[Sabamobil]], < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rU-iSTj2cTY Forgotten Format: The Sabamobil], 22 June 2017 < /ref >  which uses existing 3-inch open reels in a portable format, and the portable [[Sanyo Micro Pack 35]], < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M-Qr0wNTFHA Forgotten Format: SANYO Micro-Pack 35 Tape Recorder], 31 August 2017 < /ref >  as well as [[RCA tape cartridge]] < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Li699Qflv3g RetroTech: RCA Victor Tape Cartridge - A trailblazing failure], 22 September 2016 < /ref >  and the Sony [[Elcaset]] < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkGMJBqZawA Forgotten Audio Formats: DCC  &  Elcaset] 6 May 2014 < /ref >  with another compromise of playtime and sound quality, oddities and gimmicks on [[Compact Cassette]]s as  " reinventing the reel " , < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=50kOJRfVCQ4 TEAC O'Casse Open Cassette - Reinventing the Reel], 16 May 2015 < /ref > < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RtYmaKsWwrU Audio Craft Cassette Cartridge: More music per pocket.], 12 April 2017 < /ref >  several ways of [[autoreverse]], < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2CE_zmpHcWQ Auto-Reverse: The Hard Way], 26 February 2016 < /ref >  automatic multiple cassette players, < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MFOM3W2FBfA What a 10hr music playlist looked like in 1992], 30 December 2015 < /ref > < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RJo13FP4UpI Retro-Tech: The 1972 Desktop 'iPod'], 14 August 2016 < /ref >  [[Endless tape cartridge|endless loop cassettes]], < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xU1aYAHmaJI Cassettes: Lenticular Classics  &  Endless Loops], 13 September 2016 < /ref >  and cassette mass production technology. < ref > [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jVoSQP2yUYA Cassettes -
