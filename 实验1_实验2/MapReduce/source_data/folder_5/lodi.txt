 '''Lodi''' ({{IPA-it|ˈlɔːdi|lang|It-Lodi.ogg}}; [[Western Lombard|Lombard]]: ''Lòd'') is a city in northern [[Italy]]. Lodi is in the [[Lombardy]] Region with a population of 43,488 people (as of [[2008]]). < ref name= " istat " > source ISTAT - Italian Institute of Statistics < /ref >  The people who live in Lodi are called Lodigiani. Lodi is famous for a [[cake]] called [[Tortionata]]. 
 
 Lodi was built after the destruction of the ancient roman city of Laus Pompeia during the war with [[Milan]]. 
 
 The construction of the new city was aided by the German emperor [[Frederick I Barbarossa]].
