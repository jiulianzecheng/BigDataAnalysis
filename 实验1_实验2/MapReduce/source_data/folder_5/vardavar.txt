'''Vardavar''' is a festival in [[Armenia]] where people throw water at each other. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.mecc.org/mecc/2021/7/7/the-armenian-vardavar-between-church-celebrations-and-popular-traditions|title=The Armenian Vardavar: Between Church Celebrations and Popular Traditions|date=7 July 2021|website=[[:en:MECC|Minnesota Educational Computing Consortium]]|first=Elia|last=Nasrallah|access-date=18 October 2023}} < /ref >  It is celebrated 98 days (14 weeks) after [[Easter]]. 
 
 The celebrations related to the religious observance of [[:en:Ast≈Çik|Astghik]] were initially called  " Vartavar "  because Armenians used to offer her roses as a symbol of celebration. The word  " vart "  means  " rose "  in Armenian, and  " var "  means  " rise " . The event was traditionally celebrated during the harvest season. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/festival-culture-armenia/25041818.html|title=Armenians Celebrate The Vardavar Water Festival|date=8 July 2013|website=[[:en:Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty|Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty]]|access-date=18 October 2023}} < /ref >
