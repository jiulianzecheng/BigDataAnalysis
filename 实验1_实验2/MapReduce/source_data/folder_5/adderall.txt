'''Amphetamine mixed salts''' (also known as '''amphetamine and dextroamphetamine mixed salts''', '''amphetamine salt combo''', or simply '''amphetamine salts''') is a [[medication]] used in the treatment of [[attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder]] (ADHD) and [[narcolepsy]]. < ref > {{cite web | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2013/02/03/us/concerns-about-adhd-practices-and-amphetamine-addiction.html?pagewanted=all & _r=0/ | title = Drowned in a Stream of Prescriptions | author = Schwartz A | work = New York Times | date =2013-02-13}} < /ref >  It is sold under the [[brand]] name '''Adderall'''. The drug contains a mixture of the [[salt (chemistry)|salts]] of [[amphetamine]] and [[dextroamphetamine]]. Both of these salts act as [[stimulant]]s. The drug therefore helps people stay alert and concentrate on what they are doing. The downside of this medication is decreased fatigue, and decreased appetite. 
 
 The sustained-release (or extended-release) formulation is sold under the brand name Adderall XR. This formulation is designed to provide therapeutic effect and plasma concentrations. This is the same as taking two doses 4 hours apart. < ref name= " AdderallXR " > {{cite web|title=Medication Guide Adderall XR|url=http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2007/021303s015lbl.pdf|publisher=US Food and Drug Administration (FDA)|accessdate=19 May 2013}} < /ref >  The normal dosage of Adderall XR is 20 milligrams. 
 
 == Key ingredients of Adderall (amphetamine) == 
 ===Active ingredients=== 
 * Amphetamine aspartate 
 * Dextroamphetamine saccharate 
 * Amphetamine sulfate 
 * Dextroamphetamine sulfate 
 
 ===Inactive ingredients=== 
 * Saccharin sodium 
 * Colloidal silicon dioxide 
 * Microcrystalline cellulose 
 * Compressible sugar 
 * Corn starch  
 * Magnesium stearate
