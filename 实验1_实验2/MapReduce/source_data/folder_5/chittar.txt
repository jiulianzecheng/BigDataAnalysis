 '''Chittar''' is a village in [[Pathanamthitta district]], [[Kerala]] state, [[India]]. It is a [[Panchayat]] in the [[Ranni]] [[Tehsil|Taluk]]. Chittar was given the Nirmal Gram award from [[Pratibha Patil]], the former President of India. 
 
 == Economy == 
 
 Chittar is a [[plantation]] township.The main crops grown here are [[rubber]], [[pineapple]], [[tapioca]], nuts, ginger, and [[Black pepper|pepper]]. It was once a part of the demolished Nilakkal trade centre. 
 
 == Location == 
 
 Chittar lies in the eastern part of Pathanamthitta district. 
 
 == Administration == 
 
 The panchayath of Chittar was formed in 30 September 1970 by partitioning the Vadaseerikkara and Seethathodu panchayaths in Ranni taluk. The panchayath is divided into 13 wards.

 ==People== 
 
 The people of Chittar are mainly farmers and plantation workers.
