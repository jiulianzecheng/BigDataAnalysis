 '''Nagpur''' is a city in state of [[Maharashtra]] in [[India]]. It is in the centre of India. < ref name= " Zero Mile in Nagpur " > {{Cite web|title=Nagpur|url=http://www.maharashtra.gov.in/marathi/mahInfo/nagpur.php|publisher=Maharashtra Government|accessdate=1 June 2006}} < /ref >  In the [[Marathi language]], ''nag'' means [[snake]] and ''pur'' means [[city]]. The city is called Nagpur because of the ''Nag'' river in this city which flows like a snake. It is the winter capital of Maharashtra. < ref name= " Bhargava2006 " > {{cite book|author=S. C. Bhatt, Gopal K. Bhargava|title=Land and People of Indian States and Union Territories: In 36 Volumes. Maharashtra|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IleHyBDWbzEC & pg=PA464|accessdate=7 July 2012|year=2006|publisher=Gyan Publishing House|isbn=978-81-7835-372-2|pages=464–}} < /ref >  After [[Mumbai]] and [[Pune]], Nagpur is the third largest city in Maharashtra. It is a very clean city. It was selected as the second greenest city of India. < ref > {{cite news | url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-12-09/nagpur/30497315_1_greenest-city-trees-sustainable-cities | work=The Times Of India | title=Make Nagpur greenest city of India | date=9 December 2011 }}{{Dead link|date=April 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 Many [[Orange (fruit)|oranges]] are grown in this city, so it is also called ''Orange City''. < ref name= " Gupta2006 " > {{cite book|author=Om Gupta|title=Encyclopaedia of India, Pakistan and Bangladesh|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VY1nTMBQ9vQC & pg=PA1677|accessdate=7 July 2012|date=1 April 2006|publisher=Gyan Publishing House|isbn=978-81-8205-389-2|pages=1677–}} < /ref >
