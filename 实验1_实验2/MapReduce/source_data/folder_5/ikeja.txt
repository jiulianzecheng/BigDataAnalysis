 '''Ikeja''' is a town and local government area of [[Lagos State]], [[Nigeria]]. It serves as the capital of Lagos State. < ref name= " LLC2010 " > {{cite book|author=Books LLC|title=Local Government Areas in Lagos State: Ikeja, Amuwo-Odofin, Ikorodu, Kosofe, Lagos Island, Badagry, Oshodi-Isolo, Eti-Osa, Surulere|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pqe1SQAACAAJ|date=May 2010|publisher=General Books LLC|isbn=978-1-155-21653-9}} < /ref >  Ikeja is known for being a well-planned commercial settlement with shopping malls, pharmacies and government reservation areas. 
 
 ==History== 
 Ikeja, originally known as  " Akeja, "  was named after a god of the Awori tribe of Ota.  < ref > {{Cite web|last=Ifeoma|first=Peters|date=2017-06-08|title=Lagos-based Lawyer Tanimola Anjorin Unravels the Origin of the Word “Ikeja” - DNL Legal and Style|url=https://dnllegalandstyle.com/2017/lagos-based-lawyer-tanimola-anjorin-unravels-origin-word-ikeja/|access-date=2023-09-24|language=en-US}} < /ref > The Awori people first settled there, and the territory was attacked for slaves until the mid-nineteenth century. It formed an agricultural [[hinterland]] for Lagos in the early twentieth century.  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ikeja City Towns in Lagos Nigeria Guide|url=https://www.nigeriagalleria.com/Nigeria/States_Nigeria/Lagos/History-of-Ikeja-in-Lagos.html|access-date=2023-09-24|website=www.nigeriagalleria.com}} < /ref > The construction of the Lagos-Ibadan railway in 1901, as well as the expansion of Lagos as a port, converted Ikeja into a residential and industrial district of Lagos. In the mid-1960s, an industrial estate was constructed, and in 1976, Ikeja was designated as the state capital of Lagos.  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ikeja {{!}} Location, Facts,  &  Population {{!}} Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Ikeja|access-date=2023-09-24|website=www.britannica.com|language=en}} < /ref >
