 {{Audio|Trondheim.ogg|'''Trondheim'''}} is a [[city]] and [[municipality]] in the [[county]] of [[Sør-Trøndelag]], [[Norway]]. With {{formatnum:212660}} people that live there (as of August 2023), Trondheim is Norway's third largest [[municipality]]. < ref name= " SSB "  / >  It is also has the fourth biggest [[urban area]] in Norway, with a [[population]] of about {{formatnum:186364}}. < ref name= " SSB2 "  / > 
 
 == History == 
 The city of Trondheim was founded in 997. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_yIjfXhQY_QC & pg=PA203|first=Jan|last=Sjåvik|publisher=[[Rowman  &  Littlefield|Scarecrow Press]]|title=The A to Z of Norway (The A to Z Guide Series Book 234)|page=203|year=2010|access-date=21 August 2023|isbn=9780810872134|archive-date=28 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210528153921/https://books.google.com/books?id=_yIjfXhQY_QC & pg=PA203|url-status=live}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|date=2023-08-09|title=Trondheim {{!}} History, Culture  &  Attractions {{!}} Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Trondheim|access-date=2023-08-21|website=Encyclopædia Britannica|language=en|quote=It was founded in 997 by King Olaf I Tryggvason as the village of Kaupangr; he built a church and a royal residence, Kongsgård, there.}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Nikel|first=David|date=2020-06-19|title=The History of Trondheim|url=https://www.lifeinnorway.net/history-of-trondheim/|access-date=2023-08-21|website=Life in Norway|language=en-US|quote=Most historians accept that Olav Tryggvason founded Trondheim in the year 997.}} < /ref >  It was frequently used as the seat of the [[king]], and was [[Capital (city)|capital]] of Norway until 1217. In the [[Middle Ages]], Trondheim was the site of several [[battle]]s, including the battle between [[King Sverre]] and [[Erling Skakke]], in 1179. < ref > {{Cite web|date=3 October 2020|title=Sverresborg|url=https://berloga-workshop.com/blog/532-sverresborg.html|access-date=2023-08-21|website=Berloga Workshop|quote=During the spring of 1179, Magnus arrived with his army. The two armies met on June 19 in the Battle of Kalvskinnet (Slaget på Kalvskinnet) with the result being a clear victory for Sverre, securing his grip on Trøndelag. Magnus Erlingsson again attacked Trøndelag in the spring of 1180.}} < /ref >  The city has experienced several major fires - the most devastating in 1651 and 1681. The 1651 fire destroyed 90% of the [[building]]s in Trondheim, and the 1681 fire led to a total reconstruction of the city. 
 
 The city of Trondheim became a municipality January 1, 1838. The [[rural]] municipalities of [[Byneset]], [[Leinstrand]], [[Strinda]] and [[Tiller, Norway|Tiller]] were joined into Trondheim on January 1, 1964.
