 }}'''Fiat''' ( < small > UK: < /small >  /ˈfiːət, -æt/,  < small > US: < /small >  /-ɑːt/,  < small > Italian: < /small >  [ˈfiːat]; originally '''FIAT''', Italian: ''Fabbrica Italiana Automobili Torino'',  < small > lit.  < /small > 'Italian Automobiles Factory, Turin') is a [[car]] company in [[Italy]]. It was founded in [[Turin]] in 1899. It also made some [[aircraft|planes]] between [[1920s]] and [[1970s]]. Some of their cars by year: Fiat is currently owned by Stellantis. 
 
 == History == 
 On 11 July 1899, [[Gianni Agnelli]] was part of the group of founding members of FIAT, ''Fabbrica Italiana di Automobili Torino''. The first Fiat plant opened in 1900 < ref > {{cite web|last=Scottsdale|first=FIAT of|title=A Brief History of Fiat and Its Century of Automaking|url=https://www.fiatusaofscottsdale.com/blog/2015/april/24/a-brief-history-of-fiat.htm|access-date=12 December 2017|website=www.fiatusaofscottsdale.com|language=en-US}} < /ref >  with 35 staff making 24 cars. Known from the beginning for the talent and creativity of its engineering staff, by 1903 Fiat made a small profit and produced 135 cars; this grew to 1,149 cars by 1906. The company then went public selling shares via the [[Milan stock exchange]].
