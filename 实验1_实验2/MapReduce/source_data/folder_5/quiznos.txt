 '''QIP Holder LLC''', business known as '''Quiznos''', is a [[fast food]] [[restaurant]] brand. Quiznos is known for their toasted subs. The restaurant was created by company founder Jimmy Lambatos in 1982. The headquarters for Quiznos is in [[Denver]], [[Colorado]]. Behind [[Subway (restaurant)|Subway]], it is the second-largest [[submarine sandwich]] shop chain in [[North America]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://finance.yahoo.com/news/crisis-quickens-quiznos-004100292.html|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Crisis Quickens at Quiznos|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131214113521/http://finance.yahoo.com/news/crisis-quickens-quiznos-004100292.html|archive-date=2013-12-14}} < /ref > 
 
 On February 27 2014, the [[The Wall Street Journal|Wall Street Journal]] wrote that the company was potentially preparing to file for Bankruptcy debt protection because the country currently has $570 million of debt. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702303801304579409314001511506|accessdate=2014-03-10|title=Quiznos Moves Toward Bankruptcy Filing|newspaper=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=27 February 2014}} < /ref >  On March 14, 2014, the restaurant brand filed for [[Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code|Chapter 11 Bankruptcy]]. They said that they would still continue operating while it restructured its debt and made operational improvements. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/food/dailydish/la-dd-quiznos-files-bankruptcy-toasted-subs-20140317,0,5634813.story#axzz2wGXXTEjs|accessdate=2014-03-17|title=Quizno's files for bankruptcy, but you can still get a toasted sub|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=17 March 2014}} < /ref >
