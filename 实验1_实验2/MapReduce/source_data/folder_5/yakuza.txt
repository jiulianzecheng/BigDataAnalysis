 {{Nihongo|'''''Yakuza'''''|ヤクザ||{{IPA-ja|jaꜜkɯza|IPA}}|lead=yes}}, known also as {{Nihongo|'''''gokudō'''''|極道|| " the extreme path " , {{IPA-ja|gokɯꜜdoː|IPA}}}}, are members of transnational [[organized crime]] syndicates in [[Japan]].  
 
 Yakuza call themselves ninkyō dantai (chivalrous organizations) which the Japanese media and police call them bōryokudan (violence group). They are the world's largest criminal organization and have about 103,000 members. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/treasury-identifies-eight-gangsters-2013-1|accessdate=2014-01-20|title=US Treasury Freezes Assets Of Top Gangsters From Around The World|publisher=[[Business Insider]]}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/life/2011/11/06/life/ex-tokyo-cop-speaks-out-on-a-life-fighting-gangs-and-what-you-can-do/|accessdate=2014-01-20|title=Kiyoshi Nakabayashi: Ex-Tokyo cop speaks out on a life fighting gangs — and what you can do|date=6 November 2011|publisher=Japan Times}} < /ref >  The three biggest Yakuza families are [[Yamaguchi-gumi]], [[Sumiyoshi-kai]], and [[Inagawa-kai]]. During the [[2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami]], they opened their offices to help refugees and sent dozens of trucks with supplies to affected areas. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2011/03/18/japanese-yakuza-aid-earthquake-relief-efforts.html|accessdate=2014-01-20|title=Yakuza to the Rescue|newspaper=The Daily Beast|date=18 March 2011|last1=Adelstein|first1=Jake}} < /ref >
