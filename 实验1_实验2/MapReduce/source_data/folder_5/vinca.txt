 '''''Vinca''''' (from [[Latin]]  ''vincire''  " to bind, fetter " ) is a genus of five species in the family [[Apocynaceae]], that grows in [[Europe]], northwest [[Africa]] and southwest [[Asia]]. The common name, shared with the related genus ''[[Catharanthus]]'', is '''Periwinkle'''. 
 
 [[Image:Vinca major0.jpg|left|thumb|Large Periwinkle (''Vinca major'') flower]] 
 They are [[subshrub]]s or [[Herbs|herbaceous]], and have slender [[Plant stem|stems]] 1–2 m (3–6 feet) long but not growing more than 20–70 & nbsp;cm (8-30 inches) above ground; the stems frequently take root where they touch the ground, so the plant can spread widely
