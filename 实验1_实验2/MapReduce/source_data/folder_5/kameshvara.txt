 '''Kameshvara''' ([[sanskrit]]:कामेश्वर, IAST:kāmeśvara), '''Mahakameshvara''' is one of the highest forms of Lord [[Shiva]], the almighty god of the universe in the Shaiva tradition of Hinduism. He is mainly mentioned in the Hindu scriptures Lalita Mahatmyam of the the [[Brahmanda Purana]] and and Tripura Rahasyam. < ref > {{Cite book|last=Tagare|first=G.V.|title=The Brahmanda Purana|publisher=Motilal Banarsidass Publications|year=2020|isbn=9788120838246}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|last=Rao|first=Lakshmana|title=Shri Tripura Rahasya (Mahatmya Khanda)|year=2011|isbn=}} < /ref >  Kameshvara is the highest form of Shiva and considered to be the omnipotent deity. < ref > {{Cite book|last=Ravi|first=V|title=Lalita Sahasranama|publisher=MANBLUNDER|year=2010|isbn=9781456417635|pages=90}} < /ref > 
 
 == Nomenclature == 
 Kameshvara means Lord of desires. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Paramacharya on Soundarya-Lahari|last=Krishnamurthy|first=V|year=2023|isbn=9798888693285|location=Notion Press}} < /ref >
