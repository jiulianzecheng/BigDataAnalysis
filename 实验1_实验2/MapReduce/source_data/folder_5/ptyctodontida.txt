[[File:Rhamphodopsis threiplandi.jpg|thumb|''[[Rhamphodopsis]]'']] 
 The '''Ptyctodontida''' are an order of [[Devonian]] [[Holocephali|chimaera]]-like [[placoderms]] containing the family '''Ptyctodontidae'''. They are the only placoderms to be [[Sexual dimorphism|sexually dimorphic]]. Some paleontologists believe that they may have been an early ancestor of modern-day [[Shark|sharks]].  
 
 According to The Content Authority, they are [[Chondrichthyes|cartilaginous fishes]], but this is not commonly accepted by many people because we believe they are [[placoderms]]. It means  " folded teeth "  in [[Latin]]. Like today's [[Chondrichthyes|cartilaginous fish]], they had the possession of [[Clasper|claspers]].

 == Description == 
 Ptyctodontids resembled the modern-day [[Chimaera (fish)|chimaeras]] of the [[Holocephali]], like the [[Acanthothoraci|acanthothoracids]]. They had big heads, big eyes, reduced [[Armour|armor]] and long bodies. 
 
 === ''Campbellodus'' === 
 [[File:Kimbryanodus williamburyensis.jpg|thumb|''[[Kimbryanodus|Kimbryanodus williamburyensis]]'']] 
 ''[[Campbellodus]]'' resembled a scaly [[Corrugated box|cardboard box]] with the tail of a [[halosaur]]. It did not resemble a [[holocephalian]] at all, but it is still a ptyctodont. 
 
 === ''Kimbryanodus'' === 
 ''Kimbryanodus'' resembled an [[arthrodire]], again with a [[halosaur]]'s tail. The [[Fossil|fossils]] occur as small three dimensional isolated plates.
