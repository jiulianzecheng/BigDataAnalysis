 '''Ichnology''' is the study of [[trace fossil]]s of once-living things. < ref name=dict > {{cite web |url=http://www.dictionary.com/browse/ichnology |title=ichnology |publisher=Dictionary.com |access-date=February 6, 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Burrow]]s, [[animal track|trackways]], [[trail]]s and borings are all examples of traces made by [[organism]]s. < ref name=dict/ >  [[Scientist]]s study traces made by [[plant]]s and [[animal]]s to try to determine their [[wikt:behavior|behavior]]. < ref name=Emery > {{cite web |url=http://www.envs.emory.edu/faculty/MARTIN/ichnology/intro.htm |title=What is Ichnology? |publisher=Emery College of Arts and Sciences |access-date=February 6, 2017 |archive-date=March 18, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170318193805/http://www.envs.emory.edu/faculty/MARTIN/ichnology/intro.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  An '''ichnologist''' is a scientist whose area of [[wikt:study|study]] and [[research]] is ichnology. 
 
 A [[species]] name given to a fossil is called an [[ichnospecies]]. It is written in [[Italic type|italics]] with a [[Wikt:lower case|lower case]] [[wikt:initial|initial]]. It is shown to be an ichnospecies by the [[abbreviation]]  " isp " . < ref > {{cite book |url=http://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/9780199211944.001.0001/acref-9780199211944-e-4130 |title=ichnospecies  |website=Oxford Reference |date=20 March 2008 |publisher=Oxford University Press |isbn=978-0-19-921194-4 |access-date=February 6, 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 == Examples == 
 *Later [[Cambrian]] trace fossils from [[intertidal]] settings include ''[[Protichnites]]'' and ''[[Climactichnites]]'', and others. 
 *[[Mesozoic]] [[dinosaur]] [[footprint]]s including [[Ichnotaxon]] such as ''[[Grallator]]'' and ''[[Atreipus]]'' (bipedal theropods) and ''[[Anomoepus]]''. 
 *[[Triassic]] to [[Recent epoch|Recent]] [[termite]] mounds, which can be several [[Square kilometre|square kilometers]] of [[sediment]] 
 *Possible [[Avian]] footprints such as ''[[Saurexallopus]]''.
