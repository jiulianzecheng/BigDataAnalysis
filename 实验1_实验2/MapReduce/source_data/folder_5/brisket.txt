 '''Brisket''' is a type of [[meat]] cut. It is from the chest of either [[beef]] or [[veal]]. It has a lot of [[connective tissue]]. 
 
 Brisket has been well known to be a part of [[Texas|Texan]] cuisine. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2018-05-10|title=Brisket History|url=http://www.hopscotchbrickovenmi.com/brisket-history|access-date=2021-05-25|website=www.hopscotchbrickovenmi.com}} < /ref >  It could have due to the pricing of the brisket compared to other cuts. < ref > {{Cite web|title=How Passover Brisket Became Texas Barbecue|url=https://www.foodandwine.com/meat-poultry/beef/brief-history-brisket|access-date=2021-05-25|website=Food  &  Wine|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Etymology== 
 According to the second version of ''[[Random House Dictionary of the English Language]]'', the term comes from the [[Middle English]] word ''brusket'' which comes from the earlier [[Old Norse language|Old Norse]] ''[[wikt:brjósk|brjósk]]'', meaning [[cartilage]]. The cut includes the sternum, ribs, and connecting costal tissues. 
 
 == Preparation == 
 [[File:Friend's BBQ tasting party with the star of the show- Franklin BBQ Brisket. Tastes best without sauce or with Rudy's BBQ sauce IMHO. (16500950090).jpg|thumb|Grilled brisket]] 
 Like most beef cuts, brisket can be cooked with different methods. It can be seasoned with a spice mix or [[marinade]]. It is usually [[Grill (cooking)|grilled]] or [[Smoking (cooking)|smoked]], and then [[Basting (cooking)|basted]] when it is almost cooked.
