 '''Deneb''' (also named Arided, α Cygni, alpha Cygni, α Cyg) is the brightest [[star]] in the [[constellation]] of [[Cygnus]].  It, along with [[Vega]], and [[Altair]] make up the Summer Triangle asterism. 
 
 It has an [[apparent magnitude]] of 1.25, which means that it is the [[List of brightest stars|20th brightest star]] in the night sky. However its distance from the sun and [[luminosity]] are difficult to calculate, but it's distance is estimated to be approximately 2,600 [[Light year|light years]] away. It is thought to be between 55,000 and 196,000 times as luminous as the [[Sun]]. < ref name= " IAU-CSN " > {{cite web|url=http://www.pas.rochester.edu/~emamajek/WGSN/IAU-CSN.txt|title=IAU Catalog of Star Names|access-date=28 July 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 Deneb is a blue supergiant that is 203 times larger than the Sun. < ref > {{cite journal|url=https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/abs/2008/09/aa8590-07/aa8590-07.html|title=Quantitative spectroscopy of Deneb|first1=N.|last1=Przybilla|first2=F.|last2=Schiller|date=1 March 2008|journal=Astronomy  &  Astrophysics|volume=479|issue=3|pages=849–858|via=www.aanda.org|doi=10.1051/0004-6361:20078590|arxiv=0712.0040|bibcode=2008A & A...479..849S|s2cid=119225384}} < /ref > 
 
 Deneb will be the [[Pole Star]] in the year 9800 AD. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://stars.astro.illinois.edu/sow/deneb.html|title=Deneb|website=stars.astro.illinois.edu}} < /ref >
