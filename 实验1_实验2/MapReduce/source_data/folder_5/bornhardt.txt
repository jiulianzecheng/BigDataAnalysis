 A '''bornhardt''' ({{IPA-en|ˈbɔrnˈhɑrt|pron}}) is a big [[dome]]-shaped, steep-sided, [[wikt:bald|bald]] [[rock formation]]. < ref name= " Allaby2004 " / > < ref name= " Mayhew2004 "  / >  They are a kind of [[inselberg]]. They are usually made up of [[granite]]s or [[gneiss]]es, but can sometimes be found in other rock types like [[limestone]], [[sandstone]] and [[Conglomerate (geology)|conglomerate]]. They are named after [[Friedrich Wilhelm Conrad Eduard Bornhardt|Wilhelm Bornhardt]] (1864–1946), the German [[Geology|geologist]] who first described the feature. < ref name= " yonsei-glossery "  / > < ref name= " Twidale2005 "  / >
