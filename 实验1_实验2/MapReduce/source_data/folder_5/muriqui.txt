 The '''muriquis''', or '''woolly spider monkeys''', are the [[genus]] '''''Brachyteles'''''. < ref name= " msw3 " > Groves C.P. 2005. < /ref >  There are two species, the southern (''B. arachnoides'') and northern muriqui (''B. hypoxanthus'') muriquis. The name  " muriqui "  comes from a native Tupi word meaning 'largest monkey'. 
 
 They are the two largest [[New World monkey]]s. The northern muriqui is one of the most endangered monkeys in the world. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Chaves|first=Paulo B. |display-authors = etal |title=Genetic diversity and population history of a critically endangered primate, the northern muriqui (''Brachyteles hypoxanthus'')|journal=PLOS ONE|date= 2011 |volume=6 |issue=6 |pages=e20722 |doi=10.1371/journal.pone.0020722|pmid=21694757 |pmc=3108597 |bibcode=2011PLoSO...620722C |doi-access=free }} < /ref >   They are seen only in some forests in [[Brazil]]. 
 
 The adult muriqui is 15-23 & nbsp;inches (38–58 & nbsp;cm) long and weighs from 10-20 pounds (4.5-9 kilos) They are colored from brown to black and the bottom of their tails have no fur near the end. 
 
 Muriquis live in groups from four to 43. They do not fight about their territory. They mostly eat [[leaves]]. They sometimes eat [[fruit]] and [[flowers]] in the rainy season, as well as [[bark]], [[bamboo]], [[ferns]], [[nectar]], [[pollen]], and [[seeds]].
