 A '''fumarole''' ([[Latin]] ''fumus'', smoke) is an opening in the crust of the [[Earth]], often in the neighborhood of [[dormant volcano|dormant]] [[volcano]]es, where [[steam]] and [[gas]]es come out, for instance [[carbon dioxide]], [[sulfur dioxide]], [[hydrochloric acid]], and [[hydrogen sulfide]]. 
 
 ==Etymology== 
 The name [[solfatara]] (from the [[Italian language|Italian]] ''solfo'', sulfur), is given to fumaroles with [[sulfur]]ous gases.
