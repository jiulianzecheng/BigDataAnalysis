 '''Perch''' is the name of a [[Freshwater fish|freshwater perciform fish]] belonging to the [[genus]] '''''Perca'''''.  
 
 Perch have  " rough "  [[Scale (zoology)|scales]].  On the front end of the fish, there are two [[dorsal fin]]s. The first one is spiny and the second is soft. There is also an [[anal fin]], which is also spiny, and a [[caudal fin]]. 
 
 == Species == 
 There are three living [[species]] in this genus: 
 
 * ''[[European perch|Perca fluviatilis]]''  < small > [[Carl Linnaeus|Linnaeus]], 1758 < /small >  (European perch) 
 * ''[[Yellow perch|Perca flavescens]]''  < small > [[Samuel L. Mitchill|Mitchill]], 1814 < /small >  (Yellow perch) 
 * [[Balkhash perch|''Perca schrenkii'']]  < small > [[Karl Fedorovich Kessler|Kessler]], 1874 < /small >  (Balkhash perch) 
 
 == Habitats == 
 Perch are [[Carnivore|carnivores]], They choose waters where [[Fish|smaller fish]], [[shellfish]], and insect [[Larva|larvae]] are plentiful.  
 
 They are found in parts of the [[United States]] in freshwater [[Pond|ponds]], [[Lake|lakes]], [[Stream|streams]], or [[River|rivers]]. These fish can be found in [[fresh water]] all over the world. They live in the [[Great Lakes]] region, in particular [[Lake Erie]].  
 
 The fish like [[Body of water|bodies of water]] with [[vegetation]] and [[debris]]. In the [[spring]] when the perch [[Spawn (biology)|spawns]], they use [[vegetation]] to hide their [[Egg (biology)|eggs]] from [[Predation|predators]].
