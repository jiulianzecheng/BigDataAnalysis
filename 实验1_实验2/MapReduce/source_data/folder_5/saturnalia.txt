'''Saturnalia''' was an [[ancient Rome|ancient Roman]] [[festival]]. It honoured of the [[god]] [[Saturn (mythology)|Saturn]]. It was held on 17 December of the [[Julian calendar]]. Later it lasted longer, through to 23 December. The holiday was celebrated with a sacrifice at the [[Temple of Saturn]], in the [[Roman Forum]], and a public banquet. There was private gift-giving, continual partying, and a [[carnival]] atmosphere. [[Gambling]] was permitted, and masters served their [[slaves]]. < ref > Miller, John F.  " Roman Festivals, "  in ''The Oxford Encyclopedia of Ancient Greece and Rome'' (Oxford University Press, 2010), p. 172. < /ref >  A common custom was the election of a  " King of the Saturnalia " . The poet [[Catullus]] called it  " the best of days " . < ref > [[Catullus]] 14.15 ''(optimo dierum)'', as cited by {{harvnb|Mueller|2010|page=221}} < /ref >
