 '''Gers''' ([[Gascon language|Gascon]]: ''Gers'') is a [[departments of France|department]] in the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]] in the southwest of [[France]] named after the [[Gers (river)|Gers]] river. 
 
 ==History== 
 Gers is one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created from parts of the old provinces of [[Guyenne]] and [[Gascony]]. 
 
 The new department had six [[district]]s: Auch, Lectoure, Condom, Nogaro, L'Isle-Jourdain and Mirande. The [[Capital (city)|capital]] (now [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) was [[Auch]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/32his.html |title=Historique du Gers |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=1 April 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 in 1808, part of the Gers department was [[:wikt:transfer|transferred]] to the new [[Tarn-et-Garonne]] department'. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[Image:The Gers France summer.jpg|right|thumb|Summer in Gers. In the background, the Pyrenees.]] 
 Gers is known for its [[hill]]y [[landscape]] and [[bastide]] [[village]]s. The [[Pyrenees]] are visible to the south. 
 
 Gers is part of the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]], and is the 34th French department in area with {{Convert|6256.8|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 The department is surrounded by six departments in two [[Regions of France|regions]]: 
 * [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] region 
 ** [[Lot-et-Garonne]] (north) 
 ** [[Pyrénées-Atlantiques]] (southwest) 
 ** [[Landes]] (west) 
 * [[Occitanie]] region 
 ** [[Tarn-et-Garonne]] (northeast) 
 ** [[Haute-Garonne]] (east) 
 ** [[Hautes-Pyrénées]] (south)

 The main rivers of the department are the [[Garonne river|Garonne]] and the [[Gers (river)|Gers]], a left [[tributary]] of the Garonne. Other rivers are [[Save (Garonne)|Save]], [[Gimone]], [[Arrats]], [[Baïse]] and [[Osse (river)|Osse]]. 
 
 The highest point in the department is ''Mont de Chapelle Saint Roch'' ({{Coord|43|21|20|N|00|30|09|E|name=Mont de Chapelle Saint Roch|display=inline|region:ID_type:mountain}}), in west central [[Pyrénées]] near the border with the [[Hautes-Pyrénées]] department, with an [[altitude]] of {{Convert|377|m|ft|0|abbr=on}}. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=29285 |title=Mont de Chapelle Saint Roch, France|publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=22 September 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Gers is an oceanic climate with template summers, '''Cfb''' (Marine West Coast Climate) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Auch, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=598817 & cityname=Auch%2C+Midi-Pyr%E9n%E9es%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=4 April 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 The annual rainfall in the department varies from over {{Convert|900|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} in the southwest of the department, to less than {{Convert|700|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} in the northeast ([[Auch]], [[Condom, Gers|Condom]] and [[Lectoure]]). 
 
 Summers are very hot and dry, the temperatures often exceed {{Convert|40|°C|°F }}. Winters vary, often with [[frost]], but the climate remains mild and dry.

 ==Administration== 
 The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Gers'' in [[Auch]]. Gers is part of the region of [[Occitanie]].

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Gers are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Gersois'' (women: ''Gersoises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_departement_gers_32.html|title=Gers (32)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=2 April 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Gers has a population, in 2014, of 190,625, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|190625|6256.8|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Auch, with 87,118 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Condom and Mirande, have respectively 65,562 and 37,945 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >

 ==Economy== 
 The economy in Gers revolves primarily around [[agriculture]] and [[:wikt:culinary|culinary]] [[tourism]]. The department is a producer of [[maize]], [[wheat]], [[rapeseed]], [[sunflower]]. 
 
 Some well-known products of the region are: 
 * ''Armagnac'', a [[brandy]] 
 * ''Côtes de Gascogne'', [[wine]] 
 * ''Floc de Gascogne'', an [[apéritif]] (an alcoholic drink served before a meal) 
 * ''[[Foie gras]]'' 
 * Wild [[mushroom]]s
