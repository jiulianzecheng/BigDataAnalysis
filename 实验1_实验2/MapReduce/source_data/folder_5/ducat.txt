 A '''ducat''' is a [[gold coin]] that was used for trade in [[Europe]] until [[World War I]]. The first ducat was made in silver by Roger II of [[Sicily]] in 1140. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.taxfreegold.co.uk/austrianducatsinfo.html |title=Austrian Gold One Ducat Coins |first= |last= |work=taxfreegold.co.uk |year=2012 |accessdate=23 October 2012}} < /ref >  In 1284 the Republic of [[Venice]] started making the gold ducat which came to be widely used. The value of a ducat at the beginning of the 20th century was nine [[shilling]]s and [[Penny sterling|fourpence]] or a little more than four [[United States dollar]] < nowiki/ > s.
