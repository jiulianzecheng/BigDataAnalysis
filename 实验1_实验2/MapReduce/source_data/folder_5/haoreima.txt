 == Etymology == 
 [[File:HAOLEIMA.jpg|thumb|300px|The name  " Haoleima "  written in archaic Meetei Mayek abugida]] 
 In [[Meitei language]] ([[Manipuri language]]), the name  " Haoreima "  is derived from  " Haoleima " .  " Haoleima "  is made up of two component words,  " Hao "  and  " Leima " . < ref name= " Religion and Culture of Manipur - Moirangthem Kirti Singh - Google Books "  / >  In [[Meitei language]] ([[Manipuri language]]),  " Hao "  has multiple meanings. It may mean (1) yes (the response), (2) tasty, savoury or (3) tribe. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%8D%EA%AF%A5%EA%AF%91%EA%AF%A3 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Hao|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006 }} < /ref >  Here,  " Hao "  means a  " tribe " . In [[Meitei language]] ([[Manipuri language]]),  " Leima "  means a queen or a mistress or a lady. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%82%EA%AF%A9%EA%AF%83%EA%AF%A5 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Leima|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006 }} < /ref >   
 The name  " Haoreima "  usually refers to a tribal woman carrying a traditional elongated [[basket]] ('' " sam " '' or '' " sham " ''). < ref name= " Religion and Culture of Manipur - Moirangthem Kirti Singh - Google Books "  / > 
 
 == Namesakes == 
 There are many people with the name  " Haoreima "  in the history of [[Ancient Kangleipak]] ([[Antique Manipur]]). 
 === Haoreima Tamheibee- Khuman Princess and Meitei Queen ===  
 ''Haoreima Tamheibee'' was the daughter of King Atom Nongyai Thingkol Hanba of the [[Khuman dynasty]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GmZuAAAAMAAJ & q=haoreima|title=Genesis of Indian Tribes: An Approach to the History of Meiteis and Thais|last=Tensuba|first=Keerti Chand|date=1993|publisher=Inter-India Publications|isbn=978-81-210-0308-7|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/dli.language.1340/page/n597/mode/2up?q=%E0%A6%B9%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%93%E0%A6%B0%E0%A7%88%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%BE|title=চৈথারোল কুমপাপা|last=Brahmacharimayum Kulachandra Sharma|pages=597|language=mni}} < /ref >  She was married to Meitei King Kainou Irengba (984 AD-1074 AD). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bjkBM3qyZ00C & dq=haoreima & pg=PA85|title=Society, Politics, and Development in North East India: Essays in Memory of Dr. Basudeb Datta Ray|last1=Ray|first1=Asok Kumar|last2=Chakraborty|first2=Satyabrata|date=2008|publisher=Concept Publishing Company|isbn=978-81-8069-572-8|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 "  / >  After the marriage, she came to be known as  " Meitei Leima "  (lit. ''Meitei Queen''). < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aFluAAAAMAAJ & q=haoreima|title=Archaeology in Manipur|last=Devi|first=L. Kunjeswori|date=2003|publisher=Rajesh Publications|isbn=978-81-85891-18-7|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 "  / >
