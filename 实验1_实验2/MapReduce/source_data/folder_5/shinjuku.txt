 {{Nihongo|'''Shinjuku'''|新宿区|Shinjuku-ku|}} is one of the 23 [[special wards of Tokyo]], [[Japan]]. The administration center of the government of [[Tokyo Prefecture]] is in Shinjuku. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.city.shinjuku.tokyo.jp/foreign/english/aramashi/gaiyou/gaiyou.html |title=Shinjuku City |access-date=2012-09-10 |archive-date=2008-02-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080209165233/http://www.city.shinjuku.tokyo.jp/foreign/english/aramashi/gaiyou/gaiyou.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Also in Shinjuku is the biggest railway station in the world, [[Shinjuku Station]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 On 15 March 1947, the ward of Shinjuku was formed by combining the wards of Ushigome, Yodobashi, and Yotsuya. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.soumu.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/01soumu/archives/0714tokyo_ku.htm|script-title=ja:大東京35区物語～15区から23区へ～東京23区の歴史|publisher=[[Tokyo Metropolitan Archives]]|access-date=2020-08-03|archive-date=2020-06-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200617044050/https://www.soumu.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/01soumu/archives/0714tokyo_ku.htm|language=Japanese}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1991, the Tokyo Government moved from the [[Marunouchi]] district of [[Chiyoda, Tokyo|Chiyoda]] to the current building in Shinjuku. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Surrounding Shinjuku are six other wards: [[Chiyoda, Tokyo|Chiyoda]] to the east; [[Bunkyo, Tokyo|Bunkyo]] and [[Toshima, Tokyo (ward)|Toshima]] to the north; [[Nakano, Tokyo|Nakano]] to the west, and [[Shibuya, Tokyo|Shibuya]] and [[Minato, Tokyo|Minato]] to the south.
