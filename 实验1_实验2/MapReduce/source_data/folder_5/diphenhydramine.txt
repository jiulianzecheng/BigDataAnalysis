 '''Diphenhydramine''' ('''DPH''') is an [[antihistamine]] medication. It is mainly used to treat [[allergy|allergies]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It can also be used to treat [[insomnia]], the [[common cold]], [[tremor]] in [[parkinsonism]], and [[nausea]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It is taken [[Oral administration|by mouth]], [[injection into a vein|injected into a vein]], [[injection into a muscle|injected into a muscle]], or [[topical medication|put on the skin]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It works best two hours after taking, and works for up to seven hours. < ref name=AHSP2016 > {{cite web|title=Diphenhydramine Hydrochloride|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/diphenhydramine-hydrochloride.html|website=Drugs.com|publisher=American Society of Health-System Pharmacists|access-date=28 September 2016|date=6 September 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160915002404/https://www.drugs.com/monograph/diphenhydramine-hydrochloride.html|archive-date=15 September 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 Common side effects include [[sleep|sleepiness]], bad [[balance]] and a bad [[stomach]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It should not be used in young children or the [[elderly]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ > < ref name=schroeck2016 > {{cite journal | vauthors = Schroeck JL, Ford J, Conway EL, Kurtzhalts KE, Gee ME, Vollmer KA, Mergenhagen KA | title = Review of Safety and Efficacy of Sleep Medicines in Older Adults | journal = Clinical Therapeutics | volume = 38 | issue = 11 | pages = 2340–2372 | date = November 2016 | pmid = 27751669 | doi = 10.1016/j.clinthera.2016.09.010 | doi-access = free }} < /ref >  It can be used while [[pregnant]], but not when [[breastfeeding]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Diphenhydramine Pregnancy and Breastfeeding Warnings|url=https://www.drugs.com/pregnancy/diphenhydramine.html|website=Drugs.com|access-date=28 September 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161002114958/https://www.drugs.com/pregnancy/diphenhydramine.html|archive-date=2 October 2016}} < /ref >  It works by blocking some effects of [[histamine]] from working at the [[histamine H1 receptor|H1 receptor]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  Diphenhydramine is also an [[anticholinergic]], and is a [[deliriant]] at high doses. < ref > {{cite book|last1=Ayd|first1=Frank J.|title=Lexicon of Psychiatry, Neurology, and the Neurosciences|date=2000|publisher=Lippincott Williams  &  Wilkins|isbn=978-0-7817-2468-5 | page = 332 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=ea_QVG2BFy8C & pg=PA332 }} < /ref > 
 
 Diphenhydramine was first made by [[George Rieveschl]] and was first used in 1946. < ref > {{cite book|last1=Dörwald|first1=Florencio Zaragoza|title=Lead Optimization for Medicinal Chemists: Pharmacokinetic Properties of Functional Groups and Organic Compounds |date = 2013 | publisher = John Wiley  &  Sons | isbn = 978-3-527-64565-7 | page = 225 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=YTeY9ZEfNccC & pg=PA225 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=Benadryl|url=http://ohiohistorycentral.org/w/Benadryl?rec=2591|website=Ohio History Central|access-date=28 September 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161017192649/http://ohiohistorycentral.org/w/Benadryl?rec=2591|archive-date=17 October 2016}} < /ref >  It is available as a [[generic medication]]. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It is also sold under the brand name [[Benadryl]], as well as others. < ref name=AHSP2016/ >  It was the 241st most commoly [[prescription|prescribed]] medication in the United States in 2017. < ref > {{cite web | title = The Top 300 of 2020 | url = https://clincalc.com/DrugStats/Top300Drugs.aspx | website = ClinCalc | access-date = 11 April 2020}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web | title = Diphenhydramine Hydrochloride - Drug Usage Statistics | website = ClinCalc | url = https://clincalc.com/DrugStats/Drugs/DiphenhydramineHydrochloride | access-date = 11 April 2020 | archive-date = 29 April 2017 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170429131405/https://clincalc.com/DrugStats/Drugs/DiphenhydramineHydrochloride | url-status = dead }} < /ref >
