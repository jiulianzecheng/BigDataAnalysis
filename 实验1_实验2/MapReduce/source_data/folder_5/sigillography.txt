 '''Sigillography''' is the study of the [[Seal (emblem)|seals]] used on documents. < ref > {{cite web |title=Ancient Seals—What Were They? |url=https://www.jw.org/en/bible-teachings/history/ancient-seals/ |website=JW.ORG |access-date=11 July 2019 |language=en}} < /ref >  It is one of the [[auxiliary sciences of history]]. It has links to [[diplomatics]], [[heraldry]], [[social history]], and the [[Art history|history of art]]. 
 
 This field of study was started in the 15th century by [[Antiquarian|students of old artifacts]]. Then it became more well known in the 16th and 17th centuries. < ref > {{cite book |first1=P.D.A. |last1=Harvey |first2=Andrew |last2=McGuinness |title=A Guide to British Medieval Seals |location=London |publisher=British Library and Public Record Office |year=1996 |pages=22–6 }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book| first=Elizabeth |last=New |title=Seals and Sealing Practices |publisher=British Records Association |series=Archives and the User |volume=11 |year=2010 |location=London |ISBN=978-0-900222-15-3 |pages=29–32}} < /ref >  At first this field of study was thought of as part of [[diplomatics]]. But over time it became known as its own field of study.
