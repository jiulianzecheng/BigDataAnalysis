 '''Turkmenistan''' ({{IPAc-en|audio=En-us-Turkmenistan.ogg|t|ɜːr|k|ˈ|m|ɛ|n|ᵻ|s|t|æ|n}} or {{IPAc-en|audio=En-us-Turkmenistan-2.ogg|t|ɜːr|k|m|ɛ|n|ᵻ|ˈ|s|t|ɑː|n}}; {{lang-tk|Türkmenistan}}, {{IPA-tk|tʏɾkmønʏˈθːɑːn|pron}}; < ref > {{Cite book|last=Clark |first=Larry |title=Turkmen Reference Grammar |location=Wiesbaden |publisher=Harrassowitz Verlag |year=1998 |page=50 |language=en}} < /ref > ), also known as '''Turkmenia''', is a country in [[South Asia|South]]-[[Central Asia]]. It has borders with [[Afghanistan]], [[Iran]], [[Kazakhstan]], [[Uzbekistan]]. It has no [[ocean]] but it is on the [[Caspian Sea]] to the west.  The [[capital (city)|capital]] of Turkmenistan is [[Aşgabat|Ashghabat]]. < ref name= " Factbook " > {{cite web|url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/txq.html|title=Turkmenistan|publisher=Central Intelligence Agency - The World Factbook|accessdate=2009-09-04}}{{Dead link|date=September 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 The majority of the country is [[Muslim]] at 93%. 
 
 Turkmenistan is a non-aligned and fully neutral country. 
 
 == History == 
 Turkmenistan was part of the [[Soviet Union]] since its creation, in 1922. At first, Turkmenistan was an [[Turkmen Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic|autonomous republic]] within [[Russia|Russian SFSR]]. In 1925 it was promoted to a Soviet republic. Turkmenistan is an independent country since 1991, after the breakup of the [[Soviet Union]]. [[Saparmurat Niyazov]] became Turkmenistan’s first president in 1991.

 ==Climate== 
 Turkmenistan is one of the driest desert areas in the world. Some places have an average annual precipitation of only {{convert|12|mm|in|2|abbr=on}}. The highest temperature recorded in [[Ashgabat]] is {{convert|48.0|°C|lk=on}}. [[Kerki]], an extreme inland city located on the banks of the [[Amu Darya]] river, recorded {{convert|51.7|°C}} in July 1983, but this value is unofficial. 50.1C is the highest temperature recorded at Repetek Reserve, recognized as the highest temperature ever recorded in the whole former Soviet Union. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Turkmenistan is the world's 52nd-largest country. It is slightly smaller than [[Spain]] and somewhat larger than the US state of [[California]]. Over 80% of the country is covered by the [[Karakum Desert]]. 
 
 The Turkmen shore along the [[Caspian Sea]] is {{convert|1768|km|mi}} long. The [[Caspian Sea]] is entirely landlocked, with no access to the ocean. 
 
 The major cities include [[Ashgabat|Aşgabat]], [[Türkmenbaşy, Turkmenistan|Türkmenbaşy]] (formerly Krasnovodsk) and [[Daşoguz]]. 
 
 Turkmenistan has a [[Humidity|humid]] climate. Annual rainfall is low, particularly in summer. Winters are dry and mild. The wettest region in Turkmenistan is Köýtendağ Range. Köýtendağ is in southeastern Turkmenistan on the borders with Uzbekistan.
