 '''Sphingidae''' is a family of moths ([[Lepidoptera]]), commonly known as hawk moths, sphinx moths and hornworms. It includes about 1,450 species. < ref > {{cite journal| author=van Nieukerken| title=Order Lepidoptera Linnaeus, 1758. In: Zhang, Z.-Q. (Ed.) Animal biodiversity: An outline of higher-level classification and survey of taxonomic richness | journal=Zootaxa| volume=3148| year=2011| pages=212–221| url=http://mapress.com/zootaxa/2011/f/zt03148p221.pdf|display-authors=etal| doi=10.11646/zootaxa.3148.1.41 }} < /ref >   It is best represented in the [[tropics]] but there are species in every region. < ref name=scoble > Scoble, Malcolm J. (1995): ''The Lepidoptera: form, function and diversity''. 2nd ed. Oxford University Press  &  Natural History Museum London. {{ISBN|0-19-854952-0}} < /ref > 
 
 They can fly fast, and their wings can be linked by [[wing coupling]]. They can hover, and can move from side to side while hovering. This may be to deal with [[ambush predator]]s that lie in wait in [[flowers]]. < ref name=kitching > {{cite journal | last1 = Kitching | first1 = Ian J | year = 2002 | title = The phylogenetic relationships of Morgan's Sphinx, ''Xanthopan morganii'' (Walker), the tribe Acherontiini, and allied long-tongued hawkmoths (Lepidoptera: Sphingidae, Sphinginae) | url = | journal = Zoological Journal of the Linnean Society | volume = 135 | issue = 4| pages = 471–527 | doi = 10.1046/j.1096-3642.2002.00021.x }} < /ref > 
 
 == Species == 
 There are around 1200 species of hawk moth, classified into around 200 genera.  Some of the best known species are: 
 * [[Sphinx ligustri|Privet hawkmoth]] (''Sphinx ligustri'') 
 * [[Death's-head hawkmoth]] (''[[Acherontia]] atropos'') 
 * [[Mimas tiliae|Lime hawk-moth]] (''Mimas tiliae'') 
 * [[Laothoe populi|Poplar hawk-moth]] (''Laothoe populi'') 
 * [[Ceratomia catalpae|Catalpa sphinx]] (''Ceratomia catalpae'') 
 * [[Macroglossum stellatarum|Hummingbird hawk-moth]] (''Macroglossum stellatarum'') 
 * [[Deilephila elpenor|Elephant hawk moth]] (''Deilephila elpenor'') 
 * [[Hippotion celerio|Vine hawk moth]] (''Hippotion celerio'') 
 * [[Hyles euphorbiae|Spurge hawk moth]] (''Hyles euphorbiae'') 
 * [[Daphnis nerii|Oleander hawk moth]] (''Daphnis nerii'') 
 * [[Manduca quinquemaculata|Tomato worm]] (''Manduca quinquemaculata'')
