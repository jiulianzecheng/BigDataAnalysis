 '''Rouen''' is a [[Communes of France|commune]]. It is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of [[Upper Normandy]] and the [[Seine-Maritime]] [[Departments of France|department]]. 
 
 [[File:Rouen_RueGrosHorloge.jpg|thumb|left|Rue du Gros-Horloge]] 
 
 First settlements in that region were from the 6th century before Christ. 
 The [[Roman Empire|Romans]] founded Rotomagus about 100 A.D.. 
 Since 384 Rouen is the seat of a [[bishop]]. 
 In 841 the [[Vikings]] conquered Rouen. It became the capitol of the [[Duke|Duchy]] of [[Normandy]]. 
 In 1204 the Frensh king reconquered Rouen.  
 In 1419 the [[England|English]] crown ([[Henry IV. of England]]) conquered the city in the [[100-year War]] between France and England. On May, 30th of 1431 [[Jeanne D'Arc]] (the Virgin of Orleans) was burned in Rouen. 
 In 1494 the French got the city back. 
 In 1834 the railway connection Paris-Rouen was opened. 
 Rouen was occupied by the Germans in the Franco-German War of 1870 to 1873 and in both World Wars. In the World War II many buildings were destroyed. 
 
 Rouen has an [[oceanic climate]] (''Cfb'' in the [[Koeppen climate classification]]). It has a great marine harbor 60 & nbsp;km from the city. Tourism, transportation, the colleges and the university are most important economic factors. 
 
 The university of Rouen has about 40,000 students.

 * The ''[[Musée maritime fluvial et portuaire de Rouen|Musée maritime fluvial et portuaire]]'' is a museum about the history of the port of the city and the navigation
