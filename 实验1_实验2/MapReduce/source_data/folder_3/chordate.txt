 '''Chordates''', phylum '''Chordata''', are a [[phylum]] of [[animal]]s which have a [[notochord]]. The group includes [[vertebrate]]s, with some closely related [[invertebrate]]s.

 === Origin of chordates === 
 According to a long-standing theory, the origin of chordates may be found in transformed larvae of sea-squirts ([[tunicate]]s). Adult tunicates are [[Sessility (zoology)|sessile]], but their larvae are [[motile]], and have some features found in early vertebrates.  The process of [[paedomorphosis]], where [[juvenile]] features are retained in the adult, is the proposed mechanism. < ref > Garstang, Walter 1894. Preliminary notes on a new theory of the phylogeny of the chordates. ''Zoologischer Anzeiger'' '''17''', p122. < /ref > < ref > Garstang, Walter 1928. The morphology of the tunicata, and its bearing on the phylogeny of the Chordata. ''Quarterly Journal of Microscopical Science'' '''72''', p51. < /ref > < ref > de Beer, Gavin 1951. ''Embryos and ancestors''. 3rd ed, Oxfor, The evolution of chordates, p76. < /ref >  [[Genome]] analysis shows that tunicates are the closest living relatives of vertebrates. < ref > Delsuc, Frédéric ''et al.'' 2006.
