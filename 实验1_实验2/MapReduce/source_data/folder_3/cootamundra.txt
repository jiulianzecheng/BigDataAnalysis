 '''Cootamundra''' is a town in [[New South Wales]], [[Australia]]. In 2006 there were 5,566 people living in Cootamundra. < ref name= " ABS " > {{Census 2006 AUS 
  | id = UCL122000 
  | name = Cootamundra (Urban Centre/Locality) 
  | quick = on 
  | accessdate = 2008-03-22 
 }} < /ref >   It is on the [[Olympic Highway]] where it crosses the Muttama Creek, between [[Junee, New South Wales|Junee]] and [[Cowra, New South Wales|Cowra]].  Cootamundra's [[railway station]] is on the main [[Melbourne]]-to-[[Sydney]] [[railway]] line. 
 
 [[Image:SirDonaldBradmansBirthplaceCootamundra.jpg|thumb|250px|left|Birthplace of Sir Donald Bradman]] 
 [[Don Bradman|Sir Donald Bradman]] [[Order of Australia|AC]], [[Australia]]n [[cricket]]er said to be the greatest [[batsman]] of all time was born in Cootamundra.  The town is very proud of Bradman, and uses this in signs and tourist information. The home where ''The Don'' was born is a fully restored visitor site open to the public seven days per week. 
 
 Cootamundra is the home of the [[acacia baileyana|Cootamundra Wattle]] (Acacia Baileyana).  Every year there is a large 'Wattle Time' Festival (which happens when the wattle starts to bloom) with an art show and festivities.
