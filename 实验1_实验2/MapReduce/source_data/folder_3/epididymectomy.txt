An '''epididymectomy''' is a male sterilization surgery that removes the [[epididymis]], or the tube in the [[scrotum]] where the [[sperm]] become able to move. < ref name =  " howto " > {{cite book|url=https://open.lib.umn.edu/largeanimalsurgery/chapter/how-to-epididectomy/|title=Large Animal Surgery - Supplemental Notes|first1=Erin|last1=Malone|last2=DVM|last3=PhD|first4=Elaine|last4=Norton|first5=D. V. M.|last5=PhD|first6=Erica|last6=Dobbs|last7=DVM|first8=Ashley|last8=Ezzo|last9=DVM|publisher=University of Minnesota Libraries Publishing|via=open.lib.umn.edu}} < /ref >  An epididymectomy can be done on either one side or both sides of the scrotum. It does not always make the male animal sterile forever, because the tubing can grow back later. < ref name =  " howto " / >  It is performed on [[animals]] such as [[bulls]]. < ref name =  " howto " / >  It is sometimes done on human men if the men have pain after [[vasectomy]], a tumor, or a [[cyst]] that is difficult to treat. < ref name=StL > {{cite web|url=https://www.saintlukeskc.org/health-library/understanding-epididymectomy#|access-date=May 2, 2021|publisher=St. Luke's Hospital of Kansas City|title=Understanding Epididymectomy}} < /ref >
