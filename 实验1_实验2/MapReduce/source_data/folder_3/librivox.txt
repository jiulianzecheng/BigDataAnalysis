 '''LibriVox''' is a group of worldwide volunteers who read and record [[public domain]] texts creating free public domain [[audiobook]]s for download from their website and other [[digital library]] hosting sites on the internet. It was founded in 2005 by Hugh McGuire to provide  " Acoustical liberation of books in the public domain " < ref name= " Authorlibrivox " > " [https://librivox.org/search?primary_key=0 & search_category=author & search_page=1 & search_form=get_results/ LibriVox Author] " , LibriVox website. Retrieved 16 April 2015. < /ref >  and the LibriVox objective is  " To make all books in the public domain available, for free, in audio format on the internet " . < ref name= " aboutlibrivox " > " [http://librivox.org/pages/about-librivox/ Objective LibriVox] " , LibriVox website. Retrieved 24 August 2011. < /ref > 
 
 On 6 August 2016, the project completed project number 10,000. < ref > " [https://librivox.org/2016/08/06/another-librivox-milestone-10000-projects/ Another LibriVox Milestone: 10,000 projects!] " , librivox.org, 6 August 2016. Retrieved 3 September 2016. < /ref > < ref name= " Offeringslibrivox " > " [https://archive.org/details/librivoxaudio Offerings LibriVox] " , Archive website. Retrieved 17 April 2015. < /ref >  and from 2009â€“2017 was producing about 1,000 items per year. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://forum.librivox.org/viewtopic.php?p=1425450#p1425450 |title=LibriVox stats |publisher=LibriVox |author=MaryAnnSpiegel |date=January 1, 2018 |access-date=January 22, 2018}} < /ref >  Most releases are in the English language, but many non-English works are also available. There are multiple affiliated projects that are providing additional content. LibriVox is closely affiliated with [[Project Gutenberg]] from where the project gets some of its texts, and the [[Internet Archive]] that hosts their offerings.
