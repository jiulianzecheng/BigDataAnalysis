 '''Allstedt''' is a town in [[Saxony-Anhalt]], [[Germany]]. It is in the [[Mansfeld-Südharz]] district. It is southeast of [[Sangerhausen]]. There are 13 parts of the town. The population was about 7,600 in 2020. The main political party is [[Christian Democratic Union of Germany|CDU]].  
 
 == History == 
 The history of the town first started in the 6th century. The town housed soldiers during the [[Thirty Years' War]]. [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe]] lived in the town from 1776 to 1802. He stayed in the castle and wrote parts of [[Iphigenie]]. The town was bombed during [[World War II]]. [[Soviet Union|Russians]] took over after the war. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Stadtgeschichte Allstedt|url=http://www.allstedt.com/content2.php?linkbez= & linkid2=13|access-date=2022-07-21|website=www.allstedt.com}} < /ref >   
 
 == Tourist attractions == 
 Historic tourist attractions include the Allstedt castle, the church Allstedter Dom, the church St. Johanneskirche and the town hall (''Rathaus'').
