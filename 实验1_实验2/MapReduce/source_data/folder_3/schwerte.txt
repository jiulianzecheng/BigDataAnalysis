 '''Schwerte''' is a [[city]] in [[North Rhine-Westphalia]], [[Germany]]. About 48,000 people live there.{{when}} Schwerte is in the Ruhr valley just [[north]] of the [[mountain]]ous [[Sauerland]] [[region]]. The [[Ruhr (river)|Ruhr river]] runs through the center of Schwerte. < ref name=RT > {{cite web |url=http://www.ruhr-tourismus.de/en/staedte-im-ruhrgebiet/schwerte.html |title=Schwerte |author= |date= |website= |publisher=.Ruhr Tourismus |accessdate=15 March 2015 |archive-date=7 September 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140907150242/http://www.ruhr-tourismus.de/en/staedte-im-ruhrgebiet/schwerte.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Schwerte is a popular [[cycling]] [[wikt:destination|destination]] for [[tourist]]s. < ref name=RT/ > 
 
 ==History== 
 Schwerte received [[Civil and political rights|civic rights]] in the [[12th century]]. It was a member of the [[medieval]] [[Hanseatic League]]. During [[World War II]] Schwerte housed a branch of the [[Buchenwald concentration camp]]. The camp held 710 [[prisoner]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://aussenlager.buchenwald.de/index.php?article_id=125 |title=Schwerte |author= |date= |website= |publisher= Förderverein Buchenwald |accessdate=15 March 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 == Controversy == 
 In 2015 Schwerte [[official]]s have proposed housing [[refugee]]s at the site of the former [[concentration camp]]. < ref name=NPR > {{cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/blogs/parallels/2015/01/16/377756096/a-german-plan-house-refugees-in-an-old-concentration-camp |title=A German Plan: House Refugees In An Old Concentration Camp |author=Soraya Nelson |date= |website= |publisher=NPR |accessdate=15 March 2015}} < /ref >  The plan has been criticized as a bad idea by many [[Germany|Germans]] including the [[premier]] of North Rhine-Westphalia. < ref name=NPR/ >  Officials say the [[building]]s were built after the war and should not be a problem. Refugees coming to Germany include those from [[Syria]], [[Afghanistan]], [[Eritrea]], [[Kosovo]] and [[Serbia]]. < ref name=NPR/ >
