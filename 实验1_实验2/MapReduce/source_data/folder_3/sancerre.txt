 '''Sancerre''' is a medieval hilltop town  and [[Communes of France|commune]] in the [[Cher (department)|Cher]] [[Departments of France|department]], [[France]] overlooking the river [[Loire River|Loire]]. It is noted for its wine. 
 
 It is a natural fortress 312 meters in height. The [[Count of Champagne|Counts of Champagne]] built fortifications there in the 12th century. During the [[French Wars of Religion]] the [[Huguenots|Huguenot]] people were [[Siege|besieged]] there for 8 months in 1572/3. About 500 people died, mostly of [[starvation]].  The siege of Sancerre was written about by a [[Protestantism|Protestant]] minister Jean de Léry, who survived the battle, in ''The Memorable History of the Siege of Sancerre'' (1574).    A [[mansion]] was built on the ruins of the original Chateau de Sancerre in 1874.
