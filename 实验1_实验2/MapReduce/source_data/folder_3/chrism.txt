The '''chrism''' is a mixture of pure [[olive oil]] and balm ([[balsam]]). The two elements signify  " fullness of grace " .  The chrism is blessed by a [[bishop]] on [[Maundy Thursday]]. It is used to [[consecrate]] [[church]]es, [[chalice]]s, the solemn blessing of [[bell]]s, [[baptismal font]]s, etc. It is also used in [[baptism]]s, [[confirmation]]s, and other churchly functions.
