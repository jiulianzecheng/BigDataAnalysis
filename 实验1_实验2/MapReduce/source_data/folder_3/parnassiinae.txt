 '''Parnassiinae''' is a [[subfamily]] of [[butterfly|butterflies]].  It [[wikt:belong|belongs]] in the [[Family (biology)|family]] [[Papilionidae]].  They are commonly known as '''Apollos''' and '''Parnassians'''.  The butterflies in this subfamily are only found in the [[Holarctic Region]] (the Holarctic Region is the northern part of [[North America]] and [[Eurasia]]). < ref name=bda > Bernard D'Abrera (1990).  ''Butterflies of the Holarctic Region Part 1: Papilionidae, Pieridae, Danaidae, and Satyridae (Partim).''  Hill House, Victoria, Australia.  {{ISBN|0-646-01202-9}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[caterpillar]]s and [[adult]]s are [[poison]]ous to [[vertebrate]]s.  The caterpillars are thought to be [[Mimicry#M.C3.BCllerian mimicry|Müllerian mimics]] with [[millipede]]s.  The adult butterflies are thought to be Müllerian mimics with adult [[Pieridae|Whites]]. < ref name=jas > James A. Scott (1986).  ''The Butterflies of North America.''  Stanford University Press, Stanford, CA.  {{ISBN|0-8047-2013-4}} < /ref > 
 
 After [[Sexual reproduction|mating]], the male [[glue]]s a [[wikt:structure|structure]] to the end of the female's [[abdomen]].  This keeps the female from mating again.  This structure is called a sphragis. < ref name=jas/ >
