 '''Peltobatrachus''' (from Greek pelte, meaning shield and batrakhos, meaning frog) is an extinct genus of [[temnospondyl]] [[amphibian]]s from the late [[Permian]] period of [[Tanzania]]. They were large and slow-moving animals. They reached 70 centimeters (2.3 ft) in length. They were [[terrestrial animals|terrestrial amphibians]]. They only went back to the water to lay their eggs.
