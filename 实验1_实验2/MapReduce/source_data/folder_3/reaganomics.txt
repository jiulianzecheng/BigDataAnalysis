 '''Reaganomics''' is a [[portmanteau word]] of ''Reagan'' and ''economics'' created by [[Paul Harvey]]. < ref > {{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/02/28/AR2009022802096_2.html |newspaper=The Washington Post |title=Broadcaster Delivered 'The Rest of the Story' |accessdate=March 1, 2009 |date=March 1, 2009 | first=Joe | last=Holley}} < /ref >  It refers to the [[economics|economic]] policies created by [[United States President|U.S. President]] [[Ronald Reagan]] during the 1980s and still widely practiced. 
 
 These policies are commonly used with [[supply-side economics]], referred to as [[trickle-down economics]] by political enemies and [[free market economy|free market economics]] by political advocates. 
 
 The four main ideas of Reaganomics were to reduce the growth of [[United States federal budget|government spending]], reduce the [[federal income tax]] and [[capital gains tax]], reduce government [[regulation]], and tighten the money supply in order to reduce [[inflation]]. < ref name= " nisk_concise " > {{Cite web|title=Reaganomics, by William A. Niskanen: The Concise Encyclopedia of Economics {{!}} Library of Economics and Liberty|url=https://www.econlib.org/library/Enc1/Reaganomics.html|access-date=2022-04-08|website=www.econlib.org}} < /ref >
