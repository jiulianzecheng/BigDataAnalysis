 '''DoorDash Inc.''' is an [[United States|American]] on-demand prepared [[food]] [[delivery]] service. < ref > {{Cite web|title = DoorDash About Page|url = https://www.doordash.com/about//|website = DoorDash|access-date = June 20, 2015|archive-date = September 22, 2013|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130922100954/https://www.doordash.com/about//|url-status = dead}} < /ref >  founded by [[Stanford]] students Tony Xu, Stanley Tang, Andy Fang and Evan Moore. < ref > {{cite web |last1=Ekiel |first1=Erika |title=DoorDash CEO: Solving Problems of Time-Starved People |url=https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/insights/doordash-ceo-solving-problems-time-starved-people |website=GSB.Stanford.edu (Stanford Graduate School of Business) |publisher=Stanford University |access-date=11 November 2020 |date=November 30, 2015}} < /ref >  It is one of several [[technology company|technology companies]]. It uses [[logistics]] services to offer food deliveries from [[restaurant]]s on-demand. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/sanfrancisco/filler-app-how-tech-companies-are-disrupting-restaurant-delivery/Content?oid=2948629|title=Filler App: How Tech Companies Are Disrupting Restaurant Delivery|author=Anna Roth|date=June 26, 2013|publisher=[[SF Weekly]]|access-date=February 26, 2014|archive-date=September 6, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140906013321/http://www.sfweekly.com/sanfrancisco/filler-app-how-tech-companies-are-disrupting-restaurant-delivery/Content?oid=2948629|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  DoorDash launched in [[Palo Alto, California|Palo Alto]] and, as of May 2019, had expanded to more than 4,000 cities and offers a selection of 340,000 stores across the [[United States]], [[Canada]] and [[Australia]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://blog.doordash.com/fueling-the-last-mile-3ae0a6b45ea0|title=Fueling the Last Mile|last=DoorDash|date=2019-05-24|website=Medium|language=en|access-date=2019-10-25|archive-date=2019-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190523194009/https://blog.doordash.com/fueling-the-last-mile-3ae0a6b45ea0|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 It is expected to launch an [[IPO]] in 2020, < ref > {{Cite news|title=DoorDash Is Planning a Traditional IPO in Fourth Quarter|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-08-22/doordash-is-said-to-plan-fourth-quarter-ipo-after-pandemic-delay|access-date=2020-09-14|website=bloomberg.com|date=22 August 2020 }} < /ref >  the company is currently worth close to $16 billion < ref name= " social.techcrunch.com " > {{Cite web|title=DoorDash confirms $400M raise, IPO timing unclear|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2020/06/18/after-ipo-delays-doordash-confirms-400m-raise/|access-date=2020-09-14|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|archive-date=2020-12-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201205204238/https://social.techcrunch.com/2020/06/18/after-ipo-delays-doordash-confirms-400m-raise/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and is the largest [[Third-party logistics|third-party delivery service]] in the U.S., surpassing [[Grubhub]] in 2019. < ref > {{Cite web|title=DoorDash overtakes Grubhub in delivery market share|url=https://www.restaurantdive.com/news/doordash-overtakes-grubhub-in-delivery-market-share/550338/|website=Restaurant Dive|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-29}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.fool.com/investing/2019/11/16/why-chuys-is-fighting-to-control-food-delivery-dat.aspx|title=Why Chuy's Is Fighting to Control Food Delivery Data|last=Quast|first=Jon|date=2019-11-16|website=The Motley Fool|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}} < /ref >
