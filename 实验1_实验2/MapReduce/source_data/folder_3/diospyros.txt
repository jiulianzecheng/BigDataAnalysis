 '''''Diospyros''''' is a [[genus]] of about 450–500 [[species]] of [[deciduous]] and [[evergreen]] [[tree]]s. Most are [[native]] to the [[tropics]], with only a few species extending into [[temperate]] [[region]]s. They are commonly known as [[Ebony (tree)|ebony]] or [[persimmon]] trees. The generic name is derived from the [[Greek language|Greek]] words διός (''dios''), meaning  " of [[Zeus]] "  and πυρός (''pyros''), meaning  " [[grain]] "  and was originally applied to the [[Caucasian Persimmon]] (''D. lotus'').
