 '''Formazza''' ([[Walliser German]]: ''Pomatt'', [[Piedmontese]]: ''Formassa'') is a ''[[comune]]'' (municipality) in the [[Province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola]] in the [[Italian]] region [[Piedmont]]. 
 
 Formazza is about {{convert|160|km|mi}} northeast of [[Turin]] and {{convert|50|km|mi}} north of [[Verbania]]. It is on the border with [[Switzerland]]. 
 
 As of 2004 about 443 people lived there. It has an [[area]] of {{convert|131.8|km2|mi2}}. < ref name= " istat " > All demographics and other statistics: Italian statistical institute [[National Institute of Statistics (Italy)|Istat]]. < /ref > 
 
 The municipality of Formazza contains the ''[[frazione|frazioni]]'' (subdivisions, mainly villages and hamlets) Foppiano, Antillone, Fondovalle, Chiesa, Valdo, San Michele, Ponte, Brendo, Grovella, Canza, Sotto Frua, La Frua, and Riale. 
 
 Formazza borders the following municipalities: [[Baceno]], [[Bedretto]] (Switzerland), [[Bignasco]] (Switzerland), [[Binn]] (Switzerland), [[Bosco/Gurin]] (Switzerland), [[Cavergno]] (Switzerland), [[Premia]], [[Reckingen-Gluringen]] (Switzerland), [[Ulrichen]] (Switzerland). 
 
 The village was founded by [[Walser]] and [[Walser German]] is still spoken.
