 '''Tagbilaran''' is the [[Capital (city)|capital]] and main city of the [[Bohol (province)|Bohol]] [[province]], in the [[Bohol (island)|Bohol]] island, [[Philippines]]. Because of the Tagbilaran Airport, the city is an important way for [[tourist]]s to start trips through the rest of the island and neighboring islands. 
 
 The inhabitants of this city are known as ''tagbilaranons''. 
 
 ==History== 
 
 In 1565, a [[Spanish people|Spanish]] [[explorer]] named [[Miguel López de Legazpi]] came to Bohol looking for [[spice]]s and [[gold]]. He made a peace pact with [[Datu Sikatuna]] in a place called '''Bool''', now a district of Tagbilaran. This [[:wikt:pact|pact]] was [[Signature|signed]] with a blood pact between the two men. < ref name= " bgov-i " > {{cite web |url=http://www.bohol.gov.ph/index.php??=boholHistory |title=History of Bohol |publisher=Province of Bohol, Official Website |access-date=16 October 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 On 9 February 1742, the [[Governor]] of the [[Visayas]] Francisco Antonio Calderon de la Barca separated Tagbilaran from [[Baclayon]] and made it a town with the name of ''San Jose de Tagbilaran'' and dedicated to [[Saint Joseph|St. Joseph the Worker]]. Since then, it was part of the province of Bohol and became [[:wikt:officially|officially]] a component city (a [[city]] that is part of a province) on 1 July 1966 by the Republic Act No. 4660. < ref name= " his " > {{cite web |url=http://www.tagbilaran.gov.ph/profile/history/ |title=History |publisher=City Government of Tagbilaran |access-date=16 October 2014 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 [[File:Ph locator bohol tagbilaran.png|thumb|left|250px|Map of Bohol showing the location of Tagbilaran]] 
 Tagbilaran is in the southwestern part of the island of [[Bohol (island)|Bohol]], in front of the smaller island of [[Panglao Island|Panglao]]; between the two islands is the Tagbilaran [[Strait]]. 
 
 The city is {{Convert|630|km|mi|abbr=on}} southeast of the national capital of [[Manila]] and {{Convert|72|km|mi|abbr=on}} south of the regional capital, [[Cebu City]]. 
 
 It has a total area of {{Convert|36.50|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}}, < ref name=nscb > {{cite web |title=Province: BOHOL |url=http://www.nscb.gov.ph/activestats/psgc/province.asp?regName=REGION+VII+%28Central+Visayas%29 & regCode=07 & provCode=071200000 & provName=BOHOL |work=PSGC Interactive |publisher=National Statistical Coordination Board |access-date=16 October 2014 |archive-date=20 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131020145110/http://www.nscb.gov.ph/activestats/psgc/province.asp?regName=REGION+VII+%28Central+Visayas%29 & regCode=07 & provCode=071200000 & provName=BOHOL |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  but the total land area, without the parts that are in the sea, is {{Convert|32.70|km2|sqmi|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " city "  / >  The [[coast]]line is about {{Convert|13|km|mi|abbr=on}} long. 
 
 There is a [[:wikt:ridge|ridge]] (a long and narrow chain of small hills) with an average [[altitude]] of {{Convert|30|m|ft|abbr=on}} that runs [[parallel]] to the coastline; at both ends of the ridge there are two [[hill]]s: ''Elley Hill'' ({{Convert|100|m|ft|abbr=on |disp=x| [|]}}) in the north and ''Banat-I Hill'' ({{Convert|145|m|ft|abbr=on |disp=x| [|]}}) in the south. < ref name= " city2 " > {{cite web |url=http://www.tagbilaran.gov.ph/profile/geography/ |title=Geography |publisher=City Government of Tagbilaran |access-date=16 October 2014 |archive-date=16 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141016040144/http://www.tagbilaran.gov.ph/profile/geography/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 Tagbilaran borders with the [[Municipality|municipalities]] of [[Cortes, Bohol|Cortes]] (north), [[Corella, Bohol|Corella]] (northeast) and [[Baclayon, Bohol|Baclayon]]  (east). To the south is the ''Tagbilaran Strait'' and the [[Panglao Island|Panglao]] island, and to the west is the ''Maribojoc Bay'' (part of the [[Cebu Strait]]).

 ===Climate=== 
 Tagbilaran has, like the rest of the Philippines, a [[tropical climate]]. The [[average]] [[temperature]] is high and the [[humidity]] is also high. The dry and the wet seasons are very similar.
