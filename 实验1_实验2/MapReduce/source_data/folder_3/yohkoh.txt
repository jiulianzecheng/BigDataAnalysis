 {{nihongo|'''Yohkoh'''|ようこう||meaning  " ''sunbeam'' " }}, is the name of a [[Japan]]ese artificial [[satellite]] which was put into [[orbit]].  
 
 The launch was a project of the [[Institute of Space and Astronautical Science]] of the [[University of Tokyo]]. 
 
 The satellite was sent into orbit on August 30, 1991. < ref > JAXA, [http://www.isas.ac.jp/e/enterp/missions/catalogue.shtml   " Catalogue of ISAS Missions " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141231025049/http://www.isas.ac.jp/e/enterp/missions/catalogue.shtml |date=2014-12-31 }}; retrieved 2012-4-16. < /ref >   Its mission was to study the sun. 
 
 On September 12, 2005 the spacecraft burned up during reentry.
