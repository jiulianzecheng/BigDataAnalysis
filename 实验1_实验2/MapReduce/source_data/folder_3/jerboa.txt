 A '''jerboa''' is a small [[desert]] [[rodent]] of [[Asia]] and [[North Africa|Northern Africa]]. < ref name= " :0 " > Burton, Maurice; Burton, Robert (1970). The International Wildlife Encyclopedia. Marshall Cavendish. p. 1323. {{ISBN|978-0-7614-7266-7}}. < /ref >  It looks like a [[mouse]], but it has a long [[tail]] and very long back [[leg]]s. The jerboa moves by jumping. Jerboas have long tails, long hind legs, and short front legs. They tend to live in hot deserts. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 When they are being chased, jerboas can run up to 24 [[Kilometre|kilometers]] per [[hour]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  Some jerboa species are preyed on by [[little owl]]s in central Asia. Most species of jerboas have very good [[hearing]]. Their hearing helps them avoid being attacked by [[Predation|predators]]. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Swanson|first=Nicole|date=2007|title=Euchoreutes naso|url=https://animaldiversity.org/site/accounts/information/Euchoreutes_naso.html|access-date=2 March 2022|website=Animal Diversity Web}} < /ref >
