 '''Timor''' is an [[island]] at the south end of the [[Malay Archipelago]], north of the [[Timor Sea]]. It is divided between the independent state of [[East Timor]], and [[West Timor]], belonging to the [[Indonesia]]n province of [[East Nusa Tenggara]]. 
 
 The island's surface is 11,883 square miles (30,777 [[square kilometre|km²]]). The name is a variant of ''timur'', [[Malay language|Malay]] for “east”; it is so called because it is at the east end of a chain of islands.
