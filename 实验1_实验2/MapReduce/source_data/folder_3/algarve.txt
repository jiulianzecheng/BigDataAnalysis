 The '''Algarve''' (from {{lang-ar|الغرب}} ''al-Gharb''  " the West " ) is the southernmost region of [[continental Portugal]]. The  [[Capital city|capital]] of the Algarve is the city of [[Faro]]. There is an [[:wikt:international|international]] [[airport]] and a [[public university]] ([[University of Algarve]]). 
 
 The present region of Algarve [[:wikt:coincide|coincides]] with the old [[province]] of the same name and with the [[Faro district]]. The main [[Economy|economic]] activity in the region is [[tourism]] because its [[beach]]es and its [[History|historical]] [[building]]s. 
 
 ==History== 
 The name Algarve comes from ''Gharb Al-Andalus'' ({{lang-ar|غرب الأندلس}}, ''gharb al-ʼandalus'';  " The West of [[Al-Andalus]] " ), or just '''Al-Gharb''' ({{lang-ar|الغرب}},  ''al-gharb'';  " The West " ), the name given by the [[Muslim]]s of [[Iberian Peninsula|Iberia]] to the region to the west of [[Andalusia]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://referenceworks.brillonline.com/entries/encyclopedie-de-l-islam/gharb-al-andalus-SIM_2463?s.num=0 & s.q=Gharb+al-Ândalus & s.f.s2_parent_title=s.f.book.Encyclopédie+de+l’Islam |author= A. Huici Miranda |title= G̲h̲arb al-Andalus |publisher=Encyclopédie de l’Islam |language=fr |access-date=7 August 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Algarve has an area of {{Convert|744.57|km2|sqmi|abbr=off}}. < ref name= " ine " > {{cite web |url=https://www.ine.pt/xportal/xmain?xpid=INE & xpgid=ine_indicadores & indOcorrCod=0000611 & selTab=tab0 |title=Portal de Estatísticas Oficiais |publisher=Instituto Nacional de Estatística - Statistics Portugal |date=16 June 2014  |access-date=6 August 2016 }} < /ref >  It borders with the [[Alentejo Region|Alentejo]] region ([[Alentejo Litoral Subregion|Alentejo Litoral]] and [[Baixo Alentejo Subregion|Baixo Alentejo]] subregions) to the north, the [[Atlantic Ocean]] to the west and south, and the [[Guadiana]]  river, and [[Andalusia]] ([[Spain]]), to the east. 
 
 The Algarve region is divided in two zones, each with eight [[Municipality|municipalities]]: 
 # ''Barlavento'', the western zone, with [[Portimão]] as main city; and 
 # ''Sotavento'', the eastern zone, with [[Faro]] as main city. 
 
 The main [[mountain range]]s are ''Serra de Monchique'', la ''Serra de Espinhaço de Cão'' and ''Serra do Caldeirão''. The highest point in the region is the [[Fóia]] mountain, {{Convert|902|m|ft|abbr=off}} high, < ref > {{cite web |url=http://voyages.michelin.fr/europe/portugal/faro/monchique/mont-foia |title=Mont Fóia |publisher=Guide michelin |language=fr |access-date=7 August 2016 |archive-date=29 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220129135014/https://voyages.michelin.fr/europe/portugal/faro/monchique/mont-foia |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  in the ''Serra de Monchique'' mountain range. 
 
 The westernmost point of the region, and of [[continent]]al [[Europa]], is [[Cape St. Vincent]] ({{lang-pt|Cabo de São Vicente}}), < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.linternaute.com/voyage/europe/cotes-du-portugal/cap-saint-vincent.shtml |title= Cabo de São Vicente |publisher=Internaute.com |language= fr |access-date=7 August 2016 }} < /ref >  in the municipality of [[Vila do Bispo]].

 Among the 16 municipalities, [[Loulé]] is the largest of the region, with an área of {{Convert|763.67|km2|sqmi|abbr=off}}; this municipality and [[Silves, Portugal|Silves]] border both the [[Alentejo]] region and the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. [[Vila Real de Santo António]] is the smallest municipality with an área of {{Convert|61.25|km2|sqmi|abbr=off}}. 
 
 Only three municipalities do not border the ocean: [[Monchique]], [[São Brás de Alportel]] and [[Alcoutim]]. 
 
 ==Economy== 
 [[File:Apartment buildings at Praia da Rocha, Portimão.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Apartments overlooking the beach in Praia da Rocha, [[Portimão]].]] 
 [[Farming]] products of the region include [[ficus|fig]], [[almond]], [[Orange (fruit)|orange]], [[Carob tree|carob bean]], [[Arbutus unedo|strawberry tree]] and [[cork oak]]. [[Horticulture]] is also important. [[Fishing]] and [[aquaculture]] are important activities in the [[coastal]] area of Algarve. 
 
 The wines of the region are well-known. There are four wines in the region which have [[Protected Designation of Origin]] (''Denominação de Origem Controlada'' – DOC): Lagoa DOC, Lagos DOC, Portimão DOC and Tavira DOC. 
 
 [[Food processing]], [[cement]] and construction, are the main industries. 
 
 [[Tourism]] related activities are extensive and make the bulk of Algarve's economy during summer time.
