 '''Kajla''' is a breed of sheep, native to Pakistan. < ref name= " FAO " > {{cite web |url=http://agris.fao.org/agris-search/search.do?recordID=US201500027145 |title=Comparative growth performance of Kajli lambs suckling their dams or offered buffalo-milk, cow-milk or milk replacer during pre-weaning period |website=Agris.fao.org |date= |author= |accessdate=September 9, 2017 |archive-date=September 9, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170909191408/http://agris.fao.org/agris-search/search.do?recordID=US201500027145 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is mainly bred in [[Sargodha]] and other cities such as [[Gujranwala]], [[Lahore]], [[Faisalabad]]. The breed is bred primarily for its [[wool]], [[meat]] and [[milk]]. This breed can grow significantly large and bulky at a young age. < ref name= " FAO2 " > {{cite web |url=https://www.ajas.info/upload/pdf/12-11.pdf |title= Productive and reproductive performance of Kajli Ewes|website=AJAS.info|date=  |author= |accessdate= September 9, 2017}} < /ref >  They are considered as the ''“National Sheep of Pakistan”'' respectively.
