 A '''Maiba''' or an '''Amaiba''' (literally,  " priest " < ref name= " google1 " > {{cite book| last = Longkumer| first = Arkotong| title = Reform, Identity and Narratives of Belonging: The Heraka Movement in Northeast India| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=6SzC4XbtPKkC & pg=PA75| year = 2011| publisher = A & C Black| isbn = 978-1-4411-8734-5| page = 75 }} < /ref > < ref name= " google1 "  / > ) is a male religious leader authorised to perform the sacred rites and rituals of [[Sanamahism]] ([[Meitei religion]]), < ref name= " google1 " / >  especially as a mediatory agent between human beings and one or more deities. They have the authority or power to administer religious rites as well as sacrifices to gods. Their office or institution is called '''Maiba Loishang (Maiba Loisang)''', later renamed as '''Pandit Loisang''', during Aryanisation of [[Meitei culture]]. They also served as healers, doctors, < ref > {{cite book| author = Sarat Chandra Roy (Rai Bahadur| title = Man in India| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=xKwZAAAAMAAJ| year = 1933| publisher = A.K. Bose| isbn =  }} < /ref >  magicians, < ref name=google2001 > {{cite book| last = Mahapatra| first = Mary D.| title = Tribal Religion and Rituals: Accounts of Superstition, Sorcery and Spirits| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=yi_XAAAAMAAJ| year = 2001| publisher = Dominant Publishing.| isbn = 978-81-87336-69-3 }} < /ref >  medicine men, < ref > {{cite book| last = Raha| first = Manis Kumar| title = The People of the Himalayas: Bio-cultural and Ecological Aspects| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=utWBAAAAMAAJ| year = 1995| publisher = Anthropological Survey of India, Ministry of Human Resource Development, Department of Culture, Government of India| isbn = 978-81-85579-30-6 }} < /ref >  [[physician]]s and [[shaman]]s. < ref name= " google1 "  / > < ref > {{cite book| last = Jeyaseelan| first = L.| title = Impact of the Missionary Movement in Manipur| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=9SLkAAAAMAAJ| year = 1996| publisher = Scholar Publishing House| isbn = 978-81-7172-339-3 }} < /ref > 
 They play important roles in the [[Apokpa|ancestor worship (apokpa khurumjaba) ceremony]]. < ref > {{cite book| last = Congress| first = Indian History| title = Proceedings| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=NHQMAQAAMAAJ| year = 2004| publisher = Indian History Congress| isbn =  }} < /ref > 
 Chief priest or chief among the maibas is called Maichou, a term formed with the amalgamation of two words ( " maiba " - priest,  " achouba " - big, chief, great). < ref > {{cite book| author = Surajit Sinha| author2 = Centre for Studies in Social Sciences| title = Tribal Polities and State Systems in Pre-colonial Eastern and North Eastern India| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=S4E_AAAAMAAJ| year = 1987| publisher = Centre for Studies in Social Sciences, Calcutta| isbn = 978-81-7074-014-8 }} < /ref > 
 Since their position is not hereditary, they acquire their positions through talent and skills. < ref name= " google2001 "  / >  The female counterpart of a Maiba is a [[Maibi]].
