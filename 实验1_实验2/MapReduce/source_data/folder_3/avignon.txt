 '''Avignon''' ({{lang-la|Avenio}}; {{lang-oc|Avinhon}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in southeastern [[France]] in the [[Departments of France|department]] of [[Vaucluse]] on the left bank of the [[Rhône]] river. It is the [[Capital city|capital]] ([[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) of the Vaucluse department. 
 
 The [[:wikt:historical|historical]] centre, which includes the [[Palais des Papes]], the [[cathedral]], and the [[Pont Saint-Bénézet|Pont d'Avignon]], became a [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site]] in 1995. The [[medieval]] [[monument]]s and the annual [[Festival d'Avignon]] have helped to make the town a major centre for [[tourism]]. 
 
 == Name == 
 The earliest forms of the name were reported by the Greeks: 
 
 *Аὐενιὼν = ''Auenion'' 
 *Άουεννίων = ''Aouennion'' ([[Ptolemy]] II, x). 
 
 The Roman name ''Avennĭo Cavarum'' (Mela, II, 575, Pliny III, 36), i.e.  " Avignon of Cavares "  shows that Avignon was one of the three cities of the [[tribe]] of ''Cavares'', along with ''[[Cavaillon]]'' and ''[[Orange, Vaucluse|Orange]]''. 
 
 == History == 
 Between 1309 and 1377, during the [[Avignon Papacy]], seven successive [[pope]]s lived in Avignon and in 1348 [[Pope Clement VI]] bought the town from [[Joanna I of Naples|Joanna I]] ({{lang-it|Giovanna I}}; March 1328 – 27 July 1382) was [[Kingdom of Naples|Queen of Naples]] and [[Countess]] of [[Provence]]. 
 
 [[Papal]] control persisted until 1791 when, during the [[French Revolution]], it became part of France. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Avignon is on the left bank of the [[Rhône]] river, a few kilometres above its [[confluence]] with the [[Durance]], about {{convert|580|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} southeast of Paris, {{convert|229|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} south of [[Lyon]] and {{convert|85|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} northwest of [[Marseille]]. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|64.78|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|66|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|19|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Avignon-84000/|title=Avignon |publisher=Map-France.com |access-date=10 February 2017 }} < /ref >

 On the west Avignon shares a border with the department of [[Gard]] and to the south it borders the department of [[Bouches-du-Rhône]]. 
 
 Avignon is surrounded by the ''communes'' [[Sorgues]], [[Le Pontet, Vaucluse|Le Pontet]], [[Vedène]], [[Morières-lès-Avignon]] and [[Caumont-sur-Durance]] and the departments [[Bouches-du-Rhône]] (''communes'' of [[Noves]], [[Châteaurenard]], [[Rognonas]] and [[Barbentane]]) and [[Gard]] (''communes'' of [[Les Angles, Gard|Les Angles]] and [[Villeneuve-lès-Avignon]]).

 The [[Rhône]], the [[Durance]] ([[tributary]] of the Rhône) and the L'Anguillon (tributary of the Durance) are the rivers flow through the ''commune''. 
 
 The Rhône passes the western edge of the city but is divided into two branches: the ''Petit Rhône'' for the part that passes next to Avignon, and the ''Grand Rhône'' for the western channel which passes [[Villeneuve-lès-Avignon]] in the [[Gard]] department. The two branches are separated by an island, the ''Île de la Barthelasse''. 
 
 The Durance flows along the southern boundary of the ''commune'' into the Rhône and marks the departmental boundary with [[Bouches-du-Rhône]]. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Avignon, in the [[Köppen climate classification]], is '''Csa''' - Oceanic climate with hot summers. < ref > {{cite web |work=Annuaire-Mairie.fr |title=La ville d'Avignon |url=http://www.annuaire-mairie.fr/ville-avignon.html |language=fr |access-date=10 February 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] temperature for the year in Avignon is {{Convert|14.4|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|23.3|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|5|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Avignon is {{Convert|617.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is October with {{Convert|83.8|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is January with an average of {{Convert|22.9|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Avignon, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=164541 & cityname=Avignon%2C+Provence-Alpes-Cote+d%27Azur%2C+France & units=|access-date=10 February 2017 }} < /ref >

 == Transport == 
 
 === Rail === 
 Avignon has three train stations: Gare d'Avignon Centre, Gare d'Avignon TGV and Gare d'Avignon-Montfavet. 
 
 == Administration == 
 Avignon is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Vaucluse department since 1793. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Avignon|Avignon]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of three [[Cantons of France|cantons]], all formed with part of the commune:  
 # [[Canton of Avignon-1|Avignon-1]] : 30,134 inhabitants   < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Avignon-2|Avignon-2]] : 34,426 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Avignon-3|Avignon-3]] : 35,749 inhabitants (27,649 in Avignon and 8,100 in [[Morières-lès-Avignon]])  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Grand Avignon'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération du Grand Avignon}}).

 ==Places of interest== 
 Some interesting places in Avignon are: 
 * The ''Notre Dame des Doms'' [[cathedral]] (12th century). 
 * The ''Palais des Papes'' ( " [[Papal]] [[Palace]] " ) (14th century). 
 * The ''Pont d'Avignon'' (the ''Pont Saint-Bénézet''), a [[bridge]] best known for the famous French song ''Sur le pont d'Avignon''.
