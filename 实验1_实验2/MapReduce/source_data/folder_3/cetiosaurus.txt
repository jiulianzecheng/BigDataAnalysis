 '''''Cetiosaurus'''''  is a [[sauropod]] [[dinosaur]] whose remains were found in [[Oxfordshire]]. It was a [[herbivore]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/cetiosauriscus.html|title=Cetiosauriscus - Natural History Museum|website=www.nhm.ac.uk}} < /ref > < ref >  von Huene F. 1927. Sichtung der Grundlagen der jetzigen Kenntnis der Sauropoden [Sorting through the basis of the current knowledge of sauropods] (PDF). Eclogae Geologica Helveticae (in German). '''20''': 444–470. < /ref >   
 
 ''Cetiosaurus'' was discovered in 1842. It was the first sauropod from which bones were described, and is the most complete sauropod found in [[England]]. < ref >  Charig A.J. 1980. A diplodocid sauropod from the Lower Cretaceous of England. In Jacobs, L.L. Aspects of Vertebrate History: Essays in Honor of Edwin Harris Colbert. Museum of Northern Arizona Press. pp. 231–244. ISBN 978-0-897-34053-3. < /ref >   
 The dinosaur was  about 16 [[metres]] (52 ft) long and to have weighed roughly 11 [[tonne]]s (12 [[short ton]]s). It is now known as ''Cetiosaurus oxoniensis''.
