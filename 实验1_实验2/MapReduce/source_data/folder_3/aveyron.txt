 '''Aveyron''' ({{lang-oc|Avairon}}) is a [[France|French]] [[Departments of France|department]] found in the northeast of the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]] of southern [[France]] and named after the [[Aveyron (river)|Aveyron]] [[river]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] is [[Rodez]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Aveyron is one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created with most of the territory of the old province of [[Rouergue]]; in fact, the present flag of the department was the flag of Rouergue. 
 
 The first known [[History|historical]] inhabitants of the region were the [[Rutenii]] tribe but, before them, the area was inhabited by people that built many [[Pre-history|prehistoric]] [[:wikt:ruin|ruins]] including over 1,000 [[dolmen]]s - more than any other department in France. 
 
 In 1808, the old [[Cantons in France|canton]] of ''Saint-Antonin-Noble-Val'' was [[:wikt:transfer|transferred]] from Aveyron to the new department of [[Tarn-et-Garonne]]. < ref > [http://www.archivesdefrance.culture.gouv.fr/action-culturelle/celebrations-nationales/2008/vie-politique-et-institutions/creation-du-departement-de-tarn-et-garonne/ Création du département de Tarn-et-Garonne] < /ref > < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/12his.html |title=Historique de l'Aveyron |work=Le SPLAF |language=fr |access-date=15 April 2014 }} < /ref >

 Aveyron is in the southern half of the central [[plateau]] (''[[Massif Central]]'') of France. The [[mountain]]ous areas (the Aubrac mountains, Palanges Levezou and Lacaune) cover little more than a third of the territory, with the highest point in ''[[Signal de Mailhe-Biau]]'' (the northwest [[peak]], {{Coord|42|40|17|N|03|04|19|E|name=Signal de Mailhe-Biau |display=inline |region:ID_type:mountain }}) that is {{Convert|1463|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} high < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=29514 |title=Signal de Mailhe-Biau--Northwest Peak, France |publisher=Peakbagger.com |access-date=8 September 2016 }} < /ref >  and is in the region called ''Cazalets''; the remaining two thirds are occupied by [[hill]]s and [[plateau]]s (Ségala, Viadène, Lagast and Grands Causses), with a few valleys. 
 
 The territory is crossed, among others, by the rivers [[Lot (river)|Lot]] and [[Tarn (river)|Tarn]], both [[Tributary|tributaries]] of the [[Garonne river]]. The [[Aveyron (river)|Aveyron]] river, which gives its name to the department, is a tributary of the Tarn river. 
 
 The main rivers of Aveyron are the Lot in the north, the Aveyron in the centre and the Tarn in the south, all tributaries of the Garonne. They flow from east to west, following the general slope of the department, and divide it into four zones. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of the Aveyron is subtropical with dry-summer, '''Csb''' (Mediterranean Climate) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Millau is {{Convert|10.9|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|19.9|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|3.2|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Millau is {{Convert|731.5|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is September with {{Convert|78.7|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is July with an average of {{Convert|40.6|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Millau, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=85570 & cityname=Millau%2C+Midi-Pyr%E9n%E9es%2C+France & units= |access-date=17 April 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Aveyron is part of the region of [[Occitanie]]. The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Aveyron'' in [[Rodez]]. 
 
 ===Administrative divisions=== 
 There are 3 [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] ([[district]]s), 23 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']] and 286 [[Communes in France|''communes'']] ([[Municipality|municipalities]]) in Aveyron.

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Aveyron are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Aveyronnais'' (women: ''Aveyronnaises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_departement_aveyron_12.html|title=Aveyron (12)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=fr |access-date=17 April 2014 }} < /ref > 
 
 Aveyron has a population, in 2014, of 278,644, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|278644|8735.1|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Rodez, with 143,841 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Millau and Villefranche-de-Rouergue, have respectively 70,995 and 63,808 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / > 
 
 The city with more people living in it is the [[Capital city|capital]], [[Rodez]] (24,088). The subprefectures of [[Millau]] and [[Villefranche-de-Rouergue]] have, respectively, 22,064 and 11,822 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
