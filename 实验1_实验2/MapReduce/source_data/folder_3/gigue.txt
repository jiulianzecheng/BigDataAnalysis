 The '''gigue''' or '''giga''' is a lively [[baroque music|baroque]] [[dance]].   " Gigue "  (pronounce  " jeeg " ) is the [[French language|French]] word, and  " giga "  (pronounce  " jee-ga " ) is the [[Italian language|Italian]] word.  The [[English language|English]] word is  " [[jig]] "  and in England this refers to a [[folk dance]]. 
 
 The gigue was one of the [[movement (music)|movements]] in a series of dance movements called [[suite (music)|suite]]s written by [[Baroque music|Baroque]] composers. The Baroque suite normally had an [[Allemande]], a [[Courante]], a [[Sarabande]] and a Gigue to finish with.  Sometimes there were one or two other movements as well. 
 
 The gigue usually has a [[time signature|compound metre]] such as 6/8, 6/4, 9/8 or 12/16.   They often have a [[counterpoint|contrapuntal]] texture and each [[phrase (music)|phrase]] is usually four bars long.  Like all the dances in the Baroque suite, the gigue is in [[binary form]].  The second half will usually start with the main tune in [[inversion (music|inversion]] (upside down).
