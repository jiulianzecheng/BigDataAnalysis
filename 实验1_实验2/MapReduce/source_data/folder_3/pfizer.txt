 '''Pfizer'''({{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|aɪ|z|ər}}) < ref > {{citation|last=Wells|first=John C.|year=2008|title=Longman Pronunciation Dictionary|edition=3rd|publisher=Longman|isbn=9781405881180}} < /ref >  is an American [[pharmaceutical]] corporation. One of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies, it is ranked 64 on the 2020 [[Fortune 500|''Fortune'' 500]] list of the largest United States corporations by total revenue. < ref name= " businessweek_20122 " > {{cite magazine|date=27 March 2012|title=Pfizer moves higher amid persistent breakup talk|url=http://www.businessweek.com/ap/2012-03/D9TP13I80.htm|magazine=Bloomberg Businessweek|access-date=8 July 2012}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Fortune 500 Companies 2018: Who Made the List|url=http://fortune.com/fortune500/list/|access-date=2018-11-10|website=Fortune|language=en-US|archive-date=2014-07-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140707112353/http://fortune.com/fortune500/list/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It was founded in 1849 in [[New York City]]. It is ranked the largest drug [[company]] in the world. The company is responsible for the first [[COVID-19 vaccine|COVID-19 vaccination]]. Pfizer is known for its drugs [[Ibuprofen|Advil]] and [[ChapStick]]. The company bought [[Wyeth]], which also used to be a drug company. The deal was completed in 2009.
