 '''Sanitation''' means dealing with [[human waste]] ([[feces]] and [[urine]]) safely . < ref name=WHO > {{cite web |url=http://www.who.int/topics/sanitation/en/ |title=Sanitation |author= |date= |website= |publisher=World Health Organization (WHO) |accessdate=15 February 2015}} < /ref >  It also includes ways to maintain [[hygiene]] by [[wikt:dispose|disposing]] of [[garbage]], [[Water treatment|treating wastewater]] and managing [[hazardous waste]]. < ref name=CDC > {{cite web |url=https://www.cdc.gov/healthywater/global/sanitation/ |title=Global Water, Sanitation,  &  Hygiene (WASH) |author= |date= |website= |publisher=Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) |accessdate=15 February 2015}} < /ref >  Sanitation is important because it helps prevent the spread of [[bacteria|germs]] and [[disease]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.unicef.org/wash/index_wes_related.html |title=Water, Sanitation and Hygiene |author= |date= |website= |publisher=UNICEF |accessdate=15 February 2015 |archive-date=11 February 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150211005006/http://www.unicef.org/wash/index_wes_related.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In past centuries it meant  " [[health]] "  and later  " cleanliness " .  
 
 In poor [[country|countries]], many people have poor sanitation because they do not have good [[toilets]]. They also do not have good [[sewage system]]s. In [[Developed country|developed countries]], people usually have good sanitation because they have a sewage system to [[filter]] disease and [[toxin]]s from the water. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.thefreedictionary.com/sanitation|title=Definition of  " Sanitation "  - the disposal of sewage and waste|publisher=The Free Dictionary}} Retrieved on 15 February 2015. < /ref >  The use of [[toilet]]s protects the health of a community by keeping germs out of the [[environment]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://en.hesperian.org/hhg/New_Where_There_Is_No_Doctor:Toilets_for_Health_%28Sanitation%29 |title=Toilets for Health (Sanitation) |author= |date= |website= |publisher=Hesperian Health Guides |accessdate=15 February 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 There are also other types of toilets that are more economical, better for the environment, and do not need big sewer systems or wastewater treatment plants. Examples are [[dry toilet]] < nowiki/ > s and [[twin-pit pour-flush toilet]] < nowiki/ > s (of which many have been installed in India).
