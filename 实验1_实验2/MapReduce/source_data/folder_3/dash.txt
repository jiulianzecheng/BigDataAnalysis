The '''dash''' is a [[punctuation]] mark that is usually written in the middle of a line of text. < ref name=grammarly > {{cite web |url=http://www.grammarly.com/handbook/punctuation/dash/1/what-is-a-dash/ |title=What Is A Dash? |publisher=Grammarly Inc |access-date=January 12, 2017 |archive-date=September 12, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150912110227/http://www.grammarly.com/handbook/punctuation/dash/1/what-is-a-dash/ |url-status=bot: unknown }} < /ref >  A [[hyphen]] is sometimes called a dash, but it is used differently. Dashes are used to add more [[wikt:drama|drama]] to writing. The two main forms of dashes are: 
 * The en dash (–) is the shorter of the two. It is longer than a hyphen but shorter than an em dash. < ref name=enPG > {{cite web |url=http://www.thepunctuationguide.com/en-dash.html |title=En dash |publisher=The Punctuation Guide |access-date=January 12, 2017}} < /ref >  In normal text it is about the width of the letter  " n " . < ref name=Gilad > {{cite web |url=http://www.dummies.com/education/language-arts/grammar/using-em-dashes-and-en-dashes-properly/ |title=Using Em Dashes and En Dashes Properly |author=Suzanne Gilad |website=Dummies |publisher=John Wiley  &  Sons, Inc |access-date=January 12, 2017}} < /ref >  An en dash is used to connect two numbers that make up a range (for example pages 3–6). < ref name=Gilad/ >  It is also used to show a range of dates (2010–2011) or of time (11:30 a.m.–1:30 p.m.). < ref name=enPG/ >   
 * The em dash (—) is so-named because it is about the length of an [[Letter case|uppercase]] ''M''. < ref name=Gilad/ >  They are best used to show [[wikt:emphasis|emphasis]] or for an [[wikt:interruption|interruption]] in thought. < ref name=Gilad/ >  They can also be used in place of a [[Punctuation#Comma|comma]], [[parentheses]], or a [[colon (punctuation)|colon]]. < ref name=emPG > {{cite web |url=http://www.thepunctuationguide.com/em-dash.html |title=Em dash |publisher=The Punctuation Guide |access-date=January 12, 2017}} < /ref >  But they are less [[formal]] than the punctuation marks they replace. < ref name=emPG/ >
