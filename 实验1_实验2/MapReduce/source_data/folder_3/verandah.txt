 ==History of term== 
 The word Veranda came to English through [[India]] by way of an Indo-Portuguese creole, the word itself comes from the Portuguese ''varanda'' which means  " long [[balcony]] or terrace " . 
 
 == Verandahs in styles of architecture== 
 
 [[File:Verandah of The Hollow.jpg|thumb|Winifred Rawson nursing her son on the verandah of The Hollow, near Mackay, Australia, ca. 1873]]  
 The veranda has been an important part of local [[Australia]]n architecture. It began to become common in colonial buildings during the 1850s. 
 
 The [[Creole Townhouse]] in [[New Orleans|New Orleans, Louisiana]] is also noted for its prominent use of verandas.
