'''Menthol''' is an [[organic compound]]. It is derived from the oils of [[corn mint]], [[peppermint]], or other [[Mint (plant)|mints]]. It can also be made [[artificial]]ly. It is a [[wax]]y, [[crystalline]] substance. It is white in color. It is a [[solid]] at [[room temperature]]. It [[Melting|melts]] at a [[temperature]] that is a little bit higher than room temperature. 
 
 Menthol has local [[anaesthetic]] qualities, and reduces irritation of the throat. 
 
 [[Mentha arvensis]] is the main type of mint used to make natural menthol [[crystal]]s. It is mainly grown in the [[Uttar Pradesh]] [[region]] in [[India]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/156817834|title=PDR for herbal medicines.|date=2007|publisher=Thomson|others=Thomson Healthcare (Firm)|isbn=978-1-56363-678-3|edition=4th|location=Montvale, NJ|oclc=156817834}} < /ref > 
 
 Natural menthol is made by [[freezing]] peppermint oil and separating methanol from the frozen the peppermint oil by [[Filter|filtration]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/823726450|title=Kirk-Othmer Chemical Technology of Cosmetics.|last=Kirk-Othmer.|date=2012|publisher=Wiley|isbn=978-1-118-51898-4|location=Hoboken|oclc=823726450}} < /ref > 
 
 == Uses == 
 It is used as an [[antipruritic]] to reduce itching. It is used to relieve muscle cramps, [[sprain]]s and [[headache]]s. It is used in some medicines to treat [[sunburn]]s. It is used as a [[pesticide]] against [[tracheal mite]]s. In [[organic chemistry]], menthol is used as a [[chiral auxiliary]] in [[asymmetric synthesis]].
