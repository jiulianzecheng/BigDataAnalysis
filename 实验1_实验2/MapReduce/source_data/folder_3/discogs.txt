 '''Discogs''' is a [[website]] and [[database]] of information about [[music]] [[recording]]s. Discogs is owned by Zink Media, Inc. in [[Portland, Oregon]], [[United States]]. Discogs is one of the largest online databases of electronic music releases. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tBObDwAAQBAJ & dq=discogs+largest+database & pg=PA168|title=Innovations for Community Services: 19th International Conference, I4CS 2019, Wolfsburg, Germany, June 24-26, 2019, Proceedings|last1=Lüke|first1=Karl-Heinz|last2=Eichler|first2=Gerald|last3=Erfurth|first3=Christian|last4=Fahrnberger|first4=Günter|date=2019-05-31|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-3-030-22482-0|pages=168|language=en}} < /ref >  It is believed to be the largest online database of releases on [[vinyl record|vinyl]] also. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=F7GrCAAAQBAJ & dq=discogs+largest+database+vinyl & pg=PA207|title=Digital Libraries: 6th Italian Research Conference, IRCDL 2010, Padua, Italy, January 28-29, 2010. Revised Selected Papers|last1=Agosti|first1=Maristella|last2=Esposito|first2=Floriana|last3=Thanos|first3=Costantino|date=2010-09-11|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-3-642-15850-6|pages=207|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 The discogs.com domain name was registered in August 2000. Discogs was released in October 2000 by Kevin Lewandowski. It started as a database of his personal music collection.  The name of the site is short for [[discography|discographies]]. 
 
 == Contribution system == 
 The information on Discogs comes from members of the web site.
