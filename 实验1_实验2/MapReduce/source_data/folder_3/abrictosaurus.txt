 '''''Abrictosaurus''''' was a [[Heterodontosauridae|heterodontosaurid]] [[dinosaur]] from the [[Lower Jurassic]] of what is now southern [[Africa]]. It was a small [[bipedal]] [[herbivore]] or [[omnivore]], about 1.2 & nbsp;meters (4 & nbsp;feet) long, and weighing less than 45 & nbsp;kilograms (100 & nbsp;pounds). 
 
 ''Abrictosaurus'' is usually thought to be the most [[basal]] member of the family Heterodontosauridae. < ref name= " normanetal2004 " > Norman D.B. ''et al.'' 2004. Basal Ornithopoda. In Weishampel D.B., Dodson P.,  &  Osmolska H. (eds). ''The Dinosauria''. 2nd ed, Berkeley: University of California Press. 393-412 < /ref > < ref name= " weishampelwitmer1990 " > Weishampel D.B.  &  Witmer L.M. 1990. Heterodontosauridae. In: Weishampel D.B., Dodson P., and Osmolska H. ''The Dinosauria'' 1st ed, Berkeley: University of California Press. 486-497 < /ref >  ''Lycorhinus'' and ''Heterodontosaurus'' both had [[hypsodont|high-crowned]] cheek teeth, which went over each other in the jaw, making a continuous chewing surface [[analogy (biology)|analogous]] to those of [[Cretaceous]] [[hadrosaurid]]s. ''Abrictosaurus'' had more widely-split cheek teeth, with lower crowns, more similar to other early ornithischians.  
 
 The family as a whole has [[canine]]-like [[tusk]]s. < ref name= " sereno1986 " > Sereno, P.C. 1986. Phylogeny of the bird-hipped dinosaurs (Order Ornithischia).
