 '''Currywurst''' is a [[Germany|German dish]].  It is made from a [[pork]] [[sausage]] ({{lang-de|wurst}}), sliced and covered in a ''[[curry]] [[sauce]]'' (usually [[ketchup]] or [[tomato paste]] blended with curry) and generous amounts of [[curry powder]].  
 
 Currywurst is often sold as a [[Take-out|take-out/take-away food]], [[Schnell-Imbiss]]e, at [[diner]]s or  " [[greasy spoon]]s, "  on children's menus in [[restaurant]]s, or as a [[street food]]. 
 
 [[Image:Gedenktafel_Kantstr_101_Herta_Heuwer.JPG|thumb|Plaque at the site of Herta Heuwer's stall]]Berlin [[legend]] says that Herta Heuwer, from Königsberg, invented the currywurst sauce by experimenting at her stall in [[Charlottenburg|Berlin-Charlottenburg]] in 1949. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.morgenpost.de/content/2004/08/30/berlin/700467.html |title=Wo gibt's in Berlin die beste Currywurst? |accessdate=2008-03-06 |last= |first= |date=30 August 2004 |format= |work=Berliner Morgenpost newspaper |pages= |language= |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070404072545/http://www.morgenpost.de/content/2004/08/30/berlin/700467.html |archivedate=2007-04-04 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 
 People from the [[Ruhr area|Ruhr-area]] say that the sauce was accidentally invented by a sausage stall owner in [[Essen]], who dropped a can with curry powder into some ketchup.  
 
 Early in his career German pop singer [[Herbert Grönemeyer]], raised in [[Bochum]], wrote a song, in the Ruhr dialect, to currywurst. 
 
 Although Berlin claims currywurst as its invention and  " national dish " , all over Germany, there are more than 20 different types of currywurst.
