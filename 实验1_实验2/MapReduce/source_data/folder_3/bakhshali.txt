 '''Bakhshali''' is a village and [[Union Councils of Pakistan|Union Council]] of [[Mardan District]] in the [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]] province of [[Pakistan]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.nrb.gov.pk/lg_election/union.asp?district=53 & dn=Mardan |title=Tehsils  &  Unions in the District of Mardan – Government of Pakistan |access-date=2008-09-01 |archive-date=2012-08-05 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120805105450/http://www.nrb.gov.pk/lg_election/union.asp?district=53 & dn=Mardan |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is at 34°17'0N 72°9'0E and has a height of 307 & nbsp;metres (1010 & nbsp;ft). < ref > [http://www.fallingrain.com/world/PK/3/Bakhshali.html Location of Bakhshali – Falling Rain Genomics] < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 The village is famous for being the place where the [[Bakhshali manuscript]] was found. This is an ancient mathematical work written on [[birch bark]] and is the oldest surviving document in [[South Asia]] of [[Indian mathematics]]. < ref > {{cite book| last = Flood| first = Gavin| title = The Blackwell Companion to Hinduism| url = https://books.google.com/books?id=qSfneQ0YYY8C & pg=PA371| year = 2003| publisher = Wiley| isbn = 978-0-631-21535-6| page = 371 }} < /ref >  It was discovered in 1881 during [[British Raj|British rule]] by a [[tenant]] of Mian An-Wan-Udin, a police inspector. The  tenant discovered it while he was digging in an abandoned building. < ref > [http://www-history.mcs.st-andrews.ac.uk/HistTopics/Bakhshali_manuscript.html The Bakhshali manuscript] < /ref > 
 
 ==Bakhshali today== 
 In March 2007 there were attacks made on music shops in Bakhshali. Two people were injured and one was killed in attacks by an unidentified extremist group who attacked the [[bazaar]] shooting and killing the watchman of the Seven Stars music centre. < ref name= " free " > [http://www.freemuse.org/sw18146.asp Extremists kill one, injure two in attacks on music shops] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080827223303/http://www.freemuse.org/sw18146.asp |date=2008-08-27 }}, Marvaiz Khan, 18 March 2007, Freemuse, accessed 31 August 2008 < /ref > 
 
 In August 2007 the people of Bakhshali observed a strike against power cuts. They blocked the main [[Mardan]] to Bakshali road in protest. The protectors said that the power cuts were harming business and their children's studies. < ref > [http://www.dawn.com/2007/08/25/local34.htm MARDAN: Demo held against loadshedding] < /ref >  The village has also been affected by violence between the Taliban and the government; on 24 January 2008 the militants attacked the vehicle of former Health minister Syed Kamal Shah injuring his guard. < ref name= " Daily Times " > [http://www.dailytimes.com.pk/default.asp?page=2008\05\20\story_20-5-2008_pg7_53 Mardan residents say paying price for delay in Taliban-govt peace pact – Daily Times] < /ref >  The rebels have also been pressuring local people to stop listening to music as well launching bomb attacks in the area. < ref name=free/ > < ref name= " Daily Times " / > 
 
 On 15 August a meeting of elders in Mardan decided to set up anti-Taliban group similar to one that has been stated in neighbouring [[Buner District]]. According to Shakoor Khan, an elder from Bakhshali who attended the meeting,  " Before the Taliban resorts to torching more schools, we have decided to resist them " . < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://ipsnews.net/news.asp?idnews=43672 |title=PAKISTAN: Border Villages Rise Up Against Taliban - Inter Press Service News agency |access-date=2008-09-21 |archive-date=2008-09-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080910012058/http://www.ipsnews.net/news.asp?idnews=43672 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
