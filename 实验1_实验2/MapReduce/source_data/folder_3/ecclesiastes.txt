'''Ecclesiastes''' is a book in the [[Old Testament]] of the [[Bible]]. It is described as  " the words of the [[Philosopher]], [[King David of Israel|David]]'s son, who was King in [[Jerusalem]] "  (verse 1). This is probably [[Solomon]].  The book talks about the [[meaning of life]] and the best way to live.

 ==Impact== 
 Some of the sayings and ideas in Ecclesiastes have become important in [[Great Britain|British]] and [[United States|American]] [[culture]].  For example, the saying  " there is nothing new under the sun "  is often used in the United States. 
 
 During the [[American Civil War]], President [[Abraham Lincoln]] used a quote from Ecclesiastes in a speech to [[United States Congress|Congress]]:  " One [[generation]] passeth away, and another generation cometh: but the earth abideth for ever.... " < ref > {{cite book|last=Foote|first=Shelby|title=The Civil War, A Narrative (Vol. 1)|year=1986|publisher=Vintage Books|pages=807–808|isbn=9780307744678|url= https://books.google.com/books?id=tebyQ1bykX0C & q=the+civil+war+a+narrative+volume+1+fort+sumter+to+perryville}} < /ref >   ('' " One generation of people dies, and another generation is born, but the world will be here forever. " '')  
 
 President Joe Biden has also made a brief reference of Ecclesiastes during his winning speech after the 2020 Presidential Election:  " To everything there is a season: a time to build, a time to reap, and a time to sow and a time to heal. This is the time to heal in America.”  < ref > {{Cite web|title=Joe Biden quotes Bible, hymn 'On Eagle's Wings' in victory speech|url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/joe-biden-quotes-bible-hymn-in-victory-speech.html|access-date=2020-11-13|website=www.christianpost.com|date=8 November 2020 |language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Poets|first=Academy of American|title=Ecclesiastes 3:1-8 by Anonymous - Poems {{!}} Academy of American Poets|url=https://poets.org/poem/ecclesiastes-31-8|access-date=2020-11-13|website=poets.org}} < /ref >

 One of the most famous parts of Ecclesiastes is verses 3:1 to 3:8: 
 < blockquote > " ''To every thing there is a season, and a time to every purpose under the heaven:'' < br > 
 ''A time to be born, and a time to die; a time to [[farming|plant]], and a time to pluck up that which is planted;'' < br > 
 ''A time to kill, and a time to heal; a time to break down, and a time to build up;'' < br > 
 ''A time to weep, and a time to laugh; a time to [[mourning|mourn]], and a time to dance;'' < br > 
 ''A time to cast away stones, and a time to gather stones together; a time to embrace, and a time to refrain from embracing;'' < br > 
 ''A time to get, and a time to lose; a time to keep, and a time to cast away;'' < br > 
 ''A time to [[wikt:rend|rend]], and a time to sew; a time to keep silence, and a time to speak;'' < br > 
 ''A time to love, and a time to hate; a time of [[war]], and a time of [[peace]]. " '' < /blockquote > 
 
 [[Pete Seeger]] made a song named ''Turn, Turn, Turn!'' that included lines from these verses of Ecclesiastes. [[The Byrds]] had an international hit with their version of this song in 1965.
