'''Purim''', Hebrew: פורים , meaning ''[[allotment]]'', is a [[Jew]]ish cheerful feast in memory of the liberation of the Jewish people during their stay in [[Achaemenid Empire|ancient Persia]]. 
 
 The origins of Purim are to be found in the [[Book of Esther]]. 
 
 It is celebrated on the 14th of [[Adar]] (in [[Jerusalem]] on the 15th of Adar).
