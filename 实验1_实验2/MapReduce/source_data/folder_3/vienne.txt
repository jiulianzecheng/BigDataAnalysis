 '''Vienne''' is a [[departments of France|department]] in southwestern [[France]] in the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It is named after the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]] [[river]], the most important river in the department. 
 
 ==History== 
 Vienne is one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created from parts of the old [[province]]s of [[Poitou]], [[Touraine]] and [[Berry (province of France)|Berry]], until the 15th century part of the [[Duchy of Aquitaine]]. 
 
 The new department had six [[district]]s: Châtellerault, Civray, Loudun, Lusignan, Montmorillon and Poitiers. The [[Capital city|capital]] (now [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) was [[Poitiers]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/86his.html |title=Historique de la Vienne |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=28 July 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the six districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Châtellerault, Civray, Loudun, Montmorillon and Poitiers. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissements'' of Civray and Loudun were eliminated. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Vienne is part of the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|6990.4|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 The highest point of the department is ''Signal de Prun'', {{Convert|231|m|ft|abbr=on}} high, in the ''commune'' of [[Adriers]], in the [[Massif Central]].

 The main [[river]] in the department is the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]] that gives its name to the department; it is one of the main [[tributary]] of the [[Loire River|Loire]] river. The Vienne river flows through the department from north to south. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Poitiers is an oceanic climate with template summers, '''Cfb''' (Marine West Coast Climate) in the [[Köppen climate classification]]. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Poitiers, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=53370 & cityname=Poitiers%2C+Poitou-Charentes%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=28 July 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Poitiers is {{Convert|685.8|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is October with {{Convert|76.2|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is August with an average of {{Convert|40.6|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Poitiers is {{Convert|11.7|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|19.6|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|4.7|°C|°F }}. 
 
 ==Administration== 
 The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Vienne'' in [[Poitiers]]. Vienne is part of the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]].

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Vienne are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Viennois'' (women: ''Viennoises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/vienne-86 |title=Vienne (86)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=28 July 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Vienne has a population, in 2014, of 433,203, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|433203|6990.4|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Poitiers, with 246,038 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Châtellerault and Montmorillon, have respectively 112,313 and 74,852 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
