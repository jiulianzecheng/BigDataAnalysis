 '''Jjimjilbang''' or '''jimjilbang''' are [[Korea|Korean]] [[spa]]s.  They have bathtubs, heated floors, and hot kiln rooms that are like [[sauna]]s.  Some of them have pools or massages. < ref name=BBCT > {{cite news|publisher=[[BBC]] Travel|accessdate=July 8, 2021|title=The origins of bathhouse culture around the world|url=https://www.bbc.com/travel/article/20121129-the-origins-of-bathhouse-culture-around-the-world|date=November 29, 2012|author=Suemedha Sood|archive-date=July 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210712084652/https://www.bbc.com/travel/article/20121129-the-origins-of-bathhouse-culture-around-the-world|url-status=live}} < /ref > < ref name=LA > {{cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2021-06-25/as-la-korean-spas-reopen-what-are-covid-19-risks|accessdate=December 2, 2021|newspaper=LA Times|author=Ada Tseng|title=What's it like to go to a Korean spa? Navigating L.A.'s reopening with 'Good Trouble's' Sherry Cola |date=June 25, 2021}} < /ref > < ref name=Time > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mjf1AgAAQBAJ & dq=jimjilbang & pg=PA191|accessdate=December 3, 2021|year=2011|publisher=Time Out Guides|title=Time Out Seoul|page=191|isbn=9781846702839 }} < /ref > 
 
 There are two types of warmth in a jjimjilbang: heated floors and heated rooms similar to [[sauna]]s. < ref name=KH > {{cite news|publisher=Korea Herald|accessdate=December 1, 2021|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20181211000847|author=Kim Hye-soo|title=[Weekender] Jjimjilbang: Sanctuaries of winter warmth |date=December 13, 2018}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Architecture== 
 
 Some jjimjilbang in Korea today are open all day and night.  In some jjimjilbang, travelers can buy a place to sleep for the night, like in a hotel. < ref name=BBCT / > 
 
 It costs about ￦5000 to to go to a jjimjilbang and about ￦8000 to sleep there overnight (US$5 to $8).  People come to a jjimjilbang and take off their shoes to walk on the heated floor.  They put all their clothes in lockers.  Then they take showers with soap before going into any of the pools or saunas. < ref name=KH / > < ref name=Time / > 
 
 People make jjimjilbang out of different things for different reasons.  People think bathing in a jjimjilbang made of clay may remove [[toxin]]s from the body.  People think bathing in a jjimjilbang made of [[jade]] may stop pain in the joints, for example the knees. < ref name=BBCT / > 
 
 Today, some jjimijilbang also have fitness centers and rooms for arcade games, movies, the Internet, and fitness centers. < ref name=KH / >  Some jjimjilbang let people wear shorts and t-shirts to the entertainment areas but not in the water. < ref name=Time / > 
 
 ==History== 
 
 Jjimjilbang came from the natural [[hot spring]]s in Korea. < ref name=BBCT / >   The first jjimjilbang may have been in the [[Joseon era]] (late 1300s to late 1800s). < ref name=KH / >
