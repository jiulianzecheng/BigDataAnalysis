 '''Feliformia''' is a [[suborder]] in the [[mammal]] [[Order (biology)|order]] [[Carnivora]]. It contains all the [[Felidae]], which are cat-like animals. The suborder also contains the relatives of the cats, which includes [[hyaena]]s, [[civet]]s, [[Asiatic linsang]]s, and more. There are six [[taxonomy|families]], twelve [[taxonomy|subfamilies]], 56 [[taxonomy|genera]] and 114 [[species]] in the Feliformia suborder now. 
 
 [[DNA sequence|Molecular phylogeny]] shows the Feliformia is monophyletic. < ref > Eizirik E. ''et al'' 2010. Pattern and timing of the diversification of the mammalian order Carnivora inferred from multiple nuclear gene sequences. ''Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution'' '''56''': 49-63. {{doi|10.1016/j.ympev.2010.01.033}} 
 < /ref >  The other suborder of Carnivora is the dog-like [[Caniformia]].
