 '''Crich''' ({{pronEng|ˈkraɪtʃ}} ''kryech'') {{audio|En-crich.ogg|listen}} is a [[village]] in the [[England|English]] [[Ceremonial counties of England|county]] of [[Derbyshire]]. It is the home of the [[National Tramway Museum]] in the [[Crich Tramway Village]]. 
 
 ==Memorial Tower== 
 At the top of Crich Hill is a tower. The [[tower]] was built to remember members of the [[regiment]] who died in [[battle]], particularly in [[World War I]]. 
 
 The tower was built in 1923 on the site of an older tower called Crich Stand. The Memorial Tower is the end of an annual [[pilgrimage]] on the first Sunday in July. It is {{convert|305|m|ft|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} above [[sea level]]. The tower has 52 steps to the top. From the top of the tower, eight counties and [[landmark]]s such as the [[Humber Bridge]] and [[Lincoln Cathedral]] can be seen. < ref > [http://www.crich-memorial.org.uk/ Crich Memorial] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081017103906/http://www.crich-memorial.org.uk/ |date=2008-10-17 }}, official site < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 Crich is mentioned in 1009 by [[Ethelred the Unready]] when he gives away [[Weston-on-Trent]] to his minister. < ref name=s922 > [http://www.anglo-saxons.net/hwaet/?do=seek & query=S+922 Charter of Æthelred], The Great Council, 1009, accessible at Derby records < /ref > 
 
 A workhouse opened in 1734 and had 40 people. The workhouse accepted poor people from places, including Melbourne, Pentrich, Willington, Mercaston and Denby. < ref > Higginbotham, P. (2007), ''Workhouses of the Midlands'', Tempus, Stroud. Page 27. {{ISBN|978-0-7524-4488-8}} < /ref > 
 
 The television series ''Peak Practice'' and the 2007 movie '' " And When Did You Last See Your Father " '' were filmed in Crich.
