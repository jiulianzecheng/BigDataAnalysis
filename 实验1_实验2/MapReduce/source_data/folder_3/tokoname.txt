 {{nihongo|'''Tokoname'''|常滑市|Tokoname-shi}} is a [[Japan]]ese [[city]] in [[Aichi Prefecture]] on the island of [[Honshu]]. < ref name= " nussbaum " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA975  " Tokoname " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 975; [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA126  " ''Chūbu'' " ] at p. 126. < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:Tokoname Yakimono Sanpomichi 2009-10E.JPG|thumb|right|Huge ''Maneki neko'' at Tokoname]] 
 Tokoname has been known for making ceramics since the 12th century. < ref name= " nussbaum " / >   The city is Japan's top producer of ''[[Maneki Neko]]''. < ref > [http://members.virtualtourist.com/m/13da7c/ec9e5/   " Tokoname, Birthplace of Manekineko Lucky Cats "  at Virtualtourist.com] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130608030449/http://members.virtualtourist.com/m/13da7c/ec9e5/ |date=2013-06-08 }}; excerpt,  " About eighty percent of this type of cat figurines are produced in Tokoname... " ; retrieved 2012-12-4. < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Tokoname is on the western coast of [[Chita Peninsula]] in southern [[Aichi Prefecture]].
