'''Alphard''' (designated '''Alpha Hydrae'''), < ref > Kunitzsch, Paul; Smart, Tim (2006). ''A Dictionary of Modern star Names: A Short Guide to 254 Star Names and Their Derivations'' (2nd rev. ed.). Cambridge, Massachusetts: Sky Pub. < /ref >  is the brightest star in the constellation of [[Hydra (constellation)|Hydra]]. It is a single [[giant star]], but is cooler than the [[Sun]] and larger and more [[Luminosity|luminous]]. The star is about 177 [[Light year|light years]] away from us. 
 
 [[File:Hydra_constellation_map.svg|thumb|The star is on the top right edge, the dot labelled É‘ is the star.]] 
 The star has more designations in which of them include; Alfard, Alphart, Kalbelaphard, etc.
