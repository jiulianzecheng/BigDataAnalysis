 '''Dihya''' ([[Algerian Arabic]]: '''{{lang|ar|ديهيا}}''', [[Berber language|Berber]]: '''{{lang|ber|Daya Ult Yenfaq Tajrawt}}''', '''Dihya''', or '''Damya'''), was a [[Berber people|Berber]] queen and religious and military leader. She led the native people to resist [[Arab]] expansion in [[North Africa|Northwest Africa]]. This happened in the region then known as [[Numidia]], which is eastern [[Algeria]] today. She was born in the early 7th century and died around the end of the 7th century in what is now Algeria.
