 '''Vaisakhi''' ({{lang-pa|ਵਿਸਾਖੀ}}, {{lang-hi|बैसाखी}} ''{{IAST|vaisākhī}}'' (also known as '''Vishu''' or '''Baisaki''') is an important [[festival]] celebrated by [[Sikhs]] and [[Hindus]]. It is a very big [[Festival|celebration]] in [[South Asia]] and is celebrated around April 13. 
 
 == Observance == 
 
 Vaisakhi is the ancient [[:en:wikt:harvest festival|harvest festival]] in the [[Punjab region]].  It is also the start of a new [[:en:wikt:solar year|solar year]], and new harvest season. Baisakhi is a [[Sikh]] [[religious]] festival. < ref name =  " BBC Sikh " > {{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/religion/religions/sikhism/holydays/vaisakhi.shtml |title= Sikhism holy days: Baisakhi |accessdate=2007-07-08 |publisher=[[BBC]] }} < /ref >  It falls on the first day of the ''Baisakh'' month in the [[:en:wikt:solar calendar|solar calendar]], which corresponds to  April 13 in the [[Gregorian calendar]]. In [[Sikhism]], it remembers the creation of the [[Khalsa]] at [[Anandpur Sahib]] in 1699, by the 10th [[Sikh Guru]], [[Guru Gobind Singh]]. < ref name =  " Baisakhi Resource " > {{cite web |url=http://www.baisakhi1999.org/baisakhi.htm |title=The Historic Day of Baisakhi |accessdate=2009-03-08 |publisher=Brig. Partap Singh Ji Jaspal (Retd.) |archive-date=2009-01-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090129130248/http://www.baisakhi1999.org/baisakhi.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 This day is also observed as the beginning of the [[Hindu]] solar new year celebrated by the people of [[Nepal]] and [[India]] in [[Assam|Assam Valley]], [[Kerala]], [[Orissa]], [[West Bengal]] and some other [[regions]] of India. There are regional variation outside of [[Punjab (India)|Punjab]] too. In [[Himachal Pradesh]], the Hindu Goddess Jwalamukhi is worshipped on Vaisakhi, while in [[Bihar]], the Sun-god [[Surya]] is honoured. < ref name =  " BBC Hindu " > {{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/religion/religions/hinduism/ |title= hinduism info |accessdate=2008-02-12 |publisher=[[BBC]] }} < /ref > 
 
 == Other observances == 
 
 The festival is celebrated as  
 *''[[Bohag Bihu|Rongali Bihu]]'' in [[Assam]],  
 *''Naba Barsha'' or ''[[Pohela Boishakh]]'' in [[Bengal]] and [[Tripura]], *''[[Puthandu]]'' (Tamil New Year) in [[Tamil Nadu]],  
 *''[[Vishu]]'' (or Vaishakhi) in [[Kerala]],  
 *''Bikhu'' or ''Bikhauti'' in the [[Kumaon division|Kumaon]] region of [[Uttarakhand]], 
 * ''[[Maha Vishuba Sankranti]]'' (or Pana Sankranti) in [[Orissa]], and the  
 *[[Sinhalese New Year]] festival in [[Sri Lanka]]. 
 
 ==Calendars== 
 
 Hinduism and Sikhism have several religious calendars. Some places of [[worship]] accept one version and others use a different version. So the festival will always fall on the same day in the western [[Gregorian calendar]].
