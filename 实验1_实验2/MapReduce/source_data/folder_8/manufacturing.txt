 '''Manufacturing''' is producing [[finished goods|goods]] out of [[raw material]]s. It is a branch of [[industry]]. It includes making big, complicated things that need many steps to finish, and small, simple things. Often [[machine]]s are involved in manufacturing. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Kenton|first=Will|title=Manufacturing|url=https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/manufacturing.asp|access-date=March 8, 2022|website=Investopedia|language=en|archive-date=November 17, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201117184723/https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/manufacturing.asp|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 In the past, products were made by [[handicraft]] workers using tools and [[manual labor]]. In modern times, most manufacturing jobs were replaced by [[Automation|automated machines]] in a [[factory]], which made the [[Product stewardship|products]] much faster and in turn - cheaper than [[humans]] could.
