'''Inanna'''{{efn|{{IPAc-en|ɪ|ˈ|n|ɑː|n|ə}}; {{lang-sux|{{cuneiform|𒀭𒈹}}}} {{transl|sux| < sup > [[Dingir|D]] < /sup > inanna}}{{snf|Heffron|2016}}}} is the goddess of love, war, and fertility. She was also closely associated with feminine beauty, sex (and sexual desire), and political power in [[Sumer|Sumerian]] mythology; she is more commonly known as  " '''Ishtar''' "  {{efn|{{IPAc-en|ˈ|ɪ|ʃ|t|ɑːr|}}; {{transl|sux| < sup > [[Dingir|D]] < /sup > ištar}}{{sfn|Heffron|2016}}}}, the name under which she was worshipped by the [[Akkad|Akkadians]], [[Babylonia|Babylonians]], and [[Assyria|Assyrians]]. Known as the  " Queen of Heaven " , Innana was one of the most popular deities in Sumerian religion, with the city of Uruk being the center of her cult. Her symbols included the lion, dove and the eight-pointed star.
