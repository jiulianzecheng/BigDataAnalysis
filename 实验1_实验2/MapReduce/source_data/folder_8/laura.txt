The name '''Laura''' [ '''lahv'''-rah; ''English'' '''lah'''-vr''uh'' ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of laura {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/laura|access-date=2023-03-31|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[female]] given name in [[Latin]] [[Europe]] whose meaning,  " Bay Laurel " , translates to everyman. The name is from the bay laurel plant, which in the Greco-Roman era, was used as a symbol of victory, fame or honor. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Laura name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Laura-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-31|website=babys.name}} < /ref >   
 
 Around [[British]] [[North America]], it is likely the name ''Laura'' was strongly popular for female babies until its rapid decline in late 19th century. It rebounded various times since, although it has steadily declined in popularity in some countries. This name was ranked #391 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-31|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref > 
 
 There are many celebrities carrying the name ''Laura''. 
 
 In 2020, [[Hurricane Laura]] struck western [[Louisiana]] and southeast [[Texas]]. The storm caused $19 billion in damage. The name ''Laura'' was retired and replaced by ''Leah''
