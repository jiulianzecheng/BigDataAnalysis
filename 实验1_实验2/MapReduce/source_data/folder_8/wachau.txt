 The '''Wachau''' is an [[Austria]]n valley.  It has pretty landscape formed by the [[Danube]] river. It is one of the biggest tourist places of [[Lower Austria]].  It is midway between the towns of [[Melk]] and [[Krems an der Donau|Krems]]. It is {{convert|40|km}} in length.  People lived here in [[Prehistory|prehistoric]] times.   
 
 A well-known place and tourist attraction is [[Dürnstein]].  This is where King [[Richard I of England|Richard the Lion-Heart]] of [[England]] was held [[Captivity (animal)|captive]] by Duke [[Leopold V of Austria (Babenberg)|Leopold V]].  
 
 The Wachau is a [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage Site]].
