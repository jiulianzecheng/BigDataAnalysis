 '''Petrozavodsk''' ([[Russian language|Russian]]: Петрозаводск, [[Karelian language|Karelian]]/[[Veps language|Vepsian]]/[[Finnish language|Finnish]]: Petroskoi) is a [[city]] in northwestern [[Russia]] and the [[administrative center]] of the [[Republic of Karelia]]. The city has a population of around 280,170 people. 
 
 == History == 
 Evidence shows that people have lived in the Petrozavodsk region since seven thousand years ago. At the end of the sixteenth century, there was a settlement in the region that was named ''Onegaborg''. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://dic.academic.ru/dic.nsf/enc_geo/3842/%D0%9F%D0%B5%D1%82%D1%80%D0%BE%D0%B7%D0%B0%D0%B2%D0%BE%D0%B4%D1%81%D0%BA|title=Петрозаводск - это... Что такое Петрозаводск?|website=Словари и энциклопедии на Академике}} < /ref > 
 
 During the [[Finland|Finnish]] occupation of Petrozavodsk, the city was named ''Äänislinna'' (or ''Ääneslinna''). The city was retaken by the [[Soviet Army|Soviet army]] on 28 June 1944. The Finns set up [[concentration camp]]s in the city for people of non-[[Baltic Finns|Finnic]] ethnicity. In total, six camps were set up; which in total held 23,984 civilians of [[Russians|Russian ethnicity]]. 
 
 In 1977, the [[Petrozavodsk phenomenon]] occurred. This was an incident where a glowing object showered the city with many [[Ray (optics)|rays]]. Many people now believe that it was caused by the launch of the Soviet [[Satellite (artificial)|satellite]] [[Kosmos 955]]. < ref name= " sn " > {{cite journal |title= Soviet UFO due to secret launch|journal=[[Science News]] |volume=112 |date=October 8, 1977}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[Petrozavodsk State University]] is located in the city. The university is made up of 9 faculties and 6 institutes, and was founded on 1 September 1940. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://petrsu.ru/page/ptoday/svedenia/common |title= Основные сведения :: Петрозаводский государственный университет |publisher=Petrozavodsk State University |date=10 October 2016 |access-date=9 May 2017}} < /ref > 
 
 There are many popular landmarks in Petrozavodsk. One of them is  " Lenin Square, "  which is an oval space with a large [[Vladimir Lenin]] statue in the center. The suburb of ''Martsialnye Vody'' is the oldest [[spa]] in Russia. It was founded in 1714 by [[Peter the Great]]. Petrozavodsk is home to the Karelia Philharmonic Orchestra, < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.marius-stravinsky.com/index.php?option=com_content & task=view & id=6 & Itemid=10 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2017-05-10 |archive-date=2011-07-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714034944/http://www.marius-stravinsky.com/index.php?option=com_content & task=view & id=6 & Itemid=10 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  the Karelian Musical Theater, the National Library of Karelia, the [[National Theatre of Karelia]] and a branch of the [[Russian Academy of Sciences]]. 
 
 == Climate == 
 According to the [[Köppen climate classification]], Petrozavodsk experiences a [[subarctic climate]]. However, the city has much milder temperatures than other cities in that climate. This is mainly because of the city's position next to the [[Baltic Sea]]. The average annual temperature is 3.1 °C. Temperatures in winter range from 0 to -15 °C, in summer from +5 to +25 °C. The record high temperature was 33.9 & nbsp;°C (93.0 & nbsp;°F), and the record low temperature was −41.6 & nbsp;°C (−42.9 & nbsp;°F). < ref > {{cite web |url = http://www.pogodaiklimat.ru/climate/22820.htm |title = Pogoda.ru.net (Weather and Climate-The Climate of Petrozavodsk) |publisher = Weather and Climate |language = ru |access-date = 12 February 2017}} < /ref >
