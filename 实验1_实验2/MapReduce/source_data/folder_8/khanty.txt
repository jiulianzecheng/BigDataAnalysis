 The '''Khanty''', or '''Khants''', < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=The Red Book of the Peoples of the Russian Empire: Khants|url=https://www.eki.ee/books/redbook/khants.shtml|website=www.eki.ee}} < /ref >  are a [[Indigenous peoples|native population]] of [[Russia]]. They used to be called '''Ostyaks'''. They are an [[Ugrian]] people. They live in the [[Khanty-Mansi Autonomous Okrug]] with the [[Mansi people|Mansi]]. In the 2010 Census, < ref > {{Cite web|title=Впн-2010|url=http://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm|access-date=2022-03-10|archive-date=2016-03-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160325042553/http://www.gks.ru/free_doc/new_site/perepis2010/croc/perepis_itogi1612.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  30,943 people said they were Khanty. 
 
 == History == 
 In the [[2nd millennium BC|second millennium BC]], a population of people lived in land in the [[Kama (river)|Kama]] and the [[Irtysh River|Irtysh]] rivers. They spoke a [[Proto-Uralic]] language and had contact with [[Proto-Indo-European language|Proto-Indo-European]] speakers from the south. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Khanty, People of the Taiga: Surviving the 20th Century|url=https://archive.org/details/khantypeopleofta0000wige|last1=Wiget |first1=Andrew |last2=Balalaeva|first2=Olga|publisher=University of Alaska Press|year=2011|isbn=978-16022-3125-2|pages=[https://archive.org/details/khantypeopleofta0000wige/page/3 3]}} < /ref >  Some researchers believe this is where the Khanty people came from. Other researchers say that the Khanty people came from the south [[Eurasian Steppe|Ural steppe]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Khanty and Mansi|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Khanty#ref272005|website=Britannica}} < /ref >  They were [[Nomadic people|nomads]], which means they didn't stay in one place for very long. 
 
 Khanty probably appear in Russian records around the [[11th century]], under the name Yugra. Yugra was the word for different Uralic [[Tribe|tribes]], like the Mansis. The first time the Khanty people were mentioned alone was in 1572. They were called the Ostyaks. 
 
 == Contact with Russians and Tatars == 
 The Russians and [[Tatars]] [[Colonization|colonized]] the Khanty. Khanty children were taken as [[Hostage|hostages]] and [[Religious conversion|converted to Christianity]]. Khanty people and other indigenous peoples were punished when they did not follow church rules. Their [[Idol|idols]] were burned, but some were secretly made again and hidden. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite journal|last=Balzer|first=Marjorie Mandelstam|date=1983|title=Ethnicity without Power: The Siberian Khanty in Soviet Society|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/2497372|journal=Slavic Review|volume=42|issue=4|pages=633–648|doi=10.2307/2497372|jstor=2497372 |s2cid=155219886 |issn=0037-6779}} < /ref > 
 
 Some Khanty and other indigenous Siberian people fled into the [[tundra]]. The [[Settler|settlers]] were interested in the southern areas, which they could use for [[mining]] and [[farming]]. Some settlers married Khanty people and learned their language. < ref name= " :1 "  / > 
 
 Khanty [[Shamanism|shamans]], or spiritual leaders, were not treated well. Their burial grounds were destroyed, and Khanty children were forced to go to [[Boarding school|boarding schools]]. The Khanty resisted this, and elders led the [[Kazym rebellion]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  The [[Red Army]] beat the Khantys, burning their villages. Any Khanty who took part in traditional funeral [[Rites of passage|rites]] could be put in [[prison]], and bear hunting was no longer allowed. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref name= " :1 "  / >

 === Ways of life === 
 [[Custom|Traditionally]], the Khantys [[Fishery|fished]], hunted in the [[taiga]], and [[Herding|herded]] [[reindeer]]. They lived as [[Animal trapping|trappers]]. < ref name= " :2 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nzhq85nPrdsC|title=A History of the Peoples of Siberia: Russia's North Asian Colony 1581-1990|last=Forsyth|first=James|date=1994-09-08|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=978-0-521-47771-0|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 During the winter, the Khanty lived in huts made out of earth and branches. During the spring, they moved towards hunting and fishing grounds, where they made [[Tent|tents]] out of [[Birch bark|birch-bark]] and poles, called [[Chum (tent)|chums]]. These were for temporary shelter and could be moved, but the huts in the winter were stationary, meaning they did not move. < ref name= " :2 "  / >   
 
 The Khanty used [[Longbow|longbows]], [[Arrow|arrows]] and [[Spear|spears]] as [[Weapon|weapons]], and iron [[Helmet|helmets]] and [[chain mail]] as [[armour]]. < ref name= " :2 "  / >

 == Language == 
 The Khanty speak the [[Khanty language]], an [[Uralic languages|Uralic language]]. The Khanty language is related to [[Mansi language|Mansi]] and [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]].
