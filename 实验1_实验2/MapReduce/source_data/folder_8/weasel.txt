 '''Weasels''' are the [[genus]] '''''Mustela''''', part of the [[Mustelidae]] [[Family (biology)|family]]. The genus includes the weasels, [[polecat]]s, [[stoat]]s, [[ferret]]s and [[mink]]s.  
 
 Weasels are small, active [[predators]], long and slender with short legs. Their body shape is [[adapted]] to going down [[burrow]]s after [[prey]] such as [[rabbits]]. There are 17 species, and they live all over the world except for [[Antarctica]] and [[Australasia]]. 
 
 Weasels vary in length from 173 to 217 mm (6.8 to 8.5 in). < ref name=Weasel > {{cite web|url=http://www.mammal.org.uk/species-factsheets/Weasel|title=The Weasel|publisher=The Mammal Society|accessdate=18 January 2013|archive-date=2 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130202195359/http://www.mammal.org.uk/species-factsheets/Weasel|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  They have red or brown upper coats, white underneath. Their tails may be from 34 to 52 mm (1.3 to 2.0 in) long. < ref name=Weasel/ >   
 
 Biologically, despite looking a bit like [[Felidae|cats]], they are actually more [[suborder|related]] to  [[dogs]].
