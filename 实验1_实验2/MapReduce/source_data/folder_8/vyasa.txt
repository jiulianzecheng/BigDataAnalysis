 '''Vyasa''' ({{lang-sa|व्यास}}, {{IAST|vyāsa}}, literally “Compiler”) is an important figure in most [[Hinduism|Hindu]] [[tradition]]s. He is sometimes called '''Veda Vyāsa''' (वेदव्यास, ''veda-vyāsa'',  " the one who [[wikt:classification|classified]] the [[Vedas]] " ) or '''Krishna Dvaipāyana''' (referring to his complexion and birthplace). He is the author of the ''[[Mahabharata]]''. He is also a [[character (persona)|character]] in it. He is considered to be the [[scribe]] of both the [[Vedas]] and [[Puranas]]. According to Hindu beliefs, Vyasa is an [[avatar]] of the [[Deity|god]] [[Vishnu]]. < ref > Bhagavata Purana 1.4.14: (dvāpare samanuprāpte tṛtīye yuga-paryaye jātaḥ parāśarād yogī vāsavyāḿ kalayā hareḥ) < /ref > < ref > [[iarchive:mahabharata-kamala-subramaniam/mode/2up|Mahābhārata Kamala Subramaniam full edition]] < /ref >  Vyasa is also considered to be one of the seven [[Chiranjivins]] (long lived, or immortals), who are still in existence according to Hindu belief.

 ==In the Mahabharata== 
 Vyasa appears for the first time as the [[wikt:compile|compile]]r of, and an important character in, the ''[[Mahabharata]]''. It is said that he was the expansion of the god [[Vishnu]] who came in [[Dwaparayuga]] to make all the Vedic [[knowledge]] available in [[writing]] which was available in only in [[Spoken language|spoken form]] at that time.  He was the son of [[Satyavati]], daughter of the fisherman Dusharaj, < ref > According to legend, Vyasa was the son of the ascetic Parashara and the dasyu) Satyavati and grew up in forests, living with hermits who taught him the Vedas. ''Encyclopædia Britannica'' < /ref >  and the wandering sage [[Parashara]]. He is credited with being the author of the first Purana, [[Vishnu Purana]]). Vyasa was born on an [[island]] in the [[Yamuna|Yamuna river]]. < ref > [https://books.google.com/books?id=KnCxH85Vra4C & dq=vyasa+born+yamuna & pg=PA205 Essays on the Mahābhārata], Arvind Sharma, Motilal Banarsidass Publisher, p. 205 < /ref >  There are two different views regarding the place of his birth. One of the views suggests that he was born in the [[Tanahun]] district in western [[Nepal]]. Another view suggests that he was born on Island in Yamuna river near Kalpi, [[Uttar Pradesh]], [[India]]. Vyasa had a dark [[complexion]] and may be called by the name ''Krishna'' (black), and also the name ''Dwaipayana'', meaning 'island-born'.
