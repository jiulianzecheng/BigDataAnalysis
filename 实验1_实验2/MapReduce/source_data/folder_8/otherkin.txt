
 '''Otherkin''' is a [[subculture]] where people identify as a non-human thing. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/137242792|title=A field guide to Otherkin|last=Lupa|date=2007|publisher=Megalithica Books|isbn=978-1-905713-07-3|edition=|location=Stafford, England|oclc=137242792}} < /ref >  Some otherkin believe their identity comes from a non-human spirit, ancestry < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/154697037|title=Vampires in their own words : an anthology of vampire voices|last=Belanger|first=Michelle A.|date=2007|publisher=Llewellyn|isbn=978-0-7387-1220-8|location=Woodbury, Minn.|oclc=154697037}} < /ref > , or symbolism. People also believe that Otherkin get their identity from [[neurodivergence]]. 
 The term has since been widespread, and it usually refers to many non-human identities, including therians. 
 
 Otherkin originally came from [[Internet|Online Communities]] in the [[1990s]] when people started identifying as [[elves]] or other fantasy creatures.
