 '''Meze''' or '''mezze'''  'to [[taste]]', < ref > Oxford English Dictionary, Draft Revision Dec. 2008 < /ref > < ref > Merriam-Webster Online - [http://www.m-w.com/dictionary/meze ''definition of meze''] < /ref > < ref > ([[Arabic language|Arabic]], '''مَزة''', [[Greek language|Greek]] ''mezé'' (μεζέ), [[Bulgarian language|Bulgarian]] мезé ''mezé'', [[Macedonian language|Macedonian]] мéзе ''méze'', from [[Turkish language|Turkish]] ''meze'', all from [[Persian language|Persian]] ''maze'' (مزه) 'taste, snack'  <  ''mazīdan'' < /ref >  is the name for a number of small dishes or appetizers served before a [[meal]], often with a drink. The food is common in the [[Eastern Mediterranean]]. Drinks include [[anise]]-flavored liqueurs, such as [[Arak (distilled beverage)|''arak'']], ''[[ouzo]]'', ''[[Raki (alcoholic beverage)|raki]]'' or different [[wine]]s.  These dishes are similar to the ''[[tapas]]'' of [[Spain]] or [[finger food]]. 
 
 In [[Levant|Levantine]] [[cuisine]]s and in the [[Caucasus]] region (especially in [[Armenia]] and [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]]), these dishes are served as a part of any large-scale meal. < ref > Alan Davidson, ''The Oxford Companion to Food'', Oxford University Press, 1999, pp. 500-501. < /ref >  Not accompanied by alcohol, meze is known in the [[Arab world]] as ''muqabbilat'' ([[Arabic language|Arabic]] for  " starters " ).
