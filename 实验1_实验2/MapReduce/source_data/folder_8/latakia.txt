 '''Latakia''' ({{lang-ar|اللَاذِقِيَّة}} ''al-Lādhiqīyah''), is the main port city of [[Syria]]. It is the 4th largest city in Syria after [[Aleppo]], [[Damascus]] and [[Homs]] with 383,786 < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.cbssyr.org/new%20web%20site/General_census/census_2004/NH/TAB06-1-2004.htm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2012-07-14 |archive-date=2013-03-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130317032446/http://www.cbssyr.org/new%20web%20site/General_census/census_2004/NH/TAB06-1-2004.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  inhabitants.  It is the capital of the [[Latakia Governorate]]. [[Tishreen University]] is in the city. 
 
 Latakia is the home city of two football clubs: [[Teshrin|Teshrin Sports Club]] was founded in 1947, < ref > [http://www.weltfussballarchiv.com/Vereinsprofilnew.php?ID=22061 Teshrin SC] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131007193208/http://www.weltfussballarchiv.com/Vereinsprofilnew.php?ID=22061 |date=2013-10-07 }} Welt Fussball Archive. < /ref >  and [[Hutteen (Latakia)|Hutteen Sports Club]] was founded in 1945. < ref > [http://www.weltfussballarchiv.com/Vereinsprofilnew.php?ID=22062 Al-Hutteen SC] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131007192800/http://www.weltfussballarchiv.com/Vereinsprofilnew.php?ID=22062 |date=2013-10-07 }} Welt Fussball Archive. < /ref >  Both teams play in the [[al-Assad Stadium]]. There is also [[Latakia Sports City]] complex.  It was built in 1987 to host the [[1987 Mediterranean Games]].
