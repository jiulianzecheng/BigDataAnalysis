 The '''Quapaw''' people are a [[tribe]] of [[Native Americans in the United States|Native Americans]]. From the [[17th century]] they resided on the [[west]] side of the [[Mississippi River]] in what is now the [[States of the United States|state]] of [[Arkansas]]. The [[territory]] and state was named for them, as Europeans first learned their name as the ''Arkansea''. This was the term used by the [[Algonquian languages|Algonquian]]-speaking [[Illiniwek|Illini people]]. They are among the [[Siouan languages|Siouan]]-speaking peoples west of the Mississippi. 
 
 The Quapaw are [[federally recognized tribes|federally recognized]] as the  " Quapaw Tribe of Indians " . Since [[Indian Removal Act|removal]] in 1834, their tribal base has been in present-day [[Oklahoma]].
