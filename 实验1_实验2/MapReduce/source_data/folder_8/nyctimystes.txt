 '''''Nyctimystes''''' is a [[genus]] of tree [[frog]]s in the family [[Pelodryadidae]]. Most of them are from [[Papua New Guinea]] but some live on the [[Moluccas]] islands.  Some scientists think ''Nyctimystes'' should be considered part of ''[[Litoria]]'', another genus of frogs from New Guinea.  Other scientists think that it is right to say ''Nyctimystes'' is its own genus. < ref name= " Notes " > {{cite journal|title=Notes on tree frogs, Nyctimystes species (Anura: Hylidae) of New Guinea; the Nyctimystes papua species group|author1=James I.  All frogs in ''Nyctimystes'' have marks on their lower eyelids: lines, veins, or dots. Scientists think this helps the frog [[camouflage|harder to see]] so it can hide from larger animals that want to eat it. < ref name= " Kuduki " > {{cite journal

 These frogs live in [[tropical]] or [[subtropical]] [[rainforest]]s on mountains. Their eggs are large and scientists have seen many of them laying eggs on underwater objects in fast-flowing creeks and streams, enough that they think all ''Nyctimystes'' frogs do this. The tadpoles have large sucker-mouths and streamlined bodies with large, muscular tails. All ''Nyctimystes frogs'' have webbed feet and large discs on their toes for climbing. 
 
 Scientists say most ''Nyctimystes'' frogs became separate species during the [[Miocene]] era, about 10 to 22 million years ago. < ref name= " Zootaxa " > {{cite journal|url=http://www.cnah.org/pdf/88524.pdf|page=51|journal=Zootaxa|date= April 19, 2016|issn=1175-5326|publisher=Magnolia Press|volume=4104|issue=1|doi=10.11646/zootaxa.4104.1.1|title=Phylogenetics, classification, and biogeography of the treefrogs (Amphibia: Anura: Arboranae)|author1=William E. Duellman|author2=Angela B. Marion|author3=S. Blair Hedges|pmid=27394762|access-date=July 10, 2020}} < /ref > 
 
 The name comes from the [[Greek (language)|Greek]] ''nycktos'' for  " night "  and ''myst'' meaning  " mist. "  Together they refer to  " mysteries of the night. " < ref name= " Zootaxa "  / >
