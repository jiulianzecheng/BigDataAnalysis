 '''Senigallia''' is a ''[[comune]]'' and [[seaport]] in [[Italy]]. It is on the [[Adriatic Sea|Adriatic]] [[coastline]], in the [[province of Ancona]], in the [[Marche]] [[Regions of Italy|region]]. It is the second city of the province, after the [[Capital city|capital]], by the number of people living there. 
 
 It is an important [[seaside resort]] in the Marche region, and is visited every year by many person. Its beach is known as ''Spiaggia di Velluto'' ([[Velvet]] Beach). 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Senigallia is on the [[Adriatic Sea]], at the mouth of the [[Misa (river in Italy)|Misa]] river. It has an area of {{Convert|117.77|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its [[altitude]] is {{Convert|5|m|ft|abbr=on}}. 
 
 The ''comune'' borders with the following ''comuni''ː [[Belvedere Ostrense]], [[Mondolfo]] ([[Province of Pesaro and Urbino]]), [[Monte San Vito]], [[Montemarciano]], [[Morro d'Alba]], [[Ostra, Marche|Ostra]], [[Trecastelli]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.comuni-italiani.it/042/045/index.html |title=Comune di Senigallia |publisher=Comuni-Italiani.it |language=Italian |accessdate=20 June 2016}} < /ref >

 The people from this ''comune'' are known, in [[Italian language|Italian]], as ''senigalliesi''. 
 
 ==Main sights== 
 Despite its ancient origin the city presents a modern appearance, with wide streets. Attractions include: 
 *'''''Palazzo Comunale''''', a [[palace]] from the 17th century. Now is called ''Palazzo di Governo'' because the offices of the government of the ''comune'' are here. It is in the ''Piazza Roma''. 
 *'''''Rocca Roveresca''''' - castle of [[Gothic]] origin, was restored by [[Baccio Pontelli]] in 1492. It has a square plan with four large round tower. 
 *The '''Cathedral of Senigallia''' (''Cattedrale di San Pietro Apostolo''), a [[cathedral]] built between  1762 and 1790. 
 *[[Santa Maria delle Grazie, Senigallia|'''''Santa Maria delle Grazie''''']] church - one of the only two churches attributed to [[Baccio Pontelli]] (the other is at [[Orciano di Pesaro]], [[province of Pesaro and Urbino]], about {{convert|20|km|0|abbr=off}} to the west by road). It once housed the painting of ''[[Madonna di Senigallia]]'' by [[Piero della Francesca]]; it is now in the ''Galleria Nazionale delle Marche'' in [[Urbino]], [[Province of Pesaro and Urbino]]. 
 *[[Chiesa della Croce, Senigallia|'''''Chiesa della Croce''''']] or ''Church of the Cross'' . < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.comune.senigallia.an.it/site/senigallia/live/taxonomy/senigallia/infocitta/monumenti/chiesa-.della-croce.html |title=Comune di Senigallia |publisher=Comune di Senigalliaː Monumenti |language=Italian |accessdate=20 June 2016 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 *'''''Rotonda a mare''''' . < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.comune.senigallia.an.it/site/senigallia/live/taxonomy/senigallia/infocitta/la_rotonda/index.html |title=Comune di Senigallia |publisher=Comune di Senigalliaː La Rotonda a Mare |language=Italian |accessdate=20 June 2016 |archive-date=20 May 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160520161508/http://www.comune.senigallia.an.it/site/senigallia/live/taxonomy/senigallia/infocitta/la_rotonda/index.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
