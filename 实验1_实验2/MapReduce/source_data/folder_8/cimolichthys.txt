[[File:Cimolichthys nepaholica.png|thumb|250x250px|Restoration of ''Cimolichthys nepaholica''.]] 
 '''''Cimolichthys''''' is an extinct [[genus]] of prehistoric [[bony fish]]. It lived during the [[Upper Cretaceous]]. 
 
 == Species == 
 
 * ''Cimolichthys anceps''  < small > Cope, 1872 < /small > 
 * ''Cimolichthys nepaholica''  < small > Cope, 1872 < /small > 
 * ''Cimolichthys gladiolus''  < small > Cope, 1872 < /small > 
 * ''Cimolichthys levesiensis''  < small > Leidy, 1857 < /small > 
 * ''Cimolichthys semianceps''  < small > Cope, 1872 < /small > 
 * ''Cimolichthys sulcatus''  < small > Cope, 1872 < /small > 
 
 == Description == 
 Their bodies were covered by large, heavy [[Scute|scutes]]. Typical of this species are narrow lower jaws with several series of teeth. If ''Cimolichthys'' existed, it would look like the [[Esox|pikes]] we see in freshwater [[Lake|lakes]]. 
 
 == Distribution == 
 [[File:Cimolichthys nepaholica DMNHS54M 01.jpg|thumb|Specimen of ''Cimolichthys nepaholica'']] 
 You can find ''Cimolichthys'' fossils in [[Cretaceous]] strata of [[North America]] ([[Canada]] and [[United States]]) and [[Europe]].
