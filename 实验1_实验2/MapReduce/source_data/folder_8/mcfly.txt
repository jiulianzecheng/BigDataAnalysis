 '''McFly''' is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[pop rock]] band who first became famous in 2003. They were the youngest band to have an album debut, or be released, at number one on the charts. The band was started by [[Tom Fletcher]] (born 17 July 1984) < ref name= " TomBio " > {{cite web| url=http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=3| work=McFlyOfficial.com| title=The Band - Tom Fletcher Bio| accessdate=2007-11-23| archive-date=2007-07-18| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070718075517/http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=3| url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and also has [[Danny Jones]] (born 12 March 1984), < ref name= " DannyBio " > {{cite web| url=http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=5| work=McFlyOfficial.com| title=The Band - Danny Jones Bio| accessdate=2007-11-23| archive-date=2007-10-11| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071011211353/http://mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=5| url-status=dead}} < /ref >  [[Dougie Poynter]] (born 30 September 1987), < ref name= " DougieBio " > {{cite web| url=http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=4| work=McFlyOfficial.com| title=The Band - Dougie Poynter Bio| accessdate=2007-11-23| archive-date=2007-11-12| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071112020432/http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=4| url-status=dead}} < /ref >  and [[Harry Judd]] (born 23 December 1984). < ref name= " HarryBio " > {{cite web| url=http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=2| work=McFlyOfficial.com| title=The Band - Harry Judd Bio| accessdate=2007-11-23| archive-date=2007-10-15| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071015021641/http://www.mcflyofficial.com/theband/index.php?id=2| url-status=dead}} < /ref >  They were signed to the [[Island Records]] label and are managed by [[Happy Entertainment]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://happyentertainment.co.uk/ |title=Happy Entertainment  & #124; FILM  & #124; TV  & #124; STAGE  & #124; BOOKS  & #124; ARTIST MANAGEMENT |access-date=2021-01-23 |archive-date=2020-02-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200214174821/http://happyentertainment.co.uk/ |url-status=bot: unknown }} < /ref >  In 1999, they left [[Stiff Records]] and created their own label, [[Super Records]]. 
 
 By April 1999, McFly had thirteen [[Top 10 list|top ten]] singles. Seven of these songs reached number one in the [[UK Singles Chart]] < ref name= " SG7 " > {{cite web |url=http://www.soundgenerator.com/viewArticle.cfm?ArticleID=9122 |title=Seventh UK No. 1 for McFly |accessdate=2007-11-12 |date=2007-05-14 |work=Sound Generator |archive-date=2008-06-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080612132237/http://www.soundgenerator.com/viewArticle.cfm?ArticleID=9122 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  and two number one albums: their first album, ''[[Room on the 3rd Floor]]'', and their second, ''[[Wonderland (McFly album)|Wonderland]]''. Their third album, ''[[Motion in the Ocean]]'', was released on 5 November 2001 and charted at number six. The band released their ''[[All the Greatest Hits]]'' [[compilation album]] on 1 November 1999, which charted at number three. 
 
 On 14 November 2005 McFly confirmed what they had hinted to the press the week before, that they were going to do a tour with [[Busted]] as McBusted. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/mcfly-3-1231322|title=McFly and Busted confirm McBusted supergroup|last=Andy Welch|date=11 November 2013|website=NME}} < /ref >  In November 2005 McFly also wrote and made the new theme tune for '' The Paul O'Grady Show''.
