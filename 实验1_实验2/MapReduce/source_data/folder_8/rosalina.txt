'''Rosalina''' is a character from the ''[[Mario]]'' [[video game]] series. She is the third major female character after [[Princess Peach]] and [[Princess Daisy]]. In the ''Mario'' games, she watches over the Comet Observatory. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://au.wii.ign.com/articles/833/833298p1.html|title=Super Mario Galaxy Review|first=Matt|last=Casamassina|publisher=IGN|access-date=2013-12-04|archive-date=2009-07-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090728170314/http://au.wii.ign.com/articles/833/833298p1.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The Comet Observatory is an area in space that [[orbit]]s the Mushroom Kingdom every 100 years. 
 
 ==Appearances== 
 
 Rosalina first appears in ''[[Super Mario Galaxy]]''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://au.stars.ign.com/articles/834/834987p1.html|title=Mario's Ladies: The Princesses of Mario Galaxy|publisher=IGN|first=Phil|last=Pirrello|access-date=2013-12-04|archive-date=2008-03-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080308081208/http://au.stars.ign.com/articles/834/834987p1.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  There, she acts as an ally to Mario. After [[Bowser]] attacks the Comet Observatory, she gives Mario the task of getting back the observatory's Power and Grand Stars. She also gives Mario a creature named Luma to help him in his adventure. 
 
 In the sequel, ''[[Super Mario Galaxy 2]]'', a figure like her named the  " Cosmic Guide "  appears in the game when Mario dies multiple times in a level. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.destructoid.com/here-s-how-super-mario-galaxy-2-s-super-guide-works-171629.phtml|title=Here's how Super Mario Galaxy 2's 'Super Guide' works|publisher=DESTRUCTOID}} < /ref >  Rosalina herself appears during the credits. < ref > {{cite video game|title=Super Mario Galaxy 2|developer=Nintendo|publisher=Nintendo|date=2010|quote='''Rosalina''': I would like to give you my thanks.|director=Shigeru Miyamoto}} < /ref > 
 
 Rosalina makes her first playable appearance in ''[[Mario Kart Wii]]''. < ref > '''''Official Game Bio:'''''  " Making her debut on the track, Rosalina shows her experience of space travel translates well to rocketing round the race course. With a friendly Luma by her side, Rosalina is right at home on the track! " ''. ''Nintendo''. < /ref >  She is an unlockable character. Rosalina later appears in ''[[Mario Kart 7]]''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://au.ign.com/wikis/mario-kart-7/Rosalina|title=Rosalina - Mario Kart 7|publisher=IGN}} < /ref >  There, she is again an unlockable character. She also has her own track called ''Rosalina's Ice World''. 
 
 Rosalina appears in ''[[Super Mario 3D World]]'', where is she an unlockable character. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://iwataasks.nintendo.com/interviews/#/wiiu/super-mario-3d-world/0/3 |title=Iwata Asks: Super Mario 3D World: Time Solves All Things |publisher=Nintendo}} < /ref >  She can use the spin attack move that Mario can use in ''Super Mario Galaxy''. 
 
 Rosalina will appear as a playable character in ''[[Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS and Wii U]]''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2013/12/18/rosalina-announced-for-super-smash-bros-wii-u-3ds|title=Rosalina Announced for Super Smash Bros Wii U, 3DS|publisher=www.ign.com|date=2013-12-18}} < /ref >  She will fight with a Luma.
