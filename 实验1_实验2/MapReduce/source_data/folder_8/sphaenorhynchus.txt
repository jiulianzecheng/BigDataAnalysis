 '''''Sphaenorhynchus'''''  is a genus of [[frog]]s. They are in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=Frost/ > < ref name=AmphibiaWeb/ >  They are the '''lime treefrogs''' < ref name=Frost/ >  or '''hatchet-faced treefrogs'''. < ref name= " Araujo-Vieira et al. 2015 " / >  They live in the places where water goes into the [[Amazon basin|Amazon]] and [[Orinoco]] Rivers in South America. They live in the [[Guianas]], [[Trinidad]], and [[Brazil]]. < ref name=Frost/ >  Most of them live in Brazil in the [[Atlantic Forest]]. < ref name= " Roberto et al. 2017 " / > 
 
 ==Systematics== 
 Scientists think the frogs in ''Sphaenorhynchus'' are [[sister taxon|closely related]] to the frogs in  ''[[Scarthyla]]'' and  ''[[Scinax]]''. < ref name=Frost/ >  Faivovich and other scientists (2005) put ''Sphaenorhynchus'' in the [[Tribe (biology)|group]] Dendropsophini. ''[[Dendropsophus]]'', ''[[Lysapsus]]'', ''[[Pseudis]]'', ''Scarthyla'', ''Scinax'', and ''[[Xenohyla]]'' are in Dendropsophini too. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / > 
 
 ==Appearance== 
 ''Sphaenorhynchus'' are small and middle frogs in size. They are bright green or yellow green in color. Their faces come together to a point. Most frogs have skin flaps on each side of the [[anus|rear end]]. There is some webbed skin on the front feet and more webbed skin on the back feet. Male frogs have a [[vocal sac]] on their necks so they can make sounds. < ref name= " Goin 1957 " / >
