 '''Shalom''' (שלום) is a [[Hebrew]] word which means  " [[peace]] " . < ref > {{cite web | last=Hershey | first=Doug | title=The True Meaning of Shalom // Defining Shalom | website=FIRM | date=3 January 2020 | url=https://firm.org.il/learn/the-meaning-of-shalom/ | access-date=16 February 2021}} < /ref >  It is also used as a method of greeting like  " [[hello]] "  and  " [[goodbye]] " . 
 
 Similar to its use in [[English language|English]], it can mean peace between two [[person]]s or [[entities]]. It also means inner peace, being calm or the quietness of a person, [[well-being]], [[welfare]] or [[safety]] of an individual or a group.  
 
 The root of the word  " shalom "  can be related to  " ''shalem'' "  which means  " wholeness " , < ref > {{cite web | last=Allen | first=Leslie | title=Shalom As Wholeness: Some Biblical Implications | website=Fuller Studio | date=19 September 2017 | url=https://fullerstudio.fuller.edu/shalom-as-wholeness-embracing-the-broad-biblical-message/ | access-date=16 February 2021}} < /ref >  or to ''le-shalem'' which means  " to complete, to reward, to pay, to compensate " . < ref > {{cite web | title=Origins of shalom | website=The Dayton Jewish Observer | date=26 August 2014 | url=https://daytonjewishobserver.org/2014/08/origins-of-shalom/ | access-date=16 February 2021}} < /ref >  This means that it is more than the absence of conflict or struggle or the breaking of unfriendliness: It also means a return to the balance, to justice, and to the complete equality. 
 
 In general, one uses the word  " shalom "  as the short form of the sentence  " [[Shalom Aleichem]] "  (exactly   " Peace is with you " ) that can be translated as  " good day (morning, afternoon, high noon,...) "  (a greeting).{{citation needed|date=February 2021}} This is very similar to the [[Arabic language|Arabic]] phrase  " Salam Alaykum "  (سلام عليكم) which is again a greeting meaning  " Peace be with you " .
