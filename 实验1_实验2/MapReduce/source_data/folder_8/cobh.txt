 '''Cobh''', known from 1849 until 1920 as '''Queenstown''', is a [[tourism|tourist]] seaport [[town]] on the south coast of [[County Cork]], [[Republic of Ireland|Ireland]]. 
 
 Cobh the last port visited by the [[RMS Titanic|RMS ''Titanic'']]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Remarkable Facts about Cobh|publisher=Irish Central|url=https://www.irishcentral.com/travel/ten-remarkable-facts-about-cobh-where-25-million-irish-left-for-america-photos|access-date=March 31, 2019}} < /ref > 
 
 After the [[RMS Lusitania|RMS ''Lusitania'']] sank in May 1915, its passengers were taken to Cobh. 
 
 ==Name== 
 The port had several [[Irish language]] names. It was first called  " Cove "  in 1750. It was renamed  " Queenstown "  in 1849 to honor a visit by [[Queen Victoria]]. The name was later renamed again, this time to  " Cobh " . < ref > {{cite book|title=Cobh|publisher=Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/place/Cobh|access-date=March 31, 2019}} < /ref >
