'''Ilayaraaja''' is a famous film [[composer]] and singer in South Indian cinema especially in Tamil cinema. Ilaiyaraaja is also an instrumentalist, [[Conducting|conductor]], singer, and [[songwriter]]. He has composed more than 6000 songs and provided film music or movie soundtrack for more than 1000 films, particularly being famous for his background music. < ref name= " Baskaran2009 " > {{cite book|last=Baskaran |first=Sundararaj Theodore |title=History through the lens: perspectives on South Indian cinema |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fNgqAQAAIAAJ |access-date=1 September 2013 |date=1 January 2009 |publisher=Orient Blackswan |isbn=978-81-250-3520-6 |page=82 }} < /ref > < ref name= " Das2010 " > {{cite book|author=Emmanuel Anthony Das |title=The Best is Yet to Be |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=05oSduutr-IC & pg=RA2-PA7 |access-date=1 July 2012 |date=1 September 2010 |publisher=Pustak Mahal |isbn=978-81-223-1144-0 |page=7 }} < /ref > < ref name= " musical_man " > S. Theodore Baskaran [http://www.outlookindia.com/article.aspx?231674  " Jnana To Gana: Consistent eclecticism has kept Tamil film music virile " ] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101116115029/http://outlookindia.com/article.aspx?231674 |date=16 November 2010 }}. Outlookindia.com, 26 June 2006. < /ref > 
 
 Ilayaraaja introduced Western type of music into the Tamil cinema.He is also known for creating music by fusing [[Orchestration|symphonic orchestration]] with traditional [[Indian instruments|Indian instrumentation]].In 1993 he organised a [[List of symphony composers|full symphony]] performed by the [[Royal Philharmonic Orchestra]] in London and thus became the First Indian to compose a full symphony, < ref > {{cite web|url=https://rajasmusic.wordpress.com/2013/12/11/a-symphony-of-success-ilaiyaraaja-interview-in-1993/ |title=A SYMPHONY OF SUCCESS & nbsp;– Ilaiyaraaja Interview in 1993 |date=2013-12-11 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303173116/https://rajasmusic.wordpress.com/2013/12/11/a-symphony-of-success-ilaiyaraaja-interview-in-1993/ |archive-date= 3 March 2016 }} < /ref >  with [[Ravi Shankar]] being the only other Indian to do so. He is also the first Asian to compose a full symphony performed by the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0006137/bio |title=Ilaiyaraaja & nbsp;— IMDb |website=[[IMDb]] |date=2005-05-29 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140521031023/http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0006137/bio |archive-date=21 May 2014 }} < /ref >  He has won five Indian [[National Film Awards (India)|National Film Awards]] (the highest film honor in [[Indian cinema|India]]); three for [[National Film Award for Best Music Direction|Best Music Direction]] and two for Best Background Score. < ref > Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, India. 2006. {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070418174624/http://mib.nic.in/dff/welcome.html |date=18 April 2007 |title=Directorate of Film Festivals }}. Archived from [http://mib.nic.in/dff/welcome.html the original] on 18 April 2007. Accessed 2006-11-22. < /ref >  In 2010 he has received [[Padma Bhushan]], awarded by the [[Government of India]]. 
 He is usually referred to by the title '''Isaignani''' ({{lang-en|'''Musical Genius'''}}), or as ''The Maestro''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://tamilnadu.com/personalities/maestro-ilaiyaraaja.html|title=Ilaiyaraaja|publisher=Tamilnadu.com|date=30 October 2012|access-date=30 October 2017|archive-date=11 April 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130411214547/http://tamilnadu.com/personalities/maestro-ilaiyaraaja.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Ilaiyaraja is a gold medalist in classical guitar from [[Trinity College of Music]], London. In 2003, according to an international poll conducted by [[BBC]], more than half-a million people from 165 countries voted his composition ''[[Rakkamma Kaiya Thattu]]'' from the 1991 film ''[[Thalapathi]]'' as fourth in the world's top 10 most popular songs of all time. < ref > [http://www.bbc.co.uk/worldservice/us/features/topten/ THE WORLD'S TOP TEN] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150330073900/http://www.bbc.co.uk/worldservice/us/features/topten/ |date=30 March 2015 }}, BBC World Service.com < /ref > 
 
 In 2015, he was also honoured with the Centenary Award for lifetime achievement at the 46th [[International Film Festival of India]] (IFFI) at [[Panaji]], [[Goa]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/iffi-2015-ilaiyaraja-felicitated-with-centenary-award-for-lifetime-achievement/article7900519.ece |title=Ilaiyaraja honoured with IFFI Centenary Award |work=[[The Hindu]] |date=2015-11-20 |access-date=2015-11-20 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160117184421/http://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/iffi-2015-ilaiyaraja-felicitated-with-centenary-award-for-lifetime-achievement/article7900519.ece |archive-date=17 January 2016 }} < /ref >  On December 31, 2015, he was nominated by the Kerala Government for Nishagandi Puraskaram, the top award conferred by the Kerala Government's Tourism Ministry, for his artistic excellence and contributions to the Indian film industry. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/ilayaraja-nominated-for-top-kerala-award/ |title=Ilayaraja nominated for top Kerala award |publisher=The Indian Express |access-date=2016-01-01 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160104155537/http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/ilayaraja-nominated-for-top-kerala-award/ |archive-date= 4 January 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 In a poll conducted by [[CNN-IBN]] celebrating 100 years of Indian cinema in 2013, Ilaiyaraaja was voted as the greatest music composer of India with a maximum of 49%. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-03-08/news-interviews/37560912_1_vote-share-greatest-actor-indian-cinema |title=NTR is the greatest Indian actor: Times Of India |work=The Times of India |date=2013-03-08 |access-date=2013-04-12 |archive-date=2013-03-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130331080613/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-03-08/news-interviews/37560912_1_vote-share-greatest-actor-indian-cinema |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
