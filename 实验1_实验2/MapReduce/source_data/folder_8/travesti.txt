 In [[theatre]], '''travesti''' (meaning  " [[wikt:disguise|disguised]] " ) is a [[Character (persona)|character]] performed by a person of the opposite [[gender]]. < ref > Michael Kennedy (2006), ''The Oxford Dictionary of Music'', p 899 < /ref > < ref > John Warrack and Ewan West (1992), ''The Oxford Dictionary of Opera'', p 716 < /ref >  A woman performing as a male in male clothing is called a '''breeches role'''. Famous travesty roles in [[opera]] include Cherubino in ''[[The Marriage of Figaro]]'', Siebel in ''[[Faust]]'', and Octavian in ''[[Der Rosenkavalier]]''. In [[ballet]], the role of Frantz in ''[[Coppélia]]'' was a travesty role at the [[Paris Opéra]] until the 1950s.  Carabosse in [[Tchaikovsky]]'s [[ballet]] ''[[The Sleeping Beauty (ballet)|The Sleeping Beauty]]'' is performed by a man in woman's clothing. The Stepsisters in [[Prokofiev]]'s ballet ''[[Cinderella (ballet)|Cinderella]]'' are sometimes played by men in women's clothing. The title character is a travesty role in the [[Broadway]] musical ''[[Peter Pan]]''.
