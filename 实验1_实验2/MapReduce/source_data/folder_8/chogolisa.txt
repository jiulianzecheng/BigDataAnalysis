 '''Chogolisa''' is a {{convert|7665|m|ft}} [[mountain]] in the [[Karakoram]] [[Mountain range|range]], in [[Pakistan]]. < ref > {{cite peakbagger|pid=10532|name=Chogolisa, Pakistan|accessdate=31 May 2019}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite peakware |id=654 |name=Chogolisa |accessdate=31 May 2019}} < /ref >  It is also known as '''Bride Peak'''. < ref > {{cite summitpost |id=754138 |name=Chogolisa (7668-M), Karakoram, Pakistan |accessdate=31 May 2019}} < /ref >  It is the [[List of highest mountains|36th highest]] mountain in the world. Chogolisa has three distinct peaks. < ref > {{cite web |title=The Seven Thousanders - Photographs and map of Chogolisa / Bride Peak ( 7668m ) in the Karakoram Mountains of Pakistan |url=https://www.caingram.info/Pakistan/htm/Chogolisa.htm |website=caingram.info |access-date=31 May 2019}} < /ref >  The first people to reach the top of the highest peak were Fred Pressl and Gustav Ammerer, in 1975. < ref > {{cite magazine |last=Koblm√ºller |first=Eduard |title=Asia, Pakistan, Chogolisa |work=American Alpine Journal |publisher=American Apline Club |url=http://publications.americanalpineclub.org/articles/12197653701/Asia-Pakistan-Chogolisa |year=1976 |access-date=31 May 2019}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web |title=The Mountains of Pakistan: Chogolisa / Bride Peak 7654m |url=http://www.everestnews.com/stories004sec4002/chogolisa12122003.htm |website=EverestNews.com |access-date=31 May 2019}} < /ref >
