 In [[mathematics]], the '''quaternion''' [[number system]] extends the [[complex number]]s into four dimensions. They were first described by Irish mathematician [[William Rowan Hamilton]] in 1843. < ref > {{cite journal |title=On Quaternions; or on a new System of Imaginaries in Algebra |journal=Letter to John T. Graves |date=17 October 1843}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DRLpAFZM7uwC & pg=PA385 |title=The history of non-euclidean geometry: Evolution of the concept of a geometric space |year=1988 |publisher=Springer |first=Boris Abramovich |last=Rozenfelʹd |page=385 |isbn=9780387964584}} < /ref >  They are often used in [[computer graphics]] to compute 3-dimensional rotations.
