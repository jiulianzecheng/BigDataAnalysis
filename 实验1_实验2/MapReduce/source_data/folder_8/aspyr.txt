'''Aspyr Media, Inc.''' is an [[United States|American]] video [[Game development|game developer]] and publisher founded by Michael Rogers and Ted Staloch in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], [[Texas]]. It was originally founded to bring top gaming titles to macOS. < ref name= " Savlov " > {{Cite web|last1=Savlov|first1=Marc|last2=Fri.|last3=Nov. 19|last4=2004|title=Controlling Their Own Destiny|url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/screens/2004-11-19/238573/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=www.austinchronicle.com|language=en-US}} < /ref >  The company, since 2005, has become a [[Publishing|publisher]] and developer of entertainment for multiple gaming platforms. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Aspyr Media|url=https://www.aspyr.com/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=Aspyr Media|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Aspyr works with partners such as LucasArts, [[2K (company)|2K Games]], [[Activision]], [[Gearbox Software]]; their catalog includes franchises ''Call of Duty'', ''Sid Meier's Civilization'', ''SimCity'', ''Borderlands'' and ''Star Wars''. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ted Staloch Video Game Credits and Biography|url=https://www.mobygames.com/developer/sheet/view/developerId,164205/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=MobyGames}} < /ref > 
 
 Embracer Group bought Aspyr in February 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2021-02-03|title=Embracer Group acquires Aspyr Media for up to $450 million|url=https://venturebeat.com/2021/02/02/embracer-group-acquires-aspyr-media-for-up-to-450-million/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=VentureBeat|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 In 1996, Aspyr Media, Inc., was established by Michael Rogers and Ted Staloch in Austin, Texas. Staloch, who had a background in sales and marketing and Rogers, who worked with TechWorks, noticed that there was not enough games that available for [[macOS]], and they decided to change it. < ref name= " Savlov " / > 
 
 According to Rogers, when naming the company, they “wanted something meaning ‘to aspire and be great’” and also “memorable and unique.” Now the name of company associates with porting video games from Microsoft Windows to macOS, < ref > {{Cite web|title= " MacGameStore Feature: Aspyr Media Interview with Michael Rogers " |url=https://www.macgamestore.com/features/article-3/Aspyr-Media-Interview-with-Michael-Rogers/|url-status=dead|access-date=2021-10-15|archive-date=2021-02-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210208061037/https://www.macgamestore.com/features/article-3/Aspyr-Media-Interview-with-Michael-Rogers}} < /ref >  including popular franchises ''Call of Duty'', ''Sid Meier's Civilization'', ''Star Wars'', ''Borderlands'', and ''SimCity''. 
 
 By 2003, they owned 60 percent of the Mac entertainment market. < ref > {{Citation|title=Bungie Founder Finds Publisher - IGN|url=https://www.ign.com/articles/2004/08/31/bungie-founder-finds-publisher|accessdate=2021-10-15|language=en|first=Hilary GoldsteinUpdated: 17 Jun 2012 5:24 amPosted: 31 Aug 2004 12:54|last=am|date=31 August 2004 }} < /ref > 
 
 In 2005, Aspyr partnered with Alex Seropian of ''Wideload Games'' and released their first originally-published AAA game, ''Stubbs The Zombie'', to macOS, [[Microsoft Windows|Windows]] and [[Xbox]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Aspyr ships Stubbs the Zombie for Mac|url=https://www.macworld.com/article/177878/aspyr-42.html|access-date=2021-10-15|website=Macworld|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2015, Aspyr Media make their platform catalog bigger by releasing an iOS port of ''[[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic]]'' < ref > {{Cite web|last=LeFebvre|first=Rob|date=2014-03-06|title=Use The Force - And A Controller - With KOTOR For iOS, Now On Sale|url=https://www.cultofmac.com/268967/use-force-controller-kotor-ios-now-sale/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=Cult of Mac|language=en-us}} < /ref >  and later, Bioware's classic-action RPG, ''Jade Empire'' - a title they extended to [[Android (operating system)|Android]] as well. < ref > {{Cite web|title=BioWare Action RPG Jade Empire Now Available On iOS|url=https://kotaku.com/bioware-action-rpg-jade-empire-now-available-on-ios-1787481767|access-date=2021-10-15|website=Kotaku|date=6 October 2016 |language=en-us}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2015, Aspyr updated [[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords|Star Wars Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords]] (native widescreen resolutions up to 5K, Steam Workshop, support for controllers, < ref > {{Cite web|title=Steam Community :: STAR WARS™ Knights of the Old Republic™ II: The Sith Lords™ :: Achievements|url=https://steamcommunity.com/stats/208580/achievements|access-date=2021-10-15|website=steamcommunity.com|language=en}} < /ref >  57 achievements, etc.) and also ported it to macOS and [[Linux]]. 
 
 In 2015, Aspyr developed and published ''Fahrenheit: Indigo Prophecy Remastered'', a remaster of a game developed by Quantic Dream. 
 
 Aspyr has published over 190 games, and have added 90 plus members to their team since 1996. 
 
 Embracer Group bought Aspyr in February 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2021-02-03|title=Embracer Group to acquire Aspyr Media|url=https://www.gematsu.com/2021/02/embracer-group-to-acquire-aspyr-media|access-date=2021-10-15|website=Gematsu|language=en-US}} < /ref >
