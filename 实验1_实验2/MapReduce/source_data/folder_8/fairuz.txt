 '''Fairuz''' (born '''Nouhad Haddad''' ({{lang-ar|نهاد حداد}}, [[November 21]], [[1935]]) is a [[Lebanon|Lebanese]] [[singer]]. She is considered among the best singers in [[Arab world]]. She has been performing for over 50 years all over the [[earth|world]]. Her vocal range is [[contralto]]. Her first [[album]] was released in 1952. She has released 98 more albums since then. 
 ==Early Life== 
 [[File:Fayrouz_LB.jpg|alt=Fayrouz_LB|center|thumb|288x288px|Fairuz in 1945]] 
 Nuhad Haddad was born in 1935 in [[Beirut]], [[Lebanon]] to a [[Lebanon|Lebanese]] [[Christian]] [[Maronite Church|Maronite]] family. She was the eldest child in the family. Her father worked as a [[wikt:typesetter|typesetter]] at a [[Printing|print]] shop. < ref > {{Cite news|last=MacFarquhar|first=Neil|date=1999-05-18|title=This Pop Diva Wows 'Em In Arabic|language=en-US|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/05/18/arts/this-pop-diva-wows-em-in-arabic.html|access-date=19 October 2023|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  In 1940's, [[Mohammed Flayfel]], a musician and a teacher at the [[Conservatoire Libanais|Lebanese Conservatory]], discovered her when he was searching different schools for singers to perform in a newly opened [[radio station]]. He attended a show at the school where Nuhad was to sing. He was very impressed with her voice and adviced her family to [[wikt:enroll|enroll]] her in his [[Music school|conservatory]]. Her father allowed her to be enrolled but only on a condition that her brother would also go with her. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Biography of Fairuz|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/fairuz-mn0000163342/biography|access-date=19 October 2023|website=AllMusic|language=en}} < /ref >  Flayfel along with other staff at Lebanese Conservatory trained her for 5 years. She began singing for the [[Palestinian Cause]] and also sang for various Arab [[Capital city|Capitals]] including Beirut. Soon, she was hired at the National Radio Station as a [[Choir (music)|Chorus singer]]. During that time, many singers used [[Stage name|stage names]]. [[Halim al-Rumi|Halim Al-Rumi]], who wrote songs for her, recommended that Nahud Haddad perform under the name '''Fairuz''', means  " [[turquoise]] "  in Arabic, which she accepted. in 1954, she married [[Assi Rahbani]], who was a policeman and a part time composer. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Asmar|first=Sami|title=Fairouz: a Voice, a Star, a Mystery {{!}} Al Jadid|url=https://www.aljadid.com/content/fairouz-voice-star-mystery|access-date=2023-10-19|website=Al Jadid Magazine}} < /ref >
