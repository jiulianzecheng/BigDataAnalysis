'''Aswat''' (Arabic: أصوات) is a [[Palestinian territories|Palestinian]] group for [[lesbian]] [[Human rights|rights]]. [[Rauda Morcos]] is the co-founder. Aswat means  " voices "  in the [[Arabic language]]. < ref name= " womensenews.org " > {{cite web|url=http://womensenews.org/2007/04/lesbian-palestinians-break-social-taboos/|title=Lesbian Palestinians Break Social Taboos|publisher=}} < /ref >  The group started in 2001. < ref name= " auto " > {{cite web|url=https://electronicintifada.net/content/though-small-palestines-queer-movement-has-big-vision/12607|title=Though small, Palestine's queer movement has big vision|first=Maureen Clare|last=Murphy|date=12 July 2013|publisher=}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 Aswat started as a [[forum]] for [[discussion]] on the internet. < ref name= " womensenews.org " / > 
 
 The first group for [[LGBT]] [[Palestinian]]s was the international [[Jerusalem Open House]]. Palestinians started to meet there in 2001. After the [[Oslo Accords]] in 1993, many Palestinian groups were part of [[Israel]]i groups. But later, in the 2000s, many groups decided to be [[Independence|independent]]. In 2002,  " Aswat: Palestinian Gay Women "  started as an independent project with [[Kayan]], a Palestinian [[Feminism|feminist]] group in [[Haifa]]. < ref name= " auto " / > 
 
 In March 2007, the group had its first [[conference]] in Haifa. The meeting had 350 people. The Islamic Movement protested outside the meeting with 30 people. < ref name= " womensenews.org " / > 
 
 The group [[publish]]es Arabic [[literature]] about [[homosexuality]]. They [[published]] five [[newspaper|newsletters]] in Arabic about gays and lesbians, and they published an Arabic [[glossary]] of [[term]]s of [[sexual orientation]]. They also published a book called  " Home and Exile in Queer Experience " . < ref name= " womensenews.org " / >
