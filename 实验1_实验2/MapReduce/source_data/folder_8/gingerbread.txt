 '''Gingerbread''' is a word which describes different sweet food products from soft cakes to a [[ginger biscuit]]. [[ginger root|Ginger]] and [[honey]] (or [[treacle]]) rather than only [[sugar]], are usually the common ingredients to all the types of food product. They probably also have the same history. 
 
 ==Etymology== 
 Before, ''gingerbread'' (from [[Latin]] ''zingiber'', then [[Old French]] ''gingebras'') meant preserved [[ginger]]. < ref name= " Smith " > {{cite news|url=https://www.smithsonianmag.com/arts-culture/a-brief-history-of-gingerbread-50050265/|publisher=Smithsonian Magazine|title=A Brief History of Gingerbread: Whose bright idea was this delicious concoction, anyway?|author=Amanda Fiegl |date=December 24, 2008}} < /ref > < ref name= " PBS " > {{cite web|url=https://www.pbs.org/food/the-history-kitchen/history-gingerbread/|publisher=PBS|accessdate=July 19, 2020|date=December 20, 2013|author=Tori Avey|title=The History of Gingerbread}} < /ref >  After, it was a confection made with [[honey]] and [[spice]]s. < ref name= " PBS "  / > 
 
 ==History== 
 [[Armenians|Armenian]] monk Gregory of Nicopolis (Gregory Makar) ([[Grégoire de Nicopolis]]) brought ginger bread to [[Europe]] in 992. He left [[Nicopolis Pompeii]], in [[Lesser Armenia]] to live in [[Bondaroy]] (France) and stayed seven years. He showed French priests and Christians how to cook gingers. He died in 999 Armenian genocide. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.ville-pithiviers.fr/specialite/specialite.php |title=Le Pithiviers |access-date=2010-10-30 |archive-date=2006-12-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061230220854/http://www.ville-pithiviers.fr/specialite/specialite.php |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.monastere-saintgregoire.net/fr/histoire/presentation-du-monastere.htm |title=Monastère orthodoxe des Saints Grégoire Armeanul et Martin le Seul |access-date=2010-10-30 |archive-date=2013-12-15 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6LsfzPKIW?url=http://www.monastere-saintgregoire.net/fr/histoire/presentation-du-monastere.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.logis-de-france-loiret.com/gastronomie_dans_loiret/confrerie_pain_epices.htm |title=La Confrérie du Pain d'Epices |access-date=2010-10-30 |archive-date=2010-03-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100322220814/http://www.logis-de-france-loiret.com/gastronomie_dans_loiret/confrerie_pain_epices.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 [[Germany|German]] immigrants brought it to [[Sweden]] during the 13th century. Swedish [[nun]]s baked gingerbread to help [[indigestion]] in the year 1444. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.annas.se/artikel.asp?artikelId=34 & strukturId=55|title=Annas' history of Gingerbread ('''Swedish''')|accessdate=2010-10-30|archive-date=2010-03-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100310035724/http://www.annas.se/artikel.asp?artikelId=34 & strukturId=55|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 The custom was to bake white biscuits and paint them as [[window]] [[wikt:decorate|decoration]]s.
