 An '''accentor''' is a small bird in the genus family of [[Prunellidae]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=Accentor {{!}} bird {{!}} Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/animal/accentor|access-date=2023-06-20|website=www.britannica.com|language=en}} < /ref >  They can be found in northern [[Asia]] and [[Europe]]. The mountain accentor,  for example, lives in northern Asia, while the [[dunnock|hedge-accentor]] or dunnock can be found in [[England]]. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|title=Encyclopaedia Apollo Volume I|publisher=McGraw-Hill Far Eastern Publishers|year=1970|location=Singapore|pages=4}} < /ref >   
 
 Accentors have been described as having slender bills with sharp points. They also have rounded wings. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref name= " :1 "  / >
