 '''TeachTown''' is an [[United States|American]] [[software]] [[company]].  
 ==Service== 
 TeachTown provides educational software to schools, clinicians and parents for children with [[autism spectrum]] disorder, [[developmental disabilities]], [[intellectual disabilities]], or emotional and behavior disorders. < ref name= " TT1 " > {{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=TeachTown a service for autistic children|url=https://www.seattletimes.com/business/teachtown-a-service-for-autistic-children/|archive-url=|archive-date=|work=The Seattle Times|accessdate=16 October 2006}} < /ref > < ref name= " TT2 " > {{cite web|title=TeachTown gives families hope|url=https://www.seattlepi.com/local/article/TeachTown-gives-families-hope-1272033.php|work=The Seattle Times|accessdate=23 March 2008}} < /ref > 
 
 == Early history == 
 TeachTown was founded in [[Seattle, Washington]] in 2003. TeachTown had financial backing from Washington Research Foundation following its second-prize win of the [[University of Washington]]'s business plan competition. < ref name= " TT3 " > {{cite web|title=Water-chemistry monitoring company wins business plan competition|url=https://www.washington.edu/news/2004/05/18/water-chemistry-monitoring-company-wins-business-plan-competition/|publisher=UW News|accessdate=18 May 2004}} < /ref > < ref name= " TT4 " > {{cite web|title=Tech Digest|url=https://www.seattlepi.com/business/article/Tech-Digest-1145061.php|work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|accessdate=15 March 2011}} < /ref >   This was soon followed up with support from Seattle-based [[angel investors]], and Kevin MacDonald joining to lead technical operations. Some of the investors were parents of children with autism. < ref name= " TT4 "  / > 
 ==Management== 
 In 2006, TeachTown merged with Animated Speech Corporation. Feshbach became TeachTown's largest investor and [[CEO]]. < ref name= " TT7 " > {{cite web|title=Christina Whalen Goes to TeachTown|url=https://edtechdigest.blog/2011/03/11/interview-christina-whelan-goes-to-teachtown-2/|publisher=Ed Tech Digest|accessdate=11 March 2011}} < /ref > 
 
 Terry Thoren took on the role of president and CEO in 2009. < ref name= " TT8 " > {{cite web|title=New TeachTownÂ®: Basics 2.0|url=https://www.prweb.com/releases/teachtown_cai/for_autistic_children/prweb4845954.htm/|publisher=PR Web|accessdate=1 December 2010|archive-date=21 March 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110321001047/http://www.prweb.com/releases/TeachTown_CAI/for_autistic_children/prweb4845954.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2012 Dave Cappellucci assumed the CEO position. Dan Feshbach remained as chairman of the board. The company was re-capitalized and TeachTown became a subsidiary of Jigsaw Learning LLC. < ref > {{Cite web|title=TEACHTOWN Trademark of Jigsaw Learning, LLC - Registration Number 4735316 - Serial Number 86417071 :: Justia Trademarks|url=http://trademarks.justia.com/864/17/teachtown-86417071.html|access-date=2021-06-22|website=trademarks.justia.com|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Bain Capital]] acquired TeachTown in 2021 through its Bain Capital Double Impact program. < ref name= " TT9 " > {{cite web|title=Bain Capital Double Impact has made a growth investment in TeachTown|url=https://pitchbook.com/newsletter/bains-impact-strategy-stakes-teachtown/|publisher=PitchBook|accessdate=13 January 2021}} < /ref >
