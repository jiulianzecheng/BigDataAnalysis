 The '''Phasianidae''' is a family of [[bird]]s which includes the [[pheasant]]s, [[partridge]]s, [[grouse]], [[junglefowl]] (including [[chicken]]). < ref > Madge, Steve; McGowan, Philip J.K.  &  Kirwan, Guy M. 2002. Pheasants, partidges and grouse: a guide to the pheasants, partridges, quails, grouse, guineafowl, buttonquails and sandgrouse of the world. Christopher Helm, London. ISBN 0-7136-3966-0 < /ref >  These are terrestrial species, variable in size but generally plump, with broad relatively short [[wing]]s. Many have a spur on their [[leg]]s.  
 
 Males of the larger species are often brightly coloured. The typical diet is [[seeds]] with some [[insect]]s and [[berry|berries]].
