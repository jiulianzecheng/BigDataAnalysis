'''Haram''' is an [[Arabic language|Arabic]] word that literally means forbidden, < ref name= " Modarresi3 " > {{cite book|url=http://almodarresi.com/en/books/pdf/TheLawsofIslam.pdf|title=The Laws of Islam|author=Mohammad Taqi al-Modarresi|date=26 March 2016|publisher=Enlight Press|isbn=978-0994240989|language=en|ref=Modarresi|author-link=Mohammad Taqi al-Modarresi|access-date=22 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190802163247/http://almodarresi.com/en/books/pdf/TheLawsofIslam.pdf|archive-date=2 August 2019|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=haram adjective - Definition, pictures, pronunciation and usage notes|url=https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/haram|access-date=26 April 2019|work=Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]}} < /ref >  it may be used to refer to sacred knowledge which is hidden from the people, or the word's more common use which is for a evil and sinful action that is forbidden to be done. In [[Sharia|Islamic law]], haram is used to refer to any action that is forbidden in the [[Qur'an|Quran]] and is one of the five Islamic commandments. < ref name= " Adamec 2009 1022 " > {{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/historicaldictio00adam_560|title=Historical Dictionary of Islam, 2nd Edition|last=Adamec|first=Ludwig|publisher=Scarecrow Press, Inc|year=2009|isbn=9780810861619|location=Lanham|page=[https://archive.org/details/historicaldictio00adam_560/page/n156 102]|url-access=limited}} < /ref > < ref name= " vikor " > {{cite encyclopedia|first=Knut S.|last=Vikør|title=Sharīʿah|encyclopedia=The Oxford Encyclopedia of Islam and Politics|publisher=Oxford University Press|editor=Emad El-Din Shahin|year=2014|url=http://bridgingcultures.neh.gov/muslimjourneys/items/show/226|access-date=2017-05-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140604214623/http://bridgingcultures.neh.gov/muslimjourneys/items/show/226|archive-date=2014-06-04|url-status=dead}} < /ref >
