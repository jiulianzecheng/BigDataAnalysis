 '''Gironde''' ({{lang-oc|Gironda}}) is a [[departments of France|department]] in the southwest of [[France]]. It is named after the [[Gironde estuary]]. 
 
 The department is in the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] is [[Bordeaux]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Gironde was formed during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with parts of the old [[province]]s of [[Guyenne]] and [[Gascony]]. 
 
 From July 1793 to April 1795, the department's name was changed to '''Bec-d'Ambès''' to avoid the association with the [[Girondist]]s, a [[France|French]] [[revolution]]ary [[Political faction|group]]. 
 
 The department was divided into seven [[district]]s: Bordeaux, Libourne, La Réole, Bazas, Cadillac, Blaye and Lesparre. Its [[Capital (city)|capital]] was the city of [[Bordeaux]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/33his.html |title=Historique de la Gironde |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=20 April 2015 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Gironde is part of the [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] region, southwestern France, and is the largest department of [[Metropolitan France]] with an area of {{Convert|9975.6|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 There four main zones in the Gironde; they are: 
 # the '''[[coast]]al zone''', including part of the ''Côte d'Argent'' ( " [[Silver]] coast " ); 
 # the '''[[forest]]''' that covers the western half of the department; 
 # the '''''Bordelais''''', region around Bordeaux and where the different kinds of Bordeaux [[wine]]s are produced; and 
 # the '''''Bazadais''''', the region around the town of [[Bazas]], in the soutwest of the department and where [[farming]] is very important. 
 
 The department is bordered by the departments: [[Charente-Maritime]], [[Dordogne]], [[Lot-et-Garonne]] and [[Landes]]. The [[Atlantic Ocean]] (''[[Gulf of Biscay]]'') is to the west of the department.

 The main rivers in the department are the [[Dordogne (river)|Dordogne]] and the [[Garonne river|Garonne]] rivers; the two rivers form, after their [[confluence]], the [[Gironde estuary]], the largest [[estuary]] of France. 
 
 === Climate === 
 The climate in Bordeaux is [[Oceanic climate|Marine West Coast Climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] '''''Cfb'''''), with mild winters and warm summers. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|work= |publisher=Weatherbase |title=Bordeaux, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=1570 & cityname=Bordeaux%2C+Aquitaine%2C+France & units= |language= |accessdate=9 April 2017 }} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Bordeaux is {{Convert|13.8|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|21.4|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|6.6|°C|°F }}. 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Bordeaux is {{Convert|850.9|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is November with {{Convert|88.9|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is July with an average of {{Convert|53.3|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / > 
 
 ==Administration== 
 Gironde is part of the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]] and is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Gironde'' in Bordeaux. 
 
 ===Administrative divisions=== 
 There are 6 [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] ([[district]]s), 33 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']] and 538 [[Communes in France|''communes'']] ([[Municipality|municipalities]]) in Gironde. < ref name=insee1 > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/departement/DEP33-gironde |title=Département de la Gironde (33) |work=Géographie administrative et d'étude |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=French |accessdate=9 May 2017 |date= }} < /ref >

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Gironde are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Girondins'' (women: ''Girondines''). < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.habitants.fr/habitants_departement_gironde_33.html |title=Gironde (33)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=20 April 2015 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 The department of Gironde has a population, in 2014, of 1,526,016, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|1526016|9975.6|km2|prec=2|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Bordeaux, with 921,668 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The ''arrondissements'' of Blaye and Lesparre-Médoc are those with less people, with 88,115 and 86,560 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
