In [[law]], a '''conviction''' is the [[verdict]] that results when a [[court of law]] finds a [[defendant]] [[Guilt (law)|guilt]]y of a [[crime]]. < ref > {{cite book|title=Black's law dictionary|year=2000|publisher=West Group|location=St. Paul, Minn.|isbn=0-314-24077-2|edition=7th|editor=Garner, Bryan A.|pages=335}} < /ref >  The opposite of a conviction is an [[acquittal]] (i.e.  " not guilty " ). A '''minor conviction''' is a [[wikt:warning|warning]] conviction, and it does not affect the defendant but does serve as a warning. In [[Scotland]] there can also be a verdict of  " [[not proven]] " , which counts as an acquittal. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://scholarship.law.duke.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1129 & context=lcp |title=The Scottish Criminal Jury: A Very Peculiar Institution |author=Peter Duff |website= |publisher=Duke Law School |accessdate=28 March 2016}} < /ref >  A convicted person is also called a [[convict]].
