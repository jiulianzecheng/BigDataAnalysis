The '''col''' is a concept in [[topography]]. It is the lowest point on a [[Ridge|mountain ridge]] between two [[Summit|peaks]]. < ref name= " peakbaggerglossary " > {{cite web |title=Glossary of Terms |url=https://peakbagger.com/help/glossary.aspx |website=peakbagger.com |access-date=14 November 2019}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book |last1=Whittow |first1=J.B. |title=The Penguin dictionary of physical geography |url=https://archive.org/details/penguindictionar0000whit_b9d8 |year=1984 |publisher=Penguin |isbn=0-14-051094-X}} < /ref >   
 
 In order to find out the [[Topographic prominence|prominence]] of a peak, a '''key col''' is used. This is the highest col between the peak and its [[parent peak]]. < ref name= " peakbaggerglossary "  / >
