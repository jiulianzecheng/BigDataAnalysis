 '''Lisbon''' ({{lang-pt|Lisboa}}) is the [[capital (city)|capital city]] of [[Portugal]]. It is the largest city of Portugal. The [[city]] has a [[population]] of about half million people. In Lisbon's urban area live around 2.8 million people, being the 10th-most populous urban area in the [[European Union]]. < ref name= " World_Urban_Areas " > [http://www.demographia.com/db-worldua.pdf Demographia: World Urban Areas] - demographia.com, 06.2021 < /ref >   
 Lisbon is placed on the right bank (western) of the [[Tagus]] River, near the outfall. It has a pleasant climate and has about 220 days of [[sun]]shine each year. There are many beautiful [[beach]]es close to the city. There are also many [[seafood]] [[restaurant|restaurants]], [[history|historical]] sites and monuments, being one of the oldest cities in the world. < ref name= " Livermore1973 " > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PF0fAAAAMAAJ & q=%22Felicitas%20Julia%22|title=Portugal: A Short History|author=H. V. Livermore|publisher=Edinburgh University Press|year=1973|isbn=978-0-85224-207-0|page=4}} < /ref >

 == Transportation == 
 
 === Metro === 
 [[File:HPIM0530 (cropped).JPG|thumb|right|The [[Lisbon Metro]] is Portugal's oldest and largest subway system.]] 
 The [[Lisbon Metro]] connects the city centre to different districts, it also reaches some suburbs around the [[Lisbon metropolitan area]], like [[Amadora]] and [[Loures]]. 
 
 === Trams === 
 [[File:Alfama Lisboa 9.JPG |thumb|right|A [[Trams in Lisbon|Lisbon tram]] in [[Alfama]].]] 
 [[Tram|Trams]] are a traditional public transport in Lisbon. It was first introduced in 1901, many of the electric trams were originally imported from the [[United States|US]], < ref > {{cite book|title=Electrical World|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_FNEAQAAIAAJ & pg=PA566|year=1900|publisher=McGraw-Hill|page=566}} < /ref >  and were called ''americanos''. Trams in Lisbon are coloured yellow and is one of the tourist icons of the city, and their size is great for steep hills and narrow streets of the central city. < ref > [http://www.carris.pt/index.php?area=servicos & subarea=servicos_ascensores_bica] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070103194107/http://www.carris.pt/index.php?area=servicos & subarea=servicos_ascensores_bica |date=3 January 2007 }} Information from Carris, Lisbon transportation company. < /ref > < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.personal.u-net.com/~luso/listrams.htm |title=Luso Pages - Lisbon (Portugal) Trams |access-date=10 March 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110515002830/http://www.personal.u-net.com/~luso/listrams.htm |archive-date=15 May 2011  }} Details of Lisbon's trams, from Luso Pages < /ref >
