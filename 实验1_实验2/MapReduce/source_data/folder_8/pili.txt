 The '''pili''' ([[Central Bikol]] and [[Filipino language|Filipino]]: ''pili'', {{IPAc-en|p|iː|ˈ|l|iː}} {{respell|pee|LEE|'}};) (''Canarium ovatum'') is a [[tropical tree]] from the [[Burseraceae|torchwood family]]. It is native to the [[Philippines]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Canarium ovatum Engl. {{!}} Plants of the World Online {{!}} Kew Science|url=http://powo.science.kew.org/taxon/urn:lsid:ipni.org:names:127447-1|access-date=2021-08-09|website=Plants of the World Online|language=en}} < /ref >  The tree has nuts that have a lot of [[fat]] in them. < ref name= " JAOCS " > {{cite journal|vauthors=Kukuda Y, Jahaniaval F, Umali J|year=2000|title=Characterization of Pili Nut (Canarium ovatum) Oil: Fatty Acid and Triacylglycerol Composition and Physicochemical Properties|url=http://lib3.dss.go.th/fulltext/journal/j.aocs/j.aocs/2000/no.9/v.77n9p991-997.pdf|journal=[[Journal of the American Oil Chemists' Society]]|volume=77|issue=9|pages=991–996|doi=10.1007/s11746-000-0156-8|s2cid=83628873}} < /ref > 
 
 == The plant == 
 [[File:Pili_nut_(Canarium_ovatum).jpg|thumb|Shelled pili nuts]] 
 The pili tree is about {{convert|20|m|ft|abbr=on}} tall with [[resin]] strong wood. It can live through strong winds. [[Insect|Insects]] [[Pollination|pollinate]] the plant. Its flowers bloom much of the time.  The [[Fruit|fruits]] ripen after a while. The ovary has three [[Locule|locules]], each with two ovules; most of the time only one ovule develops. < ref > Chandler, W. H. 1958. ''Evergreen orchards''. Lea  &  Febiger, Philadelphia.{{page needed|date=December 2020}} < /ref > 
 
 The ''C. ovatum'' fruit is a [[stone fruit]], weighing {{convert|15.7|to|45.7|g|lb|abbr=on}}. The skin ([[exocarp]]) is smooth, thin, shiny, and darkens when the fruit ripens; the pulp ([[mesocarp]]) is fibrous, fleshy, and yellow in color. The nuts are 8% [[carbohydrate]], 11.5 to 13.9% [[protein]], and 70% [[fat]]. < ref name= " JAOCS "  / >  Kernels from some trees may be bitter, fibrous or have a weird smell.
