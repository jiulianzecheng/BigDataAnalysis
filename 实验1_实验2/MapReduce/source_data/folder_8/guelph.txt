 '''Guelph''' is a [[city]] in [[south]]ern [[Ontario]]. It is called the Royal City. It has often been rated as one of [[Canada]]'s best places to live. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.canadiantravelguide.net/cities/GUELPH/ |title=Guelph (Ontario) |author= < !--Staff writer(s)-- >  |date= |website= |publisher=CanadianTravelGuide.net |accessdate=28 January 2015 }} < /ref >  This is because it has a low [[Unemployment|unemployment rate]] and a low [[crime]] rate. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.aplaceformom.com/blog/12-5-14-best-places-retire-canada/ |title=Best Places to Retire in Canada |author=Ami Noss |date=5 December 2014 |website= |publisher=A Place for Mom, Inc. |accessdate=28 January 2015 |archive-date=20 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150320022022/http://www.aplaceformom.com/blog/12-5-14-best-places-retire-canada/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In the 2011 [[census]], Guelph had a [[population]] of 121,688. Guelph has warm, [[humid]] [[summer]]s that can reach up to {{convert|30|°C|°F}} and cold [[winter]]s coming down to {{convert|-25|°C|°F}}. It is classified [[Continental climate|Dfb]] in the [[Köppen climate classification]] system. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.traveltill.com/destination/Canada/Guelph/climate.php|title=Climate in Guelph |author= |date= |work= |publisher=Traveltill.com. |accessdate=28 January 2015 }} < /ref >
