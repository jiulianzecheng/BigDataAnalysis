 '''Swardspeak''' (or '''''Chuva''''') is a secret language that came from the [[code-switching|mixed language]] with the names [[Taglish and Englog]]. It is used by [[gay]] people in the [[Philippines]]. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://unitedsea.blogspot.com/2007/07/deciphering-filipino-gay-lingo.html |title= Deciphering Filipino Gay Lingo |date= 27 July 2007|work= |publisher= United SEA|accessdate=23 December 2010}} < /ref > 
 
 Swardspeak uses word from [[Tagalog language|Tagalog]], [[English language|English]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]], [[Cebuano language|Cebuano]], [[Japanese language|Japanese]], [[Sanskrit language|Sanskrit]], and other languages. Names of Celebrities and trademark brands are also used. < ref name= " Gayspeak: Not for gays only " > {{cite web|date=30 April 2010|title=Gayspeak: Not for gays only|url=http://www.thepoc.net/thepoc-features/buhay-pinoy/buhay-pinoy-features/6340-gayspeak-not-gor-gays-only.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101201034008/http://www.thepoc.net/thepoc-features/buhay-pinoy/buhay-pinoy-features/6340-gayspeak-not-gor-gays-only.html|archive-date=1 December 2010|work=|accessdate=23 December 2010}} < /ref > < ref name= " Kritika Kultura " > {{cite web|url= http://www.philjol.info/index.php/KK/article/viewPDFInterstitial/754/699|title= GAY LANGUAGE: DEFYING THE STRUCTURAL LIMITS OF ENGLISH LANGUAGE IN THE PHILIPPINES|date= August 2008|work= Kritika Kultura, Issue 11|publisher= Kritika Kultura|accessdate= 25 December 2010|archive-date= 24 March 2009|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20090324065359/http://www.philjol.info/index.php/KK/article/viewPDFInterstitial/754/699|url-status= dead}} < /ref > 
   
 Gay people use Swardpeak to make themselves feel special. < ref > {{cite web|author=|title=swardspeak|url=http://www.doubletongued.org/index.php/dictionary/swardspeak/|work=|accessdate=23 December 2010}} < /ref >  The language changes a lot. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://bangkok2005.anu.edu.au/papers/Suguitan.pdf |title= A SEMANTIC LOOK AT FEMININE SEX AND GENDER TERMS  IN PHILIPPINE GAY LINGO |work=  |publisher= University of the Philippines |accessdate= 25 December 2010 |archive-date= 19 February 2011 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20110219075318/http://bangkok2005.anu.edu.au/papers/Suguitan.pdf |url-status= dead }} < /ref >  People who use the language are called ''Bekimons''. This word is from the words ''bakla'' (gay) and ''[[Jejemon]]''. < ref name= " Gayspeak: Not for gays only " / > < ref > {{cite web|date=July 7, 2010|title=Ang Bekimon (Baklang Jejemon)|url=http://pinoylgbt.com/ang-bekimon-baklang-jejemon/|work=|accessdate=23 December 2010|archive-date=4 December 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101204162635/http://pinoylgbt.com/ang-bekimon-baklang-jejemon/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 The word  " Swardspeak "  came from the 1970's book called  " Swardspeak: A Preliminary Study " . < ref > {{cite web|date=June 5, 2006|title=The Filipino Gayspeak (Filipino Gay Lingo)|url=http://www.ncca.gov.ph/about-culture-and-arts/articles-on-c-n-a/article.php?i=289 & subcat=13|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120128201330/http://www.ncca.gov.ph/about-culture-and-arts/articles-on-c-n-a/article.php?subcat=13 & i=289|archive-date=28 January 2012|work=|accessdate=24 December 2010}} < /ref >   " Sward "  is slang for  " gay male "  in the Philippines. < ref > {{cite web|author=|date=May 13, 2005|title=GAY SPEAKS on  " SWARDSPEAK " |url=http://badinggerzie.blogspot.com/2005/05/gay-speaks-on-swardspeak.html|work=|accessdate=24 December 2010}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Rules== 
 Swardspeak is a form of [[slang]]. < ref > {{cite web|date=2008-05-05|title=On Philippine gay lingo|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/views-and-analysis/05/05/08/philippine-gay-lingo-danton-remoto|work=|accessdate=25 December 2010}} < /ref >  It changes a lot and the meaning of words are hard to know. < ref > {{cite web|author=|date=November 16, 2008|title=Gay Lingo (Made in the Philippines)|url=http://philippines-butchokoy.blogspot.com/2008/11/gay-lingo-made-in-philippines.html|work=|accessdate=23 December 2010}} < /ref >  The rules are explained below. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://unitedsea.blogspot.com/2007/07/deciphering-filipino-gay-lingo.html |title= Deciphering Filipino Gay Lingo|date= 27 July 2007|work= |publisher= United SEA|accessdate=23 December 2010}} < /ref > 
 
 *The first [[wikt:syllable|sound]] changes to the letter  " J "  or  " Sh "  or to the sounds  " Jo- " ,  " Sho- " ,  " Ju- " ,  " Shu- " ,  " Ky- " , or  " Ny- " .
