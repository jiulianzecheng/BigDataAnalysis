 '''Hajji''' ({{lang-ar|الحجّي}}) (sometimes spelled '''Hadji''', '''Haji''', '''Alhaji''', ''' Al hage''', '''Al-hajj''' or '''El-Hajj''') is an honor title which is given to a [[Muslim]] who has successfully completed the [[Hajj]] to [[Mecca]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite book|title=Islam: A very short introduction|url=https://archive.org/details/islamveryshortin00ruth|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|author=Malise Ruthven|year=1997|isbn=978-0-19-285389-9|page=[https://archive.org/details/islamveryshortin00ruth/page/n161 147]}} < /ref >  It is also often used to call an elder, since it can take years to get enough the wealth to fund the travel and in many Muslim societies to a respected man as an honorific title.
