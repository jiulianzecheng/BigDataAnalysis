 '''Noinu Thumleima''' ({{lang-mni|ꯅꯣꯢꯅꯨ ꯊꯨꯝꯂꯩꯃ}}) or '''Thumkhong Lairembi''' < ref name= " :0 "  / >  ({{lang-mni|ꯊꯨꯝꯈꯣꯡ ꯂꯥꯢꯔꯦꯝꯕꯤ}}) is the goddess of [[salt]] and [[brine|salt wells]] in [[Meitei mythology]] and [[Meitei religion|religion]] of [[Ancient Manipur]] ([[Antique Kangleipak]]). < ref name= " :2 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tIBymmBWqgsC & q=thumleima+ngareima+phouoibi & pg=PA14|title=The Lois of Manipur: Andro, Khurkhul, Phayeng and Sekmai|last=Devi|first=Lairenlakpam Bino|date=2002|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-7099-849-5|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.465422/page/n19/mode/2up|title=Leimarel Mingkhei|website=archive.org|year=1970|pages=19|language=mni}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=http://archive.org/details/folklore24folkuoft/page/425/mode/1up?q=Thumleima+salt+wells & view=theater|title=Folklore|last=Folklore Society (Great Britain)|publisher=London, Folk-lore Society|others=Robarts - University of Toronto|year=1890 }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=M0QKAAAAIAAJ & q=+Noinu+thumleima+salt+wells|title=Folklore|last1=Jacobs|first1=Joseph|last2=Nutt|first2=Alfred Trübner|last3=Wright|first3=Arthur Robinson|last4=Crooke|first4=William|date=1913|publisher=Folklore Society|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " Devi " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gxqeDwAAQBAJ & q=Thum+Leima+Imoinu+salt+ & pg=PA64|title=Glimpses of Manipuri Culture|last=Devi|first=Dr Yumlembam Gopi|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=978-0-359-72919-7|language=en}} < /ref >  She is a sister (or ''friend'') of the goddesses, [[Phouoibi]] ([[Phouleima]]), [[Ngaleima]] and [[Ereima]] ([[Ireima]]). < ref name= " :2 "  / >  People pray to her so there will be enough salt. Salt is an important part of the human [[diet]]. < ref name= " :1 "  / > 
 
 According to the  " Recent Researches in Oriental Indological Studies: Including Meiteilogy "  by Moirangthem Kirti, the goddess is a wife of [[Pakhangba]], the royal deity. < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 == Name == 
 The [[Meitei language|Meitei]] female given name  " Thumleima "  (tʰum.lə́i.mə, ꯊꯨꯝꯂꯩꯃ) is made of two words. These words are  " Thum "  (tʰum, ꯊꯨꯝ) and  " Leima "  (lə́i.mə, ꯂꯩꯃ). In [[Meitei language|Meitei]],  " Thum "  (tʰum, ꯊꯨꯝ) means [[salt]], usually the common salt. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%8A%EA%AF%A8%EA%AF%9D & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Thum|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref >  In [[Meitei language|Meitei]],  " Leima "  (lə́i.mə, ꯂꯩꯃ) means queen, mistress or lady. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%82%EA%AF%A9%EA%AF%83%EA%AF%A5 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Leima|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref >    
 [[File:THUMKHONG LAIREMBI.jpg|thumb|300px| " Thumkhong Lairembi " , another name of goddess Thumleima, written in medieval Meetei Mayek abugida]] 
 Another name of goddess Thumleima is  " Thumkhong Lairembi "  (ꯊꯨꯝꯈꯣꯡ ꯂꯥꯢꯔꯦꯝꯕꯤ). The [[Meitei language|Meitei]] word  " Thumkhong "  (tʰum.kʰoŋ, ꯊꯨꯝꯈꯣꯡ) is made up of two component words,  " Thum "  (tʰum, ꯊꯨꯝ) and  " Khong "  (kʰoŋ, ꯈꯣꯡ).  " Thum "  (ꯊꯨꯝ)  means salt.  " Khong "  (kʰoŋ, ꯈꯣꯡ) means mine (of salt or metallic ores). < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%88%EA%AF%A3%EA%AF%A1 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Khong|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref >  So,  " Thumkhong "  (tʰum.kʰoŋ, ꯊꯨꯝꯈꯣꯡ) means ''salt ditch'' or ''salt brine'' or ''salt mine'' or ''salt well''. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%8A%EA%AF%A8%EA%AF%9D & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Thum+Khong|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref >  Another [[Meitei language|Meitei]] word  " Lairembi "  (ꯂꯥꯢꯔꯦꯝꯕꯤ) is made up of three component words,  " Lai "  (ꯂꯥꯢ),  " -rem "  (-ꯔꯦꯝ) and  " bi "  (ꯕꯤ).  " Lai "  (ꯂꯥꯢ) means  " God " .  " -rem "  (-ꯔꯦꯝ) or  " -lem "  (-ꯂꯦꯝ) means  " excellent " .  " bi "  (ꯕꯤ) or  " pi "  (ꯄꯤ) is a suffix to denote feminine gender. So,  " Lairembi "  (ꯂꯥꯢꯔꯦꯝꯕꯤ) means a goddess. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://dsal.uchicago.edu/cgi-bin/app/sharma_query.py?qs=%EA%AF%82%EA%AF%A5%EA%AF%8F%EA%AF%94%EA%AF%A6%EA%AF%9D%EA%AF%95%EA%AF%A4 & searchhws=yes|title=Learners' Manipuri-English dictionary.Lairembi|website=uchicago.edu|year=2006}} < /ref > 
 
 == Association with other gods == 
 {{See|Incarnations of Leimarel Sidabi}} 
 The Goddess Thumleima is one of the divine manifestations of [[Leimarel Sidabi|Leimarel (Leimalel)]]. Leimalel is the supreme mother earth goddess. It is said that Leimalel becomes Thumleima when she is in the salt mine. < ref name= " :1 "  / > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.461915/page/n73/mode/2up?q=Thumleima+Leimalel+manifests+salt+mine|title=A Critical Study Of The Religious Philosophy|last=Singh|first=L. Bhagyachandra|date=1991|pages=73}} < /ref >  Thumleima is also considered as one of the divine incarnations of goddess [[Imoinu]]. < ref name= " Devi " / >
