
 {{nihongo|'''''Tenna'''''|天和}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Enpō]]'' and before ''[[Jōkyō]].''  This period started in September 1681 and ended in February 1684. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]].. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA959   " ''Tenna'', " ] ''Japan Encyclopedia,'' p. 959. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Reigen of Japan|Reigen]]''-tennō''|霊元天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA785  " Reigen Tennō, " ] p. 785; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA414  ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 414-415.] < /ref > 
 
 The ''[[nengō]]'' ''Tenna'' means  " Peace of Heaven " . < ref > Jenkins, Donald. (1971). ''Ukiyo-e Prints and Paintings: the Primitive Period, 1680-1745,'' p. 21. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Tenna'' era== 
 [[Image:Hiroshige, Sugura street.jpg|thumb|right|In the 3rd year of ''Tenna'', Mitusi opens for business on Surugacho (Suruga Street) in Edo]] 
 * '''1681''' (''Tenna 1''): [[Tokugawa Tsunayoshi]] is established as the fifth [[shogun]] of the [[Tokugawa shogunate]]. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA414  p. 414.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''5 February 1681''' (''Tenna 1, 28th day of the 12th month'') < !-- NengoCalc 天和一年十二月二十八日 -- > : The Great ''Tenna'' Fire in [[Edo]]. < ref name= " titsingh415 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA415  p. 415.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1681''' (''Tenna 2''): A famine afflicts [[Heian-kyō]] and the nearby areas. < ref name= " titsingh415 " / > 
 
 * '''1683''' (''Tenna 3''): Tokugawa shogunate grants permission for [[Mitsui]] money exchanges (''ryōgaeten'') to open in Edo. < ref > Hiroshi Shinjō. (1962). ''History of the Yen: 100 Years of Japanese Money-economy'', p. 11. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1683''' (''Tenna 4''): [[Hotta Masatoshi]] is assassinated. < ref > [[Beatrice Bodart-Bailey|Bodart-Bailey, Beatrice]]. (2006). ''The Dog Shogun: The Personality and Policies of Tokugawa Tsunayoshi'', p. 183. < /ref > 
 
 In Tenna, the first book was written about the coins of Japan.  The work was entitled ''Classified Collection of Coins'' (''Kwacho Kuien''). < ref > Munro, Neil Gordon. (1904). [https://books.google.com/books?id=ASlIAAAAMAAJ & dq=nengo+meaning & pg=PA63  ''The Coins of Japan,'' p. xviii.] < /ref >
