In [[law]] and in [[religion]], '''testimony''' is a formal statement that something is true. The words  " testimony "  and  " testify "  both come from the [[Latin]] word ''testis'', which refers to a [[Third-party source|third-party]] witness. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?term=testify|work=Online Etymology Dictionary |last=Harper |first=Douglas |title=testify |access-date=25 January 2012}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.etymonline.com/index.php?term=testimony|work=Online Etymology Dictionary |last=Harper |first=Douglas |title=testimony |access-date=25 January 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Law== 
 
 In the [[law]], testimony is a kind of [[evidence (law)|evidence]]. It comes from a [[witness]] who says that something is true. Testimony may be spoken or written. It is usually made by [[oath]] or [[affirmation in law|affirmation]] under penalty of [[perjury]] (which is a crime that somebody can be charged with if they give testimony that is not true). 
 
 ==Religion== 
 
 Some [[Christians]] use the term  " to testify "  or  " to give one's testimony "  to mean  " to tell the story of how one became a Christian " .
