 '''Susana Dzhamaladinova''' (born 27 August 1983), better known by her [[stage name]] '''Jamala''', is a [[Ukrainians|Ukrainian]] singer who won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]] with the song  " [[1944 (Jamala)|1944]] " . She was awarded  " People's Artist of Ukraine "  by [[Petro Poroshenko]]. < ref > {{cite web|last=Granger|first=Anthony|date=16 May 2016|title=Ukraine: Jamala Awarded Title  " People's Artist of Ukraine " |url=http://eurovoix.com/2016/05/16/ukraine-jamala-awarded-title-peoples-artist-ukraine/|publisher=Eurovoix}} < /ref >    
 
 == Personal life == 
 Susana Dzhamaladinova was born on August 27, 1983 in the [[Kirghiz Soviet Socialist Republic]] (now [[Kyrgyzstan]]) Her father was a [[Muslims|Muslim]] [[Crimean Tatars|Crimean Tatar]] and her mother was [[Armenia|Armenian]].  
 
 Her Crimean Tatar ancestors were [[Deportation|deported]] from [[Crimea]] to [[Central Asia]] under [[Joseph Stalin]]'s government during [[World War II]]. In [[1989]] her family returned to Crimea. 
 
 In 2017, Jamala married Bekir Suleimanov. They have two children together. Jamala is fluent in [[Russian language|Russian]] and [[Ukrainian language|Ukrainian]]. She also speaks [[Crimean Tatar language|Crimean Tatar]] and [[English language|English]]. In [[February]] [[2022]], Jamala and her children left Ukraine due to the [[Russian invasion of Ukraine]]. 
 
 == Career == 
 In [[2010]], she released her first single  " You Are Made of Love " . In early [[2011]], Jamala auditioned to represent [[Ukraine]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest]] with her song  " Smile " . However, she withdrew from the entry competition. 
 
 === Eurovision Song Contest === 
 [[File:ESC2016 - Ukraine 03.jpg|thumb|235x235px|Jamala performing  " 1944 " ]] 
 In [[2016]], Jamala won the first edition of ''[[Vidbir]]'', a Ukrainian music competition which selects who will represent Ukraine in Eurovision. She went on to represent Ukraine in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]] with the song  " 1944 " , which is about the [[deportation]] of the [[Crimea]]n [[Tatars]], including her family, in 1944. < ref > {{cite news|date=26 January 2016|title=Jamala entered Eurovision-2016 national selection|work=QHA.com.ua|url=http://qha.com.ua/en/culture-art/jamala-entered-eurovision2016-national-selection/135789/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303054406/http://qha.com.ua/en/culture-art/jamala-entered-eurovision2016-national-selection/135789/|archive-date=3 March 2016|accessdate=15 May 2016}} < /ref > < ref > [https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-35630395 Eurovision: Ukraine's entry aimed at Russia], [[BBC News]] (22 February 2016) < /ref > 
 
 On 14 May 2016, Jamala won the competition with 534 points. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-36295168|title=Ukraine's Jamala wins Eurovision 2016|work=BBC News|accessdate=15 May 2016}} < /ref >  Since [[2017]], Jamala has been one of the jurors on ''Vidbir.'' She also performed as one of the four past Ukrainian Eurovision entrants in the flag parade [[Eurovision Song Contest 2023]], < ref > {{Cite news|last=Hainey|first=Fionnula|date=13 May 2023|title=Eurovision 2023: Who are GO_A, Jamala, Tina Karol, and Verka Serduchka?|work=[[The Manchester Evening News]]|url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/news/tv/eurovision-2023-who-go_a-jamala-26902526|access-date=14 May 2023}} < /ref >  which was hosted in the [[United Kingdom]] instead of Ukraine due to [[Russian invasion of Ukraine|the war]].
