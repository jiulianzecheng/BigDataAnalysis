 '''Thiruvalluvar''' was a [[Tamil people|Tamil]] poet and philosopher < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.dlshq.org/saints/thiruvalluvar.htm|title=Thiruvalluvar|website=www.dlshq.org}} < /ref >  who wrote the  classic of couplets  of [[Tamil language | Tamil]], ''[[Thirukkural]]''. 
 
 == Memorials == 
 * A temple-like memorial to Thiruvalluvar, Valluvar Kottam, was built in [[Chennai]] in 1976. 
 * There is a 133-foot tall statue of Thiruvalluvar erected at the southern tip of the Indian subcontinent Kanyakumari where the [[Arabian Sea]], the [[Bay of Bengal]], and the Indian Ocean converge, the 133 feet denote Tirukkuṛaḷ's 133 Chapters or ''athikarams'' and the show of three fingers denote the three themes ''Aram'', ''Porul'', and ''Inbam'', i.e. the sections on Morals, Wealth and Love. < ref name= " TiruvaḷḷuvarSubramuniyaswami2000 " > {{cite book|author1=Tiruvaḷḷuvar|author2=Satguru Sivaya Subramuniyaswami|title=Tirukural|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TGw-ef8ZrHoC & pg=PR32|accessdate=12 December 2010|date=1 January 2000|publisher=Abhinav Publications|isbn=978-81-7017-390-8|pages=31–32}} < /ref > 
 * There is also a statue of Thiruvalluvar outside the School of Oriental and African Studies in Russell Square, London.
