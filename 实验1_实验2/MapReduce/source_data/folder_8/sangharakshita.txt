 '''Sangharakshita''' (born '''Dennis Philip Edward Lingwood''', 26 August 1925 – 30 October 2018) was a British Buddhist teacher and writer. He was born in [[Tooting]], [[London]]. He was the founder of the 10 precept [[Triratna Buddhist Community]], which was known until 2010 as the Friends of the Western Buddhist Order, or FWBO. < ref > {{citation | first = Stephen | last = Batchelor | year = 1994 | title = The Awakening of the West: The Encounter of Buddhism and Western Culture | publisher = Parallax Press | isbn = 0-938077-69-4 | page = 333 }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book|author1=George D. Chryssides|author2=Margaret Z. Wilkins|title=A Reader in New Religious Movements: Readings in the Study of New Religious|url=https://archive.org/details/readerinnewrelig0000chry|date=2006|publisher=Continuum International Publishing Group|location=London|isbn=0826461670}} < /ref > 
 
 == Biography == 
 He was one of the few Westerners to be ordained as a [[Theravadin]] [[Bhikkhu]] in the period following [[World War II]] though he later left that tradition to study with other Buddhist teachers, including some from the Tibetan and Chan schools. < ref name= " Batchelor 1994 326 " > {{citation | first = Stephen | last = Batchelor | year = 1994 | title = The Awakening of the West: The Encounter of Buddhism and Western Culture | publisher = Parallax Press | isbn = 0-938077-69-4 | page = 326 }} < /ref >  He spent over 20 years in Asia, < ref name= " Workingin1998 " > {{Citation | last = Baumann | first = Martin | author-link = Martin Baumann | title = Working in the Right Spirit: The Application of Buddhist Right Livelihood in the Friends of the Western Buddhist Order | journal = Journal of Buddhist Ethics | volume = 5 | url = http://crossasia-repository.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/124/1/Working_in_the_right_spirit.pdf | date = May 1998 | page = 132 | postscript=. }} < /ref >  where he had a number of Tibetan Buddhist teachers. < ref name= " Batchelor1994a " > {{citation | first = Stephen | last = Batchelor | year = 1994 | title = The Awakening of the West: The Encounter of Buddhism and Western Culture | publisher = Parallax Press | isbn = 0-938077-69-4 | page = 329 }} < /ref > 
 
 He was known as  " the founding father of Western Buddhism "  due to his many publications, leadership of retreats, and contributions to awareness of Buddhism in the United Kingdom. < ref name= " autogenerated303 " > {{citation | first = Stephen C | last = Berkwitz | year = 2006 | title = Buddhism in world cultures: comparative perspectives | publisher = ABC-CLIO | isbn = 1-85109-782-1 | page = 303 }} < /ref >  He retired in 1995. < ref > {{cite web|title=Adhisthana|url=http://adhisthana.org/|publisher=Triratna Buddhist Order|access-date=19 February 2014}} < /ref >   
 
 In 1997, ''[[The Guardian]]'' reported that Sangharakshita had made unwanted sexual advances to FWBO members in the 1970s and 1980s. < ref > {{citation | first = Karma Lekshe | last = Tsomo | year = 2000 | title = Innovative Buddhist Women: Swimming Against the Stream | publisher = Routledge | isbn = 978-0-7007-1219-9 | pages = 266–267 }} < /ref >  Later, he publicly apologized and said any sexual relations he was involved in with his students had consent. < ref > {{citation | last = Vajragupta | year = 2010 | title = The Triratna Story: Behind the Scenes of a New Buddhist Movement | publisher = Windhorse | isbn = 978-1-899579-92-1 }} < /ref > 
 
 Sangharakshita died on October 30, 2018 from [[pneumonia]] caused by [[sepsis]] in [[Hereford]], [[Herefordshire]] at the age of 93. < ref > [https://thebuddhistcentre.com/news/urgyen-sangharakshita-1925-2018 Urgyen Sangharakshita 1925-2018] < /ref >
