 '''MIDI''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|m|ɪ|d|i}}; '''Musical Instrument Digital Interface''') is a [[Standard|technical standard]]. It describes a [[Communication protocol|communications protocol]], [[Digital electronics|digital interface]], and [[Connector|electrical connectors]]. It connect a large variety of electronic musical instruments, digital devices for playing, editing, and recording music. < ref > {{Citation|last=Swift|first=Andrew.|title=A brief Introduction to MIDI|date=May 1997|url=http://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~nd/surprise_97/journal/vol1/aps2/|work=SURPRISE|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120830211425/http://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~nd/surprise_97/journal/vol1/aps2/|publisher=Imperial College of Science Technology and Medicine|access-date=22 August 2012|archive-date=30 August 2012}} < /ref >    
 
 A MIDI recording is not an [[Signal (electrical engineering)|audio signal]]. It is more like a piano roll, identifying the pitch, start and stop time and other properties of each individual note. < ref name= " russ2012 " > {{cite book |last=Russ |first=Martin |year=2012 |title=Sound Synthesis and Sampling |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=X9h5AgAAQBAJ & pg=PA192 |publisher=[[CRC Press]] |isbn= 978-1136122149 |page=192 |access-date=26 April 2017 }} < /ref >
