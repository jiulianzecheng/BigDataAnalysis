 The name '''Leah''' [ '''lee'''-''uh'' ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Leah {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/leah|access-date=2023-03-31|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  has [[Bible|biblical]] roots, and means ''wild cow'' in [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]]. Leah is a timeless and exquisite moniker that has been in vogue for centuries, boasting a compelling past and profound significance. This name, derived from Hebrew roots, translates to  " weary "  or  " tired, "  and has a prominent place in biblical literature, making it a beloved choice among Jewish lineages for countless generations. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Leah name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Leah-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-31|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  This name was ranked #46 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-31|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >   
 
 In [[Genesis]], the first book of the Bible, Leah is the older sister of [[Rachel]]. Leah marries [[Jacob]] and becomes the mother of six sons: [[Reuben]], [[Simeon]], [[Levi]], [[Judah (Biblical personage)]], [[Issachar]], [[Zebulun]], and a daughter, [[Dinah]].
