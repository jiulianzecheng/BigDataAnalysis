 '''Catalepsy''' is a nervous condition. The body is fixed, with [[wikt:rigid|rigid]] [[muscles]] and fixed [[wikt:posture|posture]]. The body position does not respond to [[stimuli]], and there is  decreased sensitivity to [[pain]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.medterms.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11104 |title=Medical terms |access-date=2014-08-14 |archive-date=2003-08-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030819190538/http://www.medterms.com/script/main/art.asp?articlekey=11104 |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Causes== 
 Catalepsy is a [[symptom]] of certain nervous disorders or conditions such as [[Parkinson's disease]] and [[epilepsy]]. It is also a characteristic symptom of [[cocaine]] withdrawal.
