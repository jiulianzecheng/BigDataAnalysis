 '''Indiana''' is a [[U.S. state]] in the [[Midwestern United States|midwestern]] and [[Great Lakes]] regions of North America. Its [[Capital (city)|capital]] and largest city is [[Indianapolis, Indiana|Indianapolis]]. Other cities and towns include [[Bloomington, Indiana|Bloomington]], [[Gary, Indiana|Gary]], [[Fort Wayne, Indiana|Fort Wayne]], [[South Bend, Indiana|South Bend]], [[Evansville, Indiana|Evansville]], [[Muncie, Indiana|Muncie]], [[Lafayette, Indiana|Lafayette]], and [[Marion, Indiana|Marion]]. People who live in Indiana are sometimes called ''Hoosiers''. 
 
 Indiana's state bird is the [[Northern cardinal|cardinal]]. Indiana's state flower is the [[peony]]. Indiana's state tree is a tulip tree. 
 
 Indiana has [[border]]s with four other states: [[Illinois]], [[Kentucky]], [[Michigan]], and [[Ohio]]. It also touches [[Lake Michigan]], which is the only one of the [[Great Lakes]] that is completely inside the United States. 
 
 ==History== 
 Before it was called Indiana, most people who lived there were [[Indigenous peoples|indigenous]]. [[France]] [[colonized]] the area, renaming it [[New France]]. They [[Slavery|enslaved]] the indigenous peoples, and introduced [[black]] slaves. < ref > {{Cite book|url=http://archive.org/details/indianaaredempt00dunngoog|title=Indiana: A Redemption from Slavery|last=Jacob Piatt Dunn|date=1888|publisher=Houghton, Mifflin  &  Co.|others=University of Michigan|language=English}} < /ref >  In the [[18th century]], the United States took the area from France. Many slave owners from [[Kentucky]] and [[Virginia]] moved into the area. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hgYUAAAAYAAJ|title=Travel Accounts of Indiana, 1679-1961: A Collection of Observations by Wayfaring Foreigners, Itinerants, and Peripatetic Hoosiers|last=McCord|first=Shirley S.|date=1970|publisher=Indiana Historical Bureau|isbn=9781885323248 |language=en}} < /ref >  By 1820, it had become a state called Indiana. The government ordered all slaves to be freed, but black people were still enslaved there into at least the 1830s. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://dmr.bsu.edu/digital/collection/BSMngrph/id/3|title=The Politics of Pro-Slavery Sentiment in Indiana 1816-1861|last1=Rosenberg|first1=Morton M.|last2=McClurg|first2=Dennis V.|publisher=Ball State University|year=1968|isbn=|location=|pages=|quote=Some Hoosiers continued to ignore the Lasselle verdict as the Federal Census of 1830 indicated, for three slaves were registered as still residing in Indiana. Moreover, a local census in Vincennes and Knox County revealed thirty-two slaves there.}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Attractions== 
 One attraction of Indiana is the [[Indianapolis Motor Speedway]], where the [[Indianapolis 500]] is held every year. The Indianapolis 500 is one of the biggest car races in the United States. The Indianapolis Motor Speedway is not actually in Indianapolis. It is in a town called [[Speedway]], which is completely surrounded by Indianapolis. 
 
 ==Sports== 
 The [[Indianapolis 500]] car race is based in Indianapolis. Professional sports teams include the [[Indianapolis Colts]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL) and the [[Indiana Pacers]] of the [[National Basketball Association]] (NBA). Colleges and universities in Indiana with well-known sports teams include [[Purdue University]], the [[University of Notre Dame]], and [[Indiana University]].
