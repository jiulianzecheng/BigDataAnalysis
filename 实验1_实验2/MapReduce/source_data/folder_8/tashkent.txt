 '''Tashkent''' ({{lang-uz|Toshkent or Тошкент}}, {{lang-ru|Ташкент}})) is the [[capital city]] of [[Uzbekistan]]. It is also the most populated city in [[Central Asia]], with a population of 2,603,000 in 2023. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Tashkent, Uzbekistan Metro Area Population 1950-2023|url=https://www.macrotrends.net/cities/23209/tashkent/population|access-date=2023-06-02|website=www.macrotrends.net}} < /ref >  Its name means  " Stone City "  in [[English language|English]]. 
 
 Tashkent has been inhabited by people for thousands of years. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.fergananews.com/articles/6299|title=Юбилей Ташкента. Такое бывает только раз в 2200 лет|website=Фергана - международное агентство новостей|access-date=2017-12-10}} < /ref >  It was destroyed by [[Genghis Khan]] in 1219, but later profited from the [[Silk Road]]. Tashkent was conquered by the [[Russian Empire]] in 1865, and became the capital of [[Russian Turkestan]]. < ref > Malikov A., ''The Russian conquest of the Bukharan Emirate: military and diplomatic aspects in Central Asian Survey'', Volume 33, Issue 2, 2014, pp. & nbsp;180–198. < /ref >  Under the [[Soviet Union]], Tashkent grew in population. 
 
 In 2008, [[Uzbek people|Uzbek]]s accounted for 63% of the population, and the city has large minorities of [[Russians]], [[Tatars]], [[Koreans]], and [[Tajiks]].

Tashkent is situated at around 430 & nbsp;m (1,411 & nbsp;ft) [[elevation|above sea level]].  == Main sights == 
 Tashkent is full is historical sights, and this article will just mention a few. 
 
 [[File:Timur Lane Museum, Tashkent, Uzbekistan.JPG|thumb|200px|right|The Amir Timur Museum.]] 
 [[File:Madrasah Kukaldash (Tashkent) 12-16.JPG|thumb|200px|right|The Kukeldash Madrasah in 2014.]] 
 [[File:Theatre Alisher Navoi.JPG|thumb|200px|right|The Navoi Theater.]] 
 The [[Amir Timur Museum]] was opened in 1996, and is dedicated to Mongol conqueror [[Timur]]. There are at least 5,000 artifacts on display at the museum. Around 2 million people visit the location each year, and the museum is featured on the 1,000 [[Uzbekistani soʻm|soʻm]] bank note. The museum is located downtown next to the Amir Temur Square. 
 
 The [[Kukeldash Madrasah (Tashkent)]] is an ancient [[madrasa]] located close to the [[Chorsu (Tashkent Metro)|Chorsu metro station]]. It was built in the 16th century during [[Shaybanids|Shaybanid]] rule. < ref name= " cat " > {{cite web|url=http://www.centralasia-travel.com/ru/countries/uzbekistan/places/tashkent/kukeldash|title=Медресе Кукельдаш|publisher=Central Asia Travel|language=Russian|accessdate=30 October 2015}} < /ref >  Throughout its existence, it functioned as a [[caravanserai]], [[museum]], and [[fortress]]. It was one of the few buildings to survive the [[1966 Tashkent earthquake|1966 earthquake]]. 
 
 The [[Navoi Theater]], formally the Alisher Navoi Opera and Ballet Theatre, is the possibly the most notable [[opera house]] and [[theatre]] in [[Uzbekistan]]. It was designed by [[Alexey Shchusev]], who also designed [[Lenin's Mausoleum]] in [[Moscow]]. It was partially built by the [[Japanese prisoners of war in the Soviet Union|Japanese prisoners of war]] from 1945 to 1947. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/751737081|title=Uzbekistan : the golden road to Samarkand|last=McLeod|first=Calum|date=2010|publisher=Odyssey|others=Mayhew, Bradley.|isbn=9789622178236|edition=7th|location=Hong Kong|pages=99|oclc=751737081}} < /ref >  The theatre can house at least 1,400 spectators. 
 
 == Sports == 
 Tashkent is home to some of the most successful Uzbek [[association football|football]] clubs. These include [[Pakhtakor Tashkent FK|Pakhtakor Tashkent]], [[Lokomotiv Tashkent FK|Lokomotiv Tashkent]], [[FC Bunyodkor|Bunyodkor]], and [[FC Spartak Tashkent|Spartak Tashkent]]. 
 
 Some notable athletes from Tashkent include cyclist [[Djamolidine Abdoujaparov]], gymnast [[Elvira Saadi]], and footballer [[Maksim Shatskikh]].
