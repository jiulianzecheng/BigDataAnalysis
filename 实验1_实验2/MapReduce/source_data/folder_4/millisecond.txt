 A '''millisecond''' (sometimes shortened to 'ms')  is one thousandth of a [[second]]. This page lists times between '''10 < sup > −3 < /sup >  seconds''' and 10 < sup > −2 < /sup >  seconds (1 [[milli]] < nowiki > second < /nowiki >  to 10 milliseconds). 
 
 One millisecond contains 1000 [[microsecond]]s. There are 1000 milliseconds in one second. 
 
 * 1 millisecond – the time it takes for a normal [[flash (photo)|flash]] in a camera to finish. 
 * 1 millisecond – time taken for sound to travel 34 [[Centimeter|cm]] 
 * 1.000692286 milliseconds – time taken for light to travel 300 & nbsp;km in a vacuum 
 * 2 milliseconds – [[Half-life (element)|half life]] of [[hassium]]-265 
 * 3 milliseconds – a [[housefly]]’s wing flap 
 * 3.4 milliseconds – half life of [[meitnerium]]-266 
 * 5 milliseconds – a honey[[bee]]’s wing flap 
 * 8 milliseconds – 1/125 of a second (125), a [[camera]] [[shutter speed]] 
 * 9 milliseconds – normal [[seek time]] for a 7200rpm [[hard disk]] 
 * 33.3 milliseconds  -  the amount of time one frame lasts in 30fps video 
 * 30 to 100 milliseconds  -  normal [[lag|latency]] for a [[broadband internet]] connection 
 * 100 milliseconds  -  the [[reaction speed]] of a human 
 * 102 milliseconds  -  [[Half-life (element)|half-life]] of [[bohrium]]-262 
 * 134 milliseconds  -  the amount of time it takes by [[light]] to travel around the [[earth]]'s [[equator]] 
 * 200 to 670 milliseconds  -  the [[tempo]] of modern dance music (128 - 150 [[beats per minute|BPM]]) 
 * 300 to 400 milliseconds  -  the [[blinking|blink]] of a [[eye|human eye]] 
 * 838 milliseconds  -  [[Half-life (element)|half-life]] of [[lithium]]-8 
 
 The basic interval, in time manners, in most of the programming languages is 1 millisecond . e.g. the [[Command (computing)|command]] delay() in [[C++ (programming language)|C++]]. Others use second interval.
