 '''Simlish''' is a made-up [[language]] spoken in the ''Sims'' [[video games]].  It was created because changing the Sims' words into the players' language would cost a lot of money and would make the Sims say the same words all the time.{{cn}}  Simlish is spoken by [[Sims]], the [[fictional character|characters]] in ''[[The Sims]]'', ''[[The Sims 2]]'', ''[[The Sims 3]]'' and ''[[The Sims 4]]''. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://uk.videogames.games.yahoo.com/ngc/previews/the-sims-bustin--out-e72277.html |title=The Sims Bustin' Out Designer Diary #3 |access-date=2008-07-29 |archive-date=2006-01-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060106145215/http://uk.videogames.games.yahoo.com/ngc/previews/the-sims-bustin--out-e72277.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Simlish is a language  
 
 == Sentences in Simlish == 
 All of these phrases are from ''The Sims''. 
 *[[Male]] Sims speaking -  " Homina blastima. " 
 *[[Female]] Sims speaking -  " Enama polap. " 
 *[[Child]] Sims speaking -  " Sizzaza Za " , and  " The sungoos keekaf terweek. " 
 *Female Sims playing arcade games -  " Itwack, itcack. " 
 *Adult Sims speaking on the telephone -  " A beboo. " 
 *Child Sims getting on a bus -  " Yabba doochia. " 
 *Adult male Sims saying goodbye -  " Mal-Mal. " 
 
 == Written Simlish == 
 Written Simlish is seen on ''The Sims 2'', ''The Sims 3'', and ‘‘The Sims 4’’. It looks similar to [[Zodiac]] symbols taken from the [[Wingdings]] [[font]] on a [[computer]].  Text such as the names of Sims, hints and tips and on-screen messages are all in the selected language (i.e. [[English language|English]], [[French language|French]], [[Spanish language|Spanish]]), and are usually written in the Comic Sans MS font.
