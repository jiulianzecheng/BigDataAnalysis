[[Bahrain]] was referred to by the Greeks as '''Tylos'''.  It was the centre of pearl trading, when ''Nearchus'' came to discover it serving under [[Alexander the Great]]. < ref name= " Larsen " > Life and Land Use on the Bahrain Islands: The Geoarcheology of an Ancient Society By Curtis E. Larsen p. 13 < /ref >  From the 6th to 3rd century BC Bahrain was in the [[Persian Empire]]. < ref name= " Mojtahed-Zadeh " > Security and Territoriality in the Persian Gulf: A Maritime Political Geography By Pirouz Mojtahed-Zadeh, page 119 < /ref >  The Greek historian, [[Theophrastus]], states that much of the islands were covered in cotton trees. Tylos was famous for selling walking canes that were carried in Babylon. < ref > Arnold Heeren, ibid, p441 < /ref > 
 
 It is not known whether Bahrain was part of the [[Seleucid Empire]]. < ref > Classical Greece: Ancient histories and modern archaeologies, Ian Morris, Routledge, p184 < /ref >   Alexander planned to settle the eastern shores of the Persian Gulf with Greek colonists. The upper classes spoke [[Greece|Greek]]. < ref > Phillip Ward, Bahrain: A Travel Guide, Oleander Press p68 < /ref >  Tylos became a site of Greek athletic contests. < ref > W. B. Fisher et al. The Cambridge History of Iran, Cambridge University Press 1968 p40 < /ref > 
 
 With the lessening of Greek power, Tylos was added into [[Characene]] or Mesenian. From the third century BC to the arrival of Islam in the seventh AD, Bahrain was controlled by two other Iranian dynasties of [[Parthians]] and [[Sassanids]].
