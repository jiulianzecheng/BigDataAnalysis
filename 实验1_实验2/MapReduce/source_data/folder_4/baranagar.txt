 '''Baranagar''' or '''Baranagore''' is a city and municipality of [[North 24 Parganas district]] in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[West Bengal]]. It is in the region of [[Kolkata metropolitan area|Greater Kolkata]] and therefore is a part of the area covered by [[Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority]] (KMDA). 
 
 It is home to the [[Indian Statistical Institute]] for research, teaching and application of statistics, natural sciences and social sciences. < ref name=ISI/ >  [[Baranagore Ramakrishna Mission Ashrama High School]] is one of the oldest and renowned schools in Baranagar and [[North 24 Parganas]]. < ref name= " BRKM " > {{cite web|url=https://belurmath.org/ramakrishna-mission-ashrama-baranagar-kolkata/|title=Ramakrishna Mission Ashrama, Baranagar, Kolkata|access-date=13 June 2018}} < /ref > 
 
 Baranagar is a major industrial centre for the manufacture of agricultural and industrial machinery, chemicals, castor oil, and matches. There are also many cotton-processing companies. 
 
 ==Name== 
 [[File:LL-Q9610 (ben)-Titodutta-বরাহনগর.wav|thumb|Pronunciation of ''Baranagar'' in Bengali]] 
 The word ''Baranagar'' derives from the Bengali term ''Barahanagore'' ([[Bengali language|Bengali]]: বরাহনগর), meaning  " City of the Hogs " . (বরাহ: hog, নগর: city) 
 
 [[Streynsham Master]] who visited the area in 1676 spoke of the hog factory where about 3,000 hogs a year were slaughtered and salted for export. < ref name= " Cotton " > Cotton, H.E.A., ''Calcutta Old and New'', 1909/1980, pp 806–807, General Printers and Publishers Pvt. Ltd. < /ref >
