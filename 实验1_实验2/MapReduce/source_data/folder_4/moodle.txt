 '''Moodle''' is a [[free and open-source]] [[learning management system]] written in [[PHP]] and distributed under the [[GNU General Public License]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=TFD Encyclopedia - Moodle|url=http://encyclopedia2.thefreedictionary.com/Moodle}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book|title=The Encyclopedia of Distance Learning, Vol1|isbn = 9781605661995|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sC9Le3jIwzIC & dq=moodle+in+encyclopedia & pg=PA505|last1 = Rogers|first1 = Patricia L.|last2 = Berg|first2 = Gary A.|last3 = Boettcher|first3 = Judith V.|last4 = Howard|first4 = Caroline|last5 = Justice|first5 = Lorraine|last6 = Schenk|first6 = Karen D.|date = 31 January 2009}} < /ref >  It was designed for education as online learning space. 
 
 The name ''moodle'' stands for  " Module Object Oriented Dynamic Learning Environment " . 
 
 It was developed by [[Martin Dougiamas]]. 
 
 == Development == 
 Moodle has continued to evolve since 1999 (since 2001 with the current architecture). It has been translated into over 100 different languages and is accessible in many countries worldwide. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://docs.moodle.org/en/Language_packs|title=Language packs - MoodleDocs|publisher=}} < /ref >  Institutions can add as many Moodle servers as needed without having to pay license fees.
