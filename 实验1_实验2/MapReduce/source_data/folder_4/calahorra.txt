 '''Calahorra''', ({{IPA-es|kalaˈora|pron}}), [[La Rioja (autonomous community)|La Rioja]], [[Spain]] is a [[Municipality of Spain|municipality]] in [[Rioja Baja]]. It is near the border with [[Navarre]] on the [[river]] [[Ebro]]. During [[ancient Roman]] times, Calahorra was known as ''Calagurris Fibularia''. 
 
 ==Location== 
 The city is located on a [[hill]] at an [[altitude]] of {{convert|358|m}} at the [[confluence]] of the Ebro and [[Cidacos]] rivers. It has an [[area ]]of {{convert|91.41|km2}}. Calahorra is the second-largest city in La Rioja in [[population]] and importance. 21,060 people live there. 
 
 == History == 
 People have lived in the area that is now Calahorra since the [[Paleolithic]]. It has had a stable [[population]] since the [[Iron Age]]. 
 
 [[Roman Empire|Rome]] took control of the town in 187 BC. They made it more important as an [[administration|administrative centre]] for [[region]]s near it. [[Julius Caesar]] and [[Augustus Caesar]] gave the city (then named ''Calagurris'') converted the town into a [[municipality]]. They developed its [[city planning]], [[economy]], and [[politics]]. Its [[archaeology |archeological]] remains show that it had a [[circus]], [[Roman bath|baths]], an [[amphitheatre]], and other things found in large cities. It [[minting|minted]] [[money]] and served as a [[justice]] administration centre. 
 
 After the rule of the [[Moors]] in the 9th and 10th centuries the [[Christianity|Christian]] king [[García Sánchez III of Navarre]] captured the city in 1045.
