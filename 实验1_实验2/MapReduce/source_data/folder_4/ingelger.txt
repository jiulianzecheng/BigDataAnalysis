'''Ingelger''' (or '''Ingelgarius''') ([[:wikt:†|†]] {{circa|888}}) was a [[Franks|Frankish]] [[nobleman]] who became the first [[count of Anjou]]. He began a [[:en:wikt:dynasty|dynasty]] known as [[House of Ingelger]].  
 
 == Career == 
 
 Ingelger was the son of a Frankish nobleman Tertullus and his wife Petronilla. < ref > Francis Palgrave, ''The History of Normandy and of England'', Vol. 1 (London: John Parker  &  Sons, 1851), p. 502 < /ref >  She was the niece of Raino, Bishop of Angers. < ref name=Bachrach5 > Bernard S. Bachrach, ''Fulk Nerra, the Neo-Roman Consul, 987–1040'' (Berkely: University of California Press, 1993), p. 5 < /ref >  Petronilla was also a relative of [[Hugh the Abbot]], an counselor to both [[Louis II of France|Louis II]] and [[Louis III of France]]. His mother's [[:wikt:influence|influence]] was one of the reasons for Ingelger's success. Records of the time call Ingelger a ''miles optimus'' (a great military man). < ref name=Bachrach4.5 > Bernard S. Bachrach, ''Fulk Nerra, the Neo-Roman Consul, 987–1040'' (Berkely: University of California Press, 1993), pp. 4-5 < /ref >  Louis II appointed Ingelger as [[viscount]] of [[Orléans]] which at the time was under the rule of the Bishop of Orléans. < ref name=Bachrach4.5/ >  While at Orléans Ingelger married Adelais. Later Ingelger was appointed [[prefect]] (military commander) at Tours. < ref name=Bachrach4.5/ >  Some time after this Ingelger was made [[Count of Anjou]]. At this time the county went only as far west as the [[Mayenne River]]. < ref name=Bachrach4.5/ >  He was buried in the church of Saint-Martin at [[Châteauneuf-sur-Sarthe]].
