 '''Brecqhou''' is one of the [[Channel Islands]]. It is part of the [[Bailiwick of Guernsey]]. It is just west of [[Sark]] and has a surface area of about 200 & nbsp;acres (0.81 & nbsp;km < sup > 2 < /sup > ; 0.31 sq & nbsp;mi). 
 
 It is outside of the [[United Kingdom]] and not part of the  [[European union]]. Brecqhou has a castle that belongs to the [[David and Frederick Barclay|Barclay brothers]], owners of London's ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]''. The castle is sometimes used for meetings and filing of papers outside of the United Kingdom. < ref > Nicholas Shaxson: Treasure Islands, Tax Havens and the Men Who Stole the World; The Bodley Head, London, 2011. < /ref >
