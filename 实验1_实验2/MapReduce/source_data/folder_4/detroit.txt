 '''Detroit''' ({{IPAc-en|d|ᵻ|ˈ|t|r|ɔɪ|t}} {{respell|dih|TROYT}}, {{small|locally also}} {{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|iː|t|r|ɔɪ|t}} {{respell|DEE|troyt}}; {{Lang-fr|Détroit}}, {{Literal translation|strait}}) is the most populous [[city]] in the state of [[Michigan]] in the [[United States]]. In 1950, Detroit was the fifth most populous city in the United States, with 1.8 million people. It was the 10th most populous city in the United States at the time of the [[2000 United States Census|2000 census]], with 950,000 people. By the [[2020 United States Census|2020 census]], Detroit fell to the 27th largest, because people moved away, and it had 640,000 people. Over four million people live in Detroit and its [[Metro Detroit|surrounding counties]]. The city borders [[Windsor, Ontario]] in [[Canada]]. The [[international]] [[border]] between Detroit and Windsor is one of the most crossed in the world. 
 
 The person who started the city was [[Antoine de la Mothe Cadillac]]. He was from [[France]]. Detroit was made a city in 1701. From 1805 to 1847, Detroit was the capital of Michigan. 
 
 Detroit ran out of money in March 2013. On July 18, 2013, Detroit filed for [[bankruptcy]]. This was the largest bankruptcy case of any city in U.S. history. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.economist.com/blogs/democracyinamerica/2013/11/detroit|title=Detroit: Bankruptcy or bust|date=2013-11-15|publisher=The Economist|accessdate=2013-11-20}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|title=Creditors to fight Detroit insolvency claim |website=The Detroit News |date=2013-07-18 |url=http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20130718/METRO01/307180103/#ixzz2ZQqjpHYO |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130810023805/http://www.detroitnews.com/article/20130718/METRO01/307180103/#ixzz2ZQqjpHYO| archive-date=2013-08-10}} < /ref > 
 
 == Sports == 
 Detroit is a city where many [[automobile]]s are made and this is why it is sometimes called the  " Motor City " , or  " Motown " . Many people call it the car capital of the world.  [[General Motors]], [[Ford Motor Company|Ford]] and [[Chrysler]] have their offices and many of their plants in and around Detroit. 
 
 Detroit is the home of many sport teams including the [[Detroit Lions]] ([[National Football League|NFL]]), [[Detroit Red Wings]] ([[National Hockey League|NHL]]), [[Detroit Tigers]] ([[Major League Baseball|MLB]]) and the [[Detroit City FC|Detroit City Soccer Club]]. 
 
 In 2006, the [[Super Bowl XL|Super Bowl]] was played at [[Ford Field]] in Detroit.

 == People == 
 Detroit is home to one of the largest black [[Community|communities]] in the United States, with 77% being [[African-American]]. Detroit also has a large Arab population. Many Arabs live in metro Detroit. The [[Arab Americans|Arab-American]] community in Detroit began with a small group of Syrian and Lebanese merchants who immigrated to Detroit in the late 1800s. < ref > [https://wdet.org/posts/2017/08/08/85592-curiosid-how-did-detroit-become-a-center-for-arabs-in-the-united-states/ CuriosiD: How Did Detroit Become a Center for Arabs in the United States?] < /ref >  Mexicans have recently replaced the shrinking population. < ref > [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2012-09-24/the-paradox-of-mexicantown-detroit-s-uncomfortable-relationship-with-the-immigrants-it-desperately-needs The Paradox of Mexicantown: Detroit's Uncomfortable Relationship With the Immigrants it Desperately Needs] < /ref > 
 
 Violent crime rate is one of the highest in the USA. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.neighborhoodscout.com/mi/detroit/crime/ |title=Detroit crime rates and statistics |publisher=Neighborhood Scout |access-date=July 1, 2010}} < /ref > 
 
 == Climate == 
 Detroit has a [[humid continental climate]] (''Dfa'' in the [[Köppen climate classification]]), and is influenced by the nearby [[Great Lakes]]. Detroit usually has around 38 days of snow every year.
