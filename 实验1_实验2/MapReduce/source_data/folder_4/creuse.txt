 '''Creuse''' ({{lang-oc|Cruesa}}) is a [[Departments of France|department]] in central [[France]] in the [[Regions of France|region]] of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]]. It is named after the [[Creuse (river)|Creuse]] river that flows through the department. 
 
 ==History== 
 Creuse is one of the 83 French departments made during the [[French revolution]], on 4 March 1790. It was created mainly with lands of the old [[province]] of [[County of La Marche]]. 
 
 The new department had seven [[district]]s: La Souterraine, Guéret, Boussac, Évaux, Felletin, Bourganeuf and Aubusson. The [[Capital city|capital]] (now [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]]) was [[Guéret]] alternating with [[Aubusson, Creuse|Aubusson]], but soon only Guéret remained as capital. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/23his.html |title=Historique de la Creuse |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=5 September 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the seven districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Guéret, Aubusson, Bourganeuf and Boussac. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissements'' of Bourganeuf et Boussac were eliminated. < ref name=splaf / >

 The department is in the northwestern part of the [[Massif Central]]; the ''Millevaches'' [[plateau]] is in the southeastern part of the department. The highest point is in ''Forêt de Châteauvert'' ({{Coord|45|42|38|N|02|20|45|E|name=Forêt de Châteauvert|display=inline|region:ID_type:mountain}}),  " commune "  of [[Saint-Oradoux-de-Chirouze]], southeastern of the department and in northeastern Massif Central; it is  {{convert|932|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} high; < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9690 |title=Forêt de Châteauvert, France |publisher=Peakbagger.com |language= |accessdate=5 September 2016 |date= }} < /ref >  the lowest point is in the [[valley]] of the [[Creuse (river)|Creuse]] river, {{convert|175|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} high. 
 
 The main [[river]] in the department is the [[Creuse (river)|Creuse]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]] river and that gives its name to this the deparment; it flows through the department from the southeast to the northwest. 
 
 ==Administration== 
 The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Creuse'' in [[Guéret]]. Creuse is part of the region of [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]]. 
 
 ===Administrative divisions=== 
 There are 2 [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] ([[district]]s), 15 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']] and 258 [[Communes in France|''communes'']] ([[Municipality|municipalities]]) in Haute-Vienne. < ref name=insee1 > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/departement/DEP23-creuse |title=Département de la Creuse (23) |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=French |accessdate=22 May 2017 |date= }} < /ref >

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Creuse are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Creusois'' (women: ''Creusoises''). < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/creuse-23 |title=Creuse (23)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=5 September 2016 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Creuse has a population, in 2014, of 120,581, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|120581|5565.4|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Guéret, with 83,458 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other ''arrondissement'', Aubusson, has 37,123 inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
