 '''Thucydides''' ({{IPAc-en|θj|uː|ˈ|s|ɪ|d|ɪ|d|iː|z}}; {{lang-grc-gre|Θουκυδίδης}}; born around 460 BC, died around 400 BC) was an ancient [[Ancient Greece|Greek]] [[history|historian]] best remembered for his work on the [[Peloponnesian War]]. He was the son of [[Olorus]]. < ref > {{cite book |title= Selections from Greek and Roman historians |last=Robinson Jr. |first=C.A. |date= May 1964 |publisher= Holt, Rinehart and Winston |chapter=Introduction |pages=xiv}} < /ref >
