 '''Hummus''' or '''houmous''' < ref name=camb > {{cite web|url=http://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/hummus?a=british|title=hummus meaning in the Cambridge English Dictionary|website=dictionary.cambridge.org|accessdate=27 April 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170807153452/http://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/hummus?a=british|archivedate=7 August 2017|df=dmy-all}} < /ref > < ref name=oed > {{cite web|url=http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/hummus|title=hummus - Definition of hummus in English by Oxford Dictionaries|website=Oxford Dictionaries - English|accessdate=27 April 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160717071636/http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/hummus|archivedate=17 July 2016|df=dmy-all}} < /ref >  full Arabic name: ''ḥummuṣ bi-ṭ-ṭaḥīna'' {{lang-ar|حمص بالطحينة}}, meaning 'chickpeas with tahini' is a food dip or spread that is made from [[chickpea]]s, [[tahini]], [[olive oil]], [[lemon]] [[juice]], [[Table salt|salt]] and [[garlic]].  " Hummus "  is Arabic for  " chickpeas "  that is said to be of [[Egypt|Egyptian]] origin. < ref > {{Cite web|title=14th Century Egyptian Hummus|url=https://www.mediterraneanliving.com/recipe-items/14th-century-egyptian-hummus/|access-date=2021-05-27|website=Mediterranean Living|language=en-US|archive-date=2021-05-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210508202713/https://www.mediterraneanliving.com/recipe-items/14th-century-egyptian-hummus/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Spechler|first=Diana|title=Who invented hummus?|url=https://www.bbc.com/travel/story/20171211-who-invented-hummus|access-date=2021-05-27|website=www.bbc.com|language=en}} < /ref >  The dish then spread across [[The Levant]] and [[North Africa]]. It is high in [[nutrition|nutrients]], for instance [[protein]], iron, [[vitamin C]] and B < sub > 6 < /sub > , and dietary fiber. Hummus is often eaten with [[pita]]. When hummus is served with [[bread]] the proteins of the hummus and of the bread go well together, creating a delicious taste. It has become popular in the Middle East and many other countries. It is considered a [[Healthy diet|health]] food.
