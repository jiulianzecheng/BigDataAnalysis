 '''''Kronosaurus''''' was a large [[pliosaur]], one of the largest. < ref name= " Longman, 1924 " > Longman H.A. 1924. A new gigantic marine reptile from the Queensland Cretaceous, ''Kronosaurus queenslandicus'' new genus and species. ''Memoirs of the Queensland Museum'' '''8''': 26–28. < /ref > < ref name= " Hampe, 1992 " > Hampe O. 1992. Ein großwüchsiger Pliosauride (Reptilia: Plesiosauria) aus der Unterkreide (oberes Aptium) von Kolumbien. ''Courier Forschungsinstitut Senckenberg'' '''145''': 1-32. < /ref >  It is named after the leader of the Greek [[Titans]], [[Kronos]]. It lived in the [[Lower Cretaceous]]. 
 
 Body-length estimates, based on the 1959 [[Harvard University|Harvard]] reconstruction, had put the total length of ''Kronosaurus'' at 12.8 meters (42 feet). < ref name= " Romer  &  Lewis, 1959 " > Romer A.S.  &  Lewis A.D. 1959. A mounted skeleton of the giant plesiosaur ''Kronosaurus''. ''Breviora'' '''112''': 1-15. < /ref >  However, a recent study compared fossil specimens of ''Kronosaurus'' to other pliosaurs. The Harvard reconstruction may have included too many vertebrae, exaggerating its length. Its true length was probably only 9 & ndash;10.9 meters (30 & ndash;36 feet). < ref name= " Kear, 2003 " > Kear B.P. 2003. Cretaceous marine reptiles of Australia: a review of taxonomy and distribution. ''Cretaceous Research'' '''24''': 277–303. < /ref > < ref name=McHenry2009 > McHenry, Colin R. 2009. Devourer of Gods: the palaeoecology of the Cretaceous pliosaur ''Kronosaurus queenslandicus''. The University of Newcastle, N.S.W. Australia, Web. [http://nova.newcastle.edu.au/vital/access/services/Download/uon:12164/ATTACHMENT02] < /ref >
