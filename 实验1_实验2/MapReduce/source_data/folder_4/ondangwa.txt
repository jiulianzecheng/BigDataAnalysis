 '''Ondangwa,''' formerly Ondangua is a town in the [[Oshana Region]] of northern [[Namibia]], bordering the [[Oshikoto Region]]. Ondangwa was first settled by the Finnish Missionary Society in 1890, which is [[Lutheran]]. < ref name= " split " > {{cite news|last=Nakale|first=Albertina|date=9 August 2013|title=President divides Kavango into two|newspaper=[[New Era (Namibia)|New Era]]|url=http://www.newera.com.na/articles/53580/President-divides-Kavango-into-two|url-status=dead|access-date=28 January 2022|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130809090822/http://www.newera.com.na/articles/53580/President-divides-Kavango-into-two|archive-date=9 August 2013}} < /ref > < ref name= " election2020 " > {{Cite web|title=Regional Council 2020 Election Results|publisher=[[Electoral Commission of Namibia]]|date=18 January 2021|series=''Interactive map''|url=http://elections.na/|accessdate=7 November 2021|archive-date=12 June 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210612210020/https://elections.na/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Ondangwa is located about {{convert|60|km|0|abbr=on}} from the [[Angola]]n border, with 23,000 people currently living there. Most of the residents of the town speak the language of Oshindonga. < ref > {{Cite news|year=2015|title=Know Your Local Authority|page=4|newspaper=Election Watch|publisher=Institute for Public Policy Research|issue=3}} < /ref > 
 
 Ondangwa is the district capital of the [[Ondangwa Urban|Ondangwa Urban electoral constituency]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ondangwa Constituency|publisher=Oshana Regional Council|url=http://www.oshanarc.com/ondangwa/|accessdate=15 January 2019}} < /ref >
