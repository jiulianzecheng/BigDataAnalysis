 '''Marseille''' ([[Occitan language|Occitan]]: ''Marselha'') is a city in the south of [[France]] in the [[Bouches-du-Rhône]] department. About 1.7 million people live in the metropolitan area, and about 850,000 in the [[city]] itself. This makes it the second largest city in France by number of people. Its commercial [[port]]  is the biggest in [[France]] and one of the most important in the [[Mediterranean sea]]. 
 
 Although part of the region of [[Provence]], Marseilles has its own history.  This city is the oldest in France and probably the most complex.  The city was started around [[600 BC]] by [[Ancient Greece|Greek]] sailors from [[Phocaea]] (modern day Foça, near [[İzmir]]).  This was a Greek [[colony]] in [[Asia Minor]] that is in what is now [[Turkey]]. 
 
 Marseille has a hot-summer [[Mediterranean climate]] (''Csa'' in the [[Koeppen climate classification]]).
