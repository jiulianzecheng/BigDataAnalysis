 '''Cosmology''' is the branch of [[astronomy]] that deals with the origin, structure, evolution and space-time relationships of the [[universe]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/cosmology|title=Definition of COSMOLOGY|website=www.merriam-webster.com}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book|title=Collins Dictionary of Astronomy|publisher=HarperCollins|date=2012|origyear=2006|edition=Fifth|first1=John|last1=Daintith|first2=William|last2=Gould|isbn=9780007918485|page=96}} < /ref >  [[NASA]] defines cosmology as  " The study of the structure and changes in the present universe " . < ref name= " NASA " > {{cite web|url=http://genesismission.jpl.nasa.gov/educate/scimodule/Cosmogony/CosmogonyPDF/CosCosmolTT.pdf|title=NASA education|publisher=}} < /ref >  Another definition of cosmology is  " the study of the universe, and humanity's place in it " . < ref name=bs > {{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/schools/gcsebitesize/rs/|title=Christianity: beliefs about creation and evolution|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2011-11-03}} < /ref > 
 
 Modern cosmology is dominated by the [[Big Bang]] theory, which brings together observational astronomy and [[particle physics]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/cosmology|title=cosmology - Definition of cosmology in English by Oxford Dictionaries|website=Oxford Dictionaries - English|access-date=2019-02-11|archive-date=2019-03-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190321112227/https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/cosmology|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 Though the word ''cosmology'' is recent (first used in 1730 in [[Christian Wolff]]'s ''Cosmologia Generalis''), the study of the universe has a long history. 
 
 ==History== 
 Until the [[Renaissance]] people thought the universe was only the planets up to [[Saturn (planet)|Saturn]], and [[star]]s. With the invention of the [[telescope]], we could see more of the universe. Early in the 20th century, astronomers thought the [[Milky Way]] was the entire universe. Later, with astrophotography and [[spectroscopy]], astronomers (for example [[Edwin Hubble]]) showed that the Milky Way was only one of many galaxies.  
 
 Modern cosmology is considered to have started in [[1915|1917]] with the final paper of [[Albert Einstein|Albert Einstein's]] [[General relativity|theory of general relativity]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Einstein|first=A.|date=1952|title=Cosmological considerations on the general theory of relativity|url=https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1952prel.book..175E/abstract|journal=The Principle of Relativity. Dover Books on Physics. June 1|language=en|pages=175–188|bibcode=1952prel.book..175E}} < /ref >  This made physicists realize that the universe changed. When a scientific discipline begins to change an idea that is believed by many people, it is known as a [[Paradigm Shift|paradigm shift]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|date=April 1963|''The Structure of Scientific Revolutions''. 1962. Thomas S. Kuhn. University of Chicago Press. |url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1086/ahr/68.3.700|journal=The American Historical Review|doi=10.1086/ahr/68.3.700|issn=1937-5239 | title=The Structure of Scientific Revolutions. By  < italic > Thomas S. Kuhn < /italic > . (Chicago: University of Chicago Press. 1962. Pp. Xv, 172. $4.00.) }} < /ref >  During this paradigm shift, the [[Great Debate]] took place. Many [[Scientist|scientists]] [[Debate|debated]] if there were other galaxies. The debate ended when [[Edwin Hubble]] found [[Cepheid variable|Cepheid Variables]] in the [[Andromeda galaxy|Andromeda Galaxy]] in 1926. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Falk|first=Dan|date=March 2009|title=Review: The day we found the Universe by Marcia Bartusiak |url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/s0262-4079(09)60809-5|journal=New Scientist|volume=201|issue=2700|pages=45|doi=10.1016/s0262-4079(09)60809-5|issn=0262-4079}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Hubble|first=E.  The Big Bang model was then proposed by [[Belgium|Belgian]] [[priest]], [[Georges Lemaître]] in 1927. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Martin|first=G.|date=1883|title=G. DELSAULX. — Sur une propriété de la diffraction des ondes planes; Annales de la Société scientifique de Bruxelles; 1882|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1051/jphystap:018830020017501|journal=Journal de Physique Théorique et Appliquée|volume=2|issue=1|pages=175|doi=10.1051/jphystap:018830020017501|issn=0368-3893}} < /ref >  This was supported by Edwin Hubble's [[Discovery doctrine|discovery]] of the [[Red shift|redshift]] in 1929. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Hubble|first=Edwin|title=A relation between distance and radial velocity among extra-galactic nebulae |date=1929-03-15|journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America| volume=15| issue=3| pages=168–173| doi=10.1073/pnas.15.3.168|issn=0027-8424|pmid=16577160|pmc=522427|doi-access=free}} < /ref >  Later the discovery of [[cosmic microwave background radiation]] was made. This was found by [[Arno Allan Penzias|Arno Penzias]] and [[Robert Wilson|Robert Woodrow Wilson]] in 1964. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Penzias|first1=A. A.|last2=Wilson|first2=R. W.|date=July 1965|title=A Measurement of Excess Antenna Temperature at 4080 Mc/s.|url=http://adsabs.harvard.edu/doi/10.1086/148307|journal=The Astrophysical Journal|language=en|volume=142|pages=419|doi=10.1086/148307|issn=0004-637X}} < /ref >   
 
 All of these discoveries have been further supported throughout the 21st century. Some more observations of the cosmic microwave background radiation were found by the [[Cosmic Background Explorer|COBE]], < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Boggess|first1=N. W.|last2=Mather|first2=J. Survey is this context refers to an [[astronomical survey]]. An astronomical survey looks at a place in [[Outer space|space]]. A redshift survey is a survey that looks for redshifts. 
 
 On 1 December 2014, at the ''Planck 2014'' meeting in [[Ferrara, Italy|Ferrara]], [[Italy]], astronomers reported that the universe is [[Age of the universe|13.8 billion years old]] and is composed of 4.9% regular matter, 26.6% [[dark matter]] and 68.5% [[dark energy]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|date=1987-10-12|title=New York Times Survey, December 1985|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.3886/icpsr08690|access-date=2021-01-28|website=ICPSR Data Holdings|doi=10.3886/icpsr08690}} < /ref >
