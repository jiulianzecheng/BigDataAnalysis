 '''Vedaranyam''' ({{IPA-ta|ʋeːdaːɾaɳjam|lang}}) (also known as '''Vedaraniam''' and '''Vedaranniyam''') is a town in [[Nagapattinam district]] in [[Tamil Nadu]], India. The town is named after the [[deity]], or god of the Vedaranyeswarar Temple. The known history of Vedaranyam is from the old Chola period in the 800s. It has also been ruled by the Later Cholas, Later Pandyas, [[Vijayanagara Empire|Vijayanagar Empire]] and the [[British Raj|British]] Empire. While India was fighting for [[independence]], the freedom for India to be ruled by itself, [[Chakravarti Rajagopalachari|C. Rajagopalachari]], who would later become independent India's first [[Governor-General of India|Governor-General]], started a [[Vedaranyam Salt Satyagraha|salt walk in Vedaranyam]] that was similar to the [[Salt March|Dandi March]] started by [[Mahatma Gandhi|Gandhi]] in 1930 to fight against the [[sales tax]] forced on getting salt from the sea. < ref > http://www.assembly.tn.gov.in/Const_map/map.htm < /ref > 
 
 Vedaranyam is in the Vedaranyam assembly constituency which selects a person to the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly once every five years{{Sfn|List of Assembly constituencies|2010}}. It is a part of the Nagapattinam (Lok Sabha constituency) which selects its [[Member of Parliament]] (MP) once in five years. The town is ruled by the Vedaranyam municipality, which rules an area of {{Convert|36.26|sqkm|sqmi|abbr=on}}. The town had a population of 34,266 people in 2011. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/vedaranyam/salient_roads.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2023-09-05 |archive-date=2011-08-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110823093620/http://municipality.tn.gov.in/vedaranyam/salient_roads.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Vedaranyam was a part of [[Thanjavur district|Thanjavur District]] up to 1991. The town is a part of the Cauvery delta region which is very good for [[farming]]. Most people work to raise prawn and finding salt inside the sea. Roads are the most used way of movement to Vedaranyam. The nearest airport is Tiruchirapalli Airport and it is {{Convert|135|km|mi|abbr=on}} from the town. On 17 November 2018 Vedaranyam was was badly hurt by cyclone Gaja. 
 
 == Origin of word == 
 Vetharanyam is named after Vedaranyeswarar, the main deity, or god of the Vedaranyeswarar Temple. It is a [[Mandir|Hindu temple]] that was created for [[Shiva]]. The place was also known as  " Tirumaraikadu " , this means that the oldest Hindu writing, or [[Vedas]] came from. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NLSGFW1uZboC & q=vedaranyam+temple & pg=PA392|title=South Indian Shrines: Illustrated|last=Ayyar|first=P. V. Jagadisa|date=1982|publisher=Asian Educational Services|isbn=978-81-206-0151-2|language=en}} < /ref >  The Saiva music work ''Tevaram'' by Appar and Tirugnanasambandar names the place as  " Tirumaraikadu " . Hindu writing says the Vedas practiced Shiva in this place and gave the name  " Vedaranyam "  to the place. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ORwNmkDswqwC & q=vedaranyam & pg=PA61|title=The Tyāgarāja Cult in Tamilnāḍu: A Study in Conflict and Accommodation|last=Ghose|first=Rajeshwari|date=1996|publisher=Motilal Banarsidass Publ.|isbn=978-81-208-1391-5|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://municipality.tn.gov.in/Vedaranyam/history.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2023-09-05 |archive-date=2012-04-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120417112450/http://municipality.tn.gov.in/Vedaranyam/history.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
