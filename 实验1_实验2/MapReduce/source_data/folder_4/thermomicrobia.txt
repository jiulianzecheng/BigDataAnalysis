 The '''Thermomicrobia''' is a group of [[thermophile|thermophilic]] green non-[[sulfur]] [[bacteria]].   
 
 The group is based on the [[type species]] ''Thermomicrobium roseum'' and on ''Sphaerobacter thermophilus''. < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite book|title=Bergey's Manual of Systematic Bacteriology, 2nd edn, vol. 1, The Archaea and the Deeply Branching and Phototrophic Bacteria| vauthors = Garrity GM, Holt JG |publisher=Springer|year=2001 | veditors = Boone DR, Castenholz RW, Garrity GM |location=New York|chapter=Phylum BVII. Thermomicrobia phy. nov.}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{cite journal | vauthors = Hugenholtz P, Stackebrandt E | title = Reclassification of Sphaerobacter thermophilus from the subclass Sphaerobacteridae in the phylum Actinobacteria to the class Thermomicrobia (emended description) in the phylum Chloroflexi (emended description) | journal = International Journal of Systematic and Evolutionary Microbiology | volume = 54 | issue = Pt 6 | pages = 2049–51 | date = November 2004 | pmid = 15545432 | doi = 10.1099/ijs.0.03028-0  }} < /ref >  They are [[aerobic]] and chemoorganotrophic (a term which means their [[metabolism]] is rather like most [[fungi]]).
