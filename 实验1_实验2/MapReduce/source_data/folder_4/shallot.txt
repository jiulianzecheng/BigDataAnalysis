 == Growth == 
 Shallots seldom produce [[seed]]s. < ref name=SFGATE > {{cite web |url=http://homeguides.sfgate.com/propagate-shallots-23121.html |title=How to propagate shallots |author=Nannette Richford |date= |website= |publisher=SFGATE |accessdate=6 June 2015}} < /ref >  They are increased by dividing its compound [[bulb]]s. < ref name=SFGATE/ >  These are made up of several bulblets or cloves held together at the base. The bulblets are planted the same as [[onion]] sets. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.harvesttotable.com/2012/02/early-planting-onions-shallots-and-garlic/ |title=Early planting onions, shallots, and garlic |author=Steve Albert |date= 15 February 2012|website= |publisher=Harvest to Table |accessdate=6 June 2015}} < /ref >  Each set develops into a compound set. The mature bulbs are [[harvest]]ed, [[Curing (food preservation)|cured]] and stored the same way as onions. In suitable storage, the bulbs keep from one [[Year|season]] to the next.  
 
 == Flavour ==

Leaves are also consumed as green [[vegetable]]s. Like their cousins onions and [[garlic]]s, this is also considered as non-[[vegetarian]] food in [[India]]. Most of the [[crop]] is produced for sale in the green state. But some dry bulbs are also used. They are also used for [[pickling]].
