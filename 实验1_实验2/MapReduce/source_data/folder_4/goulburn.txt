 '''Goulburn''' ({{lang-wrh|Burbong}}) is a [[city]] in the [[Southern Tablelands]] of [[New South Wales]], Australia. It is 190 [[kilometre|km]] south-west of [[Sydney, Australia|Sydney]] on the [[Hume Highway]]. It is 690 metres above sea-level. In 2006 there were 20,127 people living in Goulburn. < ref > {{Census 2006 AUS

 Goulburn was named by [[James Meehan]] after [[Henry Goulburn]], a British [[politician]]. The Governor [[Lachlan Macquarie]] approved the choice of name. The [[Indigenous Australian|Aboriginal]] name for Goulburn is Burbong. < ref > {{cite web | url=http://www.gnb.nsw.gov.au/name_search/extract?id=MaKqwpWAan | title=Geographical Names register extract | publisher=NSW Geographical Names Board www.gnb.nsw.gov.au | accessdate=2006-04-30 | archive-date=2009-10-24 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091024202503/http://www.gnb.nsw.gov.au/name_search/extract?id=MaKqwpWAan | url-status=dead }} < /ref >  This is a Murring/[[Wiradhuri language|Wiradjuri]] word meaning a special place. 
 
 Goulburn has a [[railway station]] on the Main Southern railway line. It is a service centre for the many [[farms]] in the area. It also provides a rest stop for those travelling on the [[Hume Highway]]. It has a central park and many historic buildings, including old houses near the railway station on Sloane Street. It also has two 19th-century cathedrals. It is home to the ''[[Big Merino]]'', the world's largest [[cement]] sheep.
