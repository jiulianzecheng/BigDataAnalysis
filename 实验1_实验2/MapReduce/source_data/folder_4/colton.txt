 '''Colton''' is a village and [[civil parish]] in [[South Lakeland]], [[Cumbria]], England. In 2001 there were 765 people living in Colton. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/viewFullDataset.do?instanceSelection=03070 & productId=779 & $ph=60_61 & datasetInstanceId=3070 & startColumn=1 & numberOfColumns=4 & containerAreaId=790350 |title=Neighbourhood Statistics |access-date=2012-09-18 |archive-date=2014-08-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819102846/http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/viewFullDataset.do?instanceSelection=03070 & productId=779 & $ph=60_61 & datasetInstanceId=3070 & startColumn=1 & numberOfColumns=4 & containerAreaId=790350 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In 1202 it was called Coleton, which was the [[Old English]] name for ''village''. < ref name=colton > {{cite web |url= http://www.thecumbriadirectory.com/Town_or_Village/location.php?url=colton |title=Colton, Cumbria |work=thecumbriadirectory.com |year=2012 |access-date=20 September 2012}} < /ref >  It is built beside a small stream called Colton Beck. < ref name=colton/ >  The Church of Holy Trinity was built in 1578 and listed by [[English Heritage]] as a Grade 2 building. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/en-421185-church-of-holy-trinity-colton-cumbria |title=Church of Holy Trinity - Colton |work=britishlistedbuildings.co.uk |year=2012  |access-date=20 September 2012}} < /ref >
