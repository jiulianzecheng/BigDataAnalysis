'''Ophthalmology''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|ɒ|f|θ|æ|l|ˈ|m|ɒ|l|ə|dʒ|i}} or {{IPAc-en|ˌ|ɒ|p|θ|æ|l|ˈ|m|ɒ|l|ə|dʒ|i}}) < ref > {{cite web|title=ophthalmology|url=http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/ophthalmology|access-date=14 September 2015|publisher=Oxford Dictionary|archive-date=9 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160709065259/http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/ophthalmology|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  is the branch of [[medicine]] that deals with the [[anatomy]], physiology and diseases of the [[eye|eyeball]] and [[orbit (anatomy)|orbit]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=History of Ophthalmology|url=http://www.mrcophth.com/Historyofophthalmology/Introductory.htm|publisher=mrcophth|access-date=30 August 2013|archive-date=8 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170808095547/http://www.mrcophth.com/Historyofophthalmology/Introductory.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >    
 
 An '''ophthalmologist''' is a person in medical and surgical eye disease.  Ophthalmologists are allowed to medically treat eye disease, implement laser therapy, and perform incisional surgery when needed. < ref > {{cite journal|last1=Farandos|first1=NM|last2=Yetisen|first2=AK|last3=Monteiro|first3=MJ|last4=Lowe|first4=CR|last5=Yun|first5=SH|title=Contact Lens Sensors in Ocular Diagnostics|journal=Advanced Healthcare Materials|date=2014|doi=10.1002/adhm.201400504|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adhm.201400504/abstract|pmid=25400274|volume=4|issue=6|pages=792–810|s2cid=35508652 }} < /ref >
