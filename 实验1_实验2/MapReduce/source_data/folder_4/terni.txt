 '''Terni''' ({{lang-la|Interamna Nahars}}) is a ''[[comune]]'' and city in the [[Umbria]] [[Regions of Italy|region]], in [[central Italy]]. Terni is the [[Capital city|capital]] of the [[province of Terni]]. It is one of the most important industrial towns of [[Umbria]]. 
 
 The '''University of Perugia''' ({{lang-it|Università degli Studi di Perugia}}) is a university based in [[Perugia]] that offers its main courses in Perugia and Terni. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 The ''comune'' of Terni is in the [[valley]] of the [[Nera (Italy)|Nera]] river; it has an area of {{Convert|212.43|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its [[altitude]] is {{Convert|130|m|ft|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " area "  / > 
 
 Terni borders with the  ''[[Comune|comuni]]'' [[Stroncone]], [[San Gemini]], [[Montefranco]] (a small part), [[Arrone]], [[Narni]], [[Montecastrilli]] (a very small part), [[Acquasparta]], [[Spoleto]] ([[Province of Perugia]]) and two ''comuni'' of the province [[Rieti]] in the [[Lazio]] regionː [[Rieti]] and [[Labro]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.tuttitalia.it/umbria/15-terni/73-comuni-limitrofi/ |title=Comuni limitrofi a Terni |publisher=Tuttitalia.it |language=Italian |accessdate=21 July 2016}} < /ref >

 ===''Frazioni''=== 
 The following ''[[Frazione|frazioni]]'' ([[Hamlet (place)|hamlet]]s) are part of Terniː Acquapalombo, Appecano, Battiferro, Cecalocco, Cesi, Collegiacone, Collescipoli, Collestatte, Giuncano Alto, Giuncano Scalo, Marmore, Miranda, Papigno, Piediluco, Poggio Lavarino, Polenaco, Porzano, Pracchia, Rocca San Zenone, San Carlo, San Liberatore, Titurano, Torreorsina.
