 '''Zenzizenzizenzic''' is the eighth [[Exponentiation|power]] of a number. [[Robert Recorde]] in ''[[The Whetstone of Witte]]'', published in 1557. Although his spelling had the last letter of the word as k. 
 
 This word is not used any more in math except as a curiosity. The [[Oxford English Dictionary]] has only one citation for it. It survives because it is unusual. 
 
 The root word, is the [[Italian language|Italian]] ''censo'', meaning squared. 
 
 Zenzizenzizenzic is from a time when there was no easy way of writing the powers of numbers except as squares and cubes. The fourth power was represented by the square of a square. '''Zenzizenzic''' is a condensed form of the Italian censo di censo, used by [[Fibonacci|Leonardo of Pisa]] in his famous book ''[[Liber Abaci]]'' of 1202. 
 
 The eighth power is by extension zenzizenzizenzic. Similarly the sixth power would be '''zenzicube''', the square of a cube. 
 
 Zenzizenzizenzic has more Z's than any other word in English.
