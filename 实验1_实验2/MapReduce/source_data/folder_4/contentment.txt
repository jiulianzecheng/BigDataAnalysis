 '''Contentment''' or '''Satisfaction''' is a [[wikt:pleasant|pleasant]] or [[wikt:positive|positive]] [[emotion]], [[wikt:feeling|feeling]] or [[wikt:state|state]] of mind. Satisfaction is when a desire or a [[need]] is [[wikt:fulfill|fulfill]]ed. < ref > {{cite web | url=http://www.linternaute.com/dictionnaire/fr/definition/satisfaction/|title=Satisfaction, définition|access-date=29 November 2013}} < /ref >  Satisfaction is when a person has nothing to complain about. Someone feels satisfied when they [[wikt:achieve|achieve]] a difficult [[task|goal]]. < ref > {{cite journal|last=Sheldon|first=K.M.|author2=Elliot, A.J.|title=Goal Striving, Need Satisfaction, and Longitudinal Well-Being:The Self-Concordance Model|url=https://archive.org/details/sim_journal-of-personality-and-social-psychology_1999-03_76_3/page/482|journal=Journal of Personality and Social Psychology|year=1999|volume=76|issue=3|pages=482–497|doi=10.1037/0022-3514.76.3.482|pmid=10101878}} < /ref >  A person feels satisfied by [[wikt:accomplish|accomplishment]], [[wikt:recognition|recognition]], [[invention]] and [[wikt:service|service]]. Someone can feel [[job satisfaction]] when they like their work. < ref name= " Spector 1997 " > {{cite book|last=Spector|first=P.E.|title=Job satisfaction: Application, assessment, causes and consequences|year=1997|publisher=SAGE|location=Thousand Oaks, CA}} < /ref >  A person tends to feel satisfied by basic body [[wikt:functions|functions]]. These can include [[eating]], [[Physical exercise|exercising]], [[hygiene]], [[sexual intercourse|sex]] or [[defecation]]. Some people feel satisfied when they get [[wikt:revenge|revenge]] for something that hurt them. Satisfaction is related to pleasure, [[wikt:gratification|gratification]] and [[wikt:contentment|contentment]].

 [[Humans]] and many other [[mammals]] feel satisfaction. < ref > {{cite book | last1 = Hitlin | first1=Steven|last2=Vaisey | first2=Stephen | title =Handbook of the Sociology of Morality  | url = https://archive.org/details/handbooksociolog00hitl | publisher =Springer Science  &  Business Media | date=2010 | isbn = 9781441968968 |page=[https://archive.org/details/handbooksociolog00hitl/page/n141 127]}} < /ref >  Sometimes a person lacks satisfaction. They may feel negative feelings such as [[:en:wikt:discontent|discontent]], [[emptiness]], [[bored|boredom]] or [[sadness]]. This is the opposite of satisfaction. It is called [[:en:wikt:dissatisfaction|dissatisfaction]]. Dissatisfaction occurs when we [[wikt:fail|fail]] at doing something many times. Dissatisfaction may occur when comparing yourself to another person. < ref > Schachter S. 1959. ''The psychology of affiliation: Experimental studies of the sources of gregariousness'', vol. 1. Stanford University Press. < /ref >  This is called [[social comparison theory]]. [[wikt:ongoing|Ongoing]] dissatisfaction causes [[:en:wikt:complacency|complacency]], [[apathy]] and even [[Depression (mental illness)|depression]]. < ref > {{cite journal | year = 2011 | url = https://www.academia.edu/801280 | title = The relationship between depression, and interpersonal style, self-perception, and anger | last1 = Hisli Şhain | first1 = Nesrin | last2 = Durak Batigün | first2 = Ayşegül | last3 = Koç | first3 = Volkan | journal = Turkish Journal of Psychiatry | volume = 22 | issue = 1 | access-date=30 March 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 Satisfaction does not occur [[wikt:automatic|automatically]]. Satisfaction is the process of coping with life. < ref > Kaibara, E.: ''Der Weg zur Zufriedenheit'', Frankfurt 2002. < /ref >  Humans are in a constant [[fight]] against dissatisfaction. The [[Dalai Lama]] spoke about this:  
 < blockquote > 
 " When you are discontent, you always want more, more, more. Your desire can never be satisfied. But when you practice contentment, you can say to yourself,  " Oh yes—I already have everything that I really need. " < ref > {{cite web |year= 2001 |url= http://www.oprah.com/omagazine/Oprah-Interviews-The-Dalai-Lama |title= Oprah Talks to The Dalai Lama |access-date= 30 March 2015}} < /ref > 
 < /blockquote > 
 If someone slips into total dissatisfaction, they might experience [[anguish]]. A person is more likely to be happy and satisfied if they learn how to control their negative experiences and to process positive thoughts. < ref > Wilker, J.: ''Das Einmaleins der Zufriedenheit: Glücklichsein und bleiben'', Bielefeld 2007. < /ref > < ref > Rahn, H.J.: ''Zum Sinn des Lebens'', Hamburg 2012, S. 281. < /ref >  Usually, a person does not share their satisfaction with other people. That is called [[pride]].  
 
 Satisfaction, like all [[emotion]]s, is a [[motivation|motivator]] of [[behavior]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://psychology.about.com/od/psychologytopics/a/theories-of-emotion.htm |title=Theories of Emotion |publisher=Psychology.about.com |date=13 September 2013 |access-date=11 November 2013 |archive-date=17 October 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131017213239/http://psychology.about.com/od/psychologytopics/a/theories-of-emotion.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Satisfaction is an important part of one's [[quality of life]]. In positive [[psychology]], researchers are studying what gives a person satisfaction in life. < ref > {{cite journal |article=|author=Selig man, Martin|author2=Michal Csikszentmilhalyi|title=Positive Psychology|journal=American Psychologist|date=January 2000|volume=54|issue=1|pages=5–10}} < /ref >  [[Delayed gratification]] is a part of satisfaction. Delayed gratification is when a person chooses long-term [[wikt:reward|rewards]] over immediate rewards. Delayed gratification is also when a person [[wikt:prioritize|prioritizes]] long-term [[wikt:objective|objective]]s over closer [[wikt:target|target]]s. < ref name = Carducci > {{cite book |last=Carducci |first=Bernardo J. |year=2009 |chapter=Basic Processes of Mischel's Cognitive-Affective Perspective: Delay of Gratification and Conditions of Behavioral Consistency |pages=[https://archive.org/details/psychologyofpers0000card/page/443 443]–4 |title=The Psychology of Personality: Viewpoints, Research, and Applications |url=https://archive.org/details/psychologyofpers0000card |publisher=John Wiley and Sons |isbn=978-1-4051-3635-8}} < /ref >  When a person [[wikt:delay#Verb|delays]] [[wikt:gratification|gratification]], they feel satisfied.  
 
 [[Psychologist]]s say that [[life satisfaction]] and [[wikt:well-being|well-being]] are the two main ingredients of [[happiness]]. < ref > {{cite web | year = 2009 | url = https://www.pbs.org/thisemotionallife/topic/happiness/what-happiness | title = What is happiness? | publisher = PBS| access-date = 29 March 2015}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Satisfaction and happiness==  
 Satisfaction is different from happiness. Satisfaction refers to a particular [[wikt:situation|situation]] or [[wikt:state#Noun|state]] of mind. Satisfaction is often [[temporary]]. Happiness is a [[wikt:prolonged|prolonged]] [[wikt:condition#Noun|condition]].  < ref > ’’Concise Routledge Encyclopedia of Philosophy’’ (London and New York 2000), “Happiness”. < /ref > 
 
 ==Hedonism== 
 Satisfaction is different from [[hedonism]]. A hedonist [[wikt:seek|seek]]s pleasure or [[wikt:enjoy|enjoy]]ment above everything else. < ref > {{cite journal | url = http://www.iep.utm.edu/hedonism/ | title = Hedonism | last = Weijers | first = Dan | journal =  Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy | issn = 2161-0002 | access-date=30 March 2015}} < /ref >  Satisfaction is not about doing things simply to feel pleasure. Satisfaction is not [[wikt:self-indulgence|self-indulgent]].
