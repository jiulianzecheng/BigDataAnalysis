 An '''amethyst''' is a  [[mineral]] [[gemstone]] that comes from [[quartz]]. It is usually [[wikt:transparent|transparent]] and best known for its [[Violet (color)|violet]] [[color|colour]]. Even though it is a form of quartz, it has more [[iron oxide]] (Fe < sub > 2 < /sub > O < sub > 3 < /sub > ) than any other kind of quartz. < ref name= " britannica " > {{Cite web |url= http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/20466/amethyst |title=amethyst (mineral) -- Britannica Online Encyclopedia |work=britannica.com |access-date=27 April 2010}} < /ref >  Because of this, some experts think that its color comes from the iron in it. < ref name= " britannica " / >   
 
 Heating the amethyst either takes away its color or changes it to a yellow hue. Amethysts are found in [[Brazil]], [[Uruguay]], [[Canada]] ([[Ontario]]) and [[Americans|America (]][[North Carolina]]). The rock amethyst is the [[birthstone]] of the month [[February]]. In the [[Mohs scale of mineral hardness|Mohs scale]], it has a hardness of seven. < ref name= " gemstone "  / > 
 
 == Meaning and superstition == 
 Many people believe amethysts [[wikt:protect|protect]] one from [[poison]], evil, and getting drunk. This is where the name of this rock came from, The Greek word ''amethustos'', means  " without being drunken " .  
 
 Also they are thought to be good for healing, recovering from headaches, good dreams, and more. < ref > [https://takanta.com/amethyst#Wear_Amethyst_Jewelry_to_Enjoy_its_Protective_and_Healing_Properties Amethyst Crystal - Benefits  &  Properties of Healing Powers] < /ref >  Some [[Catholic]] [[pope]]s also wear a [[rosary]] of amethyst around their necks. < ref name= " gemstone " > {{Cite web |url= http://www.bernardine.com/gemstones/amethyst.htm  |title=Amethyst Gemstone - Facts, Lore, History, Myths and Pictures |work=bernardine.com |access-date=27 April 2010}} < /ref >  Some Roman women thought gems could keep their husbands faithful to them. 
 
 Amethysts are also worn because it is believed that it makes them look [[wikt:gentle|gentle]]. The “powers” of an amethyst also include healing, [[peace]], [[love]], more [[spirituality]], [[courage]],  [[protect]]ion from robbers, and [[happy|happiness]]. 
 
 In the [[Book of Revelation]]s the amethyst was one of the twelve foundation stones of [[:en:New_Jerusalem#The_Book_of_Revelation|New Jerusalem]]. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://biblehub.com/revelation/21-20.htm|title=Revelation 21:20 the fifth sardonyx, the sixth carnelian, the seventh chrysolite, the eighth beryl, the ninth topaz, the tenth chrysoprase, the eleventh jacinth, and the twelfth amethyst.|website=biblehub.com|access-date=2019-10-18}} < /ref > 
 
 There are many stories and myths about amethysts. One of them tells how amethysts were supposedly created. According to the story, [[Bacchus]], the [[Roman mythology|Roman]] god of wine and partying, was angry because of something against him and wanted to get revenge. < ref name= " jeweler " > {{Cite web|url=http://www.cwjewelers.com/stoneameth.htm|title=amethyst Gem Information|work=cwjewelers.com|access-date=28 April 2010}} < /ref >  He ordered that the first person who was mortal to come across his path would be eaten by [[tiger]]s.  
 
 Just at that moment a beautiful maiden named Amethyst came, on her way to worship the goddess [[Diana (mythology)|Diana]]. Diana saw what was going to happen and she quickly made Amethyst into a stone to save her from the tigers. When Bacchus saw this, he [[wikt:repent|repented]] and poured [[wine]] over the stone, making its color purple. < ref name= " jeweler "  / > 
 
 == Decoration == 
 The amethyst is worn and used for fashion in a number of different ways. It is polished and shaped for [[ring]]s, [[earring]]s, and cufflinks. It may be also used for brooches, sometimes being carved into a cluster of [[grape]]s. People like the way amethysts look, especially when they are put in gold and diamonds. 
 
 The amethyst is worn by royalty and nobility. This is because its royal color was purple. When it was found in the Minoan period in Greece (c. 2500 B.C.), it was polished and shaped into cabochons (dome-shaped stones). Then, it was set in gold.  
 
 During the 15th century, the [[France|French]] ''fleur-de-lis'' brooch could only be worn by the Royal family on special times. < ref name= " jeweler "  / >  The fleur-de-lis design on it is put in with [[sapphire]]s and amethysts. < ref > {{Cite web |url= http://www.galleries.com/minerals/gemstone/amethyst/amethyst.htm |title=AMETHYST a gemstone variety of quartz |work=galleries.com |access-date=28 April 2010}} < /ref >
