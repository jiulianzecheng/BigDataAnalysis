 '''Raahe''' ({{lang-sv|Brahestad}}) is a town in [[Northern Ostrobothnia]], [[Finland]]. It was founded by Swedish statesman and Governor General of Finland [[Count Per Brahe the Younger]]. As of 28 February 2023, 24,050 people lived there. Neighbouring municipalities are [[Haapavesi]], [[Oulainen]], [[Pyhäjoki]], [[Siikajoki]] and [[Siikalatva]]. 
 
 Three municipalities have been merged with Raahe: [[Saloinen]] in 1973, [[Pattijoki]] in 2003 and [[Vihanti]] in 2013.
