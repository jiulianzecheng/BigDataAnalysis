'''Volaris''' is a Mexican [[low-cost airline]]. The airline is based in [[Mexico City]]. Its largest [[airline hub]] is at [[General Abelardo L. Rodríguez International Airport]] in [[Tijuana]]. discrimina airline 
 
 ==History== 
 Volaris was founded in 2005. < ref name= " SEC " > {{cite web|title=Form F-1 REGISTRATION STATEMENT UNDER THE SECURITIES ACT OF 1933  " Controladora Vuela Compañía de Aviación, S.A.B. de C.V. / Volaris Aviation Holding Company |url=https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1520504/000119312513249249/d500205df1.htm|publisher=Securities and Exchange Commission|accessdate=July 21, 2020}} < /ref >  [[TACA Airlines]] and [[Televisa]] were among the initial [[Shareholder|shareholders]]. In 2009, the airline started flying from [[Mexico]] to airports in the [[USA]] after it began an agreement with [[Southwest Airlines]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.dallasnews.com/business/business/2010/10/28/Southwest-Airlines-Mexican-carrier-Volaris-6612|title=Southwest Airlines, Mexican carrier Volaris to launch partnership Dec. 1|date=28 October 2010|website=Dallas News}} < /ref >  When [[Mexicana de Aviacion]] stopped flying in August 2010, Volaris took over their slots at the [[Mexico City International Airport]].  < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.flightglobal.com/news/articles/volaris-eyes-mexico-city-slots-looks-to-fill-mexicana39s-346458/|title=Volaris eyes Mexico City slots, looks to fill Mexicana's void|first=Brendan|last=Sobie|date=23 August 2010|website=Flightglobal.com}} < /ref >  Southwest and Volaris stopped their agreement in [[2013]].  < ref > Visit [https://www.farecopy.com/volaris-airlines Volaris Airlines] < /ref >  In 2015, Volaris started going to [[Guatemala City|Guatemala City, Guatemala]], [[San Juan, Puerto Rico]] and [[San José, Costa Rica]].  < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.elfinancierocr.com/negocios/aerolinea-volaris-inicia-operaciones-en-costa-rica-con-dos-nuevas-rutas-a-mexico/KJMN3K6CYRH73MKUYFNDYSZN4E/story/|title=Aerolínea Volaris inicia operaciones en Costa Rica con dos nuevas rutas a México|first=Evelyn Fernández|last=Mora|website=El Financiero, Grupo Nación}} < /ref >  In 2019, Volaris became [[Mexico|Mexico's]] largest airline in terms of [[market share]], partly because it offered lower prices.  It passed [[Aeroméxico]]. < ref name= " Market " > {{cite news|url=https://mexiconewsdaily.com/news/volaris-no-1-airline-in-mexico-for-market-share/|publisher=Mexico News Daily|accessdate=July 21, 2020|date=October 26, 2019|title=Volaris is now No. 1 airline in Mexico for market share|author=}} < /ref >
