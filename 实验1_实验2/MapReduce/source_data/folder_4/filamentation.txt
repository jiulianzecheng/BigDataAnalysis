 '''Filamentation''' is the [[wikt:anomaly|anomalous]] (not normal) growth of certain [[bacteria]], such as ''[[Escherichia coli|E. coli]]'', in which [[Cell (biology)|cells]] continue to elongate but do not divide (no [[Septum disambiguation|septa]] formation). Bacterial filamentation is a defect in completing replication and is observed in bacteria responding to a various [[stress]]es. This may happen, for example, when there is extensive [[DNA repair|DNA damage]] through the [[SOS response|SOS response system]]. Nutritional change may also cause bacterial filamentation.
