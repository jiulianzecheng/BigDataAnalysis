 '''Progesterone''' is a [[steroid]] [[hormone]]. In animals, it is made from [[cholesterol]]. It is the base for making certain [[estrogen]]s and [[androgen]]s. In animals, it is produced by their body mainly during the second half of the [[menstruation|menstrual cycle]]. During [[pregnancy]] it is produced in higher quantities. 
 Willard Myron Allen co-discovered progesterone with his [[anatomy]] [[professor]] George Washington Corner at the University of Rochester Medical School in 1933 year. Allen discovered temperature needed to melt it and its weight. He also gave it the name Progesterone based on Progestational Steroidal [[ketone]]. < ref > http://pt.wkhealth.com/pt/re/lwwgateway/landingpage.htm;jsessionid=R2dc47m048bxLF2j9vg9nZGkV4LPQ9X4PTkhCqCVdsC7dJrhTPPm!1612949135!181195629!8091!-1?sid=WKPTLP:landingpage & an=00007611-197010000-00012 < /ref >
