 '''Dizziness''' is a disability in spatial perception and stability. < ref > {{DorlandsDict|three/000031973|dizziness}} < /ref >  The term ''dizziness'' is not specific: < ref > {{MeshName|Dizziness}} < /ref >  it can mean [[vertigo]], [[presyncope]], [[Balance disorder|disequilibrium]], < ref > {{cite book |chapter-url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dons/part_2/chapter_14.html#chpt_14_presyncope |chapter=Chapter 14: Evaluation of the Dizzy Patient |author=Reeves, Alexander G. |author2=Swenson, Rand S. |title=Disorders of the Nervous System: A Primer |year=2008 |publisher=Dartmouth Medical School |url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dons/index.html |access-date=2020-03-28 |archive-date=2011-09-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110902031514/http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dons/index.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  or a non-specific feeling such as being [[Drunkenness|drunk]] or [[Humour|funny]]. < ref name=UTD > {{cite web |url=http://www.uptodate.com/contents/approach-to-the-patient-with-dizziness |title=Approach to the patient with dizziness |author=Branch Jr., William T. |author2=Barton, Jason J. S. |work=UpToDate |date=February 10, 2011}} < /ref >  Dizziness is a common medical complaint, affecting 20-30% of persons. < ref > {{cite journal |last1=Karatas |first1=Mehmet |doi=10.1097/NRL.0b013e31817533a3 |title=Central vertigo and dizziness: epidemiology, differential diagnosis, and common causes. |journal=The Neurologist |volume=14 |issue=6 |pages=355–64 |pmid=19008741  |language=ENGLISH |issn=1074-7931|year=2008 |s2cid=21444226 }} < /ref > 
 
 Dizziness is broken down into 4 main subtypes: [[vertigo]] (~25-50%), [[equilibrioception#Dysfunction|disequilibrium]] (less than ~15%), [[presyncope]] (less than ~15%) and nonspecific dizziness (~10%). < ref > {{cite journal |vauthors=Post RE, Dickerson LM |title=Dizziness: a diagnostic approach |journal=Am Fam Physician |volume=82 |issue=4 |pages=361–8, 369 |date=August 2010 |pmid=20704166 }}
