" '''Jihadism''' "  (also  " '''jihadist movement''' " ,  " '''jihadi movement''' "  and variants) is a 21st-century [[neologism]] found in [[Western world|Western]] languages to describe [[Islamism|Islamist]]  movements seen by the military to be  " rooted in Islam "  and a threat to [[Western culture|the West]]. < ref name=ccnrm-263 > Compare: {{cite book|editor1-last= Hammer|editor1-first= Olav|editor2-last= Rothstein|editor2-first= Mikael|title= The Cambridge Companion to New Religious Movements|date= 2012|publisher= Cambridge University Press.|page= 263|chapter-url= https://books.google.com/books?id=RM0AAwAAQBAJ & dq=jihadism+definition & pg=PA263|access-date= 2018-03-03|chapter= 16|isbn= 9781107493551|quote= 'Jihadism' is a term that has been constructed in Western languages to describe militant Islamic movements that are perceived as existentially threatening to the West. Western media have tended to refer to Jihadism as a military movement rooted in political Islam. }} < /ref >   
 
 The term  " jihadism "  first appeared in South Asian media; Western journalists adopted it in the aftermath of the [[September 11 attacks]] of 2001. < ref > {{cite encyclopedia|title= Jihad|author= Natana DeLong-Bas|encyclopedia= Oxford Bibliographies|publisher= Oxford University Press|location= Oxford|year= 2009|url= http://www.oxfordbibliographies.com/view/document/obo-9780195390155/obo-9780195390155-0045.xml|url-access= subscription|access-date= 2016-09-03|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160629215212/http://www.oxfordbibliographies.com/view/document/obo-9780195390155/obo-9780195390155-0045.xml|archive-date= 2016-06-29|url-status= live}} < /ref >
