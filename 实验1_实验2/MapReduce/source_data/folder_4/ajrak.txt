 '''Ajrak''' ([[Sindhi language|Sindhi]]: اجرڪ) is a traditional block printed shawl worn by [[Sindhi people]], usually by men, it is the symbol of [[Sindhi culture]].  == Description == 
 Ajrak is a block printed cloth with deep crimson red color and indigo blue background, having symmetrical patterns with dotted unprinted sparkling white motifs, mostly stars. Ajrak is mostly made of cotton. The ajrak is very ancient art of Sindh traceable from the earliest archaeological finds of the old [[Indus Valley civilization|Indus civilization]] of [[Mohenjo-daro|Moen Jo-Dero]]. It has been equally popular till today. < ref name= " :0 "  / >   
 
 The ajrak used by women was called as (Malir/Maleer) with so many designs, patterns and symbols, apart from traditional crimson red color, Malir can be of many other colors, it was used as chadar (veil) by Sindhi women, which is today used as veil, as dress and as [[Sari (clothing)|Saris]] by women. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ajrak Malir Traditional Block Printed 2pc Suit » Sindh Craft Store|url=https://sindhcraftstore.com/product/ajrak-malir-traditional-block-printed-2pc-suit/|access-date=2023-12-01|website=Sindh Craft Store|language=en-US}} < /ref >
