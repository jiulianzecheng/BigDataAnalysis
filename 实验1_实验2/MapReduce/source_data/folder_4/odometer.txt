 An '''odometer''' or '''odograph''' < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.oed.com/view/Entry/256927?redirectedFrom=odograph# |title=Oxford English Dictionary (online):' & #39;odograph' & #39; |publisher=Oed.com |access-date=2011-11-12}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/odograph |title=American Heritage Dictionary (online: Dictionary.com): ' & #39;odograph' & #39; |publisher=Dictionary.reference.com |access-date=2011-11-12}} < /ref >  is an instrument that shows the [[distance]] traveled by a vehicle, such as a [[bicycle]] or [[automobile]]. It can be electronic, mechanical, or a combination of the two. The name comes from the [[Ancient Greek|Greek]] words ''hodós'' ( " path "  or  " gateway " ) and ''métron'' ( " measure " ). It is sometimes called a '''mileometer''' or '''milometer'''.
