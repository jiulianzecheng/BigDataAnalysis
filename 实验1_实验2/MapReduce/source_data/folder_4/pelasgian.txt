'''Pelasgian''' is an extinct ancient language (or languages) that according to [[Ancient Greece|Ancient Greek]] and [[Ancient Rome|Ancient Roman]] sources existed in limited areas of [[Ancient Greece|Ancient]] [[Greece]] during the 2nd and 1st Millennium BC. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Encyclopedia Britannica|url=https://www.britannica.com/|access-date=2021-02-19|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 '''Herodotus''' 
 
 [[Herodotus]] called it  " Barbarian "  [[language]] but not the [[Pelasgians]] (it's speakers). < ref > {{Cite book|title=Histories|year=1907|url=https://archive.org/details/histories00hero_1|last=Herodotus|location=Ancient Greece}} < /ref > 
 
 '''Official origin''' 
 
 It's origin and it's form are officially unknown because no Pelasgian literature examples survived. 
 
 '''Modern theories''' 
 
 The 2 main modern theories about the Pelasgian language suggest that it was a [[Greek language|Greek]] dialect that saw extensive changes or that it was a Western [[Asia Minor|Anatolian]] language. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Harrison|year=1998|pages=25–26}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|title=Greeks and Pre-Greeks|last=Finkelberg|first=Margalit|publisher=Cambridge University Press|date=5 January 2006}} < /ref > 
 
 '''Other Theories''' 
 
 A [[Bulgaria]]n linguist suggested that the Pelasgians were speaking [[Thracians|Thracian]]. In addition he suggested that the Pelasgians were a sub group of the mysterious late [[Bronze Age]] [[Sea Peoples]] that caused chaos in the [[Mediterranean Sea|Mediterranean]] and attacked several cities in the [[Aegean Sea]]. This theory has been rejected by most researchers. 
 
 '''Obsolete theories''' 
 
 During the 19th Century an [[Austria]]n diplomat and Albanian speaker suggested a connection between Early [[Albanian language|Albanian]] and Pelasgian. Similar claims have been made by modern Albanian nationalistic organizations. This type of theories have been rejected by modern scholars  < ref > {{Cite book|title=Le Pélasgique|publisher=University of Louvain|year=1952}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite journal|last=J. Windekens|first=Albert|date=1960|title=Études Pélasgiques}} < /ref >
