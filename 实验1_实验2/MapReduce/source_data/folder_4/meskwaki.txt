 The '''Meskwaki''' (sometimes spelled '''Mesquaki''') are a [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] people. They are often called the '''Fox''' tribe. They are related to the [[Sauk people|Sauk]] people of the same language family. In the [[Meskwaki language]], the Meskwaki call themselves ''{{lang|sac|Meshkwahkihaki}}''. This means  " the Red-Earths " . This is related to their creation story. < ref > Jones, William.  " Episodes in the Culture-Hero Myth of the Sauks and Foxes " , ''The Journal of American Folklore,'' Vol. XIV, Oct-Dec. 1901. P. 239. < /ref >  Historically they lived in the [[Great Lakes]] region. They are part of the [[Indigenous peoples of the Northeastern Woodlands|Northeast Woodlands]].
