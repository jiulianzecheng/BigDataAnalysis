 '''Gannets''' are [[seabird]]s in the genus '''''Morus'''''. They are related to [[booby|boobies]]. They are large [[white]] and [[black]] with [[yellow]] heads. They have long, pointed wings and long [[beak|bills]]. 
 Gannets only lay one [[blue]] egg. 
 
 Gannets can dive from a height of {{convert|30|m|ft}}, and hit the water with great speed. < ref > {{cite magazine |last1=Gabbatiss |first1=Josh |title=Seabirds' Death-Defying Dives |url=https://www.hakaimagazine.com/news/seabirds-death-defying-dives/ |accessdate=20 March 2020 |magazine=Hakai Magazine |date=19 October 2016 |language=en}} < /ref >  This can carry them to a depth of {{convert|11|m|ft}}. < ref > {{cite web |title=The Gannet's Dive |url=https://www.jw.org/en/bible-teachings/science/was-it-designed/gannets-dive/ |website=JW.ORG |accessdate=20 March 2020 |language=en}} < /ref >  The bird can then keep going deeper using its wings and feet. Some have been known to dive deeper than {{convert|22|m|ft}}. < ref > {{cite web |title=Northern Gannet Overview |url=https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Northern_Gannet/overview |website=All About Bird, Cornell Lab of Ornithology |publisher=Cornell University |accessdate=20 March 2020 |language=en}} < /ref >
