A '''vanguard''' is a (usually small) group of people who lead a movement. It comes from two old [[French language|French]] words, ''avant'' (front) and ''garde'' (guard). < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.credoreference.com/entry/mwcollegiate/vanguard_van_g%C3%A4rd_also_va |title=Vanguard |first= |last= |work=Merriam-Webster's Collegiate(R) Dictionary |year=2004 |accessdate=14 June 2011}} < /ref >  In military terms, a vanguard is a group of soldiers who scout ahead to protect an army from surprise attacks.  
 
 == in Politics == 
 In the political [[philosophy]] of [[Leninism]]  " vanguard "  refers to the  " vanguard party " . This would be a small group of dedicated revolutionaries who would lead the [[Communist]] movement in [[Russia]]. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.credoreference.com/entry/bkpolsci/vanguard_of_the_proletariat |title=Vanguard of the Proletariat |first=Frank W. |last=Bealey|work=The Blackwell Dictionary of Political Science  |year=1999 |accessdate=14 June 2011}} < /ref >
