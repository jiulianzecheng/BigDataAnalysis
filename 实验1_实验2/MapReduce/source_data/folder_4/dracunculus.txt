 [[File:Dracunculus canariensis.jpg|right|150px|thumb|The [[fruit]]s of ''Dracunculus canariensis'']] 
 
 '''''Dracunculus''''' is a [[genus]] of [[plant]]s in the family [[Araceae]]. They have a large purple spathe and spadix, and a foul smell. 
 
 There are only three [[species]] of  ''Dracunculus''. ''Dracunculus vulgaris'' grows in [[Southern Europe]] and [[Northern Africa]]. ''Dracunculus canariensis'' grows in [[Canary Islands]] and [[Madeira]]. < ref > Bricknell, Christopher (ed) 1996. ''The RHS encyclopedia of garden plants''. Dorling Kindersly, London. p379 {{ISBN|0751304360}} < /ref > 
 
 All of these plants are [[herb]]s with [[tuber]]s. The individual flowers [[flower]]s are tiny: they are gathered in an [[inflorescence]]. 
 
 Common names for this plant are ''Snake Lily'', ''Dragon Arum'', ''Black Dragon'', ''Voodoo Lily'', ''Ragons'', ''Black Arum'', ''Dragonwort'' and ''Stink Lily''. < ref > [http://www.paghat.com/voodoolily.html Paghat.com: ''Dracunculus''] < /ref > 
 
 ''Dracunculus vulgaris'' is [[Gardening|cultivated]] as a [[garden]] plant.
