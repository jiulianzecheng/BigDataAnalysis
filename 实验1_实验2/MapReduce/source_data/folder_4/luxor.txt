 '''Luxor''' ({{lang-ar|الأقصر‎‎}}) is a city in [[Upper Egypt]]. In 2012, about 506,588 people lived there. < ref name=2010stats > {{cite web|url=http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=1280273714 & lng=de & des=wg & geo=-69 & srt=npan & col=adhoq & msz=1500 & men=gcis & lng=en|title=World Gazetteer - Egypt: largest cities and towns and statistics of their population|archiveurl=https://archive.today/20121210181859/http://www.world-gazetteer.com/wg.php?x=1280273714 & lng=de & des=wg & geo=-69 & srt=npan & col=adhoq & msz=1500 & men=gcis & lng=en|archivedate=10 December 2012|access-date=25 November 2016|url-status=dead}} (retrieved 2010-7-27) < /ref >  To tourists, it is mostly known because of [[Karnak]], as well as other tourist sites nearby. 
 
 Because the [[Ancient Egypt]]ian city of [[Thebes, Egypt|Thebes]] is located in Luxor, < ref > {{cite web|title=Luxor, Egypt|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/dna/place-lancashire/plain/A2082845|access-date=2016-11-25|archive-date=2013-04-19|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130419153726/http://news.bbc.co.uk/dna/place-lancashire/plain/A2082845|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  the city has been called the  " world's greatest open-air museum. "  On the other side of the [[Nile]] lies the [[Valley of the Kings]] and [[Valley of the Queens]].
