''Çăvaš Respubliki''), or '''Chuvashia''' ({{lang-ru|Чувашия}}, ''Chuvashiya''; {{lang-cv|Чӑваш Ен}}, transl. ''Çăvaš En''), is a [[Federal subjects of Russia|federal subject]] of [[Russia]]. Specifically, it is a [[Republics of Russia|republic]]. It is the home of the [[Chuvash people]], a [[Turkic peoples|Turkic]] [[ethnic group]]. Its [[Capital city|capital]] is the city of [[Cheboksary]]. As of the [[2010 census (Russia)|2010 Census]], its population was & nbsp;1,251,619. < ref name= " 2010Census " > {{ru-pop-ref|2010Census}} < /ref > 
 
 == Geography == 
 Chuvashia is located in the center of European Russia. It borders the [[Mari El Republic]], [[Nizhny Novgorod Oblast]], the [[Republic of Tatarstan]], the [[Republic of Mordovia]], and [[Ulyanovsk Oblast]]. Some of the major rivers in Chuvashia are the [[Volga]], the [[Sura (river)|Sura]], and the [[Tsivil]], but there are over 2,000 rivers in the republic and over 400 lakes. The climate of Chuvashia is [[Continental climate|continental]]. 
 
 === Natural resources === 
 Some of the [[natural resources]] found in Chuvashia include [[gypsum]], [[sand]], [[clay]], [[sapropel]], [[phosphorite]], and [[peat]]. There are also deposits of [[oil]] and [[natural gas]]. 30% of the land area of Chuvashia is [[forest]].

 == Demographics == 
 '''Population''': {{ru-census|p2010=1,251,619|p2002=1,313,754|p1989=1,336,066}} 
 
 The capital and largest city of Chuvashia is [[Cheboksary]] (population 464,000 in 2010). Cheboksary is located on the southern bank of the Volga. Just east of Cheboksary is the second largest city (a [[satellite city]] of Cheboksary), [[Novocheboksarsk]] (population 124,000 in 2010).

 === Ethnic groups === 
 According to the 2010 Census, ethnic [[Chuvash people|Chuvash]] make up 67.7% of the republic's population.  == Economy == 
 {{Main article|Economy of Chuvashia}} 
 
 The Chuvash Republic is the most populous and [[fertile]] area in the middle [[Volga region]]. There are woodlands on fertile black earth. In [[farming]], [[wheat]] and [[sugar-beet]], [[pigs]] and [[beef cattle]] have become more important than the [[rye]], [[oats]], [[barley]] and [[dairy cattle]] which were typical for the region.
