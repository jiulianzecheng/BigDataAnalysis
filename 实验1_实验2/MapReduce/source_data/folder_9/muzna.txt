 '''Muzna''' – whose name means ''Cloud of rain'' in Andalusian Arabic < ref > {{Cite journal|last=D. Fairchild Ruggles|date=2004|title=Mothers of a Hybrid Dynasty: Race, Genealogy, and Acculturation in al-Andalus|url=https://archive.org/details/sim_journal-of-medieval-and-early-modern-studies_winter-2004_34_1/page/65|journal=Journal of Medieval and Early Modern Studies|publisher=[[Duke University Press]]|volume=34|issue=1|page=65-94|doi=10.1215/10829636-34-1-65 |s2cid=170890527 |issn=1527-8263}} < /ref >  – was a [[slave]]-[[concubine]] of [[Emir]] [[Abdullah of Córdoba]] and a member of his imperial [[harem]]. She was the mother of [[Caliph]] [[Abd al-Rahman III]] < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Cristina de la Puente|date=2013|title=Free Fathers, Slave Mothers and their Children : a Contribution to the Study of Family Structures in Al-Andalus|url=http://www.raco.cat/index.php/ImagoTemporis/article/view/292948/381389|journal=Imago Temporis: Medium Aevum|page=|issn=1888-3931}} < /ref >  and was therefore given the title ''[[Umm Walad]]'', which used to be given to slave-concubines who had children. 
 
 She is of [[Basques|Basque]] origin (or [[Franks|Frankish]], according to French historian [[André Clot]] < ref > {{Cite book|title=L'Espagne musulmane|last=Clot|first=André|authorlink=André Clot|orig-year=1999|year=2004|publisher=[[Éditions Perrin|Perrin]]|isbn=2-262-01425-6|location=Paris|page=107|language=fr|chapter=Le califat}} < /ref > ) and was initially a [[Christianity|Christian]] before converting to [[Islam]]. < ref > {{Cite book|last=Cristina de la Puente|date=2017|title=The Ethnic Origins of Females Slaves in al-Andalus|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ysQ2DwAAQBAJ & q=Muzna & pg=PA124|journal=Concubines and Courtesans: Women and Slavery in Islamic History|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|page=|isbn=978-0-19-062219-0|issn=}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Joaquin Vallvé|date=1977|title=Sobre demografía y sociedad en al-Andalus (siglos VIII-XI)|url=https://www.proquest.com/openview/62eb246a9564cfdb4ea63521471acb3e/1?pq-origsite=gscholar & cbl=1817256|journal=Al-Andalus|language=es|location=Madrid|page=|issn=}} < /ref >  As the granddaughter of [[Fortún Garcés Cajal]], she is descended from the royal family of [[Kingdom of Navarre|Navarre]], the Aristas. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Adeline Rucquoi|date=February–March 2018|title=La Croix et le Croissant|url=|journal=Le Figaro Histoire|language=fr|issue=36 - L'Espagne musulmane d'Al-Andalus à la Reconquista|page=76-85|issn=2259-2733}} < /ref >   She died in 968. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Luisa Avila|date=1989|title=Las mujeres  " sabias "  en Al-Andalus|url=http://digital.csic.es/bitstream/10261/14481/1/Avila_Las%20mujeres.pdf|journal=La mujer en al-Andalus: Reflejos históricos de su actividad y categorías sociales|language=es|publisher=[[Autonomous University of Madrid]]|page=139-185|isbn=84-7587-117-8|issn=}} < /ref > 
 
 In his treatises and works such as ''[[The Ring of the Dove]]'', the [[polymath]] [[Ibn Hazm]] named her after her own name, '''Hazm''', and seemed to emphasize family ties between him and her. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zsQ2DwAAQBAJ & q=muzna & pg=PT166|title=Concubines and Courtesans|last1=Matthew S.
