The word ''Britannia'' in [[Latin]] could mean the [[British Isles]], the island of [[Great Britain]], or the [[Roman province]] of [[Roman Britain|Britain]] in the time of the [[Roman Empire]]. < ref > {{Citation|last1=Cannon|first1=John|title=A Dictionary of British History|year=2015|chapter=Britannia|chapter-url=https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/9780191758027.001.0001/acref-9780191758027-e-516|edition=3rd|publisher=Oxford University Press|language=en|doi=10.1093/acref/9780191758027.001.0001|isbn=978-0-19-175802-7|access-date=2021-02-15|last2=Crowcroft|first2=Robert}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{Citation|last=Hargreaves|chapter-url=https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/9780199677832.001.0001/acref-9780199677832-e-616|editor2-last=Cannon|access-date=2021-02-15|isbn=978-0-19-967783-2|doi=10.1093/acref/9780199677832.001.0001|language=en|publisher=Oxford University Press|edition=2nd|chapter=Britannia|first=A.  Images of Britannia show her sitting or lying down with a [[spear]] and shield. This image was first used on Roman [[coins]] in the 2nd [[century]] AD. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  After the Romans, the image of Britannia was first used again for coins of the [[pound sterling]] made for [[Charles II of England, Scotland, and Ireland]]. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  These were the first to show Britannia with a [[Union Flag]] on her shield. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  After many victories of Britain's [[Royal Navy]], coins with Britannia show [[Neptune (mythology)|Neptune]]'s trident from 1797. Her image wears a helmet on coins after 1825. < ref name= " :1 "  / >
