 '''Wm Morrison Supermarkets plc (Morrisons)''' ({{lse|MRW}}) is the fourth biggest supermarket in the [[United Kingdom]]. < ref name= " co-op " > {{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/7508982.stm |title=Co-op buys Somerfield for £1.57bn |publisher=BBC News |date=16 July 2008 |accessdate=26 July 2012}} < /ref >  Its main offices are in [[Bradford]], West Yorkshire, England. < ref > " [http://www.morrisons.co.uk/Store-finder/About-customer-services/Contact-Us/ Contact Us]. "  Morrisons. Retrieved 26 July 2012. < /ref >  The company is usually called '''Morrisons'''. In 2008, Sir Ken Morrison left the company. < ref > {{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/money/main.jhtml?xml=/money/2008/03/14/cnmorrison11.xml |title=Sir Ken Morrison, 76, bows out to applause and sales gift |work=The Daily Telegraph |location=UK |date=14 March 2008 |accessdate=26 July 2012 |first=James |last=Hall |archive-date=15 March 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080315174457/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/money/main.jhtml?xml=%2Fmoney%2F2008%2F03%2F14%2Fcnmorrison11.xml |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 Dalton Philips is the current head. The old CEO was Marc Bolland, who left to become CEO of Marks  &  Spencer. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.morrisons.co.uk/Corporate/About-Morrisons/Board-members/ |title=WM Morrisons Board Member Biographies |publisher=Morrisons.co.uk |accessdate=26 July 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 As of September 2009, Morrisons has 455 shops in the United Kingdom. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.morrisons.co.uk/Corporate/About-us/Our-Stores/ |title='Our stores', Morrisons website |publisher=Morrisons.co.uk |accessdate=26 July 2012}} < /ref > 
 
 On 15 March 2007,  Morrisons said that it would stop its old branding and go for a more modern brand image. Their lower price brand, ''Bettabuy'', was also changed to a more modern brand called the ''Morrisons Value''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.thegrocer.co.uk/grt_article.aspx?articleid=100523 |title=The Grocer Today – Latest News |publisher=www.thegrocer.co.uk |accessdate=26 July 2012}} < /ref >  This brand was then changed again in 2012 as Morrisons started their low price option brand called ''M Savers''. 
 
 In 2005 Morrisons bought part of the old Rathbones Bakeries for £15.5 & nbsp;million which make Rathbones and Morrisons bread. < ref > {{cite news|author=Press Association |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/business/2005/may/03/supermarkets.money |title=Morrison buys Rathbone Bakeries |work=Guardian |location=UK |date= 3 May 2005|accessdate=12 March 2011}} < /ref > 
 
 In 2011, Morrisons opened a new 767,500 square/foot centre in Bridgwater for a £11 & nbsp;million redevelopment project. This project also made 200 new jobs. < ref > {{cite web|title=Morrisons Distribution Centre Preview|url=http://www.bridgwatermercury.co.uk/news/9325024.Morrisons_distribution_centre_preview/|publisher=Bridgwater Mercury|accessdate=6 July 2012}} < /ref >
