In [[psychology]], '''anhedonia''' ( <  [[Ancient Greek language|Greek]] '''αν-''' an-, ''without'' + '''ηδονή''' hēdonē, ''pleasure'' ) is not being able to feel [[pleasure]]. This may include not enjoying things like eating, exercise, watching movies, meeting friends, social activities, or sexual interaction and activity. 
 
 In most cases, anhedonia is caused by [[mental disorder]]s. It happens in some severe cases of [[Major depressive disorder|depression]] and it is a common [[symptom]] of [[schizophrenia]], [[schizoid personality disorder]] and [[major depressive disorder]].
