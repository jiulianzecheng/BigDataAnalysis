 The '''Meuse''' ({{lang-nl|Maas}}) is a [[river]] in [[Western Europe]]. The river starts in [[France]], goes through [[Belgium]] and the [[Netherlands]]. It ends in the [[North Sea]]. < ref > Robert Pateman; Mark Elliott, ''Belgium'' (New York: Marshall Cavendish Benchmark, 2006), p. 11 < /ref >  The Meuse is fed mostly by [[rain|rainwater]]. < ref > ''Wetlands: Environmental Gradients, Boundaries, and Buffers'', ed. George Mulamoottil; et al. (CRC Press, 1996), p. 92 < /ref >  It is the oldest river in the [[wikt:world|world]]. 
 
 ==History==  
 
 From 1301, the Meuse in the [[Netherlands]] was the [[west]]ern [[border]] of the [[Holy Roman Empire]]. In [[World War II]], the Meuse was a goal for the [[Nazi Germany|Germans]] during the [[Battle of the Bulge]]. < ref > Steve Kane, ''The 1st SS Panzer Division in the Battle of the Bulge'' (Bennington, VT: Merriam Press, 1997), p. 22 < /ref >
