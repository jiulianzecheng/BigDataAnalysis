 '''Hanoi''' ({{lang-vi|Hà Nội}}) is the [[capital (city)|capital]] of [[Vietnam]]. It is also the second most populated city in the [[country]]. In 2023, about 5,253,000 < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ha Noi, Vietnam Metro Area Population 1950-2023|url=https://www.macrotrends.net/cities/22457/ha-noi/population|access-date=2023-08-22|website=www.macrotrends.net}} < /ref >  people lived in the city. It has been the capital of the [[North Vietnam|Democratic Republic of Vietnam]] (North Vietnam) since [[1945]] and the capital of the [[Vietnam|Socialist Republic of Vietnam]] (all of Vietnam) since 1976. Before that, it was the capital of the [[country]] now called Vietnam for most of the time from at least the [[11th century]] until 1802. The city is on the right of the [[Red River (Vietnam)|Red River]], and the city's name translates to English as  " inside the river " . Things made there include [[machine tool]]s, [[plywood]], [[textiles]], [[chemical industry|chemicals]] and [[handicrafts]]. Hanoi is at 21°2' North, 105°51' East, 1,760 & nbsp;km north of [[Ho Chi Minh City]] (formerly Saigon). < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://earth-info.nga.mil/gns/html/cntry_files.html |title=GNS: Country Files |access-date=2006-02-28 |archive-date=2012-08-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120823004343/http://earth-info.nga.mil/gns/html/cntry_files.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Popular tourist sites in the city include Ho Chi Minh's [[Mausoleum]], [[Hoa Lo Prison]], and the [[Palace of Literature]]. Nearby Hanoi is also where [[Phở|pho]], Vietnamese beef noodle soup, was first created.
