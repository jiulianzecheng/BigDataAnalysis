 '''Mudvayne''' is an [[United States|American]] [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] [[band]]. They formed in [[Peoria, Illinois]] in 1996. They play for the [[record label]] [[Epic Records]]. 
 
 == History == 
 === Beginnings (1996–98) === 
 In 1996, the band was formed with its original line-up. In 1997, the band released their very first EP, ''Kill, I Oughta''. In 1998, the band signed to [[Epic Records]]. 
 
 === ''L.D. 50'' (1999–2001) === 
 In 1999, the band started working on their debut album, ''L.D. 50''. It was released on August 22, 2000. The album peaked at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Heatseekers chart and No. 85 on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' 200]]. The band participated in the [[Tattoo to Earth]] tour, along with many other bands. This album has the [[Single (music)|singles]]  " Dig "  and  " Death Blooms " . 
 
 === ''The End of All Things to Come'' (2002–03) === 
 In January 2002, the band was starting to work on their second album. It was released on November 19, 2002. It was certified Gold by the RIAA in 2003. Also in 2003, the band had participated in the [[Summer Sanitarium Tour]]. This album contains the singles ''Not Falling'' and ''World So Cold''. 
 
 === ''Lost and Found'' (2004–06) === 
 The album was worked on all throughout 2004, it was set to be released in November 2004, with its first single ( " Determined " ) set to be released in September of that year, but was pushed back due to production issues, it was eventually released on April 12, 2005. The album sold 100, 000 copies at the week of it's release. It debuted at No. 2 and was certified Gold by the RIAA later that same year. This album contains the singles  " Determined "  (which was nominated at the 2006 Grammy Awards),  " Happy? " ,  " Forget to Remember "  and  " Fall into Sleep " . 
 
 === ''By the People, for the People'', ''The New Game'', self-titled album and break-up (2007–10) === 
 On November 27, 2007, Mudvayne released their compilation album. Also in 2007, they have been working on a new album, which was released on November 18, 2008. It was initially set for a late 2007 release but production was temporarily put on hold in mid 2007, before resuming in early 2008. The album debuted at No. 15 on the Billboard 200. It sold 48,000 copies at the week of its release. This album has the singles  " Do What You Do " ,  " A New Game "  and  " Scarlet Letters " .  
 
 In 2008, right after the release of their fourth album, they started to work on their final album, which was their self titled album. It was released on December 21, 2009. The album debuted at No. 54 on the ''Billboard'' 200 and sold around 34,000 copies at the week of it's release. By the next week, sales dropped a lot. This album has the single  " Scream with Me " .
