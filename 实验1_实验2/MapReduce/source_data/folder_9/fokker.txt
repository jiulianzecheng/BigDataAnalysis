 '''Fokker''' was a [[Netherlands|Dutch]] [[aircraft]] [[Manufacturing|manufacturer]] named after its founder Anthony Fokker. The company operated under several different names starting out in [[1912]] in [[Schwerin]], [[Germany]] and moving to the Netherlands in [[1919]]. During its most successful period in the 1920s and 1930s, it dominated the civil aviation market. Fokker went into [[bankruptcy]] in [[1996]] and its operations were sold to competitors. 
 
 ==History== 
 Since 1967, Fokker built parts for [[Europe|European]] [[Satellite (artificial)|satellites]]. Next year the company made the first Dutch satellite (''the Astronomical Netherlands Satellite'').
