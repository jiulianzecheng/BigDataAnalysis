 '''Ham''' is [[pork]] that comes from a [[leg]] of a [[pig]]. < ref name= " Chambers " > " Bacon: Bacon and Ham Curing "  in ''[[Chambers's Encyclopædia]]''. London: [[George Newnes Ltd|George Newnes]], 1961, Vol. 2, p. 39. < /ref >   
 
 Ham can be dry-cured or wet-cured. Dry-cured ham is made by rubbing the meat with [[salt]]. < ref > {{cite journal|last1=Wierbicki|first1=Eugen|last2=Howker|first2=John J|year=1976|title=Effect of salt, phosphates and other curing ingredients on shrinkage of lean pork meat and quality of smoked processed ham|journal=Journal of Food Science|volume=41|issue=5|pages=1116–1121|doi=10.1111/j.1365-2621.1976.tb14399.x}} < /ref >  Wet-cured ham is made with [[brine]]. < ref name= " meat " > {{cite web|title=Curing Methods|url=http://www.meatsandsausages.com/sausage-making/curing/methods|publisher=Meat and Sausages.com}} < /ref > 
 
 Types of dry-cured ham: 
 *[[Prosciutto|Prosciutto crudo]] ([[Italy|Italian]]) 
 *[[Prosciutto di Parma]] (Italian) 
 *[[Bayonne ham|Jambon de bayonne]] ([[France|French]]) 
 *[[Jamón serrano]] ([[Spain|Spanish]]) 
 *[[Jamón iberico]] (Spanish) 
 *[[Country ham]] ([[United States|American]]) 
 *[[York ham]] ([[England|English]]) 
 *[[Westphalian ham]] ([[Germany|German]])
