'''Anastasia''' or '''Anastacia''' [an-uh-stey-zhuh, ah-nuh-stah-shuh; Russian uh-nuh-stah-syi-yuh] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Anastasia {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/anastasia|access-date=2023-03-14|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a Female name that comes from the Koine [[Greek language|Greek]]. In English, it means  " She who shall rise up again " , < ref > {{Cite web|title=Anastasia name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Anastasia-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-14|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  but it is also used to mean  " [[resurrection]] " . The name, and its similar male name, [[Anastasius]], were given to children born during [[Easter]] in the early years of [[Christianity]].Ranked #181 in the nation for newborn prevalence in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/|access-date=2023-03-14|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The names  " Stacey "  (also spelled  " Stacie " ,  " Stacy "  or  " Stacee " ),  " Tasia " , and  " Stasia "  are short for Anastasia. The name “Nastya” are also common shortname for those named Anastasia.
