 The term  " lifestyle "  was used for the first time by Austrian psychologist [[Alfred Adler]] (1870-1937). < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.etymonline.com/search?q=lifestyle|title=lifestyle - Search Online Etymology Dictionary|website=www.etymonline.com}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Individual identity== 
 A lifestyle is a way of living or doing things. Lifestyle is doing things, living your life and making [[decision]]s in your own unique way. Lifestyle can be political, social, economical and personal way of seeing, doing and understanding things. Not all parts of a lifestyle are our decisions. Surrounding social and technical systems, like [[Economy|economical]] situation and [[Society|people around us]], can limit the lifestyle choices available to the individual. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pClKDwAAQBAJ & dq=Alternative%20lifestyle%20homosexuality & pg=PT64|title=Lifestyle Media in American Culture: Gender, Class, and the Politics of Ordinariness|last=Ryan|first=Maureen E.|date=2018-02-01|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-315-46495-4|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.italyanstyle.com/en/|title=Italyan Style: Puro Stile Italiano|website=Italyan Style}} < /ref >
