 '''''Thunbergia''''' [[Anders Jahan Retzius|Retz.]] is a [[genus]] of [[flowering plant]]s in the [[family (biology)|family]] [[Acanthaceae]], native to tropical regions of [[Africa]], [[Madagascar]] and southern [[Asia]]. Its members are known by various names, including '''thunbergias'''; '''clockvine''' on its own usually refers to ''Thunbergia grandiflora'', while ''Thunbergia alata'' is often known as '''Black-eyed Susan vine''' or just '''[[Black-eyed Susan]]'''  < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://lakecounty.typepad.com/life_in_lake_county/2007/04/a_tale_of_two_s.html |title=A Tale of Two Susans |access-date=2008-04-18 |archive-date=2007-04-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070417175245/http://lakecounty.typepad.com/life_in_lake_county/2007/04/a_tale_of_two_s.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref > (not to be confused with other flowers called [[Black-eyed Susan (disambiguation)|Black-eyed Susan]]). Orange clockvine is the name of ''Thunbergia gregorii''. 
 
 Thunbergia was named for [[Carl Peter Thunberg]].
