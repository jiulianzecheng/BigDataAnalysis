 The '''Ditrysia''' are a natural group or [[clade]] of [[insects]] in the [[Lepidoptera]]n [[Scientific classification|order]]. It contains both [[butterfly|butterflies]] and [[moth]]s. They are called ditrisians because the female has two distinct sexual openings: one for [[mating]], and the other for laying [[egg (biology)|eggs]].   
 
 About 98% of [[Binomial nomenclature|described]] species of Lepidoptera belong to Ditrysia. The group can be divided into [[basal]] or ''[[incertae sedis]]''  " micromoths " , and the '''Apoditrysia'''.  The Apoditrysia includes mostly the larger moths and the butterflies.
