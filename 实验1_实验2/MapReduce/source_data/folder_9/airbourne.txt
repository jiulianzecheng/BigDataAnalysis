 '''Airbourne''' are an Australian [[hard rock]] band from [[Warrnambool, Victoria]], [[Australia]]. Original members are Joel O'Keeffe, Ryan O'Keeffe, David Roads and Adam Jacobson, but Adam leaves the band in 2004 and was replaced by Justin Street. In 2004, the band release their first [[Extended Play|EP]], called  " Ready to Rock " . Their first studio album was released in 2007 and is called  " Runnin' Wild " . This album have three singles:  " Runnin' Wild " ,  " Too Much, Too Young, Too Fast "  and  " Diamond in the Rough " . In 2010, the band released their second studio album, called  " No Guts. No Glory " . The third studio album is called  " Black Dog Barking "  and was released in 2013.

 === ''Runnin' Wild'' (2006–2009) === 
 In 2006 Airbourne travelled to the [[United States]] to begin work on their first studio album, ''Runnin' Wild'', with producer Bob Marlette ([[Ozzy Osbourne]], [[Alice Cooper]]). < ref name= " EMIBio " > {{cite web | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070901143528/http://www.musichead.com.au/site/artist.asp?actID=992275 & bio=1 | url = http://www.musichead.com.au/site/artist.asp?actID=992275 & bio=1 | title = Airbourne Biography | publisher = musichead Australia ([[EMI|EMI Music Australia Pty Ltd]]) | access-date = 1 November 2011 | archive-date = 1 September 2007 }} < /ref > < ref name= " Rivadavia " > {{cite web | url = {{Allmusic|class=album|id=r1086432/review|pure_url=yes}} | title = ''Runnin' Wild''  >  Airbourne | last1 = Rivadavia | first1 = Eduardo | publisher = [[Allmusic]] ([[Rovi Corporation]]) | access-date = 30 October 2011}} < /ref > ''Runnin' Wild'' was released in Australia on 23 June 2007. Three singles were issued from the album,  " Runnin' Wild "  (May),  " Too Much, Too Young, Too Fast "  (June), a playable track on ''[[Guitar Hero World Tour]]''; and  " Diamond in the Rough " . In February, Capitol Records had cancelled their contract with Airbourne, but the album was still issued in Australia through [[EMI]]. In June, the band signed with [[Roadrunner Records]] for international distribution. < ref name= " Donovan2 " > {{cite news | url = http://www.theage.com.au/news/entertainment/airbourne-aloft-again/2007/06/19/1182019116381.html | title = Airbourne's Fortunes Take Off Again | last1 = Donovan | first1 = Patrick | work = [[The Age]] | publisher = Fairfax Media | date = 20 June 2007 | access-date = 30 October 2011 }} < /ref >

''Runnin' Wild'' was issued in North America, Europe and Japan in January 2008, after which they relocated permanently to the US. < ref name= " EMINews " > {{cite web | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20071227105433/http://www.musichead.com.au/site/artist.asp?actID=992275 | url = http://www.musichead.com.au/site/artist.asp?actID=992275 | title = Airbourne Home – News | publisher = musichead Australia (EMI Music Australia Pty Ltd) | date = 16 November 2007 | access-date = 1 November 2011 | archive-date = 27 December 2007 }} < /ref >  The album reached the Top & nbsp;100 on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and appeared on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. < ref name= " UKAlbums " > {{cite web | url = http://www.theofficialcharts.com/artist/_/airbourne/#albums | title = Airbourne – Artist Chart History – Albums | publisher = [[UK Albums Chart]] ([[The Official Charts Company]]) | access-date = 1 November 2011 }} < /ref > < ref name= " BillbAlbs " > {{cite web | url = {{BillboardURLbyName|artist=airbourne|chart=all}} | title = Airbourne Album  &  Song Chart History | publisher = [[Billboard 200]].  In a September 2008 interview with MetalSucks, Joel discussed comparisons with AC/DC,  " whoever you are when you come out, especially if you’re from Australia and you sound like we do, you’re going to get compared to anyone who is out there. It doesn’t matter who you are, you are going to get compared to somebody. To be compared to the best rock and roll band in the business, who are still going today and are about to release another album, there is no higher compliment... " < ref name= " Rosenberg " > {{cite web | url = http://www.metalsucks.net/2008/09/02/exclusive-interview-with-airbournes-joel-okeefe/ | title = MetalSucks Interview with Joel O'Keeffe | first1= Axl | last1 = Rosenburg | first2 = Vince | last2 = Neilstein | publisher = [[MetalSucks]] (Axl Rosenberg, Vince Neilstein) | date = 2 September 2008 | access-date = 1 November 2011 }} < /ref >  In November at the Astoria in London, Dan Hawkins (Stone Gods/The Darkness) joined Airbourne on stage to play the AC/DC song,  " Whole Lotta Rosie " . < ref name= " Ling " > {{cite web | url = http://www.daveling.co.uk/diarynov08.htm | title = Dave's Diary | last1 = Ling | first1 = Dave | work = Dave Ling – Music Journalist | date = 29 November 2008 | access-date = 1 November 2011 | archive-date = 2 April 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120402021525/http://www.daveling.co.uk/diarynov08.htm | url-status = dead }} < /ref >  Hawkins played Joel's guitar (white Gibson Explorer) while Joel sang without any guitar.

 Roads confirmed that in January 2009, Airbourne would enter the studio to begin recording their second album, ''No Guts. No Glory.'' < ref name= " MetalHammer " > {{cite web | url = http://www.metalhammer.co.uk/mhtv/?movieID=884 & key=1 |title = Metal Hammer » MHTV | publisher = MetalHammer (Future Publishing Limited) | access-date = 1 November 2011 }} < /ref >  In the 17 January issue of ''[[Kerrang!]]'' magazine, Joel revealed they had earlier written the tracks in the Criterion Hotel,  " We're getting all our gear plugged in and getting set for Aussie pub rock written in an Aussie rock pub! " < ref > O'Keefe, Joel.  " Airbourne Begin Work on New Album – in a Pub! " , ''[[Kerrang!]]'', 17 January 2009. < /ref > 
 
 The album was produced by Johnny K, mixed by Mike Fraser, and was released on 8 March 2010 in the UK, Europe, Canada, Japan, Australia and New Zealand, and in the U.S. on 20 April.  " Born to Kill " , was first played live at the Logan Campbell Centre, Auckland, New Zealand in October 2009. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xzxU2IGejVE & feature=PlayList & p=92DCE2110D8D1D1A & playnext=1 & playnext_from=PL & index=7 |title=Airbourne -  " Born to Kill (live) "  |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2010-07-26}} < /ref >  In January 2010, another new song,  " No Way But the Hard Way " , was played on the BBC Radio 1's ''Rock Show''. On 9 February it was available on [[iTunes]], as the first single from the album. The album reached the top & nbsp;20 on the ARIA Albums Chart, and top & nbsp;20 on albums charts in Austria, New Zealand, Finland, Greece, Sweden and Switzerland. < ref name= " AusChartsNoGuts " > {{cite web | url = http://www.australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Airbourne & titel=No+Guts%2E+No+Glory%2E & cat=a | title = Airbourne – ''No Guts. No Glory.'' | publisher =Australian Charts Portal (Hung Medien (Steffen Hung)) | access-date = 2 November 2011 }} < /ref >  In the UK it peaked at No. & nbsp;31, while on the US ''Billboard'' 200 it reached the top & nbsp;100. < ref name= " UKAlbums "  / > < ref name= " BillbAlbs "  / > 
 
 The Band went on a UK Tour supporting Iron Maiden on their Final Frontier World Tour from 20 July Starting at Glasgow SECC Arena ending at Cardiff Motorpoint Arena on 1 August 2011 
 
 === ''Black Dog Barking'' (2011-2014) === 
 [[File:Slash with Airbourne shirt at Sweden Rock Festival 2015.png|150px|right|thumb|[[Slash (musician)|Slash]] with a band shirt during the Sweden Rock Festival 2015.]] 
 
 It was announced in November 2011 through their official Facebook page that since mid-2011 the band had been working on a new album, and that updates were to follow soon. < ref name= " AirbourneFacebook " > {{cite web | url = http://www.facebook.com/airbournerock | title = Airbourne Facebook Page }} < /ref > < ref name= " AirbourneFacebookComment " > {{cite web | url = http://www.facebook.com/airbournerock/posts/10150386339638870 | title = Facebook New Album Comment }} < /ref >  The album's name, ''Black Dog Barking'', was announced in early February 2013 on their Facebook page. < ref > {{cite web | url = https://www.facebook.com/airbournerock/posts/10151280939713870 | title = Airbourne Facebook Page: Black Dog Barking. | date = 6 February 2013 | access-date = 6 February 2013 }} < /ref >  It was released on 21 May 2013 via Roadrunner. < ref name= " Stereoboard " > {{cite web | url = http://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/177224/9 | title = Airbourne Announce Release Of New Album 'Black Dog Barking' - Stereoboard UK | publisher = Stereoboard UK | date = 6 February 2013 | access-date = 6 February 2013 }} < /ref >  The cover artwork was once again created by Australian artists ''The Sharp Brothers'', who also did the cover artwork for ''No Guts. No Glory.'' According to David Roads, the idea behind the album title was, to use the Black Dog as a metaphor for the bands ability to break the rules, especially not to care for db limits. < ref > {{cite web | url = http://entertaimnet.wordpress.com/2013/12/11/airbourne/ | title = AIRBOURNE - ALIVE AND KICKING - entertaim.net Interview David | date = 11 December 2013 | access-date = 15 December 2013 }} < /ref >   At the end of 2013 Airbourne toured Europe, supported - amongst others - by the Swedish band Corroded.
