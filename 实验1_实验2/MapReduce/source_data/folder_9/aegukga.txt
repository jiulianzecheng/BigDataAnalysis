 " '''Aegukga''' "  ({{lang-ko|애국가}}, {{small|say: {{respell|ay|GOOК|gə}}}}, meaning  " The Patriotic Song " ) is the [[national anthem]] of the [[South Korea|Republic of Korea (South Korea)]]. It should not be confused with [[Aegukka|North Korea's anthem]], which has the same name. 
 
 The song was sung for the first time against the [[Empire of Japan|colonial Japanese]]. The melody of the song comes from the tune of [[Scotland|Scottish]] patriotic song  " [[Auld Lang Syne]] " .

 | < poem > {{small|'''1st verse'''}} 
 Until the East Sea's waves are dry, Mt. Baekdu worn away, 
 God watch o'er our land forever, our Korea manse. 
 
 {{small|'''Refrain:'''}} 
 Rose of Sharon, thousand miles of range and river land; 
 Guarded by her people, ever may Korea stand. 
 
 {{small|'''2nd verse'''}} 
 Like that Mt. Namsan armoured pine, standing on duty still, 
 wind or frost, unchanging ever, be our resolute will. 
 
 {{small|'''''Refrain'''''}} 
 
 {{small|'''3rd verse'''}} 
 In autumn's, arching evening sky, crystal and cloudless blue; 
 be the radiant moon our spirit, steadfast, single and true. 
 
 {{small|'''''Refrain'''''}} 
 
 {{small|'''4th verse'''}} 
 With such a will, such a spirit, loyalty, heart and hand, 
 Let us love, come grief, come gladness, this our beloved land. 
 
 {{small|'''''Refrain'''''}} < /poem >
