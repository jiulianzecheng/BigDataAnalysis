 '''Achappam''' is a deep-fried [[cookie]] made with [[rice flour]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BXluAAAAMAAJ & q=Achappam|title=Social and Cultural History of Kerala|last=Menon|first=A. Sreedhara|date=1979|publisher=Sterling|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=UdLYAAAAMAAJ & q=Achappam|title=Christian Folklore|last=Choondal|first=Chummar|date=1988|publisher=Kerala Folklore Academy|language=en}} < /ref >  They are made using [[Pattern|patterned]] irons to give any size or shape. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/1112131009|title=The food we eat, the stories we tell : contemporary Appalachian tables|others=Engelhardt, Elizabeth S. D. (Elizabeth Sanders Delwiche), 1969-, Smith, Lora E., 1979-|year=2019|isbn=978-0-8214-2391-2|location=Athens|oclc=1112131009}} < /ref >  The [[Batter (cooking)|batter]] is a mixture of [[wheat flour]], [[Egg (food)|eggs]], [[sugar]] and [[Milk|whole milk]]. The iron has a handle and a patterned mould at its end. The iron is heated to a very high temperature in [[oil]]. It is then dipped into the batter and then dipped in the hot oil again. The iron is lifted from the oil after the cookie separates from the iron. < ref > {{Cite news|last=Rajkumar|first=Shanthini|date=2015-12-31|title=Of rose cookies and kalkals|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/features/metroplus/yearend-festivities-bring-on-a-nostalgia-trip-more-so-when-associated-with-food/article8050191.ece|access-date=2020-10-25|issn=0971-751X}} < /ref >
