{{nihongo|'''Keian'''|慶安}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,|''nengō'',|lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Shoho|Shōhō]]'' and before ''[[Jōō (Edo period)|Jōō]].''  This period started in February 1648 and ended in September 1652. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA503  " ''Kei'an'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 503. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Komyo of Japan|Go-Kōmyō]]''-tennō''|後光明天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA256  " Go-Kōmyō Tennō, " ] p. 256; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA412  ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', p. 412.] < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Keian'' era== 
 [[File:Nihon Odai Ichiran 1834.jpg|thumb|right|''Nihon Ōdai Ichiran'' was first published in the 5th year of ''Keian'']] 
 * '''1648''' (''Keian 1''): Legal code about city life and business are established in Osaka < ref > [[John Whitney Hall|Hall, John Whitney]]. ''Early Modern Japan,'' p. xx. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1649''' (''Keian 2''): Five-storied [[pagoda]] at {{nihongo|[[Sensō-ji]]|金龍山浅草寺|Kinryū-zan Sensō-ji}} was built in [[Asakusa]]. < ref > Nihon Gaiji Kyōkai. (1942). [https://books.google.com/books?id=F4kTAAAAIAAJ & q=keian+  ''Contemporary Japan: a Review of Japanese Affairs,'' Vol. 11, Part 2, p. 749.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1649''' (''Keian 2''): Legal code for peasants established by [[Tokugawa Iemitsu]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA503  " ''Kei'an no ofuregake'' " ] at p. 503. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1 April 1649''' (''Keian 2, 20th day of the 2nd month'') < !-- NengoCalc  慶安二年二月二十日 -- > : There was a major earthquake in Edo. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA412 p. 412.] < /ref > 
 
 * '''1651''' (''Keian 4''): Plot to overthrow the Tokugawa, also known as the [[Keian Uprising]]. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA503  " ''Kei'an jinken'' " ] at p. 503; [[Timon Screech|Screech, Timon]]. (2006). ''Secret Memoirs of the Shogans: Isaac Titsingh and Japan, 1779-1822'', pp. 85-89. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1652''' (''Keian 5, 5th month''):  ''[[Nihon Odai Ichiran]]'' was published. < ref > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA406   pp. 406, 412.] < /ref >   The French version of this work was the first Japanese history book to be published in the West.
