 '''Hyperoliidae''' is a family of small to medium sized, brightly colored, [[frog]]s.  It has more than 250 species in 19 [[genus|genera]]. Seventeen genera live in sub-Saharan Africa. < ref > Shiotz, 1999 < /ref >   
 
 Hyperoliids range from {{convert|1.5|cm|in}} to {{convert|8|cm|in}} in body length. Many species have smooth, brightly patterned, skin that almost looks [[Enamel (glass)|enamel]]ed. < ref name=EoR > {{cite book |editor=Cogger, H.G.  &  Zweifel, R.G.|author= Zweifel, Robert G.|year=1998|title=Encyclopedia of Reptiles and Amphibians|url=https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0|publisher= Academic Press|location=San Diego|pages= [https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofre0000unse_h2i0/page/100 100]â€“101|isbn= 0-12-178560-2}} < /ref > 
 
 Most hyperoliids live in trees.  Some live on land, including several ''[[Kassina]]'' species that move by walking or running rather than hopping. Diet varies widely, with examples including ''[[Tornierella]]'', who eats snails, and ''[[Afrixalus fornasinii]]'', the only land frog known to eat eggs of other species of [[anura]]ns. 
 
 Breeding in this family begins at the start of the rainy season.  Hyperoliids come together at breeding sites. Most hyperoliids lay their eggs in water. < ref name=EoR/ >  [[Tadpole]]s are pond type [[larvae]] with large dorsal fins on the tail. 
 
 No fossil hyperoliids are known.
