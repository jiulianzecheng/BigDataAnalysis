'''Luton''' is a large town in [[Bedfordshire]], [[England]]. It is about {{convert|30|mi|km}} north of [[London]]. It is on the [[M1 motorway]] and has mainline [[trains]] from [[King's Cross station|King's Cross]] and [[St Pancras station]]s. It is an industrial town which has diversified from its original base as a manufacturer of hats and cars. It is also famous for its airport which serves flights across Europe. Its fine communications means it has many people who [[commuting|commute]] to work in London.  It has a population of 213,052 (mid-2019 estimate). < ref > {{cite web|publisher=[[Office for National Statistics]]|title=Table PHP01 2011 Census: Usual residents ... wards in England and Wales;|work=2011 Census: population and household estimates for Wards and Output Areas in England and Wales|url=http://www.ons.gov.uk/ons/rel/census/2011-census/population-and-household-estimates-for-wards-and-output-areas-in-england-and-wales/rft-table-php01-2011-wards-in-england-and-wales.zip|date=23 November 2012|accessdate=19 January 2013}}. Dunstable wards 36,253. Houghton Regis wards 17,283. < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 Luton was started in about the [[6th century]] by the [[Saxons]], based by the [[River Lea]]. < ref > [http://www.localhistories.org/luton.html History of Luton] < /ref >  It has a history of industry: making hats and [[Vauxhall]] [[cars]] for example. 
 
 ==Politics== 
 Luton is a [[unitary authority]], which means it is governed locally by its own council, Luton Borough Council. < ref > [https://www.luton.gov.uk Luton Council Website] < /ref >  It is represented by two [[Member of Parliament|members of parliament]]. 
 
 ==Sport== 
 [[Luton Town F.C.|Its football club]] is in the English football league and play at Kenilworth Road. < ref > [https://www.lutontown.co.uk/club/club-history/ History of Luton Town F.C.] < /ref >  Their nickname is 'The Hatters'. They are called this because of Luton's history of making straw [[hat]]s.
