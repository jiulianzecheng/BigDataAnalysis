 On a [[sailing]] vessel, a '''forestay''', sometimes called a '''jibstay''', or a '''headstay''', is a piece of [[standing rigging]]. < ref name=glossaryYPI > {{cite web |url=https://www.ypigroup.com/en/yacht-terms/yachting-glossary/ |title=YPI glossary |publisher=Yachting Partners International |access-date=December 29, 2016 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It keeps a [[mast]] from falling backwards. < ref name=glossaryYPI/ >   It is usually attached to the very top of the mast. < ref name=glossaryYPI/ > < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.glen-l.com/free-book/rigging-small-sailboats-2.html |title=Rigging Small Boats, Chapter 2 |publisher=Glen-L marine designs |access-date=December 29, 2016 |archive-date=December 21, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161221013831/http://www.glen-l.com/free-book/rigging-small-sailboats-2.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The other end of the forestay is attached to the [[Bow (ship)|bow]] of the boat. < ref name=glossaryYPI/ >    
 
 Often a [[sail]] is attached to the forestay. < ref name=glossaryYPI/ >   This sail may be a [[jib]] or a [[genoa (sail)|genoa]]. < ref name=glossaryYPI/ >   In a [[Cutter (ship)|cutter]] rig, the jib or jibs are flown from stays in front of the forestay, perhaps going from the masthead to a [[bowsprit]].  The sail on the forestay is then referred to as the [[staysail (sail)|staysail]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.cruisingworld.com/how/rig-staysail |title=Rig for a Staysail |author=Don Street |date=March 14, 2013 |publisher=Cruising World |access-date=December 29, 2016}} < /ref >  A forestay might be made from [[stainless steel]] [[wire]] or [[rod (geometry)|rod]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.sailboat-cruising.com/sailboat-rigging.html |title=Sailboat Rigging: Part 1 - Standing Rigging |publisher=sailboat-cruising.com |access-date=December 29, 2016}} < /ref >
