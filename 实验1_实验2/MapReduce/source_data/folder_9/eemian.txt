 The [[Holocene]] is the present interglacial.   
 
 The Eemian is part of the Middle [[Paleolithic]]. It is of some interest to the [[evolution]] of  " [[anatomy|anatomically]] modern "  [[Human|humans]]. Modern humans existed at that time. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/262692230|title=Human mitochondrial DNA and the evolution of homo sapiens|date=2006|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-3-540-31789-0|location=Berlin|page=233|oclc=262692230}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Other names== 
 The Eemian is known as the Ipswichian in the [[United Kingdom|UK]], the Mikulin interglacial in [[Russia]], the Valdivia interglacial in [[Chile]] and the Riss-Würm interglacial in the [[Alps]]. Depending on how a specific publication defines the Sangamonian Stage of [[North America]], the Eemian is equivalent to either all or part of it. 
 
 ==Climate== 
 The Eemian climate was, on average, about 1° to 2°[[Celsius|C]] (1.8° to 3.6°[[Fahrenheit|F]]) warmer than that of the Holocene. < ref > {{Cite web | url=https://www.temperaturerecord.org/ |title = Current  &  historical global temperature graph}} < /ref >  During the Eemian, the proportion of [[Carbon dioxide|CO < sub > 2 < /sub > ]] in the atmosphere was about 280 parts per million. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://mashable.com/2018/08/27/earth-warmest-temperatures-climate-change|title=Earth is the warmest it's been in 120,000 years|year=2018|work=Mashable}} < /ref >
