 '''''Osteopilus''''' is a group of [[frog]]s in the family [[Hylidae]]. These frogs have a bony bumps on their skulls. This makes their heads like helmets.  These helmet-skulls are called casques.  The name  " osteopilus "  comes from the [[Greek language|Greek]] words ''[[wikt:osteo-|osteo-]]'' for  " bone "  and ''{{transl|grc|pilos}}'' ({{lang|grc|[[wikt:πῖλος|πῖλος]]}}) for  " felt cap. "  Together, ''Osteopilus'' means  " bone cap. " < ref > {{cite book|last=Dodd|first=C. Kenneth|title=Frogs of the United States and Canada|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2Hwfz9P9gS0C & pg=PR20|volume=1|date=2013|publisher=The Johns Hopkins University Press|isbn=978-1-4214-0633-6|page=20}} < /ref >   
 These frogs can be brown, brown-gray, or olive green in color with darker markings or patterns. The disks on their toes. They do not have much webbing on their front feet. The eyes and [[Tympanum (anatomy)|eardrum]] are large.  
 These frogs live in the [[Greater Antilles]] and the [[Bahamas]], but [[introduced species|human beings have brought]] ''[[Cuban tree frog|O. septentrionalis]]'' to the [[Lesser Antilles]], [[Hawaii]], and [[Florida]].
