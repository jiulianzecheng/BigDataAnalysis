'''Charlotte''' [shahr-l''uh''t] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of charlotte {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/charlotte|access-date=2023-03-16|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[female]] [[given name]] of [[French language|French]] origin. It is a [[female]] version of '''Charles'''. The moniker Charlotte has French roots and signifies  " free man "  or  " petite. "  Its etymology can be traced back to the Old German name Karl, which connotes  " man "  or  " husband. " < ref > {{Cite web|title=Charlotte name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Charlotte-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-27|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  This name was ranked #3 on the US Popular Female Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-27|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >  There are many famous people having the name, including [[Charlotte Church]], [[Charlotte Gainsbourg]] and [[Charlotte Arnold]]. The most famous fictional character with the name is the [[spider]] in ''[[Charlotte's Web]]''.
