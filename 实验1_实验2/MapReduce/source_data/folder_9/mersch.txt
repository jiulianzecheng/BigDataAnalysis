 '''Mersch''' ({{Lang-lb|Miersch}}) is a [[Communes of Luxembourg|commune]] and town in central [[Luxembourg]], capital of the [[Mersch (canton)|canton of Mersch]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Mersch is in the centre of the country, at the confluence of the [[Alzette]], [[Mamer River|Mamer]] and [[Eisch]] rivers. 
 
 The ''commune'' has an area of {{Convert|49.7|km2|sqmi|1|abbr=on }}. < ref name=stats / >  The [[altitude]] is {{Convert|208|-|428|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. The altitude at the [[Train station|railway station]] is {{Convert|220|m|ft|abbr=on }} high. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.statistiques.public.lu/stat/TableViewer/tableView.aspx?ReportId=142 & IF_Language=eng & MainTheme=1 & FldrName=1 |title=Altitudes of the different localities per cantons (in m) |publisher=Statistics Portal - Grand Duchy of Luxembourg |access-date= 2 June 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 Mersch is surrounded by ten ''communes'' of Luxembourg; nine of them are part of the [[Mersch (canton)|Mersch]] canton. Only the [[Kehlen]] ''commune'' is not part of the Mersch canton; it is part of the [[Capellen (canton)|Capellen]] canton.

 ==Population== 
 There were 9,195 persons living in the ''commune'' in 2017, < ref name=stats2 / >  for a [[population density]] of {{Pop density|9195|49.7|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km². 
 
 Other towns within the ''communes'' are Beringen, Berschbach, Moesdorf, Pettingen, Reckange, Rollingen and Schoenfels.

 ==Places of interest== 
 The '''Mersch castle''' is one of the [[castle]]s that are in the ''Valley of the Seven Castles''. < ref group= " N " > ''Valley of the Seven Castles'' is an informal name given to the valley of the [[Eisch]] river, in central Luxembourg. It is named after the group of seven castles along the river: Mersch Castle, Schoenfels Castle, Hollenfels Castle, Ansembourg Castle, New Castle of Ansembourg, Septfontaines Castle and Koerich Castle < /ref >  It is in the centre of the town and its history goes back to the 13th century. Today the castle houses the administrative offices of the commune. < ref > {{cite web| url=http://www.associationchateaux.lu/francais/mersch/histoire/index.html |title=Le château de Mersch |publisher=Association des Châteaux luxembourgeois |access-date= 2 June 2016 |language= fr }} < /ref > 
 
 Some {{convert|3|km|}} north of Mersch is the '''Pettingen Castle''', in the village of Pettingen; it is one of the best preserved fortified castles in the country. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.mersch.lu/3b%20Mersch%20-%20Castles%20Reckange,%20Pettingen,%20Schoenfels.pdf?FileID=publications%2F3b+mersch+-+castles+reckange%2C+pettingen%2C+schoenfels.pdf |title=Mersch: Schönfels, Pettingen, Recklingen |publisher=Administration communale de Mersch |access-date=2 June 2016 |archive-date=3 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303221221/http://www.mersch.lu/3b%20Mersch%20-%20Castles%20Reckange,%20Pettingen,%20Schoenfels.pdf?FileID=publications%2F3b+mersch+-+castles+reckange%2C+pettingen%2C+schoenfels.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >
