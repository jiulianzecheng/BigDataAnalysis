 '''Kaunas''', also known by several other names, is the second biggest city in [[Lithuania]] and used to be a [[wikt:temporary|temporary]] capital. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|title=Enciklopedija apie Lietuvą|publisher=Debesų Ganyklos|year=2015|isbn=978-609-8090-79-6|location=Kaunas|pages=86, 87}} < /ref >  Kaunas is found at the point where the two biggest Lithuanian [[river]]s, the [[Nieman River|Nemunas]] and the [[Neris]], meet. It is also near the [[Kaunas Lagoon]], the largest body of water in [[Lithuania]]. 
 
 == History == 
 In 1361, Kaunas was first mentioned in [[annals]] of the [[Teutonic Knights]].
