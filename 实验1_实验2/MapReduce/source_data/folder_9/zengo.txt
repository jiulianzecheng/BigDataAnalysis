 }}'''ZenGo''' is a cryptocurrency wallet mobile application. The company is headquartered in [[Tel Aviv]], [[Israel]]. It got popular during FTX collapse in 2022. < ref > {{Cite web|title=FTX Collapse Drives Record Downloads of ZenGo’s Non-Custodial Wallet|url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ftx-collapse-drives-record-downloads-145231808.html|access-date=2022-12-07|website=finance.yahoo.com|language=en-US}} < /ref >  It works  on multi-party computation architecture. 
 
 ZenGo was founded in June 2018 by Ouriel Ohayon, Tal Be’ery < ref > {{Cite web|title=What’s next in cybersecurity|url=https://www.technologyreview.com/2022/11/28/1063703/whats-next-in-cybersecurity/|access-date=2022-12-07|website=MIT Technology Review|language=en}} < /ref >  and Omer Shlomovits. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Orbach|first=Meir|date=2021-04-27|title=ZenGo closes $20 million series A to build unique retail crypto wallet|url=https://www.calcalistech.com/ctech/articles/0,7340,L-3906409,00.html|access-date=2022-12-07|website=CTECH - www.calcalistech.com}} < /ref >  ZenGo is part of Kzen Networks Ltd. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2019-06-05|title=New cryptocurrency wallet ZenGo offers keyless entry with distributed security|url=https://siliconangle.com/2019/06/05/new-cryptocurrency-wallet-zengo-offers-keyless-entry-distributed-security/|access-date=2022-12-07|website=SiliconANGLE|language=en-US}} < /ref >
