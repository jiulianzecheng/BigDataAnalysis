 '''Dublin''' ({{lang-ga|Baile Átha Cliath}}) is the [[capital city|capital]] of the [[Republic of Ireland]], and the biggest [[city]] on the [[island]] of [[Ireland]]. < ref name= " BBC.co " > {{cite news |url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/country_profiles/1038581.stm |title=BBC News - Ireland country profile |work=[[BBC News]] |publisher=[[British Broadcasting Corporation|BBC]] |location=[[London, England|London]] |accessdate=2 February 2011}} < /ref >  In 2011, there were over 1.1 million people living in the Greater Dublin Area. < ref > {{cite web |url= http://www.cso.ie/statistics/popofeachprovcountycity2006.htm |title=CSO: Statistics: Population of each Province, County and City 2006 |work=cso.ie |year=2011 |accessdate=2 February 2011}} < /ref > 
 
 Dublin was built by the [[Vikings]] upon the [[river Liffey]]. The river divides the city into two parts, [[North Dublin]] and [[South Dublin]]. 
 
 Many famous [[writer]]s lived in Dublin. [[Oscar Wilde]] and [[George Bernard Shaw]] were born in Dublin. [[James Joyce]] is probably Dublin's best known and most [[international]] writer. 
 
 Dublin is home to [[Ireland]]'s largest stadium for all sports, [[Croke Park]]. It can hold up to 85,000 people. Croke Park is the usual venue for all Ireland [[hurling]] and [[Gaelic football|football]] finals. The Aviva Stadium hosts rugby and soccer.
