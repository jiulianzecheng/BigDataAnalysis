 '''Soyarabai Bhonsle''' (''née'' '''Mohite''') (died 1681) was one of the wives of [[Shivaji]], the founder of [[Maratha Empire|Maratha Kingdom]] in western India. She was the younger sister of Maratha army chief [[Hambirrao Mohite]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=c42KEAAAQBAJ & dq=soyarabai & pg=PT223|title=The Legend: Shivaji Trilogy Book III|last1=Bhaskar|first1=Sita|last2=Roy|first2=Sabarna|publisher=Leadstart Publishing Pvt Ltd|isbn=978-93-5559-242-2|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Early life== 
 She was married to Shivaji at a very young age in 1642. The marriage took place when Shivaji visited his father [[Shahaji]] at [[Bangalore]] with his mother [[Jijabai]]. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=NANDAKUMAR|first=PREMA|title=English Writing by Indians: Another Rich Year |date=1968|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/24157102|journal=Indian Literature|volume=11|issue=4|pages=19–32|jstor=24157102 |issn=0019-5804}} < /ref >   After the death of Jijabai in 1674, Soyarabai became more important in Shivaji's family. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1SBuAAAAMAAJ & q=soyarabai|title=Shivaji Maharaja: Maratha Chhatrapati In Bharat-varsha : Shivaji : Hindu King in Islamic India "  by J.W. Laine/2003 : False and Fluid One|last=Darwatkar|first=Anant V.|date=2005|publisher=Shree Shambhu Prerana Pakashan|pages=95, 97, 180|language=en}} < /ref >  This was also true for Maratha court politics. < ref name= " Vaidya2000 " > {{cite book | author=Sushila Vaidya | title=Role of women in Maratha politics, 1620-1752 A.D. | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wVNuAAAAMAAJ | access-date=6 March 2012 | date=1 January 2000 | publisher=Sharada Pub. House | isbn=978-81-85616-67-4}} < /ref >  Soyarabai had two children with Shivaji, daughter Balibai and son Rajaram. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KWwyDQAAQBAJ & dq=soyarabai & pg=PT90|title=Chhatrapati Shivaji|last=Rana|first=Bhawan Singh|date=2016-10-05|publisher=Diamond Pocket Books Pvt Ltd|isbn=978-93-5083-007-9|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ROBKP5hRvM0C & dq=soyarabai & pg=PA1011|title=Shivaji, the Great Maratha|last=Sardesai|first=H.  ==Imprisonment== 
 Shivaji died in 1680 at his capital without naming any successor. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=G_m1AAAAIAAJ & q=soyarabai|title=The Struggle for Hindu Supremacy|last=Kulkarni|first=Shripad Dattatraya|date=1992|publisher=Shri Bhagavan Vedavyasa Itihasa Samshodhana Mandira (Bhishma)|isbn=978-81-900113-5-8|pages=125, 139|language=en}} < /ref >  In order to continue the empire, Soyarabai got her ten-year-old son, [[Rajaram Chhatrapati|Rajaram]] as the only legal successor to the throne. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Eh1BAAAAMAAJ & q=soyarabai|title=Maharani Tarabai of Kolhapur, C. 1675-1761 A.D.|last=Pāṭīla|first=Śālinī|date=1987|publisher=S. Chand  &  Company|isbn=978-81-219-0269-4|pages=14, 22, 32|language=en}} < /ref >  But her stepson and [[heir presumptive]] [[Sambhaji]], was able to stop this. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=43gBAAAAMAAJ & q=soyarabai|title=Chhatrapati Sambhaji|last=Gokhale|first=Kamal Shrikrishna|date=1978|publisher=Navakamal Publications|pages=22, 34, 35|language=en}} < /ref >  They got help from Soyrabai's brother, army chief [[Hambirrao Mohite]] and put Soyarabai and Rajaram in [[prison]]. They took the full power and Sambhaji became the ''Chhatrapati'' on 20 July 1680.  
 
 == Death == 
 After that, she tried all ways to de-throne him. < ref name= " Jaswant2005 " > {{cite book|author=Jaswant Lal Mehta|title=Advanced study in the history of modern India 1707-1813|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=d1wUgKKzawoC & pg=PA48|access-date=6 March 2012|date=1 January 2005|publisher=Sterling Publishers Pvt. Ltd|isbn=978-1-932705-54-6|page= 48 }} < /ref >  Soyarabai's followers tried to poison Sambhaji in August 1681, but he survived. When he learnt about the plot, he had no other choice but to kill Soyarabai with poison. Many plotters including Soyarabai's relatives of the [[Mohite]] family were also slaughtered or trampled by the elephants. < ref name= " Jaswant2005 "  / >
