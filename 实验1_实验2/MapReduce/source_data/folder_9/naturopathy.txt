 '''Naturopathy''' is a form of [[alternative medicine]]. It is based on the belief that a special [[energy]] called  " vital energy "  guides bodily functions such as [[metabolism]], [[Sexual reproduction|reproduction]] and [[growth]]. < ref > {{cite book|last1=Sarris|first1=Jerome|last2=Wardle|first2=Jon|title=Clinical Naturopathy: An evidence-based guide to practice|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lpEAJ9vjPyUC & pg=PT32|accessdate=2013-09-01|year=2010|publisher=Churchill Livingstone / Elsevier Health Sciences |location= Sydney |isbn=9780729579261|pages=32–36}} < /ref >  Naturopaths use various froms of treament, such as [[herbal medicine]] and [[Massage|massaging]] with a [[holism|holistic]] view. They generally avoid the use of [[surgery]] and [[chemical synthesis|synthetic]] [[Medication|drugs]]. In Naturopathy, evidence-based medicine is thought of as being only part of the evidence. < ref name= " Jagtenberg2006 " > {{cite journal | author1 = Jagtenberg, Tom | author2 = Evans, Sue | author3 = Grant, Airdre | author4 = Howden, Ian | date = April 2006 | title = Evidence-based medicine and naturopathy | journal = Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine | volume = 12 | issue = 3 | pages = 323–328 | doi = 10.1089/acm.2006.12.323 | pmid = 16646733 | url = https://works.bepress.com/airdre_grant/4/download/ }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  Taken as a whole, naturopathy is an [[Ideology|ideologic]] system, not a [[Science|scientific]] one. < ref name= " NCAHF_np " > {{cite web |url=http://www.ncahf.org/articles/j-n/naturo.html |title= NCAHF Fact Sheet on Naturopathy |accessdate=2009-04-17 |date=January 30, 2001 |origyear= copyright 1997 |last= Jarvis |first= William T. |publisher=[[National Council Against Health Fraud]] }} < /ref >
