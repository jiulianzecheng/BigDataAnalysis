[[Image:Enoshima in the Sagami province.jpg|thumb|right| " Enoshima in [[Sagami Province]] "  by [[Hokusai]], circa 1830]] 
 {{nihongo|'''Enoshima'''|江の島}} is a small [[island]] in [[Sagami Bay]] on the Pacific Coast of the Japanese island of Honshu.  It is part of [[Fujisawa, Kanagawa|Fujisawa]] in [[Kanagawa Prefecture]]. < ref name= " nussbaum181 " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA181 & dq=  " Ensohima " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 181. < /ref > 
 
 Enoshima has an area of about 0.2 & nbsp;km². It is linked to the mainland by a bridge. < ref name= " nussbaum181 " / > 
 
 The island is at the mouth of the [[Katase River]]. 
 
 In 1927, Enoshima was [[wikt:recognize|recognized]] as one of the [[100 Landscapes of Japan (Shōwa period)|100 Landscapes of Japan]] which best showed Japan and its culture in the [[Shōwa period]]. < ref > Japan Ministry of the Environment, [http://www.env.go.jp/nature/ari_kata/shiryou/031208-4-3.pdf 日本八景(昭和2年)の選定内容 {{in lang|ja}}]; retrieved 2012-3-30. < /ref > 
 
 ==History== 
 * '''1047''' (''[[Eishō]] 2''): ''[[Enoshima Engi]]'' was written by the [[Buddhism|Buddhist]] [[monk]] [[Kōkei (monk)|Kōkei]].  The work is a history of the shrines on the island. 
 * '''1182''' (''[[Juei]] 1''): [[Minamoto no Toritomo]] gave a statue to the [[Benzaiten]] shrine on the island. < ref name= " nussbaum181 " / >   
 * '''1325''' (''[[Shōchū]] 2''): Shōjōkō-ji was established.  It is the Buddhist temple on the island. < ref name= " nussbaum181 " / > 
 * '''1874''' (''[[Meiji period|Meiji]] 7''): [[Samuel Cocking]] developed botanical gardens. < ref name= " nussbaum181 " / > 
 * '''1964''' (''[[Shōwa period|Shōwa]] 39''): Sailing [[wikt:competition|competition]]s during the [[1964 Summer Olympics]] were scheduled near the island. < ref > [http://www.la84foundation.org/6oic/OfficialReports/1964/or1964v1pt1.pdf ''1964 Summer Olympics Official Report,'' Vol. 1, Part 1, p. 115] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100707162648/http://www.la84foundation.org/6oic/OfficialReports/1964/or1964v1pt1.pdf |date=2010-07-07 }}; retrieved 2012-3-4. < /ref >
