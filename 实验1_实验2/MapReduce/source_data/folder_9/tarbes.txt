 '''Tarbes''' ({{lang-oc|Tarba}}) is a [[Communes of France|commune]] in the [[Hautes-Pyrénées]] [[Departments of France|department]] in the [[Occitanie]] [[Regions of France|region]] in southwestern France. It is part of the historical region of [[Gascony]] and is the [[Capital (city)|capital]] since 1790 of the [[Hautes-Pyrénées]] department. 
 
 It is the second largest metropolitan area of the old [[Midi-Pyrénées]] region, after [[Toulouse]]. 
 
 ==Name== 
 The city is named for the first time during the 5th century like ''Civitas Turba ubi castrum Bigorra''. It is also an important city of the ''[[Novempopulania]]''. < ref group= " N " > ''Novempopulania'' ( " country of the nine peoples " ) was a [[Roman]] province, also called ''Aquitania Tertia''. < /ref >  [[Gregory of Tours]], in the 7th century, called the city as ''Talvam vicum''. 
 
 During the [[Middle Ages]], the city was named as ''Tarbe'' (1214), ''Tursa'', ''Tarvia'' (1284) and also ''Tarbia''. 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:Cathédrale Notre-Dame-de-la-Sède de Tarbes.JPG|thumbnail|left|Notre-Dame-de-la-Sède [[cathedral]], Tarbes (Hautes-Pyrénées, France).]] 
 A small town founded in the 3rd century B.C. became a [[Roman]] [[colony]] under the name of ''Tarba''. It was destroyed by the [[Vikings]] in 840 and rebuilt by the Bishop of Bigorre or Tarbes. 
 
 From the twelfth century on, Tarbes became the capital of the County of Bigorre. < ref group= " N " > In this case,  " county "  ({{lang-fr|Comté}}) means  " land under a [[count]] (the [[United Kingdom|British]] [[earl]]) " . < /ref >  During the [[Religion|religious]] [[war]]s, the [[cathedral]] was burnt in 1569 by [[Protestantism|Protestant]]s but the [[bishop]] had it rebuilt in 1652. 
 
 In 1790, Tarbes became the capital of the new department of Hautes-Pyrénées. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Tarbes is located in the southwest of France, at the foot of the [[Pyrenees]], in a fertile [[plain]] of the valley of the river [[Adour]], at {{Convert|20|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the northeast of [[Lourdes]], {{Convert|70|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the southeast of [[Auch]], {{Convert|144|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the east of [[Bayonne]] and {{Convert|155|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the southwest of [[Toulouse]]. 
 
 It has an area of {{Convert|15.33|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|304|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|311|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.map-france.com/Tarbes-65000/ |title=Tarbes |publisher=Map-France.com |language= |accessdate=20 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Two rivers flow through the city: the [[Adour]] by the east and the [[Échez]] by the west.

 ==Administration== 
 Tarbes is the prefecture of the Hautes-Pyrénées department since 1790. It is also the capital of three ''Cantons'': 
 # [[Canton of Tarbes-1|Tarbes-1]], formed with a part of Tarbes (13,514 inhabitants)  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Tarbes-2|Tarbes-2]], formed with a part of Tarbes (13,402 inhabitants)  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Tarbes-3|Tarbes-3]], formed with a part of Tarbes (13,984 habitants)  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Le Grand Tarbes'' ({{lang-fr|Communauté d'agglomération du Grand Tarbes}}).

 ==Transport== 
 The ''Gare de Tarbes'' [[railway station]] offers direct connections with [[Paris]], [[Bordeaux]], [[Toulouse]], [[Bayonne]] and other cities in the region. There is a [[motorway]] that connects Tarbes with Bayonne and Toulouse. Tarbes is also served by the small [[Tarbes-Lourdes-Pyrénées Airport]] situated 10 kilometers away of the city centre.
