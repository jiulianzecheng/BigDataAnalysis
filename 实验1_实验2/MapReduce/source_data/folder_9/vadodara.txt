'''Vadodara''' is the Second largest city in [[Gujarat]], [[India]]. It used to be called Baroda. It is the administrative capital or headquarters of Vadodara district in Gujarat. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.gujarattourism.com/hub/4|title=Tourism Hubs Details|website=www.gujarattourism.com|access-date=2019-02-15|archive-date=2019-03-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190326060900/https://www.gujarattourism.com/hub/4|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The city is on the banks of [[Vishwamitri River]]. The city is famous for [[Lakshmi Vilas Palace]], an excellent architectural palace. It is also the place for the [[Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda]] (Vadodara), the largest university in the state. 
 
 The city is an important place of industrial, educational and cultural centre in India. Vadodara has its own airport. The [[Vadodara Airport]] has good connectivity with [[Mumbai]], [[Chennai]], [[Delhi]], [[Kolkata]] and other main airports. An international terminal was built and was inaugurated by PM [[Narendra Modi]] in 2016. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.gensler.com/projects/vadodara-international-airport |title=Vadodara International Airport  & #124; Projects  & #124; Gensler |access-date=2016-12-16 |archive-date=2016-11-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161114004035/http://www.gensler.com/projects/vadodara-international-airport |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Vadodara Railway Station is one of the busiest railway station in Gujarat. It deals with about 150 trains daily. 
 
 As of the 2011 census, Vadodara metropolitan area had a population of 3,565,771. [[Gujarati language|Gujarati]], [[Urdu]], [[Marathi]] and [[Hindi]] are the widely spoken languages in [[Vadodara]]. Vadodara has the oldest cricket ground in Asia, the [[Moti Baug Stadium]]. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/india/content/ground/58516.html|title=Reliance Stadium - India - Cricket Grounds - ESPNcricinfo.com|website=Cricinfo}} < /ref >  Vadodara is the birthplace of cricketers like [[Yusuf Pathan]], [[Irfan Pathan]], [[Nayan Mongia]], [[Deepak Hooda]], [[Hardik Pandya]], [[Krunal Pandya]], [[Ambati Rayudu]] and [[Kiran More]].
