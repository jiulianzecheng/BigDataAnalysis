 '''''Turn''''' or '''''Turn: Washington's Spies''''' is an [[United States|American]] television show. It is set during the American [[Revolutionary War]].  It is about the spies and the beginning of [[espionage]].  It was on television for four seasons.  It started in 2014. 
 
 ==Development== 
 
 ''Turn'' is based on the [[Culper spy ring|Culper Ring]], real spies from the Revolutionary War. < ref name= " MV " > {{cite news|access-date=December 26, 2020|url=https://www.mountvernon.org/george-washington/the-revolutionary-war/spying-and-espionage/turn-washingtons-spies/|publisher=Mount Vernon|title=TURN: Washington's Spies}} < /ref >   The television show was based on the book ''[[Washington's Spies: The Story of America's First Spy Ring]]'', by [[Alexander Rose]].  The book is not fiction.  Rose is one of the show's producers and writers. < ref name=Virginia > {{cite web|title= " TURN "  The Untold Story of America's First Spy Ring on AMC|publisher=Virginia.org|access-date=December 26, 2020|url=https://www.virginia.org/turn/}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Story== 
 
 A cabbage farmer named Abraham Woodhull (Jaime Bell) and his friends become spies for [[George Washington]] (Ian  Kahn).  The story covers the British plan to use [[counterfeit]] money, the time [[Benedict Arnold]] changed sides, and the execution of [[John Andr√©]]. 
 
 ==Setting== 
 
 Much of the story takes place on [[Long Island]] in [[New York]].  It also shows other parts of the American colonies, including Manhattan, Philadelphia, and [[West Point]]. 
 
 ==Accuracy== 
 According to Long Island historian Margo Arceri, the show is  " about 70 percent inaccurate. " < ref name=NYT / >
