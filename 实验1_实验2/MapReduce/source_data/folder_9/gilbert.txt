'''Gilbert''' [ '''gil'''-bert ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of gilbert {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/gilbert|access-date=2023-03-30|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a British Isles male given name. It means  " the bright pledge " , or “the bright promise” in Germanic. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Gilbert name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Gilbert-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-30|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  Many famous people have carried the name, including [[W. S. Gilbert]]. There is also a Spanish form, '''Gilberto'''. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2018-08-22|title=Gilberto|url=https://www.sheknows.com/baby-names/name/gilberto/|access-date=2022-10-07|website=SheKnows|language=en-US}} < /ref >  It was used on a [[Hurricane Gilbert|powerful Atlantic hurricane]] that struck the Caribbean and [[Mexico]] in 1988. This is a unique name (not in the top 1000 newborn names in the US in 2021). < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-30|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref > 
 
 == People == 
 
 * [[W. S. Gilbert]], English librettist of the Gilbert  &  Sullivan duo 
 * [[Gilbert Thomas Carter]], Administrator of the Government of the Colony of Trinidad and Tobago, and its Dependencies in the absence of the Governor.  
 * [[Gilbert Etienne]], Swiss economist, author and professor  
 * [[Gilbert Gottfried]], American stand-up comedian (1955–2022)
