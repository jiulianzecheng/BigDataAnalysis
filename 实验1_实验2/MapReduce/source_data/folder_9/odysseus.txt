 In [[Greek mythology]], '''Odysseus''' was the great-grandson of the Greek god [[Hermes]]. He was the [[king]] of the [[island]] [[Ithaca]]. He was [[marriage|married]] to [[Penelope]]. Odysseus and Penelope had a son called [[Telemachos]]. Odysseus is a major character in [[Homer]]'s ''[[Iliad]]'' and ''[[Odyssey]]''. 
 
 Odysseus fought in the [[Trojan War]]. He [[invention|invented]] the [[Trojan Horse]], which helped the [[Greece|Greeks]] win the [[war]]. After the war, his [[adventure|adventurous]] journey home took 10 years. The story of that [[journey]] is told in the ''Odyssey''. Odysseus angered [[Poseidon]], the god of seas, when he half blinded his [[cyclops]] son [[Polyphemos]]. In anger, [[Poseidon]] stopped him from leaving the island. The [[Latin]] name for Odysseus is  " Ulysses " . 
 
 There have been many movies about Odysseus, because of his heroic and intelligent battle strategies.
