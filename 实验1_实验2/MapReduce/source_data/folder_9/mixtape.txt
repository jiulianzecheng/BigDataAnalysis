 A '''mixtape''' (or '''mixed-tape''') is any [[Compilation album|compilation]] of [[song]]s [[Sound recording|recorded]] onto a [[Audio cassette|cassette tape]] or other [[storage medium]]. < ref > {{cite web|title=Mixtape|url=http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/mixtape|publisher=Merriam Webster|accessdate=2012-04-04}} < /ref >  A mixtape usually shows the kind of music that the person who made it likes to listen to. It can range from a randomly selected list of favourite songs, to a [[Concept album|conceptual]] mix of songs of the same [[Musical genre|genre]] or [[mood]]. Making mixtapes may be considered an [[art]] form. < ref name= " pooter " > {{cite web| url=http://www.events-in-music.com/burnlist-mix-tapes.html| title=BurnLists: The Digital  " Mix Tape "  Comes Of Age| first=Michael| last=Resnick| year=2006| accessdate=2007-01-15| archive-date=2006-12-10| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061210001213/http://www.events-in-music.com/burnlist-mix-tapes.html| url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Since the 1990s, the term  " mixtape "  can also describe releases used to promote one or more new musical artists, or as a pre-release by more established artists to promote upcoming albums. < ref > {{cite book|last=Mitchell|first=Shamika Ann|title=Icons of Hip Hop|year=2007|publisher=Greenwood Press|location=Westport, CT [u.a.]|isbn=9780313339035|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WUJDr83m4n8C & pg=PA11|edition=1. publ.|editor=Mickey Hess|accessdate=14 June 2012|page=11}} < /ref >
