 '''Opisthokonts''' are a [[kingdom (biology)|superkingdom]] of the [[Eukaryota]], including the [[animal]]s and [[Fungus|fungi]]. It is a group of [[unikont]]s. 
 
 There are several classifications of the eukaryotes which are being discussed. Each of them divides the [[domain (biology)|domain]] Eukaryota into five groups, but there is disagreement on the details. < ref > Adl S.M. ''et al'' 2005. The new higher level classification of eukaryotes with emphasis on the taxonomy of protists. ''J. Eukaryot. Microbiol''. '''52''' (5): 399–451. [http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1550-7408.2005.00053.x/abstract;jsessionid=CA65FC38622C5A8459FDEE5BCA571F64.f03t01] PMID 16248873 < /ref >   
 
 The Opisthokonta is thought to be a [[monophyletic]] [[clade]], and therefore superior to the [[Unikonta]] as a taxonomic term. < ref > Huang, Jinling, Ying Xu, and Johann Peter Gogarten. The presence of a haloarchaeal type tyrosyl-tRNA synthetase marks the opisthokonts as monophyletic. ''Molecular biology and evolution'' '''22'''.11 (2005): 2142-2146. < /ref > < ref > Steenkamp, Emma T; Jane Wright, and Sandra L. Baldauf. The protistan origins of animals and fungi. ''Molecular biology and Evolution'' '''23'''.1 (2006): 93-106. < /ref > < ref > Torruella, Guifré, et al. Phylogenetic relationships within the Opisthokonta based on phylogenomic analyses of conserved single-copy protein domains. ''Molecular Biology and Evolution'' '''29'''.2 (2012): 531-544. < /ref >
