{{nihongo|'''Meireki'''|明暦}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,| ''nengō'',| lit.  " year name " }} after ''[[Jōō (Edo period)|Jōō]]'' and before ''[[Manji (era)|Manji]].''   This period started in April 1655 and ended in July 1658. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2002). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA625  " ''Meireki'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 625. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Sai of Japan|Go-Sai]]''-tennō''|後西院天皇}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA962  " Tennō, " ] pp. 962-963; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA413 ''Annales des empereurs du japon,'' p. 413.] < /ref > 
 
 
 ==Events of the ''Meireki'' era== 
 [[File:KoreanEmbassy1655KanoTounYasunobu.jpg|thumb|right|In the 1st year of ''Meireki'', a [[Joseon dynasty|Joseon]] diplomatic mission was received]] 
 [[File:Meireki fire.JPG|thumb|right|In ''Meireki'' 3, Edo was burned in a great fire]] 
 
 * '''1655''' (''Meireki 1''): The ambassador of Korea is received in Japan. < ref name= " titsingh413 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA413 p. 413]. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1655''' (''Meireki 1''): [[Emperor Go-Mizunoo]] went for the first time to location at base of Mount Hiei where [[Shugakuin Palace]] (''Shugakuin Rikyū'') would be built. < ref > [[Richard Ponsonby-Fane|Ponsonby-Fane, Richard]]. (1956). ''Kyoto: the Old Capital, 794-1869'', p. 318. < /ref > 
 
 * '''2-3 March 1657''' (''Meireki 3, 18th-19th days of the 1st month'') < !-- NengoCalc 明暦三年一月十八日  -- > : The city of [[Edo]] was [[wikt:destruction|destroyed]] by fire, also known as the [[Great Fire of Meireki]]. < ref name= " titsingh413 " / > 
 
 In 1656-1659, Go-Mizunoo built the Imperial villa and [[Japanese garden]]s at ''Shugakuin Rikyū''. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA890  " ''Shugaku-in Rikyū'' " ] at p. 890. < /ref >
