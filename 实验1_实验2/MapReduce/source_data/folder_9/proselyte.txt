'''Proselyte''', from [[Greek language|Greek]] ''{{lang|el|προσήλυτος}}/proselytos'', is used in the [[Septuagint]] for  " stranger " , i.e. a newcomer to [[Israel]]; < ref > {{bibleverse|1|Chronicles|22:2|NRSV}} < /ref >  a sojourner in the land, < ref > {{bibleverse||Exodus|12:48|}}; {{bibleverse-nb||Exodus|20:10|}}; {{bibleverse-nb||Exodus|22:21|}} < /ref >  and in the [[New Testament]] ([http://www.blueletterbible.org/cgi-bin/words.pl?word=4339 Strong's G4339] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090903184019/http://www.blueletterbible.org/cgi-bin/words.pl?word=4339 |date=2009-09-03 }}) for a [[Religious conversion#The convert.2Fproselyte|convert]] to [[Judaism]] from [[Ancient Greek religion|Paganism]]. It is a translation of the [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] word ''{{lang|he|גר}}/ger'' ([http://www.blueletterbible.org/cgi-bin/words.pl?word=01616 Strong's H1616] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090905203022/http://www.blueletterbible.org/cgi-bin/words.pl?word=01616 |date=2009-09-05 }}). 
 
 ==Two kinds of proselyte in Judaism== 
 There are two kinds of proselyte: 
 # ''[[Ger tzedek]]'' (''righteous proselytes'', ''proselytes of righteousness'', ''religious proselyte'', ''devout proselyte'') 
 # ''[[Ger toshav]]'' (''gate proselyte'', ''proselytes of the gate'', ''limited proselyte'', ''half-proselyte'') 
 A righteous proselyte < ref > {{bibleverse||Acts|13:43|}} < /ref >  was a Gentile who had converted to Judaism, was bound to all the doctrines and precepts of the Jewish economy, and was considered a full member of the Jewish people. 
 
 A gate proselyte < ref > {{bibleverse||Exodus|20:10|}} < /ref >  was a  " resident alien "  who lived in the [[Land of Israel]] and followed some of the customs. 
 
 ==Proselytes in early Christianity== 
 The  " religious proselytes "  spoken of in [[Early Christian]] writings were righteous proselytes, as distinguished from gate proselytes.
