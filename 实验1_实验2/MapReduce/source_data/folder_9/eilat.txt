 '''Eilat''' ({{Lang-he-n|אֵילַת}}), is the southernmost city in the [[Israel]]. It serves as a port and a major tourism city. The city lies in the south of the [[Arabah]], belongs to the [[Southern District (Israel)|southern district]], and has a population of about 53,600. 
 
 Eilat is at the southern end of the [[Negev]] Desert and the northern end of the [[Gulf of Aqaba|Gulf of Aqaba or Gulf of Eilat]] on the [[Red Sea]]. It is an important [[Harbour|port]] for Israel. Ships bring many products to Israel from the [[Far East]] and other places here. 
 
 Eilat also hosts an airport, called [[Eilat Airport]]. 
 
 Many tourists from Israel and other countries visit Eilat to enjoy the desert views, beaches and all-year warm weather. The temperature can easily reach more than 40 & nbsp;°C (104 & nbsp;°F) in summer and 21 & nbsp;°C (70 & nbsp;°F) in winter. The sea water is between 20 and 26 & nbsp;°C (68 and 79 & nbsp;°F). [[Coral reef]]s grow in the sea near Eilat and people can see them by [[scuba diving]] or from an underwater observatory ([[Coral World Underwater Observatory]]). [[Kings City]] is an amusement park in Eilat.
