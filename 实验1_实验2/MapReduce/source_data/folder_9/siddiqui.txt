 == Etymology == 
 The title ''Siddiqui'' ({{Lang-ar|صديقي}}) is an attributive form of the Arabic word Siddiq ({{Lang-ar|صديق}}). < ref > {{Cite web|title=Shaikh Siddiqui Home|url=http://www.shaikhsiddiqui.com/|access-date=2019-02-08|website=www.shaikhsiddiqui.com|archive-date=2018-10-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181018011548/http://www.shaikhsiddiqui.com/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " AbuBakr-IE2 " > {{cite encyclopedia|quote=His father was Abu Quhafa ..., and he is therefore sometimes known as Ibn Abi Quhafa. ... The names ''‘Abd Allah'' and ''‘Atiq'' ('freed slave') are attributed to him as well as Abu Bakr, but the relation of these names to one another and their original significance is not clear. ... He was later known by Sunni Muslims as ''al-Siddiq'', the truthful, the upright, or the one who counts true|encyclopedia=Encyclopedia of Islam|edition=2nd|title=Abu Bakr}} < /ref >  The name Siddiq means 'the truthful'. < ref name= " AbuBakr-IE2 "  / >  It was given to [[Abu Bakr]] by Muhammad < sup > saw < /sup > .
