 {{nihongo|'''Fukuoka'''|福岡市|Fukuoka-shi}} is a [[Japan]]ese [[city]] in [[Fukuoka Prefecture]] on the island of [[Kyushu]]. < ref name= " nussbaum " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2002). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA218  " Fukuoka " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', p. 218. < /ref >   It is the [[capital city]] of the prefecture. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA218  " ''Fukuoka-ken'' " ] at p. 218. < /ref >   
 
 It has been [[wikt:recognized|recognized]] as a [[city designated by government ordinance]] since 1992. < ref > Jacobs, A.J. [http://www.hindawi.com/journals/usr/2011/692764/   " Japan's Evolving Nested Municipal Hierarchy: The Race for Local Power in the 2000s, " ] ''Urban Studies Research,'' (2011); [http://www.hindawi.com/journals/usr/2011/692764/tab1/  Table 1  < nowiki > [PDF 6 of 14] < /nowiki > ]; [[doi:10.1155/2011/692764]]; retrieved 2012-12-7. < /ref > 
 
 == History== 
 A short distance is there from Dazaifu and the Fukuoka area to Hakata-wan (博多湾). Hakata-wan was the landing site of the [[Mongol invasions of Japan]] in 1274 and 1281. < ref name= " nussbaum " / > 
 
 Fukuoka is known for its [[association football|football]] team, [[Avispa Fukuoka]]; and it is also known for its [[baseball]] team, the [[Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks]].  
 
 ==Geography== 
 Fukuoka is on the northwest coast of Kyushu. < ref name= " nussbaum " / >  It is about the same distance from [[Tokyo]] as it is from [[Shanghai]] or [[Seoul]]. < ref > Williams, Ingrid K. [http://travel.nytimes.com/2012/08/26/travel/uncovering-the-secrets-of-fukuoka.html?_r=0   " Uncovering the Secrets of Fukuoka, " ] ''New York Times'' (US). August 24, 2012; retrieved 2012-8-29. < /ref > 
 
 Fukuoka City is divided into 7 [[Wards of Japan|ward]]s.
