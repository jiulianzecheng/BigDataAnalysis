 '''Alcuin of York''' ({{circa}} 735 â€“ 19 May 804), also known as '''Alcuinus of York''', was a scholar, teacher, and [[Theology|theologian]]. He was born in [[York]], [[Northumbria]] around the year 735. He was taught at the cathedral school there. He went on to become a prominent figure at the court of Charlemagne in Aachen, where he served as an advisor and educator. 
 
 Alcuin was known for his knowledge of literature, history, and religion. He was also known for his work to help people learn throughout the [[Francia|Frankish Empire]]. He was important in making the [[Palace of Aachen|Palace School at Aachen]]. This was a place of intellectual and cultural activity in the [[Carolingian dynasty|Carolingian period]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HSWpSJINLRwC & pg=PR3|title=The Catholic Encyclopedia: An International Work of Reference on the Constitution, Doctrine, Discipline, and History of the Catholic Church|last=Herbermann|first=Charles George|date=1907|publisher=Robert Appleton Company|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 Alcuin's work in [[education]] was very important. He thought teaching children to read and write was important. He helped make a standardized curriculum for schools in Frankish Empire. He also wrote many books on [[grammar]], rhetoric, and religion. These books were widely used in schools in the [[Middle Ages]]. 
 
 Alcuin also wrote many letters. Many of them have been kept. He wrote letters to [[Charlemagne]] and other leading figures of the time. < ref name= " EB " > {{Cite web|title=Alcuin  & #124; Anglo-Saxon scholar|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Alcuin|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|date=15 May 2023 }} < /ref > 
 
 Alcuin was a [[Christian]]. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=SYP8SAAACAAJ|title=2,000 Years of Christ's Power|last=Needham|first=N.|date=1998|publisher=Grace Publications Trust|isbn=978-0-946462-56-8|language=en}} < /ref >  He wrote many theological [[Treaty|treatises]] and poems. He helped make the [[liturgy]] of the [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholic Church]]. He was [[Saint|canonized]] as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church in the eleventh century. Alcuin died in [[Tours]], [[France]], in the year 804. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fWWcAAAAMAAJ|title=Alcuin, Friend of Charlemagne: His World and His Work|last=Duckett|first=Eleanor Shipley|date=1951|publisher=Macmillan|language=en}} < /ref >
