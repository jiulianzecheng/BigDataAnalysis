 '''Kokkola''' ({{lang-sv|Karleby}}, previously ''Gamlakarleby'') is a [[town]] in [[Central Ostrobothnia]], [[Finland]]. As of January 2014, over 47,000 people lived there. It covers an area of {{convert|1485,92|km2}}, not including the sea area. Nearby municipalities are [[Halsua]], [[Kalajoki]], [[Kannus]], [[Kaustinen]], [[Kruunupyy]], [[Lestijärvi]], [[Luoto]] and [[Toholampi]]. 
 
 The former municipalities of [[Lohtaja]], [[Kälviä]] and [[Ullava]] were merged with Kokkola in 2009. 
 
 Kokkola was established in 1620. The people speak two languages: Finnish and Swedish.
