 '''Tenskwatawa''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|t|ə|n|s|k|w|ɒ|t|ɒ|w|eɪ}} (also called '''Tenskatawa''', '''Tenskwatawah''', '''Tensquatawa''' or '''Lalawethika''') (January 1775 & nbsp;– November 1836) was a [[Native American]] religious and political leader of the [[Shawnee]]. He was known as '''the Prophet''' or '''the Shawnee Prophet'''.  
 
 He was a younger brother of [[Tecumseh]], a leader of the Shawnee. In his early years Tenskwatawa was given the name Lalawithika ( " He Makes a Loud Noise " ,  " The Noise Maker " , or  " The Rattle " ) by the [[Red Sticks]], a faction of the [[Muscogee]]. < ref name=Fowltown > {{cite book |title=Fowltown. Neamthla, Tutakosi Talofa  &  the first battle of the Seminoles Wars |publisher=Old Kitchen Books |year=2017 |isbn=9780692977880 |first1=Dale |last1=Cox |first2=Rachael |last2=Conrad}} < /ref > 
 
 He fought in the [[Battle of Tippecanoe]]. 
 
 ==Early life== 
 Lalawethika was born in [[Springfield, Ohio|Old Piqua]] to Puckenshinwa and Methoataske. Puckenshinwa died in the [[battle of Fallen Timbers]] and Methoataske left the family in 1779. Lalawethika was raised by his older sister Tecumpease. He lost his right eye in a hunting accident. Under the teachings of Penagashea he trained to be a [[medicine man]] but he failed and turned to alcoholism.
