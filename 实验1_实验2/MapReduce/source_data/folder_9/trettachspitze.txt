 The '''Trettachspitze''' is a [[mountain]] in the [[Allgäu Alps]] in Germany. It is made of [[Dolomite]] and is {{convert|2595|m}} high. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://peakware.com/peaks.php?pk=516|title=Peakware - Trettachspitze Overview}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It is one of the best-known mountains in the Allgäu Alps.  
 
 It is the only high rocky mountain in the Allgäu Alps that is completely in [[Germany]]. With the [[Mädelegabel]] ({{convert|2645|m|disp=comma|abbr=off}}) and the [[Hochfrottspitze]] ({{convert|2649|m|disp=comma|abbr=off}}) it forms the famous [[threesome]] of mountains on the main ridge of the Allgäu Alps. It is found north of the Mädelegabel. It was first climbed in 1855 by 3 brothers, Urban, Alois and Mathias Jochum.
