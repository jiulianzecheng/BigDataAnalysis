'''Osteopenia''', preferably known as  " low bone mass "  or  " low bone density " , is a condition in which [[bone mineral]] [[density]] is low. < ref > {{Cite web|last=staff|first=familydoctor org editorial|title=What Is Osteopenia?|url=https://familydoctor.org/condition/osteopenia/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=familydoctor.org|language=en-US}} < /ref >  This makes the bones weaker which cause a higher risk of fractures, and some people may go on to develop [[osteoporosis]]. < ref > {{cite journal|vauthors=Cranney A, Jamal SA, Tsang JF, Josse RG, Leslie WD|date=September 2007|title=Low bone mineral density and fracture burden in postmenopausal women|journal=CMAJ|volume=177|issue=6|pages=575–80|doi=10.1503/cmaj.070234|pmc=1963365|pmid=17846439}} < /ref >   In 2010, 43 million older adults in the [[United States]] had osteopenia. < ref > {{cite journal | vauthors = Wright NC, Looker AC, Saag KG, Curtis JR, Delzell ES, Randall S, Dawson-Hughes B | title = The recent prevalence of osteoporosis and low bone mass in the United States based on bone mineral density at the femoral neck or lumbar spine | journal = Journal of Bone and Mineral Research | volume = 29 | issue = 11 | pages = 2520–6 | date = November 2014 | pmid = 24771492 | pmc = 4757905 | doi = 10.1002/jbmr.2269 }} < /ref >
