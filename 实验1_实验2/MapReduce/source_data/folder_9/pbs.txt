 The '''Public Broadcasting Service''' '''(PBS)''' is a [[non-profit]] [[public broadcasting]] [[television|TV]] service with many member [[television|TV stations]] in the [[United States]]. It is based in [[Arlington County, Virginia|Arlington, Virginia.]] The equivalent service for radio in the United States is [[National Public Radio|National Public Radio (NPR)]]. The PBS stations will shut down in the future excluding [[New Jersey]].

 == Student Reporting Labs == 
 PBS Student Reporting Labs (SRL) is an educational program operated by the Public Broadcasting Service (PBS) in the United States. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2023-09-27|title=Student research project draws attention to price disparities in low-income neighborhoods|url=https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/student-research-project-draws-attention-to-price-disparities-in-low-income-neighborhoods|access-date=2023-11-10|website=PBS NewsHour|language=en-us}} < /ref >  It aims to engage and connect high school students with public media stations to develop journalism skills. Its Student Advisory Team (SAT) includes notable students across the United States, including [[Mark Leschinsky]] and others.
