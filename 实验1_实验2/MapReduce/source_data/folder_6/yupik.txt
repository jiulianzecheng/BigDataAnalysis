 The '''Yupik''' (in the [[Central Alaskan Yup'ik language]], '''Yup'ik''', plural '''Yupiit''') people are a group of [[Indigenous peoples|native]] people who mainly live in certain parts of [[Alaska]], somewhere near [[Fairbanks, Alaska|Fairbanks]].  The [[Spanish flu|1918 influenza pandemic]] killed many of the indigenous people in Alaska. < ref name= " Penn " > {{cite web|url=https://www.peninsulaclarion.com/news/100-years-ago-spanish-flu-devastated-alaska-native-villages/|publisher=Peninsula Clarion|accessdate=July 21, 2020|title=100 years ago, Spanish flu devastated Alaska Native villages|date=June 22, 2018|author=}} < /ref >  Many died during [[famine]]s and the culture suffered when the Soviet government made villagers move. < ref name= " Smith " > {{cite web|url=https://alaska.si.edu/culture_ne_siberian.asp?subculture=Yupik%20(Asiatic%20Eskimo) & continue=1|publisher=Smithsonian Alaska Native Collections|accessdate=July 21, 2020|title=Yupik (Asiatic Eskimo)|author1=Ludmila Ainana|author2=Tatiana Achirgina-Arsiak|author3=Tasian Tein|archive-date=June 10, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200610124703/https://alaska.si.edu/culture_ne_siberian.asp?subculture=Yupik%20%28Asiatic%20Eskimo%29 & continue=1|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  Some of the Yupi'k people's traditions are celebrating the dead. They celebrate by having ceremonies with a [[shaman]], where they connect with the dead. They also name the next child born after the most recent person who has died.
