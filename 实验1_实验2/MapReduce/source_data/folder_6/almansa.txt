 '''Almansa''' is a [[Spain|Spanish]] town and [[municipality]] in the [[province of Albacete]].  The municipality borders with [[Province of Alicante|Alicante]], [[Province of Valencia|Valencia]] and [[Province of Murcia|Murcia]]. Almansa is famous for its ''[[Moros y Cristianos]]'' [[festival]] celebrated from the 1st to the 6th of May. 
 
 Almansa is built at the foot of a white [[limestone]] [[cliff]]. It has a [[Moor]]ish castle at the top. It is in the middle of a fertile and [[irrigation|irrigated]] [[plain]]. About {{convert|1|mi|km}} south of the town centre there is an [[obelisk]] to remind people of the [[Battle of Almansa]]. The battle was fought there on the 25 April 1707 during the [[War of Spanish Succession]]. The Duke of Berwick who the son of [[James II of England|James II]] defeated the allied British, Portuguese and Spanish troops. < ref > Encyclopædia Britannica Vol1 (11th ed.). Cambridge University Press. Chisholm, Hugh, ed. (1911).  " Almansa " . < /ref > 
 
 The [[Sierra del Mugrón]] is located in the Almansa city limits. 
 
 ==Main sights== 
 
 The main sightseeing attraction is the 14th-century [[Castle of Almansa]]. 
 
 There are other important monuments, such as: 
 *Assumption Church (16th-19th century) 
 *Palace of ''Los Condes de Cirat'' (16th century), today the Town  Hall 
 *Church of the ''Agustinas'' Convent (18th century) 
 *The Convent of ''San Francisco'' (17th century) 
 *Clock Tower (1780) 
 
 8 & nbsp;km from the city is the  [[reservoir]] of Almansa,  built in 1584. This reservoir is the oldest one in Europe. 12 & nbsp;km from the city is the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Belen (17th century).
