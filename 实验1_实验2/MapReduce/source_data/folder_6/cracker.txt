 A '''cracker''' is a thin and crispy piece of [[bake]]d [[bread]].  Crackers can be eaten by themselves, but they can also be eaten with things on them called toppings.  Common toppings include [[cheese]], [[peanut butter]], and sliced [[meat]]s.  Crackers are most often eaten as a [[snack]], or crumbled into [[soup]]. 
 {{wiktionary|cracker}} 
 
 ==History== 
 It was 1792 when Theodore Pearson of Newburyport, Massachusetts, made a cracker-like bread that was made from only water and flour which he called  " Pearson's Pilot Bread. "  This was the first cracker bakery in the United States, and made crackers for more than a century.
