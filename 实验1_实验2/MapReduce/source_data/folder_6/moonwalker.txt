 '''''Moonwalker''''', also known as '''''Michael Jackson: Moonwalker''''', is an [[United States|American]]-[[United Kingdom|British]]-[[Canada|Canadian]] [[movie]]. It was released in 1988 in theatres in [[Europe]] and [[South America]]. The movie is a collection of eight short films about [[Michael Jackson]]. It is 93 minutes long. It made US$67,000,000 at [[box offices]]. On January 10, 1989, it was released on video on the United States and Canada. The video had sold more than 800,000 copies in the U.S. by April 17, 1989. < ref > {{Cite web |url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=1 & table=SEARCH_RESULTS & action= & title=moonwalker & artist=michael%20jackson & startMonth=1 & endMonth=1 & startYear=1958 & endYear=2009 & sort=Artist & perPage=25 |title=RIAA - Gold  &  Platinum Searchable Database - September 24, 2015 |website=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] |access-date=2017-08-31 |archive-date=2015-09-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924153725/http://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=1 & table=SEARCH_RESULTS & action= & title=moonwalker & artist=michael%20jackson & startMonth=1 & endMonth=1 & startYear=1958 & endYear=2009 & sort=Artist & perPage=25 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The movie is named after a [[dance move]] that Jackson did called the [[moonwalk]]. [[John Lennon]]'s son [[Sean Lennon|Sean]] is in the movie. Jackson executive produced ''Moonwalker''. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0001391/|title=Michael Jackson|website=IMDb}} < /ref > 
 [[Brandon Adams|Brandon Quentin Adams]] won the 1989 [[Young Artist Award]] for Best Young Actor/Actress in a Film Made for Video for his role as Zeke, a child version of Jackson, in the movie. 
 In 1989, [[Blackthorne Publishing]] made the movie into a [[comic book]] called ''Moonwalker 3-D''.
