'''Truthout''' is a [[Politics|political]] [[website]] aimed at providing another choice to mainstream news sources. It was started after the [[U.S. presidential election, 2000|2000 presidential election]] with the idea of, in its own words,  " hoping to reach a few people, have some small [[wikt:impact|impact]] on the dialogue, and maybe try to restore a little [[wikt:integrity|integrity]]. " < ref name=About > {{cite web|url=https://truthout.org/about/|title=About Truthout|website=Truthout}} < /ref >   
 
 Some of its [[author]]s include [[William Rivers Pitt]], [[Jason Leopold]], [[Scott Galindez]], [[David Bacon]], [[Dean Baker]], [[Tom Engelhardt]], [[William Fisher]], [[Dahr Jamail]], [[Ray McGovern]], [[J. Sri Raman]], [[Norman Solomon]], [[David Swanson]] and [[James Zogby]]. The website has reported a lot on the anti-war movement and helped to make [[Cindy Sheehan]] better known by using many of her writings. Attention is also given to problems including the [[environment]], work (and work [[Ethics|ethic]]), women,  health, and [[Suffrage|voter]] rights. Its articles are now carried by press release information centers like [http://www.wn.com/ World News], [http://www.scoop.co.nz/  Scoop Newzealand] and [http://www.californianews.com/ California News] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080828040248/http://www.californianews.com/ |date=2008-08-28 }}.   
 
 Truthout is a [[501(c)(3)]] [[non-profit organization]] (a [[Charitable organization|charity]]). < ref name=About/ >  In 2012, the company took in over $1,900,000. < ref > http://www.guidestar.org/pqShowGsReport.do?partner=guidestar & npoId=100724559{{Dead link|date=October 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 The website also had a [[blog]] where visitors can talk about various issues or specific articles, which was taken down a short time ago.
