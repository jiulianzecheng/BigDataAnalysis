 The '''Unisphere''' is a {{convert|140|ft|m|adj=mid|-tall}} [[stainless steel]] [[globe]] in [[Queens]], [[New York City]]. It is in [[Flushing Meadows-Corona Park]]. It was designed by [[landscape]] [[architect]] [[Gilmore D. Clarke]]. It is a symbol of the [[borough]] of Queens. The globe weighs {{convert|700,000|lb}}. This makes it the world's largest globe. There are fountains and a [[reflecting pool]] around the Unisphere. 
 
 The Unisphere was built for the [[World's Fair|1964-1965 New York World's Fair]]. < ref name= " www.placematters.net " > [http://www.placematters.net/node/1564   " Unisphere: Built by US Steel as the symbol of the 1964-5 New York World's Fair " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924073917/http://www.placematters.net/node/1564 |date=2015-09-24 }}, ''Place Matters'', 5 February 2010 < /ref >  The Unisphere is designed to show countries around the world depending on each other. It stayed at the park after the World's Fair. The Unisphere was cleaned from 1993 to 1994. 
 
 ==History== 
 
 [[Flushing Meadows–Corona Park]] used to be a [[landfill]] in [[Queens]]. The landfill was turned into the [[1939 New York World's Fair|1939/1940 New York World's Fair]]. After the fair, it was used as a park. < ref > {{cite news|url=http://fultonhistory.com/highlighter/highlight-for-xml?altUrl=http%3A%2F%2Ffultonhistory.com%2FNewspaper%252018%2FNew%2520York%2520NY%2520Sun%2FNew%2520York%2520NY%2520Sun%25201940%2FNew%2520York%2520NY%2520Sun%25201940%2520a%2520-%25204231.pdf|title=Ready to Turn Fair Into Park; Moses Tells Mayor His Plans Are Set|date=August 15, 1940|work=[[The Sun (New York)]]|accessdate=March 27, 2017|agency=[[Fultonhistory.com]]|page=14}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite news|url=http://fultonhistory.com/highlighter/highlight-for-xml?altUrl=http%3A%2F%2Ffultonhistory.com%2FNewspaper%252014%2FLong%2520Island%2520City%2520NY%2520Star%2520Journal%2FLong%2520Island%2520City%2520NY%2520Star%2520Journal%25201949%2FLong%2520Island%2520%2520City%2520NY%2520Star%2520Journal%25201949%2520-%25204917.pdf|title=Flushing Meadow|date=July 13, 1949|work=Long Island Star-Journal|accessdate=March 27, 2017|agency=[[Fultonhistory.com]]|page=4}} < /ref >  The [[1964 New York World's Fair]] also happened at Flushing Meadows. < ref > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ruI0AQAAMAAJ & pg=SA1-PA10|title=La Guardia International Airport and John F. Kennedy International Airport, Port Authority of New York and New Jersey Airport Access Program, Automated Guideway Transit System (NY, NJ): Environmental Impact Statement|last=|first=|date=June 1994|publisher=[[Port Authority of New York and New Jersey]], [[United States Department of Transportation]], [[Federal Aviation Administration]], [[New York State Department of Transportation]]|isbn=|location=|pages=1.11}} < /ref >  [[Gilmore David Clarke]] and Michael Rapuano designed the park for 1964 Fair. < ref > {{cite web|last=|first=|date=May 3, 2015|title=Flushing Meadows-Corona Park: Historic Preservation Studio|url=https://issuu.com/barrettreiter/docs/fmcp_hpstudioii2015_printedition_re|archive-url=|archive-date=|website=|publisher=[[Columbia Graduate School of Architecture, Planning and Preservation]]|page=16|accessdate=March 31, 2017}} < /ref > < ref name= " NYCL p. 3 " > {{harvnb|ps=.|Landmarks Preservation Commission|1995|page=3}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Robert Moses]] ran the 1964 World's Fair. He announced the Unisphere in February 1961. < ref > {{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1961/02/15/archives/a-120foot-steel-unisphere-will-be-symbol-of-the-64-fair.html|title=A 120-Foot Steel 'Unisphere' Will Be Symbol of the '64 Fair|date=February 15, 1961|work=The New York Times|access-date=November 4, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  At first, Clarke designed the Unisphere as an aluminum structure with metal continents and islands. < ref name= " NYCL p. 3 "  / > < ref name= " AR 1964 "  / >  Designers at [[Peter Muller-Munk Associates]] added structural steel. < ref > {{Cite news|last=Kracklauer|first=Beth|date=November 19, 2015|title=Mass-Market Masterpieces: The Designs of Peter Muller-Munk|language=en-US|work=Wall Street Journal|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/mass-market-masterpieces-the-designs-of-peter-muller-munk-1447953339|access-date=July 27, 2020|issn=0099-9660}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite news|last=Gopnik|first=Blake|date=July 9, 2015|title=Carnegie Museum to Open a Survey of the Designer Peter Muller-Munk|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/07/10/arts/design/carnegie-museum-to-open-a-survey-of-the-designer-peter-muller-munk.html|access-date=July 27, 2020|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  The last design was a lot like the first. The last design had fountains surrounding the Unisphere. < ref name= " NYCL p. 3 "  / > 
 
 The Unisphere was built by the [[American Bridge Company]]. The American Bridge Company was part of another company called [[US Steel]]. < ref name= " NYCL p. 3 "  / > < ref name= " AR 1964 " > {{cite journal|url=https://usmodernist.org/AR/AR-1964-07.pdf|first=Mildred F.|last=Schmertz|title=Architecture at the New York World's Fair|journal=Architectural Record|volume=136|date=July 1964|page=150}} < /ref >  The Unisphere was built in 110 days. < ref > {{Cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/new-york-city-1960s-gallery-1.1038782?pmSlide=1.1038739|title=Unisphere, 1960 - Photos - New York: The 1960s|newspaper=New York Daily News|access-date=November 13, 2016}} < /ref >  The last piece of land was put on the Unishphere on August 13, 1963. < ref > {{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1963/08/13/archives/section-added-to-unisphere.html|title=Section Added to Unisphere|date=August 13, 1963|work=The New York Times|access-date=November 4, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  At night, the Unisphere was lit up to show sunrise moving across the globe. There were lights showing the capitals of countries. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 " > {{harvnb|ps=.|Landmarks Preservation Commission|1995|page=4}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1966, US Steel gave $100,000 so the Unisphere could stay at the park. < ref > {{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1966/03/21/archives/us-steel-donates-a-fund-to-maintain-unisphere-at-fair.html|title=U.S. Steel Donates A Fund to Maintain Unisphere at Fair|date=March 21, 1966|work=The New York Times|access-date=November 4, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  In 1967, the park reopened. < ref > {{Cite news|last=Schumach|first=Murray|date=June 4, 1967|title=Moses Gives City Fair Site as Park; Flushing Meadows in Queens Becomes the 2d Biggest Recreation Area Here|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1967/06/04/archives/moses-gives-city-fair-site-as-park-flushing-meadows-in-queens.html|access-date=July 27, 2020|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  The Unisphere became dirty afterward. People wrote graffiti in the pools. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > 
 
 In 1989, the [[New York City Department of Parks and Recreation]] said it would clean the Unisphere. From 1993 to 1994, the Unisphere was fixed and cleaned. The fountains were also turned back on. < ref name= " Huang 1994 " > {{cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/38447214/|title=Let us spray for Unisphere|last=Huang|first=Vivian|date=June 1, 1994|work=New York Daily News|access-date=November 4, 2019|page=293|via=newspapers.com {{open access}}}} < /ref > < ref name= " NYCL p. 5 " > {{harvnb|ps=.|Landmarks Preservation Commission|1995|page=5}} < /ref > < ref name= " Muschamp 1994 " > {{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1994/09/02/arts/summer-s-last-hurrah-the-final-fling-before-the-fall-recircling-the-globe.html|title=Summer's Last Hurrah: The Final Fling Before the Fall; Recircling the Globe|last=Muschamp|first=Herbert|date=September 2, 1994|work=The New York Times|access-date=November 4, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}} < /ref >  It became an official city landmark in May 1995. < ref > {{cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/38447505/|title=Unisphere nears landmark status|last=Avashti|first=Surabhi|date=May 15, 1995|work=New York Daily News|access-date=November 4, 2019|page=292|via=newspapers.com {{open access}}}} < /ref > < ref name= " enc-nyc " > {{Cite enc-nyc2}} < /ref >  The Unisphere's fountain was cleaned again in 2010. < ref > {{cite news |title=Fountain's Return |first=Nicholas |last=Hirshon |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/queens/2010/08/13/2010-08-13_fountains_return_after_repairs_water_flows_at_unisphere.html |newspaper=New York Daily News |date=August 13, 2010 |accessdate=September 3, 2010 |archive-date=August 25, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100825002454/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/queens/2010/08/13/2010-08-13_fountains_return_after_repairs_water_flows_at_unisphere.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|last=Carlson|first=Jen|date=August 14, 2010|title=Finally: Unisphere Fountain Restored!|url=http://gothamist.com/arts-entertainment/finally-unisphere-fountain-restored|access-date=July 27, 2020|website=Gothamist|language=en|archive-date=July 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200727180423/https://gothamist.com/arts-entertainment/finally-unisphere-fountain-restored|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " enc-nyc "  / >

 The Unisphere is the world's largest globe. It is {{convert|120|ft|0}} wide and {{convert|140|ft|0}} tall. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 " / >  The globe weighs {{convert|700,000|lb}}. Its base weighs {{convert|200,000|lb|0}}. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > < ref name= " Muschamp 1994 "  / >  The Unisphere is made of [[stainless steel]]. The continents and islands on the Unisphere were made by the [[Rigidized Metals Corporation]]. < ref > {{cite web | last=Brake | first=Alan G. | title=Focus on Fabrication >  Rigidized Metals | website=The Architect's Newspaper | date=February 26, 2014 | url=http://www.archpaper.com/news/articles.asp?id=6657 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140226052126/http://www.archpaper.com/news/articles.asp?id=6657 | archive-date=February 26, 2014 | url-status=dead | access-date=July 27, 2020}} < /ref >  The Unisphere is tilted 23.5 degrees. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > 
 
 There are three rings around the Unisphere. They show the [[orbit]]s of [[Yuri Gagarin]], [[John Glenn]], and the [[Telstar]] satellite around the Earth. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > < ref name= " Short 2016 "  / >  There were supposed to be more rings, but the designers could not do this. < ref name= " Short 2016 " > {{cite book|last1=Short|first1=Daniel|title=Unisphere: Symbol of the 1964-1965 New York World's Fair|date=2016|publisher=Arethusa Press|isbn=978-0-692-50867-1|edition=1st}} < /ref >  The rings are held up with aircraft cable. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / >  The Unisphere's continents and islands are made of many layers of metal sheets. The Unisphere has many [[latitude]] and [[longitude]] bars of different sizes. This makes the structure stable. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > 
 
 There is a [[reflecting pool]] around the Unisphere. It measures {{convert|310|ft|m}} across. There are 48 fountains in the pool. These fountains are supposed to make the Unisphere look like it is floating in space. There are also [[memorial plaque|plaque]]s around the pool. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > 
 
 The Unisphere has a concrete [[foundation (engineering)|foundation]]. < ref name= " NYCL p. 4 "  / > < ref name= " AR 1964 "  / >  The ground under the Unisphere used to be a [[marsh]]. There are 528 wooden rods under the Unisphere. < ref > {{cite book | author=American Wood Preservers Institute | author2=American Wood-Preservers' Association | title=Wood Preserving News | publisher=American Wood Preservers Institute. | issue=v. 43-44 | year=1965 | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HKDmAAAAMAAJ | access-date=July 27, 2020 | page=16}} < /ref >
