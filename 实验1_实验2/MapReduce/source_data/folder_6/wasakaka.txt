 '''Wasakaka''' (also spelled '''''Guasacaca''''') is a sauce commonly eaten in [[Venezuela]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=VenezuelaTuya|url=http://www.venezuelatuya.com/cocina/guasacaca.htm|url-status=live|access-date=13 December 2016|website=Venezuela Tuya|archive-date=31 December 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161231062216/http://www.venezuelatuya.com/cocina/guasacaca.htm}} < /ref >  It is made by mixing [[avocado]], [[parsley]], [[bell pepper]], [[coriander]], [[onion]], [[garlic]], [[salt]], [[black pepper]], and other seasonings. It is commonly eaten with [[Grill (cooking)|grilled]] meats, cakes, [[Empanada|empanadas]], [[Tequeño|tequeños]], appetizers, and other [[Venezuelan food|traditional dishes]]. < ref > {{Cite web|title=La guasacaca|url=https://cocineroaficionado.wordpress.com/tag/la-mejor-receta-de-guasacaca/|access-date=2021-08-04|archive-date=2021-08-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210804210054/https://cocineroaficionado.wordpress.com/tag/la-mejor-receta-de-guasacaca/|url-status=live}} < /ref >  It is similar to [[guacamole]], but with a stronger flavor. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=000gDgAAQBAJ & dq=Guasacaca & pg=PA50|title=MasterChef: Street Food of the World|last=Taylor|first=Genevieve|date=2017-03-09|publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing|isbn=978-1-4729-4620-1|language=en|access-date=2022-02-25|archive-date=2022-05-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220528145625/https://books.google.com/books?id=000gDgAAQBAJ & dq=Guasacaca & pg=PA50|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 == Variations == 
 The ingredients can be either chopped up or [[Blender|blended]]. Some fats like [[mayonnaise]] or extra virgin [[olive oil]] < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Rlp7DQAAQBAJ & dq=Guasacaca & pg=PT160|title=Latin American Cooking Across the U.S.A.|last1=Novas|first1=Himilce|last2=Silva|first2=Rosemary|date=2016-12-13|publisher=Knopf Doubleday Publishing Group|isbn=978-1-5247-3241-7|language=en|access-date=2022-02-25|archive-date=2022-05-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220528145625/https://books.google.com/books?id=Rlp7DQAAQBAJ & dq=Guasacaca & pg=PT160|url-status=live}} < /ref >  can also be added to the mix.
