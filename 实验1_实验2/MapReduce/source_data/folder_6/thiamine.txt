 '''Thiamine''' (or thiamin) is '''vitamin B < sub > 1 < /sub > '''. It is a water-soluble [[vitamin]] of the [[B vitamin complex|B complex]]. Its [[phosphate]] derivatives take part in many cellular processes. Thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP) is a [[coenzyme]] in the [[catabolism]] of [[sugars]] and [[amino acids]].  
 
 also it can be found in Food and Manufactured [[Dietary supplement|Dietary supplements]] also in [[Medication|Medications]] < ref name= " NIH2016 " > {{cite web|title=Thiamin Fact Sheets for Health Professionals|url=https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Thiamin-HealthProfessional/|website=Office of Dietary Supplements|access-date=30 December 2016|date=11 February 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161230231520/https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Thiamin-HealthProfessional/|archive-date=30 December 2016}} < /ref > < ref name= " medline " > {{cite web|title=Thiamine: MedlinePlus Drug Information|url=https://medlineplus.gov/druginfo/meds/a682586.html|website=medlineplus.gov|access-date=30 April 2018}} < /ref >  Foods that provide Thiamine include [[Whole grain|whole grains]], [[Legume]] < ref name= " NIH2016 "  / >   
 
 Lack of thiamine causes [[beriberi]].
