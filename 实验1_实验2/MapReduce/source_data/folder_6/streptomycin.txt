 == Uses == 
 Streptomycin has been classified by the [[World Health Organization|World Health Organisation]] as a critically important antimicrobial for human medicine. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://apps.who.int/iris/handle/10665/312266|title=Critically important antimicrobials for human medicine|last=World Health Organization|date=2019|publisher=World Health Organization|isbn=978-92-4-151552-8|edition=6th rev.|location=Geneva|hdl=10665/312266 |language=en}} < /ref >  The most common use of streptomycin today is the treatment of pulmonary [[tuberculosis]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  The antibiotic has a variety of antibacterial activity which is beneficial for the treatment of a wide range of infections. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Streptomycin - an overview {{!}} ScienceDirect Topics|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/chemistry/streptomycin#:~:text=Streptomycin%20has%20a%20broad%20spectrum,fever,%20plague,%20and%20others.|access-date=2023-07-08|website=www.sciencedirect.com}} < /ref >  Other infections treated using streptomycin include [[pneumonia]], Mycobacterium avium complex-MAC, E. coli influenza, [[Bubonic plague|plague]], and [[tularemia]]. 
 
 == Side effects == 
 Common side effects of the use of streptomycin coincide with those seen with the use of other aminoglycoside antibiotics. These include both kidney and ear toxicity, alongside chronic kidney disease. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Prayle|first1=A.|last2=Watson|first2=A.|last3=Fortnum|first3=H.|last4=Smyth|first4=A.|date=2010-07-01|title=Side effects of aminoglycosides on the kidney, ear and balance in cystic fibrosis|journal=Thorax|language=en|volume=65|issue=7|pages=654–658|doi=10.1136/thx.2009.131532|issn=0040-6376|pmc=2921289|pmid=20627927}} < /ref >  Other dangerous side effects of the use of this drug also include vestibular and auditory system disorders. < ref > {{Cite journal|last=Molitor|first=Hans|date=1947-04-01|title=Pharmacology of Streptomycin|journal=Bull N Y Acad Med.|volume=23|issue=4 |pages=196–206|pmid=19312525 |pmc=1871547 }} < /ref > 
 
 == Mechanism of action == 
 The use of streptomycin involves the disruption of [[Protein biosynthesis|protein synthesis]] within the [[ribosome]], preventing the replication of bacteria. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  The antibiotic creates ionic bonds with the negatively charged sections of the bacterial [[cell membrane]] to access the inner cytoplasmic membrane. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Rocha|first1=Deisy M.
