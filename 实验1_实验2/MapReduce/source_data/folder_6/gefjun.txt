 '''Gefjun''' (or '''Gefjon''', '''Gefion''') is one of the [[Æsir]] in [[Norse mythology]]. This name could be from [[Old Norse]] ''geð fiá'' meaning  " chaste " < ref name= " nordiskgefjun " > The article ''[http://runeberg.org/nfbi/0440.html Gefjun]'' in ''[[Nordisk familjebok]]'' (1908). < /ref >  is one of the [[Asynjur]] in [[Norse mythology]]. < ref name= " nordiskgefjun " / >  She appears only a few times in surviving sources. Medieval sources talk of her mainly as a goddess of [[chastity]]. However, modern scholarship suggests that she may originally have been a [[fertility goddess]] connected with [[ritual]] plowing, < ref name= " nationalgefjun " > The article ''Gefjon'' in ''[[Nationalencyklopedin]]'' (1992). < /ref >  and even that she was originally the same [[fertility]] goddess as [[Freyja]]. < ref name= " Shön1 " > Schön, Ebbe. (2004). ''Asa-Tors hammare, Gudar och jättar i tro och tradition''. Fält  &  Hässler, Värnamo. {{ISBN|91-89660-41-2}} p. 136. < /ref > 
 
 It has also been suggested that she is the origin of [[Grendel's mother]] who appears in the epic poem ''[[Beowulf]]''. < !-- ref name= " Battaglia415 " / -- >
