 '''Cyprian''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|ɪ|p|r|i|ən}} {{respell|SIP-ree-ən}}; {{lang-la|Thaschus Caecilius Cyprianus}}; {{circa}} 210 – September 14, 258 AD < ref name= " LotH " > ''The Liturgy of the Hours according to the Roman Rite: Vol. IV.'' New York: Catholic Book Publishing Company, 1975. p. 1406. < /ref > ) was [[bishop]] of [[Carthage]]. He was an important early [[Christianity|Christian]] writer. Cyprian was a bishop of Carthage of Berber descent, many of whose Latin works are extant. He is recognized as a saint in the Western and Eastern churches. He was probably born at the beginning of the [[3rd century]] in [[North Africa during the Classical Period|North Africa]], perhaps at Carthage. At Carthage, he learned about classical ([[Paganism]]). After becoming a Christian, he became a bishop around the year [[249]]. He died a [[martyr]] at Carthage.
