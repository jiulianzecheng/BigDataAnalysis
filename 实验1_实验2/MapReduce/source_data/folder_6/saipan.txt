 '''Saipan''' ([[International Phonetic Alphabet|IPA]]: {{IPA|[saɪ'pæn], [saɪ'pɑn], or ['saɪpæn]}} in English) is the largest [[island]] and [[Capital (city)|capital]] of the [[United States]] [[Northern Mariana Islands|Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands]] (CNMI), a chain of 15 [[tropical]] islands which are part of the [[Marianas]] [[archipelago]] in the western [[Pacific Ocean]] (15°10’51”N, 145°45’21”E) with a total area of [[1 E8 m²|115.39 km² (44.55 sq mi)]]. At the [[United States 2000 census|2000 census]], there were more than 62,392 people. < ref > [https://www.census.gov/Press-Release/www/2001/cb01cn173.html Census Bureau Releases, Census 2000 Population Counts for the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands], [[July 3]], [[2001]]. < /ref > 
 
 Saipan is at [[latitude]] of 15.25° north and [[longitude]] of 145.75° east, about 200 & nbsp;km (120 & nbsp;mi) north of [[Guam]]. Saipan is about 20 & nbsp;km (12.5 & nbsp;mi) long and 9 & nbsp;km (5.5 & nbsp;mi) wide. It is a popular tourist destination in the Pacific.
