'''Yahrzeit''' ([[Yiddish language|Yiddish]], from [[German language|German]], ''yahr,'' year, plus ''zeit,'' time) < ref > [[Jew#Jewish ethnic groups|Sephardic Jews]] usually use the Hebrew word ''nakhala,'' [[inheritance]]. < /ref >  in [[Judaism]] is the yearly [[wikt:observe|observance < sup > def. 5 < /sup > ]] of the death of a close family member. There are a number of common customs for Jews observing a yahrzeit. These include remembering the relative, lighting [[memorial]] [[candle]]s, saying special prayers, giving money to [[Charity (practice)|charity]], and visiting the [[grave]] of the relative.  
 
 The date of the yahrzeit is fixed by the [[Hebrew calendar]].
