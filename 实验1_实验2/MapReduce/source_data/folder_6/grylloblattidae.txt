 '''Grylloblattidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] of [[extremophile]] and wingless [[insect]]s that live in the cold on top of mountains. < ref name=IIBD > {{cite book |author=H.V. Hoell, J.T. Doyen  &  A.H. Purcell |year=1998 |title=Introduction to insect biology and diversity |edition=2nd |publisher=Oxford University Press |pages= 320 |isbn= 0-19-510033-6}} < /ref >   
 
 They are commonly called '''grylloblattids''', but are also sometimes called '''ice crawlers''' or '''icebugs'''.  
 
 Most are nocturnal and appear to feed on [[detritus]] (rubbish). They have long [[Antenna (biology)|antennae]] (23–45 segments) and long [[cercus|cerci]] (appendages on the rear; 5–8 segments), but no wings.  
 
 Their closest living relatives are the recently-discovered [[Mantophasmatidae]]. < ref > {{cite journal |author=Stephen L. Cameron, Stephen C. Barker  &  Michael F. Whiting |year=2006 |title=Mitochondrial genomics and the new insect order Mantophasmatodea |journal=Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution |volume=38 |issue=1 |pages=274–279 |doi=10.1016/j.ympev.2005.09.020 |pmid=16321547}} < /ref > 
 
 The family is placed in its own [[suborder]], '''Grylloblattodea''', and sometimes in its own [[Order (biology)|order]], '''Grylloblattaria'''. It contains 5 genera and 25 species. < ref name= " number " > {{cite book|author=Terry L.
