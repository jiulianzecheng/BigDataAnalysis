 '''Aisne''' is a [[Departments of France|department]] in the north of [[France]], in the [[Hauts-de-France]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It is named after the [[Aisne (river)|Aisne]] [[river]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Oise (river)|Oise]]. Its [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] is [[Laon]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Aisne is one of the original 83 departments created during the [[French Revolution]] on 4 March 1790 with Laon as its [[Capital city|capital]]. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/02his.html |title=Historique de l'Aisne |work=Le SPLAF |language=fr |access-date=30 October 2016 }} < /ref >  It was formed from parts of the former [[province]]s of [[Île-de-France]] (Laon, Soissons, Noyon,and Valois), [[Picardy]] (Thiérache Vermandois), and [[Champagne, (province)|Champagne]] (Brie and Omois). 
 
 It was divided into six [[district]]s: Château-Thierry, Chauny, Laon, Saint-Quentin, Soissons and Vervins. 
 
 In 1800, with the creation of the [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] in France, the six districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Château-Thierry, Laon, Saint-Quentin, Soissons and Vervins. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Château-Thierry was eliminated but in 1942 it was again made an ''arrondissement''. < ref name=splaf / > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Aisne is part of the [[Hauts-de-France]] [[Regions of France|region]]. It has an area of {{Convert|7369.1|km2|sqmi|0|abbr=on }}. < ref name= " insee-geo "  / > 
 
 The department borders with [[Belgium]] and 6 other departments in 3 [[Regions of France|regions]]: 
 * [[Hauts-de-France]] region 
 ** [[Nord (department)|Nord]] (north) 
 ** [[Somme]] (northwest) 
 ** [[Oise]] (west) 
 * [[Grand Est]] region 
 ** [[Ardennes (department)|Ardennes]] (east) 
 ** [[Marne]] (southeast) 
 * [[Île-de-France]] region 
 ** [[Seine-et-Marne]] (south)

 The territory of the Aisne department is formed by low [[plateau]]s and small [[hill]]s. The highest point in the department, and in the [[Hauts-de-France]] region, is ''Le Chêne Nain'' ({{Coord|49|56|59|N|04|13|50|E|name=Le Chêne Nain|display=inline|region:ID_type:mountain}}) in the [[Ardennes (forest)|Ardennes]] [[forest]] on the border with the [[Ardennes (department)|Ardennes]] department and near the border with [[Belgium]]; it is {{convert|295|-|300|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} high. < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=29287 |title=Le Chêne Nain, France|publisher=Peakbagger.com |access-date=30 October 2016 }} < /ref > 
 
 The southern part of the department is the [[Geography|geographical]] region known as ''la Brie poilleuse'', a drier plateau known for its [[dairy]] products and [[Brie]] [[cheese]]. 
 
 Several rivers flow through the department; the main rivers are the [[Scheldt|Scheldt/Escaut]], the [[Aisne (river)|Aisne]], the [[Marne (river)|Marne]], the [[Ourcq]], the [[Vesle]], the [[Somme River|Somme]], the [[Oise (river)|Oise]], and the [[Serre (river)|Serre]]. 
 
 ===Climate=== 
 The [[Köppen climate classification]] type for the climate at [[Laon]] is an  " Oceanic climate "  (also known as Marine West Coast Climate) and of the subtype  " Cfb " . < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Laon, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=135541 & cityname=Laon%2C+Picardie%2C+France & units= |access-date=30 October 2016}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[average]] amount of [[precipitation]] for the year in Laon is {{Convert|571.5|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. The month with the most precipitation on average is July with {{Convert|61|mm|in|1|abbr=on }} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is March with an average of {{Convert|30.5|mm|in|1|abbr=on }}. 
 
 In Laon, there is an average of {{Convert|17.8|cm|in|1|abbr=on }} of [[snow]]. The month with the most snow is February, with {{Convert|6.9|cm|in|1|abbr=on }} of snow. 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Laon is {{Convert|9.4|°C|°F }}. The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of {{Convert|17.2|°C|°F }}. The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of {{Convert|1.7|°C|°F }}.

 ==Administration== 
 Aisne is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Aisne'' in [[Laon]]. The department is part of the [[Hauts-de-France]] region. 
 
 ===Administrative divisions=== 
 There are 5 [[Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'']] ([[district]]s), 21 [[Cantons in France|''cantons'']] and 804 [[Communes in France|''communes'']] ([[Municipality|municipalities]]) in Aisne. < ref name=insee1 > {{cite web|url=https://www.insee.fr/fr/metadonnees/cog/departement/DEP02-aisne |title=Département de l’Aisne (02) |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=fr |access-date=7 November 2017 }} < /ref >

 ==Demographics== 
 The inhabitants of Aisne are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Axonais'' (women: ''Axonaises''), < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.habitants.fr/aisne-02|title=Habitants du départment: Aisne (02)|publisher=habitants.fr |language=fr |access-date=30 October 2016 }} < /ref >  from the [[Celts|Celtic]] name of the Aisne river, ''Axona''. 
 
 Aisne had a population, in 2014, of 539,783, < ref name=insee / >  for a population density of {{Pop density|539783|7369.1|km2|prec=1|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > . The ''arrondissement'' of Laon, with 165,489 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with the most inhabitants. < ref name=insee2 / >
