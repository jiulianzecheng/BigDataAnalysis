 The '''Komsomol''' was the Communist Youth League in the [[Soviet Union]].  It was officially called the '''All-Union Leninist Young Communist League.'''  It started in 1918.  It was for people from 14 to 28. For people under 14 there was  the [[Young Pioneers]], and for under 9 the [[Little Octobrists]].  < ref > {{cite journal |last1=Hulicka |first1=Karel |title=The Komsomol |journal=The Southwestern Social Science Quarterly |date=1962 |volume=42 |issue=4 |pages=363–373 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/42867730 |access-date=16 July 2023 |issn=0276-1742}} < /ref >    
 
 In March 1926 there were 1,750,000 members: 6% of the eligible youth population.  < ref > {{cite journal | last1 = Gorsuch | first1 = Anne E | year = 1992 | title = Soviet Youth and the Politics of Popular Culture during NEP | journal = Social History | volume = 17 | issue = 2| pages = 189–201 | jstor=4286015 | doi=10.1080/03071029208567834}} < /ref > 
 
 It didn't have much political influence  but it was important  for teaching the values of the [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union|CPSU]] to the younger generation.
