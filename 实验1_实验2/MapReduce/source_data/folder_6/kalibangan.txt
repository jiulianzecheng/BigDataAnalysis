'''Kalibangān''' is a prehistoric site at {{coord|29.47|N|74.13|E|}} in Rajasthan. It is on the left or southern banks of the Ghaggar ([[Ghaggar-Hakra River]]), < ref > {{cite web | publisher = [[Encyclopædia Britannica]] | url = http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/285248/India | access-date = 2008-12-31 | last = Calkins | first = PB |author2=Alam M | title = India }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite book | last = Lal | first = BB | title = Purātattva | publisher = [[Indian Archaeological Society]] | year = 2002 | chapter = The Homeland of Indo-European Languages and Culture: Some Thoughts | pages = 1–5}} < /ref >  [[India]]. It is on a triangle-shaped piece of land at the confluence of [[Drishadvati]] and Sarasvathi Rivers. < ref > McIntosh, Jane (2008) The Ancient Indus Calley : New Perspectives. ABC-CLIO. Page 77 < /ref >  A report on the excavations at the site were published in 2003 by the [[Archaeological Survey of India]], 34 & nbsp;years after the completion of excavations. The report said that Kalibangan was a major provincial capital in the [[Indus Valley civilization|Indus Valley]]. Discoveries at Kalibangan include unique fire [[Altar|altars]] and world's earliest known ploughed field. < ref > {{cite book | publisher = [[Archaeological Survey of India]] | year = 2003 | title = Excavations at Kalibangan, the Early Harappans, 1960-1969 | last = Lal | first = BB | pages =17, 98}} < /ref >  There is also evidence of two types of burials, namely circular graves and rectangular graves.
