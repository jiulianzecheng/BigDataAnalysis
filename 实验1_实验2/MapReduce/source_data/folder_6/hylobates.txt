 The [[genus]] '''''Hylobates''''' is one of the four genera of [[gibbon]]s. < ref name=Groves > Groves, Colin 2005. Wilson D.E.  &  Reeder, D.M. (eds) ''Mammal Species of the World''. 3rd ed, Johns Hopkins University Press, 178–181. [http://www.bucknell.edu/msw3/browse.asp?id=12100752] {{ISBN|0-801-88221-4}} < /ref > < ref name=Mootnick > {{cite journal | year = 2005 | title = A new generic name for the hoolock gibbon (Hylobatidae) | journal = International Journal of Primatology | volume = 26 | issue = 4 | pages = 971–976| doi = 10.1007/s10764-005-5332-4 | s2cid = 8394136 | last1 = Mootnick | first1 = Alan | last2 = Groves | first2 = Colin }} < /ref > < ref name=Geissmann > {{cite web | last = Geissmann | first = Thomas | title = Gibbon systematics and species identification | url = http://gibbons.de/main/system/intro.html | accessdate = 2006-04-13}} < /ref >  It is the most widespread type of gibbon; there are six or seven [[species]]. Individuals in this genus are characterized by 44 chromosomes and often have a ring of white fur around their faces. < ref name=Geissmann/ >
