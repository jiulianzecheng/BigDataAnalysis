 '''Pareidolia''' {{IPAc-en|p|ær|ᵻ|ˈ|d|oʊ|l|i|ə}} {{respell|parr-i|DOH|lee-ə}}) is a word from [[Ancient Greek language|Ancient Greek]] to describe the phenomenon, that the [[human]] [[mind]] often perceives familiar patterns in a [[stimulus]], even when they are not there.  
 
 Common examples are perceived images of animals, faces, or objects in cloud formations, the  " [[man in the moon]] " , the  " [[moon rabbit]] " , and [[hidden message]]s within recorded music played in reverse or at higher- or lower-than-normal speeds. 
 
 Pareidolia is a special case of [[clustering illusion]].
