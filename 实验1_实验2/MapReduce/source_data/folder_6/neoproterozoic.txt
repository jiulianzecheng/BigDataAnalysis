The '''Neoproterozoic''' [[era]] lasted from 1,000 to 541  [[million]] years ago (mya). < ref name=Ogg2008 > {{cite book| last = Ogg, James G; Ogg, Gabi  &  Gradstein, Felix M. 2008| title = The concise geologic time scale| date = 4 September 2008| publisher = Cambridge University Press| pages = 184| url = http://www.cambridge.org/catalogue/catalogue.asp?isbn=0521898498| isbn = 9780521898492}} < /ref > 
 
 In the first [[Period (geology)|period]], the [[Tonian]], the global [[supercontinent]] [[Rodinia]] broke up into separate land masses. 
 
 In the second period, the [[Cryogenian]], the Earth had two great [[ice age]]s, the [[Sturtian]] and [[Marinoan]] glaciations. These ice ages were so severe they are sometimes called [[Snowball Earth]].
