{{nihongo|'''''Genki'''''|元亀}} was a {{nihongo|[[Japanese era name]]|年号,| ''nengō'',| lit. 'year "  name}} after ''[[Eiroku]]'' and before ''[[Tenshō (Momoyama period)|Tenshō]].''  This period started in April 1570 and ended in July 1573. < ref > [[Louis-Frédéric|Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric]]. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA238   " ''Genki'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 238. < /ref >  During this time, the emperor was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Ogimachi of Japan|Ōgimachi]]''-tennō''|正親町天皇 }}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA739  " Ōgimachi Tennō, " ] p. 739; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac.]] (1834). [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA382  ''Annales des empereurs du Japon'',  pp. 382-389.] < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the ''Genki'' era== 
 [[File:Grave of Endō Naotsune.jpg|thumb|right|This is his grave of [[Endō Naotsune]] who was killed in the Battle of Anegawa near Nagahama in the 1st year of ''Genki'']] 
 * '''1570''' (''Genki 1, 6th month''): At the [[Battle of Anegawa]], the forces of [[Oda Nobunaga]] and [[Tokugawa Ieyasu]] won a [[wikt:victory|victory]]. < ref name= " titsingh388 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA388   p. 388]; Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA30   " ''Anegawa no Tatakai'' " ] at pp. 30-31. < /ref > 
 
 * '''1571''' (''Genki 2, 9th month''): Nobunaga ordered the [[wikt:destruction|destruction]] of temples on [[Mount Hiei]]. < ref name= " titsingh388 " / > 
 
 * '''1573''' (''Genki 4''): [[Oda Nobunaga]] causes the [[:en:Ashikaga_Yoshiaki|Ashikaga Yoshiaki]], the last Muromachi shogun, to flee Kyoto < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA55   " Ashikaga Yoshiaki " ] at p. 55. < /ref >
