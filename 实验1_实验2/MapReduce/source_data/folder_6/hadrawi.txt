 '''Mohamed Ibrahim Warsame'''{{Efn|{{Lang-so|Maxamed Ibraahim Warsame}}, {{Lang-ar|محمد ابراهيم ورسمي|link=no}}{{Lrm}}}} (1943 – 18 August 2022), known by the [[pseudonym]] '''Hadrawi''',{{Efn|{{Lang-so|Hadraawi|link=no}}, {{Lang-ar|هدراوى|link=no}}{{Lrm}}}} was a Somali poet, philosopher and songwriter. He was thought by many to be the greatest living Somali poet. His works have been compared to that of [[Shakespeare]]. < ref name= " Somsh " > {{Cite news|last=Ditmars|first=Hadani|title=Somali Shakespeare|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-4679190.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131205032813/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-4679190.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=5 December 2013|access-date=26 August 2013|newspaper=The Independent|date=30 September 1994}} < /ref >  His poetry has been translated into many languages. < ref name= " McConnell " > {{Cite news|last=McConnell|first=Tristan|title=Inside Somalia: Where poetry is revered|url=http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/africa/100210/inside-somalia-where-poetry-revered|access-date=17 September 2012|newspaper=GlobalPost|date=12 February 2010}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=Hadraawi: The Somali Shakespeare – The Documentary Podcast|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p05b91mj|access-date=2 March 2020|website=BBC World Service|date=2 August 2017}} < /ref >  Hadrawi was born in [[Burco]], [[British Somalia]]. He was honored with the [[Prince Claus Award]] in 2012. 
 
 Hadrawi died on 18 August 2022 in [[Hargeisa]], [[Somaliland]] at the age of 79. < ref > {{Cite news |url=https://www.somalitimes.co.uk/great-somali-poet-hadrawi-passed-away-in-hargeisa/ |title=Great Somali poet Hadrawi passed away in Hargeisa |work=Somali Times|date=18 August 2022 |access-date=18 August 2022}} < /ref >
