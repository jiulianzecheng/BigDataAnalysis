'''Nuyorican''' are people of Puerto Rican birth or heritage who live in New York City. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of NUYORICAN|url=https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Nuyorican|access-date=2021-07-31|website=www.merriam-webster.com|language=en}} < /ref >   The word  " Nuyorican "  is a mixture of the terms “New York "  and  " Puerto Rican”. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Poets|first=Academy of American|title=A Brief Guide to Nuyorican Poetry {{!}} Academy of American Poets|url=https://poets.org/text/brief-guide-nuyorican-poetry|access-date=2021-07-31|website=poets.org}} < /ref >   
 
 The Nuyorican Movement is a cultural movement of literature, art, and music. It celebrates Nuyorican poetry, art, music, dance, food, and festivities. It affirms Afro-[[Caribbean]] and [[Indigenous peoples|indigenous]] Caribbean identities.  Artistic themes include the power of creativity, poverty, hope, resilience, and protest. 
 
 The Puerto Rican Day Parade celebrates Nuyorican people every spring at 5th Avenue in New York City. < ref > {{Cite web|title=National Puerto Rico Day Parade brings generations of boricuas together|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/news/latino/national-puerto-rico-day-parade-brings-generations-boricuas-together-n1015521|access-date=2021-07-31|website=NBC News|language=en}} < /ref >  Many Nuyoricans proudly call themselves  " [[Boricua]] " .
