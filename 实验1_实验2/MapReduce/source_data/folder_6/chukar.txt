 The '''chukar''' (''Alectoris chukar'') is a Eurasian upland gamebird in the pheasant family [[Phasianidae]] of the order [[Galliformes]], gallinaceous birds. Other common names of this bird include 'Chukker' (sometimes misspelled as 'Chuker'), 'Chukar Partridge', 'Red-legged Partridge', 'Rock Partridge', 'Indian Hill Partridge', 'Chukka', 'Chukkar', 'Chukor', 'Chukore', 'Chikone', 'Kabk', 'Kau-Kau', and 'Keklik'. The Chukar is the [[List of national birds|National bird]] of [[Pakistan]] respectively.{{refn|name=Gcounts|group=n|''“The Official National bird and Avifaunae emblem of [[Pakistan]]” respectively''}}. 
 
 ==Distribution== 
 This partridge has its native range in [[Asia]] from [[Pakistan]] and [[Kashmir]], and [[Afghanistan]] in the east to southeastern Europe in the west, and is closely related and similar to its western equivalent, the Red-legged Partridge, Alectoris rufa. It has been introduced widely, and became established in the United States, Canada, New Zealand and Hawaii. In Great Britain, hybrids between this species and the also introduced Red-legged Partridge are common.
