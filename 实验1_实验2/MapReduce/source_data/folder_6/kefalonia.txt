 '''Kefalonia''' (and Kephalonia or Cephalonia) is a [[Greece|Greek]] island in the [[Ionian Sea]]. It is the largest of the Ionian islands and the sixth largest Greek island after Crete, Euboea, Lesbos, Rhodes and Chios. Its area is 781 square metres < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.kefaloniahome.com/islands.php |title=A site for Kefalonia island |access-date=2010-02-02 |archive-date=2012-04-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120427215140/http://www.kefaloniahome.com/islands.php |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  and its population is 36,404 according to a 2001 Greek census. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Kefalonia is home to the highest mountain in the Ionian islands. The mountain is named [[Ainos]] and its altitude is 1, 628 metres. The biggest town on the island is [[Argostoli]] which is the capital of the Kefalonia prefecture. The second biggest town is [[Lixouri]] which is located opposite Argostoli. Beside Kefalonia is the island [[Ithaca]], the place of birth of ancient hero [[Odysseus]]. Some archaeologists believe that Kefalonia is the place of birth of Odysseus. 
 
 ==Sites== 
 Kefalonia has many beautiful beaches and it is one of the most popular tourist destinations.
