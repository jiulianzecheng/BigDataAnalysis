 '''AlphaBay Market''' was an [[online]] [[Deep Web|darknet]] [[Market|marketplace]] operating on the [[Tor (anonymity network)|Tor network]]. It was shut down by the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation|FBI]] and the [[United States Department of Justice]] on July 2017.  < ref > {{Cite news|date=2017-07-20|title=AlphaBay, the Largest Online 'Dark Market,' Shut Down|language=en|url=https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/alphabay-largest-online-dark-market-shut-down|access-date=2020-09-05}} < /ref >   
 
 == History == 
 It started in November 2014 and officially launched on December 22, 2014. It had 14,000 new users in the first 90 days of its operation. The darknet informer website Gwern.net placed AlphaBay Market in the top tier of markets regarding the 6-month survival probability, and it has proven to be successful. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.gwern.net/Black-market%20survival |title=Black-market risks - Gwern.net |publisher=Gwern}} < /ref >  In October 2015, it was recognized as the largest online darknet market according to Dan Palumbo, research director at Digital Citizens Alliance. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://southwestcoalition.org/buying-drugs-online-remains-easy/ |title=Buying Drugs Online Remains Easy |publisher=Southwest Coalition}} < /ref >  It was relaunched by vendor who identifies himself as co-founder  " DeSnake "  in 2021.  < ref > {{Cite news|last=Greenberg|first=Andy|title=He Escaped the Dark Web's Biggest Bust. Now He's Back|language=en-US|work=Wired|url=https://www.wired.com/story/alphabay-desnake-dark-web-interview/|access-date=2023-01-21|issn=1059-1028}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite web|title=AlphaBay Darknet Market is Back!|url=https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/alphabay-darknet-market-back-tom-robinson|access-date=2023-01-21|website=www.linkedin.com|language=en}} < /ref >
