'''Warfarin''' (sometimes sold as '''Coumadin''') is a powerful water-soluble [[chemical compound|compound]]. It prevents blood from [[blood clotting|clotting]]. It does that by reducing the production of factors by the [[liver]] that promote clotting. It is called an  " anticoagulant agent " . It is a powerful [[rat]] [[poison]]. In concentrated form, it causes bleeding of internal organs which leads to death. < ref > {{Cite journal|last1=Tornkvist|first1=Max|last2=Smith|first2=J.  It is also used, in very low doses, to prevent [[thrombosis]] in humans. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=Warfarin Monograph for Professionals|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/warfarin.html|access-date=2022-11-14|website=Drugs.com|language=en}} < /ref >  This is the local coagulation or clotting of the blood in the circulatory system. In such cases, it makes [[heart attack]]s, [[stroke]]s, and [[pulmonary embolism]] less likely. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  The degree of anticoagulation is monitored by blood tests.
