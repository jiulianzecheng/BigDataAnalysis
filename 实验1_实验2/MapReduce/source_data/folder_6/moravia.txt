 '''Moravia''' ([[Czech language|Czech]] and [[Slovak language|Slovak]]: ''Morava;'' [[German language|German]]'':'' ''Mähren'') is a historical region in the eastern part of the [[Czech Republic]] that takes its name from the Morava River, which flows from the north to the south of the land.  < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/moravia|title=Moravia definition and meaning {{!}} Collins English Dictionary|website=www.collinsdictionary.com|language=en|access-date=2020-08-07}} < /ref > 
 
 Together with [[Bohemia]] in the west and [[Silesia]] in the north, Moravia was a historical-political region called the Czech Lands. Moravia, [[Bohemia]] and a small part of [[Silesia]] make up the current Czech Republic. 
 
 Important cities in Moravia are [[Brno]], [[Olomouc]], [[Ostrava]] and [[Zlín]].
