 '''Czechoslovakia''' or '''Czecho-Slovakia''' < ref name=covenant > {{cite web|title=THE COVENANT OF THE LEAGUE OF NATIONS.|url=http://avalon.law.yale.edu/imt/parti.asp}} < /ref >  was a country in [[Europe]]. It split off from [[Austria-Hungary]] in 1918 and split apart in 1993. 
 
 In mid-1938 [[Nazi Germany]] took over Czechoslovakia and split off [[Slovakia]]. [[Sudetenland]] was annexed by Germany, other parts of Czechia became its [[protectorate]] named Bohemia and Moravia. After [[World War II]] the [[USSR]] liberated these lands and kept [[Zakarpattia Oblast|Zakarpattia]] because of the [[Ukrainians|Ukrainian]] ([[Rusyns|Rusyn]]) majority in that region.  
 
 By 1948 pro-Soviet communists got the power finally and declared the Czechoslovak Socialist Republic.  It was a member of [[Warsaw Pact|Warsaw Treaty Organization]] and [[Council for Mutual Economic Assistance|COMECON]] , one of the richest countries of the [[Eastern Bloc]].  In the [[Prague Spring]] of the late 1960s, Czechoslovak leader [[Alexander Dubček|Alexander Dubcek]] pursued his own policy of a ‘socialism with a human face’.  In 1968 Warsaw Pact troops invaded Czechoslovakia to restore the old system. 
 
 In 1989 Czechoslovakia peacefully changed its political system in the ''[[Velvet Revolution]]''.  On 1 January 1993, Czechoslovakia split into the '''[[Czech Republic]] '''and''' [[Slovakia]]'''.  The countries separated in peace.
