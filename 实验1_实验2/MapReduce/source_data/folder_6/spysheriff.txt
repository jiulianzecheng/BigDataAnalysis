SpySheriff tries to trick the user into buying the program by making false warnings and alerts. < ref name= " winampflaw " > {{cite web|url=http://www.zdnetasia.com/news/security/0,39044215,39310016,00.htm|title=Spyware tunnels in on Winamp flaw|publisher=Joris Evers, CNET News.com, February 6, 2006|accessdate=2009-11-01|archive-date=2009-05-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090502150002/http://www.zdnetasia.com/news/security/0,39044215,39310016,00.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  It is very difficult to remove SpySheriff from computers, < ref name= " ZDNet10 " > {{cite web|url=http://blogs.zdnet.com/Spyware/?p=727|title=Top 10 rogue anti-spyware|publisher=Suze Turner, ZDNet, December 19, 2005|accessdate=2009-11-01|archive-date=2009-07-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090727091536/http://blogs.zdnet.com/Spyware/?p=727|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  since it hides its parts in  " System Restore "  folders. SpySheriff also blocks some parts of the computer's administrative tools. Like other rogue antivirus programs, SpySheriff asks the user to register when they click  " Remove found threats " . SpySheriff can be removed by real antivirus programs.
