 '''ARKive''' was a data system to help conserve the world's threatened species. It used  " the power of wildlife imagery " , < ref name= " about " > {{cite web|url=http://www.arkive.org/about/|title=About ARKive |publisher=ARKive |access-date=12 July 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110701182614/http://www.arkive.org/about/|archive-date=1 July 2011|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name= " about2017 " > {{cite web|url=http://www.arkive.org/about/|title=About Arkive |publisher=ARKive |access-date=25 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170713182635/http://www.arkive.org/about/|archive-date=13 July 2017|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 The archive has films, photographs and audio recordings of the world's [[species]] in a centralised [[Digital data|digital]] archive. < ref name= " about "  / >  Its priority was  the 17,000 species on the [[IUCN Red List]] of Threatened Species at that time. < ref name= " about "  / > 
 
 The project was an initiative of [[Wildscreen]], a UK-registered educational charity, based in [[Bristol]]. < ref name= " Wildscreen-About " > {{cite web|url=http://www.wildscreen.org.uk/about|title=Wildscreen – About|access-date=12 July 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130821233605/http://wildscreen.org.uk/about|archive-date=21 August 2013|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 The website closed on 15 February 2019. It kept its collection of images and videos stored in its archive.
