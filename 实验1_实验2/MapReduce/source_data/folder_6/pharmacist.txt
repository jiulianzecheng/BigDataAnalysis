 A '''pharmacist''' is a person who supplies [[Drug|drugs]] to people, often  [[Prescription|prescriptions]] from a [[Physician|doctor]] but sometimes on their own.   
 
 Pharmacists have to be trained and have knowledge about the [[drug]]s package. Many work in a [[pharmacy|pharmacy.]]Pharmacists interact with customers, give the prescribed medication and telling them about the drugs.  
 
 Some work in hospitals where they may deliver the drugs to the different parts of the hospital and give people the drugs they need to take home.  
 
 [[File:Man consults with pharmacist (2).jpg|thumb|new medication]] 
 
 Some work with [[General practitioner|general practitioners]] where they may check that people are getting the right drugs.  < ref > {{Cite web|last=Burns|first=Corrinne|title=Nearly 400 advanced pharmacist practitioners now working in primary care networks|url=https://pharmaceutical-journal.com/article/news/nearly-400-advanced-pharmacist-practitioners-now-working-in-primary-care-networks|access-date=2023-02-03|website=The Pharmaceutical Journal|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 Pharmacists is the one who checks the works of the pharmacy technician. Pharmacy Technician works under the supervise of the Pharmacists, Pharmacy Technician are the one who pulls out the medicines according to the prescription written and they are the one who counts the medicine, after they finish counting and putting it inside a container for [[Medicine|medicines]] purpose only. Once the Pharmacy Technician finished counting the medicine and putting it inside a container then, the Pharmacist will check the medicine in the computer to make sure the medicine that is dispensed is correct.  
 
 [[File:Schriever pharmacy offers convenience, ease of use (827698).jpeg|thumb|calling the doctor]] 
 
 Pharmacists can also help with recommending over the counter medication like when someone has sore throats or a common cold they can recommend medications that anyone can buy with a prescription from the doctor. Pharmacists may also give [[vaccines]], shots that protect you from diseases like the [[Influenza|flu]] or [[shingles]]. During the [[COVID-19]] [[pandemic|pandemic,]] they helped with giving vaccines to those who did not want to go to the hospitals.  < ref > {{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Graduate Programs|date=2022-01-04|title=What Do Pharmacists Do? Roles and Responsibilities|url=https://graduate.northeastern.edu/resources/what-do-pharmacists-do/|access-date=2023-05-31|website=Graduate Blog|language=en-US}} < /ref >   
 
 Pharmacists can also help people taking a new medication by giving them instructions for the prescription. The pharmacist also helps by telling the patient the side effects of the new medication.
