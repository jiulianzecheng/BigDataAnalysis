 '''''Goodfellas''''' is a 1990 [[United States|American]] [[biopic|biographical]] [[crime]] [[movie]] [[movie director|directed]] by [[Martin Scorsese]]. It is about life in the [[Mafia]]. 
 
 It gained six [[wikt:nominate|nominations]] for [[Academy Award|Oscar]]s. It won one (for [[Joe Pesci]]'s role).  Many people thought the movie was great. 
 
 It is set in [[New York City]]. The movie is based on the life of [[mafia|mafioso]] [[Henry Hill]] (1943-2012) from 1955 - 1980. ''Goodfellas'' uses information from New York crime reporter [[Nicholas Pileggi]]'s book ''Wiseguy''.  Scorsese and Pileggi worked together to write the movie. 
 
 ''Goodfellas'' was first shown at the 1990 [[Venice Film Festival]].  Scorsese received the [[Silver Lion]] award for Best Director. < ref name=  " Malcolm " > {{cite news | last = Malcolm | first = Derek | title = The Venice Film Festival ends in uproar | work = [[The Guardian]] | date = September 17, 1990  }} < /ref > 
 
 Director [[David Chase]] said the movie made him want to make the [[HBO]] television series ''[[The Sopranos]]''.
