In [[Greek mythology]], '''Eurystheus''' ({{IPAc-en|j|ʊəˈr|ɪ|s|θ|i|ə|s|}}; {{lang-grc-gre|Εὐρυσθεύς}} -  " broad strength " ) was the king of [[Tiryns]] and [[Mycenae]]. He was the son of [[Sthenelus (son of Perseus)|Sthenelus]]. He was the one whom [[Herakles]] had to serve as [[penance]] for the murder of his family.
