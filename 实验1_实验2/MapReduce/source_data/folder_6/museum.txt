 ==History== 
 The word, '''museum''', originates from [[Musa]] which is the goddesses of literature, art, and science who appears in [[Greek mythology]]. 
 
 According to the International Council of Museums (ICOM), the definition of museums has changed over time. < ref name= " icom " / >     
 
 The oldest museum structure in the world is the ''[[Shōsō-in]]'' in Nara, Japan. < ref name= " nussbaum671 " > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA671  " Museums " ] in ''Japan Encyclopedia'', pp. 671-672. < /ref > 
 
 Today's museums are non-profit, permanent institutions in the service of society and its development. < ref name= " icom " / > 
 
 ==Function== 
 A museum [[wikt:acquire|acquire]]s, [[Conservation (art)|conserves]], [[wikt:research|research]]es, [[wikt:communicate|communicate]]s and [[wikt:exhibit|exhibit]]s the  [[Wiktionary:tangible|tangible]] [[Wiktionary:heritage|heritage]] < ref > Miyagi Prefecture,  文化財財案内 (Cultural heritage guide); excerpt, tangible objects include [http://www.pref.miyagi.jp/bunkazai/annai.htm   " arts and crafts, ... historical documents, textiles, swords, sculpture, paintings, houses, temples and shrines " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120421022150/http://www.pref.miyagi.jp/bunkazai/annai.htm |date=2012-04-21 }}; retrieved 2012-6-27. < /ref >  and the [[Wiktionary:intangible|intangible]] heritage < ref > Miyagi, 文化財財案内; excerpt, [http://www.pref.miyagi.jp/bunkazai/annai.htm  " intangible cultural heritage is 'human technology', such as the process of traditional Japanese sword training, traditional Japanese dyeing and weaving, traditional Japanese theater like Kabuki and Noh " ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120421022150/http://www.pref.miyagi.jp/bunkazai/annai.htm |date=2012-04-21 }}; retrieved 2012-6-27. < /ref >  of [[humanity]] and the [[environment]]. < ref name= " icom " / > 
 
 Museums exist for the purposes of education, study and enjoyment. < ref name= " icom " / > 
 
 Some museums have things that visitors can do. For example, [[ecomuseum]]s exist. 
 
 Museums can be about different things such as art, national history, [[natural history]], or science. People go to museums sometimes to learn, or to simply have fun.  
 
 Museums with live [[animal]]s are called [[zoo]]s. 
 
 ===Exhibitions=== 
 *Temporary or changing exhibits 
 ::Exhibition which selects works along with some themes, e.g., a writer, a time, an area, etc. 
 *Permanent exhibits 
 ::Exhibition which displays the works which the museum possesses.
