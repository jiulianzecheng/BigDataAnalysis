'''Dysprosium''' ({{pronEng|dɪsˈproʊziəm}}) is a [[chemical element]] with the symbol '''Dy''' and [[atomic number]] 66. It has a very high melting temperature of 1,407°C. It can also absorb [[neutron]]s. This makes it very suitable for use with [[nuclear reactor]]s and [[laser]]s.  
 
 Dysprosium is a [[rare earth element]] that has a metallic, bright silver luster. 
 
 Dysprosium was first identified in [[Paris]] in 1886 by French chemist [[Paul Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran]].
