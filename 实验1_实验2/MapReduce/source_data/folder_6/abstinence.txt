 '''Abstinence''' is a voluntary restraint from following a desire or appetite for certain bodily activities that are widely experienced as giving pleasure. Most frequently, the term refers to abstention from [[sexual intercourse]], [[alcohol]] or [[food]]. Abstinence can be due to personal preferences, religious practices of practical considerations. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2010-02-11|title=What is Abstinence?|url=https://www.news-medical.net/health/What-is-Abstinence.aspx|access-date=2021-06-01|website=News-Medical.net|language=en|archive-date=2021-05-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210517080555/https://www.news-medical.net/health/What-is-Abstinence.aspx|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 In medicine abstinence also refers to discontinuation of an addictive drug. This may lead to intense craving for the drug or [[Drug withdrawal|withdrawal]] syndromes. < ref > {{Cite web|last1=Mosel|first1=Stacy|last2=LMSW|title=Steps to Sobriety: Ways to Get Sober|url=https://addiction-treatment.com/rehab-guides/journey-to-sobriety/|access-date=2021-06-01|website=Addiction Treatment|archive-date=2021-06-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210628164010/https://addiction-treatment.com/rehab-guides/journey-to-sobriety/|url-status=live}} < /ref >  Abstinence from smoking is also recommended for those intending to undergo surgery. 
 
 Abstinence may be a temporary or short-term goal meant for short durations of time. This includes refraining from compulsive eating or from compulsive drinking. The time and the measure is voluntary and is thus meant to enhance life. This is different from psychological mechanism of repression where the abstinence is not willingly adopted. 
 
 In India, [[Buddhist]]s, [[Jain]]s, and some Hindus abstain from eating meat on the grounds both of health and of reverence for all sentient forms of life. Total abstinence from feeding on the flesh of cows is a hallmark of [[Hinduism]]. 
 
 == Abstinence In Religion == 
 Fasting, abstinence from food or drink or both for health, ritualistic, religious, or ethical purposes. The abstention may be complete or partial, lengthy, of short duration, or intermittent. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Religious Beliefs on Abstinence|url=https://classroom.synonym.com/buddhist-beliefs-about-monogamy-12087292.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-01|website=classroom.synonym.com|language=en|archive-date=2021-04-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416205549/https://classroom.synonym.com/buddhist-beliefs-about-monogamy-12087292.html}} < /ref > 
 
 For [[Jewish]] people the principal day of fast is [[Yom Kippur]], the Day of Atonement. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Day of Atonement|url=https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/day-of-atonement|access-date=2021-06-01|website=www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org|archive-date=2021-04-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210418043824/https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/day-of-atonement|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 Those who follows [[Islam]] the period of fasting lasts during the whole month of [[Ramadan]]. Each day of the month of Ramadan, people of the faith, fast without anything to eat or drink from dawn to dusk. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Ramadan|url=https://www.history.com/topics/holidays/ramadan|access-date=2021-06-01|website=HISTORY|language=en|archive-date=2021-04-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210427190100/https://www.history.com/topics/holidays/ramadan|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 Hindus fast on several festivals sometimes going without food or water and sometimes living only on fruits and dairy products. Followers of Hinduism also observe certain days when they may abstain completely from eating meat, eggs or fish. This is called vegetarianism. Some faiths like Buddhism and Jainism may advocate complete vegetarianism. 
 
 [[Catholicism|Catholics]] and [[Orthodox Christianity|Orthodox]] Christians abstain from food and drink for an hour prior to taking Holy Communion, and abstain from meat on Ash Wednesday and during Lent. < ref > {{Cite web|title=What Is Ash Wednesday? Why Christians Celebrate It|url=https://www.christianity.com/church/church-life/what-is-ash-wednesday-why-do-christians-celebrate-it.html|access-date=2021-06-01|website=Christianity.com|language=en|archive-date=2021-05-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210510195149/https://www.christianity.com/church/church-life/what-is-ash-wednesday-why-do-christians-celebrate-it.html|url-status=live}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Mormonism|Mormons]] abstain from certain foods and drinks by combining spiritual discipline. Mormons also fast one day a month and the money saved by skipping meals is donated to the needy. 
 
 Both Jews and Muslims abstain from pork in their regular diet. Hindus abstain from beef in their diet.
