 '''Wikitongues''' is an American [[non-profit organization]] registered in [[New York (state)|New York]]. Its goal is to document all the languages in the world. < ref name= " HS " >  {{cite web | url= http://www.heraldscotland.com/news/13167666.Dinnae_haud_yer_wheesht_____team_collating_all_languages_on_planet_lands_in_Scotland/ | title= Dinnae haud yer wheesht ... team collating all languages on planet lands in Scotland |author= Judith Duffy | date= 28 June 2014 | publisher = Herald Scotland| access-date=9 May 2020}} < /ref >   It was founded by Frederico Andrade, Daniel Bogre Udell and Lindie Botes in 2014. < ref > {{cite web | url=http://wikitongues.org/press/ | title=Wikitongues Press Release | access-date=9 May 2020 | archive-date=30 April 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160430105326/http://wikitongues.org/press/ | url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.kickstarter.com/profile/wikitongues | title=Wikitongues: Biography | publisher=Kickstarter | access-date=9 May 2020}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Oral histories== 
 [[File:Odia_Wikimedia_Usergroup_Kiosk_01.jpg|thumb|Wikitongues contributors in Montreal during Wikimania 2017.]] 
 By May 2016, 329 videos in over 200 languages were recorded for Wikitongues. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://medium.com/wikitongues/hello-from-wikitongues-e7ad62fa3bcb|title=Hello from Wikitongues|last=Wikitongues|date=2016-11-04|website=Wikitongues|access-date=9 May 2020}} < /ref >  As of 2018, the amount of languages increased to 350 languages, or 5% of the languages in the world, with plans to increase the amount of languages to 1000 in the coming years. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://news.nationalgeographic.com/2018/04/saving-dying-disappearing-languages-wikitongues-culture/|title=The Race to Save the World's Disappearing Languages|publisher=National Geographic|author=Nina Strochlic|date=2018-04-16|access-date=9 May 2020}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Poly== 
 Poly is an open-source software program designed to be used to share and learn languages. < ref name= " PRI " > {{cite web | url=http://www.pri.org/stories/2016-02-23/nonprofit-wants-build-tool-share-and-document-all-world-s-languages | title=This nonprofit wants to build a tool to share and document all the world’s languages | publisher=PRI | date=February 23, 2016 | access-date=9 May 2020 | author=Jared Goyette}} < /ref >  Wikitongues earned $52,716 USD combined from 429 backers on [[Kickstarter]], < ref name= " qz " > {{cite web | url=http://qz.com/615467/anyone-can-contribute-to-this-dictionary-of-the-worlds-dying-languages/ | title=Anyone can contribute to this dictionary of the world’s dying languages |author= Kate Groetzinger| date=February 12, 2016| publisher=Quartz | access-date=9 May 2020}} < /ref >  which helped Poly be developed. Poly is currently under development. < ref name= " Kickstarter " > {{cite web | url=https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/wikitongues/poly-share-and-learn-every-language-in-the-world | title=Share and learn every language in the world | publisher=Kickstarter | access-date=9 May 2020 | author=Wikitongues}} < /ref >
