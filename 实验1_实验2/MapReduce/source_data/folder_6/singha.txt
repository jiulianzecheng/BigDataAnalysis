'''Singha''' ({{lang-th|สิงห์}}; {{rtgs|''Singha''}}) is a beer brand. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.singha.th/our-beer|title=Our Beer|website=Singha Corporation Co., Ltd.|access-date=26 May 2015}}{{Dead link|date=December 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref >  It is manufactured in [[Thailand]] by the Singha Corporation Co. Ltd.. Singha corp is a subsidiary of its parent company, [[Boon Rawd Brewery]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 Praya Bhirom Bhakdi received a license to brew beer from the Thai government. In May 1933, construction of a brewery began. Boon Rawd Brewery was founded on 4 August 1933. In March 1934, production was started. Three beers were brewed: Golden Kite, Singha, and Stupa. < ref > {{cite web |title=Historical timeline |url=http://www.singha.th/singha-story/historical-timeline |website=Singha Corporation, Co., Ltd. |access-date=1 September 2018 }}{{Dead link|date=December 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 A [[Royal warrant of appointment (Thailand)|royal warrant]] was bestowed on 25 October 1939. Boon Rawd is the only brewery permitted to display the royal [[Garuda]]. The Singha is a powerful mythological lion, found in ancient South Asian and Thai mythology. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.singha.th/singha-story/about-singha |title=About Singha; About Garuda |website=Singha Corporation Co., Ltd. |access-date=20 Jun 2018 }}{{Dead link|date=December 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 Boon Rawd bought two German breweries at [[Hartmannsdorf (Chemnitz)|Hartmannsdorf]] and [[Mittweida]], [[Saxony]] in 1994. Some Singha beer is brewed in Germany by Schlossbrauerei Au-Hallertau under licence from Singha Corporation Co., Ltd.. 
 
 Beginning in September 2007, a 5.0% ([[Alcohol by volume|abv]]) version of Singha replaced the 6.0% ([[Alcohol by volume|abv]]) original.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}} This new version, while still brewed in Thailand by Pathum Thani Brewery Co. Ltd., states on its label to have been brewed under the supervision of Singha Corporation Co. Ltd., instead of Boon Rawd. 
 
 From 2013, some Singha beer has reportedly been produced at [[Carlsberg Group|Carlsberg]]'s Russian plant for distribution in some markets. Singha is also said to use eight Carlsberg plants in Asia for production, as part of an agreement to share each other's production facilities and markets. < ref name=ref2013021407 > {{Cite news|last=Maierbrugger|first=Arno|title=Singha starts roll-out in Asia and Europe|website=Investvine|date=14 February 2013|url=http://investvine.com/singha-starts-roll-out-in-asia-and-europe/|access-date=2018-09-01|archive-date=2013-09-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130915153907/http://investvine.com/singha-starts-roll-out-in-asia-and-europe/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Governance== 
 Singha Corporation, established in 2001, is a subsidiary of Boon Rawd Brewery, tasked with managing of all of Boon Rawd's 150-plus subsidiary companies. It reports directly to Boon Rawd's board of directors. < ref name=Aboutus > {{cite web |title=About us |url=http://singha.com/en/about-us |website=Singha Corporation, Co., Ltd. |access-date=1 September 2018 |archive-date=1 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180901113442/http://singha.com/en/about-us |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Products== 
 Besides Singha beer, the company manufactures Singha Light, < ref > {{cite web |title=Singha Light Beer |url=http://singha.com/singhalight/home_en.html |website=Singha Corporation, Co., Ltd. |access-date=1 September 2018 |archive-date=14 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180914182651/http://singha.com/singhalight/home_en.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Leo beer, U Beer, bottled and soda water, and packaged foods. < ref name=Aboutus/ > 
 
 ==Operations== 
 In 2017, Singha Corporation sold 1.32 billion litres of Singha, Leo, and U Beer and retained a 62 percent market share in Thailand. The firm intends to sell 1.4 billion litres in 2018 and up its market share to 65 percent. < ref name= " BP-2018-09-01 " > {{cite news |last1=Jitpleecheep |first1=Pitsinee |title=Racing through a disruptive age |url=https://www.bangkokpost.com/business/news/1532094/racing-through-a-disruptive-age |access-date=1 September 2018 |work=Bangkok Post |date=1 September 2018}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Advertising== 
 Singha was the title sponsor of Francesco Cantando's [[F1 Powerboat World Championship|Formula 1 Powerboat]] team from the mid-1990s up until the end of the [[2013 F1 Powerboat World Championship season|2013 season]], making it one of the longest partnerships in the history of the sport. For [[2014 F1 Powerboat World Championship season|2014]], Motorglass.it replaced Singha as Cantando's team's new title sponsor.
