 '''Chervil''' (''Anthriscus cerefolium'') is a [[herb]] that is related to [[parsley]]. It is sometimes called '''garden chervil'''. 
 
 == Uses == 
 === Cooking === 
 [[Image:Chervil.JPG|left|thumb|200px|Chervil in [[salad]]]] 
 
 Sometimes called  " gourmet's parsley " , chervil is put in [[poultry]], [[seafood]], and small [[vegetable]]s. It is particularly famous in [[France]], where it is added to [[omelette]]s, [[salad]]s and [[soup]]s. More delicate than [[parsley]], it has a small taste of [[liquorice]]. 
 
 === Medicine === 
 Chervil had several [[tradition|traditional]] uses. Pregnant women were washed in an [[infusion]] of it; a [[wikt:lotion|lotion]] of chervil was used as for cleaning skin; and it was used medicinally as a [[blood]] [[wikt:pure|purifier]].
