 The '''Kingdom of Judah''' was an ancient [[Judaism|Jewish]] [[kingdom]] that existed in the southern part of the [[Levant]]. It was on the western shore of the [[Dead Sea]]. Its main cities were [[Jerusalem]] and [[Hebron]].  After the death of [[Saul]], [[Kingdom of Israel (united monarchy)|Israel]] picked [[David]] to rule them. ([[1 Chronicles]] 11:1) After the death of [[Solomon|King Solomon]], son of David, ten of the twelve of the [[tribes of Israel]] did not want Solomon's son, [[Rehoboam]] to lead them. This caused the kingdom to split the Northern Kingdom, the [[Kingdom of Israel]] and the '''Southern Kingdom''', the Kingdom of Judah. Jerusalem was the [[capital (city)|capital]] of the Kingdom of Judah. 
 
 In 586 BC, the king of [[Babylon]], [[Nebuchadnezzar II]], took over Jerusalem and destroyed Judah.
