 '''Dambulla''' ({{Lang-si|දඹුල්ල}} ''Dam̆bulla'', {{Lang-ta|தம்புள்ளை}} ''Tampuḷḷai'') is a town in the north of Matale District, [[Central Province, Sri Lanka|Central Province]] of [[Sri Lanka]]. It is the '''second largest town''' in the Matale District. It is {{Convert|148|km|abbr=on}} north-east of [[Colombo]], {{Convert|43|km|abbr=on}} north of Matale and {{Convert|72|km|abbr=on}} north of [[Kandy]]. The city has a famous ''vegetable market''. 
 
 It was also known as '''Dhamballai. It''' was ruled by Kings like [[Rajaraja Chola I|Raja Raja Chola]] and [[Rajendra Chola I|Rajendra Chola]] when they were kings in 900-1000 [[AD]]. 
 
 == Dambulla Cave Temple == 
 '''It is the largest cave temple in Sri Lanka'''. The rock is {{Convert|160|m|abbr=on}} high. There are 153 Buddha [[statues]], three statues of Sri Lankan kings and four statues of god and goddess including 2 statues of Hindu gods [[Vishnu]] and [[Ganesha|Ganesh]]. The walls of the caves have Buddha's story.
