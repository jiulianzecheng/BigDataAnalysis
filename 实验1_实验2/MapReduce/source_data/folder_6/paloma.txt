'''Paloma''' [ p''uh''-'''loh'''-m''uh'' ] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Paloma {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/paloma|access-date=2023-04-08|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[female]] [[given name]]. It comes from the [[Latin language|Latin]] word ''palumba'', which means  " dove " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=Paloma name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Paloma-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-04-08|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  This name is often understood to mean a Christian type of symbol of The Holy Spirit in this bird. The name is common in [[Italy]], [[Spain]] and [[Hungary]].  
 
 In November 2008, [[Hurricane Paloma]] caused heavy destruction over the [[Cayman Islands]] and [[Cuba]]. The name ''Paloma'' was then retired and replaced with ''Paulette'' for 2014. This name was ranked #886 on the US Popular Names in 2021. < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-04-08|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref >
