'''Secularization'''  means religion gets less important in modern societies. As the society goes through modernization, all the religious values and institutions decline. < ref >   " The secularization debate " , chapter 1 (pp. 3-32) in Norris, Pippa  &  Inglehart, Ronald 2004. ''Sacred and Secular: religion and politics worldwide''. Cambridge University Press. ISBN 978-0-521-83984-6 < /ref > 
 
 The 1960s saw a trend toward  secularization in [[Western Europe]], [[North America]], [[Australia]], and [[New Zealand]]. At the same time these things happened: economic prosperity, youth rebelling against the rules and conventions of society, [[Women's liberation movement | women's liberation]], radical [[theology]], and radical politics. < ref > 
 Jeffrey Cox,  " Secularization and other master narratives of religion in modern Europe. "  ''Kirchliche Zeitgeschichte'' (2001): 24-35.
