 '''''Shaheed''''' ({{Lang-ar|شهيد}} & nbsp;{{IPA-ar|ʃahiːd|}}, {{abbr|fem.|feminine}} & nbsp;{{IPA-ar|ʃahiːdah|}}, {{abbr|pl.|plural}} & nbsp;{{lang|ar|شُهَدَاء}} & nbsp;{{IPA-ar|ʃuhadaː|}}; {{lang-pa|ਸ਼ਹੀਦ}}) denotes a [[martyr]] in [[Islam]] and [[Sikhism]]. < ref > {{cite news|last1=Khalid Zaheer|title=Definition of a shaheed|url=http://www.dawn.com/news/1057801|access-date=11 January 2016|agency=Dawn|date=November 22, 2013}} < /ref >  The word is used frequently in the [[Quran]] in the generic sense of  " witness "  but only once in the sense of  " [[martyr]] "  (i.e. one who dies for his faith); the latter sense acquires wider usage in the ''[[hadith]]''. < ref > " The word shahid (plural shahada) has the meaning of  " martyr "  and is closely related in its development to the Greek martyrios in that it means both a witness and a martyr [...] in the latter sense only once is it attested (3:141).
