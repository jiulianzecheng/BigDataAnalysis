 '''Lokma''' are [[pastries]] made of [[Leavening agent|leavened]] and deep fried [[dough]], soaked in [[syrup]] or [[honey]], sometimes coated with [[cinnamon]] or other [[Ingredient|ingredients]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bIIeBQAAQBAJ & pg=PA424|title=The Oxford Companion to Food|last=Davidson|first=Alan|date=2014-08-21|publisher=OUP Oxford|isbn=978-0-19-104072-6|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :0 "  / > 
 
 == History == 
 Loukoumades where served to the winners of the [[Ancient Olympic Games|Ancient Greek olympics.]] < ref > {{cite web | url=https://www.worldfoodstory.co.uk/recipe/loukoumades | title=Loukoumades  & #124; Traditional Greek Cuisine | date=25 March 2020 }} < /ref >  ''Luqmat al-Qadi'', was first made in the early medieval period and the 13th-century [[Abbasid Caliphate]]. It is mentioned in several of the existent cook books of the time. < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=if5fAwAAQBAJ & pg=PA49|title=Sweet Delights from a Thousand and One Nights: The Story of Traditional Arab Sweets|last=Salloum|first=Habeeb|date=2013-06-25|publisher=I.B.Tauris|isbn=978-0-85773-341-2|language=en}} < /ref > < ref name= " :0 "  / >  It is also mentioned in the ''[[One Thousand and One Nights]]'', in the story ''[[The Porter and the Three Ladies of Baghdad]]''. < ref name= " :1 "  / >
