 '''''Typha''''' is a [[genus]] of plants. They are found in [[wetland]] [[habitats]]. They are commonly found in the [[Northern Hemisphere]], but may be found in wetlands elsewhere. These plants are known in [[British English]] as '''bulrush''', '''bullrush''' or '''reedmace''', < ref > ''Collins Complete British Wildlife'' < /ref >  and in [[American English]] as '''cattail''' or '''punks'''. 
 
 == Species == 
 * ''[[Typha angustifolia]]'' - Lesser Bulrush or Narrow Leaf Cattail 
 * ''Typha angustifolia x T. latifolia'' - Hybrid or White Cattail  
 * ''[[Typha domingensis]]'' - Southern Cattail 
 * ''[[Typha latifolia]]'' - Common Cattail 
 * ''[[Typha laxmannii]]'' - Laxman's Bulrush 
 * ''[[Typha minima]]'' - Dwarf Bulrush 
 * ''[[Typha orientalis]]'' and ''[[Typha muelleri]]'' < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.esperanto.org.nz/anzed/r.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2008-04-27 |archive-date=2008-10-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081014025518/http://www.esperanto.org.nz/anzed/r.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  - Raupo 
 * ''[[Typha shuttleworthii]]'' - Shuttleworth's Bulrush 
 
 == Uses by humans == 
 Cattails were once used to make paper. Cattail paper is very heavy and coarse, and is rarely used today. Cattail has also been used as a water filter to remove the poisonous chemical [[arsenic]] from drinking water.
