He was best known for promoting fusion music in [[South India]]. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.musicindiaonline.com/n/i/top_stories/1227/ |title=MusicIndiaOnline: Get drenched with the new form of love |access-date=13 January 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100211133354/http://www.musicindiaonline.com/n/i/top_stories/1227/ |archive-date=11 February 2010 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }} < /ref > < ref name=FusionMusic > {{Cite web |url=http://www.hindu.com/fr/2007/02/02/stories/2007020200870300.htm |title=Fusion Music |access-date=2018-10-02 |archive-date=2007-02-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070204054655/http://www.hindu.com/fr/2007/02/02/stories/2007020200870300.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 He had composed the soundtrack for the [[Malayalam]] movie ''[[Mangalya Pallakku]]'', and was the youngest music composer to have worked in the industry. < ref name=FusionMusic/ >  His compositions for the albums ''Ninakaai'' and ''Aadyamai'' are still among the most-sought-after romantic song collections. 
 
 He won the [[Sangeet Natak Akademi#Ustad Bismillah Khan Yuva Puruskar|Bismillah Khan Yuva Sangeetkaar Puraskaar]] < ref > {{Cite web |title=About Bismillah Khan Yuva Sangeetkaar Puraskaar |url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/2009/06/04/stories/2009060456312200.htm |access-date=2018-10-02 |archive-date=2011-06-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606101644/http://www.hinduonnet.com/2009/06/04/stories/2009060456312200.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  in 2008.
