 '''Diomedes''' ({{lang-el|Διομήδης}}, ''Diomēdēs'' -  " God-like cunning, advised by Zeus " ) was an ancient Greek hero and one of the central figures of the ''[[Iliad]]''. Son of King [[Tydeus]] and Queen Deipyle, he was the leader of the [[Argos|Argive]] contingent during the [[Trojan War]]. Diomedes was, alongside [[Ajax the Great|Greater Ajax]], regarded as one of the greatest of all the Achaean warriors, second only to [[Achilles]] in terms of prowess.
