'''Dialetheism''' is the [[Wikt:view|view]] that some statements can be both true and false at the same time. < ref name=Stanford > {{cite web |url=http://plato.stanford.edu/entries/dialetheism/ |title=Dialetheism |publisher=Metaphysics Research Lab |access-date=7 March 2015}} < /ref >  It is the opposite of the [[law of noncontradiction]] (LNC). Because of this dialetheism is completely opposite of what most [[philosopher]]s call [[common sense]]. < ref name=Stanford/ >  [[Graham Priest]] [[wikt:define|define]]s it as believing there can be true [[wikt:contradiction|contradiction]]s. < ref > Whittle, Bruno.  " Dialetheism, logical consequence and hierarchy. "  ''Analysis'' Vol. 64 Issue 4 (2004): 318-326. < /ref >  Priest gives as an [[example]] the [[liar paradox]] (or liar's paradox). < ref > {{cite web |url=http://opinionator.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/11/28/paradoxical-truth/?_r=0 |title=Paradoxical Truth |author=Priest, Graham |date=28 November 2010 |publisher=The New York Times |access-date=7 March 2015}} < /ref >  If someone says  " everything I say is a lie " , then is the statement true or false? In this [[paradox]] it can be both.
