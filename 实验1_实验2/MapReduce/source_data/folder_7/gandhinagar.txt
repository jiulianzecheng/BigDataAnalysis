 '''Gandhinagar''' is the [[wikt:capital|capital]] city of [[Gujarat]] state in [[India]]. It is {{convert|23|km|abbr=off}} from the city of [[Ahmedabad]] and 464 & nbsp;km from [[Mumbai]]. In the year 1960, the Bombay state of India was divided into two states - [[Maharashtra]] and [[Gujarat]]. Bombay (now called [[Mumbai]]) became the capital city of Maharashtra. For Gujarat, new capital was needed. Gandhinagar was then made the capital of Gujarat. < ref name= " Kalia2004 " > {{cite book|author=Ravi Kalia|title=Gandhinagar: Building National Identity in Postcolonial India|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RVhNO2MwOCAC & pg=PA4|accessdate=7 July 2012|year=2004|publisher=Univ of South Carolina Press|isbn=978-1-57003-544-9|pages=4–}} < /ref >   
 
 The famous [[Akshardham temple]] is in this city. This temple was attacked by two terrorist in the year 2002. < ref name= " A.Nirmal2009 " > {{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8EqWnqdsgZMC & pg=PA180|title=Urban Terrorism: Myths and Realities|author=N. C. Asthana  &  A.Nirmal|date=1 January 2009|publisher=Pointer Publishers|isbn=978-81-7132-598-6|pages=180–|accessdate=7 July 2012}} < /ref >
