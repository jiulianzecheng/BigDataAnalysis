 '''Privas''' is a [[communes of France|commune]] of France, [[Capital city|capital]] ([[Prefectures in France|Prefecture]]) of the [[Ardèche]] [[Departments of France|department]] of the [[Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes]] [[Regions of France|region]] in southeastern France. It is the smallest administrative centre of any department in France. 
 
 Today Privas is known for several [[confectionery]] made with [[chestnut]]s produced in the region, as the ''marron glacé''. 
 
 ==History== 
 When the department of Ardèche was created on 4 March 1790, the [[Capital city|capital]] was Privas alternating with Annonay, Aubenas, Le Bourg(-Saint-Andéol) and Tournon. In September of that year, Privas became the only capital of the department. < ref name=splaf > {{cite web |url=http://splaf.free.fr/07his.html |title=Historique de l'Ardèche |work=Le SPLAF |language=French |accessdate=27 September 2017 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 == Geography == 
 The ''commune'' of Privas is in the central eastern part of the Ardèche department, in the [[valley]] of the ''Ouvèze'' [[river]], a [[tributary]] of the [[Rhône River|Rhône]] river. The ''commune'' of Privas is in the centre of a region known as the  " Bassin Privadois " .

 ==Population== 
 The inhabitants of Privas are known, in [[French language|French]], as ''Privadois'' (women: ''Privadoises''). < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.habitants.fr/ardeche-07 |title=Ardèche |publisher=habitants.fr |language=French |accessdate=27 September 2017 |date= }} < /ref >

 == Administration == 
 Privas is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Ardèche department since 1800. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Privas|Privas]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of the canton [[Canton of Privas|Privas]], formed by 15 ''communes'', including Privas, with 20,402 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > . 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Privas Centre Ardèche'', officially named as ''Communauté d'agglomération Privas Centre Ardèche''.
