 '''Cellulitis''' is an [[infection]] of the [[skin]], caused by [[bacteria]]. < ref name=pubmed > {{cite web |url= https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmedhealth/PMH0001858/ |title=Cellulitis - PubMed Health |work=ncbi.nlm.nih.gov |date=13 May 2011 |quote=Staphylococcus and streptococcus |access-date=7 October 2012}} < /ref >  Very often, the [[face]] is affected. The disease should not be confused with [[cellulite]]. Cellulitis is caused by bacteria, usually [[staphylococcus]] or [[streptococcus]], which can get through a break in the skin. < ref name=pubmed/ >  These breaks can be caused by a [[wound]], cracks in the skin, [[insect]] bites, or problems with the bodies [[immune system]]. The infection causes the skin to become red, hot and painful. It can also cause a [[fever]]. It is usually treated by taking [[antibiotic]] drugs. < ref name=pubmed/ >
