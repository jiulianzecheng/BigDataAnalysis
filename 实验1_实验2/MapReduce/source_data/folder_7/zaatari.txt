 '''Zaatari refugee camp''' is a [[refugee camp]]. It is located in [[Jordan]], about {{Convert|6|km||abbr=}} from the [[Syria|Syrian]] border.  It is one of the world's largest refugee camps. < ref name= " NYT7514 " > [https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/05/world/middleeast/zaatari-refugee-camp-in-jordan-evolves-as-a-do-it-yourself-city.html  " Refugee Camp for Syrians in Jordan Evolves as a Do-It-Yourself City " ]  " Refugee Camp for Syrians in Jordan Evolves as a Do-It-Yourself City " ]. by Michael Kimmelman in ''[[The New York Times]]'' 4 July 2014 < /ref >  it is the world's largest camp for Syrian refugees. < ref > {{cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/world/middle-east/inside-the-world-s-largest-camp-for-syrian-refugees-1.3018821|title=Inside the world's largest camp for Syrian refugees|last=Hayden|first=Sally|date=22 March 2017|website=Irish Times|access-date=16 December 2018}} < /ref >  It developed into a permanent setltement, with a main street, and a [[market]] along the main street. In August 2015, UNHCR estimated a population of 79.900 people. < ref name= " UNHCR data Portal " > {{cite web|url=http://data.unhcr.org/syrianrefugees/settlement.php?id=176 & country=107 & region=77|title=UNHCR data Portal|author=United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)|work=UNHCR Syria Regional Refugee Response|access-date=20 November 2014|archive-date=8 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151008062605/http://data.unhcr.org/syrianrefugees/settlement.php?id=176 & country=107 & region=77|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The camp covers an  area of about {{Convert|15|km2||abbr=}}.  Over time, Zaatari  has grown to be Jordan's fourth-largest settlement.
