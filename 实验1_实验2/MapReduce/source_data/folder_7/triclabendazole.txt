 '''Triclabendazole''', sold with the name '''Egaten''' or '''Fasinex''', is a medicine for treating [[fascioliasis]] and [[paragonimiasis]], which are [[Parasitism|parasitic infections]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite journal|last1=World Health Organization|last2=Stuart|first2=Marc C.|last3=Kouimtzi|first3=Maria|last4=Hill|first4=Suzanne|date=2009|title=WHO model formulary 2008|hdl=10665/44053 |url=https://apps.who.int/iris/handle/10665/44053|language=en}} < /ref >  It is good at treating both [[Disease|diseases]]. It is taken by mouth. Usually, one or two doses are needed. < ref name= " :0 "  / >   
 
 There usually are not any side effects from taking triclabendazole. However, people taking triclabendazole can have [[Headache|headaches]] and [[Abdominal pain|abdominal]] pain. No danger has been found for using triclabendazole while [[Pregnancy|pregnant]], but it has not been studied well. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sUKMAgAAQBAJ & pg=PT173|title=Pocket Guide to GastrointestinaI Drugs|last1=Wolfe|first1=M. Michael|last2=Lowe|first2=Robert C.|date=2014-01-02|publisher=John Wiley  &  Sons|isbn=978-1-118-48155-4|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The [[United States]] let people start using triclabendazole in 2019. It is also used for animals. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Triclabendazole|url=https://www.drugs.com/international/triclabendazole.html|access-date=2022-09-21|website=Drugs.com|language=en}} < /ref >
