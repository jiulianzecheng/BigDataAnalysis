 ==Life of Sekkizhar== 
 Sekkizhar was born in the [[village]] of Kundrathur in [[Kanchipuram district|Kancheepuram district]] in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[Tamil Nadu]]. < ref name=Lal3904/ >  He was a scholar in [[vedas]] as well as [[Tirumurai]] (later his work was also been added as a tirumurai). His real name is Ramadevan. He was an inmate in a [[Vaishnavi (Matrika goddess)|Vaishnavite shrine]] near [[Kanchipuram]]. He started his works of ''Periya Puranam'' when he was in [[Chidambaram]]. < ref name= " ADictionaryofIndianLiterature " > A Dictionary of Indian Literature By Sujit Mukherjee < /ref >  Among all [[Puranas]] in [[Tamil language|Tamil]], Sekkizhar's Periya Puranam stands first.{{fact|date=December 2016}} [[Kulothunga Chola II]] began to [[wikt:restore|restore]] Tamil Shaivism that was begun by his [[ancestor]]s because he was a great [[wikt:devote|devote]]e of [[Nataraja|Lord Nataraja]] (Shiva) at [[Chidambaram]]. The king called upon Sekkizhar to compose a [[poem]] on the life of Shaiva [[saint]]s. 
 
 In Tamil [[literature]], Periya Puranam, the great poem works of Sekkizhar is considered the [[fifth veda]]. It had also took place as the twelfth tirumarai or [[Shiva]] [[Canon law|canon]]. < ref name= " TheHomeofDancingŚivan̲ " > The Home of Dancing Śivan̲ By Paul Younger < /ref >
