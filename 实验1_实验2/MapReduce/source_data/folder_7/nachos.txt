 '''Nachos''' are a [[snack]] food made with [[tortilla]] chips and [[cheese]]. Sometimes they have [[jalapeño]]s, [[sauce]], beans, guacamole or [[meat]] on them. 
 [[File:Nacho plate.jpg|thumb|Nacho plate]] 
 Ignacio  " Nacho "  Anaya created nachos at the Victory Club in 1940 when Mamie Finan, a regular customer, asked if Anaya could bring her and three other women a different snack than usual. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite news|title=Nacho's? Natch!|last=LaRoche|first=Clarence J.|date=1954|work=San Antonio Express and San Antonio News}} < /ref > < ref name= " :1 " > {{Cite book|title=Nacho's Nachos: The Story Behind the World's Favorite Snack|url=https://archive.org/details/nachosnachosstor0000nick|last=Nickel|first=Sandra and Oliver Dominguez|publisher=Lee and Low|year=2020|isbn=9781620143698}} < /ref >  Anaya went to the kitchen and spotted freshly fried pieces of corn tortillas. In a moment of culinary inspiration, he added melted cheese and pickled jalapeño strips. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref name= " :1 "  / >  After tasting the snack Anaya created, Finan asked what it was called. Anaya responded,  " Well, I guess we can just call them Nacho's Special. " < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref name= " :1 "  / >  The dish was so popular, the owner of the Victory Club, Roberto de los Santos, put Nacho's Special on the menu and the snack quickly spread throughout the world. < ref name= " :1 "  / >  When the Victory Club closed in 1961, Anaya opened his own restaurant, Nacho's. < ref name= " :1 "  / >
