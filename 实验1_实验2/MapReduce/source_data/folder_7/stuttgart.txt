 '''Stuttgart''' ({{IPA-de|ˈʃtʊtɡaʁt|lang|Stuttgart.ogg}}; [[Swabian German|Swabian]]: {{lang|swg|Schduagert|italics=yes}} {{IPA-gsw|ˈʒ̊d̥ua̯ɡ̊ɛʕd̥|}}) is a [[city]] in [[Germany]]. It is the capital of the [[States of Germany|state]] of [[Baden-Württemberg]] and of the [[Administrative District of Stuttgart]] (''Regierungsbezirk''). Stuttgart is on the river [[Neckar]]. In December 2011, 613,392 people lived there. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://www.statistik.baden-wuerttemberg.de/Veroeffentl/Statistische_Berichte/3126_11001.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-05-10 |archive-date=2013-05-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130504163001/http://www.statistik.baden-wuerttemberg.de/Veroeffentl/Statistische_Berichte/3126_11001.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  This makes it the most populous city in Baden-Württemberg. < ref > https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg_by_population < /ref > 
 
 Stuttgart is the sixth largest German city < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Germany_by_population|title = List of cities in Germany by population}} < /ref >  and Stuttgart Region is the nation's third largest [[region]]. The city is divided into 23 city [[district]]s. 
 
 Stuttgart is the home of two [[car]] manufacturers: [[Mercedes Benz]] and [[Porsche]]. Porsche is in Zuffenhausen (North) and Mercedes in Untertürkheim (Southeast). 
 
 Stuttgart has an [[oceanic climate]] (''Cfb'' in the [[Koeppen climate classification]]). 
 
 == Population == 
 Stuttgart has 613,392 people within city limits. It is the 6th largest city in Germany after [[Berlin]], [[Hamburg]], [[Munich]], [[Cologne]] and [[Frankfurt am Main]]. According to a 2005 estimate, the Stuttgart urban area had between 1,238,000 and 1,250,000 people. Its [[metropolitan area]] has a [[population]] of 5.3 & nbsp;million (2008). < ref name= " pop_metro "  / > 
 
 == Education == 
 The [[University of Stuttgart]] is one of the city's higher education institutions.
