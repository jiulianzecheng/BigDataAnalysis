 '''''Procoptodon''''' is a [[genus]] of giant short-faced [[kangaroo]] living in [[Australia]] during the [[Pleistocene]] epoch. < ref name=Mikko > {{cite web | author = Haaramo, M. | date = 2004-12-20 | access-date = 2007-03-15 | title = Mikko's Phylogeny Archive: Macropodidae - kenguroos | url = http://www.fmnh.helsinki.fi/users/haaramo/Metazoa/Deuterostoma/Chordata/Synapsida/Metatheria/Notometatheria/Diprotodontia/Macropodidae.htm | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070331021022/http://www.fmnh.helsinki.fi/users/haaramo/metazoa/Deuterostoma/chordata/Synapsida/Metatheria/Notometatheria/Diprotodontia/Macropodidae.htm | archive-date = 2007-03-31 }} < /ref > 
 
 ''P. goliah''  < !-- not  " goliath "  -- >  was the largest kangaroo known to have  existed. It stood about {{convert|2|m|ft|1|abbr=on}} tall. < ref name=AustrlianMuseum > {{cite web |url= http://australianmuseum.net.au/Procoptodon-goliah/ |title=Procoptodon goliah|publisher=Australian Museum |access-date=2012-03-22}} < /ref >  They weighed about {{convert|200|-|240|kg|abbr=on}}. Other members of the genus were smaller. ''Procoptodon gilli'' was the smallest of the genus, only about {{cvt|1|m}} tall. 
 
 They were [[browsers]], not [[grazers]] like kangaroos today. They ate [[leaves]] from [[trees]] and [[shrubs]]. Their weight makes it likely that they did not hop as their relatives do today. < ref > Janis CM; Buttrill K.  &  Figueirido B. 2014. Locomotion in extinct giant kangaroos: were sthenurines hop-less monsters? " . ''PLoS ONE''. '''9''' (10): e109888. [http://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0109888] < /ref >  A combination of [[climate change]] (cooling and shrinking of forest areas) would have reduced their natural [[habitat]]. [[Predation]] (hunting) by aboriginal humans probably happened. < ref name=Prideaux2009 > Prideaux G.J. ''et al''  2009.
