 " '''{{lang|no|Kongesangen|italic=no}}''' "  ({{small|say: {{respell|KONG-ə-SAHNG-ən}}}}; meaning  " The King's Song " ) is [[Norway]]'s [[royal anthem]]. < ref name= " Lennox562 " > Doug Lennox, ''Now You Know Royalty'' (Toronto: Dundurn Press, 2009), p. 56 < /ref >  It is based on the [[England|English]] [[wikt:royal|royal]] song  " God Save the King "  from 1741. < ref name= " “NL” " > {{Cite encyclopedia|title=Kongesangen|date=2022-12-30|accessdate=2023-05-07|language=no|url=https://snl.no/Kongesangen|last=Roede|first=Lars|encyclopedia=[[Great Norwegian Encyclopedia|Store norske leksikon]]}} < /ref >  The Norwegian [[author]] Henrik Wergeland wrote a Norwegian [[wikt:lyric|lyric]] to the English song in 1841 called  " Gud signe Kongen vor "  It was later [[wikt:rewrite|rewritten]] by P. Vogtmann under the name  " Gud sign vor Konge god " . A [[wikt:simplified|simplified]] version of this was later written by Gustav Jensen. < ref name= " NL " > {{cite web|title=Kongesangen|url=https://snl.no/Kongesangen|access-date=28 August 2015|publisher=Store norske leksikon (2005 - 2007}} < /ref >

 alltid vårt Norge lød

 | < poem > '''I''' 
 [[God]] bless our good [[Monarchy of Norway|king]]! 
 Bless him with strength and courage 
 bless [[Royal Palace, Oslo|home]] and castle! 
 (𝄆) Guide him with your [[Holy Spirit|Spirit]], 
 forge with your strong Hand 
 holy bonds of allegiance 
 around [[Norwegians|people]] and king! (𝄇) 
 
 '''II''' 
 Loudly pledge men of [[Norway]] 
 each in his calling, his station, 
 loyalty to his king. 
 (𝄆) Loyal in life and death, 
 courageous in [[List of wars involving Norway|war]] and distress, 
 always our Norway obeyed 
 God and its king. (𝄇) < /poem >
