'''Murrurundi''' is a small country town in the Upper [[Hunter Valley]] of [[New South Wales]]. Murrurundi is {{convert|193|km|mi|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} by road from [[Newcastle, New South Wales|Newcastle]] and {{convert|309|km|mi|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} km from [[Sydney]]. In 2006 there were 2162 people ([[Australian Bureau of Statistics|ABS]] 2006) living in Murrurundi. The town is circled by mountains of the [[Liverpool Range]]. It is on the [[Pages River]], which flows into the [[Hunter River]]. The name ''Murrurundi'' is from an [[Indigenous Australian|Aboriginal]] word meaning  " Five fingers " . This is the name given to a group of rocks at the north end of the town. 
 
 ==History== 
 The first people to live in the Murrurundi area were the [[Indigenous Australian|Australian aboriginal]] people, the [[Wanaruah]] and the [[Kamilaroi]]. [[Europe]]an settlement of the area began in the 1820s. Benjamin Hall, the father of [[bushranger]] [[Ben Hall]] started a cattle farm in the area in 1839. The town was started by the New South Wales government in [[1840]].  In the same year, a local land owner - Thomas Haydon - started his own town called Haydonton. Benjamin Hall opened a [[wikt:butcher|butcher's]] shop in Haydonton in 1842. Ben Hall lived here until the age of 13. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.theage.com.au/travel/travel-factsheet/murrurundi--places-to-see-20081125-6gnd.html |title=Guide to Murrindi in NSW |accessdate=2008-11-26 |format= |work=The Age Traveller |archive-date=2012-11-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121102204306/http://www.theage.com.au/travel/travel-factsheet/murrurundi--places-to-see-20081125-6gnd.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  In [[1913]], the two towns were joined together to make the modern-day town of Murrurundi. 
 
 ==Mining== 
 [[Kerosene]] shale was first mined at Mount Temi, {{convert|11|km|mi|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} north of Murrurundi in 1886. The mine was reopened in 1910. The shale was moved {{convert|10|km|mi|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} from the mine on a rope system known as an aerial ropeway. It was then loaded onto [[Railway|trains]] at a [[railway]] built at Temple Court, just west of Murrurundi. The mine closed in the [[First World War]]. < ref > '''''The British-Australian Oil Company's Railway''''' Eardley, Gifford [[Australian Railway History|Australian Railway Historical Society Bulletin]], August, 1967 < /ref > 
 
 ==Sport== 
 Every year there is a [[rodeo]] and the King of the Ranges stockman ([[cowboy]]) contest. Other sports played in the town include [[Rugby football|rugby]], touch [[football]], [[swimming]], and [[cricket]]. 
 
 ==Railway station== 
 Murrurundi railway station is on the [[Main North railway line, New South Wales|Main North railway line]], {{convert|352|km|mi|0|lk=on|abbr=on}} from Sydney. The station opened in 1872. There is a large brick station-house, a passing loop and goods yard. < ref > [http://www.nswrail.net/locations/show.php?name=NSW:Murrurundi & line=NSW:main_north:0 Murrurundi railway Station] Accessed 1 April 2008. < /ref > 
 There is a train to and from [[Sydney]] and [[Armidale, New South Wales|Armidale]]/[[Moree, New South Wales|Moree]] every day.
