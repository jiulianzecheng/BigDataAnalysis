'''Speciesism''' is thinking that [[human]]s are better than [[animal]]s (although humans are technically also animals). It is also treating animals worse than humans. For example, humans often [[kill]] animals to [[meat eating|eat]]. Humans rarely [[Cannibalism|kill and eat other humans]]. The word 'speciesism' was first used in 1970 by [[Richard D. Ryder]], an English [[philosopher]], in a [[pamphlet]] called  " Speciesism " . It was made popular by [[Peter Singer]]'s 1975 book ''Animal Liberation''. In the book the name of the fifth chapter was  " Man's Dominion ... a short history of speciesism " . The word 'speciesism' was first put in the ''[[Oxford English Dictionary]]'' in 1985.
