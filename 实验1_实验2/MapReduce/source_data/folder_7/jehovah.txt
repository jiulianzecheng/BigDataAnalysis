 The majority of scholars agree that the pronunciation of the Tetragrammaton is possibly—but most likely—[[Yahweh]]. The pronunciation of this name was simply lost because in [[Second Temple Judaism]] during the 3rd to 2nd centuries [[BCE]], saying this name began to be discouraged because it was  " too holy " . So instead of saying YHWH, [[Jew|Jews]] used other ways to refer to their [[God in Judaism|God,]] like ''Adonai''.
