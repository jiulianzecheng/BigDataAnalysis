 '''Dhritarashtra''' ([[Sanskrit]]: धृतराष्ट्र, ''Dhṛtarāṣṭra'') or Dhatarattha ([[Pali]]: धतरट्ठ  ''Dhataraṭṭha'') is one of the [[Four Heavenly Kings]] and an important person in [[Buddhism]].  
 ==Name== 
 The name Dhṛtarāṣṭra comes from two Sanskrit words:  " dhṛta "  (possessing; bearing) and  " rāṣṭra "  (kingdom; territory). < ref > {{cite web |title=Dhṛtarāṣṭra |url=https://www.wisdomlib.org/definition/dhritarashtra#buddhism |website=Wisdom Library |access-date=2019-02-20}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Character== 
 Dhṛtarāṣṭra is the guardian god of the East. He lives on the eastern part of Mount Sumeru. He is leader of the [[gandharva]]s and the [[pishaca]]s. 
 
 He is often shown in art playing a stringed instrument. 
 
 ==Theravada== 
 In the [[Pali|Pāli]] writings of the [[Theravada]] Buddhist tradition, Dhṛtarāṣṭra is called ''Dhataraṭṭha''. Dhataraṭṭha is one of the Four Great Kings, each one of which rules over a direction. Vessavaṇa's direction is the eastern quarter of the world.  
 
 He has many sons called  " Indra. "  He also has a daughter named ''Sirī''. < ref > {{cite web |title=Dhatarattha |url=http://www.palikanon.com/english/pali_names/d/dhatarattha.htm |website=Buddhist Dictionary of Pali Proper Names |access-date=2019-02-20}} < /ref > 
 
 ==Japan== 
 In Japan, '''Jikokuten''' (持国天) is often shown wearing a suit of armor. He usually holds a sword and a trident while standing on top of a demon called a ''jaki''. < ref > {{cite web |title=Jikokuten 持国天 |url=http://www.aisf.or.jp/~jaanus/ |website=JAANUS |access-date=2019-02-20}} < /ref >
