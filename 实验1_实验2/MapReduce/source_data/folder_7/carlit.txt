 '''Pic Carlit''' ({{lang-fr|Pic Carlit}}; {{lang-ca|Puig Carlit}}) is a mountain of the [[France|French]] [[Pyrenees]] and has an [[elevation]] of {{Convert|2921|m|ft|0|abbr=on}} above sea level. < ref name=pbag > {{cite web |url=http://www.peakbagger.com/peak.aspx?pid=9817|title=Pic Carlit, France |publisher=Peakbagger.com |access-date=7 July 2013 }} < /ref > 
 
 It is the highest point of the [[Departments of France|department]] of [[Pyrénées-Orientales]] and of the [[Regions of France|region]] [[Languedoc-Roussillon]], [[France]]. 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Pic Carlit is in the nortwestern part of the Regional Natural Park of the Catalan Pyrenees ({{lang-fr|Parc naturel régional des Pyrénées catalanes}}; {{lang-ca|Parc Natural Regional del Pirineu Català}}, often shortened as ''Parc del Pirineu Català''). 
 
 There are many small [[lake]]s in the region around the mountain. One of them is the man-made lake of Bouillouses, which is one of the point to begin [[hiking]] to get to the top of Pic Carlit. 
 
 The [[Source (river or stream)|source]] of [[Têt (river)|Têt]] (the largest river of the department Pyrénées-Orientales) is on the northeastern side of the mountain. 
 
 ==History== 
 The first ascent of this peak was made by [[Henry Russell]] in 1864. < ref name=korpa > {{cite web |url=http://www.korpa.fr/russell/premierrussell.html |title=Les  " premières "  de Russell |publisher=Henry Russell-Killoug - Comte des Pyrénées |language=fr |access-date=7 July 2013 }} < /ref >
