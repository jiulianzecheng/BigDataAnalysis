'''Remake''' means to [[create]] something once again. It usually means a [[movie]], [[Television program|television series]] or [[video game]]. A remake tells the same story as the original but uses a different group of actors. It may change the theme or target audience. < ref > {{cite news|last=Kepler|first=Adam W.|title=In Hollywood, It's a Reboot by Any Other Name|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/07/26/movies/in-hollywood-its-a-reboot-by-any-other-name.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=July 22, 2015|access-date=April 20, 2016}} < /ref >   
 
 A remake may change some parts of the [[Narrative|plot]]. For example, the 1968 movie ''[[The Thomas Crown Affair (1968 movie)|The Thomas Crown Affair]]'' is centered on a bank robbery. The [[The Thomas Crown Affair (1999 movie)|1999 remake]] involves the theft of a valuable painting.
