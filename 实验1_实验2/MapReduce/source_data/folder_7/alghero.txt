 '''Alghero''' (Italian pronunciation: [alˈɡɛro]; {{lang-ca|L'Alguer}}, IPA: [ɫəɫˈɣe], locally: [lalˈɣe]; {{lang-sc|S'Alighèra}}; {{lang-sdc|La Liéra}}), is a city of about 44,000 citizens in [[Italy]]. It lies in the [[Provinces of Italy|province]] of [[Province of Sassari|Sassari]] in northwestern [[Sardinia]], next to the sea. 
 
 In the 1930s, the Swedish writer [[Amelie Posse Brazdova]] wrote a book entitled ''Sardinia Side Show'', where she told the complete story of 2 years she spent  " interned "  in Alghero old town during [[World War I]].
