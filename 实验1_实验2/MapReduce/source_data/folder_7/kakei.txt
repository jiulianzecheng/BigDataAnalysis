'''Kakei''' (嘉慶) was a [[Japanese era name]] (年号, ''nengō'', lit. year name) of the [[Northern Court]] during the [[Nanboku-chō period]] after [[Shitoku]] and before [[Kōō (Nanboku-chō period)|Kōō]].  This period started in August 1387 and ended in February 1389. < ref > Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA455  " ''Kakei'' " ] in ''Japan encyclopedia'', p. 455. < /ref >   The [[pretender]] in [[Kyoto]] was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Komatsu]]|後小松天皇|''Go-Komatsu-tennō''}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA255  " Go-Komatsu Tennō, " ] p. 255; [[Isaac Titsingh|Titsingh, Isaac]]. (1834).  [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA310 ''Annales des empereurs du japon'', pp. 317-318.] < /ref >   Go-Komatsu's [[Southern Court]] rival in [[Yoshino, Nara|Yoshino]] during this time was {{nihongo|[[Emperor Go-Kameyama]]|後亀山天皇|''Go-Kameyama-tennō}}. < ref > Nussbaum, [https://books.google.com/books?id=p2QnPijAEmEC & pg=PA254  " Go-Kameyama Tennō, " ] pp. 254-255. < /ref > 
 
 ==Events of the Kakei era== 
 * '''1387''' (''Kakei 1, 1st month''): [[Nijō Yoshimoto]] is removed from his position as ''[[sesshō]]'' and ''[[daijō daijin]]'' in the Imperial court [[hierarchy]]. < ref name= " titsingh318 " > Titsingh, [https://books.google.com/books?id=18oNAAAAIAAJ & pg=PA318 p. 318]. < /ref > 
 * '''1387''' (''Kakei 1, 2nd month''): [[Konoe Kanetsugu]] is named ''sesshō''. < ref name= " titsingh318 " / > 
 * '''1388''' (''Kakei 2, 3rd month''): The ''sesshō'' [[Konoe Kanetsugu]] dies at age 29; and Yoshimoto re-assumes this role. < ref name= " titsingh318 " / > 
 * '''1388''' (''Kakei 2, 6th month''): Yoshitomo dies at age 69; and his son [[Nijō Morotsugu]] succeeds him with the title of ''[[kampaku]].'' < ref name= " titsingh318 " / > 
 * '''1389''' (''Kakei 3''): Yoshimitsu pacifies Kyūshū and distributes lands. < ref > Ackroyd, Joyce. (1982) ''Lessons from History: The  " Tokushi Yoron " '', p. 329. < /ref >
