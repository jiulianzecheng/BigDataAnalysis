 '''Thionville''' ([[Luxembourgish language|Luxembourgish]]: ''Diddenuewen'', [[German language|German]]: ''Diedenhofen'') is a [[city]] in northeast [[France]], close to the [[Luxembourg|Luxembourgian]] and [[Germany|German]] border. It is on the left side of the [[Moselle River]]. About 41,000 people were living there in 2014. 
 ==History== 
 Until [[1659]] Thionville was part of the [[Holy Roman Empire]] (as a part of [[Luxembourg]] till [[1462]]). From [[1871]] to [[1919]] it was part of the [[German Empire]], and from 1940 to [[1944]] part of [[Nazi Germany]].
