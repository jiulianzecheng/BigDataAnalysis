 '''Copra''' is the dried inside of coconuts, the fruit of the [[Coconut palm|coconut]] palm. This is usually a white substance, which is high in [[vegetable oil]]. Usually, it is processed into [[coconut oil]]. The name comes from [[Malayalam]]: In Malayalam, ''koppara'' meams dried coconut. What is left after the oil has been extracted is known as coconut cake, and used as food for [[livestock]].
