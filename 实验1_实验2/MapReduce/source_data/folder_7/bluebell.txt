 '''Bluebells''' are a [[genus]] of [[flowering plant]]s in the family [[Asparagaceae]], '''''Hyacinthoides'''''. 
 
 Bluebells are famous as indicator species (markers) for [[ancient woodland]], where they carpet the forest floor in springtime. They grow in shade rather than in sunny areas. 
 
 ''Hyacinthoides'' is differentiated by the presence of two [[bract]]s at the base of each flower, rather than one bract per flower or no bracts in other closely-related genera. < ref name= " Stace " > {{cite book |author= Stace, Clive A. 2010 |title=New Flora of the British Isles |year= 2010 |url= https://archive.org/details/newflorabritishi00stac |edition=3rd |publisher=Cambridge University Press |location=Cambridge |isbn=978-0-521-70772-5 |chapter=Asparagaceae – asparagus family |pages=[https://archive.org/details/newflorabritishi00stac/page/914 914]–923}} < /ref >
