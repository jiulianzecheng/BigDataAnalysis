'''MyWikiBiz''' is a [[wiki]] directory that lets people write about themselves. It began as a service creating [[Wikipedia]] articles for paying [[corporation]]s. The site was founded by Gregory Kohs, a market researcher. < ref name= " chron " > {{cite web|url=http://chronicle.com/wiredcampus/article/1831/wikipedia-blocks-a-pay-for-play-scheme|title=Wikipedia Blocks a Pay-for-Play Scheme|work=The Chronicle of Higher Education|date=2007-01-24|last=Read|first=Brock|accessdate=2009-11-22|archive-date=2009-04-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090404044911/http://chronicle.com/wiredcampus/article/1831/wikipedia-blocks-a-pay-for-play-scheme|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Gregory Kohs and his sister started the MyWikiBiz venture in [[Pennsylvania]] in July 2006, first as a paid editing service, writing content for [[Wikipedia]] and other  sites. < ref name= " pr " > {{cite web|url=http://www.pr.com/press-release/15184|title=MyWikiBiz press release: Wikipedia - Open For Business|publisher=PR.com|date=2006-08-08|accessdate=2015-10-18|author=MyWikiBiz.com}} < /ref >   The legitimacy of the idea was supported by the English Wikipediaâ€™s Reward Board, where interested parties would offer cash rewards or gifts to create or improve Wikipedia articles.
