 '''Shepparton''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|ʃ|ɛ|p|ɚ|t|ən}}) < ref > ''[[Macquarie Dictionary|Macquarie Dictionary, Fourth Edition]]'' (2005). Melbourne, The Macquarie Library Pty Ltd. {{ISBN|1-876429-14-3}} < /ref >  is a city on the [[floodplain]] of the [[Goulburn River (Victoria)|Goulburn River]] in the north east of [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], Australia. It is about {{convert|181|km|mi}} north-east of Melbourne. In 2006 there were 27,707 living in the city. 
 
 Shepparton began as a sheep [[Station (Australian agriculture)|station]] and river crossing. It later became an important [[railway town]]. Today it is an agricultural and manufacturing city. It is at the centre of the Goulburn Valley [[irrigation]] system, one of the largest centres of [[irrigation in Australia]]. It is a major regional service city and is the civic administration centre for the [[City of Greater Shepparton]]. The Greater Shepparton area includes the towns of [[Tatura, Victoria|Tatura]], [[Merrigum, Victoria|Merrigum]], [[Mooroopna, Victoria|Mooroopna]], [[Murchison, Victoria|Murchison]], [[Dookie, Victoria|Dookie]], and [[Grahamvale, Victoria|Grahamvale]].
