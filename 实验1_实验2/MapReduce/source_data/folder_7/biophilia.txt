 '''''Biophilia''''' is the musical project and seventh studio album from [[Iceland]]ic singer [[Björk]]. < ref name=Perpetua > {{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/bjoerk-to-debut-new-biophilia-songs-at-residency-in-england-20110317|title=Björk to Debut New 'Biophilia' Songs at Residency in England|first=Matthew|last=Perpetua|date=March 17, 2011|access-date=March 17, 2011|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|archive-date=March 20, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110320060703/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/bjoerk-to-debut-new-biophilia-songs-at-residency-in-england-20110317|url-status=dead}} < /ref > < ref name=Spicer > {{cite journal|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2011/01/bjork-song-used-in-solar-system-ipad-app.html|title=Björk Song Used in Solar System iPad App|first=Nathan|last=Spicer|date=January 6, 2011|access-date=March 17, 2011|journal=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]|publisher=Paste Media Group|archive-date=April 8, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110408212839/http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2011/01/bjork-song-used-in-solar-system-ipad-app.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The album was released in 2011. < ref name=Perpetua/ > 
 
 The album is  " partly recorded "  on an [[iPad]] and was released in the form of a series of apps. < ref > {{cite journal|url=http://www.nme.com/news/bjork/55508|title=Bjork, Damon Albarn, Snoop Dogg for Manchester International Festival|date=March 17, 2011|access-date=March 17, 2011|journal=[[NME]]|publisher=IPC Media}} < /ref >  The project is directed by [[Michel Gondry]]. < ref name=Spicer/ >  Björk has described the project as a [[multimedia]] collection  " encompassing music, apps, internet, installations and live shows " . < ref name=COS > {{Cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2011/03/bjork-readies-ipad-album-biophilia/|title=Björk readies iPad album, Biophilia|date=March 17, 2011|access-date=March 17, 2011|first=Alex|last=Young|publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]]}} < /ref >  Material from the album debuted in summer 2011 during a series of concerts at the [[Manchester International Festival]]. < ref name=Perpetua/ >
