 '''Indulkana''' (also known as '''Iwantja''') is an [[Indigenous Australians|Aboriginal]] community in [[South Australia]]. It is on the [[Anangu Pitjantjatjara Yankunytjatjara|Anangu Pitjantjatjara Yankunytjatjara Lands]]. It is about {{convert|10|km}} west of the [[Stuart Highway]] and {{convert|360|km|abbr=on}} south of [[Alice Springs]]. < ref name= " pymedia " > {{cite web|url=http://waru.org/communities/indulkana/|title=Indulkana|publisher=PY Media|work=Waru|accessdate=27 October 2012|archive-date=19 July 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070719003919/http://waru.org/organisations/ap/|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  The town was established in 1968. 
 
 There were 315 people living at Indulkana in the 2006 [[Census in Australia|census]]. < ref name= " Census2006Y " > {{cite web|author=Australian Bureau of Statistics|date=25 October 2007|title=Indulkana (L) (Urban Centre/Locality)|work=2006 Census QuickStats|url=http://www.censusdata.abs.gov.au/ABSNavigation/prenav/LocationSearch?collection=Census & period=2006 & areacode=UCL409400 & producttype=QuickStats & breadcrumb=PL & action=401|accessdate=2 August 2011}} < /ref >  Most of the people belong to the [[Yankunytjatjara]] nation. The 2001 census showed that Indulkana had South Australia's highest proportion residents under the age of 15 (35.0%). < ref > ABS, Census of Population and Housing: [http://www.abs.gov.au/ausstats/abs@.nsf/productsbytopic/563CE29E965278DDCA256CF4007EA8D1?OpenDocument Selected Characteristics for Urban Centres and Localities], South Australia, 2001. < /ref >   
 
 Indulkana has a local community store that also supplies [[diesel fuel]]. There is an unsealed [[airstrip]] located close to the town. There is also a [[school]], established in 1971. Indulkana does not have a permanent police presence, though it has a police station. State police are based at [[Marla]] and run patrols to the area. In the absence of police, the town is served by two constables from the community. < ref > {{Cite web |url=http://journal.pasa.asn.au/apps/uploadedFiles/news/293/APY_lands.pdf |title=SA Police Association |access-date=2012-10-28 |archive-date=2012-02-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120207111214/http://journal.pasa.asn.au/apps/uploadedFiles/news/293/APY_lands.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The town also has a local artists' co-operative, Iwantja Arts. It has a gallery for sale of art work from across the APY. < ref > [http://www.iwantjaarts.com.au/ Iwantja Arts Website] < /ref >
