 '''Montsoreau''' ({{IPA-fr|mɔ̃soʁo}}) is a town in [[France]]. Only 500 people live in there. It is in the east end of the region of [[Pays de la Loire]], which is in the nortwestern France.  == History == 
 The main witness of the first settlement is the [[:fr:La Pierrelée|dolmen of the Pierrelée]], which probably dates from the 3rd millennium BC. Montsoreau is located on the borders of the territories of the Gallic tribes of [[Pictones]], [[Turones]] and [[Andes (Andecavi)|Andecavi]]. Coins, shards and fragments of Gallo-Roman tiles, were found in Montsoreau, especially on the edge of the plateau, above the town. 
 
 Montsoreau was identified under the name ''Restis'' (rope or fish net) at the end of [[classical antiquity]] as a port on the Loire at the [[confluence]] of the Loire and the [[Vienne (river)|Vienne]]. It kept this name until the end of the eleventh century.  
 
 The name ''Montsoreau'' (Mount Soreau) come from a rocky promontory  in the riverbed of the Loire and surrounded by water. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.ville-montsoreau.fr/user/documents/fichiers/AVAP/1b_Diagnostic.pdf|title=Diagnostic en vue de l'établissement d'une Aire de Valorisation de l'Architecture et du Patrimoine|last=Stalder|first=Florient|year=2013|website=Ville de Montsoreau}} < /ref >  There has been three major buildings on this promontory, a [[Gallo-Roman]] temple or administrative building, a [[fortification|fortified castle]], and a [[Renaissance architecture|Renaissance palace]]. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Congrès archéologique de France|last=Prigent|first=D.|publisher=Société française d'archéologie|year=2003|location=Paris|pages=255}} < /ref > 
 
 The first fortified castle was built by the count of [[Blois]] at the end of the tenth century, immediatly taken by The [[Counts and dukes of Anjou|Count of Anjou]], [[Fulk III, Count of Anjou|Fulk Nerra]], took the fortress in 1001 and incorporated it to Anjou. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Le comte d'Anjou et son entourage au XIe siècle|last=Guillot|first=O.|year=1972|location=Paris|pages=310}} < /ref >  Fulk, who was one of the first great builders of [[Medieval castle]]s, modified it, and the fortress remained under the control of Anjou, never taken, during more than 150 years.  
 
 In 1152, Guillaume IV de Montsoreau sided with [[Geoffrey Plantagenet, Count of Anjou|Geoffroy Plantagenet]] against his brother [[Henry II of England|Henry II Plantagenet]], future [[List of English monarchs|king of England]] and husband of [[Eleanor of Aquitaine]]. Henri II besieged the castle and took it at the end of August 1152 despite the care taken at its fortification. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Les anciens seigneurs de Montsoreau|last=Desme de Chavigny|first=O.|year=1888|location=Tours|pages=18}} < /ref >  This was the one and only storming of the medieval fortress of Montsoreau between [[Fulk III, Count of Anjou|Fulk]] and Jean II de Chambes in 1450. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.paysdelaloire.co.uk/visits/castles/chateau-de-montsoreau|title=CHATEAU DE MONTSOREAU: Castles France, Pays de la Loire|website=paysdelaloire.co.uk|access-date=28 September 2018|archive-date=28 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180928200947/https://www.paysdelaloire.co.uk/visits/castles/chateau-de-montsoreau|url-status=dead}} < /ref >   
 
 At the end of the [[Hundred Years' War|Hundred Years Wa]] < nowiki/ > r, Charles VII and Louis XI installed royal power in [[Chinon]]. They encouraged or ordered their lords to build new buildings or redevelop old fortresses. Thus began the construction of buildings in a new style in France, giving birth to [[Renaissance architecture]], with the renowned  " Châteaux of the Loire Valley " . < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.valdeloire.org/Connaitre/Au-fil-de-l-histoire/Le-Val-de-Loire-siege-du-pouvoir-royal/Charles-VII-et-Louis-XI|title=Charles VII et Louis XI Val de Loire patrimoine mondial|last=Loire|first=Mission Val de|website=valdeloire.org|language=fr-FR|access-date=30 September 2018}} < /ref >   
 
 In 1450, Jean II de Chambes, First councelor of [[Charles VII of France|Charles VII]] and ambassador in [[Venice]], bought the fortress of [[Fulk III, Count of Anjou|Fulk III]] to his brother in law and destroyed it in order to build a residential palace on the top of the rock of Montsoreau (the mount Soreau). < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.les-chateaux-de-la-loire.com/fr/accueil/1-chateau-de-montsoreau.html|title=Château de Montsoreau-Musée d'Art Contemporain|website=Les Châteaux de la Loire|language=fr-fr|access-date=30 September 2018|archive-date=30 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180930193225/https://www.les-chateaux-de-la-loire.com/fr/accueil/1-chateau-de-montsoreau.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref >  In an unprecedented move, he built the [[Chateau de Montsoreau|Château de Montsoreau]] in a residential style following Italian architecture of the time which makes it the first Renaissance building in France. The Château de Montsoreau was directly on the river bank and still today, it remains the only château of the Loire Valley to have been built in the river bed of the Loire. < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://parangone.fr/2017/05/28/art-et-fleuve/|title=Château de Montsoreau, l'art contemporain à portée de fleuve|website=parangone.fr|language=fr-FR|access-date=30 September 2018|archive-date=28 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170728165818/http://parangone.fr/2017/05/28/art-et-fleuve/|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 In 1572, four days after [[St. Bartholomew's Day massacre|Saint Bartholomew's Day Massacre]] (24 August) in [[Paris]], Jean de Chambes started the  " St. Bartolomew Angevine " . He almost eliminated the Reformed Church both in Saumur and Angers. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Les nobles, la ville et le roi : l'autorité nobiliaire en Anjou pendant les Guerres de religion, 1560–1598|url=https://archive.org/details/lesnobleslaville0000bour|last=Bourquin|first=Laurent|publisher=belin|year=2001|isbn=2-7011-2976-1|location=Paris}} < /ref > 
 After the [[French Revolution]], the exploitation of a building stone, the Tuffeau stone, brutally passed its population of 600 inhabitants to more than 1000, maintained during the first half of the nineteenth century. < ref > {{Cite book|title=Fontevraud-L’Abbaye et Montsoreau, un regard sur le Saumurois |last=Stalder |first=F.|publisher=303 |year=2013|location=Nantes }} < /ref > 
 
 The [[Battle of Saumur (1940)|battle of Saumur]], is considered as the first act of [[Resistance during World War II|resistance]] of [[World War II]] in France. 
 < nowiki/ > < blockquote > In Montsoreau, Saumur and Gennes, in June 1940, teenage students of the school of cavalry, still under training and with derisory weapons (including an artillery gun from the school museum), heroically engaged an entire German panzer division for nearly three days. And in doing so became a legend in France. – ''For Honour Alone'', Roy Macnab, January 1989. < ref > {{Cite web|url=https://www.historytoday.com/roy-macnab/honour-alone|last=Macnab|first=Roy|title=History Today: For Honour Alone|date=January 1989|volume=39}} < /ref >

 ==Geography== 
 === Location === 
 Montsoreau is at the center of the [[Loire Valley]], in north-western France, {{convert|160|km|mi|abbr=on}} from the [[Atlantic Ocean]], and approximately {{convert|12|km|mi|abbr=on}} from [[Saumur]], [[Chinon]] and [[Bourgueil]]. < ref > [https://www.distancecalculator.net/ Distance Calculator] < /ref >  It is in southeastern [[Maine-et-Loire]] department, about halfway between Paris and [[Bordeaux]]. < ref > Paris is {{convert|155|mi|km}} air distance from Montsoreau, and Bordeaux is {{convert|167|mi|km}} air distance from Montsoreau. & nbsp;– [https://www.distancecalculator.net/ Distance Calculator] < /ref >  The village is at the crossroad of the three main administrative regions of, [[Pays de la Loire]], [[Centre-Val de Loire]], and [[Nouvelle-Aquitaine]], and of the three departments of [[Maine-et-Loire]], [[Indre-et-Loire]], and [[Vienne]]. 
 
 Montsoreau is part of the Metropolitan Area of [[Communauté d'agglomération Saumur Val de Loire|Saumur Val de Loire]] and share borders with municipalities both in the [[Maine-et-Loire]] and [[Indre-et-Loire]] departments. These municipalities are: [[Candes-Saint-Martin]], [[Chouzé-sur-Loire]], [[Fontevraud-l'Abbaye]], and [[Turquant]].
