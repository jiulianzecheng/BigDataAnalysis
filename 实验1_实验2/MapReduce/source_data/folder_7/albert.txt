The name '''Albert'''[al-bert] < ref > {{Cite web|title=Definition of Albert {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/albert|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is a [[masculine]] < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|title=Top Names Over the Last 100 Years|url=https://www.ssa.gov/OACT/babynames/decades/century.html|access-date=2023-03-10|website=www.ssa.gov|language=en}} < /ref >  [[given name]]. It comes from the Germanic words ''adal'' and ''beraht'', meaning  " bright "  and  " noble " . < ref > {{Cite web|title=Albert name Meaning and Origin|url=https://babys.name/detail-Albert-Meaning-Origin-Famous-Similar-Sibling.html|access-date=2023-03-10|website=babys.name}} < /ref >  The [[Spanish language|Spanish]] version of this name is '''Alberto'''. This name has been used for many famous people, including [[Albert Finney]] and scientist [[Albert Einstein]].Albert has been a popular name for the past 100 years. < ref name= " :0 "  / >  This name was ranked #564 on the US Popular Names in 2021.  < ref > {{Cite web|last=OACT|title=Popular Baby Names|url=https://www.ssa.gov/cgi-bin/popularnames.cgi|access-date=2023-03-26|website=www.ssa.gov}} < /ref > 
 
 As ''Alberto'', this name has been used for [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] cyclones every six years since 1982.
