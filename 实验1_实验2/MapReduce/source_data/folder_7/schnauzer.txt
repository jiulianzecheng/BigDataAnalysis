 A '''schnauzer''' is a type of [[dog]] [[breed]] that originated in [[Germany]] in about the [[14th century]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.pet360.com/dog/breeds/all-about-standard-schnauzers/xvEO0fx1lEuZE0W6htu2cA?intcid=LINKART%20 |title=All About Standard Schnauzers |publisher=PET360 INC |access-date=11 January 2016 |archive-date=4 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304231644/http://www.pet360.com/dog/breeds/all-about-standard-schnauzers/xvEO0fx1lEuZE0W6htu2cA?intcid=LINKART%20 |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  The Standard Schnauzer is the oldest of the three Schnauzer [[wikt:variety|varieties]]: Miniature, Standard, and Giant. < ref name=StandAKC > {{cite web |url=http://www.akc.org/dog-breeds/standard-schnauzer/ |title=Meet the Standard Schnauzer |publisher= American Kennel Club |access-date=11 January 2016}} < /ref >  All three varieties have the distinctive [[whisker]]s, [[mustache]] and [[eyebrows]], which is where they take their name from as in German schnauzer means moustache. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.petmd.com/dog/breeds/c_dg_standard_schnauzer |title=Standard Schnauzer |publisher=PetMD |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >   
 
 == History == 
 The Standard Schnauzer is the [[prototype]] from which the other two were [[Breed|bred]]. < ref name=StandAKC/ >  From the [[middle ages]] on they [[Guard dog|guarded]] the [[livestock]] and family members. < ref name=SSCA > {{cite web |url=http://www.standardschnauzer.org/ss_history.htm |title=Breed Information About the Standard Schnauzer |publisher=Standard Schnauzer Club of America |access-date=11 January 2016}} < /ref >  They are descended from [[Europe|European]] [[herd]]ing and [[guard dog]]s. < ref name=SSCA/ >  As a distinctive breed, they appear in pictures of the [[14th century|14th]] and [[15th century|15th centuries]]. < ref name=Korppret > {{cite web |url=http://www.korppret.com/history.htm |title=History of the STANDARD SCHNAUZER |publisher=korppret |access-date=17 January 2016 |archive-date=17 February 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160217212359/http://www.korppret.com/history.htm |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  As far as can be determined they were bred from the Gray Wolf [[Spitz]], the German [[poodle]] (pudel) and the Wirehaired [[Pinscher]]. < ref name=Korppret/ >  About the middle of the [[19th century]] German dog [[wikt:enthusiast|enthusiast]]s took an interest in the Schnauzer. < ref name=SSCA/ >  Dog breeders [[experiment]]ed by crossing them with other breeds. First the Miniature Schnauzer was developed, then the Giant Schnauzer. < ref name=SSCA/ > 
 
 == Three varieties == 
 * A '''Miniature Schnauzer''' is an [[wikt:energy|energetic]] and [[wikt:sturdy|sturdy]] little dog. Miniature Schnauzers are also very intelligent dogs. They stand between {{convert|14|and|16|in|mm|abbr=on}} tall at the shoulder. < ref name=APG > {{cite web |url=http://www.animalplanet.com/breed-selector/dog-breeds/terrier/miniature-schnauzer.html |title=Miniature Schnauzer Guide |publisher=Animal Planet |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >  They normally weigh between {{convert|13|and|15|lb|kg|abbr=on}}. < ref name=APG/ >  The normal colors are salt and pepper (a mix of off-white, gray and black), all black, and black and silver. < ref name=AKCdetail > {{cite web |url=http://www.akc.org/dog-breeds/miniature-schnauzer/detail/ |title=Meet the Miniature Schnauzer |publisher=American Kennel Club |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >  They resemble the Standard Schnauzer from which they were bred. They were first shown as early as 1889. < ref name=AKCdetail/ > 
 
 * The '''Standard Schnauzer''' is the original breed of Schnauzer. German farmers in  kept them as [[hunting]] dogs, [[rat]] catchers and watchdogs. < ref name=Vetstreet > {{cite web |url=http://www.vetstreet.com/dogs/standard-schnauzer |title=Standard Schnauzer |publisher=Vetstreet |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >  They are a medium-sized dog with a hard, wire-like coat. It is a strong and very quick dog for its size. It is able to cover distances easily. Like all Schnauzers they have the same whiskers, eyebrows and mustache. They stand between {{convert|17|and|20|in|mm|abbr=on}} tall at the shoulder. < ref name=AKCSSdetail > {{cite web |url=http://www.akc.org/dog-breeds/standard-schnauzer/detail/|title=Meet the Standard Schnauzer |publisher=American Kennel Club |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >  Standard Schnauzers normally weigh between {{convert|35|and|50|lb|kg|abbr=on}}. < ref name=AKCSSdetail/ >  The colors are usually salt and pepper or solid black. < ref name=Vetstreet/ >   
 
 * The '''Giant Schnauzer''' resembles the Standard Schnauzer, only they are bigger and stronger. < ref name=GSAKC > {{cite web |url=http://www.akc.org/dog-breeds/giant-schnauzer/detail/ |title=Meet the Giant Schnauzer |publisher=American Kennel Club |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >  They are very [[Muscular system|muscular]] dogs. < ref name=GSAKC/ >  They stand as much as {{convert|27.5|in|mm|abbr=on}} tall at the shoulder. < ref name=GSAKC/ >  They can weigh about {{convert|95|lb|kg|abbr=on}}. < ref name=GSAKC/ >  The double coat is either solid black or “pepper and salt.” Much the same as their two smaller cousins they have the distinctive whiskers, eyebrows and mustache. < ref name=GSAKC/ >  The breed was developed in the areas around [[Württemberg]] and [[Bavaria]] as a farm dog. < ref name=AKCCDB > American Kennel Club, ''The New Complete Dog Book'', 20th Edition (Irvine, CA: I-5 Press, 2015), p. 352 < /ref >  They were used to drive livestock to market. < ref name=AKCCDB/ >  During [[World War I]] they were used as [[Guard dog#Types_of_guard_dogs|military guard dogs]] and as [[police dog]]s. < ref name=AKCCDB/ >  They still work with police today and also as [[Search and rescue]] dogs. < ref name=AKCCDB/ >  They are also popular as [[guard dog]]s. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://giantschnauzerclubofamerica.com/Giant-Schnauzer-Right-For-You.aspx |title=Is A Giant Schnauzer the Right Dog For You? |author=Daphne Cooke |date=1988 |publisher=Giant Schnauzer Club of America |access-date=17 January 2016}} < /ref >
