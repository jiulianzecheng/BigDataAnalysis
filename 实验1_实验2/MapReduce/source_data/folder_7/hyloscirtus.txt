 '''''Hyloscirtus''''' is a genus of frogs.  They are [[Neotropical]] frogs.  They are in the family [[Hylidae]]. < ref name=Frost/ >  Scientists stopped using this genus fora  time, but they started again in 2005 after they looked at the Hylidae family again. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / >  They decided to put these species in the same genus because of their DNA: They all have the same 56 transformations in nuclear and [[mitochondria]]l proteins and [[ribosome|ribosomal]] [[gene]]s. Of these species, 28 species had been in ''[[Hyla]]'' before 2005. These frogs have fringed skin on the toes of their front and back feet. < ref name= " Faivovich et al. 2005 " / > 
 
 Most of these frogs live in the hills and mountains in the [[Andes]] mountain range.  They live as far south as [[Bolivia]] and as far north as [[Venezuela]]. A few species live in places near the Andes that are not hills are mountains.  These places are called ''[[páramo]]''. Two species (''[[Hyloscirtus colymba|H. colymba]]'' and ''[[Hyloscirtus palmeri|H. palmeri]]'') live in [[Panama]] and [[Costa Rica]]. They live near streams where they lay their eggs. Some of the species in this genus are dying out.  This is because humans change the places where they live, because of pollution, because humans brought new animals that eat the same food, because humans brought [[trout]], whuch eat them, and because the [[chytrid fungus]] ''[[Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis]]'' makes them sick. < ref > Stuart, Hoffmann, Chanson, Cox, Berridge, Ramani and Young, editors (2008). ''Threatened Amphibians of the World'', pp. 249–252. {{ISBN|978-84-96553-41-5}} < /ref > 
 
 The first frogs in ''Hyloscirtus'' existed 51 million years ago. < ref name=Des > {{cite journal|author1=Coloma LA, Carvajal-Endara S, Duenas JF, Paredes-Recalde A, Morales-Mite M, Almeida-Reinoso D, Tapia EE, Hutter CR, Toral E, Guaysamin JM |year=2012 |title=Molecular phylogenetics of stream treefrogs of the Hyloscirtus larinopygion group (Anura: Hylidae) and description of two new species from Ecuador. |journal=Zootaxa |volume=3364 |pages=1–78|url=https://www.mapress.com/zt/article/view/zootaxa.3364.1.1|doi=10.11646/zootaxa.3364.1.1|type=Abstract }} < /ref >
