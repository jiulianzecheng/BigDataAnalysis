 '''Beccles''' is a small [[market town]] in [[Suffolk]], [[England]], around the area of [[The Broads]]. The town had a population of about 9, 850 people around 2005. < ref name= " scc " > {{Cite web |url=http://www.suffolk.gov.uk/NR/rdonlyres/38314C3E-02A0-4515-92FE-8909C6FDB3A3/0/Parishestimates01to05.pdf |title=Estimates of total Population of areas in Suffolk |access-date=2008-06-21 |archive-date=2008-12-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081219023551/http://www.suffolk.gov.uk/NR/rdonlyres/38314C3E-02A0-4515-92FE-8909C6FDB3A3/0/Parishestimates01to05.pdf |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  Beccles has a [[suburb]] called [[Worlingham]] which has a combined population of about 13,580. < ref name=scc/ >   Beccles is twinned with [[Petit-Couronne]] in [[France]]. 
 
 ==History== 
 [[File:BecclesTownHallc.jpg|thumb|left|Beccles Town Hall that was built in the [[18th century]].]] 
 Beccles was once a flourishing [[Saxon people|Saxon]] riverport, but today it is in the [[Waveney]] valley. There is a popular boating centre. The town was granted its [[Charter]] in [[1584]] by [[Elizabeth I of England|Queen Elizabeth I]].
