'''Panzerchrist''' is a [[Denmark|Danish]] [[extreme metal|blackened death metal]] band. It was formed in 1993–1994 by Michael Enevoldsen (after he left [[Illdisposed]]) and Danish artist [[Lasse Hoile]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Panzerchrist recorded a few [[Demo (music)|demos]] and was signed by Serious entertainment. ''Six Seconds Kill'' was released by Serious Entertainment in 1996, and in 1998 they released ''Outpost Fort Europa''. In 2000, Panzerchrist was joined by drummer Reno Killerich (ex-Exmortem, Vile, etc.) and Bo Summer (also of [[Illdisposed]]). They recorded ''Soul Collector'', which focused on [[World War II]] and had German lyrics. ''Soul Collector'' was released by Mighty Music in 2000. < ref > [http://www.metal-district.de/cdreview.php?ID=267 PANZERCHRIST – Soul Collector *CD Review*] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060529102640/http://metal-district.de/cdreview.php?ID=267 |date=2006-05-29 }} on www.metal-district.de < /ref > 
 
 In 2002, the band was joined by Frederik O'Carroll and Rasmus Henriksen. They began work on the violent ''Room Service''. It featured compositions that were very loud and fast, including a cover of the [[Metal Church]] classic  " Metal Church " . ''Room Service'' was recorded at [[Tue Madsen]]'s  " Antfarm Studios "  ([[The Haunted]], [[Illdisposed]], [[Mnemic]] etc.) and was released by Mighty Music in 2003. < ref > [http://www.metal-archives.com/band.php?id=2864 Profile on metal-archives.com] < /ref >  The name of each song on the album ''Room Service'' are the first word(s) to be sung on each song. Each song on the album ''Room Service'' ends with the words  " Death, forever panzer "  – except the song  " Metal Church " , since it is a [[Cover version|cover]]. 
 
 In 2006, Panzerchrist drummer Killerich and Karina Bundgaard, now on keyboards, rejoined for the recording of ''Battalion Beast''. This was released by Neurotic Records in 2006. < ref > [http://www.metal.de/cdreviews.php4?was=review & id=6754 PANZERCHRIST – Battalion Beast *CD Review*] on www.metal.de < /ref >  This album features the words  " Christ, thy name is panzer "  at the end of each song.
