 '''Aeacus''' was a mythological king of the island of Aegina in the Saronic Gulf. < ref name= " :0 " > {{cite web|title=Aeacus - Greek Mythology Link|url=http://www.maicar.com/GML/Aeacus.html|access-date=2021-02-05|website=www.maicar.com}} < /ref >  Aeacus was the son of [[Zeus]] and [[Aegina]]. When Aeacus died, he became one of the three judges in [[Greek underworld|Hades]]. < ref name= " :0 "  / > < ref > {{cite web|title=Aeacus {{!}} Greek mythology|url=https://www.britannica.com/topic/Aeacus|access-date=2021-05-08|website=Encyclopedia Britannica|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 He assisted [[Poseidon]] and [[Apollo]] in building the walls of [[Troy]] and he had sanctuaries in [[Athens]] and [[Aegina]]. < references / >
