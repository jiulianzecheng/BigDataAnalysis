 A '''weka''' (''Gallirallus australis''), also known as the '''Maori hen''' or '''woodhen''', is a [[flightless bird]] in the [[rallidae|rail]] family. It is [[endemic]] to [[New Zealand]]. There are four [[subspecies]]. Weka are sturdy brown birds, about the size of a chicken. They feed mainly on [[invertebrates]] and fruit. < ref name=Gill30 > Gill, Frank B. 2007. ''Ornithology''. New York: Freeman, p. 30. < /ref >
