 '''Benvenue''' is a historic [[Plantation house in the Southern United States|plantation house]] located near [[Rocky Mount, North Carolina]]. The house was built in 1844 and was expanded and [[remodeling|remodeled]] to its present [[Second Empire architecture|Second Empire]] form in 1889. It is a large {{frac|2|1|2}}-story, three bay by three bay, frame house with a one-story [[ell]] in the back. It has a steep [[mansard roof]] with [[slate]] tiles that have flower patterns on them. It was the home of Congressman [[Benjamin H. Bunn]]. < ref name = nrhpinv > {{Cite web | author =Terrell Armistead Crow and Jim Sumner| title = Benvenue| work = National Register of Historic Places - Nomination and Inventory | date =September 1980| url = https://files.nc.gov/ncdcr/nr/NS0011.pdf | format = pdf | publisher = North Carolina State Historic Preservation Office | accessdate = 2015-02-01}} < /ref > 
 
 It was listed on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] in 1982. < ref name=nris/ >
