 '''Cullybackey''' or '''Cullybacky''' ({{etymology|ga|Coill na Baice|wood of the river bend}}) < ref > [http://www.placenamesni.org/resultsdetail.phtml?entry=1297 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717190033/http://www.placenamesni.org/resultsdetail.phtml?entry=1297 |date=July 17, 2011 }} < /ref >  is a small [[village]] in [[County Antrim]], [[Northern Ireland]]. It has got lots of cafes and a very good pub. It has won Britain in bloom for 4 years running. 
 [[Image:Cullybackey. - geograph.org.uk - 497928.jpg|thumb|Orange Order banner]] 
 It is a predominantly Protestant area, and host the twelfth every 3 years. 
 
 There are 2 schools, one is the Buick Memorial Primary School and the other is Cullybackey High School.
