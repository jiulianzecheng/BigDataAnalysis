 '''Perpignan''' ({{lang-ca|'''Perpinyà'''}}) is a [[city]] in the south of [[France]]. It is also a [[Communes of France|commune]] and the capital of the [[Pyrénées-Orientales]] [[Departments of France|department]]. Perpignan was the capital of the old [[province]] and [[county]] of [[Roussillon]] (''Rosselló'' in Catalan) and continental capital of the [[Kingdom of Majorca]] in the 13th and 14th centuries. 
 
 Perpignan was named, from 2001, as a ''City of Art and History'' ({{lang-fr|Ville d'Art et d'Histoire}}) by the [[France|French]] [[Ministry]] of [[Culture]] and [[Communication]]. < ref > {{cite web |url=http://www.vpah.culture.fr/ |title=Villes et Pays d'art et d'histoire |publisher=Direction Génerale des Patrimonies |language=French |accessdate=11 July 2013 |date= |archive-date=19 August 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200819212137/http://www.vpah.culture.fr/ |url-status=dead }} < /ref > < ref name= " tourism " > {{cite web |url=http://www.perpignantourisme.com/gb/decouvrir/history-perpignan |work=Perpignan - Office de Tourisme |title=History of Perpignan |language= |accessdate=12 July 2013 |date= |archive-date=12 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120912024413/http://www.perpignantourisme.com/gb/decouvrir/history-perpignan |url-status=dead }} < /ref > 
 
 == Name of the city == 
 Perpignan is mentioned in a document from 927 as ''Villa Perpinianum''. < ref > {{cite book |last=Basseda |first=Lluís |editor-first= |editor-last= |year=1990 |title=Toponymie historique de Catalunya Nord |publisher=Revista Terra Nostra |language= French |edition= | volume=t. 1 |url= |page=565 |place=Prades |accessdate= |isbn=}} < /ref >  Later, there are other, but similar, names: 
 * ''Villa Perpiniano'' (959) 
 * ''Pirpinianum'' (11th century) 
 * ''Perpiniani'' (1176) 
 * ''Perpenyà'' (13th century); this form was the most common until the 15th century and was used even during the 17th century. < ref > {{cite book |last= |first= |editor-first=Jean |editor-last=Sagnes |year=1985 |title=Le pays catalan (Capcir, Cerdagne, Conflent, Roussillon, Vallespir) et le Fenouilledès |publisher=Société nouv. d'éd. régionales et de diff. |language= French |edition= | volume=2 |url= |pages=579–1113 |place=Pau |accessdate= |isbn=2904610014}} < /ref > 
 
 In [[Catalan language|Catalan]], the regional language of the Pyrénées-Orientales, the name of the city is ''{{lang|ca|Perpinyà}}''. < ref > {{cite book |last=Institut d’Estudis Catalans, Université de Perpignan |authorlink= |year=2007 |title=Nomenclàtor toponímic de la Catalunya del Nord |language= Catalan, French |edition=1st |url=http://publicacions.iec.cat/repository/pdf/00000044%5C00000018.pdf |page=80|place=Barcelona|accessdate=15 July 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 == History == 
 [[Image:Sección-mapa-1745-Rosellón.png|thumb|250px|left|Historical map showing Perpignan in Roussillon.]] 
 
 ===Middle Ages=== 
 The [[Middle Ages|medieval]] town of Perpignan seems to have been founded around the beginning of the 10th century (first mentioned in a document as ''villa Perpinianum'' in 927). Soon Perpignan became the capital of the [[County]] < ref group= " N " > In this case  " county "  ({{lang-fr|Comté}}) means  " land under a [[count]] (the [[United Kingdom|British]] [[earl]]). " < /ref >  of Roussillon. The last Count of Rossillon, Girard II, gave his lands in 1172 to the [[King]] of [[Aragón]] and Count of [[Barcelona]]. 
 
 Perpignan was made a partly self-[[Government|governing]] [[Medieval commune|commune]] in 1197. The French [[king]] [[Louis IX of France|Louis IX]] gave the Roussillon to the [[Kingdom]] of Aragón by the Treaty of Corbeil (1258). < ref name=pyrcat > {{cite web |url=http://pyreneescatalanes.free.fr/Histoire/Histoire.phpp |title=Histoire du Roussillon - Chronologie des évènements historiques |publisher=A la découverte des Pyrénées Catalanes |language=French |accessdate=10 July 2013 |date= }}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > 
 
 [[Image:Palaisdesrois.jpg|thumb|left|300px|Palais des rois de Majorque.]] 
 ===Kingdom of Majorca=== 
 When [[James I of Aragon|James I the Conqueror]], king of Aragon and count of [[Barcelona]], founded in 1229 the [[Kingdom of Majorca]], Perpignan became the capital of the territory on the [[mainland]]. This continued from 1278 to 1344. The ''Castle of the Kings of Majorca'' ({{lang-fr|Palais des rois de Majorque}}) was built during this period. The different parts of the kingdom were the [[Balearic Islands]], [[Northern Catalonia]] (Roussillon and [[Cerdagne]]) and the coastal area of [[Montpellier]]. 
 
 This period is considered the golden age in the history of the city. Perpignan became an important center of [[Textile|cloth]] [[industry]], [[leather]] and [[goldsmith]]s' and other [[craft]]s. < ref name=pyrcat / > 
 
 ===Principality of Catalonia=== 
 In 1344 [[Peter IV of Aragon]] took the Kingdom of Majorca and Perpignan once more became part of the County of Barcelona. A few years later Perpignan lost approximately half of its population to the [[Black Death]].  Perpignan was attacked and occupied by [[Louis XI of France]] in 1463, but in 1493 [[Charles VIII of France]] gave it back to [[Ferdinand II of Aragon]]. < ref name=pyrcat / > 
 
 Again Perpignan was taken by the French during the [[Thirty Years' War]] in September 1642. Perpignan was finally given by [[Spain]] to France, and from then on it is part of France. < ref name=pyrcat / >

 ==Coat of arms== 
 The [[coat of arms]] of Perpignan shows, as is the case in other cities in the region, the [[patron saint]] of the city. Here [[John the Baptist|St John the Baptist]] is represented in front of the [[Catalonia|Catalan]] [[colour]]s (yellow and red) with a [[Domestic sheep|lamb]] and a [[Christian cross]] in his hands, and walking above water. < ref name=blason > {{cite web|url=http://pyreneescatalanes.free.fr/Decouvrir/Catalanite/Heraldique/Perpignan_Blason.php |title=Blason de Perpignan |publisher=A la découverte des Pyrénées Catalanes |language=French |accessdate=10 July 2013 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 ==Geography== 
 Perpignan is at the centre of Roussillon, an old province organized around an [[Agriculture|agricultural]] [[plain]]. < ref name= " tourism "  / >  The city is at approximately {{Convert|15|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} from the [[Mediterranean Sea]] and about {{Convert|60|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} from the [[Pyrenees]] [[mountain range]].

 It has an area of {{Convert|68.07|km2|sqmi|abbr=on }} and its average [[altitude]] is {{Convert|52|m|ft|abbr=on}}; at the [[City and town halls|city hall]], the altitude is {{Convert|40|m|ft|abbr=on }}. < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.map-france.com/Perpignan-66100/|title=Perpignan |publisher=Map-France.com |language= |accessdate=2 June 2014 |date= }} < /ref > 
 
 Perpignan is in the [[valley]] of the [[Têt (river)|Têt]] river, in the Roussillon plain which is bordered to the south by the Pyrenees, to the west by the region of Corbières and to the east by the Mediterranean Sea. The old city is on the right side of the Têt river, where the small river La Basse joins the Têt. 
 
 It is the southernmost of the great cities of [[metropolitan France]] ({{lang-fr|France métropolitaine}}). 
 
 The cities of Pia and Cabestany are now part of Perpignan, while Bompas, Saleilles, Canohès, Toulouges, Le Soler, Baho, Saint-Estève and Rivesaltes are all close to the city limits. Nearest cities are [[Narbonne]] to the north (65 & nbsp;km), [[Montpellier]] towards the northeast (150 & nbsp;km), [[Barcelona]] to the south (190 & nbsp;km) and [[Toulouse]] to the northwest (200 & nbsp;km). The distance to [[Paris]] is {{Convert|850|km|mi|0|abbr=on}}.

 ===Climate=== 
 The climate of Perpignan is a warm [[Mediterranean climate]] ([[Köppen climate classification]] ''Cfa''), with mild winters and dry summers and warm. < ref name= " wbase " > {{cite web|publisher=Weatherbase |title=Perpignan, France - Köppen Climate Classification |url=http://www.weatherbase.com/weather/weather-summary.php3?s=74770 & cityname=Perpignan%2C+Languedoc-Roussillon%2C+France & units=metric |language= |accessdate=14 July 2013}} < /ref > 
 
 The average temperature for the year in Perpignan is 15.6 & nbsp;°C (60.0 & nbsp;°F). The warmest month, on average, is July with an average temperature of 23.9 & nbsp;°C (75.0 & nbsp;°F). The coolest month on average is January, with an average temperature of 8.3 & nbsp;°C (47.0 & nbsp;°F). 
 
 The average amount of precipitation for the year in Perpignan is {{Convert|584.0|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. The month with the most precipitation on average is October with {{Convert|78.7|mm|in|1|abbr=on}} of precipitation. The month with the least precipitation on average is July with an average of {{Convert|20.3|mm|in|1|abbr=on}}. < ref name= " wbase "  / >

 ==Population== 
 [[File:Straatnaambord-in-Perpignan-trimmed.jpg|thumb|Perpignan street name sign in French and Catalan.]] 
 The men of Perpignan are called ''perpignanais'' (women, ''perpignanaises''); in [[Catalan language|Catalan]], ''perpinyanès'' for a man and ''perpinyanesa'' for a woman. 
 
 In Perpignan, both [[French language|French]] and [[Catalan language|Catalan]] are spoken, as can be seen in the street signs. 
 
 With a population in 2014 of 120,605, < ref name=insee > {{cite web |url=http://www.insee.fr/fr/statistiques/fichier/2525755/dep66.pdf |title=Régions, départements, arrondissements, cantons et communes |work= Populations légales 2014 |publisher=Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques - INSEE |language=French |accessdate=7 February 2017|date= }} < /ref >  Perpignan has a [[population density]] of {{Pop density|120605|68.07|km2|prec=0|disp=num}} inhabitants/km < sup > 2 < /sup > .

 == Administration == 
 Perpignan is the [[Prefectures in France|prefecture]] of the Pyrénées-Orientales department since 1790. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of [[Arrondissement of Perpignan|Perpignan]] and the [[administrative centre]] ({{lang-fr|chef-lieu}}) of six [[Cantons of France|cantons]]:  
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-1|Perpignan-1]] : part of Perpignan, with 28,922 inhabitants   < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-2|Perpignan-2]] : 3 ''communes'' ([[Bompas, Pyrénées-Orientales|Bompas]], [[Sainte-Marie, Pyrénées-Orientales|Sainte-Marie]], [[Villelongue-de-la-Salanque]]) + part of Perpignan, with 29,798 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-3|Perpignan-3]] : 1 ''commune'' ([[Cabestany]]) + part of Perpignan, with 25,633 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small >   
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-4|Perpignan-4]] : part of Perpignan, with 23,862 inhabitants   < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-5|Perpignan-5]] : 1 ''commune'' ([[Canohès]]) + part of Perpignan, with 25,119 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 # [[Canton of Perpignan-6|Perpignan-6]] : 1 ''commune'' ([[Toulouges]]) + part of Perpignan, with 23,969 inhabitants  < small > (2014) < /small > 
 
 It is part of the [[Communes of France|intercommunality]] ''Perpignan Méditerranée'' ({{lang-fr|Intercommunalité-Métropole de CU Perpignan Méditerranée (Pmcu)}}).

 == Economy == 
 Traditional [[commerce]] is in [[wine]] and [[olive oil]], [[Cork (material)|cork]]s (the [[cork oak]] ''Quercus suber'' grows in Perpignan's mild climate), [[wool]] and [[leather]], and [[iron]].
