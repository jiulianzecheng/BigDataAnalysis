'''Layoff''' (also called '''redundancy''' in [[British English]]), < ref name= " dict " > {{Cite web|title=Definition of redundancy {{!}} Dictionary.com|url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/redundancy|access-date=2022-04-14|website=www.dictionary.com|language=en}} < /ref >  is when an employee does not work due to temporary or permanent [[suspension]]. There are legal differences from [[termination of employment|being fired]]. < ref name= " wg " > {{cite web|title=What is the Difference Between Being Fired and Laid Off?|url=http://www.wisegeek.com/what-is-the-difference-between-being-fired-and-laid-off.htm|accessdate=25 December 2010}} < /ref > < ref > {{cite web|date=2010-11-25|title=Laid Off vs. Fired|url=http://docs.google.com/viewer?a=v & q=cache:Wpp0M1V_EygJ:www.oregon.gov/BOLI/TA/TA_COL_091107_Laid_Off_vs_Fired.pdf+the+difference+between+layoff+and+fired & hl=en & gl=us & pid=bl & srcid=ADGEESilc3lqdH5OVpJqTtxgHVXvC7c7s4Fioeq2RLMPJ4JEWsS45qTJm114Zb8F9yV4vDuJOqDfYUVe67z6paPAMV6tOR8DgUURBPc9vMQEWe0h4xRbEHJKPCs2r3S4FerfGYjVVnYI & sig=AHIEtbSdwwGaUVMIDFtP0S2UYxeGo4W1dg|work=oregon.gov|accessdate=25 December 2010}} < /ref >  The meaning of  " layoff "  has changed over time. It first mean that there would be a temporary [[wikt:interruption|interruption]] at places like factories. In modern times,  " laying someone off "  is usually [[wikt:permanent|permanent]], although in some special cases someone can be laid off temporarily.
