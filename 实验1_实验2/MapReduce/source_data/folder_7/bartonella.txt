 '''''Bartonella''''' is a [[genus]] of [[bacteria]]. It is the only one in the family Bartonellaceae. The [[species]] can be  [[parasitic]] and cause diseases in animals and humans. < ref name= " Bergey " > George M. Garrity, Don J. Brenner, Noel R. Krieg, James T. Staley: ''Bergey's manual of systematic bacteriology''. Vol. 2: ''The Proteobacteria Part C: The Alpha-, Beta-, Delta-, and Epsilonproteabacteria''. Springer, New York 2005, {{ISBN|978-0-387-24145-6}} < /ref >  They live mostly in the cells of the host, as intracellular parasites. 
 
 ''Bartonella'' species are [[pathogen]]s. < ref name=Baron > {{cite book | author = Walker DH | title = Rickettsiae. ''In:'' Barron's Medical Microbiology ''(Barron S et al., eds.)''| edition = 4th | publisher = Univ of Texas Medical Branch | year = 1996 | url = https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/bv.fcgi?rid=mmed.section.2138| isbn = 0-9631172-1-1 }} < /ref >  ''Bartonella'' species are transmitted by [[Vector (biology)|vectors]] such as [[tick]]s, [[flea]]s, [[sand fly|sand flies]], and [[mosquito]]es. At least eight ''Bartonella'' species or [[subspecies]] are known to infect humans. < ref name=Chomel_2005 > {{cite journal | author = Chomel BB, Boulouis HJ | title = Zoonoses dues aux bactéries du genre ''Bartonella'': nouveaux réservoirs? nouveaux vecteurs? | trans-title = Zoonotic diseases caused by bacteria of the genus ''Bartonella'': new reservoirs? new vectors? | language = French | journal = Bull. Acad. Natl. Med. | volume = 189 | issue = 3 | pages = 465–77; discussion 477–80 | year = 2005 | pmid = 16149211 | doi = | url = http://www.academie-medecine.fr/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/2005.3.pdf }} < /ref > 
 
 ''Bartonella'' is named after Alberto Leonardo Barton (1870–1950), a [[medical]]  [[microbiologist]].
