 '''Conjunctions''' are words which join phrases, clauses and sentences. < ref name=Oxford > {{cite web|url=http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/words/conjunctions|title=Conjunctions|work=Oxford Dictionaries|accessdate=27 March 2014|archive-date=26 March 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140326065501/http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/words/conjunctions|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 
 Conjunctions have three basic forms which are shown in the table below. < ref name=EnglishClub > {{cite web|url=http://www.englishclub.com/grammar/conjunctions.htm|title=Conjunctions 
 |work=English Club|accessdate=28 March 2014}} < /ref > 
 
 {| class= " wikitable " 
 |- 
 ! Form 
 ! Words 
 ! Sentences 
 |- 
 | Single Word 
 || and, but, because, although, or, so, for, etc. 
 || Do you want chips '''or''' cake? 
 |- 
 | Compound 
 || provided that, as long as, in order that/to, etc. 
 || You need to exercise '''in order to''' lose weight. 
 |- 
 | Correlative < ref name=TalkEnglish > {{cite web|url=http://www.englishclub.com/grammar/conjunctions.htmhttp://www.talkenglish.com/Grammar/conjunctions-coordinating-correlative.aspx|title=Coordinating Conjunctions and Correlative Conjunctions|work=Talk English|accessdate=29 March 2014}}{{Dead link|date=January 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} < /ref > < ref name=About > {{cite web|url=http://grammar.about.com/od/c/g/correlaconhterm.htm|title=correlative conjunction|work=About|author=Richard Nordquist|accessdate=29 March 2014|archive-date=14 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140414064018/http://grammar.about.com/od/c/g/correlaconhterm.htm|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 || both/and, either/or, neither/nor, if/then, not/but, not only/but also 
 || '''Either '''Monday '''or''' Tuesday is fine. 
 '''Not only''' should you eat fruit, '''but also''' vegetables. 
 |} 
 
 Conjunctions also have two functions, as shown below. < ref name= " Oxford " / > < ref name= " EnglishClub " / > 
 
 {| class= " wikitable " 
 |- 
 ! Type 
 ! Function 
 ! Position 
 ! Example Sentences 
 |- 
 | Coordinating conjunctions 
 || Join equal (independent) parts of a sentence. 
 || Always come between the words/clauses that they join. 
 || Jack '''and''' Jill went up the hill. 
 The water was warm, '''but''' I didn't go swimming. 
 |- 
 | Subordinating conjunctions 
 || Join subordinate clauses to main clauses. 
 || Usually come at the beginning of subordinate clauses. 
 || I went swimming '''although''' it was cold. 
 |} 
 
 Although some people say it's not correct to use conjunctions at the beginning of a sentences, many famous writers do so. < ref name= " Oxford " / > < ref name= " EnglishClub " / >
