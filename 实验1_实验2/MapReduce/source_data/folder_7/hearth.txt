 A '''hearth''' (har-th) is a [[brick]] or [[rock (geology)|stone]] lined [[fireplace]] or [[oven]] that is used for [[cooking]] and [[heating]]. Historically, the hearth was the center of the home, as it provided heat for the house. Its [[Latin]] name is ''focus''. < ref > 
 [http://www.archives.nd.edu/cgi-bin/lookup.pl?stem=focus & ending= Focus], Latin Dictionary and Grammar Aid, Notre Dame University, accessed 24 October 2008 < /ref >  That is why  " [[focus]] "  is used for many kinds of central point. 
 
 In more modern times, the hearth may be section of the fireplace that can be seen. This often includes [[tile]]s around the base of the fireplace and the floor.
