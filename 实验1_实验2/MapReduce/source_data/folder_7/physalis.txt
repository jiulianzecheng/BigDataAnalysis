 The '''''Physalis''''' is a [[genus]] of [[flowering plant|plants]] that is part of the [[Solanaceae|nightshade]] [[family (biology)|family]]. There are between 75 and 90 species. All except one are native to the [[Americas]]. < ref name=whit > Whitson, M. and P. S. Manos. (2005). [https://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/25064051?uid=3739560 & uid=2 & uid=4 & uid=3739256 & sid=21102534657483 Untangling ''Physalis'' (Solanaceae) from the physaloids: a two-gene phylogeny of the Physalinae.] ''Systematic Botany'' 30(1), 216-30. < /ref > 
 
 The plants grow small orange [[fruit]] encased in a lantern-like husk that comes from the [[sepal|calyx]]. They are similar to the [[tomato]]. Since it has a mild, refreshing acidity similar to the tomato, it can be used in many of the same ways. Many ''Physalis'' species are called '''groundcherries'''. < ref name= " ITIS " > {{ITIS |ID=30587 |taxon=''Physalis'' |accessdate=2011-05-21}} < /ref >
