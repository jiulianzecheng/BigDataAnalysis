The '''''Nunsiade''''' (or ''Anunziazion'', [[Annunciation]]) is a traditional [[Friulian]] [[Liturgy|liturgical]] [[song]] for [[Advent]]. The [[text]] is taken from the first chapter of the [[Gospel of Luke]], from verse 26 to 38. Since the [[Latin]] text begins with the words  " Missus est angelus Gabriel "  it is usually called ''Missus'' in [[Italian]]. 
 
 The tradition started from [[Patriarch]] Francesco Barbaro in the [[Church (building)|church]] of [[Mary, mother of Jesus|St. Mary]] in the [[castle]] of [[Udine|Udin]], from [[1596]] and [[1616]]. Since the end of the [[17th century|17 < sup > th < /sup >  century]] many authors wrote many ''Nunsiadis'', among the other Zuan Tite Tomadin, Antoni De Zorz, Meni Pecile, Vigji Comencin, Zuan Tite Candot, Jacop Tomadin and Vitori Franz. The version of Candot < ref > {{Cite web|title=Missus (Candotti, Giovanni Battista) - IMSLP: Free Sheet Music PDF Download|url=https://imslp.org/wiki/Missus_(Candotti,_Giovanni_Battista)|access-date=2022-09-14|website=imslp.org}} < /ref >  is the most singed nowadays. 
 
 == Lyrics == 
 The [[choir]] is divided in 3 groups: one acts as [[narrator]], one for the [[Gabriel|Angel]], and the last as [[Mary, mother of Jesus|Mary]].
