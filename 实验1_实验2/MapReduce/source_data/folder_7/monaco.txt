 '''Monaco''', officially the '''Principality of Monaco''' ([[French language|French]]: Principauté de Monaco), is the second smallest [[country]] in the world after the [[Vatican City]]. Monaco is a [[city-state]], meaning that the entire country is just one city. 
 
 Monaco is located south-east of [[France]], on the [[Mediterranean Sea]] in [[Western Europe]]. Monaco's official language is [[French language|French]] while the language of [[Monégasque dialect|Monégasque]] is also a [[national language]]. Monaco's population is around 38,000 but under 10,000 of its citizens are Monégasque nationals. 
 
 The currency is the [[Euro]], even though Monaco is not part of the [[European Union]]. Monaco is considered a [[Offshore investment|tax haven]] and is often regarded as one of the wealthiest countries in the world because many rich people live there. 
 
 Monaco is famous for the [[Monte Carlo Casino]] and the [[Opéra de Monte-Carlo|Monte Carlo Opera]], is in the north-east of the country. Monaco is also famous for two car races: the [[Monte Carlo Rally]] and the [[Monaco Grand Prix]]. 
 
 == History == 
 Monaco began as a city named Monoikos and was under the control of the [[Holy Roman Empire]], which later gave it to the [[Republic of Genoa]]. < ref name= " state1 " > {{cite web|date=16 November 2011|title=Monaco|url=https://2009-2017.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/3397.htm|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210814092549/https://2009-2017.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/3397.htm|archive-date=14 August 2021|access-date=28 May 2012|publisher=State.gov}} < /ref >   
 
 In [[1297]], [[Francesco Grimaldi]] and his army captured the fortress on the [[Rock of Monaco]], but they were evicted by the Geonoese army. However, the [[House of Grimaldi|Grimaldi family]] continued to fight for Monaco over the next century. < ref > {{cite web|title=Histoire de Monaco, famille Grimaldi  & #124; Monte-Carlo SBM|url=http://fr.montecarlosbm.com/sejour-luxe-monaco/monte-carlo/histoire/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120609075355/http://fr.montecarlosbm.com/sejour-luxe-monaco/monte-carlo/histoire/|archive-date=9 June 2012|access-date=28 May 2012|publisher=Fr.montecarlosbm.com}} < /ref >  [[Rainier I of Monaco|Rainier I]] was the first Grimaldi ruler of Monaco. 
 
 The Republic of Genoa eventually lost Monaco while fighting the [[Crown of Aragon]]. In [[1419]], the [[House of Grimaldi|Grimaldi family]] purchased Monaco from Aragon. In [[1642]], [[Honoré II, Prince of Monaco|Honoré II]], gave himself the title  " Prince of Monaco " . < ref > {{cite web|title=Monaco – The Principality of Monaco|url=http://monaco.me/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200721155700/https://monaco.me/|archive-date=21 July 2020|access-date=28 May 2012|publisher=Monaco.me}} < /ref >  Until then, the rulers of Monaco were called the  " Lords of Monaco " . 
 
 In [[1793]], Monaco was invaded by France. < ref name= " Monte-carlo.mc " > {{cite web|date=|title=Important dates – Monaco Monte-Carlo|url=http://www.monte-carlo.mc/en/general/important-dates/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120501230623/http://www.monte-carlo.mc/en/general/important-dates/|archive-date=1 May 2012|publisher=Monte-carlo.mc|accessdate=28 May 2012}} < /ref >  In [[1814]], the principality became independent again. However, a year later, the [[Congress of Vienna]] designated Monaco as a [[protectorate]] of the [[Kingdom of Sardinia]]. In [[1860]], Monaco became a French protectorate once again. 
 
 At the time, the towns of [[Menton]] and [[Roquebrune-Cap-Martin]] were part of Monaco. However, the citizens of these towns wanted to be part of France. [[Charles III, Prince of Monaco|Charles III]] gave the towns to France in exchange for Monaco's full independence. < ref > {{cite web|title=History of the Principality of Monaco – Access Properties Monaco – Real-estate Agency Monaco|url=http://www.accesspropertiesmonaco.com/en/histoire|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120809043450/http://www.accesspropertiesmonaco.com/en/histoire|archive-date=9 August 2012|access-date=28 May 2012|publisher=Access Properties Monaco}} < /ref >  A treaty was signed in [[1861]] which said that France would protect Monaco in case of a war. 
 
 In the early [[1900s]], Monaco became very popular among wealthy people because of the [[Monte Carlo|Monte Carlo casino]]. In [[1910]], there was a revolution in Monaco because the people wanted a [[democracy]]. < ref > {{cite news|date=28 March 2012|title=Monaco timeline|work=BBC News|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/country_profiles/2530539.stm|url-status=live|access-date=28 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120527123632/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/country_profiles/2530539.stm|archive-date=27 May 2012}} < /ref >  Because of this, the powers of the Prince of Monaco were reduced. During [[World War II]], Monaco was occupied by [[Italy]]. 
 
 [[Rainier III, Prince of Monaco|Prince Rainier III]] famously married [[United States|American]] actress [[Grace Kelly]], who died in a car crash. In [[2005]], [[Albert II, Prince of Monaco|Albert II]] became the new Prince of Monaco. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Monaco is entirely an [[urban area]], meaning that it is all one city. There is only one [[natural resource]] in Monaco, which is fishing. The climate is a [[Mediterranean climate]]. There are [[land reclamation]] projects ongoing. 
 
 Its surface area is 2.02 square kilometres, of which approximately 0.4 were [[Land reclamation|recovered]] from the sea since 1980. 
 
 == Politics == 
 [[File:Monaco, administrative divisions - colored.svg|thumb|206x206px|Wards of Monaco]] 
 Monaco is a [[constitutional monarchy]], meaning that the [[head of state]] is a monarch with little to no say in the way the country is governed. Monaco is a [[principality]], which is a type of monarchy where the monarch holds the title of Sovereign Prince. < ref > {{cite web|date=16 November 2011|title=Monaco|url=https://2009-2017.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/3397.htm|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210814092549/https://2009-2017.state.gov/r/pa/ei/bgn/3397.htm|archive-date=14 August 2021|access-date=22 March 2012|website=State.gov}} < /ref >   
 
 The [[constitution of Monaco]] designates three councils to run the country. The three councils are: 
 
 * The [[National Council (Monaco)|National Council]], which is the [[legislature]]. It consists of 24 elected members, who may be members of [[Political party|political parties]]. The National Council meets twice a year to discuss [[Bill (proposed law)|bills]] put forward by the Prince's government. The council is presided over by a [[president]]. 
 * The [[Council of Government]], which is the [[Executive (government)|executive]] branch. The Government consists of 6 members, one of which is the [[Minister of State (Monaco)|Minister of State]], who is appointed by the Sovereign Prince. < ref > {{cite web|title=Politics|url=http://www.monaco-iq.com/politics|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120406122848/http://www.monaco-iq.com/politics|archive-date=6 April 2012|access-date=28 May 2012|publisher=Monaco-IQ}} < /ref > 
 * The [[Communal Council of Monaco|Communal Council]] is the city council. It consists of 15 elected members, one of which is the [[mayor]]. The Communal Council is in charge of the social and cultural aspects of the country. 
 
 In addition, Monaco is divided into ten administrative wards.  
 
 Monaco has no military and is protected by France. However, it does have a police force of around 515 members. 
 
 == Economy == 
 [[File:Le casino de Monte-Carlo.JPG|left|thumb|Monte Carlo Casino]] 
 [[Tourism]] is the main [[industry]]. Tourism is extremely popular during the [[Monaco Grand Prix]]. People in Monaco pay no [[income tax]]. Many rich people live in Monaco and it is known for being very wealthy. Another large industry is gambling due to the [[Monte Carlo Casino]]. 
 
 == Culture == 
 Monaco's culture is heavily influenced by its surrounding countries such as [[France]] and [[Italy]]. The main religion is [[Catholicism]]. The [[cuisine]] is influenced by that of northern Italy and southern France. 
 
 In terms of music, Monaco has an opera house, a symphony orchestra and a classical ballet company. Monaco has participated regularly in the [[Eurovision Song Contest]], winning in [[Eurovision Song Contest 1971|1971]].  
 
 There are three museums in Monaco. 
 
 The Principality of Monaco hosts major international events such as : 
 
 * [[International Circus Festival of Monte-Carlo]] 
 * [[Mondial du Théâtre]] 
 * [[Monte-Carlo Television Festival]] 
 
 Monaco also has an annual bread festival on [[September 17|17 September]] every year. 
 
 == Education == 
 Monaco has ten state-operated schools, including: seven [[nursery]] and [[Primary school|primary schools]]; one [[secondary school]]; one ''lycée'' that provides general and technological training and one lycée that provides vocational and hotel training.  
 
 There are also two grant-aided denominational private schools. 
 
 There is one university located in Monaco, namely the International University of Monaco (IUM).
