 [[File:SHSID Cafeteria Hygiene Station.jpg|thumb|Cafeteria Hygiene Station, [[Shanghai]]]]'''Hygiene''' is the act of being [[cleanliness|clean]]. Washing the body to remove [[dirt]] and [[Pathogen|germs]], [[Toothbrush|brushing the teeth]] to keep them clean, [[shaving]], using the [[toilet]] properly, and [[dress]]ing correctly are some examples. Proper hygiene is often taught to children at a young age, and it becomes a habit. People who do not have good hygiene might smell bad, lose teeth, or become [[ill]] (sick). < ref > {{Cite web|url=http://www.ole.pl/webpage/instrukcja-mycia-rak.html|title=Instrukcja mycia rąk - oświata, handel, zakłady i instytucje|access-date=2013-04-11|archive-date=2015-04-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150414161110/http://www.ole.pl/webpage/instrukcja-mycia-rak.html|url-status=dead}} < /ref > 
 First proven use of the word in English was in 1677s. The word hygiene comes from the French word hygiène, which is the western version of Greek word ὑγιεινή (τέχνη) - hugieinē technē, meaning  " (art) of health " , from ὑγιεινός (hugieinos),  " good for the health, healthy " ,  < ref > {{cite web|url=http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.04.0057:entry=u(gieino/s|title=Henry George Liddell, Robert Scott, A Greek-English Lexicon, ὑγι-εινός|website=www.perseus.tufts.edu}} < /ref >  in turn from ὑγιής (hugiēs),  " healthful, sound, salutary, wholesome " . In ancient [[Greek religion]], Hygeia (Ὑγίεια) was the daughter of [[Asclepius]] and represented health.
