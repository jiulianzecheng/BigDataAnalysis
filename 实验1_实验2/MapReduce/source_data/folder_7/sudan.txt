 '''Sudan''' is a country in [[Africa]]. The official name of Sudan is '''The Republic of the Sudan'''. Its [[Capital (city)|capital]] and largest city is [[Khartoum]]. 
 
 == Geography == 
 Sudan borders by [[Egypt]] to the north, [[Eritrea]] and [[Ethiopia]] to the east, [[South Sudan]] to the south, the [[Central African Republic]] to the southwest, [[Chad]] to the west, and [[Libya]] to the northwest. Sudan has a sea to the northeast called the [[Red Sea]]. 
 
 Sudan used to have the largest area of all the countries in Africa.  However, on July 9, 2011, the southern part of the country left and became a new country, South Sudan. Sudan now has an area of {{convert|1,861,484|km2|abbr=off}}. < ref > {{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/su.html |title=CIA - The World Factbook |work=cia.gov |year=2011|accessdate=November 29, 2011 |archive-date=February 5, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190205154022/https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/su.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It is the third largest country in Africa by area.  The [[Nile]] flows through Sudan, providing water to crops.  There are many different tribal and [[ethnic group]] < nowiki/ > s, though the country is mainly divided between the north, which has more Arabic people, and the south, which has more people of African descent. 
 
 == Culture == 
 [[File:Simple house of sudanese nomad family.jpg|thumb|260px|House of a Sudanese nomad family]] 
 People from Sudan are called '''Sudan People'''. About 45 million people live in Sudan. Almost two million people live in the capital Khartoum. < ref > {{Cite web|title=Sudan Population (2022) - Worldometer|url=https://www.worldometers.info/world-population/sudan-population/|access-date=2022-05-03|website=www.worldometers.info|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 [[Arabic language|Arabic]] is the official language of Sudan. Most of the people speak in the Sudan People dialect, called Sudan People Arabic. Beja and Hausa are other Afro-Asiatic languages that are spoken in certain parts of the country. Dinka and Nuer are the main Nilo-Saharan languages spoken in Sudan. Several Nubian and Niger-Congo languages are also spoken. As a former colony of Great Britain, English is also spoken throughout the country. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2017-08-01|title=What Languages Are Spoken In Sudan?|url=https://www.worldatlas.com/articles/what-languages-are-spoken-in-sudan.html|access-date=2022-05-03|website=WorldAtlas|language=en-US}} < /ref > 
 
 Islam is the official state religion and the majority of Sudan people is Muslim, mostly sunni. Along the southern border there are also people who are Christian or animist. < ref > {{Cite web|title=North Sudanese Culture|url=http://culturalatlas.sbs.com.au/north-sudanese-culture/north-sudanese-culture-religion|access-date=2022-05-03|website=Cultural Atlas|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 The currency of Sudan is called the Sudan People Pound (SPG) (Jinneh Sudani). < ref > {{Cite web|title=Sudanese Pound (SDG) Definition|url=https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/sdp.asp|access-date=2022-05-03|website=Investopedia|language=en}} < /ref > 
 
 == War == 
 For several years, the [[Darfur conflict]] has been going on in Sudan. Over 400,000 people have died in it.
