 '''Arcosanti''' is an [[New town|experimental town]] in [[Yavapai County, Arizona|Yavapai County]], central [[Arizona]], [[United States]]. It is 70 mi (110 km) north of [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]], at an elevation of {{Convert|3732|ft|m|abbr=out}}. The main activity of the town is making [[Bell|bells]]. Italian-American architect Paolo Soleri (1919–2013) proposed a concept he called [[arcology]]. He started constion in 1970. He wanted to show how urban conditions could be improved, while looking at minimizing the negative impact. He influenced many architects and designers, who worked with him on the project.  
 == Overview == 
 The goal of Arcosanti is to explore the concept of [[arcology]], which combines [[architecture]] and [[ecology]]. The project also wants to  minimize resource use and keep access to the natural environment. < ref > {{Cite web|title=About Paolo Soleri|url=https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/soleri/main.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170425143314/https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/soleri/main.html|archive-date=25 April 2017|website=arcosanti.org}} < /ref >  The experimental town has been built on {{Convert|25|acres|ha|abbr=out}} of a {{Convert|4060|acre|ha|adj=on}} land preserve, {{Convert|860|acres|ha|abbr=off}} of which are owned by the Cosanti Foundation, with the remainder leased from the state. 
 
 The building has happened at varying speeds, but still continues. < ref > {{Cite web|date=2 January 2020|title=Relevance Today  &  The Future|url=https://www.arcosanti.org/history/|access-date=11 August 2020|website=History and Origins of Arcosanti}} < /ref >  The most recently completed building was finished in 1989. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Kunzelman|first=Jeffrey|title=Arcosanti : Project : Background : Timeline|url=https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/history/arcosantiTimeline.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120309150039/https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/history/arcosantiTimeline.html|archive-date=9 March 2012|website=arcosanti.org}} < /ref >  The population has varied between 50 and 150 people.  Many of them are students and volunteers. The goal was for Arcosanti to house a population of 5,000 people. < ref > {{Cite web|last=Kunzelman|first=Jeffrey|title=Arcosanti : Project : Background : History|url=https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/history/main.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120107005731/https://www.arcosanti.org/project/background/history/main.html|archive-date=7 January 2012|website=arcosanti.org}} < /ref >  So far, thirteen major structures have been built on the site.
