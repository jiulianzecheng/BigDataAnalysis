 '''Orizaba''' is a [[city]] and [[municipalities of Mexico|municipality]] in the [[Mexico|Mexican]] [[Mexican state|state]] of [[Veracruz]]. It is 20 & nbsp;km west of its [[twin city]] [[Córdoba, Veracruz|Córdoba]]. It sits at the base of [[Pico de Orizaba]], the highest [[peak]] in Mexico. < ref name=Secor93 > R. J. Secor, ''Mexico's Volcanoes: A Climbing Guide'' (Seattle, WA: Mountaineers Books, 2001), p. 93 < /ref >  It is also a [[volcano]] with a perfectly shaped snow-covered cone. < ref > ''Plate Tectonics, Volcanoes, and Earthquakes'' (New York, NY : Britannica Educational Pub. in association with Rosen Educational Services, 2011), p. 153 < /ref >  The city had a 2005 [[census]] population of 117,273 and occupies almost the same [[:wikt:area|area]] as its small municipality. The municipality includes a few small areas outside the [[city]].  It has an area of 27.97 & nbsp;km² (10.799 sq mi). 
 
 It was an important town during the [[Spanish colonization of the Americas]]. On January 27, 1774, the Spanish [[king]] [[Charles III of Spain|Carlos III]] gave town status to Orizaba, and on November 29, 1830 Orizaba was declared a city.
