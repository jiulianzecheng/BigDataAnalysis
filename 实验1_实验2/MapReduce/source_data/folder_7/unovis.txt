'''UNOVIS''' was an important group of [[Russia]]n [[artist]]s. < ref name=RAGG > {{cite web |url=http://www.russianavantgard.com/unovis-c-6.html |title=UNOVIS - Affirmers of New Art - Uchrediteli Novogo Iskusstva - УНОВИС - Учредители Нового Искусства |publisher=Russian Avant-garde Gallery |access-date=16 December 2015 |archive-date=13 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313123926/http://www.russianavantgard.com/unovis-c-6.html |url-status=dead }} < /ref >  It was taken over by [[Kazimir Malevich]] at the Vitebsk [[Art School]] in 1919. It started of with a group of [[student]]s and was called MOLPOSNOVIS. The group developed new idea about [[art]]. Once Malevich became the [[leader]] they changed their name to UNOVIS. The group only lasted until 1926. < ref name=RAGG/ > 
 UNOVIS stood for  " Utverditeli Novovo Iskusstva "  which is [[Russian language|Russian]]. In [[English language|English]] it meant  " affirmers of the new art " . < ref > ''Avant-garde Film'', eds.  Alexander Graf; Dietrich Scheunemann (Amsterdam; New York: Rodopi, 2007), p. 9 < /ref > 
 
 ==Foundation and growth== 
 The group had many changes. First known as MOLPOSNOVIS, after the school's [[teacher]]s joined and it became POSNOVIS. < ref >  R. Bruce Elder, ''Harmony and Dissent: Film and Avant-garde Art Movements in the Early Twentieth Century'' (Waterloo, ON: Wilfrid Laurier University Press, 2008), p. 189 < /ref >  In 1920, Malevich was [[wikt:invite|invite]]d them to [[Teaching|teach]] at the school in 1919 by [[Marc Chagall]]. In February of the same year Malevich became leader. < ref name=RAGG/ >
