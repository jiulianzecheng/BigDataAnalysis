 '''Kajenglei''' ({{lang-omp|Kachenglei}}), also known as '''Leitreng''' ({{lang-omp|Leitaleng}}), is a [[Meitei culture|traditional Meitei]] headdress [[crown]], worn by women. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gxqeDwAAQBAJ & dq=kajenglei+head+gear & pg=PA198|title=Glimpses of Manipuri Culture|last=Devi|first=Dr Yumlembam Gopi|publisher=Lulu.com|isbn=978-0-359-72919-7|language=en}} < /ref > < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3HCbDwAAQBAJ & dq=kajenglei+headgears & pg=PA177|title=FACETS OF CONTEMPORARY HISTORY|last1=Thilakavathy|first1=M.|last2=Maya|first2=R. K.|date=2019-06-05|publisher=MJP Publisher|language=en}} < /ref >  It is worn by brides during marriage ceremonies as well as by artists during the dance performances. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-CzSQKVmveUC & dq=kajenglei+head+ornaments+bride+marriage+ceremony+dance+performances & pg=PA289|title=Manipur, Past and Present: The Heritage and Ordeals of a Civilization|last=Sanajaoba|first=Naorem|date=1988|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-7099-853-2|language=en}} < /ref > 
 It consists of eighty to hundred brass strips. These are attached to red flannel strips one centimeter in width and tied around a circular metal ring. < ref > {{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xRbkAAAAMAAJ & q=kajenglei+leitreng+eight+hundred+strips+flannel+circular+metal+ring|title=Dances of Manipur: The Classical Tradition|last=Doshi|first=Saryu|date=1989|publisher=Marg Publications|isbn=978-81-85026-09-1|language=en}} < /ref >  Some of the renowned figures who are known for wearing the ''Kajenglei'' are goddess [[Leimarel Sidabi]], goddess [[Panthoibi]] and princess [[Moirang Thoibi]].
