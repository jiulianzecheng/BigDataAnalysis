 '''Lobbying''' is the act of trying to persuade people or [[Government|governments]] to make decisions or support something. Lobbying can be done by many sorts of people, alone or in groups. Often it is done by big [[business]]es, [[political parties]], [[social movement]]s, or [[ethnic group]]s. It can also be done by [[politicians]] and by ordinary [[citizens]]. '''Lobbyists''' are people whose [[job]] it is to lobby for big businesses or [[political parties]]. < ref name= " :0 " > {{Cite web|date=1 October 2008|title=UK Politics {{!}} Lobbying|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/politics/82529.stm|access-date=2021-03-22|website=BBC News}} < /ref >  The word lobbying comes from the [[lobby]] of the [[Palace of Westminster]] in London. The lobby is between the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons]] and the [[House of Lords]], and there people would try to meet politicians as they came in and went out. < ref name= " :0 "  / >
